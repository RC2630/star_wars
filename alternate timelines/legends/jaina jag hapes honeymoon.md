# Part 1

The journey from Sakuub to Hapes was far more comfortable than the climb up to the Sky Temple had been.

By the time they departed Sakuub, Jagged Fel's leg had healed considerably. Jaina had spent several days treating the blaster wound with a combination of Force healing techniques and bacta ointment, refusing to let him do anything strenuous until she was satisfied that the injury would not reopen.

Jag, meanwhile, had spent much of those days reminding her that none of it would have been necessary if she had not turned their honeymoon into a Jedi expedition.

"You promised Hapes afterward," he said as they boarded their yacht.

Jaina smiled.

"And we're going to Hapes."

"You also promised no more Jedi ruins."

"No more Jedi ruins."

"No mysterious Force nexuses."

"No mysterious Force nexuses."

"No ancient prophecies."

"No ancient prophecies."

Jag narrowed his eyes.

"You are being suspiciously agreeable."

"That's because I know you're right."

"I should record that."

"Don't push your luck."

The first days on Hapes were everything Jag had originally envisioned.

There were no hidden Sith.

No ancient Jedi artifacts.

No blaster fights.

No mountains.

No Hutts.

No one attempting to kill them.

For the first time since the wedding, they were simply husband and wife.

One morning they walked along white beaches beneath a clear blue sky.

Another afternoon they sailed across a tranquil bay.

In the evenings they dined together in quiet restaurants overlooking the sea.

On the third day, Jag was beginning to believe they might actually finish a week without anyone shooting at them.

That illusion lasted until lunchtime.

He and Jaina were sitting at an outdoor café overlooking the water when a small voice spoke behind them.

"Hello."

Both turned.

Standing there was an eight-year-old girl with bright red hair.

Allana Solo.

Jag stared.

Jaina stared.

Allana smiled innocently.

"We've been looking for you."

Jag blinked.

"We?"

Allana pointed at herself.

"Me."

A long silence followed.

Finally Jaina managed:

"Allana."

"Hi, Aunt Jaina."

"What are you doing here?"

"I came to visit."

"How did you get here?"

Allana shrugged.

"I traveled."

"You traveled."

"Yes."

"From the Fountain Palace."

"Yes."

"By yourself."

"Mostly."

Jag slowly lowered his cup.

"How did nobody stop you?"

Allana considered this.

"I didn't tell anyone."

"That is not an answer."

"It worked."

Jaina covered her face with one hand.

"A Force vision."

"What?"

"A Force vision," Jaina repeated. "It's the only explanation I can think of."

Allana's smile widened.

Jaina pointed at her.

"That smile is not helping your case."

Allana ignored the accusation.

Instead she announced:

"You are my parents now."

Jag nearly choked.

Jaina looked horrified.

"What?"

"You are my parents now."

"No we're not."

"Yes you are."

"No."

"Mom says families are complicated."

"That does not mean—"

"You are my parents now."

Jag and Jaina exchanged a look.

Neither knew how to respond.

Unfortunately, Allana interpreted their silence as acceptance.

"Good."

She sat down.

Neither adult could figure out how to remove her.

—

The honeymoon abruptly gained an energetic third participant.

Allana followed them everywhere.

She accompanied them to meals.

She accompanied them on walks.

She accompanied them shopping.

Whenever someone asked who she was, she cheerfully informed them:

"My parents."

Jaina gave up correcting people after the third day.

Jag gave up after the fifth.

One evening they were walking along the shoreline when Jag sighed.

"This is the strangest honeymoon in galactic history."

Allana grabbed his hand.

"I think it's nice."

Jag looked down at her.

"You kidnapped yourself."

"I traveled."

"You absolutely kidnapped yourself."

Allana grinned.

"Maybe."

—

Several days later, the inevitable happened.

Tenel Ka arrived.

The Queen Mother stormed into the resort with the expression of a woman who had spent several days searching the planet for her missing daughter.

She found Allana immediately.

The child was eating dessert with Jaina and Jag.

Tenel Ka closed her eyes.

"Allana."

"Hi, Mom."

"Do you have any idea how worried everyone has been?"

"Probably."

"Then why did you leave?"

"I wanted to visit."

Tenel Ka took a slow breath.

"You're coming home."

Allana immediately shook her head.

"No."

Tenel Ka blinked.

"No?"

"No."

Jaina stood.

"Allana—"

"No."

Jag was already rubbing his temples.

Tenel Ka folded her arms.

"And why not?"

"Because I like it here."

"That is not a sufficient reason."

"It is to me."

Tenel Ka looked toward Jaina.

Jaina looked back helplessly.

Then Allana brightened.

"I have an idea."

Everyone immediately became nervous.

Whenever a Solo announced they had an idea, trouble usually followed.

"What idea?" Jag asked.

Allana pointed dramatically.

"If Mom wants me back, she has to duel Aunt Jaina."

Silence.

Tenel Ka stared.

Jaina stared.

Jag stared.

Allana beamed.

"No."

"Why not?"

"Because that's ridiculous."

"It seems fair."

"It is not fair."

"Then I guess I'm staying."

Tenel Ka sighed.

Jaina sighed.

Jag sighed.

Allana looked delighted.

—

A short time later, two lightsabers ignited.

On one side stood Jaina Solo Fel.

On the other stood Tenel Ka Djo.

Jag and Allana sat together nearby.

Allana was practically vibrating with excitement.

"This is amazing."

Jag folded his arms.

"This is insane."

The duel began.

For several minutes the two women exchanged strikes and counters.

Tenel Ka was skilled.

Jaina was better.

Jag could see it.

Tenel Ka could see it.

Jaina could see it.

After a while, Jaina began making mistakes.

Or rather, she began pretending to make mistakes.

Tiny openings appeared.

A fraction too slow here.

A fraction too wide there.

Tenel Ka noticed immediately.

The Queen Mother gave her a look.

Jaina responded with an almost invisible shrug.

A few moments later Tenel Ka disarmed her.

Allana leapt to her feet.

"Mom won!"

Jaina bowed dramatically.

"I am defeated."

Tenel Ka rolled her eyes.

Allana looked completely satisfied.

"Okay. I'll go home now."

Jag stared.

"That's it?"

"Yep."

"You were willing to stay forever until there was a duel."

"Correct."

"And now you're leaving."

"Correct."

Jaina shook her head.

"Allana."

The girl grinned.

"I just wanted to watch you two fight."

All three adults froze.

Tenel Ka narrowed her eyes.

"What?"

"You never duel anymore."

"So you engineered all of this?"

Allana nodded proudly.

"It worked."

Jag closed his eyes.

Jaina covered her face.

Tenel Ka looked toward the sky as if questioning every life decision she had ever made.

Finally Jag spoke.

"You crossed half the planet."

"Yes."

"You infiltrated a honeymoon resort."

"Yes."

"You convinced us you had run away."

"Yes."

"Just to watch a lightsaber duel."

"Yes."

Jag sighed.

"Remarkable."

—

Eventually Tenel Ka collected her daughter.

The Queen Mother apologized repeatedly.

Jaina assured her it was not necessary.

Jag assured her that after everything he had experienced since marrying into this family, nothing surprised him anymore.

As their transport departed, Allana waved enthusiastically through the viewport.

"Bye, Mom and Dad!"

Jaina groaned.

Tenel Ka buried her face in one hand.

The ship disappeared into the distance.

Silence settled over the beach.

Jag watched the horizon.

Then he shook his head.

"All the Solos are unhinged."

Jaina laughed.

"That's not true."

"It absolutely is."

"You're exaggerating."

"Am I?"

Jaina crossed her arms.

"Yes."

Jag began counting on his fingers.

"Your grandfather routinely ignored authority."

"That's one person."

"Your father spent decades smuggling."

"Reasonable."

"No."

"Reasonable by Solo standards."

"Exactly my point."

Jaina laughed.

Jag continued.

"Your niece just orchestrated an interstellar disappearance and a lightsaber duel because she wanted entertainment."

"That was admittedly unusual."

"Unusual?"

"Very unusual."

Jag pointed toward the sky where Allana's ship had vanished.

"That child is eight."

"She's creative."

"She's dangerous."

"She's eight."

Jag shook his head.

"This is Han Solo's fault."

Jaina snorted.

"Not everything is Dad's fault."

"It is."

"It isn't."

"It is."

Jaina laughed again.

"And what exactly is my fault?"

Jag pointed at her.

"Your endless pursuit of duty, purpose, missions, causes, responsibilities, and generally saving the galaxy."

"That's Leia's fault?"

"Absolutely."

"You're impossible."

"I'm correct."

"You are not."

"I married into this family. I have become an expert witness."

Jaina slipped her arm through his.

"And your professional conclusion?"

Jag looked at her.

"My professional conclusion is that no descendant of Han and Leia has ever been normal."

Jaina pretended to consider it.

Then she nodded.

"Fair."

"See?"

"Although."

"What?"

"You married one."

Jag groaned.

"Oh no."

"Oh yes."

She kissed his cheek.

Jag sighed dramatically.

"Too late now."

Together they turned back toward the resort, finally alone again.

At least for the moment.

---

# Part 2

A few days after the incident with Allana's self-appointed adoption and the impromptu lightsaber duel, peace finally seemed to have returned.

Jaina and Jag were enjoying breakfast on a balcony overlooking the sea when Jaina's comlink chirped.

She glanced at the display.

"It's Mom."

Jag immediately looked suspicious.

"That's never a good sign."

Jaina answered.

"Mom?"

Leia Organa Solo appeared on the holo.

"Jaina. Jag. I need both of you to come to the Fountain Palace."

Jaina frowned.

"Now?"

"Now."

"What happened?"

Leia folded her arms.

"I've heard some very interesting stories."

Jaina groaned.

Jag covered his face.

"Oh no."

—

The Fountain Palace turned out to be only a short trip away.

The resort was close enough that even an adventurous eight-year-old had somehow managed to sneak from one to the other on foot.

When Jaina and Jag arrived, they found quite a gathering already waiting.

Han was there.

Leia was there.

Tenel Ka was there.

And sitting proudly in the middle of everything was Allana.

The moment she saw them she waved enthusiastically.

"Mom! Dad!"

Jaina sighed.

Han immediately burst out laughing.

Leia shot him a look.

"Han."

"What?"

"You are not helping."

"I haven't said anything."

"You don't need to."

Jag muttered:

"She's definitely a Solo."

Han pointed at him.

"See? He gets it."

Leia ignored both of them.

She turned toward Jaina.

"Sit."

Jaina sat.

Leia turned toward Jag.

"You too."

Jag sat.

The interrogation began.

—

It quickly became apparent that Han and Leia had arrived to collect Allana for another stay at the Jedi Academy on Shedu Maad.

The arrangement had existed for years.

Part of Allana's life was spent on Hapes with Tenel Ka.

Part of it was spent with Han, Leia, and the Jedi.

This was supposed to have been an ordinary pickup.

Then Tenel Ka had explained what happened.

Then Allana had explained what happened.

The two versions had differed dramatically.

According to Tenel Ka, Allana had escaped the palace, crossed half the countryside, infiltrated a honeymoon resort, and manipulated several adults into participating in a lightsaber duel.

According to Allana, she had heroically rescued Jaina and Jag's honeymoon from boredom.

Han had immediately accepted the story.

"That sounds exactly like Allana."

Leia had been less convinced.

Now she was asking questions.

Lots of questions.

Very detailed questions.

Jaina eventually found herself recounting the entire honeymoon from the beginning.

The trip to Sakuub.

The hidden purpose.

The Sky Temple.

The holocron.

The ambush.

Jag getting shot.

The Hutt intervention.

The trip to Hapes.

Allana's appearance.

The duel.

Everything.

Leia listened carefully.

Occasionally she asked Jag for confirmation.

Occasionally she asked Tenel Ka.

Occasionally she asked Allana.

The answers were not always consistent.

At one point Allana claimed she had infiltrated the resort using "advanced espionage."

Jag asked what that meant.

Allana admitted she had simply walked through the front entrance.

Eventually Leia leaned back.

Realization dawned.

She looked toward Jaina and Jag.

"You weren't involved in this."

"No."

"You had no idea she was coming."

"No."

"You didn't help her plan any of it."

"No."

Leia slowly looked toward Allana.

Allana smiled.

Leia sighed.

Han looked delighted.

"I knew it."

"Of course you knew it."

"I raised three children."

"That's not helping your argument."

"It helps my argument perfectly."

—

Afterward Leia contacted Luke.

Unfortunately Luke did not believe the story.

Not entirely.

A few hours later he arrived personally.

Ben came with him.

Luke walked into the room.

Looked around.

Looked at Allana.

Looked at Jaina.

Looked at Jag.

Then sat down.

"Explain."

Ben immediately pulled up a chair.

"This sounds good."

Han grinned.

"Oh, you're going to love this one."

—

The story had to be told again.

And again.

And again.

By the end of the evening all eight of them were gathered together.

Tenel Ka.

Allana.

Jaina.

Jag.

Han.

Leia.

Luke.

Ben.

Somehow a honeymoon had become a family summit.

Luke listened to the entire sequence of events.

The honeymoon.

The hidden Jedi expedition.

The holocron.

The firefight.

Jag's injury.

The Hapes vacation.

Allana's disappearance.

The duel.

Everything.

When the story finally ended, Luke sat silently for several seconds.

Then he turned toward Jaina.

"You took your husband on a secret Jedi mission during your honeymoon?"

Jaina pointed at Han.

"I learned from family."

Han looked pleased.

Leia looked annoyed.

Luke looked unsurprised.

Ben was laughing too hard to contribute.

Jag folded his arms.

"Thank you."

"You're welcome."

Then Luke looked toward Allana.

"And you crossed half the countryside by yourself?"

Allana nodded.

"Yes."

"Why?"

"I wanted to watch a duel."

Luke closed his eyes.

Ben nearly fell out of his chair laughing.

—

At some point the discussion ceased being an investigation and became a celebration.

The family remained together at the Fountain Palace.

Jaina and Jag quietly abandoned any hope of returning to their original honeymoon plans.

The resort no longer seemed worth it.

First Sakuub had happened.

Then Allana had happened.

Then Leia had summoned them to the palace.

At this point fate was clearly trying to tell them something.

So they stayed.

The remaining days were spent together.

Jaina spent time with Tenel Ka.

Jag spent time with Han.

Luke and Ben joined various conversations.

Allana drifted between everyone.

The palace became unexpectedly lively.

—

The final evening ended with a massive feast.

The entire family attended.

Stories were exchanged.

Toasts were offered.

Embarrassing memories were shared.

Han repeatedly referred to the honeymoon as a success.

Nobody agreed with him.

Eventually Luke raised a glass.

"To Jaina and Jag."

Everyone echoed the toast.

Jaina smiled.

Jag shook his head.

"I still got shot."

Han laughed.

"Details."

—

The next morning departures began.

Han and Leia were taking Allana back to the Jedi Academy on Shedu Maad.

Jaina and Jag were headed there as well.

As everyone gathered near the landing platforms, Han gestured proudly toward the Millennium Falcon.

"Come on, kid."

Allana looked at it.

Then looked at Jag and Jaina's yacht.

Then looked back at Han.

"I want to ride with Aunt Jaina and Uncle Jag."

Han froze.

"What?"

"Their ship is cool."

Han looked personally wounded.

"The Millennium Falcon is the most famous ship in the galaxy."

Allana shrugged.

"I still want the other one."

Han placed a hand over his heart.

Leia patted his shoulder.

"You'll survive."

"I don't know if I will."

Allana was already running toward Jaina and Jag's yacht.

Han stared after her.

"This family is unbelievable."

Jag smiled.

"Now you know how I feel."

—

Soon the departures began.

Luke and Ben left first.

Han and Leia followed.

Jaina and Jag departed with Allana aboard their yacht.

Eventually the sky grew quiet.

The palace returned to normal.

For the first time in days, Tenel Ka found herself alone.

She stood on a balcony overlooking the sea.

The silence felt strange.

She exhaled slowly.

Then sighed.

Deeply.

Very deeply.

In some ways she understood Jag's concerns.

She had not been born into the Solo family either.

She had joined it through circumstance and affection.

And she had spent years watching its members generate chaos almost effortlessly.

Now she had a Solo daughter.

A genuine Solo daughter.

The implications were terrifying.

Jag did not have children yet.

Not yet.

Tenel Ka privately hoped he would be prepared when the time came.

Watching him survive a Solo wife had been educational.

Future Solo children would be a completely different challenge.

Her thoughts drifted toward Luke and Ben.

They were different.

Not lesser.

Not greater.

Simply different.

The same courage.

The same determination.

The same intensity.

The same willingness to stand against impossible odds.

But there was less mischief.

Less recklessness.

Less talent for turning ordinary situations into extraordinary disasters.

The difference, she reflected, might be Han.

The Skywalkers possessed tremendous strength.

The Solos possessed that same strength combined with Han Solo's unique ability to charge directly into chaos and somehow emerge victorious.

It was a remarkable combination.

And a dangerous one.

Tenel Ka found herself smiling despite everything.

The family was complicated.

The family was exhausting.

The family was occasionally impossible.

But it endured.

And as she watched the departing ships vanish into the horizon, she knew it would continue to endure for many years to come.
