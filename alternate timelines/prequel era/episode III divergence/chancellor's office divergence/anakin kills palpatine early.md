# Part 1

The corridors outside the Supreme Chancellor's private office were quiet, lit by the warm glow of Coruscant's evening skyline beyond the towering windows. Anakin Skywalker stood facing Chancellor Palpatine, his lightsaber ignited and held ready.

Only moments earlier, the Chancellor had revealed knowledge no ordinary politician should possess. The conversation had turned from the Jedi, to the Force, and finally to the dark side itself. Piece by piece, Anakin had realized the truth that had been hidden in plain sight for years.

Palpatine was a Sith Lord.

The revelation hung between them.

Palpatine remained calm despite the blue blade pointed in his direction.

"I know what has been troubling you," he said. "Listen to me. Don't continue to be a pawn of the Jedi Council. Ever since I've known you, you've been searching for a life greater than that of an ordinary Jedi. A life of significance. A life of conscience."

"You're wrong," Anakin replied.

Palpatine studied him carefully.

"Are you going to kill me?"

Anakin's jaw tightened.

"I would certainly like to."

"I know you would. I can feel your anger. It gives you focus. Makes you stronger."

Anakin stepped forward until the tip of his lightsaber hovered at the Chancellor's throat.

For a long moment neither man moved.

The distant sounds of traffic echoed far below the towering government district.

Palpatine's expression remained unreadable.

Anakin thought of the war. He thought of the years of manipulation and bloodshed. He thought of every strange coincidence, every secret, every lie. He thought of the Chancellor's own admission that he possessed knowledge of the dark side.

The realization struck him with absolute certainty.

The Sith Lord standing before him was the architect of everything.

Before Palpatine could speak again, Anakin drove his lightsaber forward.

The blue blade pierced straight through the Chancellor's throat.

Palpatine's eyes widened in shock.

For the first time since Anakin had known him, the Chancellor seemed completely unprepared.

He staggered backward.

The lightsaber withdrew with a hiss.

Palpatine collapsed heavily onto the polished floor of his office.

Within seconds, he lay motionless.

Silence filled the room.

Anakin stared at the body.

The reality of what he had done settled over him immediately.

The Supreme Chancellor of the Republic was dead.

Without another word, Anakin deactivated his weapon, clipped it to his belt, and turned away.

He left the office and hurried through the corridors of the Senate District.

—

The Jedi Temple's landing platforms were busy with activity when Anakin arrived.

News from the Outer Rim had just reached Coruscant.

General Grievous, commander of the Separatist droid armies, had reportedly been destroyed by Obi-Wan Kenobi on Utapau.

The war that had consumed the galaxy for years finally seemed close to ending.

Near one of the temple gunships, four Jedi Masters prepared for departure.

Mace Windu stood at their center.

Beside him were Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar.

They were already moving toward the waiting transport when Anakin approached.

"Master Windu," he called.

Mace turned.

"What is it, Skywalker? We are in a hurry. We have just received word that Obi-Wan has destroyed General Grievous. We are on our way to make sure the Chancellor returns emergency powers back to the Senate."

Anakin stopped before them.

His expression immediately caught their attention.

Something was wrong.

"I've learned a terrible truth," Anakin said.

The four Masters exchanged brief glances.

"What truth?" Mace asked.

Anakin took a breath.

"I think Chancellor Palpatine is a Sith Lord."

The words alone would have been enough to stop them.

But Anakin was not finished.

"And so I killed him."

For several seconds, nobody spoke.

The noise of workers and machinery around the landing platform seemed to vanish.

Kit Fisto blinked.

Saesee Tiin stared at Anakin in disbelief.

Agen Kolar looked as though he was waiting for the young Jedi Knight to explain that he had misspoken.

Mace Windu's eyes narrowed.

"You did what?"

"I confronted him in his office," Anakin replied. "He admitted knowledge of the dark side. He tried to persuade me to learn from him. I became convinced he was a Sith Lord."

"And then?" Mace asked.

"I killed him."

The silence that followed felt even heavier.

Mace studied Anakin carefully.

There was no sign of deception.

No sign of panic.

Only certainty.

"You killed the Supreme Chancellor of the Republic based on your suspicion?" Agen Kolar asked.

"It wasn't a suspicion," Anakin said. "I know what I heard."

"Even so," Kit Fisto said carefully, "that decision was not yours to make."

Saesee Tiin folded his arms.

"If what you're saying is true, then the implications are enormous."

"If it's true," Mace added.

Anakin met his gaze.

"It is."

Mace considered the situation for several moments.

Finally he spoke.

"Skywalker, remain in the Temple."

Anakin frowned.

"Master—"

"Remain here," Mace repeated firmly. "Do not leave. Do not discuss this with anyone else until we know exactly what happened."

The other Masters nodded.

"We will investigate personally," Mace said. "If the Chancellor truly was a Sith Lord, evidence will remain. If not..."

He left the sentence unfinished.

Anakin understood.

The consequences would be severe.

Without another word, the four Jedi Masters boarded the gunship.

Moments later it lifted from the landing platform and sped toward the Senate District.

—

The journey to the Chancellor's office was conducted largely in silence.

None of the Masters wanted to voice the possibilities aloud.

Either Anakin Skywalker had uncovered the greatest threat the Jedi Order had faced in generations—

—or he had just murdered the head of the Republic.

There seemed little room between those two conclusions.

When they arrived at the Chancellor's office complex, security personnel were already beginning to notice something was wrong.

The Jedi moved quickly through the building.

Palpatine's personal guards attempted to question them, but Mace Windu's authority as a senior member of the Jedi Council allowed them access.

Eventually they reached the private office.

The doors slid open.

The room was exactly as Anakin had described.

Palpatine's body lay on the floor.

A fatal wound pierced his throat.

For a long moment, none of the Masters spoke.

The reality of the scene was impossible to ignore.

Mace slowly approached the corpse.

Kit Fisto examined the surrounding area.

Saesee Tiin and Agen Kolar searched the office.

The Chancellor's desk, communications systems, private chambers, and personal effects were all inspected.

At first they found nothing unusual.

Then Agen Kolar called out.

"Master Windu."

The others joined him.

Among the Chancellor's possessions was a cylindrical metal hilt unlike anything a politician should possess.

Every Jedi in the room recognized it instantly.

A lightsaber.

Mace picked it up.

The weapon felt well-used.

Not ceremonial.

Not decorative.

A real weapon.

The four Masters exchanged grim looks.

Slowly, Mace ignited it.

A crimson blade erupted from the hilt.

The red light bathed the room.

Nobody spoke.

The evidence was now impossible to dismiss.

Only practitioners of the dark side forged lightsabers with blades of that color.

Mace deactivated the weapon.

The office seemed darker once the red glow disappeared.

For the first time since arriving, the Jedi Masters began to understand that Anakin's incredible claim might have been true.

Mace clipped the recovered lightsaber to his belt.

"We're taking this back to the Temple."

The others nodded.

There was still much to investigate.

Questions remained unanswered.

But one thing had become very difficult to deny.

The dead Chancellor had possessed the weapon of a Sith.

—

Not long afterward, the Jedi gunship descended onto the Temple landing platform once more.

Anakin had remained exactly where he had been ordered to stay.

When he saw the Masters disembark, he immediately approached them.

No one spoke at first.

Mace Windu stepped forward.

Then he removed the weapon from his belt and held it up.

Palpatine's lightsaber.

Without a word, Mace ignited it.

A blade of pure red energy burst into existence between them.

The crimson glow reflected across Anakin's face.

The other Jedi stood silently beside Mace.

For the first time since arriving at the Temple, there was no disbelief in their eyes.

Only grim realization.

Mace deactivated the weapon.

The silence lingered as all five Jedi considered the implications of what they had discovered.

# Part 2

The five Jedi remained in the Temple for several hours after returning from the Chancellor's office.

Anakin Skywalker sat across from Mace Windu, Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar in one of the Temple's council chambers. The crimson lightsaber recovered from Palpatine's office rested on the table between them.

No one doubted any longer that the weapon belonged to a practitioner of the dark side.

The debate had shifted to another question entirely.

Whether Anakin had been justified in killing Palpatine immediately.

"You should have reported your suspicions to the Council," Agen Kolar said.

"And allowed a Sith Lord to continue operating?" Anakin replied.

"We don't know what would have happened," Kit Fisto answered. "That is precisely the point."

"He admitted knowledge of the dark side," Anakin said. "He practically confessed."

"Perhaps," Mace said. "But Jedi are not executioners."

Anakin folded his arms.

"Then what are we? If a Sith Lord is standing in front of us openly threatening the Republic?"

No immediate answer came.

The discussion continued for some time.

Although none of the Masters fully agreed with Anakin's actions, neither did they dismiss the seriousness of the threat he had confronted. The discovery of Palpatine's lightsaber had changed everything.

Eventually Mace suggested contacting Grand Master Yoda.

A secure Holonet connection was established to Kashyyyk, where Yoda remained with Republic forces overseeing the final operations of the war.

The small green Jedi Master appeared as a blue hologram above the projector.

Patiently, he listened while Mace explained everything from beginning to end.

Palpatine's death.

Anakin's confession.

The search of the Chancellor's office.

The discovery of the red lightsaber.

When the report was finished, Yoda closed his eyes for a long moment.

At last he spoke.

"Disturbing, this is."

The chamber remained silent.

"Punish Skywalker, I will not advise."

Anakin looked up.

Mace raised an eyebrow.

Yoda continued.

"If truly a Sith Lord the Chancellor was, grave danger the Republic faced."

Anakin allowed himself a small measure of relief.

That relief vanished almost immediately.

"However," Yoda said, "hidden this matter cannot remain."

Anakin frowned.

"What do you mean, Master?"

"Tell the Senate what you have done, you must."

The room became quiet once more.

Anakin stared at the hologram.

"The Senate?" he asked.

"Openly."

"That would be a disaster."

"Perhaps," Yoda replied.

"But necessary, it is."

Anakin shook his head.

"They'll never believe me."

"Believe you, they may not."

"Then why do it?"

"Because truth hidden should not be."

Mace nodded slowly.

"I agree."

Kit Fisto nodded as well.

"As do I."

Saesee Tiin and Agen Kolar soon followed.

Anakin looked around the room in disbelief.

Every one of them supported Yoda's decision.

"This is going to become a political circus," Anakin said.

"Already a political circus it is," Yoda replied.

The statement ended the discussion.

Though reluctant, Anakin finally agreed.

The Senate would hear the truth directly from him.

—

The following day, the Galactic Senate convened as scheduled.

Thousands of repulsorpods filled the enormous chamber.

The atmosphere was ordinary at first.

Most senators expected a routine session.

Many anticipated a speech from Supreme Chancellor Palpatine following the reported destruction of General Grievous.

Instead, confusion spread through the hall when Palpatine failed to appear.

Whispers moved from pod to pod.

Then another figure entered the central speaking platform.

Anakin Skywalker.

The young Jedi Knight immediately drew attention from every corner of the chamber.

Senators exchanged puzzled looks.

Many recognized him as the famed Hero With No Fear, one of the Republic's most celebrated military commanders.

Few understood why he was standing where the Chancellor should have been.

Anakin activated the central microphone.

The chamber gradually fell silent.

"I have an announcement to make," he said.

His voice echoed throughout the Senate.

"Yesterday, I confronted Chancellor Palpatine in his private office."

Confused murmurs spread through the hall.

Anakin continued.

"I came to believe that Chancellor Palpatine was a Sith Lord."

The chamber erupted.

Voices rose everywhere.

Some senators shouted questions.

Others demanded explanations.

The presiding officials struggled to restore order.

Anakin waited until the noise diminished.

"After confronting him," he continued, "I killed him."

The Senate exploded into chaos.

Dozens of senators rose to their feet.

Some shouted accusations.

Others demanded immediate action.

A chorus of voices called for Anakin's arrest.

"This is madness!"

"You murdered the Chancellor!"

"Detain him immediately!"

"Seize him!"

The uproar grew louder.

From the guest gallery overlooking the chamber, another voice suddenly cut through the noise.

"Senators."

Mace Windu stepped forward.

The senior Jedi Master drew immediate attention.

The Senate slowly quieted.

Mace held up a familiar metallic object.

Palpatine's lightsaber.

A ripple of confusion spread through the hall.

"What is that?" someone called.

Without answering, Mace ignited it.

A blade of crimson energy burst into existence.

Gasps echoed throughout the chamber.

The red glow illuminated nearby senators.

Many had never seen a Sith weapon before.

The significance was not lost on those who understood Jedi history.

Mace deactivated the blade.

"The weapon was recovered from Chancellor Palpatine's private office," he announced.

The Senate fell into uneasy silence.

Anakin's claims no longer sounded entirely impossible.

Immediately, a new debate erupted.

Some senators still demanded Anakin's arrest.

Others argued that the discovery of the lightsaber changed everything.

Eventually a compromise began to emerge.

Anakin would not be imprisoned immediately.

Neither would he be allowed unrestricted freedom.

Until an official investigation could determine whether Palpatine truly had been a Sith Lord, Anakin Skywalker would be placed under house arrest.

The proposal gained support.

Many senators reasoned that if Anakin was wrong, authorities could easily reach him and place him under formal arrest later.

If he was right, however, imprisoning him immediately might prove a catastrophic mistake.

The motion passed.

Attention then shifted to a practical problem.

Where exactly would Anakin remain?

"The Jedi Temple," Anakin suggested.

Several senators objected immediately.

The concerns were obvious.

If Palpatine's death turned out to be part of a broader conspiracy, some feared the Jedi Order itself might be involved.

Housing Anakin among thousands of Jedi was therefore unacceptable to them.

"The Temple is his home," a few senators argued.

"Precisely the problem," others replied.

The debate continued for several minutes.

Finally Anakin spoke again.

"I have nowhere else."

The chamber became quiet.

Then another voice entered the discussion.

Senator Padmé Amidala rose from her pod.

"He can stay in the Senate apartment complex."

Many senators turned toward her.

Padmé calmly provided the address of her apartment in the Senate District.

A number of senators recognized it immediately as her own residence.

The observation went unspoken.

From a security standpoint, most considered the arrangement preferable to the Jedi Temple.

After additional discussion, the proposal was accepted.

Anakin Skywalker would remain under house arrest at Senator Amidala's apartment while a special Senate investigative committee examined the evidence surrounding Chancellor Palpatine's death.

As the session drew toward its conclusion, Anakin raised one final request.

"I would like my astromech droid, R2-D2, to remain with me."

The request prompted little discussion.

No senator considered an astromech droid a meaningful security concern.

Permission was granted.

With that, the Senate adjourned.

The investigation would begin.

—

That evening, the atmosphere inside Padmé Amidala's apartment was considerably less formal than the Senate chamber.

Anakin sat near one of the large windows overlooking the endless lights of Coruscant.

R2-D2 rested nearby.

The little astromech emitted occasional worried whistles.

The apartment door opened.

Padmé entered after a long day of political meetings.

For several moments neither spoke.

Finally Padmé set down her belongings and looked directly at Anakin.

"I still think you overstepped."

Anakin sighed.

"I knew you were going to say that."

"Because it's true."

"He was a Sith Lord."

"Maybe."

Anakin stared at her.

"Maybe?"

Padmé held up a hand.

"Anakin, that's not the point."

"It is the point."

"No, it isn't."

She sat across from him.

"You killed the Supreme Chancellor without a trial."

"He confessed."

"You believed he confessed."

Anakin shook his head.

"This is unbelievable."

Padmé leaned forward.

"You have created a massive crisis for the Republic."

"I stopped a Sith Lord."

"You created a constitutional nightmare."

"I saved people."

Padmé rubbed her temples.

"You have absolutely no sense of optics."

Anakin frowned.

"Not everything is about optics."

"In politics, optics matter."

"Then politics is broken."

Padmé exhaled sharply.

"You don't understand politics."

Anakin immediately shot back.

"You don't understand reality."

The argument intensified.

Neither seemed willing to surrender an inch.

R2-D2's electronic beeps became increasingly anxious.

Finally another voice interrupted.

"Oh dear," said C-3PO.

The protocol droid stepped between them.

"Perhaps both of you could refrain from arguing for at least one evening?"

Neither answered.

"Master Anakin, Senator Amidala has had an exhausting day. Senator Amidala, Master Anakin has also had a rather unusual twenty-four hours."

The droid looked back and forth between them.

"Must you continue this discussion right now?"

Padmé looked away first.

Anakin followed a moment later.

The tension slowly faded.

The argument lost momentum.

R2-D2 emitted a long, relieved whistle.

For the first time all evening, silence returned to the apartment.

---

# Part 3

The investigation into Chancellor Palpatine's death had only just begun when another development shook the Republic.

A Republic cruiser arrived at Coruscant carrying an unexpected prisoner.

Maul.

The former ruler of Mandalore was brought to the capital in restraints under heavy guard. Accompanying him was Ahsoka Tano, recently returned from the campaign that had liberated Mandalore from his control.

The Senate quickly arranged a special session to hear her report.

Standing before the assembled senators, Ahsoka recounted the events in detail.

She described the siege of Mandalore, the battles fought through the streets of Sundari, her duel with Maul, and his eventual capture by the 332nd Company.

The Senate listened attentively. The liberation of Mandalore was important enough on its own, but Ahsoka had not come merely to report a military victory.

She revealed that during their confrontation, Maul had spoken extensively about a Sith Lord known as Darth Sidious.

According to Maul, Sidious had been secretly orchestrating the Clone Wars from both sides.

Even more troubling, Maul had repeatedly warned that Sidious had taken a special interest in Anakin Skywalker.

The chamber grew increasingly tense as she spoke.

Many senators exchanged uneasy glances.

The claims sounded extraordinary.

Yet they aligned disturbingly well with the evidence already under examination.

Once Ahsoka concluded her testimony, the Senate ordered Maul brought before them.

The former Sith apprentice stood defiantly at first.

His yellow eyes moved across the vast chamber.

He seemed almost amused by the situation.

For years he had sought opportunities to strike back at his former master. Now, unexpectedly, that opportunity had arrived.

The Senate made him an offer.

If he cooperated fully, he would not face execution. Instead, he would receive life imprisonment.

The proposal was debated for some time.

Many senators objected.

Others argued that the information he possessed might prove invaluable.

Eventually the offer stood.

Maul considered it.

Then, to the surprise of many observers, he accepted.

For hours he spoke.

He described his years training under Darth Sidious.

He described Sidious's methods, his ambitions, and his philosophy.

He recounted operations he had personally carried out on Sidious's orders.

Most importantly, he identified Darth Sidious as Chancellor Palpatine.

The chamber fell silent as the testimony continued.

Many senators had entered the proceedings skeptical of Anakin's story.

That skepticism was rapidly disappearing.

Maul's account matched numerous details already provided by Anakin.

It aligned with the red lightsaber recovered from Palpatine's office.

It aligned with evidence investigators had begun uncovering within the Chancellor's records.

And it aligned with Ahsoka's testimony.

By the time Maul finished speaking, the mood of the Senate had changed dramatically.

The former Sith was escorted away and transported to a secure prison facility as promised.

The Senate formally thanked him for his cooperation before remanding him to custody.

Then attention returned to the matter that had started everything.

Anakin Skywalker.

The evidence was now overwhelming.

Anakin had testified about Palpatine's own statements regarding the dark side.

Mace Windu had produced the Sith lightsaber recovered from the Chancellor's office.

Ahsoka Tano had presented Maul's warnings concerning Darth Sidious.

And Maul himself had identified Palpatine as his former master.

The conclusion was becoming unavoidable.

Palpatine had indeed been a Sith Lord.

The Senate voted accordingly.

The investigation into Anakin's conduct was concluded.

He was formally cleared of wrongdoing.

His house arrest was lifted immediately.

—

News spread quickly across Coruscant.

Before long, a familiar visitor arrived at Padmé Amidala's apartment.

By now, many people had begun casually referring to it as the Skywalker apartment. The events of the previous days had made it difficult to ignore how frequently Anakin and Padmé were found there together.

The door opened.

Ahsoka Tano stepped inside.

Anakin greeted her immediately.

For the first time in days, he looked genuinely relaxed.

"Ahsoka."

"Master."

A smile crossed both their faces.

Anakin gestured toward her.

"I heard everything. Capturing Maul couldn't have been easy."

Ahsoka laughed.

"It wasn't."

"You did well."

"So did you."

Anakin raised an eyebrow.

"Me?"

"You neutralized Darth Sidious."

Anakin shook his head.

"When you say it like that, it sounds ridiculous."

"Still true."

Padmé watched the exchange with an amused expression.

The reunion continued for some time.

They spoke about Mandalore, the war, old memories, and recent events.

For a few precious hours, the political crisis consuming the Republic seemed far away.

—

The following day Anakin returned to the Jedi Temple.

For the first time since Palpatine's death, he entered not as a suspect under investigation but as a Jedi Knight whose actions had been vindicated by the Republic itself.

The Jedi Council had assembled to meet him.

Yoda had returned from Kashyyyk and occupied his usual place among the Masters.

Mace Windu sat nearby.

Around them were the other members of the Council.

Anakin stood before them.

The atmosphere was solemn.

Yoda spoke first.

"Much has happened, Skywalker."

Anakin nodded.

"Yes, Master."

The Council discussed the matter at length.

Several members openly acknowledged their discomfort with how events had unfolded.

The decision to kill Palpatine without attempting an arrest remained controversial among many Jedi.

Even now, not every Master approved of Anakin's choice.

Yet another reality could not be ignored.

The Sith Lord who had hidden at the heart of the Republic for years was gone.

The conspiracy that had fueled the Clone Wars had been exposed.

The Republic had survived.

And Anakin Skywalker had been the one who uncovered the truth.

Eventually Mace Windu addressed him directly.

"There has been disagreement among us regarding your actions."

Anakin remained silent.

"However," Mace continued, "there is no disagreement regarding the outcome."

Several Council members nodded.

Yoda's expression remained thoughtful.

"For a thousand years, hidden the Sith have been."

The small Jedi Master looked directly at Anakin.

"Ended the greatest Sith threat in generations, you have."

Anakin listened carefully.

Yoda smiled faintly.

"Therefore, decided the Council has."

For a moment, Anakin hardly dared hope.

Mace Windu rose.

"Anakin Skywalker."

The chamber grew completely silent.

"The Council grants you the rank of Jedi Master."

For several seconds Anakin simply stood there.

The words scarcely seemed real.

For years he had believed he had earned that rank.

For years he had resented being denied it.

Now, at last, it had happened.

A wave of relief washed over him.

He bowed respectfully before the Council.

"Thank you, Masters."

The sincerity in his voice was unmistakable.

Whatever disagreements remained between him and the Council, one of the longest-running tensions of his Jedi career had finally come to an end.

For the first time in a very long while, Anakin Skywalker felt that the future might hold something other than conflict.

---

# Part 4

The days following Anakin Skywalker's promotion to Jedi Master were among the most peaceful he had experienced in years.

The Republic was still reeling from the revelations surrounding Palpatine, but for the first time since the Clone Wars had begun, the future no longer seemed dominated by crisis after crisis.

Then Padmé went into labor.

The moment she informed Anakin, all of his hard-earned calm vanished.

Within minutes he was pacing.

Doctors and medical droids assured him everything appeared normal.

Padmé assured him everything appeared normal.

Anakin remained unconvinced.

"What if something goes wrong?" he asked for perhaps the tenth time.

"It won't."

"But what if it does?"

Padmé sighed.

"Anakin."

"What?"

"I'm fine."

Anakin nodded.

Then immediately resumed worrying.

As the hours passed, his anxiety only grew.

"I should have learned more about medicine."

"That wouldn't help."

"I should have asked more questions."

"About what?"

"I don't know. Childbirth."

Padmé smiled despite herself.

"Anakin, billions of people have children."

"That's not reassuring."

"It should be."

At first she found his concern endearing.

Later she found it exhausting.

Eventually she became visibly irritated.

"Padmé, are you feeling all right?"

"Yes."

"Are you sure?"

"Yes."

"Completely sure?"

Padmé stared at him.

"Anakin."

"Yes?"

"If you ask me that one more time, I may personally throw you out of this medical center."

Anakin blinked.

The threat sounded genuine.

For a short while he managed to remain silent.

Only a short while.

"Padmé—"

She groaned.

Finally, after another round of increasingly nervous questions, she fixed him with a look that had ended many political arguments over the years.

"You are being absolutely ridiculous."

Anakin opened his mouth.

She pointed a finger at him.

"No."

He closed it again.

"Nothing is wrong. The doctors say nothing is wrong. The medical droids say nothing is wrong. I say nothing is wrong."

Anakin shifted uncomfortably.

Padmé continued.

"You need to stop worrying immediately."

The firmness of her tone finally got through to him.

Somewhat.

He still worried.

He simply did so more quietly.

—

There was one surprise that neither of them had anticipated.

Only during the childbirth itself did the medical staff discover that Padmé was carrying twins.

The announcement stunned both parents.

"Twins?" Anakin repeated.

The medical droid confirmed the diagnosis.

Anakin sat down immediately.

The revelation somehow managed to increase his anxiety and his excitement at the same time.

Several hours later, both children were born healthy.

A boy and a girl.

Most importantly, Padmé remained perfectly healthy.

The moment Anakin saw that she was safe, an enormous weight lifted from his shoulders.

Years of fear seemed to disappear all at once.

Padmé looked tired but otherwise completely fine.

Anakin looked as though he had just survived a major battle.

She laughed when she saw his expression.

"I told you."

Anakin smiled.

For perhaps the first time in days.

"You did."

After discussing names, they settled on Luke for their son and Leia for their daughter.

As Anakin held the twins for the first time, he found himself staring at them in wonder.

The war, the politics, the investigations, and the endless crises of recent years suddenly felt very far away.

For that moment, nothing seemed more important than the small family gathered in the hospital room.

—

Across the galaxy, events were moving just as rapidly.

The deaths of Palpatine, Count Dooku, and General Grievous had shattered the leadership structures of both major factions in the Clone Wars.

The Republic had lost its Chancellor.

The Confederacy of Independent Systems had lost both its political patron and its military commanders.

Many Separatist worlds no longer saw a clear path forward.

Some governments still believed passionately in the ideals that had originally led them to secede from the Republic.

Others had become exhausted by years of conflict.

The same exhaustion existed within the Republic.

Few citizens wanted to continue fighting.

An election for a new Supreme Chancellor was held in the Galactic Senate.

After considerable debate and campaigning, Senator Bail Organa of Alderaan emerged victorious.

The respected statesman entered office facing the enormous challenge of ending the war.

Rather than pursuing total military victory, Chancellor Organa chose a different path.

He opened negotiations with the Confederacy's government on Raxus Secundus.

The talks were difficult.

Years of bloodshed had created deep mistrust on both sides.

Yet the desire for peace proved stronger than the desire to continue fighting.

After months of negotiation, a treaty was finalized.

The Republic formally recognized the sovereignty of the Confederacy of Independent Systems.

The Confederacy agreed to respect Republic territory and cease all military operations against it.

Both governments committed themselves to large-scale demilitarization.

The Clone Wars officially came to an end.

Across countless worlds, celebrations erupted.

For many citizens, it was the first time in years they could imagine a future without constant conflict.

The practical consequences of the treaty followed quickly.

The Confederacy shut down its droid foundries.

Factories that had produced vast armies of battle droids ceased operation.

Meanwhile Chancellor Organa ordered the closure of the cloning facilities on Kamino.

No new clone armies would be commissioned.

The status of the existing clone troopers became a major political issue.

For years they had fought and died for the Republic while possessing few legal rights of their own.

Organa pushed through landmark legislation granting full legal personhood to every clone trooper.

They became citizens of the Republic with all accompanying rights and protections.

Many veterans chose to remain in military service.

Others retired.

For the first time in their lives, the choice belonged to them.

—

The exposure of Palpatine's conspiracy also triggered sweeping political reforms.

Investigators uncovered the extent to which power had become concentrated in the Chancellor's office during the war.

Public outrage was widespread.

Determined to prevent a similar crisis from occurring again, the Senate enacted major constitutional reforms.

Emergency powers were dramatically reduced.

New oversight mechanisms were established.

Term limits and restrictions on executive authority were strengthened.

Additional democratic safeguards were implemented throughout the Republic's institutions.

The reforms did not eliminate political disagreements.

Nothing could accomplish that.

But they significantly reduced the possibility that a future Chancellor could accumulate power on the same scale as Palpatine.

—

In the years that followed, the galaxy entered a new era.

Darth Sidious was dead.

Maul was dead.

"I think it is."

Padmé looked out over the Coruscant skyline.

"The galaxy has been living in the shadow of the Sith without even knowing it."

Padmé smiled faintly.

"Now maybe people can finally start living again."

They laughed about the recent trip to Naboo.

Padmé recalled Luke's fascination with the fountains of Theed.

Anakin joked that Leia already seemed determined to command everyone around her.

Ahsoka teased C-3PO about nearly falling into the lake at Varykino.

"It was a highly dangerous situation," Threepio protested.

R2-D2 emitted an amused whistle.

Threepio looked offended.

"I most certainly disagree with your assessment."

Laughter filled the apartment.

Padmé looked toward Anakin.

"We can't keep pretending forever."

"Our family."

She nodded.

"It was difficult before."

"Now it's almost impossible."

Outside the apartment, very few people understood the full truth.

Officially, Anakin Skywalker remained one of the Republic's most celebrated Jedi Masters.

Padmé Amidala remained a senator.

Only a handful of individuals knew that they were husband and wife.

Only Ahsoka had known the complete truth from the beginning.

Now another person had joined that small circle.

Obi-Wan.

Anakin sighed.

"I kept the twins hidden from him ever since they were born."

"You couldn't anymore," Padmé replied.

"Not after Malastare."

Anakin nodded.

When Obi-Wan had been carried into the skiff, Luke and Leia had been there.

There had been no opportunity to conceal them.

No explanation other than the obvious.

"He's going to mention it eventually," Ahsoka observed.

"Whether he means to or not."

"I know."

Silence followed.

Then Ahsoka leaned forward.

"Maybe you should tell the Council yourself."

Anakin looked at her as though she had suggested resigning from the Jedi Order.

"They'll expel me."

"I'm not so sure."

"They absolutely will."

Ahsoka shook her head.

"You exposed Darth Sidious."

"You helped defeat Maul."

"You're the Chosen One."

"Do you really think they're eager to expel someone who's done all of that?"

Anakin hesitated.

"I... hadn't thought about it that way."

"You might receive an exemption."

Anakin frowned.

"I don't want an exemption."

"What do you want?"

"I want the rule changed."

Padmé and Ahsoka both looked at him.

"The rule itself."

He stood and began pacing.

"What if relationships didn't have to be hidden anymore?"

He stopped.

"You once told me about Lux Bonteri."

Ahsoka smiled.

"I did."

"You cared about him."

"I did."

"And I can't believe we're the only ones."

The thought seemed to gather momentum as he spoke.

"How many Jedi have hidden relationships?"

"How many have kept quiet because they're afraid?"

"If everyone came forward..."

He looked toward Padmé.

"...maybe the Code itself could change."

Ahsoka slowly nodded.

"You might be right."

Several days later Obi-Wan had recovered sufficiently to leave the medical center.

His first invitation was to the Skywalker apartment.

He accepted immediately.

The atmosphere was relaxed.

There was no longer any point pretending Luke and Leia belonged to someone else.

Obi-Wan had already seen everything.

The twins were introduced properly.

Obi-Wan held each of them in turn.

"They have your eyes," he told Anakin.

"And Padmé's determination, if their parents are to be believed."

Anakin explained the idea that had taken shape after Malastare.

Changing the Jedi Code.

Ending the prohibition on attachment, romance, and marriage.

Instead, he became unusually quiet.

Finally he spoke.

"There is something I never told either of you."

Anakin and Ahsoka listened.

"I loved Satine Kryze."

The room became completely still.

Obi-Wan spoke calmly.

He described his years with the Duchess of Mandalore.

He admitted that, had circumstances been different, he would have left the Jedi Order to be with her.

He described losing her.

The grief remained visible despite the years that had passed.

When he finished, Anakin sat silently.

"So it wasn't just me."

"No."

Ahsoka smiled gently.

"I had a feeling."

Obi-Wan returned a faint smile.

"You may be right about something, Anakin."

"We're probably not alone."

After some further discussion, Obi-Wan agreed to support the effort.

Then another thought occurred to him.

"If we're doing this..."

"...we should speak with Quinlan Vos."

Obi-Wan explained that Quinlan had loved Asajj Ventress.

Another secret.

Another relationship.

Quinlan was contacted.

He agreed almost immediately.

As members of the Jedi Council, Anakin Skywalker and Obi-Wan Kenobi possessed the authority to request a special meeting.

The Council assembled.

Present as invited guests were Padmé Amidala, Ahsoka Tano, and Quinlan Vos.

Yoda looked around the chamber.

"Purpose of this meeting?"

"We seek a reform of the Jedi Code."

Murmurs spread among the Masters.

"The prohibition on attachment, romance, and marriage should be reconsidered."

Obi-Wan continued.

"We believe many Jedi already maintain such relationships in secret."

Yoda considered this carefully.

"Examples?"

"I'll begin."

She described her relationship with Lux Bonteri.

How they had grown close.

How, eventually, life had carried them in different directions.

"The relationship ended naturally," she concluded.

"But it was real."

Several Council members exchanged thoughtful looks.

Next came Obi-Wan.

His revelation concerning Satine Kryze surprised nearly everyone.

Few Masters had ever suspected the depth of those feelings.

By the time he finished, even some of the more conservative members appeared deeply moved.

Then Quinlan Vos spoke.

He explained his love for Asajj Ventress.

The Council had long known Ventress sacrificed herself to save him.

What they had never realized was why.

Viewed through the lens of romance, many past events suddenly acquired new meaning.

The Council quietly reconsidered years of assumptions.

He looked toward Padmé.

She nodded.

He took a deep breath.

"I married Senator Padmé Amidala."

She described what it had been like living in secrecy.

The constant fear.

The careful lies.

The strain of hiding a marriage from friends and colleagues.

"It affects everyone who loves them."

Unlike the previous relationships, his was ongoing.

Unlike the others, it had resulted in marriage.

Several Masters quietly began discussing disciplinary measures.

Before anyone reached a conclusion, another voice interrupted.

"If we're telling the truth..."

Every head turned.

Kit Fisto stood.

"I should tell mine as well."

Silence returned.

"I have been in a romantic relationship with Jedi Master Aayla Secura."

No one spoke.

Not only was the relationship current—

—it involved two Jedi Masters.

One of them sat on the Council itself.

Yoda stared at Kit.

Mace Windu blinked in visible surprise.

Kit remained composed.

"I support the proposed reform."

Aayla Secura was immediately summoned.

Already present within the Temple, she arrived only minutes later.

She confirmed everything.

Then she addressed the Council.

"There is no conflict between love and service."

"There is only conflict between love and secrecy."

Now five different relationships had been laid before the Council.

Ahsoka and Lux.

Obi-Wan and Satine.

Quinlan and Ventress.

Anakin and Padmé.

Kit and Aayla.

The pattern could no longer be dismissed as isolated incidents.

Three members of the twelve-member Council itself—Anakin, Obi-Wan, and Kit Fisto—now openly advocated reform.

The debate continued for hours.

Even Yoda found himself reflecting upon what he had heard.

Eventually he spoke.

"Driven underground, these bonds have been."

"Prevented... they have not."

The room remained silent.

After further deliberation, the Council reached its decision.

The reform passed.

The Jedi Code would no longer forbid romantic relationships or marriage.

For a long moment, nobody moved.

Then smiles began to spread across the room.

Padmé embraced Anakin.

Ahsoka laughed openly.

Obi-Wan smiled with a sense of peace he had not felt in many years.

Quinlan bowed his head in quiet gratitude.

Kit and Aayla exchanged a look that no longer needed to be hidden.

The Jedi Order had changed.

—

The consequences became visible almost immediately.

In the days that followed, Jedi throughout the Order began voluntarily disclosing relationships that had previously been kept secret.

Some were current.

Others belonged to years long past.

Stories emerged from every corner of the Temple.

Former romances.

Quiet marriages.

Lifelong partnerships.

Brief but meaningful relationships.

Anakin watched the steady stream of revelations with amazement.

His suspicion had been entirely correct.

The prohibition had not eliminated attachment.

It had merely concealed it.

Even some of the Council's strongest traditionalists began reconsidering their assumptions.

Mace Windu observed the disclosures with growing reflection.

Ki-Adi-Mundi, who had long navigated his own complex obligations between Jedi service and his culturally sanctioned family responsibilities, likewise acknowledged that the old system had failed to accomplish what it claimed.

The evidence had become impossible to ignore.

The Jedi had never truly been free of attachment.

They had simply become experts at hiding it.

By bringing those relationships into the open, the Order found itself built upon greater honesty than it had known for generations.

The reform ceased to be an experiment.

It became the beginning of a lasting transformation in the life of the Jedi Order.

# Alternate Ending: Part 7

The reform of the Jedi Code quickly began reshaping life within the Temple.

Among the first to embrace the new openness were Kit Fisto and Aayla Secura.

Only a short time after the Council's decision, they publicly announced their intention to marry.

The announcement generated excitement throughout the Temple.

For thousands of years, the Jedi had never celebrated an openly acknowledged marriage between two members of the Order.

Now not only would such a marriage take place, but both participants were respected Jedi Masters, one of whom sat on the Jedi Council.

The wedding was held within the Temple itself.

Jedi of every rank gathered to witness the historic occasion.

Masters, Knights, Padawans, Initiates, and Temple staff filled the ceremonial hall.

Anakin and Padmé attended together openly for the first time.

Obi-Wan stood nearby with Ahsoka.

Yoda, Mace Windu, and the rest of the Council watched from places of honor.

As part of the celebration, Kit and Aayla observed a long-standing Jedi martial tradition.

Their lightsabers were adjusted to low-power training settings, ensuring that even direct strikes would not inflict serious injury.

The ceremonial duel began.

The two Masters fought with obvious familiarity.

Neither held back.

Elegant Form I and Form IV sequences blended with Kit's fluid aquatic-inspired movements and Aayla's graceful Ataru technique.

The spectators watched in appreciative silence.

The duel remained evenly matched from beginning to end.

Finally Aayla found the narrowest of openings.

With a precise movement, she touched her blade against Kit's shoulder before he could recover.

The duel ended.

Kit laughed.

"I surrender."

Aayla smiled.

The Temple erupted into applause.

The first openly married Jedi couple embraced before their friends and fellow Jedi.

It was a day many believed marked the beginning of a new era for the Order.

—

For several months afterward, Kit and Aayla appeared to embody everything the reformers had hoped for.

They continued their Jedi duties while openly sharing their lives together.

Many younger Jedi found encouragement in seeing two accomplished Masters balancing marriage with service to the Force.

Then an unexpected complication arose.

Aayla became pregnant.

Neither of them had anticipated it.

Given that Kit was a Nautolan and Aayla a Twi'lek, they had not even known whether children between their species were biologically possible.

At first they approached the situation calmly.

"We'll simply see what happens," Kit said.

Aayla agreed.

Neither wished to rush into assumptions.

Within weeks, however, something changed.

Aayla became increasingly ill.

At first she dismissed it as an unusually difficult pregnancy.

Soon the symptoms became impossible to ignore.

She grew weaker by the day.

Kit immediately took her to one of Coruscant's foremost medical facilities.

Teams of physicians and specialists examined her extensively.

Eventually they reached a conclusion.

The lead physician spoke carefully.

"Master Secura... your condition is extremely serious."

Kit reached for Aayla's hand.

The physician continued.

"Twi'lek anatomy is not well suited to carrying a hybrid child with a Nautolan."

Silence filled the examination room.

"If the pregnancy continues," the physician said, "there is a high probability that you will not survive."

Aayla remained composed.

"And the child?"

"The fetus also has a very poor chance of surviving to term."

The physician paused before delivering the final recommendation.

"We strongly advise terminating the pregnancy immediately."

Kit looked down.

"The procedure?"

"It carries risks."

"How serious?"

"We believe it offers the best chance of saving Master Secura's life."

The physician hesitated.

"But because of the complications already present... we cannot guarantee the outcome."

Aayla absorbed the information quietly.

She and Kit thanked the medical staff for their honesty.

Before consenting to any procedure, however, they decided to seek another opinion.

This time from the Jedi healers.

—

Within the Temple's healing halls, experienced Jedi healers examined Aayla through both medicine and the Force.

After lengthy consultation, they believed another approach might be possible.

One of the senior healers explained.

"The Force may allow us to avoid conventional surgery."

Word eventually reached Yoda.

The Grand Master joined the consultation personally.

After meditating beside Aayla for a long time, he finally spoke.

"Separate presences in the Living Force, separate living beings possess."

He closed his eyes again.

"The child... its own signature has."

Working together, Yoda and the Temple healers carefully distinguished the Force signature of Aayla from that of the unborn child.

Once the distinction became clear, Yoda began discussing an ancient technique known to only a handful of Jedi Masters.

"There exists a way," he said, "to pour raw Force energy into a living being."

The gathered healers listened attentively.

"When enough gathered is, become one with the Cosmic Force, that being may."

The room fell silent.

After further reflection, however, Yoda shook his head.

"No."

The healers looked toward him.

"From outside, impossible this is."

He explained his reasoning.

Any attempt by another Jedi to direct sufficient Force energy into the fetus would necessarily require passing that energy through Aayla herself.

The amount of power involved might fatally injure her before ever reaching its intended target.

"There is another way," Yoda said.

He looked directly at Aayla.

"You must learn."

Over the following week, Yoda instructed her personally.

The training proved unlike anything Aayla had experienced before.

Rather than projecting the Force outward, she learned to gather immense quantities of raw Force energy within herself while maintaining extraordinary precision.

Kit remained beside her throughout every lesson.

As he observed the instruction, he gradually began learning the technique as well.

Yoda permitted it.

"It may serve you someday," the Grand Master said.

By the end of the week, Aayla believed she understood.

Yoda asked one final question.

"Ready, are you?"

She nodded.

"I am."

The Temple healers assembled around her.

Kit stood close by.

Aayla closed her eyes.

She reached inward.

The Force flowed through her.

Slowly at first.

Then in ever greater quantities.

The gathered energy remained entirely under her control.

She focused upon the separate presence Yoda had taught her to recognize.

The fetus.

Without hesitation, she directed the accumulated Force energy toward it.

For several long moments, no one spoke.

Then the separate presence within the Living Force gradually faded.

Its physical existence dissolved completely into the Cosmic Force.

The process ended.

Aayla opened her eyes.

Almost immediately she noticed the difference.

The pain was gone.

The weakness disappeared.

Within minutes, the alarming symptoms that had plagued her for weeks had completely subsided.

The healers examined her carefully.

Her condition had stabilized.

She would recover.

Kit embraced her with visible relief.

Neither spoke for several moments.

There was nothing that needed saying.

—

After Aayla had fully recovered, she and Kit spent a long evening together overlooking the Temple gardens.

Neither regretted the marriage.

Neither regretted the reform.

But both recognized the lesson they had learned.

Kit finally broke the silence.

"I don't think we should ever try that again."

Aayla smiled gently.

"I was thinking exactly the same thing."

They agreed together that they would never again attempt to have children.

Their lives would remain devoted to one another, to the Jedi Order, and to the service of the Force.

The difficult experience strengthened rather than weakened their commitment to each other.

Though the path they had envisioned had changed, they walked forward together, grateful that they still had the opportunity to do so.
