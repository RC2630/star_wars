# Part 1

The war had reached its final, desperate stage when Anakin Skywalker walked into Chancellor Palpatine's private office and learned a terrible truth.

Palpatine was Darth Sidious, the hidden Sith Lord who had manipulated the Republic for years and orchestrated the Clone Wars from the shadows.

Shocked and conflicted, Anakin did not strike him down. Instead, he left the office and reported everything to Master Mace Windu.

Within minutes, Windu assembled a strike team consisting of himself, Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin. They traveled directly to the Chancellor's office to arrest the Sith Lord.

The attempt had gone disastrously wrong.

By the time the duel reached its final stage, Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin lay dead across the polished floor of the Chancellor's suite.

Only Mace Windu remained.

Purple and crimson blades flashed through the office in blinding arcs. The enormous windows overlooking Coruscant reflected the battle as lightning illuminated the darkening sky beyond.

Darth Sidious attacked with terrifying speed.

To any ordinary observer, the Sith Lord seemed unstoppable.

Mace Windu endured.

His expression was grim and focused. Every movement was precise. Every strike was calculated. He absorbed Sidious's aggression and redirected it back at him.

The duel raged from one side of the office to the other.

Furniture shattered.

Walls were scorched.

Fragments of transparisteel littered the floor.

Sidious pressed forward with a scream of rage.

Windu met him.

The Jedi Master's blade turned aside a vicious thrust, swept low, and forced the Sith Lord backward.

For the first time, Sidious stumbled.

Windu advanced relentlessly.

A second exchange followed.

Then a third.

Each one drove Sidious farther toward the broken edge of the office overlooking the endless city below.

The Sith Lord's confidence began to crack.

His attacks became more desperate.

Windu's became more decisive.

At last, the Jedi Master's lightsaber trapped Sidious's blade high.

With a powerful twist, Windu tore the crimson weapon from the Sith Lord's hand.

The lightsaber flew across the room.

Sidious's eyes widened.

For a brief moment, silence filled the office.

Mace Windu stood over the defeated Sith Lord.

The bodies of three Jedi Masters lay behind him.

Years of deception.

Years of war.

Billions dead across the galaxy.

All of it traced back to the man kneeling before him.

"You're under arrest," Windu said.

Sidious stared upward.

The mask of the kindly Chancellor was gone.

Only hatred remained.

His hands rose.

Blue-white Force lightning exploded from his fingertips.

Windu crossed his blade before him and absorbed the attack.

Lightning filled the room.

The office shook.

The Sith Lord poured every ounce of power he possessed into the assault.

Windu endured it.

The Jedi Master advanced through the storm one step at a time.

Sidious's face twisted with fury.

The lightning intensified.

Still Windu advanced.

The Sith Lord was not surrendering.

He was still trying to kill him.

The realization settled in Windu's mind with terrible clarity.

There would be no imprisonment.

No trial.

No containment.

The man before him controlled governments, armies, intelligence agencies, and hidden networks throughout the galaxy. Even now, he was unleashing enough power to kill almost any being alive.

If allowed to live, he would continue fighting.

If given the smallest opportunity, he would escape.

Mace Windu reached a decision.

The lightning ceased.

Sidious faltered.

For the first time, exhaustion showed on the Sith Lord's face.

Windu moved.

His lightsaber flashed.

The purple blade swept through the air.

Darth Sidious's head separated from his shoulders.

The body collapsed.

Silence returned to the office.

The Sith Lord was dead.

Mace Windu stood motionless for several seconds.

Then the office doors opened.

Anakin Skywalker entered.

He had rushed from the Temple after informing the Council and had come as quickly as he could.

The sight before him stopped him cold.

Three dead Jedi Masters.

A ruined office.

And Chancellor Palpatine's lifeless body on the floor.

Anakin stared.

"What happened?"

Windu slowly turned toward him.

"The Sith Lord resisted arrest."

Anakin looked from the body to Windu.

"You killed him."

"Yes."

"You were supposed to arrest him."

"He was too dangerous to leave alive."

Anakin's eyes narrowed.

The words struck him harder than Windu realized.

Only moments earlier, Anakin himself had argued that Palpatine should face trial.

Now the Chancellor was dead.

Executed.

Without trial.

Without judgment by the Senate or the courts.

"That's not the Jedi way," Anakin said.

Windu's expression hardened.

"The Jedi way does not require us to allow a Sith Lord to continue murdering innocents."

"He was defeated."

"He was still attacking me."

"You killed an unarmed prisoner."

Windu took a step forward.

"And if I had shown mercy, how many more would have died?"

The tension in the room grew.

Anakin's emotions were already strained beyond endurance.

For months he had been pulled in opposite directions.

Fear.

Anger.

Doubt.

Exhaustion.

Now they surged together.

"He should have stood trial."

"And if he escaped?"

"He deserved justice."

"He received it."

The words hit like a hammer.

Anakin felt something snap.

The darkness he had struggled against erupted.

Without thinking, he ignited his lightsaber.

The blue blade sprang to life.

Windu's eyes widened slightly.

"Anakin."

"You don't get to decide who lives and dies!"

Windu activated his own weapon.

"Stand down."

Anakin charged.

The duel lasted only seconds.

Anakin attacked with tremendous power, fueled by anger and outrage.

Mace Windu had spent decades mastering combat against dark side practitioners.

Compared to the Sith Lord he had just defeated, Anakin's assault was reckless and unfocused.

Their blades met once.

Twice.

Three times.

Windu turned aside every strike effortlessly.

On the fourth exchange, the Jedi Master stepped inside Anakin's guard.

A precise movement.

A flash of purple light.

Anakin's lightsaber flew from his hand.

Before he could react, Windu struck him with a Force push.

Anakin crashed against a wall.

The air left his lungs.

Windu's blade settled inches from his throat.

The fight was over.

"Don't make this worse," Windu said quietly.

Anakin stared up at him.

The anger was still there.

But so was shock.

He had lost completely.

Temple security arrived moments later.

Anakin offered no resistance as he was placed in restraints.

The fallen Jedi was escorted from the Chancellor's office and returned to the Jedi Temple under guard.

—

News spread across Coruscant with astonishing speed.

Supreme Chancellor Palpatine had been revealed as a Sith Lord.

A confrontation with Jedi Masters had ended in his death.

The Republic government descended into confusion.

Senators demanded answers.

Military commanders requested clarification.

Investigations began almost immediately.

While the Republic struggled to understand the revelation, another matter occupied the Jedi Council.

Anakin Skywalker.

The Council chamber was silent as he stood before them in restraints.

Masters Yoda, Mace Windu, Obi-Wan Kenobi, Plo Koon, Ki-Adi-Mundi, Shaak Ti, and the others listened as Windu recounted the events in the Chancellor's office.

When he finished, no one spoke for several moments.

Obi-Wan looked devastated.

Anakin met his former master's gaze but found no comfort there.

Finally Yoda spoke.

"Attack Master Windu, you did."

Anakin remained silent.

"You ignited your weapon first," said Ki-Adi-Mundi.

"I was trying to stop him."

"You assaulted a member of the Council," said Shaak Ti.

"He killed a prisoner."

"He killed a Sith Lord responsible for a galactic war," replied Plo Koon.

The discussion continued for nearly an hour.

Anakin defended himself repeatedly.

The Council remained unmoved.

Eventually Mace Windu rose.

"The matter before us is not whether Skywalker revealed Sidious's identity."

Anakin looked toward him.

Windu continued.

"The matter before us is that he attacked a Jedi Master after the battle had already ended."

Anakin's jaw tightened.

"I was the one who exposed Sidious."

"That is true," said Obi-Wan quietly.

Anakin immediately seized on the point.

"Then how can you do this? If I hadn't come forward, none of you would even know who he was."

Several Council members exchanged glances.

Finally Yoda nodded.

"Great service, that was."

"The revelation of Sidious's identity helped save the Republic," Plo Koon agreed.

"It is a significant mitigating factor," said Ki-Adi-Mundi.

Hope briefly appeared in Anakin's eyes.

Then Yoda spoke again.

"But erase your actions afterward, it does not."

The hope vanished.

"You attacked Master Windu," said Shaak Ti.

"You attempted violence against a fellow Jedi."

"You acted in anger," said Obi-Wan.

The words hurt more coming from him than anyone else.

Anakin looked directly at his former master.

"And that's it?"

Pain flashed across Obi-Wan's face.

"No," he said. "But it is enough."

The Council reached its decision.

Yoda stood.

"Expelled from the Jedi Order, Anakin Skywalker is."

The words echoed through the chamber.

Anakin felt as though the floor had disappeared beneath him.

"No."

The Council remained silent.

"No."

His voice grew louder.

"I fought for this Order since I was a child."

No answer came.

"I led armies for you."

Still silence.

"I risked my life every day in this war."

Yoda's expression was sorrowful but firm.

"The decision is final."

Anakin looked around the chamber.

No one challenged it.

Not even Obi-Wan.

Not even Ahsoka's old defenders.

No one.

Then Mace Windu spoke.

"Your service during the Clone Wars and your revelation regarding Sidious have been considered."

Anakin turned toward him.

"Because of those actions, you will not be imprisoned."

The room remained quiet.

"You will not be turned over to Republic authorities."

"You are being released."

Anakin laughed bitterly.

Released.

Expelled.

Discarded.

After everything.

Temple guards stepped forward and removed his restraints.

The binders fell away.

Anakin rubbed his wrists.

No one moved.

No one spoke.

The Council believed the matter settled.

Then Anakin's eyes landed on his lightsaber resting beside his seat.

One of the guards had placed it there after confiscating it earlier.

Before anyone could react, he grabbed it.

Several Masters instinctively shifted.

Anakin clipped the weapon to his belt.

The room tensed.

For a moment it seemed another confrontation might begin.

Instead, Anakin turned and strode toward the exit.

"Skywalker," Obi-Wan called.

Anakin stopped but did not look back.

Several seconds passed.

Nothing more was said.

Finally he continued walking.

The great doors opened.

Then closed behind him.

The Council chamber fell silent.

Obi-Wan stared at the doors long after Anakin had disappeared.

"Should we stop him?" Shaak Ti asked.

Mace Windu considered the question.

"No."

"He remains highly emotional," Ki-Adi-Mundi warned.

"He is no longer a member of the Order," Windu replied.

Yoda slowly nodded.

"Pursue him, we should not."

Obi-Wan looked uneasy.

"He still has his lightsaber."

"Let him keep it," said Windu.

The decision surprised several Council members.

Windu folded his hands.

"He informed us about Sidious. He helped end the Sith Lord's deception. Whatever else has happened, that deserves acknowledgment."

Silence followed.

At last Yoda spoke.

"His path, his own now is."

Far below the Temple, Anakin Skywalker emerged into the endless city of Coruscant.

No Jedi accompanied him.

No guards followed him.

The Temple did not attempt to stop him.

Alone, carrying the lightsaber he had wielded throughout the Clone Wars, Anakin disappeared into the crowded streets of the capital while the Jedi Council watched from afar and chose not to pursue.

# Part 2

As evening settled over Coruscant, the apartment of Senator Padmé Amidala remained unusually quiet.

The city beyond her windows blazed with its endless sea of lights, but inside, the atmosphere was tense. News reports were already spreading conflicting stories about Chancellor Palpatine's death, the involvement of the Jedi, and growing political uncertainty throughout the Republic.

Padmé had spent most of the day trying to separate rumor from fact.

Then the apartment door opened.

She immediately rose from her seat.

"Anakin."

He looked exhausted.

Not physically exhausted, though there was some of that too. This was something deeper. His shoulders sagged. His eyes were hollow. The confidence that normally accompanied him seemed absent.

Padmé hurried across the room.

"What happened?"

For a moment, Anakin simply stared at her.

Then he said quietly,

"I've been expelled from the Jedi Order."

Padmé froze.

"What?"

The words seemed impossible.

Expelled?

Not suspended. Not reprimanded.

Expelled.

Anakin walked past her and sank heavily into a chair.

Padmé followed.

"What happened?"

Anakin was silent for several seconds.

Then he began explaining everything.

He told her how he had gone to Palpatine's office after informing the Council that the Chancellor was the Sith Lord they had been searching for.

He described arriving to find the office devastated.

The dead Jedi Masters.

Palpatine's body.

Mace Windu standing over him.

Padmé listened without interruption.

Anakin described his confrontation with Windu.

How he accused the Jedi Master of abandoning Jedi principles.

How Windu insisted Palpatine had been too dangerous to leave alive.

How the argument escalated.

How his anger had finally exploded.

Then came the duel.

Anakin's expression darkened as he described it.

"I barely lasted seconds."

Padmé sat beside him.

"He defeated you?"

"Easily."

The admission clearly stung.

"He disarmed me almost immediately."

Anakin looked away.

"I never even had a chance."

He continued.

He explained being arrested.

Being returned to the Temple in restraints.

Standing before the Council.

Watching them deliberate his fate.

Hearing their final decision.

Padmé's expression grew increasingly troubled.

"They expelled you for attacking Master Windu?"

"Yes."

"Even after you exposed Palpatine?"

"That was part of the discussion."

Anakin's voice became bitter.

"They acknowledged it. They acknowledged that I was the one who told them who Sidious was."

He laughed humorlessly.

"They even said my service during the war counted in my favor."

Padmé frowned.

"Then why expel you?"

"Because attacking a member of the Council outweighed everything else."

He shook his head.

"They said those things were the reason they weren't imprisoning me or turning me over to the Republic courts."

Padmé stared at him.

The thought of Anakin being imprisoned was disturbing enough.

The fact that the Council had seriously considered it was even worse.

"What happened after that?"

"They removed my restraints."

Anakin leaned back.

"I took my lightsaber and left."

"They let you?"

"They decided not to stop me."

Silence settled over the room.

Padmé slowly processed everything she had heard.

The Clone Wars hero.

The famous Jedi Knight.

The Chosen One many within the Order had placed such hopes upon.

Gone from the Jedi Order in a single day.

Finally she spoke.

"Anakin..."

He looked at her.

"I understand why you're upset."

"Do you?"

"Yes."

She gently took his hand.

"But I don't think you've told me the entire story."

Anakin frowned.

"What do you mean?"

Padmé studied him carefully.

"You said you attacked Master Windu because you believed killing Palpatine was wrong."

"That's exactly what happened."

"Partly."

Anakin looked away.

Padmé knew him too well.

Years together had taught her how to recognize the moments when he was withholding something.

"You genuinely believe Palpatine should have faced trial."

"Yes."

"But that's not enough to explain what happened."

Anakin remained silent.

Padmé continued.

"You challenged one of the most respected Jedi Masters alive."

Still silence.

"You ignited your lightsaber against him."

Anakin's jaw tightened.

"You got yourself expelled from the Order."

She squeezed his hand.

"Why?"

Anakin immediately answered.

"Because Windu was wrong."

Padmé shook her head.

"No."

His expression hardened.

"Yes."

"No."

The answer came instantly.

Anakin looked frustrated.

Padmé held his gaze.

"If it was only about principle, you would have argued."

"I did argue."

"You would have reported your concerns to the Council."

"I did."

"But you wouldn't have attacked him."

Anakin looked away again.

Padmé waited.

Several long moments passed.

Neither spoke.

Finally Anakin stood and walked toward the enormous windows overlooking the city.

Padmé remained where she was.

"What was the real reason?"

Anakin didn't answer.

"Anakin."

Still silence.

"Please."

His shoulders slowly slumped.

Padmé immediately knew she was getting closer to the truth.

He stared out at Coruscant's endless skyline.

"I was trying to save you."

The words were barely audible.

Padmé blinked.

"Save me?"

Anakin nodded.

She stood and approached him.

"What are you talking about?"

Anakin remained facing the window.

For several moments he seemed uncertain whether to continue.

Then he finally spoke.

"I've been having visions."

Padmé's expression grew concerned.

"What kind of visions?"

His voice was strained.

"Visions of you."

Padmé waited.

Anakin swallowed.

"I've seen you die."

The room became very still.

Padmé stared at him.

"What?"

"I've seen it over and over."

He finally turned around.

Fear filled his eyes.

Real fear.

Not the fear of battle.

Not the fear of failure.

Something far more personal.

"You die during childbirth."

Padmé's breath caught.

For several seconds neither spoke.

Then she quietly asked,

"How does that have anything to do with Chancellor Palpatine?"

Anakin hesitated.

He had never fully explained this before.

Now there was little point in hiding it.

He sat back down.

Padmé joined him.

Slowly, Anakin recounted a conversation that had taken place weeks earlier at the Galaxies Opera House.

He explained how Palpatine had spoken about ancient Sith knowledge.

About powers some considered unnatural.

About a Sith Lord who could supposedly influence life itself.

Padmé listened carefully.

Anakin finished.

"He told me there might be a way to prevent death."

Padmé remained thoughtful.

"And you believed him?"

"I didn't know."

"But you hoped."

"Yes."

The answer came immediately.

Padmé nodded slowly.

"And when Master Windu killed him..."

Anakin looked down.

"The knowledge died with him."

Neither spoke for a while.

The weight of the situation hung heavily between them.

Eventually Padmé broke the silence.

"Anakin."

He looked up.

"Let me make sure I understand."

She spoke gently.

"You believed Palpatine knew how to save me."

"Yes."

"Now he's dead."

"Yes."

"You think you've lost the only chance to prevent what you've seen."

Anakin nodded.

Padmé was quiet for a moment.

Then, unexpectedly, she smiled.

Anakin looked confused.

"What?"

Padmé reached for his hand again.

"If you truly believe that."

He frowned.

"Believe what?"

"That you've lost the only way to save me."

Anakin's expression darkened.

"I do."

"Then wouldn't you want to spend as much time with me as possible?"

The question caught him completely off guard.

"What?"

Padmé smiled softly.

"If you think I'm doomed anyway."

Anakin immediately shook his head.

"I don't want to think that."

"I know."

"But if you do believe it..."

She squeezed his hand.

"Why would you spend whatever time remains worrying about things you can't change?"

Anakin opened his mouth.

Then closed it again.

Padmé continued.

"Why wouldn't you spend that time with me instead?"

He stared at her.

The idea seemed almost absurdly simple.

Yet somehow it had never occurred to him.

For weeks, every thought had revolved around finding a solution.

Finding power.

Finding answers.

Finding some way to stop the future he feared.

Padmé was proposing something entirely different.

If he truly believed the future was unavoidable, then every remaining moment suddenly became precious.

She smiled.

"Stay with me."

Anakin said nothing.

"Don't leave."

His expression softened.

"Padmé..."

"You don't have Jedi duties anymore."

The words still sounded strange.

"You don't have missions."

She moved closer.

"You don't have Council meetings."

A small smile appeared.

"No Council at all, actually."

For the first time that day, Anakin almost laughed.

Almost.

Padmé rested her head against his shoulder.

"Just stay with me."

The apartment was quiet.

The city lights reflected in the windows.

Anakin thought about everything that had happened.

Palpatine was dead.

The Jedi Order had expelled him.

The future felt uncertain in ways he had never imagined.

Yet Padmé was here.

Alive.

Warm.

Real.

Not a vision.

Not a nightmare.

Not a possibility.

Here.

Now.

Slowly, he wrapped an arm around her.

"Okay."

Padmé smiled.

"Okay?"

Anakin nodded.

"Okay."

From that day forward, he kept his promise.

Without Jedi responsibilities, there was little demanding his time.

Padmé continued her duties as a senator, navigating the increasingly chaotic political situation created by Palpatine's death and the revelations surrounding his secret identity.

Wherever she went, Anakin was usually close behind.

If she attended a Senate session, he accompanied her.

If she met with political allies, he waited nearby.

If she traveled across Coruscant, he traveled with her.

At first Padmé found it amusing.

Then slightly excessive.

Then simply normal.

The sight of Anakin Skywalker accompanying Senator Amidala became increasingly common.

Some assumed he was acting as an unofficial bodyguard.

Others assumed he was helping investigate the aftermath of Palpatine's downfall.

Very few knew the truth.

Anakin remained near her because he wanted to spend every possible moment with her.

If she was in a room, he preferred being in the same room.

If she crossed the city, he crossed it too.

If she spent an evening working through mountains of Senate documents, he sat nearby.

Sometimes he spoke.

Sometimes he didn't.

Often he simply watched, content that she was there.

For the first time in many years, Anakin Skywalker had nowhere else he needed to be.

And so he stayed exactly where he wanted to be: at Padmé Amidala's side.

# Part 3

The days following Palpatine's death were among the most turbulent in the history of the Republic.

Every news channel on Coruscant discussed little else.

The Supreme Chancellor had been exposed as a Sith Lord.

Three Jedi Masters had died attempting to arrest him.

Mace Windu had killed him during the confrontation.

The Clone Wars still raged in distant sectors.

No one knew exactly what would happen next.

Amid the uncertainty, the Galactic Senate formally requested that two Jedi appear before the entire Senate body to explain the events.

The request named Mace Windu, who had personally confronted and killed Palpatine, and Grand Master Yoda, the leader of the Jedi Order.

The hearing would be broadcast across the Republic.

When Anakin learned of it, he immediately wanted to attend.

"I should be there," he said.

Padmé looked up from her desk.

The two of them were sitting in her apartment overlooking the city. As had become normal over the past several days, Anakin was nearby while she worked through Senate correspondence.

Padmé considered the situation carefully.

She understood why he wanted to go.

The entire galaxy was discussing events that had changed his life forever.

Yet she also knew emotions remained raw.

Many senators were angry.

Many Jedi were angry.

And Anakin himself still carried a great deal of resentment regarding the Council's decision.

A public confrontation would help nobody.

Padmé set down her datapad.

"Then I suppose I should attend as well."

Anakin nodded.

"Probably."

Padmé studied him for a moment.

Then she smiled slightly.

"Actually, I've decided not to."

Anakin blinked.

"What?"

"I'll stay home."

He looked surprised.

"The Senate hearing?"

"I can watch it on the Holonet."

Anakin frowned.

"You don't want to go?"

Padmé shrugged.

"I've spent enough time in the Senate recently."

Anakin looked uncertain.

Then he glanced toward the enormous window overlooking Coruscant.

Finally he sighed.

"Then I'll stay too."

Padmé hid a smile.

The strategy had worked exactly as intended.

"Good."

And so, on the day of the hearing, neither of them attended.

Instead they sat together in Padmé's apartment and watched the proceedings through a live Holonet transmission.

—

The Senate Rotunda was packed.

Thousands of floating repulsorpods filled the enormous chamber.

Countless worlds were represented.

The mood was tense.

At the center platform stood two figures.

Grand Master Yoda.

Master Mace Windu.

The broadcast began with a brief summary of recent events.

Then the questioning started.

Senators from across the Republic demanded answers.

Yoda and Windu answered as thoroughly as they could.

Windu described the confrontation in Palpatine's office.

He recounted the deaths of Agen Kolar, Saesee Tiin, and Kit Fisto.

He explained how Palpatine had resisted arrest.

How he had wielded a red lightsaber.

How he had attacked the Jedi Masters without hesitation.

Many senators listened in stunned silence.

The image of the respected Chancellor personally killing Jedi Masters was difficult to comprehend.

Windu continued.

He described the Force lightning.

The unnatural energy unleashed by the Sith Lord.

The overwhelming power Palpatine had displayed.

The evidence collected from Palpatine's private records.

The connections to Separatist leadership.

The long-running deception.

Yoda then addressed the Senate.

Though brief, his words carried enormous weight.

"A Sith Lord, Chancellor Palpatine was."

Murmurs spread throughout the chamber.

"Manipulated the Republic, he did."

More murmurs.

"Manipulated the war."

Shock rippled through countless Senate pods.

Many senators had suspected corruption.

Few had imagined anything on this scale.

Questions continued for hours.

The debate that followed proved even longer.

One faction argued passionately that the Jedi had saved the Republic.

Without Anakin's revelation and Windu's actions, they argued, Palpatine might have continued his rule indefinitely.

Senators pointed to the evidence.

The Sith identity.

The murders of Jedi Masters.

The connection to the Clone Wars.

The manipulation of Republic institutions.

These senators repeatedly referred to the Jedi as heroes.

Others disagreed.

Not with the evidence itself, but with Windu's final decision.

Palpatine had been the legally elected Supreme Chancellor.

He had not stood trial.

He had not appeared before a court.

They argued that executing him without due process set a dangerous precedent.

Some accused the Jedi of overstepping their authority.

A few even used the word traitors.

The argument consumed the Senate.

Hours passed.

Pods drifted through the chamber as senators took turns speaking.

At Padmé's apartment, Anakin watched with folded arms.

"They don't know what they're talking about."

Padmé glanced toward him.

"Which ones?"

Anakin opened his mouth.

Then paused.

Eventually he answered,

"Most of them."

Padmé laughed softly.

For the first time in days, Anakin managed a small smile.

The debate continued well into the afternoon.

Gradually, however, the balance shifted.

More and more senators concluded that whatever concerns existed regarding procedure, Palpatine had indeed been a Sith Lord responsible for catastrophic crimes.

The deaths of three Jedi Masters attempting an arrest carried enormous weight.

By the end of the proceedings, the dominant position within the Senate was clear.

The Jedi were viewed as heroes who had exposed and stopped a hidden tyrant.

Not everyone agreed.

Some senators remained skeptical.

Others remained deeply uncomfortable with Palpatine's death.

But they had become the minority.

When the final statements concluded, much of the Senate chamber erupted into applause directed toward Yoda and Mace Windu.

The two Jedi accepted the reaction with visible restraint.

Neither appeared interested in celebration.

They had lost comrades.

The Republic remained wounded.

There was still much work ahead.

Yet for the first time since Palpatine's death, public support seemed firmly behind them.

—

The next matter before the Senate was no less important.

The Republic required a new Supreme Chancellor.

Candidates were discussed.

Names circulated rapidly through the chamber.

One name appeared repeatedly.

Padmé Amidala.

Even though she was absent, many senators viewed her as an ideal candidate.

Her reputation had grown considerably during the war.

She was respected by numerous political factions.

Several senators openly declared their intention to support her.

But Padmé had anticipated the possibility.

The previous day she had met privately with Jar Jar Binks.

Now Jar Jar stood before the Senate on her behalf.

The Gungan representative adjusted himself nervously as countless eyes turned toward him.

"Mesa have a message from Senator Amidala."

The chamber quieted.

Jar Jar cleared his throat.

"Senator Amidala is honored by the support she has received."

More senators listened attentively.

"But if senators wish to support her, she asks that those votes instead be given to Senator Bail Organa."

A wave of surprise swept through the chamber.

Even Anakin looked slightly surprised from Padmé's apartment.

"You never told me that."

Padmé smiled.

"You never asked."

Back in the Senate, discussion immediately intensified.

Bail Organa was already a highly respected senator.

He possessed years of experience.

He was widely trusted.

And now many of the votes that might have gone to Padmé began shifting toward him.

The election proceeded.

Rounds of voting followed.

When the final count was completed, the result was decisive.

Bail Organa had won.

Applause spread throughout the chamber.

Bail rose from his pod and moved toward the central platform.

The newly elected Supreme Chancellor paused for a moment before speaking.

The Senate chamber fell silent.

"My fellow senators."

His voice echoed throughout the vast rotunda.

"The Republic has endured terrible trials."

Nods came from all directions.

Not as a Jedi.

Not as a soldier.

Not as a general.

Simply as himself.

Alongside Ahsoka, he traveled throughout the galaxy.

Sometimes they resolved local conflicts.

Sometimes they protected communities.

Sometimes they investigated mysteries connected to the Force.

They continued studying.

Continued learning.

Continued growing.

Outside the structure of the Jedi Order.

Yet still committed to the light.

Meanwhile Padmé continued her work in the Senate.

Luke and Leia grew steadily.

And across the galaxy, the fragile peace between the Republic and the Confederacy slowly took root.

For the first time in many years, Anakin Skywalker no longer felt trapped between duty and fear.

He had a family.

He had friends.

He had purpose.

And though his path no longer resembled the one he once expected to walk, it was a path he was increasingly happy to follow.

# Part 7

Several years passed.

The Republic and the Confederacy maintained their uneasy peace.

The great battles of the Clone Wars gradually faded into history.

Luke and Leia Skywalker grew from infants into energetic toddlers whose curiosity seemed boundless.

Anakin often joked that they had inherited the most dangerous traits from both sides of the family.

Luke possessed his father's tendency to explore anything that looked interesting.

Leia possessed her mother's determination to do exactly what she wanted.

Together they could transform a peaceful apartment into chaos with remarkable efficiency.

Padmé found it exhausting.

Anakin found it hilarious.

Most days.

One afternoon, however, an unexpected visitor arrived.

Mace Windu.

The request had come through official channels beforehand. The Jedi Master had made it clear that he wished to speak privately with Anakin and Padmé regarding an important matter.

When Anakin heard who was coming, his first assumption was obvious.

He expected some sort of attempt to finally settle the conflict that had begun in Palpatine's office years earlier.

He did not look particularly excited about the prospect.

When Mace arrived, the atmosphere was polite but tense.

Padmé welcomed him into the apartment.

Anakin remained courteous.

Neither man seemed especially eager to revisit old history.

Fortunately, Mace wasted little time.

After a few moments of formal conversation, he got directly to the point.

"The Jedi Council asked me to speak with you."

Anakin folded his arms.

"About what?"

Mace glanced toward the twins.

Luke was attempting to stack objects that clearly were not meant to be stacked.

Leia was supervising his efforts while issuing instructions.

Mace returned his attention to their parents.

"The Council believes your children are Force-sensitive."

Anakin blinked.

"That's the emergency?"

Mace ignored the remark.

"They show all the signs."

Padmé exchanged a glance with Anakin.

Neither seemed particularly surprised.

Anakin had privately suspected it for years.

"The Council believes they would make exceptional Jedi younglings."

Now Anakin understood.

Mace continued.

"We would like to bring them to the Temple for training."

The room became quiet.

Padmé looked at Mace.

Then at Anakin.

She said nothing.

The decision, she knew, would largely come down to him.

Anakin considered the proposal.

For several seconds he remained silent.

Then he answered.

"No."

The answer came immediately.

Mace looked genuinely surprised.

Not because he had expected acceptance.

The refusal itself was unsurprising.

What surprised him was how quickly it came.

Anakin hadn't even hesitated.

"No?"

"No."

Mace studied him carefully.

"May I ask why?"

Anakin leaned back in his chair.

"Because I've spent a lot of time thinking about the Jedi Order since I left."

Mace listened.

"I don't agree with everything anymore."

The Jedi Master's expression remained neutral.

Anakin continued.

"When I was a child, being taken into the Order seemed normal."

He glanced toward Luke and Leia.

"Now I look at them and I see it differently."

The twins remained blissfully unaware that their future was being debated.

Luke had now abandoned stacking and was trying to dismantle something instead.

Anakin pointed toward them.

"They have a family."

Mace nodded.

"Yes."

"They have parents."

"Yes."

"They have grandparents, uncles, friends, people who care about them."

Again Mace nodded.

Anakin's expression hardened slightly.

"And I don't believe children should be taken away from all of that."

The room remained silent.

Mace considered his response carefully.

"The Jedi do not view it as taking children away."

Anakin smiled faintly.

"Of course you don't."

The comment carried more edge than humor.

Mace continued.

"The purpose is to provide proper guidance."

"I know."

"The Temple offers structure."

"I know."

"Experience."

"I know."

Mace folded his hands.

"The system exists for a reason."

Anakin nodded.

"I know that too."

Several seconds passed.

Then Mace asked,

"Will they receive training at all?"

Anakin answered immediately.

"Eventually."

Mace raised an eyebrow.

"Eventually?"

"When they're older."

The Jedi Master's expression tightened slightly.

"You know the Order doesn't accept students beyond a certain age."

"I know."

"And if they become too old?"

Anakin shrugged.

"I'll train them."

Mace stared at him.

Anakin continued.

"Or Ahsoka will help."

That answer clearly concerned the Jedi Master.

"Anakin."

"What?"

"Force training is not a casual undertaking."

"I never said it was."

"The Jedi have spent thousands of years developing methods."

"And they aren't the only people capable of teaching the Force."

Mace's gaze sharpened.

"The Temple exists to provide stability."

Anakin immediately countered.

"The Temple also creates rigidity."

Neither man raised his voice.

Yet the tension steadily increased.

Mace spoke carefully.

"Discipline protects students."

"Sometimes."

"It prevents dangerous mistakes."

"Sometimes."

"It helps guard against the dark side."

Anakin leaned forward.

"And sometimes it teaches people that there is only one acceptable way to live."

The room grew quiet.

Years earlier, this conversation might have become explosive very quickly.

Now both men were older.

More experienced.

Yet the underlying disagreement remained.

Mace believed strongly in the Jedi system.

Anakin no longer did.

Not entirely.

Mace continued.

"The Order's methods exist because they work."

Anakin immediately replied.

"They work for some people."

The debate continued.

Back and forth.

Neither side convinced the other.

Mace emphasized the dangers of unstructured Force training.

Anakin emphasized the value of family and personal choice.

Mace warned about emotional attachment leading students astray.

Anakin argued that healthy attachments and support networks could provide stability rather than danger.

Eventually Padmé decided the discussion had gone far enough.

She stepped between them before the disagreement could escalate further.

"Both of you."

The room immediately quieted.

Padmé looked at Mace first.

"You've made your concerns clear."

Then she looked at Anakin.

"And so have you."

Neither interrupted.

Padmé folded her arms.

"Luke and Leia are toddlers."

That statement alone immediately made the argument sound somewhat ridiculous.

"They are not becoming Jedi tomorrow."

Neither man disagreed.

"They are not becoming Force scholars tomorrow."

Still no disagreement.

"They are currently occupied with much more important matters."

As if on cue, a loud crashing sound came from the next room.

All three adults looked over.

Luke and Leia immediately pretended they had done nothing.

Padmé sighed.

"There."

She pointed.

"Those are the pressing concerns in this household."

Even Mace couldn't suppress a faint smile.

Padmé turned serious again.

"They are our children."

Mace nodded.

"Of course."

"And their parents will make decisions regarding their future."

Another nod.

The point was impossible to dispute.

Eventually Mace accepted the outcome.

The discussion was over.

The answer remained no.

Before leaving, he offered a final observation.

"The Council expected this answer."

Anakin raised an eyebrow.

"Then why ask?"

"Because it was worth asking."

That, at least, Anakin could understand.

A short time later, Mace departed.

—

Back at the Jedi Temple, Mace reported the conversation to the Council.

The rejection itself generated little reaction.

Most Council members had expected it.

Anakin's relationship with the Order remained complicated.

Very few had seriously believed he would agree to send Luke and Leia to the Temple.

The more significant issue was something else.

Anakin intended to train them himself.

That immediately sparked discussion.

Some Council members found the idea deeply concerning.

"The dangers are obvious," Ki-Adi-Mundi argued.

"Without proper oversight, mistakes become more likely."

"The Force is not something to approach casually," Coleman Kcaj agreed.

Others took a more nuanced view.

Quinlan Vos was among them.

"The Force doesn't belong exclusively to the Jedi."

Several Masters glanced toward him.

Quinlan continued.

"We didn't invent it."

"No," Ki-Adi-Mundi replied, "but we have centuries of experience teaching it."

"And Anakin has considerable experience himself."

The debate continued.

Luminara emphasized the value of structured training.

Plo Koon questioned whether structure necessarily required Temple residency.

Kelleran Beq pointed out that teaching methods could exist outside traditional institutions.

Oppo Rancisis noted that history contained examples supporting both perspectives.

Yoda listened quietly for much of the discussion.

Eventually it became clear that no consensus would emerge.

The more conservative members worried about insufficient guidance.

The more progressive members questioned whether the Jedi should effectively monopolize light-side instruction.

Neither side persuaded the other.

In the end, practical reality settled the matter.

The Council possessed no authority over Anakin's decisions.

Luke and Leia were not Jedi.

Their parents were not requesting Jedi involvement.

There was nothing to decide.

Gradually the debates faded.

Other matters demanded attention.

The Republic still required guidance.

The galaxy remained complex.

Life moved on.

—

Meanwhile, Luke and Leia continued growing up completely unaware of the conversations occurring around them.

They knew nothing about Council debates.

Nothing about Force-training philosophies.

Nothing about disagreements between Jedi Masters.

Their world remained much simpler.

Family.

Friends.

Games.

Stories.

Adventures.

Luke loved hearing tales about distant worlds.

Leia loved asking questions until adults ran out of answers.

Anakin and Padmé watched them grow with pride.

Whatever the future held, it could wait.

There would be time later for difficult decisions.

Time later for training.

Time later for questions about the Force.

For now, Luke and Leia were simply children.

And that was enough.
