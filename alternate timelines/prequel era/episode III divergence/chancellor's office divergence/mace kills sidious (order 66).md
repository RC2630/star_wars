# Part 1

The duel in the Chancellor's office had already become a scene of devastation.

Three Jedi Masters lay dead.

Master Kit Fisto's body rested near the shattered window overlooking Coruscant's endless cityscape. Masters Agen Kolar and Saesee Tiin lay nearby, cut down only moments after the confrontation had begun. The elegant office of Supreme Chancellor Palpatine had been transformed into a battlefield of broken furniture, scorched walls, and shattered transparisteel.

Only two combatants remained standing.

Mace Windu advanced steadily, his purple lightsaber casting flashes of violet across the ruined chamber.

Across from him stood Chancellor Palpatine—Darth Sidious, Dark Lord of the Sith.

Their blades collided again.

Blue-white lightning crackled outside the towering windows as a storm rolled across the city. The flashes illuminated two masters of the Force locked in a struggle that would determine the fate of the Republic.

Sidious attacked with terrifying speed.

His crimson blade became a blur of violence, driving at Windu's defenses from impossible angles. The Sith Lord's face twisted with fury as he pressed forward.

Windu gave ground only when necessary.

His expression remained controlled, focused. Every strike that came toward him was turned aside. Every opening was exploited. The distinctive rhythms of Vaapad guided his movements as he channeled the fury of his opponent back against him.

The battle carried them across the office.

Furniture exploded into splinters under stray blows. Decorative statues crashed to the floor. The polished surface of Palpatine's desk was sliced apart.

Then Windu saw it.

A flaw.

A fraction of a second.

He moved.

The purple blade slipped through Sidious's defense and struck.

The Sith Lord's lightsaber spun from his hand.

The crimson weapon flew across the room and disappeared into the darkness below the shattered windows.

For the first time, Sidious looked uncertain.

Windu stepped forward.

"You are under arrest."

Sidious's eyes narrowed.

Then darkness erupted from his hands.

Force lightning exploded across the room.

Bolts of blue-white energy slammed into Windu. The Jedi Master crossed his lightsaber before him, catching the torrent on the blade.

The office shook.

The lightning reflected back toward its source.

Sidious screamed.

The energy washed over his own body, twisting his features with pain. His face seemed to age before Windu's eyes.

Still the Sith Lord poured more power into the attack.

The duel had become a contest of will.

For several long seconds neither man yielded.

Then Windu pushed forward.

Step by step.

The reflected lightning intensified.

Sidious staggered backward.

The storm of energy finally collapsed.

The Sith Lord fell to one knee.

Silence filled the office.

Mace Windu stood above him.

His lightsaber hummed.

The battle was over.

"You have lost," Windu said.

Sidious looked up.

His expression changed.

The desperation vanished.

Something cold entered his eyes.

Then he spoke three simple words.

"Execute Order 66."

Windu frowned.

The phrase meant nothing to him.

A strange order to give in defeat.

But he did not hesitate.

The Sith Lord was among the most dangerous beings in the galaxy. Countless deaths, conspiracies, and years of deception rested upon his shoulders. Allowing him another opportunity to act would be madness.

Windu raised his lightsaber.

Sidious opened his mouth as if to speak again.

The purple blade swept downward.

Darth Sidious died.

The chamber became still.

Only the distant thunder remained.

Half a second later the office doors burst open.

Anakin Skywalker rushed inside.

He had moved as quickly as possible after learning that Mace Windu intended to confront the Chancellor. Fear, uncertainty, and conflicting loyalties had driven him through the corridors of the Senate District.

What he found stunned him.

The office was destroyed.

Three Jedi Masters lay dead.

The Chancellor's body rested on the floor.

And Mace Windu stood over it with an ignited lightsaber.

For several seconds neither man spoke.

Then Anakin stared at the body.

"You killed him."

Windu deactivated his weapon.

"He was a Sith Lord."

"He was unarmed."

"He was dangerous."

Anakin looked from the dead Chancellor to the fallen Jedi around him.

"You said he was under arrest."

"He was too dangerous to leave alive."

The words struck Anakin like a blow.

"The Jedi don't execute prisoners."

"The Sith Lord who orchestrated this war was not a prisoner."

Anakin's jaw tightened.

"He should have stood trial."

"And if he escaped? If he manipulated the courts? If he corrupted more worlds?" Windu's voice remained hard. "The threat ended here."

Anakin took a step forward.

"That's not justice."

"And allowing him another chance to destroy the Republic would have been?"

The tension in the room rose sharply.

Both men were exhausted.

Both were angry.

Both believed they were right.

The argument grew more heated with every sentence.

Then suddenly blaster fire erupted.

The office doors exploded inward.

A platoon of clone troopers stormed into the chamber.

Without warning they opened fire.

Neither Jedi had time to think.

Instinct took over.

Blue and purple lightsabers ignited simultaneously.

Blaster bolts filled the air.

"What are they doing?" Anakin shouted as he deflected a barrage.

"I have no idea!" Windu replied.

The clones continued firing.

Not at criminals.

Not at Sith.

At Jedi.

A trooper hurled a thermal detonator.

Anakin seized it with the Force and flung it back. The explosion hurled several clones across the office.

Others advanced through the smoke.

Windu charged directly into them.

His blade became a whirlwind of violet light.

Clone armor split apart under precise strikes.

Anakin joined him.

Together they carved through the platoon.

The battle was brief but savage.

When it ended, dead and wounded clones littered the office floor.

The silence that followed felt almost unreal.

Anakin stared at the fallen soldiers.

"They attacked us."

Windu looked grim.

"Deliberately."

"They've fought beside the Jedi for years."

"I know."

Another distant explosion echoed somewhere outside.

Then came the sound of blaster fire from beyond the office.

The two Jedi exchanged a glance.

Something was terribly wrong.

"The Temple," Windu said.

Anakin immediately understood.

If clones were attacking Jedi here, they might be attacking Jedi everywhere.

Without another word they ran.

They left the ruined office and entered the Senate complex.

The corridors were chaos.

More clone troopers appeared almost immediately.

Again they attacked without hesitation.

The Jedi cut their way through.

As they moved through the massive government district, fragments of confused communications crackled from clone helmets.

"...all Jedi are traitors..."

"...Order 66 confirmed..."

"...eliminate targets..."

The words raised more questions than answers.

Order 66.

The same phrase Sidious had spoken moments before his death.

Neither Jedi knew what it meant, but the connection was impossible to ignore.

"The Chancellor said those words before I killed him," Windu said during a brief lull.

Anakin glanced at him.

"What words?"

"'Execute Order 66.'"

Another squad of clones emerged from an intersection.

The conversation ended instantly.

The fighting resumed.

They crossed plazas and elevated walkways under relentless attack.

Gunships appeared overhead.

Clone pilots targeted them with laser fire.

Speeders exploded around them.

Civilian crowds fled in panic.

At one point an entire company of clone troopers attempted to trap them inside a transit station.

The effort failed.

Anakin and Windu fought side by side with growing efficiency.

Their disagreement remained unresolved, but survival demanded cooperation.

Anakin's aggressive power complemented Windu's disciplined precision.

Again and again they broke through encirclements.

Again and again more clones appeared.

Neither Jedi could understand it.

The Grand Army of the Republic had served alongside the Jedi throughout the Clone Wars. Thousands of Jedi had commanded clone forces in battle.

Yet now the soldiers attacked with unquestioning determination.

Hours seemed compressed into a blur of running, fighting, and confusion.

As they drew closer to the Jedi Temple, signs of catastrophe became impossible to ignore.

Smoke rose into the sky.

Emergency vehicles raced through nearby districts.

Distant flashes of combat lit the horizon.

More than once Anakin sensed deaths through the Force.

Jedi deaths.

The realization chilled him.

Finally the great pyramid of the Jedi Temple came into view.

Both men slowed.

The enormous structure stood against the darkening sky.

But something was wrong.

Blaster fire flashed around the upper levels.

Smoke drifted from several sections of the complex.

Clone gunships circled overhead.

Additional clone forces were visible around the Temple grounds.

Anakin and Mace Windu reached the broad approach leading to the main entrance.

Both stopped.

The journey from the Chancellor's office had answered none of their questions.

If anything, it had created more.

The dead Chancellor's final command echoed in their minds.

Order 66.

Ahead of them stood the Jedi Temple, surrounded by signs of battle.

Neither man knew what they were about to find inside.

# Part 2

Anakin Skywalker and Mace Windu advanced toward the Jedi Temple's main entrance and immediately understood the scale of the disaster.

The broad plaza before the Temple had become a battlefield.

Clone troopers surrounded the immense structure on all sides. Defensive positions had been established behind wrecked speeders, portable barricades, and damaged statuary. Heavy weapons teams fired continuously at the Temple's entrances and windows.

Scattered across the plaza were the bodies of fallen Temple Guards.

The guards who remained alive fought with desperate determination.

Golden lightsabers flashed through smoke and blaster fire as they attempted to hold back overwhelming numbers. Again and again the clones pushed forward. Again and again the surviving guards drove them back.

The moment Anakin and Windu appeared, the clones redirected part of their fire toward the two Jedi.

The response was immediate.

Blue and purple blades ignited.

The pair charged directly into the battle.

The sudden arrival of two powerful Jedi Masters shattered the nearest clone assault. Troopers were forced backward as Windu cut through one formation while Anakin leaped into another, deflecting blaster bolts in every direction.

Within minutes, they had reached the Temple entrance.

What they found inside was no less chaotic.

Several groups of clone troopers had already breached the outer defenses and infiltrated the Temple itself. Blaster fire echoed through the halls.

Near the entrance, Jedi Master Shaak Ti was fighting almost alone against a squad of advancing clones.

Her blue lightsaber moved in sweeping arcs as she defended a corridor leading deeper into the Temple.

When she saw Anakin and Windu, relief flashed briefly across her face.

"Master Windu! Skywalker!"

The infiltrating clones never had a chance.

The three Jedi struck from different directions.

Within moments the corridor was secured.

"What's happening?" Anakin asked as the last trooper fell.

Shaak Ti shook her head.

"I don't know. They attacked without warning."

"The Chancellor spoke the words 'Order 66' moments before he died," Windu said.

Shaak Ti frowned.

"The clones have been repeating those same words over their communications."

None of them had answers.

Only more fighting.

Throughout the Temple, alarms continued to sound.

The battle moved deeper into the complex.

Meanwhile, farther within the Temple's inner chambers, Jedi Master Kelleran Beq had been carrying out a different mission.

He had gathered every youngling he could find.

The children had already sensed fear spreading through the Force.

Many were frightened.

Some were crying.

Others looked to him for reassurance.

Kelleran moved swiftly through the Temple's corridors, escorting groups of younglings away from danger and toward one of the Temple's most secure inner sanctums.

Every minute counted.

Several times he heard distant combat drawing closer.

Several times he sensed Jedi lives abruptly vanishing through the Force.

But he remained focused on his responsibility.

Protect the children.

At last, after gathering all the younglings he could locate, he secured them within a heavily protected chamber deep inside the Temple.

The doors sealed shut.

Security systems activated.

Only then did Kelleran allow himself to turn away.

He ignited both of his lightsabers.

The green blades illuminated the corridor.

The younglings would not face the battle.

He would.

Returning toward the fighting, Kelleran soon encountered Anakin, Windu, and Shaak Ti as they pushed another group of infiltrators from the Temple's interior.

The four Jedi quickly fell into a coordinated defense.

And so began the long struggle for the Temple.

Most of the Jedi Order's Knights and Masters were scattered across the galaxy, leading Republic forces on distant battlefields. Few were present on Coruscant.

The Temple's defense rested largely upon four Jedi.

Mace Windu.

Anakin Skywalker.

Shaak Ti.

Kelleran Beq.

Again and again the clones attacked.

Again and again the defenders stopped them.

The clones attempted to force entry through the main entrance.

They were repelled.

They attempted rooftop insertions from gunships.

They were repelled.

They breached side entrances.

They were repelled.

At one point clone commandos infiltrated through maintenance passages beneath the Temple.

Kelleran and Shaak Ti intercepted them before they could reach the younglings.

Hours passed.

The fighting continued.

Temple Guards fought beside the Jedi whenever possible.

Many gave their lives holding corridors and entrances.

The surviving guards earned precious time for the defenders.

Though exhausted, the four Jedi refused to yield.

Outside the Temple, the clone encirclement gradually tightened.

Inside, however, every attempted breakthrough failed.

The Temple remained in Jedi hands.

Far away, on Kashyyyk, Grand Master Yoda had felt the disturbance almost the moment it began.

The Force had cried out in pain.

Jedi deaths echoed across the galaxy.

Near him, Quinlan Vos sensed it as well.

Neither needed words to understand the severity of what was happening.

The two Jedi departed Kashyyyk as quickly as circumstances allowed and began the journey back to Coruscant.

Elsewhere, on Utapau, Obi-Wan Kenobi had only recently defeated General Grievous when the same wave of death and confusion rippled through the Force.

Clone troopers had turned against him there as well.

Through skill, experience, and no small amount of luck, Obi-Wan survived and escaped the planet.

Like Yoda and Quinlan, he immediately set course for Coruscant.

By the time the three Jedi finally arrived at the Temple, much of the heaviest fighting had already subsided.

The defenders had succeeded.

The clone attacks had slowed considerably after repeated failures to seize the Temple.

When Yoda, Obi-Wan, and Quinlan entered the battle, the remaining clone forces quickly found themselves hopelessly outmatched.

The arrival of three more experienced Jedi Masters transformed the situation entirely.

The surviving attackers were driven away from the Temple grounds.

Additional defensive perimeters were established.

Entrances were secured.

Internal security sweeps were conducted room by room.

Wounded Temple Guards were located and evacuated to medical facilities within the Temple.

Surviving Jedi, Temple staff, and civilians who had sought refuge there were gathered and protected.

The younglings remained safely hidden within the secure chamber Kelleran had prepared.

Only after the immediate danger had passed did the exhausted defenders finally have a chance to speak.

In one of the Temple's council chambers, seven Jedi now stood together.

Yoda.

Mace Windu.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Anakin Skywalker.

Shaak Ti.

Quinlan Vos.

Kelleran Beq.

Around them, healers and Temple personnel continued emergency operations.

Outside, smoke still rose from parts of Coruscant.

Inside, uncertainty remained.

The Republic's clone army had attacked the Jedi across the galaxy.

Countless Jedi had undoubtedly been killed.

The Supreme Chancellor was dead.

No one yet knew who commanded the Republic.

No one yet understood the true nature of Order 66.

But the Jedi Temple still stood.

Its defenders had held.

And for the moment, those who remained focused on a single objective:

protect the survivors, safeguard the younglings, and determine what had happened to the Republic they had spent years fighting to defend.

# Part 3

The hours following the defense of the Jedi Temple were filled not with celebration, but with grim work.

The Temple had survived.

Many of its inhabitants had survived.

The younglings were safe.

Yet as communications began flowing across the galaxy, the defenders slowly realized the true scale of the catastrophe.

Every available communications center inside the Temple was activated.

Jedi technicians, Temple staff, and surviving Knights worked continuously to establish contact with Jedi stationed on distant worlds.

The results were devastating.

Again and again, transmissions went unanswered.

Again and again, calls ended in static.

Entire sectors of the galaxy simply fell silent.

But some calls did connect.

And each successful connection painted the same horrifying picture.

Clone troopers had turned on the Jedi.

Without warning.

Without explanation.

Without provocation.

Padawans called from hidden locations, their voices trembling as they reported the deaths of their masters.

Masters reported losing their Padawans.

Some Jedi had managed to escape.

Others were trapped on hostile worlds and desperately sought evacuation.

Many communications ended abruptly as clones discovered their positions.

Each new report added another name to the growing list of the dead.

Inside the Temple's communications chambers, silence often followed each transmission.

The losses became impossible to ignore.

Master Ki-Adi-Mundi had been killed.

Master Plo Koon had been killed.

Master Stass Allie had been killed.

Master Luminara Unduli had been killed.

Master Jaro Tapal had been killed.

Master Depa Billaba had been killed.

The names continued.

Knights.

Masters.

Padawans.

Entire generations of Jedi had vanished in the span of a few terrible hours.

Nobody yet knew the final number.

Only that it was enormous.

One small mercy remained.

The youngest members of the Order had largely been spared.

Because the Temple had held, because Kelleran Beq and others had secured the younglings early, hundreds of children remained safe deep within the Temple's protected chambers.

As reports continued arriving, Grand Master Yoda issued a message through every channel still available to the Jedi Order.

His instructions were simple.

Any Jedi who survived and could safely travel should come to Coruscant.

The Temple was secure.

The landing platforms surrounding it had been locked down and fortified.

Defensive patrols had been established.

The Temple would serve as a refuge for survivors.

Over the next day, survivors began arriving.

The first arrivals were often exhausted, wounded, and grieving.

Many had narrowly escaped death.

Others arrived carrying the burden of having watched friends and students die.

Each arrival was welcomed.

Each survivor strengthened the Temple's defenses.

Among those who reached Coruscant were Jedi Masters Coleman Kcaj and Oppo Rancisis.

Their arrivals were met with visible relief.

Every surviving Master represented knowledge, leadership, and stability during a moment when all three were desperately needed.

Yoda also made a controversial decision.

He extended invitations beyond the active Jedi Order.

Messages were sent to former Jedi who had left the Order but whom Yoda still trusted.

Among them were Eeth Koth and Ahsoka Tano.

Neither was obligated to return.

Both had chosen different paths.

Yet when they learned what had happened, neither ignored the call.

Eeth Koth returned to Coruscant.

Ahsoka Tano did as well.

Whatever their relationship with the Order had become, neither could stand aside while so many lives hung in the balance.

Their arrivals brought comfort to many survivors.

Especially among younger Jedi who had known them personally.

As the number of survivors within the Temple gradually increased, a difficult question emerged.

The dead deserved to be honored.

Many Jedi wished to hold proper funerals.

The losses were beyond counting.

The victims included those killed during Order 66 across the galaxy.

The Temple Guards who had fallen defending the Temple.

And the three Masters who had died confronting Darth Sidious in the Chancellor's office: Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin.

But circumstances made traditional ceremonies impossible.

The crisis remained ongoing.

Rescue efforts continued.

Survivors still needed to be located.

The Temple required constant defense.

Reluctantly, the senior Jedi agreed that extensive funeral rites would have to wait.

Instead, a collective memorial service was held.

The gathering took place within one of the Temple's largest halls.

Hundreds attended.

Masters.

Knights.

Padawans.

Temple staff.

Younglings.

The wounded.

The exhausted.

The grieving.

Yoda stood before them.

The ancient Grand Master spoke briefly.

He did not attempt to lessen the pain.

He did not promise that everything would be repaired.

He simply honored those who had fallen and reminded the survivors that the dead lived on through the Force.

When the memorial concluded, many remained in silent reflection long afterward.

Yet the Order still required leadership.

Soon after the service, Yoda called a meeting to determine the state of the Jedi Council.

The findings were sobering.

Of the twelve Council members who had guided the Order before the catastrophe, only six remained alive and present at the Temple.

Yoda.

Mace Windu.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Shaak Ti.

Coleman Kcaj.

Anakin Skywalker.

The others had fallen.

Kit Fisto.

Agen Kolar.

Saesee Tiin.

Plo Koon.

Ki-Adi-Mundi.

Stass Allie.

Half the Council was gone.

The realization weighed heavily upon everyone present.

For a time, nobody spoke.

Then Yoda rose.

Before addressing the vacancies, he turned toward Anakin.

"Much done for the Temple's defense, you have."

Anakin looked surprised.

Many of the surviving Jedi were watching.

Yoda continued.

"Against impossible odds, stood you. Defended the Temple, protected the younglings, and helped preserve the Order."

Mace Windu gave a slight nod.

Obi-Wan smiled faintly.

The other Masters remained attentive.

"In recognition of your service," Yoda said, "the rank of Jedi Master, grant you now, the Council does."

For a moment Anakin simply stared.

The words took several seconds to fully register.

Years of struggle, achievement, frustration, and sacrifice suddenly culminated in a single sentence.

Then he bowed respectfully.

"Thank you, Master."

The gathered Jedi offered quiet approval.

Under ordinary circumstances, such a promotion might have been accompanied by formal ceremonies.

Instead it occurred amid a crisis that threatened the survival of the entire Order.

Nevertheless, the significance was not lost on anyone present.

The Council now turned to the problem of replacing its fallen members.

Yoda first invited Kelleran Beq.

The response came immediately.

"I accept."

There was little hesitation.

Kelleran had proven himself during the defense of the Temple and had already earned widespread respect among the survivors.

Next came Oppo Rancisis.

The serpentine Master appeared thoughtful.

Unlike Kelleran, he had previously served on the Council before voluntarily stepping away from the position.

"I have already carried those responsibilities once," Oppo said carefully.

"And now we need those responsibilities carried again," Yoda replied.

The Grand Master's voice remained gentle but firm.

"The Order needs experience."

After a long pause, Oppo inclined his head.

"Very well. I will serve."

Attention then shifted toward Quinlan Vos.

The request clearly made him uncomfortable.

Quinlan remained silent for several moments before speaking.

"You know my history."

Everyone in the room did.

Quinlan had once fallen to darkness before ultimately returning to the light.

The memory remained painful.

"I do know your history," Yoda replied.

"And trust you, I do."

Several Masters nodded in agreement.

Obi-Wan among them.

The room remained quiet.

Eventually Quinlan exhaled.

"If you truly believe I can help..."

Yoda's expression remained calm.

"I do."

At last, Quinlan nodded.

"Then I accept."

The final invitation went to Eeth Koth.

Unlike the others, he did not hesitate.

But his answer was not the one many hoped for.

"I am honored," Eeth said.

"However, my answer is no."

No one interrupted.

Eeth continued.

"I chose my path when I left the Order. I have responsibilities beyond the Temple now. I have a life outside the Jedi Order."

His tone remained respectful but unwavering.

"I came because people needed help. That doesn't mean I can return to serving on the Council."

Yoda listened carefully.

Then he nodded.

"No pressure from me, there shall be."

Eeth bowed gratefully.

The matter ended there.

His decision was accepted without resentment.

When the discussions concluded, the Jedi Council stood at nine members.

Grand Master Yoda.

Mace Windu.

Anakin Skywalker.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Shaak Ti.

Coleman Kcaj.

Quinlan Vos.

Kelleran Beq.

Oppo Rancisis.

Three seats remained empty.

The Council considered additional candidates but found no immediate solution.

Many potential candidates were dead.

Others were missing.

Still others were engaged in rescue efforts across the galaxy.

Eventually Yoda made a practical decision.

The Council would temporarily shrink.

Rather than rush appointments during a crisis, the Jedi Council would operate with nine members for the foreseeable future.

The remaining three seats would stay vacant until suitable Masters could be found, whether through future rescues of survivors or eventual promotions from the ranks of Knights.

The decision was unanimously accepted.

As the meeting adjourned, the nine Council members remained aware of the immense challenges ahead.

The Jedi Order had survived.

But it had been wounded more severely than at any point in living memory.

Across the galaxy, survivors still needed rescue.

Entire sectors remained hostile.

The mystery of Order 66 remained unsolved.

The Republic's government stood leaderless following the death of Chancellor Palpatine.

And countless worlds awaited answers.

Yet for the first time since the catastrophe began, the Jedi possessed something they had lacked during those first terrible hours.

A functioning Council.

A secure Temple.

And a growing community of survivors determined to rebuild what had nearly been destroyed.

# Part 4

While the Jedi Temple struggled to understand the disaster that had befallen the Order, another crisis was quietly unfolding elsewhere on Coruscant.

The Galactic Senate had not yet learned what had happened inside Chancellor Palpatine's office.

The following morning, senators gathered beneath the vast dome of the Senate Chamber for what was expected to be a routine session.

The Clone Wars still raged across the galaxy.

Important military updates were expected.

Most significantly, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine was scheduled to address the entire Senate.

The Chancellor never arrived.

At first, nobody was particularly alarmed.

Meetings occasionally ran long. Delays happened.

The Senate waited.

Ten minutes passed.

Then twenty.

Then thirty.

Murmurs spread throughout the immense chamber.

This was unusual.

Palpatine had built a reputation for punctuality and meticulous attention to his duties. Missing a Senate session without explanation was completely out of character.

Eventually a group of concerned senators volunteered to investigate.

The delegation departed the Senate Chamber and made its way through the government district toward the Chancellor's office.

What they found left them horrified.

The office looked like a battlefield.

Walls were scorched.

Furniture lay shattered across the floor.

The enormous windows overlooking Coruscant had been destroyed.

And among the wreckage lay bodies.

Three dead Jedi Masters.

Kit Fisto.

Agen Kolar.

Saesee Tiin.

And near the center of the ruined chamber lay the body of Supreme Chancellor Palpatine himself.

The senators immediately withdrew.

Within minutes they returned to the Senate Chamber and reported what they had found.

The revelation plunged the Senate into chaos.

Thousands of voices erupted at once.

Questions filled the chamber.

How had the Chancellor died?

Why were Jedi Masters dead beside him?

Who had attacked the office?

Was Coruscant under attack?

Had there been a coup?

Had the Separatists somehow infiltrated the capital?

The Senate quickly reached out to the Jedi Temple.

A secure holocommunication link was established.

When the transmission activated, the senators saw several senior Jedi assembled within the Temple.

After brief discussion among the Jedi, Mace Windu stepped forward.

The chamber gradually quieted.

What followed was one of the most consequential explanations ever delivered before the Galactic Senate.

Windu told them everything.

He explained that Anakin Skywalker had discovered evidence identifying Chancellor Palpatine as Darth Sidious, a Sith Lord.

He explained that a team of Jedi Masters had gone to arrest the Chancellor.

He explained how the confrontation had escalated into a lightsaber duel.

He described the deaths of Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin.

He described the red lightsaber wielded by the Chancellor.

He described the Force lightning.

He described Palpatine's final words.

"Execute Order 66."

He described killing the Sith Lord.

He described Anakin arriving moments later.

He described clone troopers suddenly attacking both Jedi without provocation.

He described the running battles through Coruscant.

He described the assault on the Jedi Temple.

He described the defense of the Temple and the survival of the younglings.

Throughout the chamber, senators listened in stunned silence.

Some had personally witnessed signs of fighting throughout Coruscant.

Many had seen smoke rising over parts of the city.

Some had observed military activity and scattered combat.

Yet none of them had understood what they were witnessing.

The clones had not attacked senators.

The clones had not attacked government buildings.

The clones had targeted the Jedi.

Only now did the larger picture become clear.

When Windu finished speaking, the Senate Chamber erupted into intense debate.

The Republic suddenly faced multiple crises simultaneously.

Its Chancellor was dead.

Its military had apparently turned against its own generals.

The Jedi Order had suffered catastrophic losses.

The Clone Wars still raged.

And nobody knew why any of it had happened.

The debate lasted for hours.

Eventually several major decisions emerged.

The first concerned the war itself.

Earlier, during communications with the Senate, Obi-Wan Kenobi had reported that he had successfully eliminated General Grievous on Utapau.

Count Dooku was already dead.

Now Chancellor Palpatine had also been revealed as Darth Sidious and killed.

The Separatist leadership had been shattered.

At the same time, the Republic itself was reeling from the actions of its clone army.

Neither side was in a position to continue fighting effectively.

The Senate therefore authorized immediate ceasefire negotiations with the Confederacy of Independent Systems.

The response from the Separatists came surprisingly quickly.

Many within the Confederacy had already begun questioning the future of the war following Grievous's death.

With both Grievous and Dooku gone, entire sectors of the Separatist military chain of command were collapsing.

Neither side had much appetite for continued conflict.

The ceasefire proposal was welcomed.

Negotiations moved rapidly.

Within a remarkably short period, both governments formally agreed to suspend hostilities while each addressed its own internal emergencies.

For the first time in years, large portions of the galaxy experienced peace.

The second issue was leadership.

Palpatine's death had left the Republic without a Chancellor.

Ordinarily, an election would be held.

Yet the Senate concluded that organizing a galactic election amid a military crisis and ongoing investigations would be impractical.

A temporary solution was adopted.

Until a proper election could be arranged, the Senate itself would govern collectively.

Republic decisions would be made through direct Senate votes rather than through a Chancellor's office.

The third issue concerned Coruscant's security.

Many senators feared further violence if large numbers of clone troopers remained stationed on the capital.

At the same time, many Jedi remained confined to the Temple due to concerns about additional attacks.

After lengthy debate, the Senate approved a temporary relocation of all clone military personnel stationed on Coruscant.

The objective was simple.

Separate the Jedi and the clones.

Prevent further bloodshed.

Restore stability to the capital.

To avoid provoking additional conflict, the Senate took special precautions.

No Jedi participated in the operation.

No Jedi supervised it.

No Jedi were involved in planning it.

Instead, civilian administrators, transport officials, and Senate personnel carried out the relocation entirely on their own.

The result surprised many observers.

The clones offered no resistance.

They boarded transports when instructed.

They followed orders.

They relocated peacefully.

No battles occurred.

No incidents were reported.

The strange behavior only deepened the mystery.
