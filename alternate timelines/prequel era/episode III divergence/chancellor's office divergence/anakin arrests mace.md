# Part 1

The office of the Supreme Chancellor had become a battlefield.

The polished floor was scarred by molten cuts. Smoke curled from shattered furnishings. Rain lashed against the towering windows overlooking Coruscant, and every flash of lightning illuminated the bodies of Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin where they had fallen only moments earlier.

Mace Windu stood alone.

Across from him, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine no longer made any attempt to conceal what he was.

His crimson lightsaber hissed through the air in blinding arcs as yellow eyes burned with hatred.

"You are finished, Master Windu," Palpatine snarled.

"You've murdered enough people today."

Their blades collided again.

Purple against red.

The impact shook the room.

Palpatine lunged with impossible speed, forcing Mace backward across the ruined office. Mace's muscles screamed with exhaustion. He had watched three Jedi Masters die within seconds. Every exchange with the Sith Lord demanded absolute concentration.

Palpatine laughed.

"Is this the legendary Master Windu? Is this all the Jedi Council can offer?"

Mace answered with silence.

Vaapad.

Every ounce of Palpatine's fury fed into the form. Every hateful strike became strength redirected against its source.

Palpatine unleashed a storm of attacks.

Mace gave ground.

Another strike.

Another.

Another.

Then, almost imperceptibly, the rhythm changed.

Palpatine overextended.

Mace pivoted.

Purple flashed.

Palpatine's lightsaber flew from his hand and spun across the chamber before clattering onto the floor.

The Sith Lord staggered backward.

Mace's blade stopped at his throat.

"It is over."

Palpatine's expression changed instantly.

Gone was the triumphant predator.

Now he looked frail.

Defenseless.

"I... I am unarmed..."

Mace did not lower his weapon.

"You are a Sith Lord."

"I can stand trial."

"You've controlled the Republic for years."

"I surrender."

"You murdered three Jedi Masters less than a minute ago."

"I surrender."

Lightning crackled around Palpatine's fingertips.

It was only the slightest twitch.

Only the smallest movement.

But Mace saw it.

He remembered Kit Fisto collapsing.

Saesee Tiin dying before he could react.

Agen Kolar cut down almost instantly.

He remembered the sheer speed.

The overwhelming power.

Mace's grip tightened.

"You are too dangerous to be left alive."

Palpatine's eyes narrowed.

"So... it comes to this."

Mace answered with one swift motion.

His lightsaber passed cleanly through the Chancellor's neck.

Palpatine's body collapsed.

Silence filled the office.

Mace stood motionless.

Breathing heavily.

Rain poured through the shattered windows.

Then the office doors burst open.

"Master Windu!"

Anakin Skywalker rushed inside.

He froze.

Palpatine's body lay across the polished floor.

The three fallen Jedi surrounded it.

Mace slowly turned.

"Skywalker..."

Anakin stared.

"...You killed him."

Mace looked down at Palpatine.

"Yes."

"You killed the Chancellor."

"He was a Sith Lord."

"I know he was. I was the one who told you he was the Sith Lord you were searching for."

For a brief moment neither man spoke.

Then Anakin looked back at Palpatine.

"You killed him."

"He was too dangerous to leave alive."

Anakin's jaw tightened.

"That isn't the Jedi way."

"He slaughtered three Jedi Masters."

"I know."

"He was about to attack again."

"I know."

"He could not be allowed another opportunity."

"You should have arrested him."

Mace's expression hardened.

"Arrested him?"

"Yes."

"You didn't see what happened here."

"I can see enough."

"No, Skywalker."

Mace pointed toward the bodies.

"Kit Fisto."

Then another.

"Saesee Tiin."

Another.

"Agen Kolar."

"They're dead."

"I can see that."

"They came to arrest him."

"I know."

"He killed all 3."

"I know."

"He proved beyond question that he was too dangerous to be left alive."

Anakin shook his head.

"No."

Mace stared at him.

"No?"

"The Jedi arrest people."

"They defend the Republic."

"They don't execute prisoners."

"He wasn't a prisoner."

"He was disarmed."

"He was still the most dangerous Sith Lord in the galaxy."

"He was still entitled to a trial."

"You weren't here."

"I am here now."

"You don't understand."

"I understand perfectly."

Anakin's voice became sharper.

"You had him defeated."

"Yes."

"You had won."

"Yes."

"And then you killed him."

"Yes."

"That was wrong."

Mace's eyes narrowed.

"You think I should have gambled the Republic on the hope that the most powerful Sith Lord alive would stay peacefully in custody?"

"I think you should have followed the Jedi way."

"The Jedi way is to protect innocent lives."

"And the Jedi way is justice."

"The Sith do not believe in justice."

"The Jedi do."

For several seconds they simply stared at each other.

Mace finally spoke.

"Stand aside."

"What?"

"I said stand aside."

"What are you going to do?"

"I must report what happened."

"You'll answer for what happened."

"I already am."

"No."

Anakin ignited his lightsaber.

"You'll answer before the Senate."

Mace slowly activated his own blade.

"So that's what this has become."

"You killed the Supreme Chancellor."

"I killed a Sith Lord."

"You killed an unarmed man."

"I killed someone too dangerous to leave alive."

Neither moved.

Rain echoed through the shattered office.

Then Anakin attacked.

His blue blade came down with astonishing speed.

Mace blocked.

The impact nearly drove him to one knee.

He was exhausted.

Every muscle burned from his battle with Palpatine.

His breathing remained uneven.

Anakin attacked again.

And again.

Each strike carried all the strength and fury of the Chosen One.

Mace answered with flawless technique, but every defense demanded more effort than the last.

"You should have arrested him!"

Another clash.

"He murdered 3 Jedi!"

Another.

"That doesn't justify execution!"

"You didn't witness what he could do!"

"I didn't need to!"

Mace's boots slid across the polished floor.

His vision blurred for an instant.

Fatigue.

Palpatine had pushed him beyond his limits.

Anakin sensed it.

His assault intensified.

Blue flashes overwhelmed the office.

Mace parried high.

Low.

Side.

High again.

His arms grew heavier.

His reactions slowed.

Finally Anakin feinted left before spinning into a powerful overhead strike.

Mace barely intercepted it.

The impact jarred his numb hands.

His lightsaber flew from his grasp.

It skidded across the floor.

Before he could recover, Anakin's blade rested inches from his chest.

The room fell silent.

Mace looked at him.

"You've won."

"I don't want to kill you."

"I know."

"I'm placing you under arrest."

Mace gave a weary laugh.

"For killing Palpatine."

"For killing the Chancellor."

"He was a Sith Lord."

"I know exactly what he was."

Anakin deactivated his lightsaber.

"You'll answer before the Senate."

Mace did not resist as security restraints were fastened around his wrists.

Later, under heavy escort, Anakin entered the Senate Rotunda.

The chamber buzzed with confusion.

Senators filled their pods.

Whispers spread rapidly as they saw Mace Windu restrained.

Mas Amedda stood near the Chancellor's podium, visibly shaken.

"What is the meaning of this?" someone demanded.

Anakin stepped forward into the center.

"The Supreme Chancellor is dead."

Gasps erupted across the chamber.

"I arrested Master Mace Windu."

Silence followed.

Anakin continued.

"He killed Chancellor Palpatine."

Every senator looked toward Mace.

Mace lifted his head.

"I did."

The chamber exploded into shouting.

"Bring him to trial!"

"Murder!"

"What happened?"

"Order!"

"Silence!"

Eventually the chamber quieted enough for Mace to speak.

"Palpatine was a Sith Lord."

Confused murmurs swept through the Senate.

"A Sith Lord?" one senator repeated.

"What does that even mean?"

Mace raised his voice.

"He was the enemy the Jedi Order has been searching for. He orchestrated this war. When I confronted him, he murdered Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar."

More murmurs.

Anakin nodded.

"Everything Master Windu has just said is true."

The chamber became noticeably quieter.

One senator frowned.

"You are confirming this?"

"Yes."

"You agree the Chancellor was a Sith Lord?"

"Yes."

"You agree he killed 3 Jedi Masters?"

"Yes."

"You witnessed this?"

"I arrived after the fight ended."

A senator interrupted.

"Then how do you know?"

"Because Chancellor Palpatine personally revealed his identity to me before Master Windu confronted him."

The chamber erupted again.

"You knew?"

"You told the Jedi?"

Anakin nodded.

"Yes."

"I informed Master Windu and the other Jedi Masters because I believed the Chancellor needed to be investigated and arrested."

Mace looked directly at him.

"You told us because you knew what he was."

"I told you so he would face justice."

"He did."

"No."

Mace's voice echoed through the chamber.

"He was too dangerous to leave alive."

Anakin answered immediately.

"He should have been arrested."

"He had already killed 3 Jedi Masters."

"I know."

"He proved he could not safely be taken into custody."

"I disagree."

"You weren't there."

"I arrived moments later."

"You didn't witness what he did."

"I know exactly what he did."

Anakin looked around the Senate.

"I do not dispute the facts."

He pointed toward Mace.

"Master Windu is telling the truth about who Palpatine was."

More senators leaned forward.

"He was a Sith Lord."

"Yes."

"He killed 3 Jedi Masters?"

"Yes."

"You informed the Jedi yourself?"

"Yes."

"Then why is Master Windu on trial instead of being honored?"

Anakin answered without hesitation.

"Because the Jedi are not executioners."

He looked directly at Mace.

"I told the Jedi about Chancellor Palpatine because I expected them to investigate him, defeat him if necessary, arrest him, and bring him before the Senate for trial."

He shook his head.

"I never intended for them to kill him."

Mace stepped forward as far as his restraints allowed.

"He would have escaped."

"You don't know that."

"I do."

"He had already killed Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin."

"I know."

"He was too dangerous."

"I disagree."

Mace's voice sharpened.

"You are the one who told us Palpatine was a Sith Lord."

"Yes."

"You asked us to act."

"Yes."

"And now you stand against the Jedi."

"I stand against killing a defeated prisoner."

"I never intended to kill him."

Anakin frowned.

"What?"

"I went there to arrest him."

The chamber quieted.

"I intended to place him under arrest."

Mace looked around the Senate.

"But after I watched him effortlessly kill Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin, I realized the truth."

He looked back at Anakin.

"He had proven himself too dangerous to leave alive."

Anakin answered calmly.

"And after I witnessed you kill him, I dueled you."

He gestured toward Mace's restraints.

"I defeated you."

"I disarmed you."

"And I arrested you instead of killing you."

The Senate listened intently.

Anakin continued.

"I believe that is the Jedi way."

Several senators nodded.

"You brought him here."

"Yes."

"You could have killed him?"

"Yes."

"But I didn't."

Mace clenched his fists.

"You compare me to yourself?"

"I compare our choices."

"I had just fought the most powerful Sith Lord alive."

"I know."

"My strength was nearly exhausted."

"I know."

"I made the only decision that could guarantee the Republic's safety."

"And I believe the safer decision for the Republic is that no Jedi decides who lives and who dies without trial."

The senators exchanged uneasy glances.

Most of them understood very little about Sith Lords.

The title itself meant almost nothing to many of them.

What they did understand was that the Supreme Chancellor had died by the hand of a Jedi Master, and another Jedi had chosen to arrest that Master rather than execute him.

That seemed simpler.

More lawful.

The mood of the chamber slowly shifted.

Not because they rejected Mace's account of what Palpatine had been—Anakin himself had confirmed every significant fact—but because they increasingly saw the dispute as one of principle rather than truth.

The facts were largely uncontested.

Palpatine had been a Sith Lord.

Palpatine had killed 3 Jedi Masters.

Anakin had informed Mace of Palpatine's identity.

Mace had killed Palpatine.

Anakin had defeated and arrested Mace instead of killing him.

The disagreement rested entirely on what should have happened after Palpatine had been disarmed.

Mace could feel opinion slipping away from him.

He searched the chamber instinctively for allies.

There were none.

Kit Fisto.

Saesee Tiin.

Agen Kolar.

All dead.

Yoda was still returning from Kashyyyk.

Obi-Wan Kenobi remained away from Coruscant after his mission.

Neither was present to explain to the Senate what a Sith Lord truly represented or why Mace believed no prison could safely contain one.

Standing alone before hundreds of senators who barely understood the danger he had faced, Mace struggled to make them see beyond the image before their eyes: a Jedi Master who had slain the Supreme Chancellor.

The debate continued, the fate of Mace Windu still undecided as voices rose once more across the Senate chamber.

# Part 2

The Senate chamber remained tense.

Voices continued to echo from every direction as senators debated whether Mace Windu had saved the Republic or violated the very principles the Jedi claimed to uphold.

Then another voice rose above the chamber.

"General Skywalker."

The chamber gradually quieted.

Senator Padmé Amidala stood from her pod.

Anakin turned toward her.

For the briefest instant, his expression softened.

Then she spoke.

"You claim to be a defender of the Jedi way."

Anakin nodded once.

"I do."

"And yet you killed an unarmed prisoner yourself."

Silence spread through the Senate.

Padmé continued.

"When you defeated Count Dooku, you executed him on the spot instead of arresting him."

The silence became absolute.

Anakin simply stared at her.

His thoughts raced.

*How does she...?*

Then he remembered.

He had told her himself.

In private.

He had trusted her completely.

Never had he imagined she would repeat those words before the entire Galactic Senate.

Hundreds of eyes settled upon him.

He chose his next words with extraordinary care.

"Senator Amidala..."

He paused.

"...that information is... classified."

A murmur swept across the chamber.

Mace looked sharply toward Anakin.

"I am not aware of this, Skywalker."

His eyes narrowed.

"Why is Senator Amidala aware of this, but none of the Jedi are?"

He turned toward Padmé.

"Senator Amidala, where did you learn this information?"

Padmé remained silent.

She knew exactly where she had learned it.

But she also knew she could not answer honestly.

She hesitated.

Before she could find another response, a familiar golden protocol droid unexpectedly stood up from behind her pod.

"It seems that Master Anakin—"

Padmé reacted instantly.

She reached over and placed a hand firmly across the droid's mouth.

"Threepio!"

She leaned close and whispered urgently.

"You can't call him 'Master Anakin' here!"

Threepio's photoreceptors widened.

"Oh! My apologies, Senator."

He quietly sat back down without saying another word.

The awkward silence lingered.

At last Anakin spoke again.

"Senator Amidala..."

His voice was calm.

"It is not right to compare Count Dooku to Chancellor Palpatine."

Padmé folded her arms.

"Why not?"

"Dooku was the leader of the enemy during the war."

"And Palpatine was our own Chancellor."

Padmé answered immediately.

"And Palpatine was not an enemy?"

Anakin opened his mouth.

"You are calling a Sith Lord not an enemy?"

"No, of course not."

He answered without hesitation.

"All Sith Lords are enemies."

He took a measured breath.

"Palpatine is also an enemy because he is a Sith Lord, but—"

The great doors of the Senate chamber opened.

Every head turned.

Yoda entered first, moving with remarkable speed despite his age.

Beside him walked Obi-Wan Kenobi, still wearing the dust and scars of recent battle.

The moment the senators recognized him, the chamber erupted.

"General Kenobi!"

"He defeated General Grievous!"

"The war may finally be ending!"

One by one, senators rose to their feet.

Within moments, nearly the entire Senate was standing.

Applause thundered through the chamber.

Obi-Wan looked visibly uncomfortable.

He gave only a slight bow.

"Thank you," he said quietly. "But there are more pressing matters before us."

The applause slowly faded.

Yoda wasted no time.

"Late we are. Apologize, I must."

He looked toward Mace.

"Message through the Force, Master Windu sent."

Mace let out a slow breath of relief.

At last.

He was no longer alone.

Obi-Wan stepped forward.

"Masters, Senators..."

He looked across the chamber.

"If Master Windu concluded that Chancellor Palpatine was too dangerous to leave alive, I ask that you understand the circumstances under which that decision was made."

A senator raised a hand.

"You are saying he was justified?"

Obi-Wan answered carefully.

"I am saying that a Sith Lord possesses abilities unlike those of ordinary criminals."

Yoda nodded.

"Dangerous beyond understanding, the Sith are."

Another senator frowned.

"But General Skywalker has already confirmed Palpatine was defeated."

Yoda answered.

"Defeated, yes."

"Harmless, no."

Obi-Wan continued.

"The Jedi prefer arrest whenever possible."

He glanced toward Mace.

"But if a Jedi Master who has just witnessed 3 fellow Masters killed concludes that no prison can safely contain such an opponent..."

He allowed the sentence to hang.

"...that conclusion deserves serious consideration."

Several senators nodded thoughtfully.

Others remained unconvinced.

Questions came from every direction.

"If Sith Lords are so dangerous, why has the Republic never heard this before?"

"Could no security measures have contained him?"

"Would execution become acceptable whenever a Jedi claimed someone was too dangerous?"

Hours seemed to pass as arguments continued.

Yoda spoke.

Obi-Wan spoke.

Mace defended his decision.

Anakin defended his own.

The debate remained respectful, but the Senate never fully embraced the Jedi Masters' explanation.

Many respected Yoda beyond measure.

Many admired Obi-Wan's integrity.

Yet respect alone did not erase their unease.

They still saw a Jedi Master who had slain the Supreme Chancellor without trial.

Eventually Mas Amedda struck the staff of office against the floor.

"The Senate must decide."

After further deliberation, the presiding officials announced the chamber's conclusion.

"Given the extraordinary circumstances..."

"The office of Supreme Chancellor now stands vacant."

"The Republic must immediately address the question of national leadership."

"Accordingly, Master Mace Windu shall be placed under temporary Republic custody while the Senate attends to matters of immediate governmental necessity."

"His final disposition shall be determined after further deliberation."

The chamber buzzed with agreement.

It was not an acquittal.

It was not a conviction.

It was a postponement.

Mace closed his eyes briefly.

Then he nodded.

"I will not resist."

Republic security officers approached.

This time Mace offered his wrists without objection.

As they led him away, he looked once toward Yoda and Obi-Wan.

Neither could promise victory.

Only that the debate was not yet over.

The Senate session finally adjourned.

Outside the Senate building, evening had settled over Coruscant.

Traffic streamed endlessly through the sky.

Anakin descended the great staircase alone.

"Anakin."

He stopped.

Yoda stood waiting.

Beside him was Obi-Wan.

Obi-Wan studied his former apprentice with troubled eyes.

"Why?"

Anakin sighed.

"I've already explained."

"You arrested Master Windu."

"Yes."

"You fought him."

"Yes."

"You brought him before the Senate."

"Yes."

"Why?"

Anakin answered quietly.

"Because it wasn't the Jedi way to kill Palpatine like that."

Yoda watched him carefully.

"Question, I have."

Anakin nodded.

"Ask."

"True what Senator Amidala said?"

A pause.

"Executed Count Dooku, did you?"

Anakin lowered his eyes.

"...Yes."

Obi-Wan looked at him in disbelief.

"You never told me."

"I couldn't."

Yoda continued.

"Then explain."

Anakin looked back at them.

"When I defeated Count Dooku..."

He hesitated.

"...he was disarmed."

Yoda waited.

"Palpatine ordered me to kill him."

"You obeyed."

"I did."

"Wrong, that was."

"I know."

Silence lingered.

Finally Anakin continued.

"I've regretted it ever since."

He looked directly at Yoda.

"I allowed someone else to pressure me into abandoning the Jedi way."

"And today?"

"Today I refused to let it happen again."

Yoda slowly shook his head.

"Different, these situations were."

"How?"

"Pressure, Master Windu also faced."

Anakin frowned.

"No one told him to kill Palpatine."

"No person did."

Yoda's ears lowered slightly.

"But pressure from the deaths of 3 Jedi Masters."

He gestured toward the Senate building.

"Pressure from protecting the Republic."

"Pressure from facing a Sith Lord unlike any seen in generations."

Anakin remained unconvinced.

"He still chose."

"So did you."

Neither spoke for several moments.

Then Yoda continued.

"Remember also, hierarchy you must."

Anakin's expression hardened.

"You were a Jedi Knight."

"Master Windu..."

"...Master of the Order he is."

"A senior member of the Jedi Council."

"A Jedi Master respected throughout the Order."

"You challenged him."

"You dueled him."

"You arrested him."

"You spoke against him before the Senate."

Yoda's voice became firmer.

"Not acceptable, this insubordination is."

Anakin answered immediately.

"I care more about justice than hierarchy."

Obi-Wan stepped forward.

"Anakin..."

"I do."

He looked at both of them.

"If hierarchy protects wrongdoing, then hierarchy is wrong."

Obi-Wan frowned.

"You truly believe Master Windu committed wrongdoing?"

"I do."

"He believed he was protecting the Republic."

"And I believe he abandoned the principles we're supposed to uphold."

Yoda studied him for a long time.

Then he spoke quietly.

"So certain, you are."

"I am."

"So unwilling to accept the judgment of your Masters."

"If my Masters defend executing a disarmed combatant, then yes."

Yoda closed his eyes.

When he opened them again, sadness filled his expression.

"If so firmly against the Jedi in this matter you stand..."

He paused.

"...perhaps a Jedi no longer you should be."

Obi-Wan's head snapped toward Yoda.

"Master!"

Yoda remained silent.

Obi-Wan turned immediately to Anakin.

"Don't listen to that."

Anakin looked between them.

Obi-Wan stepped closer.

"Whatever has happened today..."

"We can work through it."

"We've disagreed before."

"The Order needs you."

"I need you."

"Stay."

For a moment, Anakin said nothing.

His face was unreadable.

Then he slowly shook his head.

"The Jedi Order has truly lost its way."

Obi-Wan's expression fell.

"It now defends executing a disarmed combatant."

He looked toward the Senate building behind them.

"And when I objected, I was told that hierarchy matters more than principle."

Yoda answered quietly.

"That is not what I said."

"It is what I heard."

Obi-Wan reached toward him.

"Anakin—"

"No."

Anakin took a slow breath.

"You offered me a choice, Master Yoda."

He met the Grand Master's eyes.

"I'll accept it."

Obi-Wan stared.

"What are you saying?"

"I resign."

The words hung in the air.

"I resign from the Jedi Order."

Neither Yoda nor Obi-Wan interrupted.

Anakin turned.

Without another word, he walked away into the endless crowds of Coruscant.

His lightsaber remained clipped to his belt.

Obi-Wan watched him disappear.

His hand twitched instinctively, as though he wished to call Anakin back.

But no words came.

Yoda stood beside him in silence.

Neither of them asked Anakin to surrender his lightsaber.

Neither attempted to stop him.

Together they watched until he vanished into the sea of people beneath the lights of the galactic capital.

# Part 3

The corridors of the Senate building slowly emptied into the endless evening of Coruscant.

Anakin Skywalker walked without destination for several minutes.

The words of Yoda echoed in his mind.

*Perhaps a Jedi no longer you should be.*

His own reply echoed just as loudly.

*I resign from the Jedi Order.*

He scarcely noticed the crowds as he crossed landing platforms and skybridges until he finally arrived outside Padmé Amidala's apartment.

She had returned only moments earlier.

The door slid open.

Padmé looked at him quietly.

Neither spoke for several seconds.

Finally Anakin stepped inside.

The door closed behind him.

"Why?"

Padmé said nothing.

"Why did you betray me?"

She remained calm.

"You stood before the entire Senate..."

He shook his head in disbelief.

"...and told them about Count Dooku."

"I did."

"I trusted you."

"I know."

"I told you that in confidence."

"I know."

"Then why?"

Padmé folded her arms.

"Because I had listened to you all day defend 'the Jedi way.'"

Anakin frowned.

"I was defending principle."

"Were you?"

"You watched Master Windu kill an unarmed man."

"And only days earlier you killed an unarmed Count Dooku."

Anakin looked away.

Padmé continued.

"You kept telling the Senate that Master Windu had violated the Jedi way."

"He did."

"And what did you do?"

Silence.

Padmé's voice remained steady.

"You did exactly the same thing."

"I was different."

"You keep saying that."

"It was."

"You executed a defeated prisoner."

Anakin exhaled sharply.

"I know."

"You call Master Windu wrong."

"I do."

"You call yourself right."

"I never said that."

Padmé looked directly into his eyes.

"No."

"You only acted as though you had the authority to lecture him before the entire Senate."

Anakin's shoulders tightened.

Padmé continued, more firmly now.

"Anakin, you are a hypocrite."

Anakin was stunned in surprise.

"You called me a hypocrite."

"Because you were being one."

He looked genuinely hurt.

Padmé noticed.

She did not soften.

"I was the only person who knew what happened to Count Dooku."

She spoke quietly.

"If I had stayed silent, nobody else could have raised it."

"I had to."

"You *had* to?"

"Yes."

"I believed the Senate deserved to hear it."

Anakin paced across the room.

"You broke my trust."

"I know."

"You humiliated me."

"I know."

"You could have spoken to me privately."

"I already knew what you believed."

She sighed.

"And I wasn't going to let you condemn another man for something you yourself had done."

Anakin opened his mouth to argue again.

Padmé simply raised one hand.

"I'm going to sleep."

He stopped.

"You can come with me."

She spoke without anger.

"Or you can stay out here."

She shrugged slightly.

"Your choice."

Without another word she turned and disappeared into the bedroom.

The apartment became quiet.

Anakin sat heavily on the couch.

The city lights shimmered through the windows.

He stared at nothing.

Minutes passed.

Perhaps longer.

Eventually soft metallic footsteps approached.

C-3PO stopped beside him.

"Oh... Master Anakin..."

Anakin did not respond.

The protocol droid clasped his hands together.

"I... I don't really understand everything that's happened today."

Anakin gave a tired laugh.

"Neither do I."

Threepio tilted his head.

"I only know that you don't seem very happy."

"I'm not."

"I wish you were."

Anakin looked up at him.

"I'm terribly sorry if that's obvious."

"It is."

Threepio shuffled awkwardly.

"It makes me rather sad to see you like this."

"I only wanted to cheer you up."

Anakin managed a faint smile.

"I appreciate that, Threepio."

"I really do."

"But..."

He looked back toward the city.

"...I think I need some time alone."

"Oh."

Threepio nodded.

"Of course."

"I completely understand."

He quietly retreated to the far side of the room.

The apartment fell silent once more.

Time drifted by.

Eventually Anakin looked up again.

Threepio noticed immediately.

"If I may make one small suggestion..."

Anakin nodded slightly.

"You should probably try to sleep."

Anakin considered the advice.

Finally he stood.

He walked slowly into the bedroom.

Padmé was already asleep.

Moonlight from Coruscant's skyline illuminated her face.

She looked peaceful.

Untouched by the arguments.

Untouched by the Senate.

Untouched by politics.

# Part 4

The days following the difficult meeting in Padmé Amidala's apartment were quiet.
