# Part 1

The upper levels of Galactic City were shrouded in the glow of evening traffic when four Jedi Masters stepped onto the landing platform outside the Supreme Chancellor's private offices.

Mace Windu led the group with calm determination. Behind him walked Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin. At the Jedi Temple, Anakin Skywalker had been ordered to remain behind.

"You are under arrest, Chancellor," Mace said as the doors opened.

Palpatine slowly rose from his chair.

"Are you threatening me, Master Jedi?"

"The Senate will decide your fate."

Palpatine's expression twisted into something inhuman.

"I am the Senate."

His crimson lightsaber ignited with a violent hiss.

The room exploded into motion.

Palpatine launched himself across the office with impossible speed. Agen Kolar barely had time to raise his blade before the Chancellor's lightsaber cut him down.

Saesee Tiin intercepted the second strike for only an instant before he, too, fell.

Kit Fisto met Palpatine head-on, exchanging several rapid blows before the Dark Lord broke through his defense and struck him dead.

Within moments, only Mace Windu remained.

Purple and red blades crashed together, illuminating the Chancellor's shattered office.

The duel carried them across polished floors, through broken windows, and onto the open ledge overlooking Coruscant's endless skyline.

Lightning burst from Palpatine's fingertips.

Mace absorbed the torrent with his lightsaber, slowly forcing it back.

Palpatine staggered.

The barrage ceased.

Mace advanced.

"You have failed."

The Chancellor collapsed onto the durasteel platform, exhausted and cornered.

Mace raised his lightsaber.

At the last possible moment, Palpatine reached toward the comlink attached beneath his robes.

His fingers found the activation switch.

He spoke only three words.

"Execute order sixty..."

The purple blade came down.

The lightsaber passed cleanly through Palpatine.

The Sith Lord fell lifeless onto the platform.

Silence settled over the Chancellor's office.

Far away, on worlds scattered across the Republic, clone troopers received a priority transmission.

The message consisted of only a single instruction.

"Execute Order 60."

On Mygeeto, a squad of clones paused in the middle of advancing.

One trooper blinked.

"Order 60?"

Another frowned.

"Do you remember Order 60?"

A third scratched the side of his helmet.

"Is this the order to kill all senators?"

"No," another clone answered confidently. "That's Order 68."

A moment later he hesitated.

"...I think."

Someone else shook his head.

"No, I think Order 60 is the one to kill all aliens."

"No, that's Order 70."

The entire squad looked toward their commander.

"Commander, do you know Order 60?"

The commander stood motionless for several seconds.

"I remember being trained about it once on Kamino."

"You do?"

"They didn't talk about this one a lot. We moved on to Order 61 almost right after. I remember Order 61 was much more important and emphasized in training."

Another clone nodded.

"Order 61 was the one to destroy all starships within your vicinity, right?"

"Yes," the commander replied. "That one. I remember training for it."

The clones exchanged uncertain looks.

One of them suddenly ran back toward a supply crate.

"Guys! Wait!"

He dug frantically through a stack of old military records.

"GUYS YOU WON'T BELIEVE IT!"

The others turned.

"What?"

"Order 60 is the one that immediately relieves us of military duty and permits us to live as private citizens!"

Several helmets tilted in disbelief.

"You can't be serious."

"You're making this up, right?"

"No."

The clone held up a worn military datapad.

"Look."

The squad crowded around as the screen illuminated.

It displayed an official Grand Army of the Republic directive.

"ORDER 60: Any clone trooper who receives this order is immediately relieved of his military duties, discharged from the Grand Army of the Republic, and made a civilian and citizen of the Galactic Republic with all associated rights and freedoms."

The commander stared at the screen.

"...This is authentic?"

A captain standing nearby answered immediately.

"Sir, my captain brought this datapad straight from Kamino. This is absolutely authentic."

No one spoke for several long moments.

Finally one clone quietly asked,

"So..."

"We're civilians now?"

The commander looked at the transmission one final time.

"It appears we are."

Across the galaxy, nearly identical conversations unfolded.

On Kashyyyk.

On Felucia.

On Saleucami.

On Cato Neimoidia.

On countless Republic battlefronts.

Confusion quickly became verification.

Verification became celebration.

By the end of the day, clone troopers everywhere had accepted the authenticity of Order 60.

Entire battalions abandoned trenches.

Star Destroyers suddenly found themselves without crews.

Gunships landed in open fields as their pilots walked away.

Armor was discarded by the thousands.

Some clones immediately boarded civilian transports.

Others wandered into nearby cities to discover ordinary life for the first time.

Many simply stood beneath open skies, unsure where to go next now that every decision belonged to them alone.

The Grand Army of the Republic dissolved into millions of newly freed citizens in the span of a single day.

Meanwhile, deep within the Klegger Corp Mining Facility on Mustafar, the Separatist Council gathered around their conference table.

The atmosphere was grim.

Nute Gunray looked around the room.

"So, we just received news that General Grievous has been killed by Obi-Wan Kenobi on Utapau."

Silence followed.

He folded his hands.

"I fear that this war may be coming to an end. Without him, I don't believe we can sustain our efforts for much longer."

Across the table, Poggle the Lesser answered in Geonosian.

The translation droid spoke in Basic.

"True. Now that both Dooku and Grievous are gone, I fear that the Republic may forcibly reabsorb the entirety of our Confederacy. Maybe we should flee and go into hiding before the Republic comes looking for us..."

Before anyone could answer, the conference room doors burst open.

Rune Haako rushed inside, breathing heavily.

"Sir! Sir!"

Nute turned.

"What is it?"

"We have just heard reports that all clone troopers of the Republic are no longer fighting!"

The room froze.

"They apparently all think they are off-duty now."

Every member of the Separatist Council stared at him.

"What?!"

Rune nodded frantically.

"The reports are coming from multiple systems. Republic formations are collapsing. The clone army is simply... leaving."

Nute Gunray slowly leaned back in his chair.

A smile spread across his face.

"Well..."

He glanced around the conference table.

"I suppose we might not need to surrender then."

Poggle spoke excitedly in Geonosian.

The translation droid immediately relayed his words.

"Maybe we can still win this war?"

Nute stood.

His earlier despair had vanished.

"I am in favour of continuing the war."

He looked at every member of the council.

"If the clones are no longer willing to fight, then we must seize our chance."

He raised a hand decisively.

"Long live the Confederacy of Independent Systems!"

Around the table, heads nodded in agreement as hope returned to the room.

# Part 2

Mace Windu returned to the Jedi Temple without delay.

The Temple felt unusually quiet.

He walked through its familiar corridors until he reached the Jedi Council Chamber. There, sitting alone and waiting in silence, was Anakin Skywalker.

Anakin stood as soon as Mace entered.

"Master Windu. How did it go? Where are the other Masters?"

Mace's expression remained solemn.

"Skywalker... you were right."

Anakin looked up.

"Palpatine really was the Sith Lord we have been looking for."

The words hung heavily in the chamber.

"I believe the Sith are now extinct."

For a brief moment, neither of them spoke.

Mace finally continued.

"However, the price..."

His voice became quieter.

"He was extraordinarily powerful. Unfortunately, Masters Fisto, Tiin, and Kolar did not survive."

Anakin lowered his head.

"That is... horrible."

He looked toward the empty Council seats surrounding them.

"What should we do now?"

Mace glanced around the chamber.

"Who else in the Jedi Council are here with us right now?"

"I believe we only have Masters Shaak Ti and Coleman Kcaj with us right now. Masters Obi-Wan Kenobi, Yoda, Stass Allie, Plo Koon, and Ki-Adi-Mundi are all offworld right now on their respective campaigns."

Mace reached for his comlink.

"Master Shaak Ti, Master Coleman Kcaj, can you quickly come to the Council Chamber right away? This is urgent. We need you here right away."

The reply came almost immediately.

"We're on our way."

Only moments later, the doors opened.

Shaak Ti and Coleman Kcaj entered together, immediately noticing the grave expressions on Mace and Anakin's faces.

"What has happened?" Shaak asked.

"Take your seats," Mace replied. "We'll explain everything."

Within minutes, an emergency meeting of the Jedi Council had begun.

Mace Windu, Shaak Ti, Coleman Kcaj, and Anakin Skywalker sat physically in the Council Chamber.

The remaining Masters appeared as blue holograms in their respective seats.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Yoda.

Plo Koon.

Ki-Adi-Mundi.

Stass Allie.

Three seats remained dark and empty.

The seats that had belonged to Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar.

Every member of the Council noticed them.

Mace stood before the gathering.

"Masters, thank you for responding so quickly."

He took a slow breath.

"This meeting concerns the death of Supreme Chancellor Palpatine."

The holograms immediately became attentive.

Mace recounted everything.

He explained how Anakin had informed him that Palpatine was a Sith Lord.

He described gathering Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin before confronting the Chancellor.

He detailed the battle inside the Chancellor's office.

"The Chancellor killed Masters Kolar, Tiin, and Fisto."

Silence filled the chamber.

"I eventually defeated him."

Mace paused.

"Just before I struck the final blow, he reached for a comlink."

"He spoke only three words."

"'Execute order sixty...'"

"I struck before he could say anything further."

"The Chancellor died immediately afterward."

Yoda slowly closed his eyes.

"Gone, our Masters are. A great loss, it is. But one with the Force, they have become."

No one answered.

Eventually Obi-Wan broke the silence.

"Order 60, you said?"

"Yes," Mace replied.

Obi-Wan nodded.

"Our clones have all abruptly abandoned their battle."

Several Masters looked toward his hologram.

"I managed to catch up to Commander Cody and asked him why."

"What did he say?" Shaak Ti asked.

"He said he received Order 60 and is thus now a civilian."

Obi-Wan gave a tired shake of his head.

"I thought it was ridiculous."

"But all the other clones were saying the same thing."

"So I took it that something strange has happened."

"I'm in hyperspace right now, heading to Coruscant."

Plo Koon spoke next.

"Mine too."

Ki-Adi-Mundi nodded.

"Same for me."

Stass Allie added quietly,

"Same."

All eyes turned toward Yoda.

"Deserted their duties, our clones have too."

He folded his hands.

"Kept fighting on, the Wookiees did, however."

Anakin frowned.

"But why would Palpatine execute Order 60 just before he died?"

Mace answered almost immediately.

"I think he intended to execute something else."

"He was clearly not finished speaking when I killed him."

Shaak Ti considered that.

"Do you have any clue what order he might have been trying to execute?"

Obi-Wan suddenly looked down.

"Hang on."

He reached beside his pilot's seat.

"I have a datapad that Commander Cody gave me that shows all 150 contingency orders."

He activated the device.

"Let me see what I can find."

Coleman Kcaj leaned forward.

"Tell us what all orders starting with the word 'sixty' say."

Obi-Wan searched the files.

"I've found them."

He began reading.

"Order 60 discharges all clone troopers from the military and makes them civilians."

"Order 61 asks them to destroy all starships in their vicinity."

"Order 62 asks them to all return to Kamino at once."

"Order 63 likewise, but return to Coruscant instead."

"Order 64 asks all clones to immediately arrest their clone commander or captain."

"Order 65 declares the Supreme Chancellor of the Republic an enemy."

Several Masters exchanged surprised glances.

Obi-Wan continued.

"Order 66 declares all Jedi as traitors and enemies and orders their immediate execution on sight."

No one interrupted.

"Order 67 asks all clones to dance and clean their armour."

Anakin blinked.

Obi-Wan looked just as puzzled before continuing.

"Order 68 declares all senators as enemies and orders their execution."

"Order 69 asks all clones to report their current location to Coruscant via comlink."

He lowered the datapad.

The Council sat in thoughtful silence.

Finally Mace spoke.

"I believe Palpatine was trying to execute Order 66."

Everyone looked toward him.

"That one makes the most sense."

"Seeing as we the Jedi have finally identified him as a Sith Lord, he probably wanted revenge on the Jedi and wanted all the clone troopers to kill us all."

Yoda nodded slowly.

"Agree with you, I do. Perfect sense, that makes."

Anakin let out a quiet breath.

"Well... thank the Force that Order 66 didn't go through, then."

Plo Koon spoke next.

"Order 60 is certainly much better than Order 66."

He folded his arms.

"But it is still a disaster."

"We have just lost our Grand Army of the Republic."

"We can't fight on for long when nearly our entire military force is the clone troopers."

Ki-Adi-Mundi agreed.

"We still have the Republic Navy."

"And we still have all of us, the Jedi."

"I think we can hold the lines for a bit."

"But we definitely can't push through millions and millions of battle droids without our clones."

Stass Allie leaned forward.

"We have to convince them to rejoin the military somehow."

She thought for a moment.

"Nothing about Order 60 seems to say that the clones are forbidden from rejoining the military."

"It seems to just say that they are now free to do what they want."

"If we can somehow convince them with incentives that re-enlisting is optimal, then maybe a lot of them will do it."

Yoda slowly shook his head.

"A task for the Senate, that is."

"A task for the Jedi, that is not."

Mace nodded.

"Agreed."

"Then we will go to the Senate and ask them to push through something to get the clones back into fighting."

Shaak Ti raised another concern.

"I think we first need to explain why their Chancellor is dead and why we had to kill him."

Mace closed his eyes for a brief moment.

"...Right."

He sighed.

"Well, that seems to be a pain."

He looked toward the holograms.

"We will need all of us."

"Masters Yoda, Kenobi, Allie, Koon, and Mundi, please return to Coruscant as early as possible."

Each of the holograms acknowledged the instruction.

"We're already on our way," Obi-Wan said.

The emergency meeting came to an end.

Across the galaxy, the Jedi Masters still commanding Republic campaigns immediately set course for Coruscant.

Inside the Council Chamber, the holograms faded one by one until only Mace Windu, Anakin Skywalker, Shaak Ti, and Coleman Kcaj remained.

None of them stood.

None of them spoke.

The empty seats of Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar seemed larger than before.

The four Masters sat together in exhausted silence, surrounded by uncertainty, mourning their fallen companions while trying to comprehend a Republic that had lost both its Supreme Chancellor and nearly its entire army within the span of a single day.

# Part 3

By the time Obi-Wan Kenobi, Yoda, Plo Koon, Ki-Adi-Mundi, and Stass Allie had all returned to Coruscant, the entire Jedi Council was once again assembled, save for the three seats left empty by the deaths of Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar.

Their first decision was unanimous.

Before addressing the Senate, they needed to gather whatever evidence remained inside the late Chancellor's office.

The group departed the Jedi Temple and returned to the Republic Executive Building.

The office still bore the scars of the duel.

Furniture lay overturned.

Walls had been scorched by lightsabers and Force lightning.

The shattered transparisteel windows remained open to the skyline of Coruscant.

Palpatine's body still lay where Mace Windu had struck him down.

Obi-Wan looked around the room.

"Are there security recordings of the incident?"

Mace walked toward a damaged control panel.

He inspected it briefly before shaking his head.

"No."

He looked around the office.

"Looks like Palpatine switched all cameras off prior to us coming."

He folded his arms.

"This guy is really well prepared."

After another moment, he added quietly,

"I'm impressed that he thought about this."

The others began searching the office for anything that might support their account of what had happened.

Anakin knelt beside Palpatine's body and carefully searched through the Chancellor's robes.

A moment later he smiled.

"Hey, look what I found!"

He withdrew a lightsaber.

The hilt was unfamiliar to everyone except the dead man lying before them.

Anakin activated it.

A crimson blade sprang to life.

"Aha!"

He looked around excitedly.

"This is great!"

Several of the Masters stared at the weapon.

Mace frowned.

"But I thought I kicked his lightsaber out of the shattered window during our duel."

He looked toward the broken window overlooking the city.

Anakin deactivated the weapon.

"This means he must have a second lightsaber."

Mace considered that.

"So then why didn't he use it during the duel?"

Anakin shrugged.

"That is indeed strange."

He clipped the weapon to his belt.

"Oh well."

Obi-Wan looked from the red lightsaber to the bodies lying on the floor.

"We should bring all the bodies to the Senate."

He gestured toward Palpatine.

"Not just Palpatine's."

Then toward the fallen Jedi.

"Our fallen Masters' too."

"All of this could be good evidence."

No one objected.

The Jedi carefully prepared the bodies of Palpatine, Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar for transport.

Anakin retained possession of the recovered red lightsaber.

Together, the Masters departed for the Senate District.

Outside the Senate Chamber, they waited quietly until the day's session formally convened.

As senators filled the enormous rotunda and the session was called to order, the Jedi entered together.

The arrival of the entire Jedi Council immediately drew the attention of every senator present.

Mace Windu stepped onto the central speaking platform.

His expression was solemn.

"My fellow senators."

The chamber gradually quieted.

"Supreme Chancellor Palpatine was secretly a Sith Lord."

Silence lasted only an instant.

"We finally discovered his true identity, and we eliminated him."

The Senate erupted.

"The Jedi betrayed the Republic!"

"How do you know he's a Sith Lord?"

"What evidence do you have?"

"Why should we believe you?"

"You have no right to kill our Supreme Chancellor!"

"What's a Sith Lord?"

"What does 'Sith' mean again?"

"Isn't 'Sith' some ancient religion that has been extinct for 1000 years?"

The chamber dissolved into overlapping arguments.

On the Jedi platform, the Masters exchanged uncertain glances.

Plo Koon leaned toward the others.

"The senators don't even know what 'Sith' means."

He looked around the chamber.

"How are we going to explain all this to them?"

Yoda rested both hands upon his cane.

"Patience, my friend."

His ears twitched slightly.

"A way, we will figure out together."

The Jedi allowed the Senate's shouting to settle before Mace stepped forward once more.

"My fellow senators."

His voice carried throughout the chamber.

"This is truly an extraordinary time."

The arguments gradually faded.

"I understand that many of you may not believe us, but we ask that you trust us."

He slowly looked around the Senate.

"We have fought loyally with you for the past three years."

"We have made countless sacrifices across numerous worlds across the galaxy in our struggle against the Separatists."

"And we will continue to be loyal to you."

"We will never betray you."

Many senators listened quietly now.

Mace continued.

"The Sith are an ancient organization of evil and power-seeking Force users who, unlike the Jedi, only have their own interests at heart and not that of the galaxy."

"They have successfully made the galaxy think that they went extinct about 1000 years ago."

"But we discovered that they had secretly survived when Darth Maul unexpectedly emerged 13 years ago."

He gestured toward Obi-Wan.

"After Master Kenobi defeated Maul, we knew that the ancient Sith Rule of Two meant there had to be another."

"So we continued hunting for the remaining Sith Lord."

"Over time, it became clear that this remaining Sith Lord took Count Dooku as his apprentice."

"After Skywalker defeated Dooku aboard the Invisible Hand, we realized that if we acted fast enough we could eliminate the Sith for good before the remaining Sith Lord took on yet another apprentice."

He paused.

"Unexpectedly, we found him hidden right at the heart of the Republic."

Mace's gaze swept across the Senate.

"Yes."

"It was Supreme Chancellor Palpatine all along."

The rotunda had become almost completely silent.

Mace extended a hand toward Anakin.

Anakin handed him the recovered weapon.

Mace ignited it.

The crimson blade extended with its unmistakable hiss.

"The signature weapon of a Sith Lord is a red lightsaber, made up of a kyber crystal that has been bled red through pure rage, hatred, fear, and pain."

He held the weapon high.

"And here we have Palpatine's own red lightsaber."

Many senators stared in astonishment.

"This was found on Palpatine's body after his defeat."

"We consider this definitive evidence that he was a Sith Lord, even if you may not accept our conclusion yet."

Mace deactivated the blade.

"We did not execute Palpatine."

"We tried to arrest him and bring him to the Senate for a trial."

"Instead, he pulled out a red lightsaber and almost instantly killed Masters Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar."

At Mace's signal, the Jedi uncovered the bodies of their fallen companions.

A murmur spread throughout the chamber.

Mace looked upon the three Masters before addressing the Senate once again.

"As you can see, this was not effortless."

"In the end, I had to kill Sidious in self-defense."

"If I didn't, I would have likely suffered the same fate as our fallen Masters."

He bowed his head briefly.

"Thus I ask that you understand that our actions were necessary."

The Jedi stepped back.

The Senate entered deliberation.

One senator rose.

"The Jedi have done nothing against the Republic since before I was born."

He looked toward the Jedi platform.

"They truly are an honourable organization."

"I choose to believe them."

Another senator nodded.

"They really don't seem like the kind of people to betray us."

"They don't seem to ask to take over power or become the new Chancellor either."

"They are simply here to tell us the truth."

A third added,

"If they betrayed us, why are they being so honest and cooperative with us right now?"

Another gestured toward the three fallen Masters.

"Three of their own fell."

"The evidence is before our eyes."

"If it was all a lie, why did the three Masters have to die?"

One senator remained cautious.

"I'm still skeptical."

"But what the Jedi says makes sense."

Another senator suddenly leaned back in disbelief.

"Wait."

"Dooku was a Sith Lord and Palpatine was a Sith Lord?"

He stared into the distance.

"So then this entire war is..."

His voice became almost a whisper.

"Oh stars..."

"This entire war was a lie."

The chamber fell quiet again before another senator spoke.

"We don't even have a Chancellor anymore."

He looked around the rotunda.

"How about we argue about this later and elect a new Chancellor first?"

Several voices immediately answered.

"Agreed."

"We need a Chancellor."

"We can deal with the Jedi later."

The proposal rapidly gained support.

Although debate over Palpatine's identity and the Jedi's actions continued in quieter conversations throughout the chamber, the Senate gradually settled upon a single practical conclusion.

The Republic required a new Supreme Chancellor before it could address anything else.

The question of Palpatine's secret life, the Jedi's actions, and the revelations surrounding the Sith would wait until after new leadership had been chosen.

# Part 4

The Senate's debate shifted from the fate of Palpatine to the immediate question of leadership.

With the office of Supreme Chancellor vacant during a galactic war, the senators agreed that the Republic could not afford delay.

The Acting Presiding Officer addressed the chamber.

"The office of Supreme Chancellor is vacant. Nominations are now open."

Names were proposed from across the Senate.

After the nominations concluded, voting began.

The process took time, but eventually the final tally appeared throughout the chamber.

Senator Padmé Amidala of Naboo had received the greatest number of votes.

The Presiding Officer stood.

"By the authority vested in this Senate, Senator Padmé Amidala is hereby elected Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic."

Applause echoed throughout the rotunda.

Padmé walked to the central podium.

She paused for a moment before addressing the Senate.

"I am honoured to have the confidence of the Senate to serve the Republic."

She looked across the countless floating platforms.

"I promise that I will resolve the Clone Wars and restore peace and stability to the galaxy."

The applause resumed.

When it subsided, Padmé turned toward the platform occupied by the Jedi.

"I recommend forming a committee to investigate the truth behind Chancellor Palpatine and the actions of the Jedi."

The proposal was quickly put to a vote.

The motion carried.

A formal investigative committee was established.

Padmé continued.

"In these turbulent times, I feel that it is necessary that, as the Chancellor, I receive sufficient protection from the Separatists and other threats."

She glanced toward Anakin.

"I would like to propose a Jedi bodyguard to be assigned to me for protection."

A few senators nodded in agreement.

"I recommend General Anakin Skywalker for this role, as he had been assigned to protect me three years ago and performed his duties with remarkable competency."

She met his eyes.

"General Skywalker, do you accept this assignment?"

Anakin stood.

There was the briefest hesitation before he answered.

"...Yes, Senator Amidala."

He caught himself.

"I mean... Chancellor Amidala."

Laughter spread gently through the Senate.

Even some of the Jedi smiled.

On another platform, Bail Organa leaned toward Mon Mothma.

"I suppose we have to get used to calling her Chancellor instead of Senator now."

Mon Mothma smiled.

"I imagine all of us will."

Once the laughter faded, Padmé addressed the Jedi again.

"Masters Jedi, do any of you have more to inform us?"

Mace Windu stepped forward.

"Yes."

He looked around the chamber.

"Just before I killed Palpatine, he activated his comlink and issued Order 60 to all clone troopers, which relieved them of their duties, discharged them from the military, and made them civilians."

The Senate murmured in surprise.

"After carefully reviewing the clones' contingency orders, we believe that Palpatine was trying to execute Order 66, which made all clone troopers treat all Jedi as enemies and traitors and ordered them to execute all Jedi on sight."

Several senators gasped.

"Because I killed him before that final word 'six' could be said out loud, he managed to accidentally trigger Order 60."

Mace's expression grew more serious.

"However, now all of our clone troopers are no longer fighting with us."

"The Grand Army of the Republic is effectively gone."

"Our fronts are collapsing."

"Separatist battle droids are overrunning Republic positions."

"We need to quickly find a way to rope the clones back into the army before it's too late."

Padmé nodded.

"Very well."

She looked toward several senior senators.

"A committee shall be arranged to work with you to discuss how to proceed from here."

Then she turned toward Anakin.

"General Skywalker, please follow me."

She struck the ceremonial gavel.

"This session is adjourned."

The Senate gradually emptied.

Anakin crossed the chamber toward the Chancellor's platform.

Meanwhile, Padmé appointed a committee of senators to coordinate directly with the Jedi Council regarding the Republic's worsening military situation.

Together, the committee and the assembled Jedi Masters—excluding Anakin, who remained with Chancellor Amidala—departed for the Jedi Temple to begin discussing possible responses to the collapse of the Republic's army.

Elsewhere, on Mustafar, the Separatist Council convened once again.

Nute Gunray stood at the head of the conference table.

"Lord Sidious has not responded to our calls for a long time."

He looked around uneasily.

"Why is he not excited that the clones are all no longer fighting?"

Passel Argente activated a holoprojector displaying recent Republic news transmissions.

"The Republic Senate has just broadcast that Chancellor Palpatine is dead."

The room grew quiet.

"Some senators have claimed that Palpatine is Lord Sidious himself."

Nute's eyes widened.

"Impossible."

He shook his head.

"He can't be."

"Lord Sidious serves with us, not the Republic."

Poggle the Lesser spoke rapidly in Geonosian.

His translation droid replied.

"This would at least explain why he is not answering our calls."

Po Nudo folded his arms.

"The Republic Senate seems to have produced decent evidence that Palpatine is Lord Sidious."

Nute looked down thoughtfully.

"If true..."

His voice became colder.

"...this means we have been duped by him all along."

Po Nudo nodded.

"I agree."

"I think we should be glad that he is dead."

"If true, then he would never have permitted Dooku and Grievous to die if he had our interests in mind."

Passel Argente frowned.

"Do you think he wanted us dead next?"

Nute slowly shook his head.

"We can't say."

"It doesn't matter now."

He straightened.

"We must take the Confederacy back into our own hands."

"We won't try to contact Lord Sidious anymore."

"We have only ourselves now."

Poggle spoke again.

The droid translated.

"We just won another 15 battles due to all the clones retreating for seemingly no reason."

He pointed toward a strategic display.

"If we keep this up, we could pressure Coruscant again directly."

Nute narrowed his eyes.

"You want another siege of Coruscant?"

Poggle answered immediately.

"If it achieves our goals, then why not?"

"If we can force the Republic to surrender, then they will give us whatever we want."

The translation droid emphasized the final words.

"ON OUR OWN TERMS!"

Nute nodded firmly.

"So be it."

He struck the conference table with his hand.

"Mobilize all battle droids."

Back on Coruscant, Padmé and Anakin entered the Chancellor's private apartment.

The door slid shut behind them.

The moment they were alone, Padmé turned toward him.

"Ani."

"We will not impede negotiations."

With the Council no longer directing the Confederacy's war policy, discussions between the Republic and the civilian government on Raxus Secundus progressed rapidly.

Negotiators from both governments met repeatedly.

This time, agreements were reached.

Compromises were accepted.

Months of warfare finally gave way to diplomacy.

Eventually, representatives of both governments gathered for the signing ceremony.

Padmé addressed those assembled.

"Today, we end a war that has lasted far too long."

Across the table, representatives of the Confederacy nodded.

The treaty was signed.

By its terms, the Galactic Republic formally acknowledged and recognized the Confederacy of Independent Systems as an independent, sovereign, legitimate entity with legal jurisdiction over the worlds that it controlled at the moment the treaty was signed.

After four years of conflict, the Clone Wars were finally over.

Padmé fulfilled every promise the Republic had made.

The captured members of the Separatist Council received the lenient prison sentences that had been offered in exchange for their cooperation.

Likewise, the Republic honoured its commitment to the clone troopers.

Following the end of the war, they were formally discharged from military service once more.

Each received the promised lifetime pension.

Housing assistance programs were established throughout the Republic to help the former soldiers begin civilian lives.

The peace treaty also required substantial reductions in the Confederacy's military production.

Battle droid foundries sharply reduced their output.

Many factories that had once produced armies instead transitioned to manufacturing civilian droids for commerce, industry, and everyday life.

The galaxy entered a new political era.

Two great governments now stood side by side.

The Galactic Republic.

The Confederacy of Independent Systems.

Relations remained cautious.

The peace was fragile.

Yet after four years of relentless warfare, the first three secretly manipulated by Darth Sidious and the final year fought as a genuine conflict between two independent governments, few on either side wished to see another war begin.

For the first time in many years, there was reason to hope that peace might endure.

Life gradually settled into a quieter rhythm.

Within the Chancellor's apartment on Coruscant, Anakin Skywalker devoted himself to raising Luke and Leia.

Days that had once been spent commanding armies were now spent teaching his children their first words and watching them take their first steps.

Padmé remained occupied with the immense responsibilities of serving as Supreme Chancellor.

Her leadership during both war and peace earned widespread respect throughout the Republic.

Many senators who had initially doubted her found themselves impressed by her ability to guide the Republic through both crisis and reconstruction.

With the war concluded, the Jedi Order withdrew from its military role.

The Temple once again focused upon diplomacy, mediation, scholarship, and the guidance of future generations of Jedi.

In time, the four vacancies on the Jedi Council created by the deaths of Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, Saesee Tiin, and the resignation of Anakin Skywalker were filled by new Masters.

Ahsoka Tano declined every invitation to return to the Jedi Order.

Instead, she continued following her own path.

She traveled throughout the galaxy, helping those in need wherever she found them while remaining independent of both the Republic and the Jedi.

Captain Rex fulfilled his promise to himself as well.

Having served through the entirety of the Clone Wars, he accepted his final discharge from military service.

This time, he never reenlisted.

He settled permanently on Coruscant and embraced the civilian life that Order 60 had briefly introduced him to months earlier.

The galaxy that emerged from the Clone Wars looked very different from the one that had entered them.

The Sith had been destroyed.

The Republic had survived.

The Confederacy had secured its independence through negotiation rather than conquest.

Millions of clone troopers had gained the freedom to determine their own futures.

And in one apartment overlooking the endless skyline of Coruscant, two young children named Luke and Leia grew up with both of their parents beside them, while the galaxy around them cautiously embraced an uncertain but hopeful peace.
