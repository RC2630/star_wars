# Part 1

The Clone Wars were ending.

Across the galaxy, Separatist strongholds were collapsing one after another. The Republic's armies advanced without resistance. On Coruscant, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine had revealed himself as the Sith Lord Darth Sidious and had reshaped the Republic into the first Galactic Empire. Order 66 had been issued. The Jedi Order was in ruins.

On Mustafar, the final loose ends were being tied off.

Anakin Skywalker stood alone in a dark conference room overlooking rivers of lava. The leaders of the Separatist Alliance lay dead behind him. The war was over.

For years, Anakin had fought to protect the Republic. For years, he had feared one thing above all others: losing Padmé Amidala.

Now he believed he finally possessed the power to prevent that loss.

A warning tone sounded.

A ship was approaching.

Anakin turned toward the viewport.

A sleek Naboo skiff descended through the volcanic haze toward the landing platform outside.

Padmé.

A smile spread across his face.

He hurried from the conference room.

—

The skiff touched down beside Anakin's green starfighter.

The boarding ramp lowered.

Padmé emerged and immediately ran toward him.

"Padmé!"

They embraced tightly.

"Oh, Anakin!"

"It's all right," he said softly. "You're safe now. What are you doing out here?"

She looked up at him, worry written across her face.

"I was so worried about you. Obi-Wan told me terrible things."

Anakin's expression hardened.

"What things?"

"He said you have turned to the dark side... that you killed younglings."

"Obi-Wan is trying to turn you against me."

"He cares about us."

Anakin frowned.

"Us?"

"He knows. He wants to help you."

"Is Obi-Wan going to protect you?" Anakin asked bitterly. "He can't help you. He's not strong enough."

"Anakin, all I want is your love."

"Love won't save you, Padmé. Only my new powers can do that."

"At what cost?" she pleaded. "You are a good person. Don't do this."

His voice rose.

"I won't lose you the way I lost my mother. I've become more powerful than any Jedi has ever dreamed of, and I've done it for you. To protect you."

Padmé stepped closer.

"Come away with me. Help me raise our child. Leave everything else behind while we still can."

For a brief moment Anakin hesitated.

Then he shook his head.

"Don't you see? We don't have to run away anymore. I have brought peace to the Republic. I am more powerful than the Chancellor. I can overthrow him, and together you and I can rule the galaxy. Make things the way we want them to be."

Padmé stared at him.

She had expected many things.

Not this.

She opened her mouth to answer—

—and movement caught her eye.

Her heart dropped.

Obi-Wan Kenobi stood in the doorway of the skiff.

"No!" she exclaimed.

Anakin turned sharply.

His expression darkened.

Padmé immediately stepped between them.

"Obi-Wan, why are you here?"

Obi-Wan looked from Padmé to Anakin.

His face was heavy with exhaustion.

"I needed to see for myself what Anakin has become."

"No," Padmé said firmly. "Go back to the skiff and stay there. I will handle him."

Obi-Wan's eyes widened.

"Padmé—"

"Trust me."

"He has fallen too deep."

"Just go."

For several seconds neither moved.

Obi-Wan studied Anakin carefully.

Anakin met the stare without flinching.

The tension was unbearable.

Finally Obi-Wan nodded once.

Reluctantly.

He turned and walked back up the boarding ramp.

The door closed behind him.

Padmé let out a slow breath.

Anakin's eyes remained fixed on the skiff.

Only when he was sure Obi-Wan had withdrawn did he look back at her.

Silence hung between them.

The distant roar of lava echoed across the platform.

At last Padmé spoke.

"Listen, Anakin."

His expression softened slightly.

"I don't think the two of us should rule the galaxy."

Anakin's face tightened.

"But," she continued carefully, "I'm willing to give it a try."

The tension vanished instantly.

A smile broke across Anakin's face.

Relief.

Joy.

Validation.

"There’s my angel."

He took both of her hands.

"I knew you would understand."

Padmé forced herself to smile back.

She wasn't certain she understood anything anymore.

But she knew one thing.

If she lost her influence over him now, she might lose him forever.

Anakin glanced toward the horizon.

"Very well then."

His confidence returned.

"Let's wait for Palpatine."

—

Time passed.

The volcanic winds howled around the platform.

Far above Mustafar's surface, another vessel emerged from hyperspace.

A dark shuttle descended toward the mining complex.

Anakin watched its approach with satisfaction.

Padmé watched with concern.

The shuttle landed.

Its ramp lowered.

Palpatine emerged.

No longer merely the Chancellor.

No longer hiding.

The Dark Lord of the Sith walked down the ramp with his hands folded behind his back.

His yellow eyes immediately found Anakin.

"My apprentice."

Anakin stepped forward.

"Master. I have killed all the Separatist leaders as you instructed."

"Very good."

Palpatine smiled.

"The Clone Wars are finally over."

He looked out across the volcanic landscape.

"At last. We have won."

The old man's voice dripped with triumph.

"The Republic has become the Empire. The Jedi have been destroyed. The galaxy belongs to us."

His eyes returned to Anakin.

"Come. We shall return to Coruscant."

Anakin nodded.

Then he reached for Padmé's hand.

"Padmé, let's go."

Palpatine's smile vanished.

His gaze shifted to Padmé.

For a long moment he said nothing.

Then:

"Oh, no."

His voice became cold.

"She's not coming with us."

The atmosphere changed instantly.

Padmé felt Anakin stiffen beside her.

Anakin's grip on her hand tightened.

"If she can't come with me," Anakin said calmly, "I won't go with you."

Palpatine blinked.

For perhaps the first time in many years, someone had answered him with a refusal.

"You what?"

"If she can't come with me, I won't go."

The Sith Lord's expression darkened.

"You do not dictate terms to your master."

"I am not a powerless pawn."

The words came evenly.

Deliberately.

"I have my bottom lines."

Palpatine stepped closer.

"Be very careful, Lord Vader."

Anakin did not move.

"Do you want me standing beside your Empire or not?"

Palpatine's eyes narrowed.

"Because I am strong enough to defy you at any point."

The volcanic winds seemed to grow louder.

Padmé remained silent.

She could feel the hostility building between them.

Master and apprentice.

Both dangerous.

Both proud.

Palpatine's face twisted with fury.

"How dare you."

Anakin held his ground.

"I mean it."

The two stared at one another.

Neither willing to blink.

Neither willing to retreat.

For several long seconds, it seemed as though the war might begin anew right there on the landing platform.

Then Palpatine calculated.

The Jedi were nearly gone.

The Empire had been declared.

The Senate had accepted his rule.

Everything was proceeding exactly as planned.

There would be time later to deal with Padmé Amidala.

For now, there was no benefit in provoking a confrontation with Anakin Skywalker on a volcanic world hundreds of light-years from Coruscant.

The old man slowly exhaled.

"...Fine."

The word sounded like poison.

Padmé remained expressionless.

Anakin merely nodded.

The matter, as far as he was concerned, was settled.

Without another word, he led Padmé toward the shuttle.

Palpatine followed.

The three disappeared inside.

Moments later the ramp rose.

The shuttle lifted from the platform and climbed into the dark skies of Mustafar.

—

Across the landing area, the hatch of the Naboo skiff opened once more.

Obi-Wan stepped outside.

He watched the shuttle vanish into the clouds.

His stomach sank.

Whatever chance remained of reaching Anakin had just become far more complicated.

Behind him, C-3PO emerged nervously.

"Oh dear," the protocol droid muttered. "That doesn't look good at all."

R2-D2 let out a worried whistle.

Obi-Wan remained silent.

Far above, the shuttle carrying Darth Sidious, Anakin Skywalker, and Padmé Amidala disappeared into hyperspace.

The fate of the galaxy had become even more uncertain.

# Part 2

Two days after returning to Coruscant, the galaxy paused for a moment that none of its citizens would ever know had occurred.

Far above the sprawling city-world, fleets moved into new positions. Imperial decrees were drafted. Surviving Jedi hid in remote systems. Senators whispered anxiously about the future.

Inside a secure medical facility, none of that mattered.

Padmé Amidala was in labor.

Anakin Skywalker refused to leave her side.

For hours he paced the room, sat beside her, stood again, then paced once more.

Every few minutes he found himself staring at the medical monitors.

Every few minutes the same thought returned.

*Please survive.*

He had spent months haunted by visions of her death.

Months convincing himself that only power could save her.

Months sacrificing everything to prevent this exact moment.

Now there was nothing left to do except wait.

And hope.

The medical staff worked calmly.

Padmé gripped Anakin's hand hard enough to hurt him.

He welcomed the pain.

It meant she was still alive.

Eventually one of the droids looked up from its instruments.

"There appears to be a second infant."

Anakin blinked.

"A second what?"

The droid repeated itself.

Padmé stared.

Anakin stared.

Neither of them had expected that.

Hours later, two healthy children entered the world.

A boy.

And a girl.

Padmé held them both while Anakin sat beside her in stunned silence.

The boy was named Luke.

The girl was named Leia.

Most importantly of all, Padmé remained alive.

The medical droids completed their examinations.

The reports were excellent.

Mother healthy.

Children healthy.

No complications.

No signs of the death Anakin had feared for so long.

The moment the final report was delivered, a tension seemed to leave his body all at once.

Padmé noticed.

For the first time in months, he looked genuinely relieved.

Not victorious.

Not powerful.

Simply relieved.

That evening she found him sitting quietly beside the nursery.

Watching.

Luke slept peacefully.

Leia was awake, staring upward.

Anakin sat there for nearly an hour without speaking.

Padmé approached.

"You can stop worrying now."

He looked at her.

A faint smile appeared.

"I don't think I can."

She laughed softly.

For a few moments, the galaxy's problems seemed very far away.

—

The peace lasted only a few days.

One afternoon, Anakin and Padmé were meeting privately in their residence when a summons arrived from Emperor Palpatine.

The old man appeared via hologram almost immediately.

His scarred face materialized above the projector.

"My apprentice."

Anakin nodded.

"Master."

Palpatine wasted no time.

"I have a task for you."

Anakin waited.

"The Kaminoans have outlived their usefulness."

Padmé immediately frowned.

Palpatine continued.

"The Clone Wars are over. Their facilities are no longer required."

An image of Kamino appeared beside the hologram.

Storm-covered oceans.

Towering platforms.

Tipoca City.

"I want you to proceed to Kamino and eliminate the city."

Anakin stared at him.

Palpatine's voice remained cold.

"Orbital bombardment."

Padmé looked up sharply.

"What?"

The Emperor ignored her.

"Reduce it to rubble."

Padmé stood.

"No."

Palpatine slowly turned toward her.

"No?" he repeated.

"Destroying an entire city is never justified."

The room became very still.

Padmé stepped closer to the hologram.

"There are civilians there. Workers. Families. Children."

"The city has served its purpose."

"It doesn't matter."

Her voice remained steady.

"You cannot simply erase people because they are inconvenient."

Palpatine's expression darkened.

"I did not ask for your opinion."

"You should have."

The Emperor's eyes narrowed.

"You dare."

"I absolutely dare."

The words left her mouth before she could reconsider them.

For several seconds nobody spoke.

Then Palpatine made his decision.

"Very well."

His voice was suddenly ice.

"Senator Amidala will be arrested immediately."

Anakin froze.

Palpatine's hologram flickered.

Then the transmission ended.

For several seconds neither Anakin nor Padmé moved.

"What is he doing?" Padmé asked quietly.

Anakin already knew the answer.

The Emperor would not trust a matter like this to subordinates.

Not now.

Not when Anakin had become so powerful.

A few minutes later alarms sounded throughout the residence.

The sound echoed through the corridors.

Anakin looked toward the entrance.

Imperial security personnel were arriving.

Not to arrest Padmé.

To secure the area.

The realization settled over him immediately.

Palpatine was coming himself.

Outside, a shuttle descended toward one of the nearby landing platforms.

Moments later the main doors of the residence opened.

Imperial guards entered first.

They formed two lines along the corridor.

Then Emperor Palpatine stepped through the doorway.

His yellow eyes immediately found Padmé.

Then Anakin.

The room seemed to darken around him.

Palpatine stopped several meters away.

No holograms.

No intermediaries.

No Senate.

No advisors.

Only master and apprentice.

Only power.

"I will personally take Senator Amidala into Imperial custody pending investigation for insubordination against the Empire." the Emperor said, his voice echoing through the room.

Padmé stared at Palpatine.

Then she looked at Anakin.

Anakin's face had changed.

The relief and happiness of the previous days vanished.

Something darker appeared in its place.

Dangerous.

Focused.

Palpatine noticed it too.

"My apprentice—"

"No."

The word came quietly.

Palpatine blinked.

"What?"

"No."

Anakin stood.

"You will not touch her."

The Emperor's eyes flashed yellow.

"You forget your place."

Anakin took a step forward.

"I know exactly where my place is."

The room seemed to vibrate with tension.

Palpatine's voice grew harsher.

"You belong to me."

Anakin laughed once.

It was not a pleasant sound.

"No."

The old Sith Lord took a step toward his apprentice.

"Careful."

Anakin's hand drifted toward his lightsaber.

"You just threatened my wife."

The realization struck both men simultaneously.

The confrontation that had been inevitable for years had finally arrived.

Palpatine straightened.

"You would challenge me?"

Anakin ignited his lightsaber.

The blue blade filled the room with light.

"Yes."

For the first time in many years, genuine surprise appeared on Palpatine's face.

Then it vanished.

A crimson blade exploded from his own lightsaber.

Padmé instinctively backed away.

The duel began.

—

The two Sith crashed into one another like storms.

Blue and red collided.

Furniture shattered.

Windows exploded outward.

The room descended into chaos.

Palpatine attacked with terrifying speed.

His blade moved in vicious arcs.

Anakin met every strike.

Neither held back.

The Emperor's fury grew with every passing minute.

"You ungrateful fool!"

Their lightsabers locked.

"I gave you everything!"

Anakin pushed him backward.

"You gave me chains!"

Palpatine attacked again.

"You were nothing before me!"

"I was never yours!"

The duel moved through the residence.

Walls cracked.

Tables overturned.

Security personnel wisely stayed away.

No ordinary being could intervene.

Again and again they clashed.

Master and apprentice.

Sith against Sith.

The Rule of Two reaching its inevitable conclusion.

At one point Palpatine unleashed a torrent of Force lightning.

Anakin absorbed the attack through his blade and advanced directly through it.

The effort nearly broke him.

Still he continued forward.

Palpatine's eyes widened.

Impossible.

The young man was growing stronger.

Not weaker.

The duel continued.

Minutes stretched into eternity.

Eventually they reached one of the grand reception halls.

Both combatants were breathing heavily.

Palpatine glared at his apprentice.

"It is far too soon."

Anakin said nothing.

"You are not ready."

"I disagree."

"You lack the power to overthrow your master."

Anakin raised his lightsaber.

"Then prove it."

They charged.

One final exchange.

A blur of movement.

A shower of sparks.

Then silence.

Something spun through the air.

Palpatine's lightsaber.

It struck the floor several meters away.

The Emperor stared at his empty hand.

For the first time in decades, fear appeared in his eyes.

Anakin stood before him.

Weapon ready.

Breathing hard.

Victorious.

Palpatine slowly backed away.

"Anakin."

No response.

"You need me."

Anakin remembered every lie.

Every manipulation.

Every death.

Every promise.

Every sacrifice.

"No."

The blue blade rose.

Palpatine finally understood.

There would be no mercy.

No hesitation.

No second chance.

The apprentice had become strong enough.

The Rule of Two had been fulfilled.

The blade came down.

And Darth Sidious died.

—

Silence filled the ruined hall.

The most powerful Sith Lord in a thousand years lay motionless on the floor.

His Empire.

His plans.

His victories.

All ended.

Anakin stared at the body.

For a long moment he said nothing.

Then he extinguished his lightsaber.

Slowly he turned.

Padmé stood nearby.

Watching.

Waiting.

Anakin looked down at his hands.

Then toward the city beyond the shattered windows.

Coruscant stretched endlessly in every direction.

The center of the galaxy.

The center of power.

And now his.

At last he spoke.

"Darth Sidious is dead."

No one answered.

Anakin continued.

"I am now the Dark Lord of the Sith."

The words echoed through the damaged chamber.

"The sole Sith Master."

He looked out toward the skyline.

"And as his successor, I am Emperor of the Galactic Empire."

Padmé remained silent.

The body of Palpatine lay between them.

The future of the galaxy hung in the balance.

And neither of them yet knew what would come next.

# Part 3

The death of Darth Sidious sent shockwaves through the Empire.

Official announcements described the event carefully. The Emperor had fallen. His successor, Anakin Skywalker, had assumed the throne. The transition had been swift enough that no major power blocs had time to challenge it.

For the first time in years, however, the center of political power looked very different.

Palpatine had ruled largely alone.

Anakin did not.

Padmé Amidala remained constantly at his side.

Many observers found this reassuring.

Others found it deeply concerning.

Neither side yet understood how much influence she truly possessed.

—

Several days after assuming the throne, Anakin and Padmé were reviewing reports together in one of the Imperial residence's private chambers.

Most concerned reconstruction efforts following the Clone Wars.

One report did not.

Anakin tossed it onto the table.

"Tatooine."

Padmé glanced at it.

The file concerned Tusken Raider activity.

"What about it?" she asked.

Anakin's expression hardened.

"I've made a decision."

Padmé immediately became wary.

"What decision?"

"I want them eliminated."

She stared at him.

"The Tusken Raiders."

The room fell silent.

"Anakin..."

"They killed my mother."

His voice was calm, but only barely.

"I will never forgive them for that."

Padmé slowly set down the report she was reading.

"Anakin, you already massacred an entire Tusken settlement years ago."

"I know."

"Then let it go."

"No."

The answer came immediately.

"They murdered her."

"They didn't murder her yesterday."

"That changes nothing."

Padmé folded her arms.

"You are talking about exterminating an entire people."

"I'm talking about eliminating a threat."

"You are talking about revenge."

Anakin's jaw tightened.

"Call it whatever you want."

"No."

The single word echoed through the room.

Anakin looked at her.

"No?"

"No."

For several moments they simply stared at one another.

Then Anakin stood.

"I am the Emperor."

"And?"

"And I have made my decision."

Padmé rose as well.

"I veto it."

Anakin blinked.

"You what?"

"I veto it."

"You don't have veto powers."

Padmé's expression became almost mischievous.

"Really?"

Anakin suddenly had a bad feeling.

Padmé continued.

"Do you remember what you told me on Mustafar?"

His face immediately soured.

"Padmé..."

"'Together you and I can rule the galaxy.'"

Anakin sighed.

"'Make things the way we want them to be.'"

"Padmé..."

"You said together."

She pointed directly at him.

"Not 'I will rule the galaxy.'"

Anakin rubbed his forehead.

"Not 'I will make all the decisions.'"

He already knew where this was going.

"'Together you and I can rule the galaxy.'"

Padmé smiled slightly.

"Your words. Not mine."

Anakin stared at the ceiling.

For perhaps the first time since becoming Emperor, he wished he had been less dramatic.

Eventually he groaned.

"Fine."

Padmé smiled.

"I knew you'd see reason."

"No, I see my own words being used against me, which is very different."

"It isn't."

"It is."

"It absolutely isn't."

—

The argument did not end there.

Padmé, being Padmé, decided to formalize the matter.

If they truly intended to govern together, then the arrangement needed rules.

Several days later she presented Anakin with a proposal.

He read it twice.

Then a third time.

Then he stared at her.

"You are serious."

"Completely."

The document established a simple principle.

Neither of them could take major governmental action unilaterally.

Both had to approve.

If either objected, the action would not proceed.

Anakin looked horrified.

"This is going to make everything take twice as long."

"Probably."

"This is a terrible system."

"Then you shouldn't have offered to rule the galaxy together."

Anakin muttered something under his breath.

"What was that?"

"Nothing."

"It didn't sound like nothing."

"It was definitely nothing."

Padmé smiled.

Several days later the agreement became official.

Reluctantly.

Very reluctantly.

Anakin signed it.

—

The arrangement immediately began reshaping Imperial policy.

Again and again Anakin proposed actions.

Again and again Padmé intervened.

Sometimes she agreed.

Sometimes she modified proposals.

Occasionally she vetoed them outright.

The most dramatic disputes became infamous among their closest advisors.

The Tusken proposal disappeared permanently.

A later suggestion involving the destruction of several Hutt criminal territories was blocked.

An idea concerning collective punishment against Geonosian populations lasted less than ten minutes before Padmé rejected it.

Numerous other plans met similar fates.

Each time the discussions followed a familiar pattern.

Anakin proposed.

Padmé objected.

Anakin argued.

Padmé argued back.

Eventually either a compromise emerged or the proposal died.

Months passed.

The Empire remained stable.

To Anakin's growing frustration, many of Padmé's objections turned out to be politically useful.

Worlds that might have resisted instead cooperated.

Trade recovered faster than expected.

Reconstruction projects succeeded.

Even some Senators who distrusted the Empire found themselves cautiously supportive of specific reforms.

None of this improved Anakin's mood.

Because every success strengthened Padmé's position.

One evening, after yet another lengthy policy debate, Anakin finally snapped.

They were alone.

The latest argument had concerned another military operation Padmé considered excessive.

Anakin dropped a stack of reports onto the table.

"You know what?"

Padmé looked up.

"What?"

"If you're just going to block everything I try to do, then I'm not going to try anymore."

She raised an eyebrow.

Anakin threw up his hands.

"You take the throne."

Padmé blinked.

"We'll see how you fare."

The room became quiet.

Anakin expected an argument.

Instead Padmé considered the idea seriously.

To his surprise, she nodded.

"That's not a bad idea."

Now it was Anakin's turn to blink.

"What?"

"If that's how you feel, we should make it official."

"You can't possibly be serious."

"I am."

Anakin stared at her.

Padmé stared back.

Several seconds passed.

Then, slowly, a smile spread across her face.

Anakin immediately realized she was not joking.

At all.

—

The proposal shocked the Imperial government.

The Emperor himself intended to transfer authority.

Many assumed it was impossible.

Others assumed it was a trick.

Neither assumption proved correct.

Anakin personally ordered preparations for a formal ceremony.

The event was held on Coruscant before representatives from across the Empire.

Military leaders attended.

Governors attended.

Senators attended.

Countless citizens watched through public broadcasts.

Anakin stood before the assembled crowd in ceremonial robes.

Padmé stood beside him.

The hall was silent.

At last Anakin stepped forward.

His voice echoed throughout the chamber.

"When I assumed leadership of the Empire, I promised a new era."

The audience listened carefully.

"In recent months I have come to recognize that governance requires wisdom as well as strength."

His eyes briefly shifted toward Padmé.

"Therefore, by my own authority, I transfer the office of Emperor to Padmé Amidala."

Gasps spread throughout the chamber.

Some Senators nearly fell out of their seats.

Others looked delighted.

Others looked terrified.

Anakin ignored them all.

The legal documents were brought forward.

The transfer was executed publicly.

Officially.

Irreversibly.

By the end of the ceremony, Padmé Amidala had become the new ruler of the Galactic Empire.

And Anakin Skywalker no longer held the throne.

# Part 4

The transfer of power had produced an unexpected result.

Anakin Skywalker was happier than he had been in years.

The endless meetings were gone.

The legislative debates were gone.

The diplomatic receptions were gone.

No more discussions about budgets, taxation, jurisdictional disputes, or trade regulations.

Instead, whenever a problem emerged somewhere in the galaxy, Padmé would simply hand it to him.

At first the requests were small.

A dispute between planetary governments on the edge of the Mid Rim.

Pirates harassing shipping routes.

A colony that had lost contact with the wider galaxy.

Anakin would depart aboard his starship, investigate personally, solve the problem, and return.

Then the missions grew larger.

Criminal syndicates.

Hostage situations.

Separatist holdouts.

Corrupt governors abusing their authority.

Smuggling networks operating across entire sectors.

Padmé quickly discovered something important.

Anakin needed purpose.

Not power.

Purpose.

As long as he had meaningful challenges to solve and people to protect, he remained focused, energetic, and surprisingly content.

Whenever he completed one assignment, another would already be waiting.

Anakin loved it.

He could act.

He could help people directly.

He could see immediate results.

Most importantly, he no longer had to spend every day arguing over policy.

—

While Anakin traveled across the galaxy, Padmé quietly reshaped it.

Mon Mothma became one of her closest advisors.

Bail Organa became another.

Together they began the difficult task of reforming the Empire from within.

The process took years.

Imperial hardliners gradually lost influence.

Some retired.

Some were voted out of office.

Others simply found themselves increasingly isolated as reformists gained support throughout the Senate.

Padmé moved carefully.

Too quickly, and resistance would unite against her.

Too slowly, and the opportunity might disappear.

One reform followed another.

Emergency powers were reduced.

Then reduced again.

The authority of the Emperor was restricted.

More power was transferred to the Senate.

Oversight committees were established.

Independent courts were strengthened.

New protections for planetary governments were enacted.

Entire sections of Imperial law were rewritten.

Many of the flaws that had crippled the old Republic were addressed as well.

Corporate influence over legislation was curtailed.

Procedural bottlenecks were streamlined.

Anti-corruption measures were expanded.

Transparency requirements increased.

The Senate became more functional than it had been in decades.

Many citizens barely noticed how dramatic the changes truly were.

The symbols of the Empire remained.

The uniforms remained.

The banners remained.

The name remained.

But year after year, the structure beneath them changed.

Slowly.

Patiently.

Deliberately.

—

Meanwhile, Anakin remained almost completely unaware.

Whenever he returned from one mission, another crisis awaited his attention.

He spent months at a time away from Coruscant.

Sometimes longer.

When Padmé discussed legislation, he often nodded absently while reviewing mission reports.

When she mentioned constitutional reforms, he usually responded with some variation of:

"That sounds fine."

Then he would leave again.

Years passed.

Luke and Leia grew from infants into energetic toddlers.

The galaxy stabilized.

The Clone Wars faded into history.

For the first time in living memory, genuine peace began to emerge.

—

Eventually, Anakin noticed something.

One day he was reading through a government briefing and frowned.

"Wait."

Padmé looked up.

"What?"

Anakin flipped through several pages.

"Why does the Senate have authority over this?"

Padmé tried very hard not to smile.

"Because the law says so."

Anakin narrowed his eyes.

"What law?"

"The one passed three years ago."

Anakin kept reading.

His expression grew increasingly suspicious.

Then increasingly confused.

Then mildly horrified.

He spent the next several hours reviewing legislation.

By the end of it, he understood exactly what had happened.

The Emperor's authority had been reduced repeatedly.

The Senate possessed more influence than ever.

Independent institutions had been strengthened.

Checks and balances existed everywhere.

The government was barely recognizable compared to the one he had inherited.

Anakin slowly lowered the final document.

Padmé waited.

At last he looked up.

"You dismantled the Empire."

"Mostly."

"You dismantled it while I wasn't looking."

"You were busy."

Anakin stared at her.

Padmé smiled innocently.

Anakin considered arguing.

Then he thought about all the missions he had completed.

All the people he had helped.

All the years that had passed.

And, surprisingly, he discovered he did not particularly care.

He laughed.

Padmé blinked.

"What?"

"I can't even be angry."

"Good."

"You planned this from the beginning."

Padmé wisely chose not to answer.

—

Several years later, she decided the process was complete.

A special session of the Senate was convened.

Representatives from across the galaxy gathered.

The chamber was full.

The occasion would be historic.

Padmé addressed the assembly from the central podium.

"The Empire was created during a time of fear."

The Senate listened.

"It was created during war."

She paused.

"The war is over."

Silence filled the chamber.

"The emergency has ended."

Many senators already suspected where the speech was leading.

Padmé continued.

"The institutions of democracy have been restored."

She looked around the room.

"The time has come to complete the transition."

The announcement followed.

The Galactic Empire would be dissolved.

The Republic would be restored.

A new election would be held.

The office of Emperor would cease to exist.

The Senate erupted.

Some cheered.

Some protested.

Most simply stared in disbelief.

The vote that followed was historic.

The motion passed.

The Empire ended.

The Republic returned.

—

Padmé declined to participate in the election.

Many urged her to run.

She refused.

She had spent years wielding extraordinary authority.

Now she wanted distance from power.

The election proceeded without her.

Candidates campaigned across the galaxy.

Debates were held.

Votes were cast.

When the results were finally announced, Bail Organa emerged victorious.

The Republic had a new Chancellor.

One chosen through democratic election.

Exactly as Padmé had intended.

—

With her work complete, Padmé quietly stepped away from politics.

No new office.

No advisory position.

No special authority.

Just retirement.

Many found the decision surprising.

Those who knew her best did not.

She had spent much of her life carrying responsibility for entire worlds.

For the first time, she wanted something simpler.

Anakin made a similar choice.

The old Jedi Order was gone.

The Empire was gone.

The war was gone.

The causes that had defined his entire adult life had vanished.

So he left public life as well.

No throne.

No military command.

No political office.

No title beyond his own name.

—

Together, they returned to Naboo.

To Varykino Villa.

The same lakeside retreat that had once been a refuge during another, simpler time.

Luke and Leia accompanied them.

The twins were old enough now to run through the gardens, chase one another along the shoreline, and fill the villa with noise from morning until night.

The days settled into a rhythm.

Peaceful.

Ordinary.

Padmé spent long afternoons reading by the lake.

Anakin repaired things that did not need repairing.

The twins explored every corner of the estate.

Evenings were spent together.

No Senate sessions.

No military briefings.

No galactic crises.

Just family.

Years earlier, neither of them would have believed such a life possible.

Yet somehow, after war, tragedy, power, and revolution, it was where they finally arrived.

Not on a throne.

Not at the center of the galaxy.

But beside a quiet lake on Naboo.

Together.

And for the first time in a very long while, the future felt uncertain in the best possible way.

# Part 5

The years passed peacefully on Naboo.

Luke and Leia grew.

The Republic endured.

Chancellor Bail Organa proved a capable leader, and for the first time in generations the galaxy was not dominated by either war or fear.

Varykino Villa became exactly what Padmé and Anakin had hoped it would become:

A home.

Not a hiding place.

Not a command center.

Not a palace.

Just a home.

—

One afternoon, a small vessel descended through the skies above the lake.

Anakin noticed it first.

He was standing on a balcony overlooking the water.

Padmé joined him moments later.

"What is it?"

Anakin smiled faintly.

"I think I know."

The ship landed.

A familiar figure emerged from the ramp.

Then another.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

And Master Yoda.

Padmé laughed softly.

"Well. It's about time."

—

The reunion was somewhat awkward.

Not hostile.

Not even particularly tense.

Just awkward.

There was a great deal of history between the three men.

Some of it good.

Some of it very bad.

Fortunately, age and peace had softened many old wounds.

After greetings were exchanged and everyone settled into the villa, Yoda decided to address what he apparently considered the most important matter immediately.

His ears twitched.

"A question first, Skywalker."

Anakin sighed.

"I had a feeling."

"The one and only Dark Lord of the Sith and Sith Master, are you still?"

Padmé immediately started laughing.

Obi-Wan tried—and failed—not to.

Anakin buried his face in his hands.

"Oh stars."

"What?" Obi-Wan asked.

"I completely forgot about that."

Yoda blinked.

"Forgot?"

"I genuinely forgot."

Anakin sat back in his chair.

"That was years ago."

Yoda continued staring.

"So... are you?"

Anakin thought about it for perhaps two seconds.

"No."

The answer came easily.

"No, I'm not."

He looked out toward the lake.

"I mentally abandoned the Sith a long time ago."

Then he looked back at them.

"If it makes everyone feel better, I officially renounce the Sith right now."

Yoda nodded slowly.

"Good."

"Honestly," Anakin admitted, "I don't think I've thought about Sith philosophy in years."

Padmé smiled.

"I can confirm that."

With those words, the Banite Sith lineage effectively ended.

No master.

No apprentice.

No successor.

Nothing.

A thousand years of Sith succession reached its conclusion not with a grand battle, but with a retired former Emperor sitting in a lakeside villa saying:

"I forgot."

—

A short while later, Obi-Wan raised another point.

"There is one Sith-related matter."

Anakin looked up.

"What?"

"Maul."

Anakin blinked.

"Oh."

Obi-Wan waited.

"Yes?"

Anakin shrugged.

"I killed him."

The room became quiet.

Yoda looked surprised.

"When?"

"During the Imperial years."

"You found him?"

"He found me, actually."

Anakin leaned back.

"He was trying to build some kind of criminal empire."

"That sounds like Maul."

"It didn't work out."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"I see."

The matter was considered settled.

—

Eventually the conversation shifted to more important topics.

Anakin looked between Obi-Wan and Yoda.

"What about you two?"

"What about us?" Obi-Wan asked.

"You disappeared."

Yoda nodded.

"Hide, we did."

"At first."

Obi-Wan's expression became more serious.

"After Order 66, we believed we were witnessing the complete triumph of the Sith."

Anakin looked away.

Neither Jedi commented.

After a moment Obi-Wan continued.

"We went into hiding."

"Reasonable."

"Very."

"But after Sidious died..."

"And especially after Padmé took over," Yoda added.

"We gradually emerged again."

Anakin nodded.

That made sense.

Without Sidious, the situation had changed dramatically.

Without a Sith Lord actively hunting them, there was little reason for permanent exile.

"We've been considering something," Obi-Wan said.

Anakin already knew where this was going.

"The Jedi."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"The Jedi."

—

The discussion lasted for hours.

Perhaps longer.

Padmé eventually left them alone and returned later to find them still talking.

The old Jedi Order.

Its successes.

Its failures.

Its traditions.

Its blind spots.

Its collapse.

Everything was examined.

Nothing was treated as sacred.

Not even by Yoda.

Especially not by Yoda.

The Grand Master had spent many years reflecting on what had happened.

More than once he openly acknowledged mistakes.

The old Order's relationship with attachment.

Its increasing integration into government.

Its rigidity.

Its inability to recognize dangers developing directly in front of it.

Its growing distance from ordinary people.

The conversation was remarkably honest.

At one point Obi-Wan laughed softly.

"If someone had told me years ago that the three of us would be sitting around discussing how the Jedi failed, I would have assumed they were insane."

"Reasonable assumption," Anakin replied.

—

Eventually the central question emerged.

"Rebuild the Jedi Order?" Yoda asked.

Silence followed.

Then Anakin surprised both of them.

"No."

Obi-Wan blinked.

"No?"

"No."

Anakin looked around the villa.

"The old Order is gone."

Neither Jedi interrupted.

"And honestly?"

He paused.

"I think that's probably for the best."

Yoda closed his eyes.

Not in disagreement.

In contemplation.

Anakin continued.

"If something new grows someday, fine."

"But rebuilding the old structure exactly as it was?"

He shook his head.

"I don't think that's the answer."

Obi-Wan slowly nodded.

To Anakin's surprise, he appeared to agree.

Eventually Yoda opened his eyes.

"Perhaps correct, you are."

—

The conversation might have ended there.

Instead Yoda looked out a nearby window.

Luke and Leia were running through the gardens.

Chasing one another.

Laughing.

The sight drew a thoughtful expression from the old Jedi Master.

"Train them, will you?"

Anakin followed his gaze.

"Yes."

Yoda's ears tilted slightly.

"When older."

"Yes."

"Hmm."

Anakin smiled.

"I know exactly what you're thinking."

"Do you?"

"Too old."

Yoda said nothing.

"Attachment issues."

Still nothing.

"Their father being their teacher."

Yoda continued staring.

"I know."

Finally Yoda nodded.

"Concerned, I am."

"Fair."

For a moment neither spoke.

Then Obi-Wan unexpectedly entered the discussion.

"I think he should try."

Yoda looked at him.

Obi-Wan shrugged.

"The old Order had many strengths."

"Agreed."

"But perhaps we were not correct about everything."

Yoda remained silent.

Obi-Wan continued.

"We never truly tested alternatives."

Anakin looked mildly shocked.

"You sound almost rebellious."

"Don't get used to it."

Padmé, listening from across the room, laughed.

Eventually Yoda sighed.

A long, thoughtful sigh.

"Certain, I am not."

That alone was remarkable.

The old Yoda would rarely have admitted uncertainty on such a fundamental issue.

After another moment he nodded.

"Try, you should."

Anakin smiled.

"Thank you, Master."

—

Before departing, Obi-Wan revealed one final surprise.

A familiar astromech rolled into the room.

A golden protocol droid followed behind.

R2-D2 emitted an enthusiastic whistle.

C-3PO raised his arms dramatically.

"Oh my!"

Luke and Leia immediately ran over.

Anakin stared.

"R2?"

The astromech chirped happily.

"And Threepio."

"Oh dear," Threepio said. "It has been quite some time."

Obi-Wan smiled.

"I think it's long overdue."

Anakin looked confused.

"What is?"

"Their return."

He gestured toward the droids.

"They belong with your family."

For a moment nobody spoke.

Then R2 rolled directly into Anakin's leg.

The astromech's version of a hug.

Anakin laughed.

"Good to see you too."

Threepio immediately began introducing himself to Luke and Leia despite the fact that they clearly did not need an introduction.

Within minutes the twins were completely fascinated.

—

Eventually it was time to leave.

The sun was setting over the lake.

Long shadows stretched across the gardens.

Obi-Wan and Yoda prepared to depart.

No dramatic farewells were needed.

No grand declarations.

Just old acquaintances parting after a pleasant visit.

As the ship prepared for takeoff, Obi-Wan paused.

He looked at Anakin.

For a moment, memories seemed to pass silently between them.

Master and apprentice.

Friends.

Enemies.

Then neither.

Obi-Wan smiled.

"You seem happy."

Anakin looked back toward the villa.

Padmé was standing on the balcony.

Luke and Leia were playing with the droids.

The evening light reflected across the lake.

"Yeah."

For perhaps the first time in his life, the answer came without hesitation.

"Yeah, I am."

Obi-Wan nodded.

Then he boarded the ship.

Moments later it lifted into the sky.

Anakin watched until it disappeared beyond the horizon.

He remained in contact with Obi-Wan and Yoda in the years that followed.

They never became especially close again.

Too much history stood between them for that.

But they were no longer enemies.

No longer rivals.

No longer haunted by old conflicts.

Simply three people who had survived the same extraordinary age and lived long enough to see what came after.

And for all of them, that was enough.
