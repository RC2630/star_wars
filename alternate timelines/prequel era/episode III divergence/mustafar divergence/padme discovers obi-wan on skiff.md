# Part 1

The Republic was collapsing into darkness.

Supreme Chancellor Palpatine had declared the Jedi traitors to the Republic. Across the galaxy, clone troopers had turned on the Jedi generals they had fought beside for years. The Jedi Temple on Coruscant had been overrun. Count Dooku was dead. General Grievous had been destroyed on Utapau by Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Meanwhile, Anakin Skywalker, once hailed as the Chosen One of the Jedi Order, had pledged himself to Darth Sidious. Given the name Darth Vader, he had marched into the Jedi Temple at the head of the 501st Legion and helped wipe out those inside. His final assignment had sent him to the volcanic world of Mustafar, where the leaders of the Separatist Council waited under the false belief that Sidious still protected them.

On Coruscant, Senator Padmé Amidala had watched the Republic unravel with growing horror. She had learned enough to know that Anakin had become involved in terrible events, but she refused to believe he was beyond redemption. Determined to find him herself, she secretly departed aboard her Naboo skiff.

Unknown to her, Obi-Wan Kenobi had hidden aboard the vessel before it left.

The ship had already entered hyperspace when Padmé sensed a faint movement behind the cockpit.

She turned sharply.

"Who's there?"

Obi-Wan slowly stepped into view from the rear compartment.

Padmé stared at him in disbelief.

"Obi-Wan?"

He gave her a weary nod.

"I'm sorry."

Her expression hardened.

"You hid on my ship."

"I had no other choice."

"You had every choice."

He took a slow breath.

"I need to find Anakin. I believe you're flying to him."

Padmé folded her arms.

"And if I am?"

"Then I must come."

"No."

She reached for the controls.

"I'm not taking you to him."

Without another word, she banked the skiff around and began entering a new course.

Obi-Wan's eyes narrowed.

"Padmé... what are you doing?"

"I'm leaving."

"You're turning away."

"Yes."

"You know where he is."

"I do."

"Then take me there."

"No."

Obi-Wan stepped closer.

"Padmé, the galaxy is falling apart. I have to stop him."

"I won't help you kill my husband."

"I don't want to kill him."

"But you will if you think you have to."

Silence lingered between them.

Padmé kept flying.

Obi-Wan finally spoke.

"I'm sorry."

She glanced sideways.

"For what?"

He raised a hand toward her.

"You will take this ship to Anakin."

His voice became calm and deliberate.

"You will change course."

Padmé looked at him without the slightest sign of hesitation.

"No."

Obi-Wan blinked.

"You will fly to him."

"I said no."

The Jedi Master slowly lowered his hand.

Padmé met his eyes.

"Jedi mind tricks only work on weak minds."

She shook her head.

"I have a strong mind, Obi-Wan. That won't work."

For a long moment he remained silent.

"I was hoping it wouldn't come to this."

Padmé frowned.

"What do you mean?"

He looked down.

"Master Yoda ordered me to find Anakin."

"And?"

"If I have to..."

He looked back at her.

"...I'll resort to a Force probe."

Padmé's face lost its color.

"No."

"I'm sorry."

"You don't have the right."

"I know."

She immediately tried to focus her thoughts elsewhere.

She forced herself to think about Naboo.

About her childhood.

About the lakes.

About Theed.

Anything except Mustafar.

Obi-Wan closed his eyes.

The Force reached into her thoughts.

Padmé struggled to resist.

"No..."

Sweat formed across her brow as she desperately pushed back.

Obi-Wan's expression tightened with concentration.

Fragments.

Fire.

Black stone.

Rivers of lava.

A fortress.

Mustafar.

His eyes opened.

"Mustafar."

Padmé looked away.

"You found it."

"I did."

He stepped toward the controls.

"Take us to Mustafar."

She looked back at him defiantly.

"This is my ship."

"I know."

"You can't order me around."

Obi-Wan hesitated.

Then, with visible reluctance, he reached for his lightsaber.

The blue blade ignited with its familiar hum.

Padmé stared at it.

"You'd threaten me?"

"I don't want to."

"You already are."

"I must reach him."

She slowly stood from the pilot's seat.

"You would really force me?"

"I'm sorry."

Keeping the lightsaber ready, Obi-Wan carefully slid into the pilot's chair.

He entered the coordinates for Mustafar.

The skiff changed course.

Padmé quietly stepped behind him.

Her expression was filled with sadness.

"I'm sorry too."

Obi-Wan looked back just enough to begin turning toward her.

A blaster shot rang through the cockpit.

The bolt struck his leg.

"Agh!"

The lightsaber flew from his hand as he collapsed sideways in agony.

Smoke rose from the wound.

Padmé immediately caught the weapon before it could slide away.

Obi-Wan gritted his teeth, struggling to rise.

His injured leg refused to support him.

Padmé lowered her own blaster.

"I'm sorry."

Pain crossed Obi-Wan's face.

"You... shot me."

"I did what I had to."

"You can't stop this."

"I have to prevent you from reaching Anakin."

Obi-Wan reached toward his belt.

Padmé was already there.

She unclipped his comlink and slipped it into her own pocket.

"No calls."

She then carefully knelt beside him.

"I'm not leaving you here."

Despite everything that had happened moments before, she gently helped him to his feet.

He leaned heavily against her as she guided him through the skiff toward one of its built-in medical cots.

Every step sent pain through his wounded leg.

Padmé activated the cot's emergency medical systems.

The automated equipment immediately began treating the blaster wound.

She adjusted the restraints only enough to keep him from accidentally falling while the ship maneuvered.

"There."

Obi-Wan looked up at her.

"Padmé..."

"I'm taking you somewhere you can receive proper treatment."

"You have to go to Mustafar."

"No."

She gave him one last sorrowful look.

"Stay here."

"I don't exactly have much choice."

She offered a faint, regretful smile.

"I know."

Returning to the cockpit, Padmé entered new coordinates.

The skiff turned away from Mustafar.

Its destination was Naboo.

Far away, on the volcanic world of Mustafar, Darth Vader completed his assignment.

The terrified members of the Separatist Council begged for mercy as he entered their headquarters.

"There is no war," one pleaded.

"The war is over."

"We were promised safety."

Vader answered only with his lightsaber.

One by one, every member of the council fell.

The chamber became silent except for the crackle of damaged machinery.

After transmitting confirmation that the Separatist leadership had been eliminated, Vader departed Mustafar aboard his starfighter.

His course was set for Coruscant.

By the time Padmé's skiff descended through the skies of Naboo, Vader was already well on his journey back to the capital.

The elegant towers of Theed soon appeared beneath the clouds.

Medical personnel rushed to the landing platform as Padmé's ship touched down.

They hurried aboard with repulsor stretchers.

One of the physicians looked at Obi-Wan's robes and immediately recognized him.

"Master Kenobi?"

Obi-Wan managed a strained nod.

"I've... had a difficult journey."

The physicians carefully transferred him onto a stretcher.

Padmé walked beside them.

"The wound is from a blaster shot."

The physician examined the injury.

"We'll get him into surgery immediately."

Obi-Wan looked toward Padmé.

"You still have time."

She knew exactly what he meant.

"I'm not taking you to Anakin."

"He needs to be stopped."

"He needs help."

"If help fails?"

Padmé remained silent.

The medical team wheeled Obi-Wan into the facility.

As the doors closed behind them, Padmé stood alone in the entrance hall, still carrying his comlink in one hand.

She quietly slipped it into a pocket.

For the moment, Obi-Wan Kenobi was alive, receiving treatment for his injured leg in a medical facility at Theed.

And far across the stars, Anakin Skywalker continued his return flight to Coruscant, where Darth Sidious awaited him.

# Part 2

Anakin Skywalker's starfighter descended through the endless lanes of Coruscant until it reached the Senate District.

He landed without ceremony and made his way directly to the Supreme Chancellor's private office.

The guards admitted him immediately.

The great doors slid shut behind him.

Palpatine stood before the panoramic window overlooking the city.

Without turning around, he smiled.

"You have returned."

"I have."

Palpatine slowly faced him.

"And the Separatist leaders?"

"They're dead."

A pleased smile spread across Palpatine's face.

"Well done."

Anakin remained silent.

Palpatine clasped his hands together.

"The Clone War is finished. The Jedi Order has been broken. The Republic has been secured."

Anakin took a step forward.

"I believe I've completed everything you asked of me."

"You have served magnificently, my apprentice."

Anakin's eyes locked onto his.

"Now it's your turn."

Palpatine's smile faded slightly.

"My turn?"

"You promised to teach me how to save Padmé."

There was a pause.

Palpatine nodded slowly.

"Yes..."

Anakin waited.

Palpatine began pacing.

"The power over life... is not a simple technique."

"I know."

"It requires patience."

"I have patience."

"It requires deeper understanding."

"I'll learn."

Palpatine continued speaking.

"There are mysteries within the dark side that are... difficult to explain."

"Then explain them."

"I need time."

"For what?"

"To teach you."

Anakin frowned.

"You said you knew."

"I do."

"Then teach me."

"The knowledge is... complicated."

Anakin's expression hardened.

Palpatine kept talking.

"It is something that must be explored together."

"You told me Darth Plagueis had learned the secret."

"He did."

"And you said you could teach me."

"I said that together we would discover—"

Anakin interrupted him.

"No."

Silence filled the office.

Palpatine's eyes narrowed.

Anakin slowly shook his head.

"You don't know."

Palpatine remained composed.

"I understand your frustration."

"You don't know."

"The dark side—"

"You lied to me."

"I offered you power."

"You promised me you could save Padmé."

"I promised you a path."

"You promised me the knowledge."

Palpatine's silence answered him more clearly than any words.

Anakin felt every terrible realization crash into him at once.

The Jedi Temple.

The younglings.

The slaughter.

Mustafar.

Every choice he had made.

Every life he had taken.

His breathing grew heavy.

"You used me."

Palpatine raised a calming hand.

"Anakin—"

"You used me!"

The Force exploded outward.

An invisible wave slammed into Palpatine with tremendous violence.

He flew across the office and crashed into the far wall with enough force to crack the decorative stone behind him.

Furniture toppled.

Statues shattered.

The great windows rattled.

Palpatine slowly climbed back to his feet.

His face twisted with fury.

"How dare you attack your master."

Anakin stared at him.

"I am no longer your apprentice."

His voice was steady.

"I am no longer Darth Vader."

He ignited his blue lightsaber.

The blade illuminated the ruined office.

"I am Anakin Skywalker."

Palpatine's eyes burned with hatred.

"So be it."

His crimson blade snapped to life.

"You have outlived your usefulness."

He lunged.

Anakin barely brought his own blade up in time.

The lightsabers crashed together.

The duel erupted instantly.

Palpatine attacked with astonishing speed, his crimson blade becoming a blur of violent strikes.

Anakin matched every movement.

Blue and red flashed across the office.

Palpatine thrust out his free hand.

A Force push blasted toward Anakin.

Anakin planted his feet and answered with one of his own.

The two invisible waves collided between them, splintering the marble floor and sending broken stone in every direction.

Palpatine leaped over the debris and attacked again.

Anakin blocked.

Countered.

Advanced.

Furniture flew through the air.

A heavy table hurtled toward Anakin.

He sliced it apart.

The halves continued flying past him.

Palpatine ripped decorative pillars from the walls and hurled them one after another.

Anakin batted one aside with the Force.

He shattered another with his lightsaber.

The third struck him in the shoulder, sending him sliding backward across the floor.

Palpatine extended a hand.

Invisible fingers closed around Anakin's throat.

Anakin staggered.

He felt the crushing grip tighten.

With immense effort he reached out through the Force.

Palpatine's own throat suddenly tightened.

The Sith Lord released his grip to protect himself.

Both men gasped for breath.

Palpatine's yellow eyes blazed.

Lightning burst from his fingertips.

Jagged blue-white arcs raced toward Anakin.

Anakin caught them on his lightsaber.

Electricity crawled along the blade.

The energy forced him backward step after step.

His boots carved deep grooves into the polished floor.

With a roar, Anakin redirected the torrent.

The lightning splashed across the room, blasting apart walls and filling the office with smoke.

The duel continued.

Minutes stretched onward.

Every trick either of them knew became a weapon.

Lightsabers.

Force pushes.

Force chokes.

Force lightning.

Pieces of the office flew like missiles.

Massive sculptures became projectiles.

Broken stone swirled through the air.

Windows shattered.

The skyline of Coruscant became visible through the ruined walls.

At last, fatigue began to slow Palpatine.

Anakin saw it.

Palpatine struck high.

Anakin parried.

Palpatine spun.

Anakin anticipated the movement.

Blue met red.

With a powerful two-handed strike, Anakin knocked Palpatine's lightsaber spinning across the room.

The crimson weapon disappeared into the rubble.

Before Palpatine could react, Anakin's blade rested at his throat.

The office fell silent.

Palpatine slowly raised both hands.

"Mercy."

Anakin said nothing.

"I may still know how to save Padmé."

Anakin's eyes remained fixed on him.

"I simply need more time."

Silence.

"I can figure it out."

More silence.

"I swear it."

Anakin finally spoke.

"No."

Palpatine's confidence faltered.

"You've already lied to me once."

"I can still—"

"No."

Anakin tightened his grip.

"I don't believe you anymore."

For a brief instant, fear appeared on Palpatine's face.

Then Anakin swung.

The blue blade passed cleanly through him.

Palpatine collapsed to the ruined floor.

The room became still.

Only the distant sounds of Coruscant drifted through the shattered walls.

Anakin extinguished his lightsaber.

He stood motionless.

His breathing slowed.

He looked around the devastated office.

The destruction.

The bodies.

The silence.

For the first time since leaving the Jedi Temple, he allowed himself to think.

He remembered the frightened faces of the younglings.

He remembered the men and women on Mustafar begging for mercy.

He remembered every decision that had brought him here.

His hand trembled.

Slowly, he reached for his comlink.

He activated it.

After several moments, Padmé's face appeared.

"Anakin?"

Relief flooded his features.

"Padmé."

She studied him.

"You look exhausted."

"I need to see you."

"I'm in Theed."

"Naboo?"

"Yes."

"I'll come right away."

"I'll be waiting."

The transmission ended.

Without another glance at Palpatine's body, Anakin left the ruined office.

He boarded his starfighter and departed Coruscant.

Hours later, his ship descended through Naboo's peaceful skies.

The contrast to Coruscant could not have been greater.

Green fields.

Blue lakes.

The shining domes of Theed.

He landed near one of the city's medical facilities.

Padmé was already waiting outside the main entrance.

She had deliberately come alone, not wanting Anakin to see Obi-Wan while the Jedi Master was still recovering inside the facility.

The moment Anakin stepped down from his starfighter, they looked at one another.

Neither spoke.

Padmé crossed the distance first.

Anakin met her halfway.

They embraced tightly, holding each other as though afraid the other might disappear if they let go.

# Part 3

Outside the medical facility in Theed, Padmé and Anakin remained locked in their embrace for a long time.

Eventually, Padmé stepped back.

There was relief in her eyes simply from seeing him alive, but there was also uncertainty.

She looked at him carefully.

"Obi-Wan told me what he believed had happened."

Anakin lowered his gaze.

"He said you fell to the dark side."

Silence.

"He said you helped destroy the Jedi."

Still silence.

"He said..."

Her voice nearly failed her.

"...he said you killed younglings."

Anakin's shoulders sagged.

"Is it true?"

He tried to answer.

Nothing came out.

"Anakin."

His legs suddenly gave way beneath him.

He collapsed onto the stone walkway outside the entrance.

His breathing became ragged.

"Yes."

Padmé closed her eyes.

He covered his face with both hands.

"I helped kill Master Windu."

His voice shook violently.

"I chose Sidious."

"I pledged myself to him."

"He gave me the name Darth Vader."

Each sentence seemed harder to say than the last.

"I led the attack on the Jedi Temple."

Tears ran freely down his face.

"I killed Jedi."

He swallowed.

"I killed the younglings."

Padmé stared at him without moving.

"I went to Mustafar."

"I killed the Separatist leaders."

"They begged for mercy."

"I didn't listen."

He clenched his fists.

"When I came back to Coruscant, I demanded Sidious teach me how to save you."

His voice became hollow.

"He couldn't."

"He never knew."

"He lied."

"I realized everything I'd done had been for nothing."

"I attacked him."

"We fought."

"I killed him."

Silence filled the space between them.

Padmé simply looked at him.

He lay on the ground before her, utterly broken.

She struggled to find words.

When she finally spoke, her voice was quiet.

"How could you do such a thing?"

Anakin shut his eyes.

"How could you kill innocent younglings?"

He answered without looking at her.

"I have no good excuse."

Another tear rolled down his face.

"The only explanation I have..."

He paused.

"...is that I believed I was saving you."

"I believed if I didn't do those things, you would die."

Padmé let out a long, exhausted sigh.

She stood there for several moments before finally speaking.

"Come with me."

Together they entered the medical facility.

Obi-Wan was awake.

His injured leg had been treated, though he remained confined to bed while it healed.

He looked toward the doorway as they entered.

His eyes settled on Anakin.

Padmé spoke first.

"Obi-Wan... I should explain your leg."

She looked apologetic.

"I shot him."

Anakin looked at her in surprise.

"You what?"

"I had to stop him from reaching you."

She met Obi-Wan's eyes.

"I put him in the medical cot and brought him here."

Obi-Wan nodded faintly.

"That is an accurate summary."

The room fell quiet.

Finally, Anakin stepped forward.

"I fell to the dark side."

Obi-Wan said nothing.

"I became Darth Vader."

"I served Sidious."

"I did everything you feared."

He looked directly at his former master.

"But I renounced it."

"I killed Sidious."

Obi-Wan's face remained solemn.

"Do you think renouncing the dark side undoes what happened in the Jedi Temple?"

Anakin immediately answered.

"No."

His voice carried no hesitation.

"No, it doesn't."

He lowered his head.

"But after everything I'd already done..."

He looked back up.

"...killing Sidious was probably the best thing I could do in that moment."

Obi-Wan slowly nodded.

"I'm glad I no longer have to kill you."

Those words hung heavily in the room.

"But..."

His expression became even sadder.

"...I think you may have done the unforgivable."

Anakin did not argue.

Obi-Wan continued.

"I won't judge that myself."

He took a slow breath.

"I'll defer judgment to Master Yoda."

Before anyone could say more, a medical droid entered the room.

It scanned Padmé.

A series of tones sounded.

The droid looked up.

"Senator Amidala, you are entering labor."

Padmé blinked.

"Already?"

"Yes."

"Please follow me immediately to a maternity room."

Anakin instantly stood.

"I'm coming."

The droid nodded.

"You may accompany her."

The next several hours passed in anxious anticipation.

Anakin never left Padmé's side.

He held her hand through every contraction.

When she cried out in pain, he whispered encouragement.

"You can do this."

"I'm here."

Finally, after hours of labor, the cries of newborn children filled the room.

The medical staff smiled.

"A healthy boy."

Moments later—

"And a healthy girl."

The droid carefully wrapped both infants.

Padmé, exhausted but alive, smiled through tears.

The twins were placed in her arms.

She looked down lovingly.

"Luke."

She kissed the little boy's forehead.

Then she looked at the little girl.

"Leia."

Anakin stared at all three of them.

Padmé was alive.

She was breathing.

She was smiling.

The children were healthy.

He slowly backed away.

"No..."

He sank into a chair.

"No..."

Everything crashed down upon him at once.

Palpatine had never known how to save Padmé.

There had never been anything to save her from.

His visions had driven him into terror.

His terror had driven him into Sidious's hands.

Every terrible deed.

Every innocent life.

Every betrayal.

All committed to prevent something that had never happened.

He buried his face in his hands.

A broken whisper escaped him.

"It was all unnecessary."

He began sobbing.

"I betrayed everyone..."

His voice cracked.

"...for nothing."

He looked up at Padmé.

"I don't even know what to believe anymore."

"I feel..."

He searched for words.

"...betrayed by life itself."

Padmé said nothing.

She simply held Luke and Leia closer.

Some time later, Padmé finally returned Obi-Wan's comlink.

"I've kept this long enough."

Obi-Wan accepted it gratefully.

He activated the device immediately.

"Master Yoda."

Static filled the transmission.

Then Yoda's familiar face appeared.

"Obi-Wan."

Relief crossed Yoda's features.

"Alive, you are."

"Barely."

"Where are you?"

"Theed."

Yoda's expression became serious.

"Sidious escaped me."

Obi-Wan frowned.

"What happened?"

"We dueled in the Senate chamber."

Yoda sighed.

"Defeat him, I could not."

"Bail Organa and I escaped aboard his speeder."

Obi-Wan looked toward Anakin.

"You should come here."

"There is much to discuss."

Yoda nodded.

"Come at once, we will."

The transmission ended.

Several hours later, Bail Organa's ship landed in Theed.

Yoda and Bail entered the medical facility together.

Yoda immediately noticed Anakin.

The room became still.

Obi-Wan spoke.

"Master."

Yoda looked at him.

"He has something to tell you."

Anakin slowly stood.

"I'll tell you everything."

He did.

He began with the nightmares.

"The Force showed me visions of Padmé dying during childbirth."

He described every desperate attempt to prevent that future.

His growing fear.

His conversation with Palpatine about Darth Plagueis.

Helping kill Mace Windu.

His oath to Sidious.

The attack on the Jedi Temple.

Mustafar.

His return.

His confrontation with Sidious.

The duel.

The killing blow.

Finally he spoke about the birth itself.

"Padmé survived."

He looked toward the twins.

"They're both healthy."

Yoda listened without interrupting.

When Anakin finished, silence lingered.

Yoda finally turned toward Padmé.

"The children, may I see?"

Padmé gently lifted both infants.

Yoda smiled softly.

"Strong in the Force, they are."

He looked back at Anakin.

"What happened in the Temple..."

His voice became firm.

"...would have justified immediate incarceration within the Temple itself."

He paused.

"Or turning you over to the Republic's courts for justice."

Anakin nodded.

"I understand."

Yoda continued.

"But gone, the Temple is."

"The Republic..."

He glanced toward Bail.

"...in great disorder it is, after recent events and Sidious's death."

He folded his hands.

"Renounced the dark side, you have."

"Defeated and killed Darth Sidious, you did."

He looked at Luke and Leia.

"And now..."

A faint smile appeared.

"...a new father, you are."

Anakin waited silently.

Yoda spoke his decision.

"Time to meditate and reflect, you need."

He extended one hand.

"Your lightsaber."

Without hesitation, Anakin unclipped it from his belt.

He placed it into Yoda's small hand.

Yoda accepted it.

"For your own good..."

He looked steadily at Anakin.

"...under house arrest, you should remain."

"So that stay still, you finally learn."

"Meditate."

"Reflect deeply."

Anakin nodded.

"I understand."

He hesitated.

"May I stay at Varykino Villa?"

Yoda looked toward Padmé.

"Your property, it is."

"Consent, yours."

Padmé considered it.

Finally she nodded.

"I approve."

Yoda inclined his head.

"So be it."

Anakin spoke again.

"May R2 stay with me?"

The little astromech let out a hopeful whistle.

Yoda smiled.

"Company, useful he will be."

"Remain with you, he may."

Padmé then looked around the room.

"I've made another decision."

Everyone turned toward her.

"I'm placing Luke and Leia in Sola's custody for now."

Anakin blinked.

"What?"

She met his eyes.

"I don't think you're ready."

He looked down.

"I..."

"You're still too dangerous and too unstable to be responsible for two newborn infants."

He wanted to object.

Instead, he remained silent.

Padmé continued.

"I also have to return to Coruscant immediately."

She looked toward Bail.

"The Republic is leaderless."

"The Senate needs to function."

"We have to prevent everything from collapsing."

She looked back at Anakin.

"That will consume nearly all of my time and energy."

"I won't be able to properly care for two newborns while trying to hold the Republic together."

She gently rocked Leia.

"Sola is the most reasonable choice."

Anakin slowly nodded.

He had expected far worse from everyone around him.

He had no grounds to argue.

"I understand."

Yoda and Bail remained at the medical facility with Obi-Wan while he continued recovering.

Padmé, carrying Luke, and Anakin, carrying Leia, departed together.

R2-D2 rolled faithfully behind them.

C-3PO followed with nervous, awkward steps.

The journey to the Naberrie home was quiet.

When they arrived, the front door opened almost immediately.

Sola smiled.

"Padmé!"

Behind her stood little Ryoo and Pooja.

Ruwee and Jobal soon joined them.

Warm smiles greeted the family.

Then they noticed everyone's serious expressions.

Sola frowned.

"What happened?"

Padmé carefully explained.

"The Republic is in crisis."

"I have to return to Coruscant."

She looked down at the twins.

"I need someone I trust completely to care for them."

She looked back up.

"Will you?"

Sola's expression softened immediately.

She reached out and accepted Leia.

"Of course."

She smiled warmly.

"They're family."

"As long as you need."

Jobal gently accepted Luke into her own arms.

"They'll be loved here."

Ruwee nodded.

"You have our word."

Padmé smiled gratefully.

"Thank you."

After saying their farewells, Padmé personally piloted Anakin to Varykino Villa.

The peaceful retreat overlooked Naboo's beautiful lake country.

The ship landed near the familiar residence.

Anakin stepped onto the grass.

Padmé remained beside the ramp.

She looked at him seriously.

"Behave yourself."

"I will."

"Don't leave without authorization."

He nodded.

After a moment he asked quietly,

"May I at least walk outside?"

Padmé considered the request.

Finally she answered.

"Yes."

Relief appeared on his face.

"But only within the lake country."

She pointed toward the surrounding landscape.

"No matter what you do, stay within the lake country."

"Do not leave it."

Anakin nodded immediately.

"I won't."

"I promise."

Padmé accepted the answer.

C-3PO stepped back aboard her ship.

# Part 5

The months that followed became a time of rebuilding.

The galaxy had lost its Supreme Chancellor.

The Jedi Order had been devastated.

The Clone War had finally ended.

Yet, for the first time in years, there was also hope.

On Coruscant, Padmé Amidala threw herself completely into the work of repairing the Republic.

Palpatine had formally proclaimed the Galactic Empire before his death. His supporters insisted that proclamation remained binding.

Sate Pestage argued before the Senate that the Empire was the lawful government.

Mas Amedda attempted to continue administering the government under Imperial authority.

Wilhuff Tarkin spoke repeatedly of the need for strong centralized rule, insisting that abandoning the Empire would only invite another galactic war.

But something had changed.

Palpatine himself was gone.

Without him, the confidence behind those arguments steadily weakened.

The Senate chambers became the center of constant debate.

One afternoon, Bail Organa rose from his seat.

"The office of Supreme Chancellor is vacant."

A senator stood.

"There is no Supreme Chancellor."

"There is an Emperor."

Several senators murmured in agreement.

Bail answered calmly.

"The Senate did not elect an Emperor."

Another voice called out.

"The proclamation has already been made."

Padmé stood.

"The proclamation was made under emergency powers accumulated through years of manufactured crisis."

She looked around the chamber.

"If this Senate believes the Republic still has meaning, then let the Senate do what it has always done."

She paused.

"Vote."

The chamber erupted into discussion.

Eventually the proposal carried.

The Senate would elect a new Chancellor.

The election lasted several days.

When the final count was announced, Bail Organa had secured the necessary support.

He stood before the Senate as the newly elected Supreme Chancellor of the Republic.

His first address was brief.

"The Republic governs this galaxy."

"The proclamation establishing the Galactic Empire is illegitimate."

"It has no legal standing under Republic law."

"The Republic endures."

Many senators stood in applause.

Not everyone.

Sate Pestage remained seated.

Mas Amedda did the same.

Tarkin silently watched.

Yet as weeks became months, their influence steadily declined.

More and more systems recognized Bail's government.

Administrative offices resumed operating under Republican authority.

The courts rejected Imperial decrees.

Military commands returned to Senate oversight.

Eventually people simply stopped referring to the Empire at all.

It became a failed attempt to replace the Republic rather than its successor.

Elsewhere in the galaxy, another difficult question remained.

The Confederacy of Independent Systems still existed.

Count Dooku was dead.

General Grievous was dead.

Every member of the Separatist Council had been killed.

The civilian government on Raxus Secundus prepared formal surrender documents.

Their representatives arrived expecting unconditional terms.

Instead, they found Chancellor Bail Organa waiting.

One delegate bowed.

"Chancellor."

"We are prepared to dissolve the Confederacy and return all member worlds to the Republic."

Bail quietly folded the prepared surrender documents.

"No."

Confused silence followed.

The delegates looked at one another.

"No?"

Bail smiled gently.

"I don't want your surrender."

One of the delegates blinked.

"Then... what do you want?"

"I want peace."

Another representative frowned.

"We lost."

"Yes."

"We expected occupation."

"No."

"We expected our government to be dissolved."

"It won't be."

The delegates stared.

Bail continued.

"If any world wishes to rejoin the Republic voluntarily, it will be welcomed."

He paused.

"If any world wishes to remain independent within the Confederacy of Independent Systems..."

He looked around the room.

"...it may do so peacefully."

The room became utterly silent.

One elderly delegate slowly stood.

"You would recognize our independence?"

"Yes."

"But..."

His voice shook.

"...why?"

Bail answered without hesitation.

"If I force unwilling worlds back into the Republic..."

"...then resentment will grow."

"Separatism will return."

"And someday another war will begin."

He folded his hands.

"I would rather accept a peaceful neighbor than create a resentful citizen."

Another delegate quietly asked,

"So we truly have a choice?"

"You do."

Many of those present lowered their heads in gratitude.

One representative spoke softly.

"Thank you."

For the first time since the Clone War had begun, both governments left negotiations satisfied.

The Republic gained peace.

The Confederacy gained the independence many of its member worlds had sought from the beginning.

The Clone War finally ended not with occupation, but with mutual recognition.

Although the Republic had changed governments, the clone army still posed a danger.

The clones still treated the Jedi as enemies.

Yoda judged that the time was not yet safe.

"Come with me, Obi-Wan."

"Where are we going?"

"A place difficult to find."

The inhibitor chips.

The Senate immediately approved a galaxy-wide medical program.

Every clone would have the inhibitor chip removed.

Clone after clone underwent surgery.

Many awoke horrified upon learning what they had done.

Some openly wept.

Some sought forgiveness from surviving Jedi.

"It is safe."

"The Temple..."

His eyes became moist.

"...home once more."

Long sessions filled Senate committees.

Emergency powers were dramatically reduced.

Executive authority was divided and limited.

Greater judicial oversight was established.

Additional protections against abuses of power became law.

Independent review mechanisms were strengthened.

The Masters.

The Temple Guards.

The Separatist leaders.

Obi-Wan.

Yoda.

Ahsoka.

Herself.

Anakin's apology to the younger Jedi General he once had been.

Pooja hugged Luke.
