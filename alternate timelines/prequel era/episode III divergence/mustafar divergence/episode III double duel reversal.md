# Part 1

The volcanic world of Mustafar burned beneath rivers of molten rock as the final moments of the duel between Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker arrived.

The two Jedi stood upon separate banks of flowing lava, their lightsabers casting blue light through the smoke.

Obi-Wan raised his blade.

"It's over, Anakin. I have the high ground."

Anakin's yellow eyes narrowed.

"You underestimate my power."

Without another word, he leaped.

Obi-Wan expected a reckless attack. He shifted his stance, preparing to bring his lightsaber down as Anakin passed over him.

Instead, while still soaring through the air, Anakin thrust his free hand toward his former master.

A crushing wave of the Force exploded outward.

Obi-Wan's eyes widened in surprise.

The invisible impact hurled him backward with tremendous force. He slammed into the black volcanic ground, his lightsaber flying wide as the breath was driven from his lungs.

Before Obi-Wan could recover, Anakin landed cleanly on the ash-covered slope.

He sprinted forward.

Obi-Wan reached toward his fallen weapon, but he was too slow.

Anakin brought his lightsaber down with one final, decisive strike.

The blade cut through Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Silence settled over the lava river except for the endless roar of fire.

For several long moments Anakin simply stood there, breathing heavily.

His former master lay motionless.

Anakin extinguished his own lightsaber. He walked over, picked up Obi-Wan's fallen weapon, and looked at it quietly.

Then he clipped Obi-Wan's lightsaber beside his own on his belt.

Without another glance behind him, he began walking back toward the Naboo skiff.

As he crossed the refinery complex, a familiar series of electronic whistles echoed through the smoke.

R2-D2 rolled toward him.

The astromech stopped, looking up at Anakin.

After a brief pause, Anakin simply nodded.

"Come on."

R2 followed him without hesitation.

By the time they reached the landing platform, C-3PO had already managed to carry the unconscious Padmé Amidala aboard the skiff.

"Oh!" Threepio exclaimed upon seeing Anakin. "Master Anakin! Thank goodness you've returned."

Anakin stepped aboard with R2 following behind.

The engines began warming.

A few moments later, Padmé stirred.

Her eyes slowly opened.

She looked around weakly before spotting Anakin sitting beside her.

"...Anakin..."

"I'm here."

Relief briefly crossed her face before another thought entered her mind.

"Where's Obi-Wan?"

Anakin answered without hesitation.

"I killed him."

Padmé stared at him in disbelief.

"You... what?"

"I killed him."

"Why would you do such a thing?"

Anakin met her eyes.

"Because Obi-Wan would have killed me if I hadn't killed him."

Padmé shook her head weakly.

"No... I don't believe that. Obi-Wan wouldn't—"

"If that's not what he wanted to do," Anakin interrupted, "then why would he have come to Mustafar?"

Padmé searched for an answer but found none immediately.

"He came because..." she began.

Anakin gently placed a hand on her shoulder.

"Padmé."

She looked at him.

"You're too weak. You shouldn't be talking so much."

A flash of irritation appeared despite her exhaustion.

"I'm only weak because you choked me earlier."

The words struck him harder than any lightsaber.

He lowered his eyes.

"...You're right."

For several seconds he said nothing.

Then he quietly spoke.

"I'm sorry."

Padmé remained silent.

"I was too triggered by Obi-Wan's appearance at that moment," Anakin continued. "I wasn't thinking clearly. I should not have choked you."

She studied his face.

For the first time since arriving on Mustafar, she heard genuine regret in his voice.

After a long silence, she nodded faintly.

"I accept your apology."

Anakin gave a small nod.

"I'm taking you to Coruscant. You need proper medical treatment."

"No."

He looked surprised.

"No?"

"We're not going to Coruscant."

"You need a medical facility."

"We're going to Naboo."

"Padmé—"

She interrupted him with unexpected firmness.

"Take me to Naboo."

Though physically weak, the authority in her voice was unmistakable.

For a moment Anakin considered arguing.

Instead, he simply nodded.

"...All right."

Padmé relaxed slightly.

Her strength faded once more, and within moments she drifted back into unconsciousness.

Anakin moved to the controls.

R2-D2 connected himself to the ship's systems.

C-3PO settled nervously into his seat.

The skiff lifted away from Mustafar and turned toward the stars.

Its destination was Naboo.

At nearly the same time, far above Coruscant, another battle had reached its own conclusion.

Within the Grand Convocation Chamber of the Senate, Yoda and Darth Sidious faced one another atop the floating Senate pods.

The Dark Lord attacked relentlessly.

Yoda met every strike.

At last, with a sudden burst of speed, Yoda twisted beneath Sidious's guard and struck his lightsaber.

Sidious's crimson blade flew from his hand, disappearing into the depths of the chamber.

The Emperor immediately reached out with the Force, intending either to reclaim his weapon or unleash another torrent of Force lightning.

He never had the chance.

Yoda stepped forward in one smooth motion.

His green blade pierced straight through Sidious's chest.

The Dark Lord gasped.

His yellow eyes stared into Yoda's.

Then the life left them.

Yoda withdrew his lightsaber.

Sidious collapsed onto the Senate pod, motionless.

The chamber fell silent.

Yoda looked at the fallen Sith Lord for only a moment.

"There is no more time."

He hurried from the Senate building.

Outside, Bail Organa had been waiting anxiously in his speeder.

As Yoda climbed aboard, Bail immediately accelerated away.

"Master Yoda," Bail asked, "what happened?"

"The Sith Lord is dead."

Bail looked over in astonishment.

"You defeated him?"

"I did."

Relief washed over Bail's face, but Yoda's expression remained troubled.

"What is wrong?"

"The last message received from Obi-Wan was after arriving on Mustafar."

Yoda looked out over Coruscant's skyline.

"Since then... silence."

"You've tried contacting him?"

"Several times."

"No response."

Bail frowned.

"Then we should find him."

Yoda nodded.

"To Mustafar, we must go."

Bail drove directly toward the Tantive III.

Captain Raymus Antilles greeted them as they arrived.

"Senator. Master Yoda."

"We're departing immediately," Bail said. "Mustafar."

"No delay?"

"No delay."

"At once."

The Tantive III lifted into space and entered hyperspace.

Some time later, the corvette emerged above Mustafar.

Yoda, Bail Organa, and Captain Antilles descended to the surface with a small search party.

Smoke drifted across the industrial complex.

The battle was over.

No living Jedi could be found.

Eventually one of the crew called out.

"Over here!"

The others hurried over.

Obi-Wan Kenobi lay where he had fallen, his body marked by combat and lightly burned by the intense volcanic heat surrounding him.

Bail stopped in shock.

"...No."

Yoda slowly approached.

For a long time he simply looked at his former student.

Sadness settled over the old Jedi Master's face.

"Gone he is."

No one spoke.

At last Yoda quietly knelt.

"Bring him with us."

The search party carefully lifted Obi-Wan's body onto a stretcher.

They carried him back aboard the Tantive III.

Once everyone had returned, Bail turned toward the navigation crew.

"Coruscant is likely descending into chaos."

Captain Antilles nodded.

"Where shall we go instead?"

Bail looked toward Yoda.

Yoda answered.

"To Alderaan."

Bail understood immediately.

"He deserves a proper funeral."

"Yes."

The Tantive III turned away from Mustafar.

Its engines carried it into hyperspace toward Alderaan.

Back on Coruscant, the body of Darth Sidious remained where it had fallen until Mas Amedda entered the Senate chamber.

He froze.

The Emperor lay dead.

A moment later another set of footsteps echoed across the enormous room.

Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin arrived.

He stopped beside Mas Amedda.

Neither man spoke for several seconds.

Finally Mas Amedda broke the silence.

"The Emperor is dead."

"So it would appear," Tarkin answered.

Mas Amedda straightened his robes.

"As Grand Vizier, I was second in command."

Tarkin looked at him.

"I am now the Emperor."

Tarkin's expression hardened.

"No."

Mas Amedda frowned.

"No?"

"You will not become Emperor."

"I am the rightful successor."

"You are a bureaucrat."

"I administered the Empire."

"You served it."

"I stood beside the Emperor."

"And I commanded his military."

The atmosphere grew colder.

Mas Amedda folded his hands.

"Tomorrow I shall convene a special session of the Imperial Senate."

Tarkin's eyes narrowed.

"I will declare myself the new Emperor."

Tarkin stepped closer.

"Don't you dare try."

Mas Amedda met his gaze.

"I have every intention of doing exactly that."

"If you attempt it," Tarkin said evenly, "there will be severe consequences."

The two men stared at one another in silence beside the body of the dead Emperor.

Neither yielded.

At last Mas Amedda turned and walked away.

Tarkin remained where he was for another few moments before departing through a different exit, already considering his own next move.

# Part 2

The Naboo skiff emerged from hyperspace and descended through the clear skies of Naboo.

Green fields, shimmering lakes, and the elegant spires of Theed replaced the smoke and fire of Mustafar.

The moment the skiff landed, Anakin wasted no time.

Medical personnel rushed out to meet them as Padmé was carefully transferred onto a repulsor stretcher.

"This way!" one of the doctors called.

Anakin stayed close behind as they hurried through the corridors of Theed's finest medical facility.

For the next several hours, doctors and medical droids performed every examination they considered necessary.

Anakin remained outside the treatment room, pacing constantly.

R2-D2 stayed nearby, occasionally emitting anxious whistles.

C-3PO folded his hands together.

"I'm certain everything will be perfectly all right," he said, sounding far less certain than his words suggested.

At last, the doors opened.

The lead physician stepped outside.

"Senator Amidala is perfectly healthy."

Anakin visibly relaxed.

"There is no lasting damage from the compression to her neck," the doctor continued. "She should make a complete recovery."

Without another word, Anakin hurried inside.

Padmé was sitting upright in bed, fully conscious and alert.

She smiled faintly as he entered.

"You look better," Anakin said.

"I feel better."

He sat beside her.

"I'm glad you're all right."

He looked down briefly.

"And... I'm glad the Force choke didn't cause any lasting damage."

Padmé gave a small nod.

"So am I."

Anakin stood.

"Then let's get you discharged."

Before either of them could move, the lead doctor entered again carrying a datapad.

"I'm afraid we have one recommendation."

Padmé looked toward him.

"What is it?"

"Our examinations indicate that you are likely to go into labor within the next twenty-four hours."

Both of them stared.

"So soon?" Padmé asked.

The doctor nodded.

"There is something else."

He looked between them.

"You're having twins."

For a moment neither of them spoke.

Then Padmé smiled in surprise.

"Twins?"

The doctor smiled back.

"A healthy boy and a healthy girl, as best our scans can determine."

Anakin slowly sat back down.

"Twins..."

The doctor continued.

"My professional recommendation is that you remain here until the children are born."

Padmé looked at Anakin.

"I think that makes sense."

Anakin nodded.

"Agreed."

The doctor smiled.

"Very good. We'll prepare a suite for you."

Later that evening, the two of them sat together watching the HoloNet news.

Every major broadcast carried the same story.

Images of the Galactic Senate filled the screen.

"...confirmed reports indicate that Emperor Palpatine has died inside the Senate building. Imperial officials have not yet announced a clear line of succession..."

Another commentator added,

"Conflicting reports suggest increasing political instability on Coruscant as senior Imperial figures attempt to establish authority."

Anakin's expression slowly changed.

Padmé noticed immediately.

"Anakin?"

He didn't answer.

She muted the holoprojector.

"What's wrong?"

"I'm fine."

"No, you're not."

He remained silent.

She reached over and took his hand.

"Tell me."

Several long moments passed.

Finally he spoke quietly.

"Palpatine told me..."

His voice faltered.

"He said he knew a way to save you from dying in childbirth."

Padmé watched him carefully.

"He promised he could teach me."

Anakin lowered his head.

"Now he's dead."

His voice became barely audible.

"And whatever that way was... it's gone forever."

Padmé squeezed his hand.

"I'm not going to die in childbirth."

"You don't know that."

"I do."

He looked at her.

She continued calmly.

"You've already done enough terrible things trying to save me."

He looked away.

"Enough is enough."

"There had to be another way—"

"No."

Her voice became firmer.

"You are the one in the wrong here."

Anakin fell silent.

"You have a great deal to reflect on."

He nodded slightly.

Padmé asked gently,

"Tell me everything."

He looked surprised.

"Everything?"

"Everything."

He took a slow breath.

"It started on General Grievous's flagship."

He recounted every step.

"I defeated Count Dooku."

He paused.

"Palpatine told me to execute him."

"You did?"

"I cut off his head."

Padmé remained silent.

He continued.

"After that I began having visions."

"What kind?"

"You."

He swallowed.

"You dying during childbirth."

Padmé listened without interrupting.

"The Jedi Council asked me to spy on Chancellor Palpatine."

He continued describing every event.

"Palpatine told me the story of Darth Plagueis."

"He claimed Plagueis had discovered how to prevent death."

"I realized Palpatine was a Sith Lord."

"I reported him to Master Windu."

He closed his eyes.

"Mace Windu defeated him."

"What happened then?" Padmé asked quietly.

"Palpatine said I needed him alive."

"I panicked."

"I cut off Master Windu's hand."

"He fell."

"There was no going back after that."

His voice became hollow.

"I pledged myself to Palpatine."

He continued.

"I led the Five Hundred and First Legion into the Jedi Temple."

Padmé remained perfectly still.

"I came to Mustafar."

"I executed the Separatist Council."

Finally he reached the end.

"And then..."

He looked down.

"I fought Obi-Wan."

"I killed him."

Silence filled the room.

Padmé never interrupted once.

When he finally stopped speaking, she asked only one question.

"Do you regret any of it?"

He answered after a long pause.

"If the children are born..."

He looked directly at her.

"...and you survive..."

Another pause.

"...then I will regret everything."

Padmé studied him.

She understood immediately.

Even now, everything still revolved around her survival.

The deeper conversation would have to wait.

"We'll talk about that after the babies are born."

Anakin nodded.

"All right."

The following day, Padmé's labor began.

Doctors and nurses quickly gathered.

Anakin immediately became restless.

He paced endlessly around the room.

"How much longer?"

One doctor answered calmly.

"We're making excellent progress."

Five minutes later Anakin asked another doctor.

"Is everything all right?"

"Everything is proceeding normally."

He resumed pacing.

"They should be here by now..."

He began muttering to himself.

"They have to be all right... both of them... Padmé has to be all right..."

Padmé watched him for several minutes before sighing.

"Doctor."

The lead physician approached.

"Yes, Senator?"

Padmé glanced toward Anakin, who had resumed pacing while mumbling anxiously to himself.

"Could you administer a sedative?"

The doctor blinked.

"To... Mr. Skywalker?"

"Yes."

Anakin looked over.

"What?"

"You're not helping."

"I am helping."

"No."

Padmé smiled apologetically.

"You're pacing, muttering, and making everyone more nervous."

"I—"

Before he could continue, the doctor gently injected a fast-acting sedative.

Within moments Anakin's eyelids became heavy.

"I don't... think..."

He yawned.

"...that was..."

He slumped peacefully into a nearby medical bed.

"...fair..."

Seconds later he was asleep.

The delivery room became considerably quieter.

The doctors resumed their work.

The labor proceeded smoothly.

Several hours later, the first cry echoed through the room.

"A healthy boy," one doctor announced.

Only minutes afterward came another cry.

"And a healthy girl."

Padmé smiled through exhausted tears.

The lead physician completed his final examinations.

"Mother is perfectly healthy."

Everything had gone exactly as hoped.

Much later, Anakin slowly opened his eyes.

He blinked several times.

The room was quiet.

Padmé sat comfortably nearby, holding two infants.

She smiled.

"You're awake."

Anakin sat upright immediately.

"Padmé!"

He looked her over frantically.

"You're..."

"I'm perfectly fine."

He looked at the children.

"...They're..."

"They're perfectly healthy too."

The tension that had gripped him for so long simply vanished.

He let out one long breath.

Relief flooded across his face.

After several moments, he quietly said,

"I regret everything."

Padmé looked at him.

"Everything?"

He nodded.

"I was wrong."

Silence followed.

Finally he asked,

"How do I make up for everything I've done?"

Padmé answered without hesitation.

"You listen to me."

He waited.

"And you follow my instructions from now on."

"You have a plan?"

"I do."

"What is it?"

"I'm not telling you yet."

He nodded slowly.

"I'll do it."

She looked at him carefully.

"No."

He frowned.

"No?"

"I need more than that."

She met his eyes.

"I want your promise."

"What promise?"

"That you will unconditionally listen to me and follow my commands."

Anakin hesitated.

She noticed.

"If you truly want to redeem yourself," she said quietly, "this is part of the deal."

He remained silent for nearly a minute.

Finally he nodded.

"...I promise."

Padmé accepted the answer.

"Good."

She looked down at the sleeping twins.

"The first instruction is simple."

Anakin listened carefully.

"We're giving temporary custody of Luke and Leia to Sola."

He blinked.

"What?"

"They'll be safer with her."

"Why?"

"Because you and I are going to undertake some high-risk operations."

"What operations?"

"I'm not telling you yet."

Anakin frowned.

"I don't like this."

Padmé looked at him.

"You made a promise."

He closed his eyes briefly.

When he opened them again, he nodded.

"...I did."

A short time later, after receiving medical clearance, the family departed the medical facility.

The skiff carried them through the familiar streets of Theed toward the Naberrie residence.

Sola Naberrie opened the door.

"Padmé!"

She embraced her sister before noticing the infants.

Her eyes widened.

"You had them!"

Ryoo and Pooja rushed over excitedly.

"Babies!"

"They're so tiny!"

Padmé smiled.

"Meet Luke."

She gently introduced the boy.

"And this is Leia."

The two girls looked absolutely delighted.

"They're beautiful," Ryoo whispered.

Sola looked at Padmé.

"What do you need?"

Padmé answered honestly.

"I need you to take care of them for a while."

Sola became serious.

"For how long?"

"I don't know."

"Why?"

"There are things Anakin and I need to do."

"They're dangerous."

"I can't explain more right now."

Sola looked from Padmé to Anakin.

After a few moments she nodded.

"I'll take care of them."

Padmé smiled warmly.

"Thank you."

"You don't have to thank me."

Sola gently accepted the twins into her arms.

"I'll protect them as though they were my own."

Ryoo and Pooja could barely contain their excitement.

"Can we help?"

"Can we hold them?"

Sola laughed softly.

"In time."

Padmé watched her children one last time before turning away.

She trusted her sister completely.

Later that day, Anakin, Padmé, R2-D2, and C-3PO boarded the Naboo skiff once again.

They left Theed behind and flew across Naboo's peaceful countryside.

As evening approached, the elegant lakeside villa at Varykino came into view.

The skiff settled gently onto its landing platform.

The familiar estate, quiet and secluded, became their new refuge as the next stage of Padmé's plans quietly began.

# Part 3

Far above Coruscant, warships began assembling in disciplined formation.

One after another, Star Destroyers and supporting vessels took up positions over the Galactic Senate Building until an imposing fleet hung over the capital.

Inside the command bridge of one of the lead warships, Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin stood before a holographic projection of the Senate district.

An officer approached.

"The fleet is in position, Grand Moff."

Tarkin nodded.

"Have all captains acknowledge my orders."

"They are awaiting your command."

Tarkin looked at the Senate Building below.

"If I issue the order, every vessel will fire upon this building simultaneously."

The officer hesitated only briefly.

"Understood."

Tarkin clasped his hands behind his back.

"No one is to fire unless I personally give the command."

"Yes, Grand Moff."

Meanwhile, inside the Senate Building itself, the emergency special session had begun.

Every available senator had gathered.

The atmosphere was tense long before the proceedings officially started.

Mas Amedda stepped to the central podium.

He surveyed the chamber before speaking.

"My fellow senators."

His amplified voice echoed throughout the immense hall.

"We gather today under tragic circumstances."

He lowered his head respectfully.

"Our beloved Emperor Palpatine has fallen."

A murmur spread through the chamber.

"For many years he guided this galaxy through war and uncertainty."

He paused.

"We mourn his passing."

Many senators bowed their heads.

Others remained expressionless.

Mas Amedda continued.

"The Empire must now move forward with stable leadership."

He straightened his robes.

"It therefore falls upon—"

A voice interrupted him.

"No."

Hundreds of heads turned.

Grand Moff Tarkin stood from his Senate pod.

He spoke loudly enough for the entire chamber to hear.

"I have a fleet assembled above this building right now."

The chamber fell silent.

"If the office of Emperor goes to anybody except myself, I have the authority to order them to fire on this building at any moment."

Gasps echoed around the Senate.

Tarkin's face remained cold.

"If I can't be Emperor, nobody else shall."

His eyes swept across the assembled senators.

"And I am not afraid to show my power through fear."

The chamber erupted.

Senators shouted over one another.

"This is madness!"

"You cannot threaten the Senate!"

"He has a fleet!"

"We have families!"

"Remain calm!"

Mas Amedda slammed both hands against the podium.

"This is outrageous!"

Several senators immediately turned toward Tarkin.

"He controls the military!"

"If he gives the order, we're all dead!"

Another senator shouted back.

"So we're simply supposed to surrender to extortion?"

The arguments multiplied across every section of the chamber.

One senator yelled,

"We should accept Tarkin's claim before anyone dies!"

Another immediately answered,

"If fear decides our government, then government has already failed!"

Amid the chaos, Mon Mothma activated her podium.

Her calm voice gradually cut through the shouting.

"Senators."

Many turned toward her.

"I reject both of these claims."

The chamber quieted slightly.

"Neither threats nor political maneuvering should determine the future of this galaxy."

She looked toward both Mas Amedda and Tarkin.

"The Republic should be restored."

Some senators applauded.

Others shook their heads.

Mon continued.

"We should not replace one claimant with another."

She looked around the chamber.

"Had Senator Amidala and Senator Organa been able to attend this session in person, I believe they would stand with me in supporting the restoration of the Republic."

The chamber immediately descended back into competing arguments.

Outside the Senate Building, the fleet remained motionless.

Its weapons stayed trained on the structure below.

Far away on Naboo, inside the villa at Varykino, Padmé Amidala and Anakin Skywalker watched the Senate proceedings through a HoloNet transmission.

Neither spoke during Mas Amedda's address.

Neither interrupted Tarkin's threat.

They watched as the Senate dissolved into complete disorder.

Padmé deactivated the transmission.

She turned toward Anakin.

"Anakin."

He looked at her.

"I need you to go to Coruscant and claim yourself as the new Emperor."

He blinked.

"Myself?"

"Officially, you are still Chancellor Palpatine's apprentice."

She continued thoughtfully.

"If you claim that title, you should be able to challenge the position of legitimate successor."

Anakin frowned.

"Tarkin and Mas Amedda would never agree to me being the successor."

Padmé answered immediately.

"Then make them."

He looked uncertain.

"By any means necessary."

He studied her face.

"And if I have to kill them?"

"Then do it."

He stared in surprise.

"But... why?"

He shook his head.

"Why would you want me, of all people, in power?"

He looked away.

"After everything I've done..."

His voice became quieter.

"Wouldn't chasing more power be exactly the wrong thing if I'm trying to redeem myself?"

Padmé met his eyes.

"Because of your promise."

"My promise?"

"You promised to follow my instructions."

"I did."

"If you're Emperor," she said, "then in practice you're allowing me to be the one guiding that power."

Anakin frowned.

"I thought you opposed authoritarianism."

"I do."

She answered without hesitation.

"But these are not ordinary times."

She walked toward the window overlooking Naboo's lakes.

"I hate authoritarianism."

She folded her arms.

"But authoritarian rule is already the reality."

She turned back toward him.

"I have my principles."

She paused.

"I'm also pragmatic enough to understand that sometimes we have to do uncomfortable things to reach a better outcome."

Anakin considered her words.

"So you're saying..."

Padmé finished the thought.

"If democracy is already gone, better to have me directing the dictator than Tarkin."

Silence filled the room.

Finally Anakin nodded.

"Got it."

Padmé smiled slightly.

"Oh."

"There is one more thing."

"I'm coming with you."

He looked alarmed.

"What?"

"I'm coming."

"It's not safe."

He gestured around them.

"Here it's safe."

"You should stay here."

Padmé raised an eyebrow.

"Do you not remember that I'm good with a blaster?"

He smiled despite himself.

"Do you still remember Geonosis?"

He gave a reluctant nod.

"Fair."

"But this isn't ordinary combat."

"I know."

She smiled.

"That isn't even the main reason."

"Then why?"

"Because I have plans for after you've become Emperor."

"What plans?"

"I can't tell you yet."

He sighed.

"Are you sure there isn't another way?"

She gave him a patient look.

"Anakin."

He already knew what she was about to say.

"Remember your promise."

He closed his eyes briefly.

When he opened them again, he nodded.

"I remember."

Before leaving Varykino, Padmé activated her comlink.

Two holograms appeared.

One was Mon Mothma, still seated inside the increasingly chaotic Senate chamber.

The other was Bail Organa.

His surroundings were quiet.

Behind him, flames rose into the sky.

On Alderaan, Obi-Wan Kenobi's funeral had just begun.

His funeral pyre burned beneath the open sky.

Yoda stood nearby with Bail Organa and Queen Breha Organa.

The three observed the ceremony in respectful silence while Yoda had spoken solemn words honoring Obi-Wan's life and service.

Now only the crackling of the fire remained.

Bail's comlink buzzed.

He answered immediately.

"Padmé."

Mon Mothma also looked toward her hologram.

Padmé spoke without delay.

"Listen, Bail and Mon."

She took a breath.

"I know you might not like this."

She glanced toward Anakin.

"I'm setting Anakin up to become the new Emperor."

Mon's eyes widened.

"What?"

She immediately tilted her device so everyone could see the Senate chamber behind her.

The session had become complete disorder.

Senators shouted over one another while Tarkin and Mas Amedda glared across the chamber.

"With the way Tarkin and Mas Amedda are going at each other right now," Mon said, "there's no way either of them will accept Anakin as Emperor."

Bail looked deeply troubled.

"I... would advise caution."

He looked toward Anakin's hologram.

"Padmé... Anakin may not be a great Emperor."

Before Padmé could answer, Yoda stepped beside Bail so that he too appeared within the hologram.

His expression was grave.

"Killed Obi-Wan Kenobi, Anakin has."

Anakin lowered his eyes.

"Fallen to the dark side, he has."

Yoda shook his head.

"A suitable ruler of the galaxy, he is not."

He spoke even more firmly.

"Absolutely not."

He looked directly at Padmé.

"Senator Amidala, this decision, I will not approve."

Padmé stepped closer to the hologram.

"Master Yoda."

Her voice remained calm.

"Please listen to me."

She looked briefly toward Anakin before returning her attention to Yoda.

"I know what Anakin has done."

"He has told me everything."

"Every single thing."

She nodded slowly.

"Yes."

"What he did was terrible."

There was no hesitation in her admission.

"But he is now fully under my control."

Anakin quietly listened beside her.

"I promise you."

"Nothing bad will happen once he's Emperor."

She spoke more softly.

"Please don't fight him."

"Please don't go to Coruscant and duel him."

She looked directly into Yoda's eyes.

"Trust my judgment this time."

"I beg you, Master Yoda."

Yoda closed his eyes.

The silence lasted several long seconds.

At last he opened them again.

"Hmm."

Another thoughtful pause followed.

"Very well then, Senator Amidala."

He inclined his head slightly.

"Ended, the Jedi Order already has."

His voice became quieter.

"Defer to your judgment, I shall."

Relief appeared on Padmé's face.

"Thank you, Master Yoda."

Yoda gave a small nod.

The communication ended.

The funeral pyre continued burning beneath Alderaan's sky.

Back on Naboo, Padmé looked toward Anakin.

"Everything is ready."

He nodded.

Without another word, he, Padmé, R2-D2, and C-3PO boarded the Naboo skiff.

The engines came to life.

The vessel lifted away from the peaceful lakes of Varykino and climbed into the evening sky.

Moments later, it entered hyperspace on a direct course for Coruscant, where the struggle for control of the Empire was reaching its decisive moment.

# Part 4

The Naboo skiff descended through the crowded skies of Coruscant and settled on a landing platform just outside the Senate Building.

Inside, the emergency session had descended even further into disorder.

Arguments echoed from every direction.

Some senators continued urging support for Mas Amedda.

Others insisted Tarkin's military strength made resistance impossible.

Still others demanded the restoration of the Republic.

The chamber was so loud that many senators could no longer hear those seated only a few platforms away.

Then a new voice cut through the chaos.

"I shall be the new Emperor."

The declaration rang across the entire chamber.

Every conversation stopped.

Hundreds of eyes turned toward the center of the Senate.

Anakin Skywalker had entered the chamber, vaulted high through the air with the aid of the Force, and landed upon an unoccupied central Senate pod.

He stood upright with complete confidence.

Mas Amedda stared down at him.

"And what is your claim, Skywalker?"

Anakin answered without hesitation.

"Emperor Palpatine was the Sith Lord Darth Sidious."

A ripple of surprise spread through the chamber.

"I am Darth Vader, his Sith apprentice."

More murmurs followed.

"Under Sith succession laws, I am now the new Sith Master."

He looked directly at Mas Amedda.

"Thus I shall also inherit the throne from him."

Mas Amedda shook his head.

"The Sith and the Empire are separate institutions."

He raised one hand.

"You may inherit the title of 'Sith Master' upon his death, but not that of Emperor."

Without another word, Anakin ignited his blue lightsaber.

Its familiar hum filled the suddenly silent chamber.

Gasps echoed throughout the Senate.

Security personnel stationed around the room immediately drew their blasters.

Anakin remained perfectly still.

"Grand Vizier Amedda."

His voice carried throughout the chamber.

"I am giving you one last chance."

He pointed the lightsaber toward him.

"Support me as your new Emperor..."

A brief pause followed.

"...or face certain doom."

Mas Amedda stood his ground.

"No."

He spoke firmly.

"I shall not give up my claim."

Anakin nodded once.

"Very well then."

With a powerful leap, he crossed the vast distance between their platforms in an instant.

He landed directly before Mas Amedda.

Before anyone could react, his lightsaber flashed once.

Mas Amedda collapsed, mortally wounded, and fell from the platform into the depths below.

A horrified gasp swept across the Senate.

For several long seconds, no one moved.

Then, amid the stunned silence, Tarkin subtly raised a comlink to his mouth.

He spoke quietly enough that only the device could hear.

"I am leaving the Senate chamber."

On the bridge of the command Star Destroyer above Coruscant, a captain answered immediately.

"Yes, sir."

Tarkin continued.

"As soon as I'm far enough away from the building, I will give the order to fire."

"We'll be ready."

The transmission ended.

Tarkin turned toward one of the chamber exits.

He had taken only a few steps when another figure dropped directly into his path.

Anakin had crossed the chamber again in a single leap.

He stood between Tarkin and the exit.

"Grand Moff Tarkin."

Anakin's voice was calm.

"Where are you going?"

Tarkin met his gaze.

"I'm going to destroy the Senate."

The surrounding senators froze.

"You shall not be Emperor."

Tarkin's expression hardened.

"Only I shall be the Emperor."

Anakin regarded him for a moment.

"Very brave of you to say this after watching what I did to Amedda."

Tarkin drew a blaster with practiced speed.

He fired.

The bolt never reached its target.

Anakin effortlessly deflected it aside with his lightsaber.

He lowered the blade slightly.

"All right."

"I understand."

"You want to fight me."

Anakin surged forward with another Force-assisted leap.

The confrontation lasted only an instant.

When it was over, Tarkin lay dead upon the Senate platform.

His comlink clattered across the floor.

On the Star Destroyer overhead, officers waited for an order that never came.

Minutes passed.

Then more.

Finally one officer frowned.

"The Grand Moff has gone silent."

Back inside the Senate chamber, Anakin returned to the central platform.

He extinguished his lightsaber.

His voice echoed through the vast hall.

"Does anybody here still dare challenge my claim to be the new Emperor?"

No one answered.

The chamber had become utterly silent.

Anakin slowly looked around the Senate.

"Very well then."

He stood at the center of the chamber.

"From now on, I shall be known as Emperor Skywalker, the second Emperor of the First Galactic Empire."

For a heartbeat, the room remained motionless.

Then applause broke out.

It spread rapidly from platform to platform until the entire Senate chamber thundered with applause.

At that very moment, Anakin's comlink vibrated.

A secure message from Padmé appeared.

It contained only one instruction.

"Read exactly what this message says right now."

Anakin opened it.

Without altering a single word, he began reading aloud.

"I have claimed the office of Emperor as the rightful heir of Palpatine by means of being his Sith apprentice."

The applause gradually quieted.

"However, I am and have always been a general, not a politician."

Many senators exchanged curious looks.

"Therefore, in recognition of my own shortcomings in the areas of politics and governance, I hereby legally transfer the office of Emperor to Senator Padmé Amidala of Naboo."

For a brief moment, stunned silence returned.

Then an even greater wave of applause erupted.

At the entrance to the chamber, Padmé Amidala appeared.

She walked steadily toward the central platform.

Anakin stepped aside, allowing her to take the foremost position before the Senate.

She surveyed the chamber before speaking.

"My fellow senators."

The applause gradually subsided.

"It is a great honor to be your newest Emperor."

She smiled slightly.

"Or shall I say..."

Another small smile.

"...Empress."

Warm laughter and applause answered her.

She continued.

"War has torn our galaxy apart for three years."

Her voice became more solemn.

"And now we finally have peace and stability again."

She paused.

"With the return of peace, I propose a return to normalcy."

The chamber grew attentive.

"The emergency powers accumulated in the executive office during the war are no longer required to maintain the peace."

She looked across the assembled senators.

"And so, under my guidance, they shall be gradually removed."

Applause swept through the Senate once more.

When it settled, Padmé continued.

"I beg each and every one of you to place your trust in me."

She looked around the chamber she had served in for years.

"I understand that not all of you agree with my political views."

She nodded respectfully.

"And not all of you believe I am fit to rule the galaxy."

Her voice remained steady.

"Yet I have fought for Naboo and for our galaxy for the past nine years as a senator in this very chamber."

She placed one hand over her heart.

"I am dedicated to continuing that fight."

She smiled warmly.

"Please allow me some time."

"I promise that I will do everything in my power to ensure our galaxy emerges better than before."

The Senate answered with another prolonged standing ovation.

Even many who remained uncertain about the day's unprecedented events applauded the hope offered in her words.

With no further business to conduct, the emergency session concluded.

Later that day, Anakin and Padmé returned to their apartment on Coruscant.

The city stretched endlessly outside the windows.

For the first time since arriving, the apartment was quiet.

Anakin removed his cloak and looked toward Padmé.

"Now what?"

She sat down calmly.

"Now you will become my personal bodyguard."

He looked surprised.

"Bodyguard?"

She nodded.

"I've likely made a great many political enemies today."

She folded her hands.

"Some of them may decide that removing me by force or arranging my assassination is easier than opposing me politically."

She looked directly at him.

"I need you here."

"I need you protecting me."

"I need you making sure that never happens."

Anakin answered immediately.

"Of course."

His expression softened.

"The only thing I want..."

He smiled gently.

"...is for you to be safe and healthy."

Padmé smiled back.

"Then we're in agreement."

From that day forward, their relationship entered a new phase.

Padmé assumed the responsibilities of governing the Empire, while Anakin remained constantly at her side, serving as her personal protector under the promise he had made to follow her guidance.

# Part 5

Three years passed.

The years immediately following Padmé Amidala's accession to the imperial throne proved unlike any period the galaxy had experienced in living memory.

Many senators had initially feared that the concentration of power in a single ruler would only deepen the authoritarian system established during the Clone Wars. Instead, they watched as Padmé steadily began dismantling it.

She rarely acted alone.

Senator Bail Organa, Senator Mon Mothma, and many other experienced legislators met with her regularly inside the Imperial Palace on Coruscant.

During one such meeting, Bail unfolded a series of legislative proposals across a conference table.

"If we repeal these emergency decrees," he explained, "the Senate will recover much of its legislative authority."

Mon Mothma nodded.

"And independent courts should be restored as well."

Padmé read through the documents.

"How quickly can this be done?"

"Not all at once," Bail replied. "Too sudden a change could create instability."

Mon added, "If we proceed carefully, the institutions themselves will become strong enough that they won't depend upon any one person."

Padmé smiled.

"Then that's what we'll do."

Year after year, emergency powers disappeared.

Military authority returned to civilian oversight.

Regional governments regained increasing autonomy.

Emergency wartime legislation quietly expired without renewal.

The Senate once again became a place where debate mattered.

Not everyone welcomed these changes.

A handful of ambitious officials who had prospered under Palpatine's rule attempted repeatedly to halt Padmé's reforms.

Some tried political obstruction.

Others sought darker methods.

On more than one occasion, assassination attempts were uncovered before they could succeed.

Each time, Anakin Skywalker stood between the danger and Padmé.

One evening, after security forces had arrested another would-be assassin, Padmé found Anakin standing outside her office.

"You knew," she said.

He nodded.

"I sensed him before he drew the weapon."

She looked at the captured attacker being escorted away.

"You saved my life again."

Anakin shrugged slightly.

"I told you."

"The only thing I want..."

He smiled.

"...is for you to be safe."

Across the galaxy, ordinary citizens watched these years unfold with growing optimism.

Trade routes became safer.

Reconstruction projects expanded.

The fear that had defined the years of the Clone Wars gradually faded.

Many who had once viewed Padmé simply as a respected senator now came to admire her as a wise ruler.

Others became fiercely protective of her personally, believing that her leadership had brought stability where endless conflict had once existed.

By the end of the third year, Padmé believed the work was finally complete.

The institutions of representative government had been rebuilt.

The emergency powers that had once justified imperial rule had largely disappeared.

Only one final step remained.

Another special session of the Galactic Senate was called.

Senators from across the galaxy filled the familiar chamber.

Padmé stood at the central podium one final time as Empress.

"My fellow senators."

The chamber quieted.

"Three years ago, I accepted the office of Emperor during one of the most dangerous moments our galaxy had ever faced."

She looked around the vast hall.

"At that time, I believed stability had to come before anything else."

She smiled gently.

"Today, stability has returned."

Applause echoed briefly before fading.

She continued.

"The institutions of representative government have been restored."

"Our worlds are rebuilding."

"Our citizens are no longer living under the constant shadow of war."

She took a slow breath.

"Therefore..."

A pause filled the chamber.

"...the Galactic Empire has come to an end."

The Senate erupted into applause.

"The Galactic Republic shall be reinstated effective immediately."

The applause became even louder.

Padmé waited patiently until the chamber settled again.

"There is one final matter."

She smiled warmly.

"The Republic requires a Chancellor."

She looked around the chamber.

"I therefore call for an immediate election."

Several senators applauded.

Padmé then surprised many of them.

"I have ruled for long enough."

She shook her head.

"I will not be a candidate."

A murmur spread across the Senate.

"My duty was to guide our galaxy back to representative government."

She looked around proudly.

"That duty is complete."

The Senate immediately began the election process.

After speeches, nominations, and voting, one name emerged with overwhelming support.

Bail Organa.

The presiding officer announced the result.

"By decision of the Galactic Senate, Bail Prestor Organa is hereby elected the first Chancellor of the restored Galactic Republic."

The chamber rose in unanimous applause.

Bail approached the central podium.

He first turned toward Padmé.

"You've given us back our Republic."

Padmé smiled.

"Now keep it."

Bail nodded solemnly.

"I intend to."

With the formal transfer of authority complete, Padmé Amidala stepped away from galactic government.

Her public service had lasted sixteen years, from her election as Queen of Naboo to her final day as ruler of the galaxy.

Soon afterward, she and Anakin boarded their ship for the journey home.

Not to Coruscant.

To Naboo.

Their first destination was the Naberrie family home in Theed.

Sola opened the door with a smile.

"You made it."

Before another word could be spoken, two small children came racing through the house.

"Mom!"

"Dad!"

Luke and Leia, now three years old, threw themselves into their parents' arms.

Anakin laughed as he lifted Luke into the air.

"You've gotten heavy."

"I'm big now!" Luke declared proudly.

Leia wrapped both arms around Padmé.

"We missed you."

Padmé held her daughter close.

"I missed you too."

Ryoo and Pooja soon joined them.

Although older now, they remained just as fond of their younger cousins.

The family spent the afternoon together.

As evening approached, Sola looked toward the packed travel bags.

"So..."

She smiled sadly.

"I suppose this day finally arrived."

Padmé nodded.

"It did."

Sola looked toward the twins.

"I'm going to miss having them here every day."

Ryoo hugged Leia.

"We're going to miss you."

Pooja hugged Luke.

"You have to come visit."

"We will," Luke promised with all the confidence only a three-year-old could possess.

Padmé smiled.

"We all still live on Naboo."

She placed a reassuring hand on Sola's shoulder.

"This isn't goodbye."

"We'll visit often."

Sola smiled again.

"I know."

She embraced her sister tightly.

"Take good care of them."

"I will."

Later that day, Anakin, Padmé, Luke, Leia, R2-D2, and C-3PO departed Theed together.

Their journey across Naboo was a short one.

The familiar villa at Varykino appeared beside the lake, peaceful as ever.

This time there would be no secret meetings.

No political crises.

No hurried departures.

They unpacked their belongings.

The villa finally became what it had always been meant to be.

A home.

Several evenings later, after Luke and Leia had fallen asleep, Padmé and Anakin sat together outside overlooking the lake.

The water reflected the evening sky.

Padmé broke the comfortable silence.

"Anakin."

He looked over.

"Yes?"

"I have something to tell you."

He listened quietly.

"Your phase of unconditionally following my commands..."

She smiled.

"...is over."

He looked surprised.

"It is?"

She nodded.

"We can have a normal relationship again."

He smiled back.

"I'd like that."

After another quiet moment, she asked,

"Do you understand now why I asked you to make that promise?"

Anakin watched the lake for a long time before answering.

"I do."

He nodded slowly.

"At the time..."

He searched for the right words.

"...I wasn't thinking clearly."

"I was still willing to do almost anything if I believed it would protect you."

He looked back at her.

"If you hadn't guided me..."

He shook his head.

"I don't know where I would have ended up."

He smiled gently.

"It was necessary."

Padmé returned the smile.

"I'm glad you understand."

The two sat together in silence as the sun disappeared beyond the horizon.

Looking back upon those turbulent years, Padmé knew there were wounds that could never truly heal.

Obi-Wan Kenobi was gone.

The Jedi Order had been all but destroyed.

Countless lives had been lost during the Clone Wars and the violence that followed.

Those tragedies could never be undone.

Yet the Republic had been restored.

Representative government had returned.

The galaxy had emerged into an era more peaceful and more stable than the one that had preceded the war.

As for Anakin, the transformation that had begun with the birth of Luke and Leia had endured.

Once Padmé had survived childbirth, the fear that had driven him into darkness lost its hold over him.

From that day onward, he no longer identified himself as a Sith.

With Darth Sidious dead and no successor carrying on the Sith tradition, the Sith lineage came to its end.

The Sith became extinct.

The Jedi Order, too, had been reduced to only a handful of survivors scattered across the galaxy.

Their ancient order had nearly vanished.

But for the first time in generations, the long struggle between Jedi and Sith had ended.

At Varykino, far from the halls of power, Anakin and Padmé devoted themselves to raising Luke and Leia.

The droids remained with the family as they always had.

The villa filled with laughter instead of strategy, with children's games instead of political debates.

For the Skywalker family, the years of war and upheaval were finally over.
