# Part 1

The volcanic world of Mustafar roared beneath a blackened sky.

Rivers of molten rock carved glowing paths through the shattered landscape while ash drifted through the air. On a narrow bank overlooking the lava, two Jedi who had once fought side by side stood facing each other with ignited lightsabers.

Anakin Skywalker breathed heavily, his eyes burning with fury.

"I have brought peace, freedom, justice, and security to my new Empire."

Obi-Wan Kenobi's face was filled with grief.

"Your new Empire?"

"Don't make me kill you."

"My allegiance is to the Republic, to democracy!"

"If you're not with me, then you're my enemy."

Obi-Wan lowered his stance.

"Only a Sith deals in absolutes. I will do what I must."

"You will try."

Their duel had already scarred the mining facility behind them and carried them across rivers of lava until both men stood on separate floating platforms. Obi-Wan leapt from his platform to the black volcanic bank, gaining stable footing. Anakin's platform drifted lower in the lava river.

Obi-Wan looked down at him.

"It's over, Anakin! I have the high ground!"

"You underestimate my power!"

Anakin launched himself upward in a powerful leap, lightsaber raised overhead.

Obi-Wan reacted instantly.

His blue blade flashed in a single, perfectly timed arc.

The strike intercepted Anakin in midair.

The lightsaber cut cleanly through Anakin's remaining organic limbs in one devastating motion. His left arm and both legs separated at once, leaving only his mechanical right arm attached to his body.

Anakin cried out in agony as he crashed onto the black volcanic rock. His blue lightsaber spun away from his mechanical hand, clattering across the ground several meters away.

Obi-Wan landed and stared for only the briefest moment.

The horror of what had happened overtook every other thought.

"No..."

He deactivated his lightsaber and rushed forward.

Anakin struggled to breathe, shock rapidly overtaking the pain. His body trembled violently as blood loss and trauma began to overwhelm him.

Obi-Wan knelt beside him.

"Anakin! Stay with me!"

Anakin's eyes opened only halfway. He stared at his former master in disbelief.

"You..."

His voice was barely audible.

"You... didn't..."

"I'm not leaving you here," Obi-Wan said firmly.

Carefully lifting what remained of Anakin's body into his arms, Obi-Wan stood despite the weight and the danger surrounding them.

Before leaving, he spotted Anakin's fallen lightsaber lying on the volcanic stone.

He walked over, retrieved it, and clipped it securely beside his own on his belt.

Then he began carrying Anakin back toward the Naboo skiff.

Each step was difficult across the uneven terrain.

Anakin remained conscious only in fragments.

He expected another blow.

He expected abandonment.

Instead he found himself supported by the very man he had tried to kill.

His breathing became slower.

"...Master..."

The word escaped almost involuntarily.

Obi-Wan glanced down.

"I'm here."

Anakin closed his eyes again. The hatred that had consumed him only moments earlier gave way, if only slightly, to confusion and reluctant gratitude. He no longer had the strength—or even the desire—to strike at Obi-Wan as he was carried away.

The tremendous trauma from losing three limbs quickly overcame him.

Before they reached the landing area, Anakin lost consciousness completely.

Moments later Obi-Wan approached the waiting Naboo skiff.

R2-D2 rolled toward him, emitting a rapid series of confused whistles and beeps as the astromech saw the unconscious, limbless Anakin being carried in Obi-Wan's arms.

Obi-Wan shook his head.

"I know, Artoo. There's no time to explain. I'll tell you later."

The little droid let out another worried chirp before following him aboard.

Inside the skiff, C-3PO had already helped the unconscious Padmé Amidala onto one of the medical cots.

"Oh, dear," Threepio exclaimed as Obi-Wan entered. "Master Anakin!"

Obi-Wan gently laid Anakin onto the neighboring medical cot, positioning him as carefully as possible.

"We need to get to Polis Massa immediately," Obi-Wan said. "His injuries are critical."

"Right away, sir," said Threepio.

Obi-Wan gave Anakin one last look before heading toward the cockpit.

As he reached the front of the vessel, Padmé stirred.

Her eyes slowly opened.

She looked around weakly before spotting Obi-Wan.

"Obi-Wan..."

"I'm here."

She struggled to speak.

"Is Anakin... alright?"

Obi-Wan's expression answered before his words did.

"No," he admitted quietly. "But he's still alive."

Relief mixed instantly with fear.

"Where is he?"

Obi-Wan looked toward the rear compartment.

"Look beside you."

Padmé slowly turned her head.

Her eyes fell upon Anakin lying motionless on the neighboring cot.

His robes had been hastily wrapped around the terrible wounds where his left arm and both legs had once been. His face was pale, his breathing shallow.

She gasped.

"No..."

The shock overwhelmed her weakened body.

Her eyes rolled shut, and she slipped back into unconsciousness.

Threepio looked helplessly between the two patients.

"Oh dear... oh my..."

Obi-Wan placed a hand on the protocol droid's shoulder.

"We have to keep flying."

Together they entered the cockpit.

R2-D2 connected himself to the skiff's systems, letting out determined beeps as the engines powered to full thrust.

The small vessel lifted away from the black shores of Mustafar and disappeared into the sky, setting a course for Polis Massa.

Some time later another shuttle descended through Mustafar's smoky atmosphere.

Supreme Chancellor Palpatine—now openly Darth Sidious—emerged with his crimson guards and began searching the battlefield.

His sharp eyes scanned the volcanic banks.

He found scorched stone.

He found evidence of a fierce duel.

He found blood.

But Anakin Skywalker was nowhere to be seen.

Sidious searched the surrounding area, his frustration growing with every passing minute.

"Lord Vader!" he called into the smoke.

Only the distant rumble of the volcano answered him.

His guards searched nearby landing sites and ruined structures, but no trace of Anakin remained.

At last Sidious's expression hardened.

"They have taken him."

With nothing more to find on Mustafar, he returned to his shuttle.

The vessel rose into the dark sky and departed the volcanic world, setting course for Coruscant as the Naboo skiff carrying Obi-Wan, Padmé, Anakin, R2-D2, and C-3PO sped through hyperspace toward Polis Massa.

# Part 2

The journey from Mustafar to Polis Massa was quiet except for the steady hum of the Naboo skiff's engines.

Padmé Amidala and Anakin Skywalker remained unconscious for much of the flight while Obi-Wan Kenobi stayed in the cockpit with C-3PO and R2-D2, urging the vessel onward as quickly as possible.

Eventually, Anakin stirred.

His eyes slowly opened, and he looked down instinctively. His body felt strangely light, and a wave of pain reminded him of what had happened on Mustafar. He turned his head and saw Padmé lying on the neighboring medical cot.

A moment later, she awoke as well.

Their eyes met.

Anakin spoke first, his voice filled with regret.

"I'm sorry, Padmé. I should not have choked you."

She managed a faint smile despite her exhaustion.

"Save your strength, Anakin. You are hurt much more badly than I am."

He closed his eyes briefly.

"What have I done? I tried to kill Obi-Wan... and instead I was almost killed by him..."

"Obi-Wan did what he had to," Padmé replied gently. "I'm glad he didn't kill you."

Anakin stared at the ceiling.

"He could have. Instead, he decided to save me."

"Because he's a Jedi, Anakin. That's what Jedi do."

Silence lingered between them before Anakin finally answered.

"...It takes being on the receiving end to understand why the Jedi way is the way it is."

Padmé looked at him thoughtfully.

"And what do you think the Jedi way is now?"

He hesitated.

"...I shouldn't have betrayed the Jedi."

He swallowed.

"But I had to. Only the dark side knows how to save you from dying in childbirth."

Padmé looked directly at him.

"Look at yourself. Maybe you have more pressing concerns than that now."

"What more pressing concerns?" Anakin asked immediately. "I can die, but you must survive."

"Calm down. I'll be fine. I told you this many times by now, but you never listen."

"Because you don't experience Force visions and I do!"

Padmé sighed.

"Alright. Just save your strength. We can talk about this later."

Neither of them had the energy to continue.

After several minutes of silence, both drifted back into unconsciousness.

By the time the skiff reached Polis Massa, Yoda and Senator Bail Organa had already arrived and were waiting outside the medical facility.

The landing ramp lowered.

Obi-Wan emerged carrying the unconscious Padmé in his arms.

Behind him, C-3PO carefully carried the unconscious Anakin. The protocol droid walked as steadily as his joints allowed before reaching Bail Organa.

"If you would be so kind, Senator."

"Of course."

Bail gently accepted Anakin from Threepio's arms.

Together they hurried into the medical center.

Medical droids immediately separated the two patients into different treatment rooms.

Anakin regained consciousness before the examinations were complete.

He looked around frantically.

"Where's Padmé?"

A medical droid answered calmly.

"She is receiving treatment."

"I want to see her."

"That is not possible at this time."

"I said I want to see her."

"The procedure cannot be interrupted."

Anakin's breathing became faster.

"I need to see her."

"The answer remains no."

The room suddenly trembled.

Equipment rattled.

Small instruments slid across nearby trays as the Force answered Anakin's growing panic.

Obi-Wan stepped into the room.

"Anakin."

"I have to see her."

Obi-Wan studied him for a moment before nodding.

"If we don't let you, you'll keep escalating."

Anakin said nothing.

Obi-Wan unlocked the medical cot's repulsor system.

"Come on."

He wheeled Anakin through the corridors toward Padmé's room.

Inside, Padmé's labor had already begun.

Medical droids surrounded her bed while monitors displayed the condition of both mother and children.

Obi-Wan positioned Anakin's cot beside hers on the left so that Anakin's remaining mechanical right arm could reach across to her.

The sight before him stripped away every trace of composure.

Anakin began muttering under his breath.

"No... no... please... everything's going wrong... don't let anything happen... please..."

Padmé turned toward him.

"Anakin! You're not helping."

One of the medical droids glanced at its instruments.

"It appears that she is perfectly healthy. There is no need to worry."

Outside the delivery room, Yoda stood beside Obi-Wan after the latter quietly stepped into the hallway.

"Young Skywalker, we must watch," Yoda said softly. "If anything, he tries... be prepared to stop him, we must."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"I'm watching him. He'll hopefully be fine once the babies are here."

Inside the room, Padmé cried out as another contraction struck.

Anakin instinctively reached across with his mechanical hand and clamped tightly onto her left hand.

"Ow!" Padmé exclaimed. "Anakin, you are making it worse."

"I'm just holding your hand."

"You're about to squeeze my hand off."

Anakin immediately looked horrified.

"Sorry. I didn't realize."

Padmé forced a tired smile despite the pain.

"Gentle, please."

His grip loosened immediately.

"I've got you."

Hours seemed to pass, though in truth it was much less.

Finally, the cries of two newborn infants filled the room.

The medical staff smiled.

"The children have both been delivered successfully."

Padmé collapsed back onto the pillow, utterly exhausted but alive.

The twins were wrapped carefully in blankets.

Obi-Wan entered again.

He smiled with unmistakable relief.

"Congratulations."

Anakin looked from one infant to the other, tears forming in his eyes.

"They're... they're beautiful."

Obi-Wan looked toward him.

"Alright, Anakin. It's your turn now. We're taking you back to your room."

"No."

The answer came instantly.

"I need to stay here and make sure Padmé remains alive."

A medical droid approached.

"The longer you wait, the harder it will be to stabilize you and fit new prosthetics for you."

"You don't understand. I need to—"

Padmé interrupted him.

"Go."

Anakin looked at her helplessly.

"But—"

"I'm alive now," she said firmly. "And I'll still be alive when you're finished."

He still hesitated.

"Go."

Her tone became unmistakably commanding.

"GO."

Anakin finally lowered his head.

"...Alright."

Without another word, Obi-Wan wheeled him back toward the operating room.

The medical droids resumed their work.

Five minutes passed.

"Is Padmé alright?"

"Yes," one droid answered.

Five more minutes.

"Is Padmé still alright?"

"Yes."

Another five minutes.

"Is Padmé still alright?"

The medical droid paused before producing a sedative injector.

"I have never seen a patient like this before."

The sedative entered Anakin's bloodstream.

Within seconds his eyes closed.

He fell into a medically induced sleep.

Over the next several hours, the surgeons completed their work.

Anakin's original mechanical right arm was cleaned, repaired, and reattached securely. New prosthetic legs replaced the ones he had lost on Mustafar. A matching prosthetic left arm completed his reconstruction.

By the time he awoke again, he was physically stable.

His medical cot was rolled into the common recovery area.

A medical droid addressed the waiting group.

"We are keeping this patient mildly sedated so he can talk and think but would be too weak to get up from the bed."

Obi-Wan nodded approvingly.

"Good choice."

A few moments later another team rolled Padmé into the same recovery area.

She looked pale but alert.

Bail Organa followed behind them, holding the newborn twins wrapped securely in his arms.

The lead medical droid reported,

"She is a bit weak still, but she will be alright."

Anakin immediately relaxed.

"Good."

"In time she will fully recover."

"Excellent."

The droid hesitated.

"Oh, and also... we have sensed that her will to live has been diminished."

Anakin's eyes widened.

"Diminished?!"

"We do not know why. But hopefully that will can be fully restored... if things go well."

"And how do we know if things go well or not?"

"Unfortunately, that is beyond our medical expertise."

Padmé looked toward him.

"I know... Anakin, and you know too."

He frowned.

"Think about it."

"Think about what?"

"What you have done... what you have become... what the galaxy has become..."

Anakin looked down.

"But we have twins to raise. How can you..."

"Anakin," she said gently, "there are greater concerns in this galaxy than our family."

"I don't believe you. There is nothing more important..."

Yoda stepped closer.

"In time, understand, you will, young Skywalker."

Anakin looked up.

"Master Yoda."

The old Jedi studied him carefully.

"Still call me Master, you do?"

Anakin managed a faint smile.

"Perhaps I should not?"

"Your faith in the dark side is shattered, I sense."

"Why would you think so?"

"You joined the dark side to save your wife."

Anakin blinked.

"How do you know she's my wife?"

Across the room, Obi-Wan looked toward Bail Organa.

Bail looked back.

The two men stared at one another for a moment before simultaneously chuckling.

Yoda remained perfectly composed.

"And renounce the dark side, you will, when your wife survives."

Anakin let out a weary laugh.

"...Alright. No wonder you are the Grand Master."

He shook his head.

"You know exactly how I think, don't you?"

"Nine hundred years I have been around for," Yoda replied. "Many things I have seen. Perfect I am not. But experience I do have."

Anakin slowly nodded.

"You're right."

He looked toward Padmé.

"If Padmé survives... and it looks like she most likely will... then I have no business following the dark side anymore."

Yoda's expression remained thoughtful.

"Return to the Jedi, you may not."

Anakin looked around the room.

"Well, there's not much of a 'Jedi' to return to at this point."

"But work with us, you still may."

Yoda smiled faintly.

"And work with us, we now invite you to."

"Thank you, Master Yoda."

The old Jedi's expression became serious once again.

"Darth Sidious. Emperor Palpatine. An enemy he is. Agree, young Skywalker?"

After a long silence, Anakin answered.

"...Yes."

"Good."

Yoda leaned upon his cane.

"Then destroy him, together we will. End his reign, we will. End the Sith, we will."

Obi-Wan looked toward the recovering Anakin, whose body was still too weak even to sit up without assistance.

"Well, that's not happening anytime soon, is it?"

"Patient, we may have to be," Yoda replied. "But the Chosen One, young Skywalker remains. Thus hope we must still have in him."

The room fell quiet.

About an hour passed peacefully.

Padmé steadily regained her strength. Though still confined to her bed, her voice returned to its normal confidence, and the weakness that had filled every sentence earlier had faded.

Bail continued gently rocking the sleeping twins in his arms.

Finally he spoke.

"So what should we do with the babies? Anakin and Padmé are clearly too weak to take care of them for now. They still need time to recover themselves without the extra duties of caring for two infants."

He looked toward Padmé.

"Perhaps I can take them back to Alderaan..."

Padmé answered immediately.

"No."

Everyone looked at her.

"Take them to Naboo. My sister, Sola, can take care of them. When we are strong enough, we can take them back."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"Sounds like a plan."

Bail smiled.

"Alright. I won't do anything against the wishes of the twins' mother."

With that settled, the tension in the room eased.

The twins slept peacefully in Bail's arms.

Padmé and Anakin rested side by side on their medical cots, now fully conscious and quietly talking together.

Nearby, Obi-Wan, Yoda, Bail, R2-D2, and C-3PO spoke among themselves while keeping a watchful eye on the recovering couple, each knowing that the difficult days ahead would demand patience, healing, and unity.

# Part 3

Several weeks passed on Polis Massa.

Under the care of the medical staff, Anakin Skywalker's body continued to heal. Learning to use two new prosthetic legs and a new prosthetic left arm was frustrating at first, but years of discipline and determination allowed him to adapt quickly. Every day he walked a little farther, moved a little more naturally, and relied a little less on assistance.

Padmé's recovery progressed just as steadily. The strain of childbirth gradually faded, her strength returned, and before long she was able to walk the station's corridors without difficulty.

When the physicians finally declared both of them fit to travel, preparations began.

The group gathered beside the waiting Naboo skiff.

Queen Breha Organa of Alderaan arrived on Polis Massa in her own ship to transport Bail Organa, Obi-Wan Kenobi, and Yoda to Alderaan with her.

Anakin and Padmé would travel to Naboo with R2-D2, C-3PO, and their newborn twins before temporarily leaving the children with Padmé's family.

Before everyone boarded their respective ships, Anakin approached Obi-Wan.

Obi-Wan still carried two lightsabers on his belt.

Anakin glanced at the familiar hilt.

"Can I have my lightsaber back?"

Obi-Wan folded his arms.

"No."

Anakin blinked.

"Why not?"

"You really think I would give the lightsaber back to you after Mustafar?"

Anakin looked down for a moment before nodding.

"...Fair enough."

Obi-Wan's expression softened.

"In time, I will give it back to you. But you will have to earn it."

Anakin met his former master's eyes.

"Yes, Master."

Obi-Wan gave a small, approving nod.

"Good."

Soon afterward, the two groups departed.

The Naboo skiff entered hyperspace.

Padmé settled into one of the passenger seats while the twins slept peacefully in their bassinets nearby. R2-D2 remained connected to the ship's systems while C-3PO busied himself making certain everything aboard was in perfect order.

Padmé glanced toward the cockpit.

"Well, I'm glad Obi-Wan kept the lightsaber away from you for now."

Anakin looked over.

"I think you're still a bit too unstable for that."

He simply nodded.

A few seconds later he looked toward the pilot's controls.

"Can I fly this?"

Padmé stared at him.

"Anakin, you just got new limbs, and you're still weak. And you already want to fly this?"

"Well," Anakin replied, "you just gave birth and aren't exactly strong either."

He gestured toward the controls.

"Besides, this is just a fairly standard transport ship. I've flown much harder stuff through much harsher conditions before."

A grin spread across his face.

"Remember the time I flew half an Invisible Hand into Coruscant?"

Padmé crossed her arms.

"And nearly got yourself and Obi-Wan killed?"

Anakin stared back for a long moment.

"...Anyway, you get my point."

Padmé tried to suppress a smile.

After another moment she sighed.

"Fine."

She pointed a finger at him.

"But if you feel even slightly uncomfortable, I take over immediately."

"You have my word."

Anakin carefully settled into the pilot's seat.

His new prosthetic hands rested on the controls.

For a brief moment he hesitated.

Then the familiar motions returned almost instinctively.

The skiff responded smoothly as he guided it through hyperspace.

Some time later, while the ship cruised peacefully toward Naboo, Anakin broke the silence.

"You know... I didn't think I could ever fly again after Mustafar."

Padmé looked over at him.

"Well, consider yourself lucky."

He smiled quietly.

"Sometimes it's simply joys like this... like being a pilot... that make me feel like myself again."

Padmé smiled back.

"Then perhaps you should lean more into that."

She tilted her head.

"Less 'Sith Lord' nonsense."

She gave him a warm look.

"More normal life."

"How's that sound?"

Anakin did not answer.

He simply grinned and continued flying.

When Naboo finally came into view, the blue-and-green world seemed more peaceful than either of them remembered.

The skiff descended toward Theed.

After landing, Anakin shut down the engines and stretched carefully.

"I think we are pretty strong now."

He looked toward the bassinets.

"Are you sure we still need to give our babies to Sola?"

Padmé nodded immediately.

"Yes."

She smiled gently.

"We need some time to ourselves."

She searched for the right words.

"To actually... uh... 'fix' you."

Anakin looked thoroughly offended.

"I don't need fixing!!"

Padmé simply stared at him.

"Oh, come on, Anakin."

She raised an eyebrow.

"Surely you know you do."

Anakin opened his mouth to answer.

Nothing came out.

Instead, he quietly picked up one of the bassinets.

The group made its way through the streets of Theed before arriving at the Naberrie family home.

Sola answered the door.

The moment she saw Padmé, she smiled.

"Padmé!"

The sisters embraced warmly.

Ryoo and Pooja hurried into the room moments later.

"Aunt Padmé!"

The two girls stopped as they noticed the bundled infants.

They looked back and forth between the babies and the adults.

"Who are they?" Ryoo asked.

Padmé smiled.

"They're your new cousins."

Both girls' eyes lit up instantly.

"Really?"

"They're so tiny!"

Pooja leaned closer.

"Can we hold them?"

"Soon," Padmé said with a laugh.

"But first, your mother and I need to talk."

A few minutes later, away from the children, Padmé and Anakin sat down with Sola in a private room.

Padmé explained everything.

She spoke about the war's end, Palpatine's rise, Anakin's injuries, the birth of the twins, and the danger that still surrounded them.

When she finished, Sola remained silent for several moments.

Finally she nodded.

"They'll stay here."

She smiled reassuringly.

"I'll take care of them until the two of you are ready."

Padmé reached across the table and squeezed her sister's hand.

"Thank you."

"You don't have to thank me."

Sola glanced toward the nursery.

"They're family."

After spending time with the twins, Anakin and Padmé reluctantly said goodbye.

R2-D2 and C-3PO followed them as they departed for Varykino Villa.

Nestled beside Naboo's peaceful lakes, the villa offered exactly what neither of them had experienced for a very long time.

Quiet.

The days became simple.

Morning walks through the gardens.

Conversations over meals.

Anakin continuing to master his new prosthetic limbs.

Padmé gradually rebuilding her strength.

Evenings spent watching the sunlight reflect across the lake.

For the first time in many months, they tried simply to live as ordinary people.

Far away on Alderaan, another conversation unfolded within the Royal Palace.

Bail Organa, Queen Breha, Obi-Wan Kenobi, and Yoda gathered around a table overlooking the palace gardens.

Bail folded his hands.

"Padmé has told me about the Naberries before."

He smiled.

"They are good people."

"I'm sure the twins will be in good hands under their care."

Yoda remained thoughtful.

"One concern, there remains."

Obi-Wan looked toward him.

"Go on."

Yoda lowered his ears slightly.

"Naboo, the home of Senator Amidala is."

He paused.

"Naboo, the home of the Naberries is."

Another pause followed.

"Yet... Naboo, the home of Palpatine also is."

The room became completely silent.

Yoda continued.

"Palpatine... Darth Sidious... know about the twins, he does."

His voice grew more serious.

"Search for them on his own homeworld, he will."

He looked around the table.

"Safe, they will not be, if they are to remain on Naboo."

Obi-Wan nodded slowly.

"And your solution?"

"Hmm."

Yoda shook his head.

"Have one, I do not."

He tapped his cane against the floor.

"Talk to them soon, we must."

"Move them somewhere safer."

"Hide them."

"Protect them."

"Ensure find them, Palpatine does not."

Bail considered the problem carefully.

"Palpatine is likely occupied with consolidating the Empire at this time."

"He likely won't be searching for the twins yet."

He looked toward the others.

"We still have time."

Queen Breha spoke next.

"They are always welcome here on Alderaan if you believe that is the safest choice, and if that is what Anakin and Padmé want."

She smiled warmly.

"Alderaan will always welcome them with open arms."

Obi-Wan inclined his head respectfully.

"Thank you, Your Majesty."

"You have been very generous with this offer."

Bail looked around the room.

"For now, we shouldn't do anything."

"Let the twins stay under the Naberries' care."

"Let Anakin and Padmé rest on Naboo."

"In time, we will decide what must be done."

He gave a weary smile.

"But that's a problem for another day."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"Agreed."

Breha nodded.

"Agreed."

Yoda closed his eyes briefly.

"Agreed."

Meanwhile, on Coruscant, another meeting reached a far less peaceful conclusion.

Within the Imperial Palace, Emperor Palpatine paced before a wide viewport overlooking the endless city.

His patience had worn thin.

"I'm going to need my apprentice back."

His voice echoed through the chamber.

"I can't just let him get away like this."

Mas Amedda bowed respectfully.

"Well... what do we do about it?"

Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin stepped forward.

"Your Excellency."

"I could send out a fleet to search the galaxy for him if need be."

Palpatine turned toward him.

A slow smile spread across his face.

"Good."

His yellow eyes narrowed.

"Then do it."

"And make sure you succeed."

Tarkin bowed.

"I will not fail you, sir."

As Imperial orders spread outward from Coruscant, countless warships began receiving new assignments.

Somewhere in the vastness of the galaxy, the Empire's lost apprentice was waiting to be found.

# Part 4

The quiet days at Varykino Villa gradually became weeks.

Each sunrise over Naboo's lakes brought a little more peace than the last. Anakin's body continued to strengthen as he grew accustomed to his prosthetic limbs. By the end of the month he could walk naturally, spar lightly against training remotes without a lightsaber, and once again assist with the ordinary chores of daily life.

The physical healing came far more easily than the emotional healing.

One evening, as the sun disappeared behind the hills and the twins slept peacefully in another room, Anakin and Padmé sat together on the villa's balcony overlooking the water.

For a long time, neither of them spoke.

Finally, Anakin broke the silence.

"I've never told anyone everything."

Padmé turned toward him.

"I'm listening."

He took a slow breath.

"It began before the Battle of Coruscant."

Over the next several hours he told her everything.

He spoke of the terrifying Force visions that showed her dying during childbirth.

He described the growing panic that followed him every day afterward.

He recounted Chancellor Palpatine's strange story of Darth Plagueis.

"He said there was once a Sith Lord who had learned to keep those he cared about from dying."

Padmé listened quietly.

"He implied that knowledge still existed," Anakin continued. "He made me believe only he could teach it."

He lowered his head.

"Then the Council asked me to spy on him."

"You felt trapped."

"I was trapped."

He nodded.

"If I obeyed the Council, I risked losing the only chance to save you."

"If I obeyed Palpatine, I betrayed the Jedi."

He sighed.

"I kept telling myself I'd find another way."

"But I never did."

He described confronting Palpatine after learning the Chancellor was Darth Sidious.

He described informing Mace Windu.

Then he came to the moment that still haunted him most.

"When I reached Palpatine's office..."
