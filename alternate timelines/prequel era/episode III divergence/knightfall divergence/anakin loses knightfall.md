# Part 1

The war had reached its breaking point.

Only days earlier, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine had been rescued from General Grievous' flagship by Jedi Knight Anakin Skywalker and Jedi Master Obi-Wan Kenobi. Grievous had escaped, only to be hunted across the galaxy by Obi-Wan, while Anakin remained on Coruscant. Behind the scenes, the Chancellor had quietly set the final pieces of his design into motion.

When the time came, Palpatine revealed himself to Anakin as a Sith Lord. Rather than arresting him immediately, Anakin found himself caught between fear, loyalty, and desperation. In the end, he pledged himself to the Chancellor, who bestowed upon him a new mission.

"The Jedi have betrayed the Republic," Palpatine had declared. "Go to the Jedi Temple. Wipe out everyone there. Do not hesitate. Lord Vader, you know what must be done."

Leading the 501st Legion, Anakin marched through the streets of Coruscant toward the Jedi Temple.

The massive gates opened only after the Temple Guards recognized the famous Jedi Knight approaching with Republic soldiers. They had no reason to suspect betrayal until the first blaster bolts erupted from behind him.

The entrance hall exploded into chaos.

Clone troopers poured through the gates, firing relentlessly as stunned Jedi scattered for cover. Temple Guards ignited their yellow lightsabers and attempted to organize a defense.

Anakin strode through the smoke, blue lightsaber in hand.

Then another blade ignited.

A brilliant blue beam flashed to life before him.

Shaak Ti stepped forward.

She stared at Anakin in disbelief.

"Skywalker..." she said, horror filling her voice. "What are you doing?"

Anakin raised his weapon.

"Stand aside, Master."

"No."

Her expression hardened.

"I don't know what has happened to you, but I will not allow this."

Without another word, Anakin lunged.

Their lightsabers crashed together.

The battle was fierce from the opening exchange.

Anakin attacked with overwhelming aggression, his strikes fueled by anger and certainty. Shaak Ti answered with calm precision, allowing his momentum to work against him.

Around them, clones fired into groups of Jedi while Temple Guards rushed to reinforce the entrance. The Temple became a battlefield.

Anakin pressed harder.

"You can't stop what's happening!"

"I don't intend to stop it by killing you," Shaak Ti answered.

She slipped around one of his attacks, striking his wrist with the Force before spinning behind him.

Anakin stumbled.

She swept his legs out from under him.

Before he could recover, invisible pressure slammed him flat onto the polished floor.

He struggled.

The pressure only increased.

His lightsaber skidded across the chamber.

Shaak Ti stretched out one hand.

"Enough."

Anakin strained with every ounce of strength he possessed, but exhaustion and surprise had broken his rhythm.

Metal restraints flew from a nearby security station into Shaak Ti's waiting hand.

Within moments Anakin's wrists were bound.

Several nearby Jedi looked over in disbelief.

"Master Shaak Ti...?"

"He's secured," she said. "Do not release him."

One of the Temple Guards stared at the restrained Anakin.

"What happened to him?"

"I don't know."

She looked toward the continuing battle.

"But something is terribly wrong."

Another explosion echoed through the Temple.

More clone reinforcements entered from another corridor.

Shaak Ti looked at the restrained Jedi Knight.

"If Skywalker is leading this attack..."

She looked toward the clones cutting through Republic banners.

"...then nowhere here is safe."

She seized Anakin with the Force, lifting him effortlessly.

"With me!"

Several Jedi immediately joined her.

Temple defenders formed a moving shield as Shaak Ti carried the restrained Anakin through the Temple toward the closest hangar.

Every corridor became another battle.

Clone squads attempted to cut them off.

Jedi deflected blaster bolts back into advancing formations.

Several Knights fell.

Several clones fell with them.

Throughout the Temple, alarms blared continuously.

Masters rallied defenders.

Padawans old enough to fight joined the battle wherever they could.

The younglings were given entirely different orders.

"Go!" one instructor shouted.

"Into the lower sanctuaries!"

"Hurry!"

The youngest Jedi disappeared deeper into the Temple's hidden chambers while older Jedi remained behind to protect every passage leading toward them.

By the time Shaak Ti reached the hangar, more Jedi had joined her escort.

She lowered Anakin onto the deck of a waiting transport.

The restrained Jedi glared at her.

"You have no idea what you've done."

Shaak Ti climbed into the cockpit.

"No."

She sealed the hatch.

"But I intend to find out."

The transport blasted from the Temple hangar.

Coruscant disappeared behind her as the stars stretched into brilliant lines.

She entered hyperspace.

Only then did she allow herself a moment to breathe.

Hyperspace surrounded the vessel in blue streams of light.

She looked back toward the restrained Anakin.

"You are not escaping."

He remained silent.

Shaak Ti activated the communications console.

One by one she contacted every member of the Jedi Council.

"Plo Koon."

No answer.

"Master Stass Allie."

Silence.

"Master Ki-Adi-Mundi."

Nothing.

Her expression grew heavier.

She attempted to contact the strike team sent to confront Chancellor Palpatine.

"Mace Windu."

No response.

"Kit Fisto."

Nothing.

"Agen Kolar."

Silence.

"Saesee Tiin."

Nothing.

She closed her eyes briefly.

Then another transmission connected.

Obi-Wan Kenobi appeared, visibly exhausted inside General Grievous' stolen starfighter.

"Master Shaak Ti."

"Obi-Wan."

He immediately noticed Anakin restrained behind her.

"...What happened?"

Shaak Ti answered without hesitation.

"Anakin Skywalker led an assault on the Jedi Temple."

Obi-Wan stared at her.

"No."

"The 501st Legion attacked alongside him."

He slowly shook his head.

"That's impossible."

"I dueled him myself. He has been captured."

Obi-Wan remained speechless for several seconds.

Finally he managed,

"I've just defeated General Grievous."

He glanced behind himself at the controls.

"The clone troopers assigned to me suddenly turned their weapons on me. I escaped by taking Grievous' starfighter. I've already left Utapau."

Another signal connected.

Grand Master Yoda appeared in the cockpit of his ship.

His robes bore fresh burns from blaster fire.

"Master Yoda," Shaak Ti said.

Yoda nodded solemnly.

"A great disturbance, there has been."

"I felt countless Jedi die."

"The clone troopers assigned to me attacked without warning."

"I sensed their intentions before they fired."

His ears lowered.

"Alive because of that, I am."

Shaak Ti looked between them.

"So it is everywhere."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"It seems so."

Yoda looked toward the restrained figure behind Shaak Ti.

"Captured Skywalker is?"

"Yes."

Silence settled over the three surviving Council members.

Finally Obi-Wan spoke.

"What about the others?"

Shaak Ti slowly shook her head.

"No response from Plo Koon."

"No response from Stass Allie."

"No response from Ki-Adi-Mundi."

"The entire strike team sent to arrest Chancellor Palpatine has also gone silent."

Obi-Wan lowered his head.

"I understand."

Shaak Ti continued.

"Coleman Kcaj has not answered either."

She looked thoughtful.

"But I last saw him inside the Temple leading the defense against the clones. I believe he's simply occupied."

Yoda folded his hands.

"Hostile all clones must be considered."

Obi-Wan nodded immediately.

"I agree."

"So do I," Shaak Ti said.

Obi-Wan adjusted his controls.

"I'm heading back to Coruscant."

"No," Shaak Ti interrupted.

He looked up.

"If Skywalker attacked the Temple and every clone has turned against us, Coruscant is almost certainly compromised."

Yoda nodded.

"Return there, we should not."

"Then where?" Obi-Wan asked.

Yoda answered quietly.

"Dagobah."

Obi-Wan blinked.

"The swamp world?"

"Hidden in the Force, it is."

Shaak Ti immediately agreed.

"It will do."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"Very well."

"Dagobah."

All three ships altered course.

Far away, the blue rivers of hyperspace carried them toward the remote world.

Back on Coruscant, the battle for the Jedi Temple continued.

Without Anakin directing the assault, clone commanders attempted to overwhelm the defenders through sheer numbers.

The Jedi refused to yield.

Temple Guards held choke points with disciplined precision.

Knights fought beside Masters in corridor after corridor.

Every staircase became contested ground.

Every meditation chamber became a fortress.

Clone casualties mounted.

The defenders suffered losses as well, but the Temple's architecture favored those who knew it best.

As the hours passed, the surviving Jedi coordinated their efforts.

Squads of clones found themselves isolated and surrounded.

Blaster fire gradually diminished.

Then another squad was driven back through the main entrance.

Finally, after one last desperate assault collapsed, the surviving clone troopers retreated completely from the Temple.

Silence slowly returned.

Smoke drifted through shattered halls.

Medical teams immediately began treating the wounded.

Younglings cautiously emerged from the hidden sanctuaries where they had waited throughout the battle.

Master Coleman Kcaj stood near the battered entrance, his robes scorched and his green lightsaber still active.

One of the Temple technicians approached.

"Master."

"The communications console has been flashing for quite some time."

Coleman frowned.

"I've been rather busy."

He deactivated his lightsaber and opened the channel.

Almost immediately the faces of Yoda, Obi-Wan, and Shaak Ti appeared.

"Coleman," Obi-Wan said with visible relief.

"You survived."

Coleman gave a tired nod.

"The Temple stands."

He looked over the devastated entrance hall.

"We've repelled every clone inside the complex."

"The last of them withdrew only moments ago."

Shaak Ti allowed herself the smallest smile.

"The Temple is secure?"

"For now."

Coleman sighed.

"We suffered losses."

"But we held."

Yoda nodded approvingly.

"Come to Dagobah, you must."

Coleman looked surprised.

"Immediately?"

"Immediately," Shaak Ti answered.

"There is much we need to discuss."

Coleman looked once around the scarred Temple.

"I'll leave at once."

The transmission ended.

Far beneath the towering Senate building, inside his private office, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine stood before the great windows overlooking Coruscant.

A clone officer knelt before him.

"My lord..."

Palpatine's yellow eyes burned with fury.

"Speak."

"The assault on the Jedi Temple has failed."

The room fell deathly silent.

"The 501st has been driven out."

The clone hesitated.

"Lord Vader has not returned."

Palpatine's hands slowly tightened behind his back.

"What?"

"Our reports indicate he was defeated by Master Shaak Ti."

"He was taken from the Temple aboard a Jedi transport."

The Chancellor's expression twisted with rage.

"The Temple still stands."

"The Jedi live."

"And Skywalker has been captured."

His voice became cold enough to freeze the room.

"Find them."

The clone bowed lower.

"Yes, my lord."

When the officer had gone, Palpatine stood alone.

Years of careful planning had unraveled in a matter of hours.

The Jedi had survived.

Anakin Skywalker was no longer at his side.

And somewhere beyond the stars, four Jedi Masters were preparing to gather beyond his reach.

# Part 2

Obi-Wan Kenobi reached Dagobah first.

General Grievous' stolen starfighter descended through the thick mist, settling onto a small patch of solid ground surrounded by endless swamp. The Jedi Master stepped out, scanning the gloomy landscape before beginning preparations for the others' arrival.

Some time later, another vessel emerged from hyperspace.

Shaak Ti's transport landed nearby.

The rear ramp lowered.

Shaak Ti walked down first.

Behind her, Anakin Skywalker followed under Force restraint, his wrists still bound. He looked exhausted, his expression caught somewhere between anger and regret.

Obi-Wan stared at his former apprentice.

"I never imagined seeing you like this."

Anakin avoided his gaze.

Before either could continue, another ship descended through the clouds.

Yoda arrived.

The Grand Master stepped down quietly.

At last, Coleman Kcaj's vessel completed the gathering.

The four surviving members of the Jedi Council who had managed to reach safety stood together at the predetermined meeting place.

No one spoke for several moments.

Finally, Yoda broke the silence.

"Explain yourself, Skywalker."

Anakin remained silent.

Shaak Ti folded her arms.

"You attacked the Jedi Temple."

Coleman added, "The clones attacked every Jedi they encountered."

Obi-Wan looked directly into Anakin's eyes.

"We deserve to know why."

Still nothing.

The silence stretched.

Finally Obi-Wan spoke again.

"If you remain silent, you will remain restrained."

Shaak Ti nodded.

"You will not be released."

Coleman continued,

"You will not be allowed to leave."

Yoda finished the thought.

"A long time, wait we can."

The silence resumed.

Minutes passed.

The swamp remained eerily still except for distant creatures calling through the fog.

Anakin lowered his head.

He knew exactly what waiting meant.

Padmé's pregnancy was already well advanced.

If the Jedi simply left him restrained here indefinitely, time would continue moving without him. Eventually she would go into labor.

If Palpatine truly possessed the knowledge to save her...

That chance would disappear forever.

He also knew another truth.

There was no returning to life as an ordinary Jedi after everything he had done.

His marriage no longer served any purpose as a secret.

Slowly, he looked up.

"...I'll tell you."

The four Masters listened.

"I married Padmé Amidala."

Obi-Wan closed his eyes.

"I suspected," he admitted quietly.

"I never wanted to believe it."

Anakin continued.

"It was kept secret."

"Then she became pregnant."

His voice grew softer.

"I began having visions."

"They always ended the same way."

"She died giving birth."

Silence filled the clearing.

"I couldn't stop seeing it."

"I couldn't stop thinking about it."

He swallowed.

"Then Chancellor Palpatine told me a story."

Yoda's ears lifted slightly.

"He spoke about a Sith Lord named Darth Plagueis."

"He claimed Plagueis had learned how to keep people from dying."

Shaak Ti's expression darkened.

Anakin continued.

"Later..."

"He revealed the truth."

"He told me he was a Sith Lord."

None of the Masters interrupted.

"I reported him to Master Windu."

Obi-Wan nodded once.

"I knew that much was right."

"Mace ordered me to remain at the Temple."

"I didn't."

Anakin stared at the ground.

"I went to the Chancellor's office."

"I found Master Windu preparing to strike Palpatine down."

"I stopped him."

"I cut off his hand."

"And then Palpatine killed him."

No one spoke.

Anakin continued.

"I believed..."

He struggled to say the words.

"...that if Palpatine died..."

"...then the last chance to save Padmé would die with him."

His shoulders sagged.

"After that..."

"I felt there was no going back."

"I pledged myself to him."

"He named me Darth Vader."

The name lingered uncomfortably in the air.

"Then he ordered me to lead the 501st Legion to the Jedi Temple."

"He ordered me to kill everyone."

"I entered the Temple."

"Master Shaak Ti confronted me almost immediately."

He looked toward her.

"She defeated me."

"I was captured."

"And brought here."

When he finished, silence settled over the clearing once again.

No one seemed able to respond.

Coleman Kcaj slowly exhaled.

"I..."

He stopped.

"I don't even know where to begin."

Shaak Ti shook her head slowly.

"I have never heard anything like this."

Obi-Wan looked away into the swamp.

Yoda remained perfectly still.

Several long minutes passed before the Grand Master finally spoke.

"Done, what is done."

"Decide what comes next, we must."

Obi-Wan looked back toward Anakin.

"You've told us more about Palpatine than anyone else alive."

Shaak Ti nodded.

"You have been closer to him than any of us."

Coleman stepped forward.

"If we're going to stop him..."

"...you are a valuable source of information."

Obi-Wan spoke carefully.

"If you cooperate with us..."

"...we are prepared to show remarkable leniency despite everything that has happened."

Shaak Ti's voice became firmer.

"If you refuse to cooperate..."

"...your future will be a very unhappy one."

Anakin considered the offer.

He was restrained.

He was stranded on an isolated world.

There was nowhere to run.

No allies to call.

At last he nodded.

"I'll cooperate."

The four Masters watched him carefully.

"But I have one condition."

"What condition?" Obi-Wan asked.

"You do everything in your power..."

His voice cracked slightly.

"...to save Padmé."

"I don't care what it takes."

"I won't abandon her."

The Masters exchanged glances.

Finally Yoda answered.

"Everything in our power..."

"...we will do."

The agreement settled the matter.

Shaak Ti began immediately.

"Do you know why the clones attacked us?"

Anakin frowned.

"I don't know exactly."

"What do you mean?" Coleman asked.

"While we marched toward the Temple..."

"I kept hearing the clones saying the same thing."

"'Execute Order 66.'"

He looked around.

"I suspect that's connected."

Obi-Wan leaned forward.

"Do you know what Order 66 is?"

"No."

"Do you know how it's activated?"

"No."

"Do you know what forces the clones to obey it?"

Anakin shook his head.

"I genuinely don't."

"I never asked."

"I only heard them repeating it."

The Masters exchanged uneasy looks.

"So," Shaak Ti said quietly, "we still don't know how the clones were turned against us."

"No," Anakin answered.

"We don't."

The discussion shifted.

Coleman looked toward the others.

"If Palpatine still controls the Republic..."

"...he can simply gather more clone troopers."

Shaak Ti nodded.

"He already attempted one assault."

"There is nothing preventing another."

Obi-Wan's expression became serious.

"He may even lead it personally."

Yoda raised his cane.

"Leave the Temple..."

"...everyone must."

Without delay they established a secure communication with Coruscant.

The image of a weary Temple Guard appeared.

"Master Yoda!"

Yoda spoke immediately.

"Hear this, all Jedi must."

Shaak Ti continued.

"The Temple is no longer defensible."

"Evacuate immediately."

Coleman added,

"Everyone."

"Knights."

"Padawans."

"Temple Guards."

"Younglings."

"Every last person."

Obi-Wan concluded,

"Board every available ship."

"Enter hyperspace as quickly as possible."

"Your destination is Dagobah."

The Temple Guard bowed.

"It will be done."

Across the Temple, evacuation began at once.

Every operational starship in the hangars was brought online.

There were nowhere near enough seats.

Jedi packed themselves into every compartment.

Passengers sat shoulder to shoulder.

Some crouched in cargo holds.

Others squeezed into maintenance corridors.

Several transports exceeded their intended capacity.

The conditions were uncomfortable, but no one complained.

One after another, ships lifted from the Temple hangars.

Moments later they disappeared into hyperspace, all bound for Dagobah.

The clearing grew quiet once more.

Then Anakin looked toward Obi-Wan.

"I have another request."

Obi-Wan waited.

"I want to see Padmé."

Obi-Wan's expression hardened.

"You think you deserve that after what you've done?"

Anakin met his gaze.

"I've cooperated."

"I told you everything."

"I've answered every question honestly."

He took a slow breath.

"I'm asking for one small favor."

He hesitated.

"And..."

"I'll continue helping."

"I'll tell you anything else I remember."

"Anything that might help."

Obi-Wan remained silent.

Shaak Ti looked toward him.

Coleman said nothing.

Even Yoda simply waited.

After a lengthy discussion among the four Masters, Obi-Wan finally nodded.

"Very well."

Anakin quietly recited a private communications frequency.

Obi-Wan entered it into the console.

After several moments, the transmission connected.

Padmé Amidala appeared inside her apartment on Coruscant.

"Obi-Wan?"

She immediately looked concerned.

"What's happened?"

Obi-Wan spoke gently.

"Padmé..."

"I need you to come to Dagobah."

She blinked.

"Dagobah?"

"Yes."

She looked more worried.

"Why?"

Obi-Wan answered truthfully.

"Anakin is here."

Her eyes widened.

"Anakin?"

"He's alive?"

"Yes."

"I believe you should come."

She didn't hesitate.

"I'm leaving immediately."

The transmission ended.

Within the hour, Padmé boarded her Naboo skiff.

C-3PO hurried after her.

"Oh, my goodness! Mistress Padmé, are you certain this journey is safe?"

"I don't know," she answered.

"But Anakin needs me."

The sleek vessel departed Coruscant and entered hyperspace for Dagobah.

Meanwhile, events unfolded exactly as the Jedi Masters had anticipated.

Inside the Senate District, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine assembled fresh forces.

The 446th Legion.

The 729th Legion.

With two entire clone legions under his direct command, he personally led the renewed assault on the Jedi Temple.

The clone army smashed through the entrance without resistance.

Palpatine strode confidently into the Grand Hall.

His footsteps echoed.

No Jedi emerged.

No Temple Guards challenged him.

No lightsabers ignited.

The meditation chambers stood empty.

The archives were deserted.

Training halls had been abandoned.

Dormitories contained nothing but hastily vacated belongings.

The hangars were bare.

The Temple had been completely evacuated.

Palpatine's face twisted with restrained fury.

He had arrived too late.

Every surviving Jedi had slipped beyond his grasp.

His cloak swirled behind him as he turned sharply.

"Leave."

The clone troopers obeyed immediately.

Palpatine returned to the Senate building with nothing to show for the assault except an empty Temple and growing frustration.

# Part 3

After the discussion concluded, Obi-Wan looked toward the growing stream of incoming hyperspace signatures on his sensors.

"They're almost here."

He turned to Anakin.

"You shouldn't be present when the Temple survivors arrive."

Shaak Ti nodded immediately.

"I agree."

Coleman Kcaj folded his arms.

"Many of them saw you leading the assault."

"Some fought you directly."

"And others watched clone troopers kill their friends."

Yoda rested both hands upon his cane.

"Wisdom, this is."

Obi-Wan walked over to the stolen starfighter.

"I'm taking him elsewhere."

He looked at Anakin.

"Come with me."

Still restrained, Anakin boarded without protest.

The starfighter lifted from the swamp and disappeared across the skies of Dagobah.

Obi-Wan flew for a long distance, crossing endless marshes and tangled forests until he reached another isolated clearing roughly halfway around the planet from the original meeting point.

After landing, he transmitted his new coordinates directly to Padmé.

"This is Obi-Wan."

"We've relocated."

"Meet us here."

Padmé's voice answered almost immediately.

"I've received the coordinates."

"I'm on my way."

Back at the original rendezvous point, Yoda, Shaak Ti, and Coleman waited as the first evacuation transports emerged from hyperspace.

One vessel landed.

Then another.

Then five more.

Soon the skies were full of arriving Jedi craft.

The hatches opened almost as quickly as the landing gear touched the ground.

Jedi poured out of every transport.

Many stretched aching muscles immediately.

One Knight laughed tiredly.

"I don't think this transport was designed for 34 passengers."

Another rubbed his back.

"I spent half the trip folded into a maintenance compartment."

A Temple Guard smiled despite the exhaustion.

"I never thought I'd be so happy to stand on swamp mud."

Laughter spread through the gathering.

Despite everything they had endured, simple relief washed over them.

The surviving Jedi embraced old friends.

Masters greeted former Padawans.

Temple Guards clasped forearms with Knights they had fought beside only hours earlier.

Younglings hurried toward familiar instructors.

"Master Yoda!"

One little girl nearly tripped in the mud as she ran.

Yoda smiled warmly.

"Safe you are."

"Good."

Shaak Ti welcomed another group arriving from one of the last transports.

"You all made it?"

A Jedi Knight nodded.

"We lost no one during the evacuation."

Coleman Kcaj looked over the growing camp with visible relief.

"The Order still lives."

Meanwhile, halfway around Dagobah, Obi-Wan and Anakin waited beside the landed starfighter.

Eventually another vessel descended through the mist.

Padmé's Naboo skiff settled gracefully onto the clearing.

The boarding ramp lowered.

Padmé hurried down first.

C-3PO followed behind.

"Oh, my!" the protocol droid said. "Master Obi-Wan! Master Anakin! What has happened?"

Padmé's eyes immediately found Anakin sitting quietly with his wrists restrained.

She hurried toward him.

"Anakin..."

She looked between the two men.

"What happened?"

Obi-Wan answered calmly.

"I think Anakin should explain."

Anakin looked at Padmé.

"Could we speak alone?"

She nodded.

"I'd like that."

Obi-Wan stepped between them.

"Before I leave..."

He looked directly at Padmé.

"I need your promise."

She frowned.

"What promise?"

"You will not release him from his restraints."

She looked surprised.

"Obi-Wan..."

He continued.

"I don't believe he'll become violent toward you."

"But I cannot take unnecessary risks."

He knelt beside Anakin.

The restraints Shaak Ti had applied were removed.

Immediately Obi-Wan replaced them with another set of binders.

These locked with a coded mechanism.

He secured them carefully before standing again.

"Only I have the means to unlock these."

Padmé sighed.

"That's excessive."

She looked at Anakin.

"He's never tried to use a mind trick on me."

Obi-Wan answered quietly.

"I know."

"But these are unusual circumstances."

"I'm trying to take every necessary precaution."

Padmé looked at him for a long moment.

Finally she nodded.

"...All right."

"I understand."

Obi-Wan relaxed slightly.

"There's one more thing."

She waited.

"Please remain on Dagobah."

"Don't leave in your ship."

She nodded without hesitation.

"I won't."

"Thank you."

Obi-Wan gave Anakin one final look before boarding the starfighter once more.

Within moments he disappeared back into the sky.

Silence settled over the clearing.

Padmé sat down beside Anakin.

"You can tell me now."

He took a slow breath.

"I've already told the Jedi everything."

"Then tell me."

Anakin nodded.

He began at the beginning.

"I told them about our marriage."

Padmé quietly listened.

"I admitted we'd kept it secret."

"I told them about your pregnancy."

She nodded.

"And your visions?"

"I told them about those too."

Anakin paused for a moment before speaking again.

"Okay. I believe that is the extent of what you already know."

Padmé nodded again.

"Yes, that is all I know. What happened after that?"

Anakin continued.

"Chancellor Palpatine spoke to me about Darth Plagueis."

"He claimed Plagueis discovered how to prevent death."

Padmé's expression became increasingly troubled.

"Then Palpatine admitted he was a Sith Lord."

"I reported him to Master Windu."

"You did the right thing," Padmé said softly.

Anakin looked down.

"I should have stopped there."

He described Mace Windu's order to remain in the Temple.

He admitted disobeying it.

He described returning to the Chancellor's office.

He confessed stopping Windu from striking Palpatine.

He admitted that his actions had allowed Palpatine to kill the Jedi Master.

Padmé covered her mouth.

"Oh, Anakin..."

He continued speaking.

"I believed Palpatine was my last chance to save you."

"I thought if he died..."

"...I'd lose that chance forever."

He explained pledging himself to Palpatine.

Receiving the name Darth Vader.

Being ordered to attack the Jedi Temple alongside the 501st Legion.

Being stopped almost immediately by Shaak Ti.

Being defeated.

Captured.

Brought to Dagobah.

When he finished, only the sounds of distant swamp creatures remained.

Padmé finally spoke.

"What you did..."

She shook her head.

"...was wrong."

Anakin looked up.

"I did it for you."

"I know why you think you did it."

She met his eyes.

"But I don't need saving."

"You don't know that."

"I do."

She placed a hand gently against her stomach.

"You're terrified because of your visions."

"Anakin..."

Her voice softened.

"You're becoming paranoid."

He looked away.

She continued.

"And even if I really were in danger..."

"...I would never want you to join the Sith to save me."

She sighed.

"You made every one of these decisions without asking me."

He remained silent.

"You kept telling yourself you were protecting me."

"But you never stopped to ask what I wanted."

She looked directly at him.

"You never asked the opinion of the very person you claimed to be trying to save."

Anakin had no answer.

Padmé watched him for several long moments.

Then she quietly shifted closer to him without another word.

No lecture followed.

No accusation.

She simply remained there, sitting beside him.

The silence itself gave Anakin room to think.

After some time, C-3PO cautiously approached the couple.

"Excuse me..."

He looked from one to the other.

"Is Master Anakin quite all right?"

"And if I may ask..."

"...why is he restrained?"

Padmé looked back toward the droid.

"Threepio."
