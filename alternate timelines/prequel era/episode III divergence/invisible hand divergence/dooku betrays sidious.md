# Part 1

The bridge of the Invisible Hand shook under another barrage of turbolaser fire. Warning sirens echoed through the command deck while shattered transparisteel littered the floor. Smoke drifted from ruined consoles, and the battle over Coruscant raged beyond the great forward viewport.

Anakin Skywalker stood over Count Dooku.

The duel was over.

Dooku's curved lightsaber lay in Anakin's left hand. His own blue blade burned in his right. Both of Dooku's hands had fallen to the deck only moments before, severed cleanly at the wrists.

The defeated Count knelt silently, breathing hard, glaring upward.

Behind Anakin, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine remained seated in the restraint chair where General Grievous had held him prisoner.

Palpatine smiled.

"Good, Anakin... good. Kill him. Kill him now."

For a split second, Dooku's expression changed completely.

The confidence disappeared.

His face became raw panic.

He screamed with every bit of strength he had.

"NO, WAIT!"

The sudden roar was so loud that Anakin instinctively flinched and lowered the crossed blades a few centimeters.

Dooku shouted before anyone could interrupt him.

"PALPATINE IS A SITH LORD. HE IS DARTH SIDIOUS. I AM HIS APPRENTICE. HE JUST BETRAYED ME. DON'T LISTEN TO HIM. I'M DONE WITH HIM. I'M DONE LISTENING TO HIM. THIS KIDNAPPING WAS HIS IDEA. WE ORCHESTRATED THIS KIDNAPPING TOGETHER. THE ENTIRE WAR IS MANUFACTURED BY HIM. THE CLONES ARE A TOOL TO WIPE OUT THE JEDI LATER. IT'S CALLED ORDER 66. ONLY PALPATINE CAN EXECUTE IT. THE CLONES HAVE CHIPS THAT OVERRIDE THEIR WILL. HE WANTS TO CREATE AN EMPIRE TO OVERTHROW THE REPUBLIC. HE IS ALREADY BUILDING A WEAPON THAT CAN DESTROY AN ENTIRE PLANET. PLEASE... LISTEN TO ME AND TRUST ME FOR ONCE."

Silence followed.

Anakin simply stared.

His thoughts refused to settle.

Every accusation sounded impossible.

Every accusation was also painfully specific.

Behind him came Palpatine's calm voice.

"Anakin, would you really trust these blatant LIES coming from the mouth of the head of the Separatists? Have some reason. You know the Separatists are evil. You know Dooku is evil. You know he is spewing nonsense here."

Dooku's eyes snapped toward the Chancellor.

"MASTER WHY WOULD YOU DO THIS TO ME? I HAVE SERVED YOU LOYALLY FOR SO MANY YEARS AND THIS IS HOW YOU TREAT ME NOW..."

Palpatine's voice sharpened.

"Kill him! Kill him before he can corrupt your mind with his nonsense!"

Anakin hesitated.

He looked from one man to the other.

His instincts screamed that something was terribly wrong.

Slowly, he lowered both lightsabers.

Then he took several cautious steps backward.

Dooku had no hands.

No weapon.

No obvious way to continue fighting.

Anakin kept both sabers ignited, one blue and one red.

Palpatine sounded almost desperate.

"Anakin, why are you not killing him? Don't you see that he is turning you to the dark side?"

Dooku slowly leaned forward.

Without hands, the movement looked almost ridiculous.

A small comlink floated out from beneath his cape, lifted by the Force.

It hovered in front of his face.

Using only the tip of his nose, he activated it.

"General Grievous."

A metallic voice answered immediately.

"Yes, Count."

"Palpatine has just betrayed us. Take an escape pod out and set this ship on a rapid crash course immediately."

"Copy."

The transmission ended.

Palpatine shouted.

"What are you doing?!"

Dooku laughed bitterly.

"Master, if you won't let me live, I won't let you live either."

Palpatine turned toward Anakin.

"See, Anakin? He's evil. He wants to kill us all!"

Dooku met Anakin's eyes.

"Anakin, listen. What evil person willingly sacrifices themselves in order to get others killed?"

Anakin frowned.

The question struck him strangely.

He didn't answer immediately.

Instead, he asked something else.

"Dooku... if I don't kill you, will you fully collaborate with the Republic? Your inside knowledge of the Separatists may come in handy for us."

Dooku answered without hesitation.

"Anything. Anything that doesn't end in me killed."

"What?" Palpatine burst out. "No, Anakin, no. What are you doing?"

Anakin looked toward the Chancellor.

"Maybe you are a Sith Lord after all."

Palpatine looked almost offended.

"How dare you, Anakin?! I don't even know how to use the Force!"

Before Anakin could respond, Dooku suddenly closed his eyes.

His face twisted with concentration.

His entire body trembled.

Anakin felt the disturbance through the Force before he understood what was happening.

A violent burst of blue lightning erupted from Dooku's feet, blasting through the soles of his boots.

The torrent shot directly across the bridge.

Straight toward Palpatine.

There was no time to think.

No time to move.

Palpatine thrust out both hands.

Blue lightning exploded from his fingertips.

The two streams collided with a deafening crack.

For an instant they fought each other.

Then Palpatine's blast overwhelmed Dooku's.

The reflected energy crashed back into the kneeling Count.

Dooku screamed.

His body convulsed violently.

The smell of burning cloth and flesh filled the room.

Seconds later, he collapsed onto the deck.

Motionless.

The crackling energy faded.

Silence returned.

Palpatine lowered his hands.

He looked down at Dooku's body.

"Excellent. Dooku is finally dead."

Anakin stared.

His heart pounded.

His mouth felt dry.

"Palpatine..."

The Chancellor slowly turned toward him.

"...did you just block and reflect that lightning? How..."

The pieces suddenly fit together.

"Oh... you really must be a Sith Lord then."

Palpatine answered quickly.

"Master Yoda can block lightning too, and he is certainly no Sith!"

Anakin took another cautious step backward.

"But a Jedi would have no reason to hide his abilities from the public. So you are certainly no Jedi."

He kept both lightsabers pointed toward the Chancellor.

"And you know far too much to be innocent."

Palpatine remained silent.

"You just blocked lightning. And you talk about the Jedi and the Sith like you know them well."

Anakin's expression hardened.

"I'm going to treat you as a Sith Lord."

For a heartbeat neither man moved.

Then Palpatine slowly reached into his robes.

Anakin tightened his grip.

Instead of a weapon, the Chancellor produced a comlink.

He activated it.

"Execute Order 66."

Anakin froze.

The exact words echoed in his memory.

Order 66.

Only Palpatine can execute it.

Exactly as Dooku had claimed.

He looked at the Chancellor in disbelief.

"Well... okay. Now you are guilty beyond doubt."

Palpatine's expression changed.

The friendly warmth vanished.

"So," Anakin continued quietly, "you just executed the exact order that Dooku just told us about."

Palpatine's eyes narrowed.

"Then I hope you are prepared when doom arrives."

Before Anakin could react, the Chancellor moved.

The restraints that had supposedly held him prisoner snapped apart as though they were made of thread.

He surged upward with impossible speed.

A tremendous wave of the Force slammed into the bridge.

Anakin stumbled sideways.

Consoles exploded.

The massive forward viewport shattered outward.

Atmosphere screamed into space.

Emergency blast shields began dropping from the ceiling.

Palpatine launched himself through the broken opening before they could seal completely.

Anakin barely caught a glimpse of a dark figure vanishing into the void above Coruscant.

Then the blast shields crashed into place with a thunderous clang.

The bridge shook violently.

A warning klaxon changed pitch.

The ship lurched.

Anakin nearly lost his footing.

He looked toward Dooku's comlink, still lying on the deck.

General Grievous had obeyed.

The Invisible Hand was no longer fighting.

It was falling.

He ran to Obi-Wan, who still lay unconscious where Dooku had thrown him earlier in the duel.

"Master!"

No response.

Anakin shook him.

"Obi-Wan! Wake up!"

Another violent impact rocked the cruiser.

Somewhere deep within the ship, metal screamed as enormous sections of the vessel began tearing themselves apart during the uncontrolled plunge toward Coruscant.

# Part 2

The Invisible Hand groaned around Anakin as its uncontrolled descent accelerated.

Warning lights flashed across every console.

"Come on..." he muttered.

He sprinted to the helm.

The bridge was heavily damaged, and many systems refused to respond, but enough controls remained intact for manual flight. He slid into the pilot's seat, seized the controls, and fought the enormous cruiser as it tried to tumble through Coruscant's atmosphere.

The ship resisted every correction.

Its nose dipped.

The starboard engines flared unevenly.

Another alarm screamed.

Anakin gritted his teeth.

"You're not taking us down that easily."

Slowly, almost imperceptibly at first, the descent became less chaotic.

He redirected what little engine power remained, vented damaged thrusters, and adjusted the surviving control surfaces. The massive vessel still fell, but no longer like a stone.

He might actually survive the landing.

Keeping one hand on the controls, he activated his comlink.

"Jedi Temple, this is Anakin Skywalker. Emergency."

Static crackled.

Then a familiar voice answered.

"Temple communications. Go ahead."

Anakin spoke as quickly as he could.

"Order 66 has been activated. Treat every clone as hostile immediately. Palpatine is Darth Sidious. I repeat, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine is Darth Sidious. He orchestrated the war. Warn every Jedi you can."

Silence lasted only a heartbeat.

Then the operator answered.

"Understood."

The channel erupted into activity.

"Broadcast to every offworld Jedi immediately!"

"Open every long-range frequency!"

"Alert the Council!"

Anakin heard voices overlapping one another.

"This is the Jedi Temple. Emergency transmission. Order 66 has been activated. All clone forces are to be considered hostile. Repeat, all clone forces are hostile. Supreme Chancellor Palpatine has been identified as Darth Sidious..."

The transmission repeated again and again as the Temple relayed it across the galaxy.

Anakin could only hope it would arrive in time.

Some Jedi would already be standing beside clone troopers.

Others might hear the warning before the first shot.

He had no way of knowing who would live.

Another alert interrupted his thoughts.

"Large clone formation approaching the Temple."

A different voice shouted across the open channel.

"They're attacking! Defensive positions!"

Blaster fire echoed faintly through the comlink before the transmission dissolved into frantic reports.

Anakin swallowed.

He opened another frequency.

"Ahsoka."

Only static answered.

"Ahsoka, come in."

Nothing.

His stomach tightened.

He switched frequencies again.

"Captain Rex."

Silence.

"Rex, answer me."

Nothing.

Anakin slowly lowered the comlink.

He closed his eyes for just a second.

"I hope you're both alive."

The rapidly approaching skyline pulled his attention back to the controls.

The surface was coming fast.

Footsteps sounded behind him.

Obi-Wan groaned.

"What..."

He rubbed his forehead before looking around the ruined bridge.

"...what's happening?"

Without taking his eyes off the controls, Anakin answered.

"Dooku is dead."

"What?"

"Palpatine killed him with Force lightning."

Obi-Wan blinked.

Anakin continued before he could interrupt.

"He's a Sith Lord. The entire kidnapping and the war were orchestrated by him. He also just executed an order that makes all clones turn on us."

Obi-Wan stared.

"And Palpatine escaped."

There was a long pause.

Finally Obi-Wan spoke.

"Are you out of your mind?"

"Ask the Jedi Temple."

Obi-Wan activated his own comlink.

"This is Obi-Wan Kenobi."

The response came immediately.

"Master Kenobi! The Temple is under attack! Clone forces have entered the grounds!"

Obi-Wan's face lost all color.

"The clones?"

"Yes, Master. They're attacking everyone."

The sounds of explosions and blaster fire filled the channel.

"We're taking heavy casualties."

Obi-Wan looked at Anakin.

He said nothing.

He simply nodded once.

He believed him.

The ground filled the viewport.

"Brace yourself!" Anakin shouted.

The Invisible Hand slammed into Coruscant.

The impact hurled both Jedi forward.

Metal screamed.

Entire sections of the cruiser tore away.

The ship skidded through buildings, clipping towers and throwing debris across the city before finally grinding to a halt.

Silence settled.

Smoke drifted through the bridge.

Obi-Wan climbed to his feet.

He looked around at the devastation.

Then he sighed.

"Another happy landing."

Anakin couldn't help giving the faintest smile.

They hurried toward the nearest exit.

The hatch burst open.

Fresh air rushed inside.

They had taken only a few steps onto the battered hull when nearby clone troopers spotted them.

"JEDI!" one shouted.

Another immediately raised his rifle.

"TRAITORS! EXECUTE ORDER 66!"

A storm of blaster bolts filled the air.

Two blue lightsabers ignited together.

Anakin and Obi-Wan moved as one.

Bolts ricocheted in every direction.

Several returned to their shooters.

Others were sliced apart.

The surviving clones advanced relentlessly.

As they ran for cover, Anakin shouted over the blaster fire.

"See? The inhibitor chips really do override their wills."

Obi-Wan deflected another bolt.

"Right. Normally no clone would do this to us. Not after fighting alongside us for so long."

They ducked behind a chunk of fallen hull plating.

Anakin peeked around the edge.

More clones were arriving.

"We need a plan."

Another barrage struck the wreckage.

"We can't fight infinitely many clones."

Obi-Wan looked toward the nearby streets.

"We should see if we can get ourselves to the Temple."

Another explosion rocked the area.

"We have to help defend it."

Anakin looked around.

"How are we even going to get to the Temple?"

Obi-Wan pointed.

A civilian speeder sat abandoned beside a damaged building.

"Take that thing."

Another wave of clones rounded the corner.

"Now."

He ignited his lightsaber again.

"Go before the clones reach us again."

They sprinted.

Blaster bolts chased them across the street.

Anakin vaulted into the driver's seat while Obi-Wan leaped beside him.

The engines roared.

The speeder shot upward.

Clone fire continued until they climbed beyond effective blaster range.

Only then did the shooting finally stop.

An incoming transmission appeared.

Mace Windu's voice emerged immediately.

"I believe Darth Sidious himself has arrived. We are struggling hard. Are you able to come help?"

Obi-Wan answered.

"We are coming."

"Hurry. We don't have enough defenders."

The transmission ended.

Anakin pushed the speeder even faster.

Smoke rose over the Jedi Temple long before they reached it.

Flames licked through shattered windows.

Clone gunships circled overhead.

Destroyed walkers burned across the plaza.

Anakin landed hard.

Before the engines had fully powered down, both Jedi jumped from the speeder.

Their lightsabers ignited simultaneously.

Clone troopers rushed them.

The blue blades carved through the ranks while deflecting incoming fire.

Every hallway echoed with blaster bolts.

Fallen Jedi and fallen clones lay scattered throughout the Temple.

Anakin forced himself not to stop.

They had to reach the center of the fighting.

Eventually they entered one of the great chambers.

There they found Mace Windu locked in an intense duel with Palpatine.

Purple and crimson blades crashed together in a blur.

Palpatine's face twisted with concentration.

Without hesitation, Anakin and Obi-Wan joined the fight.

Blue.

Blue.

Purple.

Three Jedi attacked from different angles.

Palpatine moved with terrifying speed, turning aside strike after strike.

Lightning burst from one hand.

Mace absorbed it.

Obi-Wan drove him backward.

Anakin nearly landed a decisive blow before Palpatine twisted away at the last instant.

For several minutes the battle raged.

Finally Palpatine glanced toward the surrounding corridors.

More Jedi were arriving.

He was becoming surrounded.

His expression hardened.

With a powerful Force wave he blasted all three opponents backward.

Before anyone recovered, he leapt through a shattered archway.

By the time Anakin reached it, the corridor beyond was empty.

"He escaped!" Anakin shouted.

The remaining clone forces gradually lost momentum.

Without Palpatine directing the assault, their attack became disorganized.

The surviving Jedi pushed them back room by room.

Eventually the final clone trooper fell.

Silence settled over the ruined Temple.

Smoke drifted through broken ceilings.

Mace deactivated his lightsaber.

"Well, he is just going to come back with more clones and try again."

He looked around the devastated chamber.

"We have to get out of here, fast."

Kit Fisto nodded.

"So we evacuate?"

Yoda rested both hands on his cane.

"Evacuate, we must. To Dagobah we shall go."

Obi-Wan looked toward the Council members.

"Do we have enough ships?"

Saesee Tiin answered.

"We should have enough to evacuate all the Masters and younglings. Some Knights and Padawans may need to stay behind. We can evacuate them on the second round."

Shaak Ti nodded immediately.

"The younglings are the most vulnerable, so we definitely evacuate them first. We need to evacuate the Masters too because if they fall, the Order will lose the spine holding it together. So I agree."

Coleman Kcaj looked around the gathering.

"Then it's decided. Let's go to the hangars."

Yoda activated his comlink.

His voice carried throughout the Temple.

"All younglings please report to the nearest hangar in the Temple immediately."

Within minutes, corridors filled with frightened children escorted by Jedi.

Masters directed the evacuation while Knights loaded supplies and organized the departing vessels.

Anakin helped shepherd one group of younglings aboard a transport.

Many looked frightened.

Some were crying quietly.

One little girl tugged gently on his sleeve.

"Master Skywalker... are we going to be okay?"

Anakin looked at her for a moment before answering.

"We're going to do everything we can."

It was the only promise he felt he could honestly make.

Not long afterward, his transport lifted off.

Obi-Wan sat nearby, watching the shrinking skyline of Coruscant through a side viewport.

Around them, numerous Jedi vessels formed into a loose convoy.

Each carried Masters, younglings, and as many Knights and Padawans as space allowed.

Their destination was Dagobah.

Behind them, smoke continued to rise from the battered Jedi Temple as Coruscant slowly disappeared into the darkness of space.

# Part 3

The convoy of Jedi vessels crossed through hyperspace in tight formation.

Inside one of the transports, the rescued younglings had finally begun to settle down. Some had fallen asleep against one another. Others sat quietly, still shaken by the attack on the Temple.

Anakin remained awake.

He stared into the blue tunnel of hyperspace, unable to stop replaying the last few hours in his mind.

Dooku's desperate confession.

Palpatine's Force lightning.

Order 66.

The burning Temple.

His comlink chimed.

The encrypted frequency appeared on the display.

Padmé.

Anakin answered instantly.

"Padmé?"

Her voice came through, frantic and terrified.

"Anakin! I have been kidnapped by Palpatine. I'm in his office. I desperately need help."

The transmission ended.

Anakin was already on his feet.

Obi-Wan looked up immediately.

"I need to go," Anakin said. "Now."

"I heard the comm."

Obi-Wan stood between him and the exit.

"Anakin, please don't do this. We have a mission right now."

"You can take the younglings to Dagobah yourself perfectly fine. You don't need me."

Obi-Wan's expression grew more serious.

"This sounds like a trap. If you go, you may not come back."

Anakin didn't hesitate.

"Still better than letting Padmé die at Palpatine's hands."

"I'm serious," Obi-Wan said. "Think this through."

But Anakin was already moving.

He reached the emergency escape pod dock.

The hatch sealed behind him.

Moments later, the pod detached from the transport and accelerated away from the convoy.

Inside the cramped cockpit, he repeatedly tried Padmé's private frequency.

"Padmé?"

No answer.

"Padmé, come in."

Silence.

Again.

Nothing.

He tightened his grip on the controls.

Obi-Wan's warning echoed through his thoughts.

*This sounds like a trap. If you go, you may not come back.*

He knew Obi-Wan was probably right.

Everything about this felt calculated.

Palpatine almost certainly wanted him to come.

But if Padmé truly was being held prisoner...

He couldn't abandon her.

The escape pod dropped from hyperspace over Coruscant.

Without waiting for clearance, Anakin flew directly toward the Chancellor's office.

He landed on the roof.

Drawing his lightsaber, he ignited the familiar blue blade.

The humming weapon cut a perfect circle through the roof.

The section of metal dropped away.

Anakin lowered himself into the building.

He moved silently through the upper corridors.

Every few steps he reached out through the Force.

Searching.

Feeling.

Then he found them.

Padmé.

Alive.

And another presence nearby.

Palpatine.

Anakin edged closer until he could look into the office.

Padmé was restrained by her wrists.

Palpatine stood over her.

"You've already lost," Palpatine said coldly. "The Jedi are scattered. Your Republic is collapsing. Even your husband cannot save you now."

Padmé glared at him but remained silent.

Anakin steadied his breathing.

*Don't fight him.*

That became his entire plan.

*Get Padmé out.*

Nothing else mattered.

He waited.

Palpatine turned slightly toward the far side of the office.

That was enough.

Anakin exploded into motion.

He surged forward with a burst of Force speed.

At the same instant, he hurled a powerful Force push toward the enormous office window.

The transparisteel shattered outward in a deafening explosion.

Without slowing, Anakin seized Padmé with the Force.

Still bound at the wrists, she flew toward him.

He caught her around the waist.

Then they both plunged through the broken window.

Wind howled around them.

Coruscant rushed upward impossibly fast.

Padmé gasped.

Anakin focused every ounce of concentration into slowing their fall.

The Force resisted.

Their momentum bled away.

Not enough.

They struck the ground with crushing force.

Pain exploded through Anakin's back.

For several seconds he couldn't breathe.

Nearby pedestrians screamed and scattered.

Thankfully, there were no clone patrols in sight.

Padmé grimaced through the pain.

"They'll send clones after us any second."

She struggled to catch her breath.

"Contact Bail Organa. Immediate evacuation."

Anakin activated his comlink.

"Bail."

The senator answered immediately.

"Anakin?"

"We need extraction. Right now."

"Location?"

Anakin transmitted his coordinates.

"I'm coming."

The transmission ended.

Anakin painfully ignited his lightsaber.

Carefully, he sliced through Padmé's restraints without touching her.

The bindings fell away.

She rubbed her freed wrists.

Both of them attempted to stand.

Neither managed more than a few centimeters before collapsing again.

"My back..." Anakin muttered.

Padmé nodded weakly.

"I think... mine too."

Minutes felt like hours.

Every distant engine made Anakin fear the arrival of clone gunships.

Instead, a familiar speeder descended.

Bail Organa climbed out.

Without wasting time, he helped lift Padmé into the back.

Then, with considerable effort, he and Padmé managed to pull Anakin aboard as well.

The speeder accelerated away.

Only after several minutes did Bail finally speak.

"You both need medical treatment immediately."

He looked over his shoulder.

"But not on Coruscant."

He changed course.

The speeder headed for the Tantive III.

The corvette departed almost immediately after they boarded.

As Coruscant disappeared behind them, Padmé finally looked at Anakin.

"There is something you need to know."

He turned toward her.

"I'm pregnant."

Anakin stared.

"What?"

"I'm pregnant."

A small smile crossed her face despite everything.

Then her expression became more serious.

"What you did back there was reckless."

"I know."

"You threw both of us out of a skyscraper."

"It was the fastest way to get you away from him."

"It easily could have killed the baby."

Anakin closed his eyes briefly.

"I didn't know."

She nodded.

"I know."

After a quiet moment, she continued.

"The message I sent you..."

She sighed.

"Palpatine forced me to record it."

Anakin frowned.

"He wanted you to come."

"He said exactly that. He intended to use me to blackmail you into cooperating with him."

She looked away.

"After I recorded the message, he took my comlink away."

Anakin slowly nodded.

"That's why you never answered."

"Yes."

For several minutes, neither spoke.

Eventually Padmé broke the silence.

"What happened after you left me?"

Anakin leaned back carefully against the medical support.

He told her everything.

The confrontation with Dooku.

The revelation about Palpatine.

Order 66.

The crash of the Invisible Hand.

The clone attack.

The battle at the Temple.

The evacuation to Dagobah.

"And then your message came."

Padmé listened quietly.

When he finished, Anakin looked down.

"Obi-Wan will never forgive me."

She looked at him in disbelief.

"Anakin."

He met her eyes.

"That should be the least of your concerns right now."

She gestured toward both of them.

"You're too injured to even stand."

The remainder of the journey passed quietly.

Eventually the Tantive III emerged above Alderaan.

The peaceful world stood in sharp contrast to everything they had left behind.

Medical teams met them immediately upon landing in Aldera, the capital city.

Doctors rushed both patients into treatment.

Hours became days.

Scans confirmed what both Anakin and Padmé had already suspected.

Each had suffered a broken back.

Recovery would take considerable time.

One afternoon, the chief physician entered their room carrying another datapad.

"I have good news."

Anakin and Padmé both looked up.

"We've completed additional examinations."

The doctor smiled.

"Senator Amidala, you're expecting twins."

Padmé's eyes widened.

"Twins?"

The doctor nodded.

"And despite everything that happened, both children appear completely unharmed."

Padmé exhaled with enormous relief.

Anakin reached over and gently squeezed her hand.

For the first time in days, both of them smiled without reservation.

Several more days passed.

After continued treatment, the doctors determined that neither patient could safely walk yet.

Both were fitted with repulsorlift chairs.

The physician gave his final instructions.

"You'll need these for at least a while. Recovery is progressing well, but your spines require time to heal."

With that, they were formally discharged.

Outside the medical facility, Bail Organa and Queen Breha Organa waited for them.

Bail smiled warmly.

"You don't have anywhere safer to recover right now."

Breha nodded.

"The royal palace has more than enough room."

She looked at both of them kindly.

"We would be honored if you stayed with us until you've recovered."

Anakin exchanged a glance with Padmé.

Neither needed to discuss it.

"Thank you," Padmé said.

"We'd be grateful."

Together, the four of them departed for the royal palace in Aldera, where, for the first time since the war had turned against the Jedi, Anakin and Padmé found a place where they could begin to heal.

# Part 4

Dagobah was unlike any world most of the Jedi had ever seen.

Mist drifted constantly between enormous twisted trees. The air was heavy with moisture, strange calls echoed through the swamps, and every path seemed to disappear beneath vines and murky water.

Yet it was hidden.

That alone made it invaluable.

Within hours of arriving, the Jedi had begun constructing shelters from fallen timber, broad leaves, reeds, and whatever else the swamp provided. Masters and Padawans worked side by side. Even the youngest children carried bundles of branches wherever they could.

Several days later, a rough settlement stood in a broad clearing.

It was primitive, but it functioned.

Small huts surrounded a communal fire. Raised wooden walkways connected the drier patches of ground. Storage shelters had been built for food and supplies salvaged from the evacuation.

The Jedi Order had lost its Temple.

It had not lost itself.

One afternoon, Mace Windu studied a hand-drawn map spread across a rough wooden table.

"We need better intelligence," he said. "Right now we know almost nothing about the situation beyond Coruscant."

Kit Fisto nodded.

"We've received scattered reports. Some Jedi survived because they heard the Temple's warning before their clone escorts attacked."

"Others never received it," Shaak Ti said quietly.

Silence settled over the gathering.

Yoda rested both hands upon his walking stick.

"Survive, we must. Hidden, we remain until understand the galaxy we do."

Nearby, the sounds of lightsabers echoed through the clearing.

Padawans continued training under their masters despite the difficult conditions.

Elsewhere, Yoda knelt before a circle of younglings.

The children sat attentively on woven mats.

Yoda smiled.

"Patience. A Jedi's greatest ally, patience is."

One little boy raised a hand.

"Master Yoda?"

"Yes?"

"Will we have a Temple again someday?"

Yoda regarded the child for several long moments.

"Hope, always there is. Lose it, we must not."

The lesson continued.

For a short time, the laughter of children replaced the sounds of war.

Far away, Coruscant had become anything but peaceful.

Clone transports filled the skies over the abandoned Jedi Temple.

Two full clone legions surrounded the massive structure.

At their head stood Palpatine.

The evacuation had taken nearly every surviving Master, many Knights, the Padawans, and all of the younglings away from Coruscant.

Those who had remained behind because there had not been enough ships now stood alone.

The battle was brief.

The defenders fought with courage.

But they were too few.

One by one, the remaining Knights and Padawans fell beneath overwhelming clone numbers.

By day's end, no Jedi remained inside the Temple.

Clone banners replaced the symbols of the Order.

Palpatine slowly walked through the silent halls.

The Temple belonged to him.

Later that day, every major communications network carried the Chancellor's address.

Palpatine stood before countless cameras.

His voice echoed across the galaxy.

Luke yawned loudly.

Everyone laughed.

Soon afterward, Anakin and Padmé made one final decision together.

They had spent years surrounded by war.

Neither wanted that life anymore.

"I've had enough fighting," Anakin said quietly.

"I'm retiring from active warfare."

Padmé nodded.

"And I'm taking a break from politics."

She looked toward Luke and Leia.

"I think we've earned that much."

There was only one place they truly wanted to go.

Naboo.

They traveled home together.

The green hills, blue lakes, and quiet countryside welcomed them back.

When they arrived at Varykino Villa, everything felt strangely familiar despite how much the galaxy had changed.

Anakin carried Luke inside.

Padmé followed with Leia.

Outside, the waters of the lake reflected the afternoon sun.

There were no battles to fight.

No Senate debates to attend.

No emergency messages demanding immediate action.

Only a peaceful home.

For the first time in many years, Anakin Skywalker and Padmé Amidala could devote themselves not to saving the galaxy, but to raising the family they had fought so hard to protect.
