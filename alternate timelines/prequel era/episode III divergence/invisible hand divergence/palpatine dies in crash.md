# Part 1

The battle above Coruscant had already torn the flagship of General Grievous apart by the time Count Dooku fell.

Inside the shattered command chamber of the Invisible Hand, Anakin Skywalker stood over the fallen Sith Lord, breathing heavily as Obi-Wan Kenobi slowly climbed back to his feet after recovering from his injuries.

Dooku's body lay motionless on the deck.

General Grievous had already fled.

The Kaleesh cyborg had not merely escaped—he had done everything he could to ensure no one else would leave safely. Before abandoning the flagship, he had ordered every remaining escape pod launched empty into space and personally shut down the ship's computer systems. Navigation, stabilization, emergency guidance—everything had gone dark.

"He's gone," Obi-Wan said grimly, looking toward the ruined bridge.

Anakin ignited a damaged control panel with his lightsaber before prying it open with both hands.

"He sabotaged everything."

"We're still heading straight for Coruscant."

The entire front section of the cruiser shuddered violently.

Obi-Wan grabbed a support beam.

"Can you fly this thing?"

"I don't know," Anakin admitted. "But I'm going to try."

There was no other choice.

Working with dead controls, severed power lines, and manual overrides that were never intended to be used during atmospheric reentry, Anakin fought the crippled warship every second of its descent.

The vessel screamed through Coruscant's atmosphere.

Fire engulfed its hull.

The rear half finally tore away under the tremendous stress, vanishing behind them in an explosion of flaming debris.

Anakin gripped the manual controls with both hands.

"Come on..."

The cruiser answered with another violent spin.

Obi-Wan looked through the fractured viewport.

"Easy... easy..."

"I'm trying!"

The nose dipped sharply.

The ruined cruiser clipped the edge of one skyscraper, ricocheted across another, and slammed into the endless cityscape below with catastrophic force.

Metal folded like paper.

Entire decks collapsed into one another.

The front half of the Invisible Hand plowed across multiple landing platforms before finally grinding to a halt amid a mountain of wreckage.

Silence followed.

Smoke poured from every opening.

Deep inside the ruined flagship, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine never moved again.

Nearby, Anakin Skywalker and Obi-Wan Kenobi lay buried beneath twisted metal, both still alive but unconscious.

Far above the crash site, emergency beacons lit up across Coruscant.

Rescue teams arrived within minutes.

Republic security forces, firefighters, medics, engineers, and clone troopers surrounded the wreckage while aircraft circled overhead.

"Life signs detected!"

"Cut through here!"

"We've got survivors!"

Rescuers reached what remained of the command chamber first.

One medic knelt beside Palpatine.

"No pulse."

Another checked again.

"He's gone."

A heavy silence settled over the team.

One clone removed his helmet.

"The Chancellor..."

The body was carefully lifted onto a stretcher and immediately transported away under armed escort.

Elsewhere in the wreckage another rescue team finally uncovered Obi-Wan.

"He's breathing!"

"Severe trauma!"

"Stabilize him!"

A little farther inside the twisted corridors another shout echoed.

"I've got another one!"

The workers peeled away sheets of mangled durasteel until Anakin became visible beneath them.

"Jedi General Skywalker!"

"Still alive!"

"Careful—careful!"

The young Jedi's breathing was shallow. His body was covered in burns and injuries from the impact.

"Move him now!"

Both Jedi were rushed onto separate medical transports and flown to one of Coruscant's nearest trauma facilities.

Not far from where Anakin had been found, a rescue worker discovered the shattered remains of a familiar astromech.

"This unit's destroyed."

Another technician knelt beside the broken droid.

"Memory core's damaged..."

He connected a portable recovery device.

"Wait."

Lights flickered.

"I can still copy part of it."

"Do it."

Several tense minutes later the technician disconnected the cable.

"I've got a memory copy. The droid's beyond repair."

"Take it with the other recovered evidence."

The damaged remains of R2-D2 were left behind while the copied memory was secured for later analysis.

Across Coruscant, emergency broadcasts interrupted every holonet transmission.

"The Supreme Chancellor has been confirmed dead following the crash of General Grievous' flagship."

"Jedi Generals Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker have been recovered alive but remain in critical condition."

"Count Dooku is believed to have been killed during the operation."

"General Grievous remains at large. His current location is unknown."

The news spread through the Republic faster than any fleet could travel.

Inside the Senate building, conversations stopped all at once.

Senators stared at suspended holoscreens.

Others whispered in disbelief.

Padmé Amidala stood frozen.

"...Anakin..."

Without another word she hurried from the chamber.

One of her aides hurried after her.

"Senator! Where are you going?"

"I need to know where General Skywalker was taken."

"That information is restricted."

"Then find someone who can tell me."

The aide hesitated only a moment before following.

Padmé called in every favor she possessed.

She spoke with senators, Republic officials, military administrators, and medical coordinators.

Again and again she met polite refusals.

"I'm sorry, Senator. The locations of injured military personnel are classified."

"I understand," she answered. "But I need that information."

Hours later persistence finally succeeded.

A sympathetic official quietly handed her a location.

"I shouldn't be telling you this."

"You won't regret it."

"I hope not."

She departed immediately.

The medical facility was already overflowing with casualties from the battle.

Clone guards stood outside secured wings.

Doctors hurried between operating rooms.

Medical droids moved continuously through the corridors.

Padmé approached the reception station.

"I need to see General Skywalker."

"I'm sorry, Senator. He's in critical condition."

"I understand."

"No visitors."

She leaned forward.

"Please."

The receptionist looked uncertain.

"I won't interfere."

Several minutes and multiple conversations later, one of the supervising physicians finally appeared.

"You may have only a few moments."

"Thank you."

The physician led her into a quiet recovery chamber.

Anakin had only recently been brought out of surgery.

He lay unconscious on a medical bed beneath numerous monitoring devices. Bandages covered much of his body. Mechanical equipment steadily measured every heartbeat and breath.

Padmé slowly walked to his bedside.

She looked at the injuries and struggled to hold back tears.

She gently took his hand.

"Anakin..."

There was no response.

"I know you can't hear me."

Her voice trembled.

"But I'm here."

She squeezed his hand softly.

"You always told me everything would be all right."

A tear rolled down her cheek.

"So now it's my turn."

She smiled faintly through her grief.

"I have something I wanted to tell you."

She leaned closer.

"I'm pregnant."

The room remained silent except for the steady rhythm of the medical monitors.

"We're going to have a baby."

She laughed quietly through her tears.

"Actually..."

She rested her forehead gently against his hand.

"...babies."

"You have to come back."

"I know you can."

She remained there beside him until the doors opened again.

Several doctors entered with medical droids.

"We're taking him for another surgery," the lead physician said.

Padmé stepped back immediately.

"How is he?"

The physician paused before answering honestly.

"We still don't know whether he'll survive."

Her expression fell.

"We've repaired what we could, but the extent of his injuries is severe."

"If he lives..."

The doctor glanced toward Anakin.

"...he may never make a full recovery."

Padmé listened without interrupting.

"He has suffered extensive trauma throughout his body. Even if everything heals as well as we hope, it is highly unlikely he will ever be capable of returning to military service as a warrior."

She looked at Anakin as the bed was wheeled toward the operating theater.

"Thank you for telling me."

The doors closed behind him.

Padmé remained standing alone in the hallway.

The physician's words echoed in her thoughts.

He may never be capable of returning to military service as a warrior.

For years they had hidden their marriage from almost everyone they knew.

It had been necessary.

Anakin was a Jedi.

She was a senator.

Both understood the consequences.

But if Anakin could no longer serve as a Jedi in the way he once had...

She quietly reached a decision.

If the moment ever came when hiding the truth endangered him or stood in the way of his future, she would reveal their relationship herself.

No matter the political cost.

No matter what anyone thought.

She would not abandon him.

Several more anxious hours passed.

Finally the operating room doors opened once again.

The lead physician approached.

"The surgery was successful."

Padmé released a breath she had been holding.

"He'll live?"

"We believe so."

Relief flooded her face.

"Thank you."

"He remains in critical condition, but we no longer believe his life is in immediate danger."

She looked past the doctor.

"May I see him?"

"Yes."

The physician led her into another recovery room.

This time Anakin floated motionless inside a cylindrical bacta tank.

The healing fluid surrounded him completely while countless tubes and sensors monitored his condition.

"He'll remain here while his body recovers," the physician explained.

"How long?"

"We don't know."

"When will he wake up?"

"We don't know that either."

She nodded slowly.

"The injuries were extensive. It may take days."

He hesitated.

"It may take considerably longer."

After the doctors quietly departed, Padmé remained alone with the softly glowing bacta tank.

She placed one hand gently against the transparent surface.

Anakin floated peacefully within the healing fluid, unconscious but alive.

"I'll be here," she whispered.

"No matter how long it takes."

She pulled a chair beside the tank and sat down, never taking her eyes off him as the quiet hum of the medical equipment filled the room.

# Part 2

The following morning, the medical facility remained under heavy guard.

Doctors continued moving from room to room treating casualties from the battle over Coruscant. Anakin Skywalker remained suspended within his bacta tank, his condition stable but unchanged. Padmé Amidala had scarcely left his side except when the medical staff insisted she rest for a short time.

Shortly after sunrise, a group of Jedi arrived.

Their robes drew respectful glances from the hospital staff as they entered the secure ward. Several Jedi healers accompanied a small escort.

The lead physician met them outside the recovery rooms.

"You've come for Generals Kenobi and Skywalker?"

"Yes," the senior Jedi replied. "The Council has decided they should be returned to the Temple. Our healers will continue their treatment with Force-assisted healing."

"We've already discussed the transfer."

"The paperwork has been completed."

The physician nodded.

"Everything is ready."

The Jedi first entered Obi-Wan Kenobi's room.

He remained unconscious, though his breathing had become stronger overnight.

One of the healers placed a hand near Obi-Wan's forehead for a moment.

"He is improving."

"He still requires extensive treatment," another said, "but he is stable enough for transport."

The doctors carefully disconnected the medical equipment and transferred Obi-Wan onto a specialized hover stretcher.

The remaining Jedi proceeded to Anakin's recovery room.

Padmé immediately stood.

The senior Jedi inclined his head politely.

"Senator Amidala."

She looked from the Jedi to the bacta tank.

"You're taking him too?"

"Yes."

"He and Master Kenobi will both continue their recovery under the care of the Jedi healers."

Padmé looked at Anakin floating silently within the glowing bacta.

"Will I be able to see him in the Temple's medical wing?"

The Jedi answered gently.

"No."

She blinked.

"No?"

"No visitors are permitted."

He paused.

"And especially no non-Jedi are allowed anywhere in the Temple at this time."

Padmé's expression hardened.

"Does he have to go to the Temple?"

"Yes."

"He is a Jedi, and we have internally decided to take Obi-Wan and him back to the Temple for Force healing. We have already reached an agreement with the medical staff here."

Padmé folded her arms.

"I don't agree."

The room became quiet.

"My request is that he remains here."

The Jedi's voice remained calm.

"Senator, I am very sorry, but you don't have the authority to override our decision."

He looked toward the bacta tank.

"As he is a Jedi, we have the right to decide to take him back to the Temple."

Padmé was silent for only a heartbeat.

Then she realized there was no longer any reason to keep the truth hidden.

She met the Jedi's eyes.

"No."

Her voice was firm and unwavering.

"I am his wife."

Every Jedi in the room stared at her.

"We have been married for three years."

Silence settled over the chamber.

"Under Republic law," Padmé continued, "I believe I have the final say on Anakin's medical decisions when he is incapacitated."

The Jedi exchanged astonished glances.

One finally spoke.

"...And what evidence do you have of such a marriage?"

Padmé answered immediately.

"Threepio?"

Silence.

Her confident expression faded.

"..."

She rubbed her forehead.

"Oh, come on."

In her desperate rush to reach Anakin the previous day, she had left C-3PO behind at the Senate building.

She thought quickly.

"Okay... let me think..."

Then another possibility occurred to her.

"R2?"

She looked around.

"Have any of you seen R2-D2?"

One of the Jedi answered quietly.

"I'm sorry, Senator, but I believe R2-D2 was destroyed in yesterday's crash."

Padmé closed her eyes briefly.

"Do you have any part of him left?"

"We have a working copy of the droid's memories."

She looked up instantly.

"What?"

"The technicians who recovered what remained of the astromech managed to preserve a copy of his memory core."

Another Jedi added,

"A copy was sent to the Senate yesterday."

"And another was delivered to the Jedi Temple."

Padmé took a step forward.

"I need it."

The senior Jedi frowned slightly.

"Why?"

"The memories contain the evidence you're looking for."

She pointed toward the door.

"Can you go back to the Temple and fetch the copy you have?"

The Jedi shook his head.

"Senator, we don't have time to go back to the Temple for such trivial matters."

He glanced toward Anakin's bacta tank.

"We need to leave soon."

Padmé thought rapidly.

Then she activated her comlink.

After only a moment, Bail Organa's face appeared.

"Padmé?"

"Bail."

He immediately noticed the worry in her voice.

"What is it?"

"Can you check if you have access to a copy of R2-D2's memories?"

Bail turned toward a nearby terminal.

"Give me a moment."

His fingers moved rapidly across the controls.

Seconds later he nodded.

"Yes."

"You found it?"

"It was delivered to the Senate yesterday."

Padmé exhaled in relief.

"I need you to come here right now with the memories and a device able to project them."

"I'll leave immediately."

The transmission ended.

Padmé looked back at the Jedi.

"It won't take long."

The senior Jedi hesitated.

Finally he nodded.

"We'll wait."

He turned to the rest of the group.

"You."

He gestured to half the Jedi.

"Take Master Kenobi to the Temple."

They bowed.

"Yes."

Within moments they departed with Obi-Wan's medical transport.

The remaining Jedi stayed behind.

Time passed slowly.

The atmosphere inside the room grew increasingly tense.

One Jedi quietly checked the time.

Another looked toward the bacta tank.

Finally the senior Jedi sighed.

"We cannot delay much longer."

Padmé remained standing in front of the tank.

"He'll be here."

Another several minutes passed.

At last the senior Jedi nodded toward the healers.

"Prepare the transfer."

The healers stepped toward the bacta tank.

Just then the doors opened.

Bail Organa hurried into the room carrying a secure data case beneath one arm and a portable hologram projector in the other.

"I'm here."

Padmé smiled with visible relief.

"Thank you."

Bail handed her the memory storage device.

"This is everything recovered from R2."

She inserted the memory copy into the projector.

The small machine hummed to life.

Thousands of recordings appeared in chronological order.

Padmé searched quickly.

"Come on..."

She scrolled through years of recordings.

Political events.

Journeys.

Conversations.

The Clone Wars.

Then she stopped.

"There."

She activated the recording.

A blue holographic image sprang into the air.

Padmé looked at the Jedi.

"All right."

She pointed toward the recording.

"Three years ago."

The hologram shifted to a peaceful lakeside on Naboo.

"Just after the Battle of Geonosis."

The recording continued.

"Anakin and I were married on Naboo."

The Jedi watched silently.

The image showed Anakin Skywalker and Padmé Amidala standing together beneath the open sky.

A Naboo holy man officiated the ceremony.

Standing nearby were the only witnesses.

C-3PO.

R2-D2.

The ceremony concluded as Anakin and Padmé embraced.

The recording ended.

No one spoke for several moments.

Finally the senior Jedi lowered his head slightly.

"...Very well."

He looked at Padmé.

"We had our suspicions."

Another Jedi quietly nodded.

"But this is indeed proof beyond doubt."

The room remained solemn.

After a long pause, the senior Jedi spoke again.

"We still believe that Anakin would make a better recovery if you would allow us to take him to the Temple."

He looked toward the bacta tank.

"Force healing is known to be quite effective on Jedi."

Padmé nodded.

"I understand."

She looked at Anakin floating peacefully inside the healing fluid.

"But I cannot accept that the Temple would not allow me to see him."

She gently rested one hand against the transparent surface of the tank.

"The doctors here already said he will likely survive and is making progress on recovery."

She looked back at the Jedi.

"And I am sure he will do just fine here."

The senior Jedi gave a slow nod.

"I hope you reconsider."

Padmé met his gaze without hesitation.

"Once again, I'm reminding you that I have the final say on this matter."

She spoke quietly but firmly.

"Please leave."

The Jedi stood in silence.

For several long moments they looked at Anakin suspended within the bacta tank.

Then the senior Jedi bowed respectfully.

"As you wish."

Without another word, the group turned and departed.

The doors closed behind them.

The room became quiet once again.

Bail looked toward Padmé.

"You've kept this secret for a long time."

"We had to."

He nodded thoughtfully.

"I suppose that secret won't remain hidden much longer."

Padmé looked at the now-dark hologram projector, then back at Anakin.

"No."

Her voice was soft.

"I don't think it will."

Bail quietly collected the projector and gave her shoulder a reassuring squeeze before leaving her alone.

Once again, only the gentle glow of the bacta tank illuminated the room.

Padmé pulled her chair close and sat beside Anakin.

With the Temple's healers gone and the transfer abandoned, she settled in to continue her vigil, watching over her unconscious husband as the steady rhythm of the medical equipment filled the silence.

# Part 3

While Anakin Skywalker floated unconscious in a bacta tank, Coruscant began to fracture under the weight of uncertainty.

The death of Supreme Chancellor Palpatine had left a vacuum at the very center of the Republic.

The Senate convened in emergency session.

What was intended to be a solemn gathering quickly dissolved into heated arguments that echoed through the vast chamber.

Bail Organa stood at his pod.

"The Republic cannot afford disorder. The office of Supreme Chancellor is too important to leave in dispute. We must preserve our institutions and hold an election to determine the next Chancellor."

Several senators applauded.

Mon Mothma rose next.

"Whatever our political differences, we must remember that the Republic is governed by law, not by opportunism. Our citizens deserve a lawful and transparent transition."

Across the chamber, Mas Amedda responded.

"The Republic is in the middle of a galactic war. This is not the time to organize a political campaign."

He spread his hands toward the Senate.

"The death of a Chancellor does not automatically require an immediate election. We should appoint someone to carry out the office until the proper time arrives."

Sate Pestage nodded.

"Continuity is essential."

Another pod floated forward.

Wilhuff Tarkin addressed the assembly in his usual measured tone.

"Our fleets require leadership. The clone army requires leadership. Every day spent debating procedure is another day our enemies exploit our uncertainty."

The chamber erupted once more.

"No!"

"The Republic must vote!"

"We need stability!"

"This is a power grab!"

"The law is clear!"

"The law is not clear!"

The shouting became so loud that the presiding officials struggled to restore order.

Inside a quiet hospital recovery room, Padmé watched the Senate proceedings through a small holoprojector.

She leaned back in her chair and rubbed her tired eyes.

She had spent over a week sleeping only in short intervals beside Anakin.

Now the Republic itself seemed determined to collapse into political chaos.

She switched off the transmission.

"I don't have the energy for this."

Her comlink chimed.

"Senator Amidala?"

It was Jar Jar Binks.

"Yes, Jar Jar?"

"The Senate's asking where yousa is."

Padmé closed her eyes for a moment before answering.

"Jar Jar... I'm stepping away from my Senate duties for now."

"Oh."

"I need you to represent Naboo in my place until I return."

Jar Jar blinked.

"Mesa?"

"Yes."

Padmé's expression became serious.

"Jar Jar."

"Yes?"

"If you are unsure about anything, contact me and ask me."

"I will."

"We are in a crisis."

His ears drooped slightly.

"Don't make decisions without fully understanding what you are saying."

Jar Jar nodded vigorously.

"Of course! Yousa has mesa word!"

Padmé sighed.

"All right then."

She managed a faint smile.

"Remember, this is not a game."

"I know."

"I'll do mesa best."

"I know you will."

The transmission ended.

Padmé looked back toward Anakin.

"For both our sakes..."

Anakin is still floating around peacefully.

"...I hope nothing important comes up."

Several days passed.

Anakin's condition steadily improved.

The doctors eventually gathered around his bacta tank one morning.

"His tissue regeneration has progressed well."

"Vital signs remain excellent."

"I think we're ready."

The bacta slowly drained away.

Medical droids carefully lifted Anakin from the tank and dried the remaining healing fluid from his skin before transferring him onto a hospital bed.

Padmé stood nearby, hardly daring to breathe.

The lead physician smiled reassuringly.

"His body is fully stable now."

Padmé looked hopefully toward Anakin's peaceful face.

"When will he wake up?"

"We still don't know."

The doctor's expression remained calm.

"His injuries have healed far better than we expected."

He adjusted one of the monitors.

"We'll continue observing him for a few more days."

"And after that?"

"We'll release him into your care."

She looked surprised.

"Mine?"

The physician nodded.

"Yes."

"He no longer requires intensive medical treatment."

"We believe he should recover in a familiar environment."

He smiled.

"Eventually he will wake from the coma."

"So... no more operations?"

"No."

"No more machines?"

"Nothing beyond ordinary observation."

Padmé let out a relieved breath.

"Thank you."

From then on, she spent the days sitting beside Anakin's bed.

She talked constantly.

Sometimes she described the weather outside.

Sometimes she told him stories from the Senate.

Sometimes she simply spoke about their future.

"I've been thinking," she whispered one afternoon.

"When you wake up..."

She smiled.

"...we should spend more time on Naboo."

Another day she gently held his hand.

"The doctors say you're doing well."

She laughed softly.

"I knew you wouldn't let a crashed battleship defeat you."

Another evening she leaned close to his ear.

"I miss hearing your terrible jokes."

"You'd better wake up before I forget what your voice sounds like."

She knew he could not hear her.

Still, she talked anyway.

Two days after Anakin left the bacta tank, the door opened unexpectedly.

Padmé turned.

Her eyes widened.

"Obi-Wan!"

The Jedi Master smiled.

"Hello, Padmé."

She stood immediately.

"How are you here already?"

Obi-Wan looked down at himself.

"My injuries really aren't that bad."

He stretched one arm experimentally.

"Based on what the healers at the Temple told me, Anakin was much more seriously injured than I was."

He looked toward the sleeping Jedi.

"So, yes."

"I see Anakin still hasn't woken up yet."

Padmé nodded.

"No."

Obi-Wan smiled reassuringly.

"Don't worry."

"It will happen soon."

"I definitely hope so."

A brief silence followed.

Obi-Wan folded his arms.

"So..."

Padmé looked at him suspiciously.

"...I heard you're married, huh?"

She covered her face for a moment.

"So they told you."

"Oh, they told everyone."

She groaned quietly.

"The entire Temple knows."

He chuckled.

"I'm pretty sure half the Senate knows by now too, even though the healers told us not to tell people outside the Temple about your marriage."

He shrugged.

"Stuff like this can't be kept secret once it's out."

He smiled.

"Everybody wants to hear about the rumours of the famous Jedi General marrying a famous senator."

Padmé laughed despite herself.

"All right, all right."

"I get it."

She looked back toward Anakin.

"I don't really care if it gets out at this point."

Her smile softened.

"I'd just be happy if Anakin can recover from this."

For a few moments neither of them spoke.

Finally Obi-Wan broke the silence.

"The Council has asked me to go hunt down General Grievous."

Padmé looked back at him.

"They think I am the most fit for destroying him."

"Is he still attacking?"

"Somewhat less, ever since Dooku died."

He nodded thoughtfully.

"But yes."

"The Separatists are definitely still attacking."

He clasped his hands behind his back.

"We're hoping that once Grievous is removed, we can negotiate an end to the war."

Padmé smiled.

"That's great."

She stood and offered him her hand.

"Well, good luck then, Obi-Wan."

"I'm sure you can make Grievous regret his life decisions."

Obi-Wan laughed.

"I'll certainly do my best."

They both chuckled.

After one last glance at his former apprentice, Obi-Wan quietly departed.

A few more peaceful days passed.

The physicians completed one final examination.

The lead doctor looked over the results one last time before nodding.

"Everything looks good."

Medical droids carefully transferred Anakin onto a specialized hover stretcher.

Padmé stood ready beside him.

The doctor smiled.

"He is ready to leave now."

She looked down at Anakin.

"He should go somewhere quiet and peaceful to rest."

The physician folded his datapad.

"It will be good for him."

He smiled reassuringly.

"And hopefully he will wake up sooner in that condition."

"Thank you."

Padmé signed the final release forms.

Together with the medical staff, she escorted Anakin from the hospital.

The journey back to her Coruscant apartment was quiet.

When the apartment door opened, a familiar golden protocol droid hurried toward her.

"Oh my goodness, Mistress Padmé!"

C-3PO threw his arms into the air.

"I have been looking for you for over a week!"

"Where have you been?"

Padmé managed a tired smile.

"Long story."

"I'll tell you about it later."

She carefully guided Anakin's stretcher farther inside.

"Right now..."

She walked toward the enormous window overlooking Coruscant.

The city beyond remained restless.

News broadcasts flashed across towering holoscreens.

Political demonstrations crowded plazas.

Republic gunships patrolled the skies.

The crisis remained unresolved.

Padmé stared at it for several seconds.

"...we go to Naboo."

Threepio tilted his head.

"Oh?"

"Anakin needs somewhere quiet to recover."

The droid looked toward the unconscious Jedi.

"What happened to Master Anakin?"

Padmé looked back at him.

"Later."

"I promise I'll tell you everything."

Within hours they departed Coruscant aboard Padmé's Naboo skiff.

Anakin rested securely inside the passenger compartment while C-3PO remained nearby.

The stars drifted past as the vessel entered hyperspace.

During the long journey, Padmé finally recounted everything that had happened aboard the Invisible Hand, the crash, the hospital, the surgeries, and R2-D2's destruction.

Threepio became quieter with every passing minute.

At last he spoke softly.

"R2 has been destroyed?"

Padmé nodded.

"I'm afraid so."

"Oh no."

The protocol droid lowered his head.

"I really liked him."

"So did I."

He remained silent for several moments.

"Maybe Master Anakin will get himself a new astromech one day to keep me company."

Threepio gave a tiny shake of his head.

"That still can't replace R2."

His photoreceptors dimmed slightly.

"Oh..."

"I miss him so much."

"And now I won't be able to see him ever again..."

Padmé opened her mouth to answer.

Then stopped.

Only days earlier, she had believed she might never see Anakin again.

She had stood beside his bed wondering whether he would survive another hour.

The fear returned to her for just a moment.

She reached over and gently rested a hand on Threepio's shoulder.

"I'm sorry."

The droid nodded sadly.

The remainder of the journey passed in quiet reflection.

When they finally arrived on Naboo, the familiar countryside welcomed them with calm skies, gentle breezes, and the peaceful sounds of the lake.

The contrast with Coruscant was overwhelming.

Together, Padmé and Threepio carefully carried Anakin into Varykino Villa.

They settled him into a comfortable bed beside an open window overlooking the water.

Fresh air drifted into the room.

Birds sang outside.

Padmé sat beside him.

She gently brushed a strand of hair from his forehead and lightly stroked his cheek.

"All right."

Her voice was barely above a whisper.

"You are home now."

She looked around the quiet villa.

"Home."

She smiled softly.

"Here."

# Part 4

The Clone Wars were finally over.

Celebrations spread across countless worlds.

Clone troopers who had fought since the beginning of the conflict finally laid down their weapons.

Across former Separatist territory, battle droid foundries dramatically reduced military production.

Morning sunlight continued to spill across the bedroom floor.

Luke and Leia gradually grew stronger with each passing day.

Threepio happily devoted himself to helping care for the twins, though he frequently admitted that childcare had never been one of his primary specialties.

"The Republic is finally at peace."

"You would have liked to see it."

She looked toward the nursery where Luke and Leia slept.

"Our children are beautiful."

A tear rolled gently down her cheek.

"They're waiting to meet you."

She leaned forward and kissed his forehead.

"So am I."

Anakin remained peacefully asleep, his breathing slow and even, while the peaceful sounds of Naboo drifted through the open window.

Padmé never stopped believing that, one day, he would open his eyes and come home to the family waiting beside him.

# Part 5

Nearly half a year after the crash of the Invisible Hand, peace had become the ordinary rhythm of the galaxy.

The Clone Wars had ended.

The Republic and the Confederacy of Independent Systems maintained their hard-won peace.

Bail Organa governed from Coruscant.

The Senate had returned to debating trade, infrastructure, and reconstruction instead of emergency military appropriations.

On Naboo, however, the greatest victory had yet to come.

The sun had already set behind the hills surrounding Varykino Villa.

Luke and Leia had fallen asleep after an evening of enthusiastic crying that had left C-3PO insisting he was developing entirely theoretical stress circuits.

Padmé smiled apologetically.

"You've done wonderfully, Threepio."

"You've missed so much."

"The gardens are beautiful again."

"I've started reading to you again."

"You know..."

"Where's R2-D2?"

"What..."

"Stay in your bed."

"Where am I?"

How Chancellor Palpatine had been killed in the impact.

How Bail Organa had become Chancellor.

How the war had ended.

The twins.

"Eventually word got out."

"Now everybody knows."

"I don't blame you at all."

"You did exactly what you should have done."

"I would have done the same."

Padmé smiled with visible relief.

"Thank you."

Luke slept peacefully in Anakin's arms.

Leia rested against Padmé's shoulder.

The quiet was interrupted only by birdsong.

Padmé smiled.

"The Republic is safe now."

"The war is over."

"Nobody is fighting anymore."

"We have a great future ahead of us, Ani."

"Not until the Sith Lord we've been hunting since Maul is defeated."

"Oh."

"Speaking of Maul..."

"If the Sith Lord eventually emerges again, we can deal with him then."

At the peaceful lake.

At the children sleeping safely in their parents' arms.

Protecting his family.

Watching his children grow.

Building a peaceful home.

A peaceful life with the people he loved.
