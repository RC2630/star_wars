# Part 1

The corridors of the Galactic Executive Building echoed beneath Anakin Skywalker's boots as he sprinted toward the Chancellor's private office. Every instinct screamed that he was already too late.

When he reached the great doors, they stood open.

The office beyond was in ruins.

Splintered furniture lay scattered across the polished floor. Broken transparisteel glittered near the shattered windows where Coruscant's endless lights stretched into the night. The bodies of Jedi Masters Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin lay motionless where they had fallen.

Only two men remained standing.

Mace Windu stood near the broken window, purple lightsaber blazing.

Across from him, Chancellor Palpatine crouched against the window frame, his face twisted with exhaustion and fury.

"I am too weak," Palpatine rasped. "The Jedi are taking over!"

Anakin's eyes darted between them.

Master Windu had the advantage.

The Chancellor was beaten.

No...

Panic surged through Anakin's body.

Palpatine had promised there was knowledge that could save Padmé. If the Chancellor died, that knowledge would die with him.

"Master Windu!" Anakin shouted. "Don't!"

Windu did not turn.

"He controls the Senate and the courts," Windu said coldly. "He is too dangerous to be left alive."

Anakin saw Windu's lightsaber begin its final descent.

He acted without thinking.

His blue blade flashed to life as he lunged toward Windu's exposed side.

But Windu had already seen it.

The Jedi Master's expression did not change.

In one impossibly fast motion, Windu pivoted.

His purple blade intercepted Anakin's attack before it had even begun.

There was a violent crack.

Anakin's lightsaber spun from his hand and clattered across the floor.

Before Anakin could recover, Windu had already turned back toward Palpatine.

The purple blade came down.

Palpatine's eyes widened.

"No—"

The lightsaber struck cleanly.

Silence filled the office.

The Chancellor collapsed.

His body slid to the floor beside the broken window.

The room became still.

Anakin stared.

His breathing became ragged.

"No..."

Windu deactivated his lightsaber and finally looked at Anakin.

"You attempted to strike a member of the Jedi Council while I was carrying out my duty."

Anakin backed away.

"You killed him."

"I ended the life of a Sith Lord."

"You killed him!"

"The Chancellor was Darth Sidious."

"I don't care!" Anakin shouted, his voice breaking. "You don't understand!"

Windu walked forward.

"I understand enough."

With a swift motion, he drew a pair of restraint binders from his belt.

"Anakin Skywalker, you are under arrest for attempting to assault a senior Jedi Council Master."

Anakin glared at him.

"I should fight you."

"You are welcome to try."

Anakin looked toward where his lightsaber had landed.

Windu followed his gaze.

The purple blade ignited once more.

"I would advise against it."

Several agonizing seconds passed.

Finally Anakin lowered his head.

Without another word, Windu secured the binders around his wrists.

The confiscated lightsaber disappeared into Windu's robes.

"Come with me."

Neither of them looked back at Palpatine's body.

Not long afterward, the Jedi Temple entered a state of emergency.

News spread quickly among the Temple's inhabitants.

The Chancellor was dead.

Three Jedi Masters had fallen in the confrontation.

Knight Anakin Skywalker had been arrested by Master Windu.

Within the Council Chamber, only two Council members were physically present.

Mace Windu stood beside his seat.

Shaak Ti had arrived moments earlier, still trying to process the reports.

Around them, holograms flickered into existence.

Master Yoda appeared from Kashyyyk.

Obi-Wan Kenobi from Utapau.

Plo Koon from Cato Neimoidia.

Ki-Adi-Mundi from Mygeeto.

Many other Council Masters remained unreachable, engaged in battles across the galaxy and unable to attend either in person or through hologram.

The chamber felt unusually empty.

Yoda spoke first.

"Explain what happened, Master Windu."

Windu folded his hands behind his back.

"The Chancellor revealed himself as Darth Sidious."

The holographic council members became silent.

"I confronted him with Masters Tiin, Kolar, and Fisto. All three were killed during the engagement."

Obi-Wan lowered his head.

"Anakin informed you?" he asked.

"He did."

"And then?"

"I instructed Anakin to remain in the Temple."

Windu's voice remained calm.

"He agreed."

Shaak Ti frowned.

"He left anyway?"

"He did."

Windu continued.

"He entered the Chancellor's office while I was concluding the duel. As I prepared to end Darth Sidious' life, Anakin attempted to strike me from behind."

Obi-Wan looked stunned.

"No..."

"I anticipated the attack through the Force," Windu continued. "I disarmed him before his blade reached me. I then ended Darth Sidious' life and placed Anakin under arrest."

Silence settled over the chamber.

Finally Ki-Adi-Mundi spoke.

"If this account is accurate..."

"It is," Windu answered.

"...then he disobeyed a direct order."

"Correct."

"And attacked a Council Master."

"Correct."

Shaak Ti looked troubled.

"There must be some explanation."

"There may be," Windu replied. "But explanation is not excuse."

Yoda closed his eyes for several long moments.

"When brought before us, has he been?"

"He refuses to explain his actions beyond insisting that I should not have killed the Chancellor."

Obi-Wan spoke quietly.

"I don't understand him."

"Neither do I," Windu answered.

Plo Koon finally broke the silence.

"The Order cannot tolerate assault upon the Council."

"No," Ki-Adi-Mundi agreed. "Nor deliberate insubordination."

Yoda slowly opened his eyes.

"A grave matter, this is."

One by one, each Council member gave an opinion.

The discussion stretched on.

No one suggested imprisonment.

No one suggested execution.

But every member agreed that the combination of defying a direct order and attempting to attack a senior Council Master could not be overlooked.

Finally Yoda spoke.

"Expelled from the Jedi Order, Anakin Skywalker shall be."

No one objected.

The decision was unanimous.

A short time later Anakin was escorted into the Council Chamber.

His wrists were still bound.

His face was pale with exhaustion and anger.

He looked around.

Obi-Wan met his eyes through the hologram.

There was only sadness there.

Yoda spoke.

"Knight Skywalker."

Anakin said nothing.

"You disobeyed Master Windu's command to remain in the Temple."

"I did."

"You attempted to interfere in combat."

"I did."

"You attacked Master Windu."

"I was trying to stop him!"

"You attacked me," Windu said.

Anakin glared.

"Yes."

Yoda nodded once.

"Your actions, unacceptable they are."

Obi-Wan finally found his voice.

"Anakin..."

Anakin looked at him.

"You could have trusted us."

"I did trust you."

His voice cracked.

"And look where it got me."

Yoda lowered his head.

"By unanimous decision of those assembled, expelled from the Jedi Order you are."

Anakin stood frozen.

"No..."

"Your lightsaber has already been confiscated."

Windu stepped forward.

"You are no longer authorized to carry the weapon of a Jedi."

Anakin looked from one face to another.

Obi-Wan could not meet his gaze.

Shaak Ti looked deeply saddened.

Plo Koon remained silent.

Ki-Adi-Mundi sat motionless.

Yoda simply closed his eyes.

Windu removed the binders from Anakin's wrists.

"You are free to leave."

Anakin rubbed his wrists.

"That's it?"

"That is the Council's decision."

"My whole life..."

No one answered.

"My entire life was this Order."

Still no one spoke.

Anakin turned.

Without another word, he walked out of the Council Chamber.

No one stopped him.

By the time Anakin reached Padmé Amidala's apartment, Coruscant's endless skyline had fallen into the quiet glow of deep night.

The apartment was dark.

Padmé had already gone to bed.

Anakin quietly entered.

Threepio looked up from where he had been standing near the entrance.

"Oh! Master Anakin. You're back rather late."

Anakin did not answer.

He walked into the living area and collapsed onto one of the couches.

Threepio tilted his head.

"Is everything all right?"

For several long seconds there was only silence.

Then the words came pouring out.

"No."

Threepio stepped closer.

"What happened?"

"They killed him."

"I'm terribly sorry, sir, but... who?"

"Palpatine."

Threepio froze.

"The Chancellor?"

"Mace Windu killed him."

"Oh dear..."

"He was the only one who could help me."

Threepio looked confused.

"Help you?"

"He promised there was a way to save Padmé."

Threepio's photoreceptors widened.

"Master Anakin..."

"They took everything."

Anakin stood and began pacing.

"They raised me. They trained me. They told me I was family."

His breathing grew heavier.

"Then today they threw me away."

"You mean the Jedi?"

"They expelled me."

Threepio actually staggered backward.

"Expelled?"

"I don't have a lightsaber anymore."

Threepio looked horrified.

"Oh my..."

"They arrested me."

"Arrested?"

"They said I attacked Master Windu."

"You... did?"

"I tried to stop him!"

Threepio struggled to process everything.

"I'm certain there must have been some dreadful misunderstanding."

"There wasn't!"

Anakin shouted.

"There wasn't any misunderstanding! Windu killed the only man who had an answer! Then he arrested me! Then the Council threw me out like I was nothing!"

Threepio nervously wrung his metallic hands.

"I... I really think perhaps you should try to calm yourself."

"Oh, should I?"

"Yes, sir. You're understandably upset."

"Upset?"

"Perhaps after some sleep—"

"Sleep?"

Anakin laughed bitterly.

"You think sleep fixes this?"

"No, sir, but it may help you think more clearly."

Anakin stepped closer.

"Threepio."

"Y-yes?"

"You should perhaps consider what it's like to be me right now."

"I cannot truly know that, sir."

"No. You can't."

Threepio tried again.

"Nevertheless, shouting will not improve matters."

"So now you're giving me advice?"

"I'm only trying to help."

"Help?"

Anakin's voice rose again.

"Everyone keeps saying they're helping!"

Threepio took another cautious step backward.

"I assure you I mean no offense."

"You think I should just forget everything?"

"I said nothing of the sort."

"You might as well have."

"I most certainly did not!"

The argument escalated.

Threepio became increasingly flustered.

Anakin became increasingly furious.

Neither noticed the small astromech rolling into the room until R2-D2 emitted an irritated series of sharp whistles.

Neither paid attention.

R2 whistled again.

Still they argued.

The astromech gave one final indignant beep.

Then his utility arm extended.

A blue electrical arc burst from the stun probe.

The charge struck Anakin squarely in the side.

"Agh!"

His muscles locked instantly.

He collapsed face-first onto the floor.

Silence returned to the apartment.

Threepio stared.

"Oh dear."

R2 let out a satisfied electronic chirp.

Threepio looked from the unconscious Anakin to the astromech.

"You know... I suppose that was one way to end the discussion."

R2 answered with a short, unapologetic whistle before retracting his stun probe.

# Part 2

Morning light filtered through the broad windows of Senator Padmé Amidala's apartment, washing the polished floor in soft gold.

Padmé slowly opened her eyes.

She immediately noticed that Anakin was not beside her.

A knot formed in her stomach.

She quickly rose from bed and walked into the main living area.

Anakin lay unconscious on the floor where he had fallen the night before.

"Anakin!"

She hurried to his side, knelt beside him, and gently touched his shoulder.

He was breathing steadily.

Relief washed over her.

With considerable effort, she managed to pull him into a sitting position before carefully helping him onto the nearby couch.

"Threepio!"

The golden protocol droid hurried into the room.

"Oh, thank goodness, Mistress Padmé."

"What happened?"

Threepio hesitated.

"It was... quite an eventful evening."

"Tell me everything."

The protocol droid did exactly that.

He explained how Anakin had arrived late in the night after being expelled from the Jedi Order.

He described Anakin's grief over Chancellor Palpatine's death.

He recounted how Anakin had spoken of losing the only person who claimed to possess a way to save Padmé from dying.

He explained that Anakin had confessed to trying to stop Master Windu from killing the Chancellor.

He admitted that the conversation had slowly become an argument.

"And then," Threepio concluded, "Artoo apparently reached the conclusion that the disagreement had gone on quite long enough."

Padmé looked toward the astromech.

R2-D2 gave an innocent whistle.

"He stunned him?"

R2 emitted an affirmative chirp.

"I see."

Padmé looked back toward the sleeping Anakin.

Pieces of the puzzle began fitting together.

Several weeks earlier Anakin had reluctantly admitted that he had begun suffering terrifying Force visions of her dying during childbirth.

She had tried to reassure him.

She had believed they were only nightmares.

She knew he had become increasingly close to Chancellor Palpatine over the course of the war.

She had never imagined just how desperate Anakin had become.

Nor had she understood how deeply those visions had begun to dominate his decisions.

Now everything suddenly made sense.

Palpatine had offered hope.

Master Windu had taken that hope away.

Anakin had lashed out.

Padmé slowly stood.

"He needs help."

Threepio nodded vigorously.

"I quite agree, Mistress Padmé."

She looked back toward Anakin.

"I'm going to the Jedi Temple."

Threepio looked uncertain.

"Do you think they'll receive you?"

"They're going to."

Padmé knelt beside Anakin one last time.

She gently brushed a strand of hair from his forehead.

"You aren't facing this alone."

She rose, collected her cloak, and left the apartment.

The Jedi Temple remained unusually tense.

News of Chancellor Palpatine's death had transformed the Temple into a center of constant activity.

Messengers hurried through the corridors.

Clone officers entered and departed.

Communications from distant battlefronts arrived every few minutes.

Padmé was escorted to the Council reception chamber.

Master Windu met her there.

"Senator Amidala."

"Master Windu."

"I understand you requested an audience with the Council."

"I did."

"The Council is occupied."

Padmé held his gaze.

"This is an emergency."

"The Council is managing a galactic war."

"So am I."

Windu remained silent.

"I need to speak with the Council."

"There isn't time."

"There has to be."

"There isn't."

Padmé took one step closer.

"Please."

Her voice was steady but desperate.

"This concerns Anakin."

Windu studied her expression.

Finally he sighed.

"Very well."

A short time later Padmé entered a smaller Council chamber.

Only Master Windu was physically present.

Moments later two holograms appeared.

Master Yoda.

Master Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Obi-Wan looked exhausted, still wearing the dust and damage of Utapau.

Yoda stood quietly with his cane upon Kashyyyk.

Windu nodded once.

"You asked for this meeting."

Padmé took a slow breath.

"I am Senator Padmé Amidala of Naboo."

The room became completely silent.

"I am also Anakin Skywalker's wife."

Obi-Wan blinked.

Padmé continued before anyone could interrupt.

"We have been married for three years."

Obi-Wan stared in astonishment.

"I am also currently pregnant with Anakin's child."

No one spoke.

Padmé continued.

"Lately Anakin has been repeatedly experiencing Force visions of me dying during childbirth, and it has been affecting his judgment a great deal."

Yoda listened carefully.

"I have recently learned that Chancellor Palpatine privately spoke with Anakin and offered him a way to purportedly save me from dying."

Padmé lowered her head briefly.

"I have concluded that Anakin's attack on Master Windu is a direct consequence of that."

She looked directly at Windu.

"I understand why you felt the need to expel Anakin."

Her voice remained calm despite the emotion beneath it.

"I admit his actions were not acceptable."

She paused.

"However, Anakin needs help."

Her eyes moved from one Master to the next.

"He desperately needs help."

Another pause.

"If any of you could spare some time to provide some help to Anakin, I would greatly appreciate it."

Master Windu answered first.

"We have a war to fight."

Padmé met his gaze without flinching.

"We don't have the resources to help a single senator and an expelled Jedi Knight."

Padmé frowned.

"What war?"

Windu looked puzzled.

"Dooku is dead."

He remained silent.

"Grievous is dead."

Still he said nothing.

"What war remains that is so important that you cannot even consider helping a former friend of yours?"

The question lingered in the chamber.

Obi-Wan finally spoke.

"Padmé is right."

Everyone turned toward his hologram.

"We should not abandon Anakin just because of what he did in the Chancellor's office."

He sighed.

"He does need help."

Padmé looked at him gratefully.

"Once I get back from Utapau, I'll take a look at him."

He managed a faint smile.

"Although I'm not really an expert in Force visions."

He glanced toward Yoda.

"That would be Master Yoda's specialty."

Yoda slowly nodded.

"Hmm."

He leaned lightly upon his cane.

"Visit young Skywalker, I will, when I return from Kashyyyk."

His ears drooped slightly.

"Terrible distress, he must be in."

Padmé felt some of the weight lift from her shoulders.

"Thank you, Masters."

She bowed respectfully.

"When you are ready to find him, he should be at my apartment in the Senate apartment complex."

Neither Obi-Wan nor Yoda objected.

The meeting ended.

Padmé quietly departed the Temple.

Some time later, back at the apartment, Anakin stirred.

He groaned.

His eyes slowly opened.

The ceiling came into focus.

He blinked several times before noticing he was lying on the couch instead of the floor.

He sat upright.

His head pounded.

R2-D2 rolled over and emitted a cheerful whistle.

Anakin rubbed the side of his head.

"R2..."

The astromech chirped innocently.

"What did you do to me?"

R2 answered with several brief beeps that sounded entirely too innocent.

Anakin narrowed his eyes.

"I don't believe that for a second."

R2 gave another harmless little whistle.

Anakin sighed.

He looked around the apartment.

It was quiet.

"Where's Padmé?"

Before Threepio could answer, R2 extended his holographic projector.

A blue image of Padmé appeared above the astromech.

Her recorded message began.

"Anakin, I am going to seek some help for your Force visions."

Anakin watched silently.

"Don't worry about me."

She smiled gently.

"Please take care of yourself and stay here at home."

Her expression became slightly firmer.

"Treat the droids well."

R2 gave a pleased beep.

"Do not lash out at them; they are innocent."

Threepio nodded emphatically.

"Do not destroy my apartment."

Anakin couldn't help letting out a quiet, embarrassed sigh.

"Try to remain calm."

She smiled again.

"I am sure we can find a solution to this together and it will all end soon."

The recording drew to a close.

"I love you."

The hologram disappeared.

Silence returned.

Anakin slowly stood.

He began pacing across the apartment.

Threepio watched nervously.

After several laps around the room, the protocol droid disappeared briefly into the kitchen.

He returned carrying a steaming cup.

"I thought perhaps... some hot caf might be agreeable."

Anakin stopped pacing.

He accepted the cup.

"Thank you."

Threepio seemed relieved.

"You're quite welcome, sir."

Anakin took a slow sip.

The warmth helped.

Threepio searched desperately for a subject that might occupy Anakin's thoughts.

"If I may ask..."

Anakin looked over.

"What was it like serving with the clone army?"

Anakin blinked.

"What?"

"The war."

Threepio gave an awkward smile.

"You've served on so many worlds. I imagine there must have been rather remarkable experiences."

For the first time that day, Anakin's expression softened.

"You really want to hear about that?"

"Oh yes, sir."

Anakin leaned back into the couch.

"Well..."

He stared into the cup for a moment.

"There was this one mission on Christophsis..."

Threepio listened attentively.

Anakin found himself describing desperate sieges, impossible rescues, clever clone officers, dangerous improvisations, and victories earned at tremendous cost.

Threepio occasionally interrupted with polite questions.

"My goodness. And Captain Rex simply volunteered for that?"

"He usually did."

"Extraordinary."

Anakin chuckled quietly.

"He's stubborn."

"I had gathered as much."

As the stories continued, Anakin's pacing stopped entirely.

His grip on the caf relaxed.

For a little while, instead of dwelling on everything he had lost, he found himself remembering comrades, battles, friendships, and moments of courage shared across the long years of the Clone Wars.

Even R2-D2 remained nearby, quietly listening, punctuating the stories now and then with an indignant whistle whenever Anakin omitted one of the astromech's own heroic exploits.

# Part 3

The afternoon sun had begun its slow descent over Coruscant by the time the apartment door slid open.

Padmé stepped inside.

Anakin immediately stood from the couch.

"There you are."

She smiled warmly.

"There I am."

They embraced without another word.

For a long moment neither of them spoke.

When they finally separated, Padmé rested a hand against his cheek.

"How are you feeling?"

Anakin managed a tired smile.

"Better than last night."

"Threepio tells me you've been talking."

Threepio straightened proudly.

"Master Anakin has been recounting several fascinating Clone Wars campaigns, Mistress Padmé."

Anakin gave the protocol droid a small nod.

"It helped."

"I'm glad."

Padmé led him toward the couch.

"I have something to tell you."

Anakin's smile faded.

"What happened?"

"I went to the Jedi Temple."

His expression immediately darkened.

"You did?"

"I asked the Council for help."

Anakin sighed.

"Padmé..."

"They agreed."

He looked surprised.

"They did?"

"Master Obi-Wan and Master Yoda are both coming to see you."

The surprise quickly disappeared.

"So Yoda's coming."

There was unmistakable bitterness in his voice.

Padmé noticed it immediately.

"Anakin..."

"He helped expel me."

"It wasn't only Master Yoda."

"He was still part of the decision."

Padmé sat beside him.

"They're still your old friends."

He looked away.

"They care about you."

"They certainly have a strange way of showing it."

"They're wise."

Anakin let out a short, humorless laugh.

"They don't understand at all."

Padmé gently took his hand.

"What makes you say that?"

"I already talked to Yoda."

She blinked.

"You did?"

"About the visions."

"What did he say?"

"He told me I should let go of everything I was afraid to lose."

Padmé listened quietly.

"It didn't help at all."

She remained thoughtful for several moments.

"Did you tell him who was dying in the visions?"

Anakin hesitated.

"No."

Padmé nodded slowly.

"I think that's the problem."

He frowned.

"What do you mean?"

"He didn't know what you were actually dealing with."

"He knew enough."

"No."

She shook her head.

"He knew you were afraid."

"That's obvious."

"But he didn't know why."

Anakin folded his arms.

"I don't think that changes anything."

"I do."

She smiled gently.

"Now he knows."

Anakin looked unconvinced.

"You think he'll suddenly have something better to say than 'let go'?"

"I think he'll understand your situation better."

He looked toward the window.

"I doubt it."

Padmé squeezed his hand.

"Just give him a chance."

Several seconds passed.

Finally Anakin nodded.

"I'll try."

Padmé smiled.

"That's all I'm asking."

For a little while the apartment became quiet.

Padmé watched him carefully.

She had expected the anger.

She had expected the exhaustion.

What she had not expected was how completely consumed he had become.

Everything seemed to revolve around one thought.

Her death.

Nothing else appeared capable of reaching him.

She decided to test that impression.

"The Senate has already begun discussing the election for a new Chancellor."

Anakin nodded absentmindedly.

"I heard."

"What do you think?"

"I don't."

She blinked.

"You don't?"

"I don't care."

Padmé studied him.

"Obi-Wan defeated General Grievous."

"I know."

"What do you think about that?"

"I'm glad he's alive."

"And?"

Anakin shrugged.

"That's all."

She tried again.

"When I was at the Temple today, they were making preparations for the funerals of Masters Tiin, Kolar, and Fisto."

He quietly stared into his untouched cup of caf.

"I see."

"I wonder who they'll appoint to replace them on the Council."

Anakin looked up.

"I don't know."

"You don't have any guesses?"

"No."

His answer came immediately.

Padmé slowly exhaled.

She changed direction.

"I've been thinking about the baby."

That finally drew a little more attention.

Anakin looked toward her.

"Oh?"

"Do you think it'll be a boy or a girl?"

"I don't know."

She smiled.

"Do you have any names in mind?"

He thought for a moment.

"Not really."

"Where would you like our child to grow up?"

He hesitated.

"Somewhere safe."

It was more engagement than before, but only slightly.

Padmé saw it immediately.

She reached over and placed both hands around one of his.

"Anakin."

He looked at her.

"Your obsession with this vision is destroying what's right in front of you."

He remained silent.

"It's making it difficult for you to live in the present."

"The present doesn't matter."

"It does."

"Not if the future is doomed."

Padmé shook her head.

"The future isn't doomed."

"You don't know that."

"Only you think it's doomed."

"You've never had Force visions."

"No."

"You don't know what they're like."

"I don't."

She met his eyes.

"But I don't need to experience Force phenomena myself to understand basic principles."

He frowned.

"What principles?"

"That fear changes the way people think."

Before Anakin could answer, Threepio cautiously raised one hand.

"If I may, Mistress Padmé?"

She turned toward him.

"Of course."

"I believe I may have accidentally discovered something useful earlier."

"You did?"

"When Master Anakin first awoke, he was becoming quite distressed."

Anakin smiled faintly.

"That's one way to describe it."

"I found that asking him about his experiences during the Clone Wars distracted him remarkably well."

Threepio sounded rather pleased with himself.

"It occurred to me that even if Master Anakin is terribly preoccupied with the future and rather indifferent toward the present..."

He paused dramatically.

"...he may still be distracted by the past."

Padmé looked thoughtfully at the protocol droid.

"The past..."

She smiled.

"I think you're onto something."

Threepio beamed.

"Oh! Thank you, Mistress Padmé."

Padmé turned back toward Anakin.

"Can I tell you a story?"

He looked puzzled.

"What kind of story?"

"About when I was little."

He shrugged.

"Sure."

She smiled.

"When I was a child on Naboo, I once became absolutely convinced that one of our shaaks had decided it lived inside our kitchen..."

Anakin raised an eyebrow.

"It lived in your kitchen?"

"So I believed."

He chuckled.

Padmé continued.

She spoke about growing up in Theed.

About climbing trees despite being told not to.

About festivals.

About her parents.

About school.

About Lake Country.

About learning responsibility long before entering politics.

Occasionally Anakin asked questions.

"You actually fell into the river?"

"I absolutely did."

"And your sister laughed?"

"For weeks."

He laughed again.

Hours quietly slipped by.

For the first time in many days, Anakin found himself thinking about something other than terrifying visions.

The apartment's door chimed.

Padmé stood.

"I think that's Obi-Wan."

The door opened.

Obi-Wan Kenobi stepped inside.

He looked exhausted from his long journey but smiled warmly nonetheless.

"Hello."

Padmé greeted him first.

"Master Obi-Wan."

"It's good to see you."

His eyes shifted toward Anakin.

"Hello, Anakin."

Anakin managed a tired smile.

"Master."

Obi-Wan crossed the room.

"How are you feeling?"

Anakin considered the question.

"Not great."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"I think that's understandable."

He sat opposite him.

"You've been through quite a lot."

Anakin quietly stared at the floor.

Obi-Wan spoke gently.

"You're self-destructing."

Anakin looked up.

"I know."

"And doing that won't help Padmé survive."

# Part 4

The quiet of Naboo settled over Varykino Villa as evening gave way to night.

The lakes outside reflected the stars with scarcely a ripple.

Gone were the endless streams of traffic that filled Coruscant's sky.

Gone were the constant reports from the Senate and the war.

Only the wind moved through the trees.

Inside the villa, Anakin and Padmé slept peacefully.

For the first time in many weeks, Anakin did not toss restlessly.

His hand remained gently wrapped around Padmé's.

As sleep carried him deeper into the Force, another vision came.

He saw himself sitting beside Padmé on the villa's broad stone balcony.

The lake stretched peacefully before them beneath the afternoon sun.

Padmé was smiling.

Not with relief.

Not with uncertainty.

Simply smiling.

Laughter echoed nearby.

Anakin turned.

Two young children were racing across the terrace.

A boy.

A girl.

The boy laughed as he chased his sister around one of the stone columns.

The girl laughed even harder as she escaped him.

Padmé leaned comfortably against Anakin's shoulder.

Neither of them spoke.

They simply watched.

The vision faded.

Morning sunlight entered through the bedroom windows.

Anakin opened his eyes.

He felt... lighter.

The crushing weight that had followed him for weeks was simply absent.

He looked beside him.

Padmé was already awake.

She immediately noticed the change.

She smiled.

"You look a lot better today."

Anakin smiled back.

"I feel a lot better."

"What happened?"

He sat up and looked out toward the lake.

"We made the right decision in coming to Naboo."

Padmé leaned against the headboard.

"Why do you say that?"

He thought for a moment.

"Something feels different here."

She listened quietly.

"Even the Force behaves differently here."

Padmé smiled warmly.

"Oh, that is great to hear."

"Oh."

He looked back at her with an almost amused expression.

"And, by the way..."

"Yes?"

"I think we're having twins."

Padmé stared.

"...What?"

"I think we're having twins."

She laughed.

"How do you know?"

Anakin simply smiled.

"The Force works in mysterious ways."

Padmé laughed again.

"I suppose that's one answer."

"It is."

Over the days that followed, the transformation became impossible to ignore.

The fear that had dominated Anakin's every waking moment steadily loosened its grip.

He smiled more often.

He slept soundly.

He spent long afternoons walking with Padmé through the gardens surrounding Varykino.

Sometimes they rowed together across the lake.

Sometimes they simply sat on the balcony watching the sunset.

Most importantly, Padmé could finally speak with him about subjects other than the visions.

One afternoon she carried a datapad onto the terrace.

"The Senate has elected a new Chancellor."

Anakin looked up from the lake.

"Who?"

"Bail Organa."

He smiled.

"I'm glad."

"So am I."

Padmé visibly relaxed.

"I trust him."

"I'm happy for you."

It was such an ordinary conversation that it almost brought tears to her eyes.

Weeks earlier he would scarcely have cared enough to ask.

Now he genuinely wanted to know.

As the pregnancy progressed, Anakin increasingly found himself thinking about the future in an entirely different way.

One evening they sat together overlooking the water.

"We should stay here."

Padmé smiled.

"For a while?"

"For good."

She looked at him.

"I mean it."

He gestured toward the peaceful landscape.

"We could raise our family here."

"Naboo is beautiful."

"It doesn't have to be temporary."

Padmé considered his words carefully.

"I still want to return to politics eventually."

"I know."

"I've devoted my life to public service."

"And I don't want to take that away from you."

She reached for his hand.

"But..."

He nodded.

"...I would like you to stay here after the children are born."

Padmé smiled.

"I was already planning to take maternity leave."

Anakin looked relieved.

"Really?"

"Really."

She laughed softly.

"I think our children deserve a little time with both of their parents."

Secretly, Padmé felt even greater relief than she admitted.

For weeks Anakin had spoken only about preventing her death.

Now he spoke about raising children.

About family.

About the years ahead.

He had stopped fixating on childbirth itself.

Instead, he had begun imagining life beyond it.

One afternoon, while repairing a small mechanical device in the workshop behind the villa, Anakin became unusually quiet.

Padmé noticed.

"What are you thinking about?"

"The Jedi."

She waited.

"I won't be going back."

Padmé sat beside him.

"No."

"I've been expelled."

He looked down at the tools in his hands.

"And now they know about us."

He smiled faintly.

"Our marriage."

"There isn't any chance they'll readmit me."

Padmé gently rested a hand on his shoulder.

"Do you miss it?"

"I miss parts of it."

He looked toward the horizon.

"I'd like my lightsaber back someday."

She understood immediately.

"I don't think the Council is likely to return it."

"I know."

He sighed.

"It isn't just a weapon."

"What is it?"

"It's part of who I am."

Padmé thought for a long moment before answering.

"It was part of who you were."

He looked at her.

"That's different."

"Your identity isn't exactly the same anymore."

She smiled gently.

"You're someone's husband."

He smiled.

"You will soon be someone's father."

He looked down thoughtfully.

"You don't have to spend the rest of your life trying to hold onto the identity you had as a Jedi."

She squeezed his hand.

"You can build a new one here."

He considered that for several quiet minutes.

"I hadn't thought about it that way."

"You don't stop being yourself because your life changes."

He slowly nodded.

"I suppose not."

The conversation lingered in his thoughts long afterward.

Little by little, he stopped defining himself only by what he had lost.

Instead, he began noticing what he still had.

Padmé.

Their home.

The children who would soon arrive.

She quietly touched Anakin's shoulder.

He opened his eyes immediately.

"I think it's time."

For a brief moment she expected to see the old panic return.

Instead, she found calm determination.

He never let go of her hand.

She smiled at the tiny boy.

The twins rested peacefully in their arms.

Several hours later, the family returned to Varykino Villa.

The journey home felt different from every journey that had come before.

The villa was no longer simply a place to escape the turmoil of Coruscant.

Days soon settled into a gentle rhythm.

Morning walks through the gardens became walks carrying Luke or Leia.

Quiet evenings on the balcony became evenings rocking sleeping children while the sun disappeared behind the lake.

The nursery filled with soft laughter instead of anxious conversations.

Threepio eagerly assisted with every imaginable household task.

"I must say, Master Luke and Mistress Leia are both exceptionally well-behaved."

R2-D2 answered with a doubtful whistle that suggested Threepio had somehow missed several recent incidents.

Padmé laughed.

Anakin looked across the room at his family.

Weeks earlier he had believed the future contained only loss.

Now Luke slept peacefully in his arms while Leia rested against Padmé's shoulder.

The future he had feared had never arrived.

A different future had.

One that began each morning beside the quiet waters of Naboo, surrounded by the family he had fought so desperately to protect.
