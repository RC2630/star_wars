# Part 1

The Republic gunships roared across the red skies of Geonosis.

Below them, the battlefield still burned from the opening clash of the Clone Wars. Jedi and clone troopers were scattered across the desert, fighting pockets of Separatist forces amid smoking wreckage and drifting dust.

Inside one of the gunships, Obi-Wan Kenobi stood near the cockpit while Anakin Skywalker watched the horizon ahead. Count Dooku's solar sailer was visible in the distance, racing toward a hangar.

"We're gaining on him," one of the clone pilots reported.

Then the gunship lurched violently.

Padmé Amidala lost her grip.

"Padmé!" Anakin shouted.

He lunged forward, but she was already gone.

The senator tumbled from the open side of the gunship, disappearing into the swirling sands far below.

"Stop the ship!" Anakin yelled.

The pilot looked toward Obi-Wan.

"Turn around! Now!"

"No," Obi-Wan said immediately.

Anakin spun toward him.

"She fell out!"

"We have a chance to catch Dooku."

"Padmé could be dying!"

"There are thousands of clone troopers on this battlefield. Someone will find her."

"You don't know that!"

Obi-Wan's expression hardened.

"Anakin, your duty is here."

"My duty?" Anakin snapped. "I was assigned to protect her!"

"And if Dooku escapes, this war becomes far more dangerous for the entire Republic."

"Then let someone else chase him!"

"We are the closest."

The gunship continued forward.

Anakin stared at Obi-Wan in disbelief.

"You'd just leave her there?"

"I would complete the mission."

For a moment it looked as though Anakin might physically force his way into the cockpit.

Instead he clenched his fists so tightly that his knuckles turned white.

The rest of the flight passed in angry silence.

By the time they reached the hangar, neither man spoke to the other.

The pursuit continued.

Dooku escaped deeper into the complex.

Obi-Wan and Anakin confronted him.

The duel that followed was brief and disastrous.

Obi-Wan was injured.

Anakin charged ahead recklessly and paid dearly for it, losing his right arm to Dooku's blade.

Only the arrival of Master Yoda prevented a complete victory for the Count.

Even then, Dooku escaped.

When it was over, Anakin lay on the floor of the hangar in agony while clone medics rushed toward him.

The first thing he asked was not about his arm.

"Padmé."

The medic looked confused.

"Sir?"

"Senator Amidala. Was she found?"

The clone shook his head.

"I don't know, General."

Anakin closed his eyes.

Nobody else seemed to know either.

Nobody had seen her.

Nobody had reported finding her.

And the battle was over.

Hours later, aboard a Republic transport leaving Geonosis, Anakin sat in the medical section staring out a viewport with a bandaged stump where his arm had been.

Obi-Wan entered quietly.

For several moments neither spoke.

Finally Obi-Wan said, "How are you feeling?"

Anakin laughed bitterly.

"Which part?"

The older Jedi sat across from him.

"The doctors say you'll recover."

"Dooku escaped."

"Yes."

"I lost my arm."

"Yes."

"Dooku still escaped."

Obi-Wan sighed.

"Anakin—"

"And Padmé is dead."

The words hung in the air.

Obi-Wan looked away.

"We don't know that."

"No one found her."

"The battlefield was chaotic."

"She fell from a gunship."

Silence.

Then Anakin looked directly at him.

"You killed her."

Obi-Wan's eyes widened.

"Anakin."

"If you had stopped the ship—"

"We were pursuing Dooku."

"And what did that accomplish?"

Obi-Wan's face tightened.

"That isn't fair."

"Fair?"

Anakin stood.

Pain shot through his body, but he barely noticed.

"She fell. I told you to stop."

"You were emotional."

"Of course I was emotional!"

The medical staff nearby pretended not to listen.

Obi-Wan lowered his voice.

"Your judgment was compromised."

"My judgment was right."

"No."

"She needed help!"

"We had a mission."

"And we failed!"

The words echoed through the compartment.

Several clones glanced over.

Neither Jedi noticed.

Anakin's breathing had become uneven.

Obi-Wan studied him carefully.

Something finally clicked into place.

Something he should have recognized sooner.

"Anakin," he said quietly, "why does this affect you so deeply?"

Anakin looked away.

"I told you."

"No."

"I was assigned to protect her."

"That isn't the real answer."

Anakin remained silent.

Obi-Wan continued.

"You spent months with her."

Anakin said nothing.

"You care about her."

Still silence.

"You care far more than you should."

Anakin's jaw tightened.

Obi-Wan suddenly felt cold.

"Anakin..."

The younger Jedi finally met his gaze.

Neither needed to say the words aloud.

Obi-Wan understood.

The realization struck him like a physical blow.

"You loved her."

Anakin looked away.

That was answer enough.

The silence stretched.

When Obi-Wan finally spoke, his voice was filled with disappointment.

"You know what that means."

"I don't care."

"You are a Jedi."

"I don't care."

"Anakin—"

"I said I don't care!"

Several people turned to stare.

Anakin no longer cared.

"If she's dead, then nothing else matters."

Obi-Wan rose to his feet.

"You're not thinking clearly."

"My life is over because of you."

"That isn't true."

"I'll never forgive you."

The words hit harder than either of them expected.

Obi-Wan looked genuinely wounded.

"Anakin..."

"I mean it."

The younger Jedi's eyes burned with anger and grief.

"I never want to see you again."

A long silence followed.

Finally Obi-Wan straightened.

"As long as you remain in the Order, that's impossible."

Something changed in Anakin's expression.

A decision.

Sudden.

Impulsive.

Absolute.

"Then I won't remain in the Order."

Obi-Wan stared.

"What?"

Anakin reached up with his left hand.

He ignited his temporary lightsaber.

The green blade sprang to life.

Without hesitation, he sliced through his Padawan braid.

The severed braid fell to the floor.

Every conversation nearby stopped.

Every clone medic froze.

Anakin deactivated the weapon.

Then he tossed it.

Obi-Wan caught it automatically.

For several seconds neither moved.

The lightsaber felt unexpectedly heavy in Obi-Wan's hands.

Anakin looked at him one final time.

"I quit."

Then he turned and walked away.

"Anakin!"

The younger man never looked back.

By the time the transport reached Coruscant, the news had already spread through portions of the Temple.

Anakin Skywalker had left the Jedi Order.

Meanwhile, far away on Geonosis, the desert remained silent.

Hours passed.

Then more hours.

The battlefield emptied.

Clone forces withdrew.

Separatist forces retreated.

Eventually there was nobody left.

Half-buried in red sand, Padmé Amidala slowly opened her eyes.

Pain exploded through her body.

She groaned.

The sky above had begun to darken.

For several moments she simply lay there.

Then memory returned.

The gunship.

The fall.

The battle.

"Anakin..."

Her voice barely carried.

With immense effort she sat up.

Every muscle protested.

The wounds from the nexu's claws still ached beneath hastily treated bandages. The fall had left her bruised and exhausted.

But she was alive.

That much was certain.

She slowly got to her feet.

The horizon seemed endless.

Then she spotted something familiar.

A Republic transport.

Her ship.

Far away.

Very far away.

Padmé sighed.

Then she started walking.

Hours later she stumbled into the abandoned arena.

The great petranaki arena stood eerily silent.

Broken droids littered the ground.

Burn marks scarred the stone.

And sitting near one of the entrances were two familiar figures.

"Artoo?"

The astromech immediately whistled excitedly.

"Senator Amidala!" C-3PO exclaimed. "Oh my goodness!"

The protocol droid hurried toward her.

"We thought everyone had already departed!"

Padmé smiled weakly.

"So did I."

"You appear injured."

"I am."

"Oh dear."

Artoo rolled beside her, beeping happily.

Padmé reached down and touched his dome.

"It's good to see you too."

Together they made their way back toward her ship.

The journey was slow.

But eventually they arrived.

The vessel was still intact.

Padmé collapsed into the pilot's seat.

"Home," she said.

The ship lifted into the sky.

Hours later she arrived on Naboo.

Rather than returning immediately to Theed, she traveled to Varykino Villa.

The lakeside retreat was quiet and secluded.

Exactly what she needed.

Her injuries required rest.

The droids remained with her.

Artoo occupied himself around the villa grounds while C-3PO fussed endlessly over her recovery.

"Miss Amidala, you should remain seated."

"I have been seated all morning."

"Yes, but sitting is excellent for healing."

Padmé laughed.

"You're impossible."

"Thank you."

"That wasn't a compliment."

Several more days passed.

Her strength gradually returned.

The bruises began to fade.

The cuts started healing.

One afternoon she sat on a balcony overlooking the lake while Artoo rested nearby and C-3PO organized supplies inside.

For the first time since Geonosis, peace seemed possible.

Then a distant sound interrupted the quiet.

A vehicle approaching.

Padmé frowned.

She had not been expecting visitors.

The sound grew louder.

A moment later the front entrance opened.

Footsteps echoed through the villa.

C-3PO turned.

"Oh!"

The protocol droid sounded startled.

Padmé rose slowly from her chair.

Someone entered the room.

Anakin Skywalker.

He looked exhausted.

His Jedi robes were dusty from travel.

His right arm was gone, replaced only by bandages.

His expression carried the hollow look of someone who had been grieving for days.

He had come to Varykino for one reason only.

To say goodbye to a place filled with memories of someone he believed he had lost forever.

Instead he stopped dead in the doorway.

Padmé stared at him.

Anakin stared back.

Neither spoke.

Artoo emitted a cheerful whistle.

C-3PO blinked.

"Oh dear," the droid said softly.

For several seconds Anakin simply stood frozen, unable to process what he was seeing.

Padmé was alive.

Standing.

Breathing.

Looking directly at him.

And suddenly nothing else in the galaxy seemed to matter.

# Part 2

For several seconds after Anakin entered the villa, neither he nor Padmé spoke.

The silence was broken only by the gentle sounds of the lake outside and an occasional whistle from R2-D2.

Then Padmé took a step forward.

"Anakin?"

His eyes remained fixed on her.

"You're alive."

"I am."

He shook his head slightly.

"I thought you were dead."

Padmé looked equally bewildered.

"I didn't know what happened to you."

Anakin laughed once, though there was little humor in it.

"I didn't know what happened to you either."

They stood staring at one another, both struggling to reconcile days of grief with the reality before them.

Finally Padmé noticed the bandages where his right arm should have been.

Her expression immediately changed.

"Anakin..."

Instinctively, he glanced down.

"It's gone."

"What happened?"

"I'll explain."

Padmé's voice became firm.

"First, I'm taking you to Theed."

Anakin blinked.

"What?"

"You need a prosthetic arm."

"I can manage."

"No."

Padmé folded her arms.

"You're getting a prosthetic arm."

Even injured and still recovering, she sounded every bit the senator who could command entire committees.

Anakin found himself smiling.

"I've missed that tone."

"Good."

She pointed toward the door.

"Let's go."

C-3PO looked delighted.

"Oh, excellent. Someone is finally being sensible."

"Nobody asked you," Anakin said.

"Of course not."

The trip to Theed began shortly afterward.

As the transport crossed Naboo's breathtaking landscapes, Padmé finally received the answers she had been seeking.

She sat beside Anakin while the green fields passed beneath them.

"Tell me everything."

So he did.

He told her about the pursuit of Dooku.

About the argument aboard the gunship.

About reaching the hangar.

About the duel.

About losing his arm.

Padmé listened quietly.

Then he described the confrontation with Obi-Wan aboard the transport leaving Geonosis.

The anger.

The accusations.

The revelation that Obi-Wan had figured out how much Padmé meant to him.

And finally his departure from the Jedi Order.

When he finished, the cabin was silent.

Padmé stared out the viewport for a long moment.

Then she turned back.

"Would you go back now?"

Anakin frowned.

"Go back where?"

"To the Jedi."

He considered the question.

"You survived."

"Yes."

"So the reason I left no longer exists."

Padmé nodded.

"That's one way of looking at it."

Anakin shook his head.

"No."

"What do you mean?"

He looked down at his remaining hand.

"I made my choice."

Padmé remained silent.

Anakin continued.

"I was angry when I left."

"Very angry."

"Maybe."

Padmé raised an eyebrow.

"Maybe?"

He laughed softly.

"Fine. Extremely angry."

"That's more accurate."

"But even now..." he said. "I don't think I can go back."

"Because of Obi-Wan?"

"Partly."

Padmé waited.

Anakin took a breath.

"I've spent years trying to be the Jedi they wanted me to be."

He looked toward the horizon.

"And now I don't want to anymore."

Padmé studied him carefully.

There was no anger in his voice now.

Only certainty.

"So you've made up your mind."

"Yes."

She nodded slowly.

"Then I won't ask again."

Anakin smiled gratefully.

"Thank you."

A short time later they arrived in Theed.

The physicians immediately began arranging treatment.

The process was lengthy.

Measurements were taken.

Interfaces were calibrated.

The surgical specialists explained every detail.

Padmé remained nearby throughout the procedure.

Eventually Anakin emerged with a fully functional prosthetic arm.

The fingers flexed smoothly.

The wrist rotated naturally.

He stared at it in fascination.

"It feels strange."

"It will for a while," one physician said.

Anakin opened and closed his new hand.

"Strange is better than nothing."

Padmé smiled.

"Much better."

As they departed the medical center, Anakin glanced at her injuries.

Though still visible, they were healing.

The deep scratches left by the nexu had begun to close.

The bruising from the fall had faded considerably.

"You're recovering well."

Padmé looked down.

"I've had time."

"Keep resting."

She laughed.

"That sounds suspiciously like medical advice."

"It is."

"Now you're giving orders?"

"I'm retired."

"Then you can't give orders anymore."

"I can still try."

The journey back to Varykino Villa felt very different from the journey to Theed.

The crisis had passed.

For the first time since Geonosis, they had the opportunity to think about the future.

As Naboo's countryside drifted past beneath them, the conversation eventually turned toward a subject neither could avoid.

Padmé spoke first.

"What happens now?"

Anakin knew exactly what she meant.

He looked at her.

"You told me you loved me."

The memory returned instantly.

The arena.

The beasts.

The possibility of death.

Padmé smiled.

"I remember."

"I told you I loved you."

"I remember that too."

Anakin leaned back in his seat.

"I don't think either of us can pretend those conversations never happened."

"No."

"And I don't want to."

Padmé reached over and took his left hand.

"Neither do I."

For several moments neither spoke.

Then Anakin asked quietly:

"Will you marry me?"

Padmé smiled before he had even finished the sentence.

"Yes."

There was no hesitation.

No uncertainty.

No debate.

Only relief.

The decision felt less like a beginning than an acknowledgment of something both had already accepted.

Several days later a Naboo holy man arrived at Varykino Villa.

The ceremony was intentionally private.

No senators.

No Jedi.

No political allies.

No family.

Only those absolutely necessary.

The holy man stood before the lake.

Padmé stood beside Anakin.

R2-D2 positioned himself nearby.

C-3PO stood proudly beside him.

"I believe this makes us witnesses," the protocol droid announced.

Artoo whistled.

"Quite right."

The ceremony itself was simple.

The holy man spoke of devotion, trust, and commitment.

Then he asked the final question.

"Do you willingly join your lives together?"

"I do," Padmé said.

"I do," Anakin replied.

The holy man smiled.

"Then before the eyes of the Force, before the people of Naboo, and before those gathered here today, you are husband and wife."

Padmé kissed Anakin.

Artoo emitted an enthusiastic series of whistles.

C-3PO applauded.

"Oh my. How wonderful."

For the first time in many months, both Anakin and Padmé felt truly at peace.

The following weeks passed quietly.

Padmé continued recovering.

Anakin learned to use his prosthetic arm.

At first he struggled.

Simple actions required concentration.

Picking up delicate objects.

Adjusting clothing.

Using tools.

But his natural coordination gradually adapted.

One afternoon, several weeks after the wedding, a communication signal arrived.

Anakin activated the receiver.

The hologram of Master Yoda appeared.

Anakin immediately straightened.

"Master Yoda."

Yoda nodded.

"Skywalker."

Padmé looked up from across the room.

Yoda's eyes shifted toward her.

"Alive, Senator Amidala is."

Padmé smiled.

"Very much so, Master Yoda."

Yoda seemed genuinely pleased.

"Good news, that is."

Anakin folded his arms.

"I didn't know she survived until I arrived here."

"Hmm."

The old Jedi nodded slowly.

"Learned that, we have."

For a moment neither spoke.

Then Yoda continued.

"Contact you, Obi-Wan did not wish to."

Anakin's expression darkened slightly.

Yoda noticed.

"Hurt by your last conversation, he remains."

Anakin looked away.

"I know."

"So speak with you, he asked me to."

The younger man nodded.

Yoda waited.

Finally Anakin spoke.

"Padmé survived."

"So she did."

"And we got married."

Padmé nearly laughed at how casually he delivered the announcement.

Yoda's ears lifted slightly.

For several seconds he simply stared.

"Married."

"Yes."

"Hmm."

Yoda's expression became difficult to read.

At last he nodded once.

"Done is done."

Nobody added anything further to that observation.

Eventually Yoda asked:

"Relations with the Jedi Order, maintain will you?"

Anakin considered carefully.

"I think so."

"Think so?"

"I'm not interested in fighting with the Jedi."

Yoda listened.

"But I'm also not planning to come back."

The old Master's eyes softened.

"Certain, are you?"

"Yes."

Yoda nodded.

"Accept that, we will."

Anakin looked surprised.

Yoda noticed.

"Choice yours, it is."

For several moments silence settled between them.

Then Yoda spoke again.

"When ready, if wish to, come to the Temple."

Anakin frowned.

"For what?"

"Build a new lightsaber."

The younger man glanced toward the empty space at his belt.

The lightsaber he had surrendered before leaving the Order remained on Coruscant.

Yoda continued.

"Jedi no longer be, you may choose."

Anakin listened carefully.

"But a lightsaber, build you still can."

Anakin thought about that.

"I'll think about it."

Yoda nodded.

"Good."

The hologram began fading.

"Take care of yourself, Skywalker."

"I will."

"Senator."

"Master Yoda."

The transmission ended.

For a long time afterward, neither Anakin nor Padmé spoke.

Then Padmé smiled.

"That went better than expected."

"It did."

Three more months passed.

Summer gradually gave way to autumn on Naboo.

Padmé's injuries healed almost completely.

Anakin became increasingly comfortable with his prosthetic arm.

Life settled into a peaceful routine.

Then one morning Padmé entered the villa carrying a datapad.

Anakin looked up.

"What is it?"

She smiled.

"A message from Coruscant."

His expression immediately became suspicious.

"That sounds dangerous."

"It means I'm returning to work."

Anakin sighed dramatically.

"I was afraid you were going to say that."

Padmé laughed.

"The Senate has survived without me for several months."

"Barely."

"Possibly."

She sat beside him.

"I'm going back."

Anakin nodded.

He had known this moment would eventually arrive.

Padmé studied him.

"Are you ready to come with me?"

The answer arrived instantly.

"Yes."

She blinked.

"That was fast."

"I'm not staying here while you go halfway across the galaxy."

Padmé smiled warmly.

"I thought that might be your answer."

Preparations began immediately.

Soon they departed Naboo together.

As their transport crossed hyperspace toward Coruscant, Padmé decided there was one conversation they needed to have.

She folded her arms.

"Anakin."

His tone immediately became cautious.

"Yes?"

"I know that voice."

"What voice?"

"The voice you use when you're about to lecture me."

Padmé ignored the comment.

"When we arrive on Coruscant, I need you to promise me something."

Anakin sighed.

"There it is."

"Promise."

"What?"

"Don't get yourself into trouble."

"I never get into trouble."

Padmé stared at him.

Anakin lasted three seconds before laughing.

"Fine. Continue."

"No conflicts with the Jedi."

Anakin nodded.

"Agreed."

"No arguments."

"Agreed."

"No confrontations."

"Agreed."

"No dramatic speeches."

He looked offended.

"I give excellent speeches."

"Anakin."

"Fine."

Padmé smiled.

"Remain low-profile."

"For now."

"For now?"

Anakin grinned.

"For now."

Padmé shook her head.

But she was smiling too.

Together they watched the stars streaming past the viewport as Coruscant drew closer.

A new chapter of their lives awaited them there.

# Part 3

Several weeks after arriving on Coruscant, Anakin finally made his decision.

One evening, while he and Padmé were eating dinner in their apartment overlooking the endless cityscape, he set down his glass.

"I think I'm going to do it."

Padmé looked up.

"Do what?"

"Go back to the Temple."

Her eyebrow rose immediately.

"To rejoin the Jedi?"

"No."

Anakin shook his head.

"To build the lightsaber Yoda offered."

Padmé considered this.

"You want me to come with you?"

Anakin smiled.

"You were going to ask anyway."

"I was."

"You can come."

Padmé nodded.

"Good."

She hesitated.

"I'm still not entirely sure I trust you around the Jedi."

Anakin looked offended.

"You make me sound dangerous."

"You once quit the Order by cutting off your braid with a lightsaber and throwing your weapon at your master."

Anakin opened his mouth.

Paused.

Closed it again.

"Fair point."

The following day they traveled to the Jedi Temple together.

Though Anakin had not been there since his departure months earlier, the familiar corridors immediately brought back memories.

Some pleasant.

Some not.

Neither he nor Padmé missed the curious looks from various Jedi and Temple staff as they passed.

Eventually they arrived at a quiet chamber where Yoda was waiting.

The Grand Master smiled faintly.

"Skywalker."

"Master Yoda."

Yoda's eyes shifted toward Padmé.

"Senator."

"Master Yoda."

The old Jedi leaned on his cane.

"Returned, you have."

"I said I'd think about it."

"Hmm."

Yoda nodded.

"Thought about it, you did."

Anakin smiled.

"I did."

For a moment Yoda studied him.

Then he asked:

"The green lightsaber, how did it feel?"

Anakin blinked.

"The green one?"

"The one from Geonosis."

Yoda gestured toward the Temple.

"Still here, it is."

Anakin looked surprised.

"You kept it?"

"Hmm."

Yoda nodded.

"If want it back, you do, take it you may."

Anakin considered.

"It worked well enough."

"But?"

"It never really felt like mine."

Yoda smiled slightly.

"Understand, I do."

Anakin folded his arms.

"I'd rather build my own."

"Then come."

The old Master led them deeper into the Temple.

Eventually they reached a secure chamber containing numerous kyber crystals.

The room glowed faintly from hundreds of subtle reflections.

Anakin stepped forward.

Immediately he felt the familiar pull of the Force.

The crystals seemed alive.

Calling.

Waiting.

For several minutes he moved through the chamber in silence.

Then his attention settled upon a single crystal.

He reached toward it.

The moment his fingers touched it, certainty washed over him.

"This one."

Yoda nodded.

"Chosen, it has."

The crystal was carefully removed.

Additional materials were provided.

Then Yoda escorted Anakin and Padmé to a private meditation chamber.

"There, work you may."

Anakin accepted the supplies.

"Thank you, Master."

Yoda inclined his head.

Then he departed.

The chamber became quiet.

Anakin sat cross-legged on the floor.

The various components lay arranged before him.

Padmé settled nearby and watched.

For several hours the work continued.

Anakin carefully assembled the internal systems.

Focused the crystal.

Adjusted the alignment mechanisms.

Refined the energy channels.

Padmé occasionally asked questions.

"What does that piece do?"

"Power regulation."

"And that one?"

"Crystal stabilization."

"And if you put it in backwards?"

Anakin looked up.

"The lightsaber explodes."

Padmé stared.

Anakin grinned.

"I'm kidding."

She narrowed her eyes.

"I don't believe you."

"You shouldn't."

The work continued.

The atmosphere remained peaceful.

Until someone knocked.

Anakin looked up.

Padmé glanced toward the door.

A familiar voice came from outside.

"Anakin?"

The room became still.

Obi-Wan.

There was a brief pause.

Then:

"May I come in?"

Anakin looked at Padmé.

She gave a small nod.

He stood and opened the door.

Obi-Wan stood outside.

For a moment neither spoke.

Months had passed since their last conversation.

Neither had forgotten it.

Finally Anakin stepped aside.

"Come in."

Obi-Wan entered.

Padmé offered a polite smile.

"Master Kenobi."

"Senator."

The door closed.

An uncomfortable silence followed.

Eventually Anakin broke it.

"I owe you an apology."

Obi-Wan looked surprised.

Anakin took a breath.

"What I said on the transport..."

He looked away.

"I shouldn't have said it."

Obi-Wan remained silent.

Anakin continued.

"I was angry."

"Yes."

"I thought Padmé was dead."

"I know."

Anakin sighed.

"I said things I didn't mean."

Obi-Wan studied him for several moments.

Then he nodded.

"I shouldn't have let our argument reach that point either."

Anakin looked up.

The older Jedi continued.

"We both failed each other."

Neither spoke for several seconds.

Then Obi-Wan stepped forward.

Anakin met him halfway.

The two men clasped forearms.

The tension that had lingered between them for months finally began to dissolve.

"I'm glad she's alive," Obi-Wan said quietly.

"So am I."

Padmé smiled from across the room.

The conversation gradually became easier after that.

They discussed recent events.

The war.

Coruscant.

Life outside the Order.

Eventually Obi-Wan's expression grew more serious.

"There is something else."

Anakin immediately recognized the tone.

"What?"

Obi-Wan folded his arms.

"Some of your recent behavior concerns me."

Anakin sighed.

"There it is."

"I'm serious."

"So am I."

Obi-Wan shook his head.

"You came dangerously close to losing yourself."

Anakin did not immediately respond.

The older Jedi continued.

"Your anger."

"Obi-Wan—"

"Listen."

Anakin fell silent.

Obi-Wan's voice softened.

"I understand grief."

"I understand fear."

"I understand attachment more than you might think."

That caught Anakin's attention.

"But if you allow those emotions to control you..."

He paused.

"They can lead you somewhere very dark."

The room became quiet.

Obi-Wan looked directly at him.

"You need to watch yourself."

Anakin nodded slowly.

"Especially now."

Obi-Wan glanced toward Padmé.

Anakin immediately understood the implication.

"Especially now that you're married."

Their purpose was simple.

Record everything.

Whenever Anakin and Palpatine met privately, their conversations were preserved.

Padmé reviewed the recordings later.

At first she listened casually.

Then more carefully.

Then with growing concern.

Certain patterns became impossible to ignore.

One recording captured Palpatine discussing the Jedi.

"The Order is increasingly rigid."

Anakin frowned.

"I wouldn't say that."

"Wouldn't you?"

Palpatine smiled.

"You have always been different from them."

Another conversation focused on Obi-Wan.

"Master Kenobi is a capable man."

"He is."

"But surely you have surpassed him by now."

Anakin laughed.

"Obi-Wan would disagree."

Palpatine leaned forward.

"Obi-Wan's opinion is not necessarily reality."

In another recording, the Chancellor's praise became even more extravagant.

"You possess extraordinary gifts, Anakin."

"I've heard that before."

"Not enough."

Palpatine's voice lowered.

"Many Jedi spend entire lifetimes without approaching your potential."

Anakin looked uncomfortable.

The Chancellor continued.

"Frankly, I suspect your abilities may eventually eclipse those of Master Windu."

Anakin shook his head.

"I don't know about that."

"And perhaps even Master Yoda."

That statement was followed by several seconds of silence.

Even listening afterward, Padmé could hear Anakin's skepticism.

Yet the pattern concerned her.

The conversations continued.

Again and again Palpatine emphasized power.

Individual greatness.

The supposed limitations of the Jedi.

The failure of others to appreciate Anakin properly.

The recordings accumulated.

Padmé listened.

Took notes.

Compared discussions.

Eventually she reached a conclusion.

Palpatine was trying to shape Anakin's thinking.

Toward what goal she did not yet know.

But she no longer believed it was innocent.

One afternoon she gathered the recordings and went directly to the Jedi Temple.

The Council chamber fell silent as she presented her evidence.

Mace Windu listened carefully.

Ki-Adi-Mundi listened.

Plo Koon listened.

Yoda listened.

Recording after recording played.

Conversation after conversation unfolded.

By the end, nobody in the room looked comfortable.

Mace folded his arms.

"Disturbing."

"Manipulative, it is," Yoda agreed.

Padmé nodded.

"I thought you should hear it yourselves."

The Council spent hours reviewing the material.

When they finished, a consensus had emerged.

Whatever Palpatine's intentions were, they deserved investigation.

Quietly.

Carefully.

Without alerting him.

Jedi observers began paying closer attention to the Chancellor.

Movements.

Meetings.

Communications.

Associates.

Nothing overt.

Nothing that would reveal their suspicions.

Simply observation.

Then, several weeks later, the Council requested a meeting.

Both Anakin and Padmé were invited.

When they arrived, the atmosphere felt unusually serious.

Anakin looked around the chamber.

"What's this about?"

Yoda glanced toward Padmé.

She nodded.

Then Yoda spoke.

"Something to show you, we have."

Several recordings were played.

Anakin listened quietly.

Conversation after conversation echoed through the chamber.

Recognition slowly spread across his face.

By the end, he understood exactly what he was hearing.

Silence followed.

Then Mace Windu spoke.

"These recordings were made in your apartment."

Anakin looked toward Padmé.

She met his gaze steadily.

"By you?"

"Yes."

Anakin nodded once.

"That makes sense."

Several Council members looked mildly surprised that he had not reacted more strongly.

Padmé noticed as well.

"You aren't angry?"

Anakin thought about it.

Then shook his head.

"Not really."

The Council listened.

Anakin folded his arms.

"Honestly, I noticed some of this myself."

That drew immediate interest.

"You did?" asked Obi-Wan.

Anakin nodded.

"Not at first."

"But after a while..."

He searched for the right words.

"It started feeling like he was trying to steer me somewhere."

Yoda leaned forward slightly.

"Against the Jedi?"

"Sometimes."

Anakin glanced toward Obi-Wan.

"He spent a lot of time talking about how powerful I supposedly am."

Obi-Wan looked amused.

"That sounds familiar."

Anakin laughed.

"Trust me, he was much more enthusiastic about it than you ever were."

A few members of the Council smiled.

Then Yoda spoke again.

"A question for you, Skywalker."

Anakin nodded.

"What?"

The old Master studied him carefully.

"Help us watch him, would you?"

The room became quiet.

Anakin considered.

"You want me to spy on the Chancellor?"

"Observe him," Mace corrected.

Anakin looked from one Council member to another.

Then toward Padmé.

She simply waited.

Finally he nodded.

"I already meet with him regularly."

"Useful, that is," Yoda said.

"I can do it."

The arrangement began shortly afterward.

Nothing outwardly changed.

Palpatine continued requesting meetings.

Anakin continued attending them.

The conversations proceeded much as before.

The difference was what happened afterward.

Following many of those meetings, Anakin visited the Jedi Temple.

Sometimes he met with Yoda.

Sometimes Obi-Wan.

Sometimes the entire Council.

He relayed details.

Conversations.

Statements.

Observations.

Anything unusual.

The reports gradually accumulated.

One evening after delivering another update, Anakin found himself walking through the Temple with Obi-Wan.

The older Jedi glanced sideways at him.

"Never thought I'd see the day."

Anakin smirked.

"The day I voluntarily report to the Council?"

"Something like that."

They walked in silence for a moment.

Then Obi-Wan added:

"You've handled this remarkably well."

Anakin looked amused.

"That's almost a compliment."

"It is a compliment."

"Careful. People might think you're becoming sentimental."

Obi-Wan rolled his eyes.

"I'll try to maintain my reputation."

Anakin laughed.

For all the uncertainty surrounding Palpatine, one thing had become increasingly clear.

The Chancellor no longer held the influence over Anakin that he once might have possessed.

Years earlier, his praise and encouragement had often fallen upon fertile ground.

Now there were other voices in Anakin's life.

Padmé.

Obi-Wan.

Yoda.

The friends and mentors who challenged him instead of simply flattering him.

And because of that, whenever Palpatine attempted to guide him toward resentment, isolation, or distrust, there was always someone else helping him recognize what was happening.

The result was not that Anakin stopped meeting the Chancellor.

The result was that he stopped listening uncritically.

And that made all the difference.
