# Part 1

The Battle of Geonosis marked the beginning of a new war.

As Republic gunships lifted away from the smoking battlefield and the wounded were gathered for transport, most attention was focused on Count Dooku's escape and the sudden creation of the Grand Army of the Republic. Few paid attention to a brief moment near one of the landing zones.

Padmé Amidala had rushed toward Anakin Skywalker after the fighting ended. The two embraced openly. Their relief at finding each other alive was impossible to miss.

Obi-Wan Kenobi noticed.

So did Yoda.

Neither commented on it at the time.

The wounded needed treatment. The dead needed honoring. The Republic was mobilizing for a galactic conflict.

But the image stayed with Obi-Wan.

Several days later, after returning to Coruscant, Obi-Wan sought out Yoda in the Temple gardens.

The old Jedi Master was seated beneath one of the towering trees, eyes closed in meditation.

"Master Yoda."

Yoda opened his eyes.

"Troubled, you are."

Obi-Wan folded his arms inside his robes.

"It's Anakin."

Yoda's expression did not change.

"Hmm."

"I know he cares for Senator Amidala. I knew that much before Geonosis. But what I saw there..."

"You fear more than friendship, do you?"

"I do."

Yoda was silent for several moments.

Finally he said, "And what would you do?"

"I would like to travel to Naboo."

Yoda tilted his head.

"To Naboo?"

"Anakin escorted Senator Amidala home after the battle. I don't think they should be left alone together."

A long pause followed.

Then Yoda nodded.

"Go, you may."

Obi-Wan bowed.

"Thank you, Master."

"Remember, Obi-Wan. Guide him, you must. Judge him too quickly, you should not."

"I understand."

Within hours Obi-Wan's transport was departing Coruscant.

By the following day he was descending through the skies of Naboo.

The planet looked peaceful.

Too peaceful.

After landing, Obi-Wan attempted to locate his former Padawan.

He activated his comlink.

"Anakin."

A moment later the familiar voice answered.

"Master? I wasn't expecting your call."

"Where are you?"

Anakin did not hesitate.

"I'm at Varykino Villa."

Obi-Wan frowned.

"Varykino?"

"Padmé's family estate. I'm helping her get settled in."

"How much longer will you be there?"

"Not long. I'll be finished soon and then I'll return to Coruscant."

Obi-Wan's frown deepened.

"Very good."

The transmission ended.

Anakin had sounded relaxed.

Far too relaxed.

Obi-Wan immediately arranged transport to Varykino.

The villa stood beside a lake, surrounded by rolling green hills and flowering gardens. It was quiet and secluded.

As Obi-Wan approached, he noticed something unusual.

There were very few attendants.

Almost no security.

And a small gathering on one of the balconies overlooking the water.

His eyes narrowed.

Then widened.

A Naboo holy man stood before Anakin Skywalker and Padmé Amidala.

R2-D2 and C-3PO stood nearby.

Anakin was dressed in formal clothing.

Padmé wore a wedding gown.

The holy man was in the middle of speaking.

"Do you, Anakin Skywalker—"

"Stop."

The single word cut across the balcony like a vibroblade.

Everyone froze.

Anakin turned.

His face immediately lost all color.

"Master..."

Padmé looked equally shocked.

Obi-Wan walked forward slowly.

The holy man glanced nervously between them.

"I... should perhaps—"

"No," Obi-Wan said. "Stay exactly where you are."

The holy man obeyed.

Anakin recovered first.

"Master, I can explain."

"Can you?"

Obi-Wan's voice was calm.

Too calm.

Anakin visibly preferred anger.

"Master—"

"You promised me."

Anakin stopped speaking.

Obi-Wan took another step forward.

"You promised me you would not make major decisions without consulting either me or the Council."

Anakin searched for words.

"This is different."

"Different?"

"Yes."

"You are standing at your wedding."

Anakin said nothing.

Padmé finally spoke.

"Obi-Wan—"

"Senator, with respect, I am speaking to my former Padawan."

The silence became unbearable.

Finally Obi-Wan asked the obvious question.

"When were you planning to tell anyone?"

Neither answered.

Obi-Wan nodded grimly.

"I see."

Anakin's jaw tightened.

"We weren't hurting anyone."

"No. You were merely concealing a marriage from the Order whose rules you swore to uphold."

Anakin crossed his arms.

"I love her."

"I don't doubt that."

The answer seemed to surprise him.

Obi-Wan sighed.

"Anakin, listen to me carefully. I cannot continue training you."

"What?"

"I cannot."

Anakin stared at him.

"You are nearly ready for knighthood."

"I know."

"You knowingly violated one of the Order's most fundamental rules."

"So that's it?" Anakin snapped. "Everything we've been through?"

"That is not what I said."

"It sounds like it."

Obi-Wan's patience finally began to crack.

"No, Anakin. What it sounds like is that you expected nobody to notice."

The younger Jedi's expression hardened.

"You don't understand."

"Then help me understand."

Anakin spread his hands.

"I finally have something that's mine."

"Padmé is not property."

"You know what I mean."

"I do."

For a moment neither spoke.

Then Obi-Wan said quietly:

"You have a choice."

Anakin waited.

"You may leave the Jedi Order peacefully."

The words struck like a blaster bolt.

Padmé's eyes widened.

Anakin looked stunned.

"You want me to quit?"

"I want you to stop pretending you can have both lives."

The lake breeze stirred their clothing.

Obi-Wan continued.

"Leave the Order. Live openly. Be with the woman you love."

Anakin blinked.

The suggestion was not what he had expected.

"But..."

"The alternative is deception."

Padmé looked at Anakin.

For the first time since Obi-Wan arrived, hope appeared on her face.

"Anakin."

He looked at her.

"Maybe he's right."

Anakin stared.

"Padmé..."

"We don't have to hide."

The words hit him harder than any criticism from Obi-Wan.

Padmé stepped closer.

"We can have a life together."

The anger slowly drained from him.

At last he nodded.

"Fine."

Obi-Wan exhaled.

"Then we're agreed."

"Not completely."

Obi-Wan looked up.

Anakin's hand rested on the hilt of his lightsaber.

"I won't surrender this."

The atmosphere immediately changed.

"Anakin," Obi-Wan warned.

"No."

"You know the regulations."

"I don't care."

"If you leave the Order, your lightsaber remains Jedi property."

"It's mine."

"It is not."

"It is."

Padmé looked horrified.

"Anakin—"

"No."

Obi-Wan folded his arms.

"You intend to keep it?"

"Yes."

"Then hand it over."

"No."

The two men stared at one another.

Finally Obi-Wan spoke.

"I am not leaving without it."

Anakin's eyes narrowed.

"Then if you want it..."

His blue lightsaber snapped to life.

"...you'll have to fight for it."

Padmé covered her face.

"Oh no."

Obi-Wan closed his eyes briefly.

"Of course."

His own blade ignited.

The duel began.

The balcony exploded into motion.

Anakin attacked first.

Obi-Wan expected nothing less.

Blue and blue crashed together in a shower of sparks.

The Naboo holy man fled for cover almost immediately.

C-3PO loudly declared that this was not a standard wedding procedure.

Neither combatant listened.

They moved from the balcony into the gardens below.

Anakin fought with all the aggression and power for which he was already becoming famous.

Obi-Wan answered with patience and precision.

Minutes passed.

Neither gained a decisive advantage.

The duel carried them across pathways, fountains, and open lawns.

At one point a statue lost its head.

At another, an entire flowerbed was accidentally destroyed.

Eventually both men found themselves standing several meters apart near the lakeshore.

Breathing hard.

Lightsabers still raised.

Neither willing to continue.

Obi-Wan looked at his former student.

Anakin looked back.

Finally Obi-Wan deactivated his blade.

The humming ceased.

"Enough."

Anakin hesitated.

Then he deactivated his own.

The silence returned.

"You've made your point," Obi-Wan said.

"And you've made yours."

They stood there for several moments.

At last Obi-Wan nodded.

"You may keep the lightsaber."

Anakin visibly relaxed.

"But," Obi-Wan continued, "I will inform the Council."

Anakin grimaced.

"I figured."

"I will tell them everything."

"Fine."

"The relationship. The marriage. All of it."

Anakin looked toward Padmé.

Then back to Obi-Wan.

"Fine."

The tension finally began to dissipate.

Obi-Wan extended his hand.

After a moment, Anakin shook it.

No further words were needed.

A few hours later Obi-Wan departed Varykino and boarded a transport back to Coruscant.

He spent most of the journey staring out the viewport, wondering how exactly he was going to explain this to the Jedi Council.

Back at the villa, silence lingered after his departure.

Padmé stood beside Anakin on the balcony.

"So..."

"So," Anakin agreed.

They looked at the holy man.

The holy man looked back.

His expression suggested he had experienced the single most confusing day of his professional life.

After a long pause he cleared his throat.

"Shall... shall we continue?"

Anakin and Padmé exchanged glances.

Then both laughed.

"Yes," Padmé said.

"Please continue," Anakin added.

The bewildered holy man nodded.

R2-D2 rolled back into position.

C-3PO reluctantly resumed his place as witness.

As the sun began to set over the lake, the ceremony restarted from the beginning.

This time nobody interrupted it.

And so, with only a confused holy man, one astromech droid, and one protocol droid in attendance, Anakin Skywalker and Padmé Amidala were finally married on the balcony of Varykino Villa.

# Part 2

The report to the Jedi Council took nearly three hours.

Every member of the Council listened in silence as Obi-Wan Kenobi described exactly what had happened on Naboo.

He spoke of the relationship between Anakin Skywalker and Senator Padmé Amidala.

He described arriving at Varykino Villa.

He explained finding them in the middle of a wedding ceremony.

He recounted the confrontation, Anakin's agreement to leave the Jedi Order, and the duel that followed.

Finally, he described Anakin's refusal to surrender his lightsaber.

When he finished, the Council chamber remained quiet.

Mace Windu was the first to speak.

"He challenged you over the weapon?"

"He did," Obi-Wan answered.

"And you permitted him to keep it."

"I did."

Mace folded his hands.

"A questionable decision."

"Perhaps."

Yoda tapped his cane against the floor.

"Yet fight to the death over a lightsaber, we should not."

Ki-Adi-Mundi nodded.

"The young man has already left the Order."

"Formerly one of us, he is," Yoda agreed. "No longer one of us, he is not."

The discussion continued for some time.

Several Masters expressed concern.

Others pointed out that Anakin was no longer a Jedi and therefore outside the Order's authority except in matters directly affecting Republic security.

Eventually a consensus emerged.

Mace Windu summarized it.

"We will not pursue him over the lightsaber."

Obi-Wan looked up.

The Council chamber remained solemn.

Mace continued.

"We will observe him."

"Monitor him," added Plo Koon.

"If he becomes a threat," said Saesee Tiin, "we will reevaluate."

"But if he does not?" Obi-Wan asked.

Yoda answered.

"Then live his life, he may."

The decision was entered into the records.

Anakin Skywalker would not be hunted.

The Jedi Order would keep its distance.

For the moment, the matter was considered closed.

Unfortunately, Obi-Wan did not feel any sense of closure.

Over the following weeks he threw himself into military planning and Jedi duties.

Yet whenever he found himself alone, his thoughts drifted back to Anakin.

His Padawan was gone.

Not dead.

Not lost.

Gone.

One evening Yoda found him standing alone on a Temple balcony overlooking Coruscant's endless cityscape.

Without turning, Obi-Wan said quietly,

"I failed him."

Yoda approached slowly.

"No."

Obi-Wan sighed.

"I was responsible for him."

"Responsible for guidance, yes."

"I should have seen it sooner."

"Hmm."

"I should have done something."

"You did."

Obi-Wan finally turned.

Yoda's expression was calm.

"The choice was Anakin's."

"I trained him."

"And choose for him, could you not."

Obi-Wan looked away.

For several moments neither spoke.

Then Yoda rested a hand on his cane.

"When Dooku left the Order, responsible I felt."

Obi-Wan blinked.

Yoda rarely spoke of his former apprentice.

"Many years, I questioned myself."

The old Master looked toward the city lights.

"Wondered what I could have done differently."

"And?"

Yoda smiled faintly.

"His choices were his own."

The words hung in the air.

"Anakin's choices were his own as well."

Obi-Wan remained silent.

Eventually Yoda continued.

"A good teacher, you were."

"He left."

"Yes."

"That doesn't feel like success."

Yoda chuckled softly.

"Success and failure, often less simple than we wish."

The old Master began walking away.

Then he paused.

"A new Padawan, perhaps soon."

Obi-Wan looked surprised.

Yoda's eyes twinkled.

"Much experience, you now have."

For the first time in weeks, Obi-Wan managed a faint smile.

"Thank you, Master."

Meanwhile, life at Varykino Villa settled into a surprisingly peaceful routine.

Padmé and Anakin spent most of their time recovering from Geonosis.

Padmé's injuries healed slowly.

The deep lashes across her back, inflicted by the nexu in the execution arena, required regular treatment.

Some days movement remained painful.

Anakin faced his own challenges.

His right hand was gone.

In its place was a prosthetic limb.

He spent hours learning how to control it naturally.

One afternoon, while they sat together overlooking the lake, Padmé held out her hand.

"Anakin."

"Yes?"

"Give me your lightsaber."

Anakin blinked.

"My what?"

"Your lightsaber."

He stared at her.

"You can't be serious."

"I'm completely serious."

His eyes widened.

"You want to take my lightsaber?"

"Temporarily."

Anakin nearly laughed.

"Padmé, I literally dueled Obi-Wan over this."

"I know."

"And now you're doing the same thing."

"I'm not."

"It sounds very similar."

Padmé folded her arms.

"Anakin."

"No."

"Anakin."

"No."

"Anakin."

He groaned.

She smiled slightly.

"You challenged your former master to a duel at our wedding."

"He started it."

"He absolutely did not."

"He kind of did."

She raised an eyebrow.

He immediately looked away.

Padmé continued.

"You're emotional."

"I'm always emotional."

"Exactly."

Anakin opened his mouth.

Then closed it again.

Padmé extended her hand.

"I'll give it back."

"When?"

"When you're ready."

"I am ready."

"No."

"Yes."

"No."

Anakin stared at her.

Then sighed dramatically.

"You sound like Obi-Wan."

"Thank you."

"That wasn't a compliment."

She smiled.

Eventually he unclipped the lightsaber and placed it in her hand.

"There."

"Thank you."

"I still think this is ridiculous."

"I know."

Despite his complaints, he let her keep it.

Mostly because she had promised it was temporary.

And because she was Padmé.

Several weeks later Padmé raised another subject.

They were sitting together in one of the villa's libraries.

"I'll be returning to the Senate."

Anakin looked up.

"So soon?"

"My injuries are healing."

"You should rest longer."

"I've been resting for weeks."

"You got attacked by an assassin."

"Several assassins."

"Exactly."

Padmé smiled.

"The people of Naboo still need representation."

Anakin leaned back.

"I suppose."

She studied him carefully.

"The investigation is finished."

Anakin nodded.

"Jango Fett is dead."

"Yes."

"Zam Wesell is dead."

"Yes."

"The people responsible have been uncovered."

"For now."

Padmé set aside her datapad.

"I'll be safe."

Anakin looked unconvinced.

She continued.

"If you want to stay with me on Coruscant, you could join my staff."

He stared.

"Your what?"

"My personal staff."

Anakin looked horrified.

"You want me to do politics?"

"I want you to learn politics."

"That sounds even worse."

Padmé laughed.

"It isn't."

"It definitely is."

She leaned forward.

"You spend a lot of time talking about how governments should solve problems."

"So?"

"So perhaps you should learn how governments actually work."

Anakin opened his mouth.

Then paused.

The idea had some logic.

Unfortunately it still sounded incredibly boring.

"I'll think about it."

Padmé smiled knowingly.

"What if it means we spend more time together?"

Anakin immediately reconsidered.

"...tell me more."

That was how the lessons began.

At first they went terribly.

Padmé would explain legislation.

Anakin would zone out.

Padmé would discuss committee procedures.

Anakin would stare out the window.

Padmé would describe budget negotiations.

Anakin would nearly fall asleep.

One afternoon she finally stopped in the middle of a lesson.

"Anakin."

"Hm?"

"You're not listening."

"I am."

"You absolutely are not."

Anakin smiled innocently.

Padmé sighed.

Then she tried something different.

Padmé occasionally told stories to her young nieces Ryoo and Pooja during family gatherings using a particularly gentle voice.

Warm.

Sweet.

Calm.

Without thinking much of it, she began explaining a Senate procedure using that same voice.

To her surprise, Anakin immediately looked up.

And listened.

The entire time.

Padmé noticed.

Interesting.

The next day she tried it again.

The same result.

Anakin paid attention to every word.

The day after that she tested it once more.

Perfect concentration.

Eventually she confronted him.

"Anakin."

"What?"

"Why do you only pay attention when I use that voice?"

He looked confused.

"What voice?"

"That voice."

"I don't know what you're talking about."

Padmé gave him a look.

Anakin immediately looked guilty.

"I just like hearing you talk."

"Uh-huh."

"That's all."

Padmé smiled.

From that point forward, more and more lessons were delivered in that soothing voice.

And to her amusement, Anakin absorbed nearly everything.

Months passed.

The results surprised even Padmé.

Anakin learned rapidly.

He studied Republic law.

Senate procedure.

Diplomatic negotiations.

Constitutional limitations.

The history of democratic institutions.

The dangers of concentrating power in too few hands.

The value of checks and balances.

The importance of accountability.

They argued often.

Sometimes for hours.

But gradually Anakin's views began to change.

He became less eager to solve every problem through force.

Less dismissive of procedure.

More patient.

More thoughtful.

He still possessed strong opinions.

But he increasingly questioned his own impulses before acting on them.

One evening Padmé finished reviewing a policy briefing with him and closed the datapad.

"Congratulations."

Anakin looked up.

"For what?"

"You're ready."

"Ready for what?"

"To join my staff."

He blinked.

"Really?"

"Really."

Anakin looked unexpectedly pleased.

Padmé smiled.

"You've worked hard."

"So have you."

"I know."

A few weeks later they stood together at the spaceport.

Their belongings had already been loaded.

The transport to Coruscant was preparing for departure.

The time had come to leave Naboo.

Padmé reached into a case she had carried personally.

Anakin immediately recognized its contents.

"My lightsaber."

She nodded.

Then handed it to him.

For a moment he simply stared at it.

Months earlier he had come to blows with Obi-Wan over the weapon.

Now it felt different.

Calmer.

Less important.

He clipped it back onto his belt.

Padmé smiled.

"I think you're ready to carry it again."

Anakin looked down at the hilt.

Then back at her.

"You trust me?"

"I do."

He nodded.

"Thank you."

Together they boarded the transport.

As Naboo's green fields disappeared beneath the clouds, Padmé Amidala returned to the Republic Senate.

And beside her, no longer a Jedi but no longer directionless either, Anakin Skywalker began the next chapter of his life as a member of her personal staff.

# Part 3

Life in the Senate proved far different from life in the Jedi Order.

For the first several months after returning to Coruscant, Padmé kept Anakin on a very short leash politically.

Every time he tried to involve himself in a discussion, she would stop him.

"Observe."

Every time he tried to offer a solution to a dispute, she would shake her head.

"Observe."

Every time he tried to jump directly into negotiations, committee meetings, or legislative debates, she repeated the same instruction.

"Observe."

One afternoon, after sitting through six hours of committee hearings, Anakin finally groaned.

"I think I've observed enough."

Padmé didn't even look up from her datapad.

"No."

"I know how it works."

"No."

"I really do."

"No."

Anakin slumped into his chair.

"Politics is mostly people talking."

Padmé finally looked up.

"That's because you've only been watching for three months."

"Three months is a long time."

"Not in politics."

Anakin sighed.

"At least battles are straightforward."

Padmé smiled.

"Nothing is straightforward."

Eventually she began allowing him limited participation.

At first he simply drafted summaries.

Then policy recommendations.

Then briefing documents.

Later he sat in on negotiations.

After that he was allowed to speak during some meetings.

To Padmé's surprise, he turned out to be very good at understanding people's motivations.

Years of Jedi training had made him unusually perceptive.

His tendency toward impulsiveness remained, but he had become noticeably more disciplined than before.

Gradually, members of Padmé's staff stopped seeing him as merely the senator's husband.

He became a respected adviser in his own right.

It was during this period that Chancellor Palpatine began taking a renewed interest in him.

One afternoon a message arrived requesting Anakin's presence at the Chancellor's office.

The meeting appeared innocent enough.

Palpatine greeted him warmly.

"Anakin. It's good to see you."

"You as well, Chancellor."

They sat across from one another.

For a while they discussed the war.

Then Palpatine's tone changed.

"Tell me," he said casually, "do you feel bitter toward Obi-Wan Kenobi?"

Anakin blinked.

The question caught him off guard.

"Bitter?"

"Or the Jedi Order in general."

Anakin considered.

Finally he shrugged.

"Somewhat."

Palpatine nodded sympathetically.

"A natural reaction."

"I don't hate them."

"I didn't ask if you hated them."

Anakin remained silent.

Palpatine leaned back.

"Do you feel they failed you?"

The question lingered.

After several moments Anakin answered honestly.

"To a degree."

Palpatine nodded again.

"I suspected as much."

Anakin said nothing.

The Chancellor's expression became thoughtful.

"You were extraordinary."

Anakin looked uncomfortable.

"I was a Padawan."

"You were much more than that."

Palpatine folded his hands.

"I believed you were on track to become the most powerful Jedi of your generation."

Anakin looked away.

"And yet they forced you to leave over a marriage."

The room fell silent.

Anakin offered no response.

Eventually the conversation moved elsewhere.

But the exchange remained in his thoughts.

That evening, back at the apartment he shared with Padmé, he told her everything.

Padmé listened carefully.

When he finished, she sat quietly for several seconds.

Then she asked,

"Did he criticize Obi-Wan specifically?"

"Not directly."

"The Council?"

"Not directly."

"But he guided the conversation there."

Anakin frowned.

"I suppose."

Padmé looked troubled.

"For some reason, Chancellor Palpatine seems interested in encouraging your resentment."

Anakin shook his head.

"I think you're overthinking it."

"Maybe."

But she did not sound convinced.

Several weeks later another invitation arrived.

The Chancellor wished to see Anakin again.

This time Padmé was uneasy.

Very uneasy.

After considerable thought, she made a decision.

Without informing Anakin, she discreetly followed him.

When he entered Palpatine's office, she positioned herself outside where she could hear the conversation through the partially opened doorway.

Inside, Palpatine welcomed Anakin warmly.

They exchanged pleasantries.

Then the Chancellor steered the discussion in a familiar direction.

"Tell me, Anakin."

"Yes?"

"Do you believe the Jedi are weak?"

Padmé immediately stiffened.

Inside the office, Anakin hesitated.

"No."

Palpatine smiled.

"But?"

"They have weaknesses."

"Everyone does."

Anakin nodded.

Palpatine continued.

"Do you believe you are stronger than they are?"

Anakin thought carefully.

"Some of them."

"Some?"

"Most Jedi Knights."

"And the Masters?"

Anakin leaned back.

"Not yet."

The Chancellor appeared interested.

"Not yet?"

"No."

"What about Yoda?"

Anakin laughed.

"No."

"Not yet?"

Anakin's smile widened.

"Definitely not yet."

Palpatine nodded.

"But one day?"

Anakin's expression became serious.

"One day I want to surpass all of them."

The Chancellor's eyes narrowed slightly.

"Even Master Yoda?"

"Even Yoda."

For a moment silence filled the office.

Then Palpatine smiled.

"Good."

Outside the room, Padmé felt a chill.

Inside, Palpatine continued.

"I believe you can."

Anakin looked surprised.

"I do."

The Chancellor's voice carried a note of genuine enthusiasm.

"You possess tremendous potential."

Anakin remained silent.

"Continue training."

Palpatine leaned forward.

"Continue growing stronger."

His smile widened.

"And one day perhaps you will achieve that goal."

The conversation eventually moved on.

But Padmé had heard enough.

The moment the meeting ended, she left before Anakin could see her.

Her destination was immediate and unquestionable.

The Jedi Temple.

She arrived visibly shaken.

Temple personnel quickly arranged an audience with Yoda.

The Grand Master met her in a private chamber.

Concern immediately appeared on his face.

"Troubled, Senator Amidala is."

Padmé sat down.

"Master Yoda, I need to tell you something."

Yoda listened carefully as she described the first conversation Anakin had reported to her.

Then she described the second conversation in full.

Every question.

Every answer.

Every remark from the Chancellor.

When she finished, Yoda sat quietly.

The old Master's eyes were closed.

His expression had become grave.

Finally he opened them.

"Disturbing, this is."

Padmé nodded.

"You hear it too?"

"Hmm."

Yoda tapped his cane lightly against the floor.

"Against the Jedi, Chancellor Palpatine pushes."

"That's exactly what I thought."

"Questioning Anakin's trust in us."

Padmé folded her hands.

"But why?"

Yoda was silent.

After several moments he answered.

"Know, I do not."

The answer did not reassure her.

By the following day the Jedi Council had convened.

Padmé's testimony was presented in full.

The Council listened carefully.

When the report ended, Mace Windu spoke first.

"The pattern is obvious."

Ki-Adi-Mundi nodded.

"Repeated attempts to encourage dissatisfaction with the Order."

"Particularly concerning given Skywalker's history," added Plo Koon.

Several Masters agreed.

The discussion lasted for hours.

Not because they disagreed.

Because they were trying to understand why the Supreme Chancellor of the Republic would behave this way.

Eventually a consensus emerged.

Mace Windu summarized the Council's conclusion.

"Palpatine should be monitored."

Several heads nodded.

Yoda remained thoughtful.

"Carefully, we must proceed."

"Agreed," Mace said.

"He remains Chancellor."

"Proof of wrongdoing, we do not have."

"Not yet."

The meeting concluded shortly thereafter.

For the first time in many years, however, the Jedi Council found itself quietly watching not a Separatist leader, criminal syndicate, or suspected Sith agent.

Their attention had shifted toward the Supreme Chancellor himself.

And none of them were entirely comfortable with what that might mean.

The Jedi expected surrender.

Then Palpatine emerged from a side door in the central command chamber.

Mace stepped forward.

"It is over."

Palpatine stood beside the central command console.

For a moment, he looked almost tired.

Then he smiled.

"No."

The air changed.

Palpatine raised both hands.

Force lightning exploded outward.

Blue-white energy struck the gathered Jedi.

Several Masters were thrown backward. Others raised their lightsabers in time. The chamber filled with smoke, sparks, and the violent crackle of lightning.

Palpatine ignited a red lightsaber.

The Jedi froze.

Obi-Wan stared at him.

"You're a Sith."

Palpatine's smile widened.

"At last."

The truth spread through the chamber.

Palpatine was Darth Sidious.

The man who had served as Chancellor.

The man who had joined Dooku.

The man who had manipulated the clone army.

The man who had triggered Order 66.

The man who had threatened the Republic with a hidden superweapon.

He had been behind it all.

The duel began with chaos.

Sidious moved faster than anyone expected. His red blade flashed through the chamber. Force lightning struck in sudden bursts. He fought with a violence and speed that overwhelmed the first Jedi who approached him.

Kit Fisto engaged him near the command platform.

The exchange lasted only seconds.

Sidious struck once.

Kit blocked.

Sidious struck again.

Kit fell.

The Jedi Master did not rise.

Obi-Wan moved forward with several others.

Sidious unleashed lightning again.

Obi-Wan raised his lightsaber and redirected the blast.

Once.

Twice.

A third time.

The strain nearly drove him to his knees.

"Fall back!" Mace ordered.

Obi-Wan retreated, breathing hard.

Sidious turned toward the remaining Jedi.

Then he raised one hand.

The Force itself bent around the chamber.

A shimmering barrier erupted between Mace and the others.

Mace stood on one side.

Sidious stood with him.

The rest of the Jedi were cut off.

"Mace!" Obi-Wan shouted.

The barrier held.

Mace ignited his purple blade.

Sidious smiled.

"At last, Master Windu."

The two attacked.

Their duel became a storm of red and purple light.

Sidious struck with fury.

Mace met every blow.

Sidious pushed him across the chamber.

Mace drove him back.

The Sith Lord's power was immense, but Vaapad turned that darkness against him. Every burst of aggression Sidious unleashed became something Mace could redirect and use.

The duel grew more violent.

Sidious threw objects through the air.

Mace cut them apart.

Sidious unleashed lightning.

Mace deflected it.

Sidious attacked with speed and rage.

Mace absorbed the assault and returned it.

At last, Mace broke Sidious' guard.

The red lightsaber flew from Palpatine's hand.

Sidious stumbled backward.

For the first time, fear appeared in his eyes.

"Wait," Sidious said.

Mace raised his blade.

"You concealed your identity for decades."

Sidious backed toward the command platform.

"You do not understand what is at stake."

"I understand enough."

Mace thought of the clone army.

Order 66.

The dead Jedi.

The war.

The deception.

Sidious was too dangerous to leave alive.

Mace struck once.

Cleanly.

Darth Sidious died.

The Force barrier vanished.

The remaining Jedi entered the chamber.

For a long moment, nobody spoke.

Then Obi-Wan looked toward Kit Fisto's body.

The victory did not feel joyful.

It felt necessary.

Outside the headquarters, news spread quickly.

Count Dooku was dead.

General Grievous was dead.

Palpatine was dead.

Serenno had fallen.

The remaining pro-Dooku commanders lost whatever will remained to fight.

Some surrendered immediately.

Others attempted to flee.

Most were captured.

Within days, the pro-Dooku Confederacy formally surrendered.

The Clone Wars ended.

Chancellor Bail Organa honored the promise he had made to the anti-Dooku Confederacy.

The Separatist worlds would remain independent.

They would not be forced back into the Republic.

Treaties were signed between Coruscant and Raxus Secundus.

Borders were recognized.

Trade agreements were negotiated.

Demilitarized zones were established.

For the first time in years, Republic and Separatist representatives met without armies waiting outside the door.

The cloning facilities on Kamino were shut down.

No more clone batches were commissioned.

No more children were designed for war.

The surviving clone troopers received formal legal recognition.

The Republic declared that every clone was a person.

Every clone was a citizen.

Every clone had rights.

Some remained in military service.

Others retired.

Some settled on worlds they had helped defend.

Others formed communities with their brothers.

For many, freedom was unfamiliar.

But it was theirs.

The battle droid foundries on Geonosis and other worlds were drastically reduced.

The galaxy no longer needed endless armies of war machines.

Many factories transitioned to civilian production.

Construction droids.

Agricultural droids.

Medical droids.

Transport droids.

The machinery of war slowly became machinery for rebuilding.

Coruscant repaired its damaged districts.

Serenno rebuilt its government.

Raxus Secundus became the capital of an independent Confederacy at peace with the Republic.

On Naboo, people held memorials for the dead.

On Kamino, the cloning vats went silent.

Inside the Jedi Temple, the surviving Masters mourned those lost during Order 66 and the long war that followed.

Yoda returned from Serenno exhausted but alive.

He spent many days in quiet meditation.

Mace Windu carried the memory of Sidious with him for the rest of his life.

Obi-Wan remembered both Dooku and Kit Fisto.

And far from the battlefields, Anakin Skywalker and Padmé Amidala watched the news from their restored apartment on Coruscant.

The war was over.

The galaxy had survived.

And after years of fear, death, betrayal, and destruction, peace had finally begun.
