# Part 1

The battle for Naboo ended in triumph, but victory carried a terrible cost.

Deep within the Theed power generator complex, Jedi Master Qui-Gon Jinn fell beneath the blade of the Sith warrior Darth Maul. His final moments were spent looking into the eyes of his apprentice.

"Promise me..." Qui-Gon's voice was weak, each breath harder than the last. "Train the boy."

Obi-Wan Kenobi knelt beside him, grief threatening to overwhelm him.

"I will, Master."

Qui-Gon's expression softened.

"He is the Chosen One... Train him..."

His eyes closed forever.

Not long afterward, Darth Maul himself fell, cut in half by Obi-Wan's lightsaber and cast into the endless depths below.

The invasion ended. The Trade Federation's droid army shut down when young Anakin Skywalker, a 9 year old former slave from Tatooine, destroyed the droid control ship during a desperate and largely accidental attack in his starfighter. Naboo was liberated, and celebrations spread across the world.

Yet one question remained unanswered.

What would become of Anakin?

In the days following the victory, the Jedi Council assembled on Coruscant.

The atmosphere inside the Council Chamber was solemn.

Mace Windu regarded Obi-Wan steadily.

"You have fulfilled your trials, Obi-Wan Kenobi."

Yoda nodded.

"Knighted, you shall be."

Obi-Wan bowed respectfully.

"I am honored."

His expression immediately became more serious.

"But there is another matter."

The room grew quiet.

"My master made me promise that I would train Anakin Skywalker."

Mace folded his hands.

"The Council has already reached its decision."

"The boy is too old," another Master said.

"He has begun his training in fear."

"We cannot permit it."

Obi-Wan remained composed.

"With respect, Masters, Qui-Gon believed otherwise."

"Qui-Gon often disagreed with this Council," Mace replied evenly.

"He was one of the greatest Jedi who ever lived," Obi-Wan answered. "He saw something in Anakin that cannot simply be ignored."

Yoda's ears lowered slightly.

"Believe in Qui-Gon, we do. But agree with him, we do not."

"I gave him my word."

"The answer remains no."

Obi-Wan looked from face to face.

"If the Council will not assign him to another Master, then I will train him myself."

Silence filled the chamber.

Mace's expression hardened.

"You will not."

"I have already promised."

"The Council forbids it."

Obi-Wan's voice became firmer.

"Then I will honor my promise without the Council's permission."

Several Masters exchanged concerned glances.

Mace spoke carefully.

"You are a Jedi Knight now."

"I am."

"Then you understand what defiance of the Council means."

Obi-Wan did not look away.

"I understand."

Yoda sighed deeply.

"Do this... you must not."

"I cannot abandon my master's final wish."

The meeting ended without agreement.

Over the following weeks, Obi-Wan continued making appeals.

He met with individual Council members.

He requested additional hearings.

He argued that Anakin had nowhere to go and deserved guidance.

Every request was denied.

The Council, increasingly concerned that Obi-Wan intended to ignore its ruling, quietly instructed other Jedi to observe him. They did not imprison him or strip him of his rank, but they ensured that Anakin would never be left alone with him long enough for any instruction to begin.

Obi-Wan quickly realized what was happening.

One afternoon, as he left another unsuccessful meeting, he found Master Plo Koon waiting outside.

Plo Koon spoke gently.

"They fear your determination."

"I made a promise."

"I know."

"They're watching me."

Plo Koon did not deny it.

"They hope that, in time, you will accept the Council's judgment."

Obi-Wan looked toward the distant skylanes outside the Temple.

"I don't know how to break a promise to my master."

"You may discover that there are promises which cannot be kept."

Weeks turned into months.

Every avenue closed.

Every request failed.

Eventually, with visible reluctance, Obi-Wan stopped trying.

Not because he had ceased believing in Qui-Gon's wish, but because every attempt only tightened the Council's resolve.

He could not train Anakin.

With the matter settled, attention returned to Naboo.

The celebrations honoring the liberation of the planet continued for days.

Anakin found himself standing outside one of the palace balconies overlooking Theed.

The cheering crowds below still remembered him as the boy who had destroyed the droid control ship.

Yet he felt strangely alone.

Padmé Amidala, only 14 years old despite carrying the responsibilities of a queen, stepped onto the balcony.

She smiled warmly.

"There you are."

Anakin looked up.

"Hi."

"You've been avoiding the celebrations."

"They're kind of loud."

Padmé laughed softly.

"They are."

They stood together in silence for a while.

Finally Anakin spoke.

"I guess... I should go back to Tatooine."

Padmé looked at him.

"You want to?"

"My mom's still there."

He stared toward the horizon.

"She's still a slave."

"I don't want her to be alone."

Later that day, Obi-Wan met privately with Anakin.

The young Jedi Knight knelt so they were eye level.

"You've been thinking about returning."

Anakin nodded.

"My mother's there."

"I understand."

"I don't want her to think I forgot her."

Obi-Wan was quiet before answering.

"If you return to Tatooine now, you'll be returning to the life you escaped."

"My mom's there."

"I know."

"I can help her."

"You are still a child."

Anakin looked away.

"I don't have anywhere else."

Obi-Wan rested a hand gently on his shoulder.

"Listen to me carefully."

Anakin met his eyes.

"I cannot tell you what the future holds."

"But I do know this."

"If you return to Tatooine now, your opportunities will become very small."

"Naboo has seen what you did."

"The people here know your courage."

"Your future will be better if you don't return."

Anakin frowned.

"But what about Mom?"

Obi-Wan's voice softened.

"I wish I had an answer for that."

"So do I."

The conversation stayed with Anakin for days.

Eventually Padmé invited him into one of the palace gardens.

She had changed from her ceremonial robes into much simpler clothing.

Today she looked much closer to the 14 year old girl she actually was.

She sat beside him on a stone bench.

"I've been thinking."

"About what?"

"You."

Anakin smiled awkwardly.

"I'm not very interesting."

"I disagree."

She looked toward the palace.

"The people of Naboo think you're a hero."

"I just got lucky."

"You still saved us."

She turned back toward him.

"You don't have to leave."

Anakin blinked.

"I don't?"

"You can stay here."

"Really?"

"This world owes you more than a thank you."

He looked surprised.

"I could actually live here?"

"You'd be welcome."

For a brief time, Padmé considered bringing Anakin directly into the royal palace.

She imagined arranging tutors, giving him a room, making sure he was cared for.

But each time she reviewed her daily schedule, reality became unavoidable.

Meetings.

Ceremonies.

Diplomatic negotiations.

Travel.

State business.

She was queen, and her duties often occupied nearly every hour of every day.

One evening she sat with Captain Panaka.

"I've been trying to find the best place for Anakin."

Panaka listened.

"You were thinking about keeping him here."

"I was."

"And now?"

Padmé sighed.

"He deserves someone who can actually be there."

"You don't think you can?"

"I know I can't."

She looked disappointed in herself.

"I'd barely see him."

"He needs a family."

Not servants.

Not guards.

Not a palace.

A family.

The answer came almost immediately afterward.

The Naberrie household.

Padmé's parents lived in Theed.

Her father, Ruwee Naberrie, was a respected man known for his calm wisdom.

Her mother, Jobal Naberrie, possessed a warmth that made visitors feel welcome almost instantly.

Padmé's older sister, Sola, was 18 years old and had always been deeply protective of those around her.

The house was close enough that Padmé could visit whenever royal duties allowed.

She arrived one evening and explained everything.

"I wanted to ask something."

Jobal smiled.

"Anything."

"It's about Anakin."

Ruwee listened quietly.

"He needs somewhere to live."

Padmé hesitated.

"I thought about bringing him into the palace."

Jobal immediately understood.

"But you wouldn't really be raising him."

Padmé nodded.

"I wouldn't."

She looked at both of them.

"I was wondering..."

"...would you consider taking him in?"

Neither parent answered immediately.

Instead they looked at one another.

Finally Ruwee smiled.

"I think we'd like to meet him first."

The following afternoon, Anakin nervously stood outside the Naberrie home.

Padmé opened the door.

"Come in."

The atmosphere inside was completely unlike the palace.

It felt lived in.

Comfortable.

Jobal greeted him first.

"You must be Anakin."

"Yes, ma'am."

"You don't have to call me ma'am."

She smiled.

"I'm Jobal."

Ruwee extended a hand.

"I'm Ruwee."

Anakin shook it.

"It's nice to meet you."

Sola appeared from another room.

"So this is the famous pilot."

Anakin blushed.

"I guess."

"You really blew up that huge ship?"

"I didn't exactly mean to."

She laughed.

"I like him already."

Dinner stretched late into the evening.

Nobody questioned him like an official.

Nobody treated him like a hero.

Instead they simply talked.

About podracing.

About Tatooine.

About Naboo.

About food.

About machines.

By the time the meal ended, Anakin had smiled more than he had in weeks.

As he prepared to leave, Jobal stopped him.

"Would you like to come back tomorrow?"

"Really?"

"Of course."

He looked at Padmé.

She simply smiled.

Several days later, Ruwee and Jobal reached their decision.

Anakin sat nervously in their sitting room.

Ruwee spoke first.

"If you're willing..."

Jobal continued.

"...we would like you to stay."

Anakin stared.

"You mean..."

"As part of our family."

His voice became very small.

"For how long?"

"As long as you need."

Tears appeared in Anakin's eyes before he realized they were coming.

"I've never..."

He couldn't finish.

Jobal walked over and embraced him.

"You don't have to earn a family."

"You simply have one."

Not long afterward, the adoption was completed through Naboo's legal system.

From that day onward, Ruwee and Jobal treated Anakin exactly as they treated their own children.

He was never made to feel like a guest.

Never made to feel like an obligation.

Never reminded that he had once belonged somewhere else.

He belonged here now.

Sola naturally slipped into the role of an older sister.

She teased him.

Scolded him when he tracked mud through the house.

Helped him understand Naboo customs.

Encouraged him whenever he doubted himself.

One afternoon she caught him taking apart a broken household appliance.

"What are you doing?"

"Fixing it."

"It wasn't broken."

"It is now."

She stared at the pile of parts.

"You took it apart."

"So I can put it back together."

"Can you?"

"...Probably."

She burst into laughter.

Over the following years, Anakin became another child of the Naberrie household.

He attended school in Theed.

He made friends.

He learned Naboo history, literature, mathematics, and engineering.

Teachers quickly discovered that he possessed an extraordinary instinct for machines.

Broken speeders somehow found their way into his hands.

Damaged household devices rarely stayed damaged for long.

Neighbors occasionally knocked on the Naberrie door.

"Is Anakin home?"

"What broke this time?" Sola would ask.

"Our irrigation pump."

"He'll take a look."

Life slowly settled into an ordinary rhythm.

Anakin still missed his mother every day.

Some evenings he would sit quietly outside, looking toward the stars.

Jobal often joined him.

"You miss her."

"I do."

"You can talk about her."

He smiled sadly.

"She was always kind."

"I wish you could meet her."

"I wish I could too."

She squeezed his shoulder.

"You'll always be able to tell us about her."

As the years passed, Naboo became home.

The streets of Theed.

The lakes.

The forests.

The quiet evenings with the Naberries around the dinner table.

The celebrations in the city squares.

The familiar neighbors.

The ordinary routines.

The former slave boy from Tatooine gradually grew into a normal young citizen of Naboo, surrounded not by the discipline of the Jedi Order, but by the steady love of a family that had chosen him as their own.

# Part 2

The years that followed were peaceful.

For Anakin Skywalker, life settled into a comfortable rhythm within the Naberrie household.

He grew taller with each passing year. His interests remained much the same. He spent countless hours repairing machines, studying engines, and finding excuses to visit landing fields around Theed.

Ruwee often joked that Anakin could identify a starship by sound alone.

"I'm serious," Ruwee told Jobal one evening.

"He can."

"No one can do that."

"I'm standing right here," Anakin said.

Jobal folded her arms.

"Can you identify the ship outside?"

Anakin listened for a few seconds.

"Nubian 327 transport."

Ruwee grinned.

"Told you."

Several moments later, the transport emerged above the nearby rooftops.

Jobal sighed.

"I don't know why I keep testing him."

Life changed again when Anakin reached 13 years old.

Padmé Amidala's service as Queen of Naboo came to an end after completing her second term.

The evening before her departure for Coruscant, the Naberrie family gathered for dinner.

Padmé looked thoughtful throughout the meal.

"You seem nervous," Sola observed.

Padmé smiled slightly.

"I am."

"You're becoming a senator."

"I know."

"Most people would be excited."

Padmé looked at her family.

"I've spent most of my life serving Naboo from here."

She glanced toward the window.

"Coruscant is very far away."

Anakin leaned forward.

"You'll do great."

Padmé laughed.

"Thank you."

"I mean it."

"You've handled everything else."

Padmé smiled warmly.

"I'll miss all of you."

The next morning, she departed for Coruscant to serve as Naboo's representative in the Galactic Senate.

Although her duties took her far from home, she remained close to her family. Messages arrived regularly, and whenever her schedule permitted, she returned to Naboo to visit.

That same year, another joyful event transformed the Naberrie household.

Sola gave birth to a daughter.

The family gathered around the newborn.

Jobal gently held the infant while Ruwee stood beside her.

"She's beautiful," he said.

Sola smiled tiredly from her bed.

"I think so too."

Anakin cautiously approached.

The baby yawned.

"She's tiny."

"That's generally how babies work," Sola replied.

He ignored the comment.

"What's her name?"

"Ryoo."

The family welcomed Ryoo with excitement.

Questions naturally arose regarding the child's father.

Sola simply refused to discuss it.

One evening Jobal carefully approached the subject.

"You know nobody is trying to judge you."

"I know."

"Then why won't you tell us?"

Sola smiled.

"Because I don't want to."

Jobal sighed.

"That's your answer?"

"That's my answer."

And that was the end of the discussion.

The family accepted it.

Two years later, Sola gave birth to another daughter.

This time the child was named Pooja.

Again questions arose.

Again Sola provided no answers.

One afternoon Anakin sat beside her while she cradled the newborn.

"So who's the father?"

Sola looked at him.

"You too?"

"I was curious."

"Not happening."

"Same father as Ryoo?"

"No comment."

Anakin laughed.

"You're impossible."

"Correct."

Whether the two girls shared the same father or had different fathers remained a mystery known only to Sola.

The family eventually stopped asking.

What mattered was that Ryoo and Pooja were loved.

Anakin especially adored them.

From the moment they could walk, both girls seemed determined to follow him everywhere.

Whenever he worked on machines in the garage, they appeared.

Whenever he visited local landing fields, they wanted to come along.

Whenever he sat down to eat, somehow they ended up climbing into his lap.

One afternoon Anakin was rebuilding a speeder engine when he noticed silence behind him.

Silence was dangerous whenever small children were involved.

He turned.

Ryoo and Pooja were staring at him.

"What?"

Ryoo pointed.

"What's that?"

"A repulsorlift stabilizer."

Pooja pointed somewhere else.

"What's that?"

"A coolant regulator."

Ryoo pointed again.

"What's that?"

"A fuel injector."

The questioning continued for nearly an hour.

By the end, Anakin was exhausted.

Sola walked into the room.

"They've been bothering you all day?"

"Interrogating me."

Ryoo crossed her arms.

"We're helping."

"You're not."

Pooja nodded confidently.

"We're mechanics."

"You are definitely not mechanics."

The girls ignored him.

As they grew older, they became inseparable from him.

Neither child ever called him by his name very often.

Instead they settled on another title.

"Uncle Anakin!"

The first time Ryoo said it, the entire family smiled.

The name stuck immediately.

Anakin pretended not to care.

Secretly, he loved it.

The years continued passing.

Eventually Anakin completed Naboo's educational system.

Like many young adults, he faced the question of what to do next.

For him, the answer had never really been in doubt.

One evening he sat with Ruwee beside the lake near their home.

"So," Ruwee asked, "have you decided?"

Anakin nodded.

"I want to fly."

Ruwee smiled.

"That doesn't surprise me."

"It's the only thing I've ever wanted."

"You loved ships before you loved almost anything else."

"That's true."

"What kind of pilot?"

Anakin grinned.

"All of them."

And he meant it.

Over the next several years, Anakin pursued flight training with relentless enthusiasm.

He learned to fly N-1 starfighters.

Then skiffs.

Then shuttles.

Then transports.

Then speeders.

Whenever an opportunity appeared, he learned something new.

Pilots throughout Naboo quickly realized they were dealing with someone unusual.

Most students required months to master difficult maneuvers.

Anakin sometimes mastered them after seeing them once.

Most pilots improved gradually.

Anakin improved frighteningly fast.

At a training facility outside Theed, one instructor watched Anakin complete a difficult course.

The instructor lowered his datapad.

"How long have you been flying?"

Anakin shrugged.

"A while."

The instructor stared.

"A while."

"Yeah."

"No."

The instructor pointed toward the course.

"Nobody does that after 'a while.'"

Anakin smiled sheepishly.

Another instructor eventually developed a habit of warning newcomers.

"If Skywalker is in your group, don't compare yourself to him."

"Why not?"

"Because you'll feel terrible."

As his reputation spread, he gained opportunities to fly increasingly larger vessels.

Freighters.

Heavy transports.

Occasionally even capital ships under supervision.

Each new challenge seemed to excite him.

One captain watched Anakin guide a vessel through a difficult docking sequence.

Afterward he shook his head.

"You're how old?"

Anakin told him.

The captain frowned.

"I don't like that answer."

"Why?"

"Because I've been flying longer than you've been alive."

"Sorry."

"And you're better than me."

"Sorry."

The captain laughed.

"Stop apologizing."

By his late teens, Anakin had become one of the most respected young pilots on Naboo.

His name was well known among local flight instructors, transport companies, and security forces.

Whenever difficult flying was required, someone inevitably suggested him.

Meanwhile, life at the Naberrie household remained much the same.

Padmé continued her work as Naboo's senator on Coruscant.

Ruwee and Jobal remained the steady center of the family.

Sola continued raising Ryoo and Pooja while stubbornly refusing to reveal anything about their fathers.

The girls continued growing.

And Anakin remained exactly what he had always been to them.

Their beloved Uncle Anakin, who could fix anything, fly anything, and somehow always found time to play with them no matter how busy he became.

# Part 3

By the time Anakin reached 19 years of age, he had become one of Naboo's most trusted young pilots.

The landing fields around Theed knew him well. Security officers requested him for difficult transport assignments. Mechanics often sought his opinion before consulting one another.

Despite his growing reputation, he still spent most evenings at the Naberrie home.

Ryoo and Pooja still raced to the door whenever he returned.

"Uncle Anakin!"

He barely had time to step inside before they collided with him.

"I was gone for three hours."

"It felt longer," Ryoo complained.

Pooja tugged at his sleeve.

"Did you bring us anything?"

"I brought myself."

Pooja frowned dramatically.

"I was hoping for sweets."

One afternoon, a sleek Nubian starship descended toward Theed.

Ruwee looked up from the garden.

"Someone's coming."

Jobal stepped outside.

"I wonder if Padmé remembered to tell us she was visiting."

The polished silver vessel settled gently onto the landing platform.

The ramp lowered.

Padmé emerged first.

She smiled as she saw her family.

Before anyone could greet her, another familiar figure appeared behind her.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

His beard was neatly trimmed now, and the years had given him a calmer, more seasoned appearance.

Rolling down the ramp behind him came a blue-and-silver astromech droid.

R2-D2 emitted an enthusiastic whistle.

Padmé was immediately embraced by her family.

"It's good to be home," she said.

Then her eyes found Anakin.

"It's been too long."

"It has."

She smiled.

"You've gotten taller."

"I'd hope so."

"And I hear you're one of Naboo's best pilots."

"I try."

Obi-Wan stepped forward.

"Anakin."

Anakin smiled.

"Master Kenobi."

Obi-Wan shook his head.

"Just Obi-Wan is fine."

For a moment they simply regarded one another.

The last time they had truly spoken, Anakin had been a frightened 9 year old.

Now he stood before him as a confident young man.

"I've heard quite a lot about you," Obi-Wan said.

"Mostly good, I hope."

"Entirely good."

He smiled warmly.

"I'm glad to see you've done well for yourself."

Anakin looked slightly embarrassed.

"The Naberries deserve most of the credit."

"They gave me a home."

"They're good people."

"They certainly are."

R2-D2 rolled up beside Anakin and chirped excitedly.

Anakin laughed.

"I remember you."

The astromech answered with another series of cheerful beeps.

"You still talk too much."

R2 protested indignantly.

Inside the house, the mood became more serious.

Padmé waited until everyone had gathered before speaking.

"I wasn't planning to visit."

Ruwee immediately noticed the tension in her voice.

"What happened?"

Padmé exchanged a glance with Obi-Wan.

"I survived two assassination attempts."

The room fell silent.

Jobal stared.

"...Two?"

Padmé nodded.

"They happened on Coruscant."

Ryoo looked confused.

"Someone wanted to hurt Aunt Padmé?"

Padmé knelt beside her.

"Someone tried."

"But I'm alright."

Pooja hugged her tightly.

"Don't leave again."

Padmé smiled sadly.

"I'll stay for a little while."

Obi-Wan spoke next.

"Supreme Chancellor Palpatine strongly encouraged Senator Amidala to leave Coruscant for her own safety."

"So you came here," Ruwee said.

"Yes."

"And you?" Jobal asked Obi-Wan.

"I've been assigned as her bodyguard."

The family accepted that answer.

After dinner, Ruwee made a suggestion.

"You should both stay here."

"Our home is your home."

Jobal nodded.

"We have plenty of room."

Padmé smiled gratefully.

"I appreciate it."

"But I don't think we should."

"Why not?"

"Theed is still the capital."

She looked toward the window.

"If someone is trying to kill me, they'll expect me to come here."

Obi-Wan nodded in agreement.

"We've already discussed it."

Padmé continued.

"I'd rather stay at Varykino."

"The villa?" Sola asked.

"It's quieter."

"More isolated."

"And easier to secure."

Ruwee slowly nodded.

"I understand."

Just as Padmé and Obi-Wan prepared to leave, Jobal folded her arms.

"Not so fast."

Padmé blinked.

"What?"

"You've told us someone tried to kill you."

"You've told us almost nothing else."

Ruwee nodded.

"We deserve a little more than that."

Obi-Wan exchanged another glance with Padmé.

Finally he sighed.

"There isn't much we know with certainty."

"What do you know?" Ruwee asked.

Obi-Wan answered carefully.

"We captured the assassin responsible for placing the device."

"Before we could question her..."

"...she was killed."

"Killed by whom?" Sola asked.

"A bounty hunter."

"Did you identify him?"

"We're still investigating."

He hesitated.

"There is one lead."

Everyone leaned forward.

"A planet called Kamino."

Anakin frowned.

"I've never heard of it."

"Few people have."

"It's located far out in Wild Space."

Ruwee frowned.

"So you're going there?"

Obi-Wan shook his head.

"I intend to eventually."

"The location is difficult to determine."

Anakin thought quietly.

Then he spoke.

"I think I can find it."

Everyone turned toward him.

Obi-Wan looked curious.

"You do?"

"I'm a pilot."

"I've spent years navigating difficult routes."

"If there are enough clues, I can probably locate it."

Obi-Wan smiled politely.

"It isn't that simple."

"I know."

"But I'd still like to try."

Padmé looked surprised.

"Anakin..."

"I'm serious."

"If Kamino is connected to whoever's trying to kill you..."

"...then somebody should investigate."

Obi-Wan remained silent for several moments.

Finally he asked,

"You truly believe you can find it?"

"I do."

The discussion continued well into the evening.

Obi-Wan raised every objection he could think of.

The route was uncertain.

The destination unknown.

The people there might be hostile.

Anakin answered each concern calmly.

Eventually Obi-Wan nodded.

"Very well."

Anakin blinked.

"Really?"

"You've earned a reputation as an exceptional pilot."

"I trust your abilities."

He looked toward R2-D2.

"I'd like R2-D2 to accompany you."

The astromech beeped proudly.

Padmé added,

"And you can borrow my N-1."

Anakin smiled.

"I'll bring it back in one piece."

R2 whistled skeptically.

The following morning, Padmé and Obi-Wan departed for Varykino Villa.

Its quiet lakeside setting offered privacy and distance from the capital.

Meanwhile, Anakin climbed into the cockpit of the yellow-and-chrome N-1 starfighter.

R2-D2 locked himself into the astromech socket.

"Ready?"

R2 answered enthusiastically.

The engines ignited.

Moments later, the sleek fighter streaked into Naboo's blue sky before vanishing into hyperspace.

Finding Kamino proved difficult.

Ancient navigation records.

Obscure hyperspace routes.

Patient deduction.

Hours became days.

Finally, a blue ocean world appeared before him.

"Knew we'd find it."

R2 replied with a triumphant whistle.

The Kaminoans welcomed him with surprising politeness.

One of them introduced herself.

"I am Taun We."

"I'm Anakin Skywalker."

"You have arrived at an interesting time."

"What do you mean?"

"The Grand Army of the Republic is nearly complete."

Anakin frowned.

"The what?"

She seemed surprised by the question.

"You were not informed?"

"I don't think so."

Over the next several hours, Anakin learned astonishing things.

An enormous clone army had been created in secret.

Millions of soldiers.

Raised from a single genetic template.

Prepared for deployment.

He also met the template himself.

Jango Fett.

A heavily armored bounty hunter.

Beside him stood a young boy.

"My son, Boba."

Anakin remained polite while quietly memorizing every detail he could.

The moment he had privacy, he opened a secure communication.

Padmé and Obi-Wan appeared on the display from Varykino.

"What have you found?" Obi-Wan asked.

"A lot."

Anakin took a breath.

"There's a massive clone army here."

Obi-Wan stared.

"A clone army?"

"Built for the Republic."

Padmé looked equally stunned.

"Who ordered it?"

"I don't know."

"But there's a man here named Jango Fett."

"He says he's the genetic template."

Obi-Wan immediately became alert.

"Jango Fett."

Two more years of war scarred the Republic.

World after world became another battlefield.

Names that had once appeared only on navigation charts became symbols of sieges, evacuations, and desperate victories.

Anakin remained one of the Republic's most accomplished pilots.

His assignments took him across the galaxy.

Some days he escorted diplomatic shuttles.

Other days he flew interceptors against Separatist fleets.

When necessary, he commanded the helm of Republic capital ships through dangerous engagements.

Between campaigns, Obi-Wan and Ahsoka continued his instruction.

His lightsaber technique steadily improved.

His control over the Force grew stronger.

He still never considered himself a Jedi.

Whenever someone referred to him as one, he corrected them.

"I'm a Republic pilot."

"You carry a lightsaber," Rex had once replied.

"I do."

"You use the Force."

"I do."

"You train with Jedi."

"I also fly ships."

Rex laughed.

"I suppose that's true."

The war, however, was changing the Republic in ways that reached far beyond the battlefield.

On Coruscant, concern spread through the Senate.

Emergency powers granted to Supreme Chancellor Palpatine early in the conflict had expanded steadily over the years.

Measures originally presented as temporary remained in force.

New authorities were added.

Old limitations quietly disappeared.

Eventually a large coalition of senators came together.

They became known as the Delegation of 2000.

Among its leading voices stood Senator Padmé Amidala of Naboo.

One afternoon, the Senate chamber filled to capacity.

The Delegation formally presented its concerns.

Padmé stepped forward.

"The Republic faces a grave war."

"No one here denies that."

She looked around the enormous chamber.

"But we must also remember what we are fighting to preserve."

"Mere victory is not enough."

"If we lose our democracy in the process..."

"...then we will already have lost."

Several senators applauded.

Others remained silent.

Padmé continued.

"The emergency powers granted to Chancellor Palpatine have expanded continuously."

"Authorities once described as temporary have become permanent."

"The balance between the Senate and the Chancellor has shifted dangerously."

She took a slow breath.

"The Republic is moving toward authoritarian government."

Murmurs spread throughout the chamber.

One senator rose.

"I share many of Senator Amidala's concerns."

"But removing the Chancellor during an active war may create instability."

"Our fleets require leadership."

"Our armies require direction."

"A transition now could cost lives."

Several senators nodded.

Padmé answered immediately.

"That concern is genuine."

"I respect it."

She looked toward the speaker.

"But our Constitution provides for exactly this situation."

"If the Senate removes one Chancellor..."

"...it may elect another almost immediately."

"The Republic will not spend months leaderless."

"The transition can occur rapidly."

She spread her hands.

"The risk of temporary disruption does not outweigh the long-term danger of concentrating executive authority indefinitely."

The chamber grew increasingly divided.

Finally Palpatine himself requested permission to speak.

He stood calmly.

"My fellow senators."

"I have accepted extraordinary responsibilities only because extraordinary circumstances demanded them."

"I have sought neither dictatorship nor personal glory."

"My sole objective has been victory."

He looked across the chamber.

"If you believe another Chancellor can better guide the Republic through this war..."

"...that is your right."

"But I ask you to remember the sacrifices already made."

"The enemy remains powerful."

"Division now serves only the Separatists."

His speech received considerable applause.

Yet the debate continued for hours.

When voting finally began, tension filled every corner of the chamber.

The result appeared overhead.

The motion had passed.

The Senate had withdrawn its confidence.

Palpatine was no longer Supreme Chancellor.

The chamber erupted into countless conversations.

Later that same day, nominations opened for a successor.

After another lengthy session, Senator Bail Organa of Alderaan secured the necessary support.

He took the oath of office before the Senate.

The Republic had a new Supreme Chancellor.

That evening, in a private chamber far from the Senate floor, Palpatine activated a secure HoloNet transmitter.

His expression had become cold.

The transmission reached clone commanders across the galaxy.

He spoke only four words.

"Execute Order 66."

Far away on Lola Sayu, Obi-Wan, Ahsoka, Commander Cody, Captain Rex, and their clone forces fought fiercely against Separatist positions.

Above them, Anakin circled in his starfighter, providing close air support.

"Target destroyed," Anakin reported.

"Good shooting," Obi-Wan answered.

Then everything changed.

Without warning, clone rifles swung away from the battle droids.

Toward the Jedi.

Rex's expression became strangely vacant.

He raised his weapon.

"Captain?" Ahsoka asked.

The first blaster bolts erupted.

Obi-Wan's lightsaber ignited instantly.

Ahsoka's followed a heartbeat later.

Blue blades intercepted the incoming fire.

"What are you doing?" Obi-Wan shouted.

No answer came.

More clones joined in.

Cody fired.

Rex fired.

Entire platoons turned simultaneously.

"The clones have gone rogue!" Ahsoka yelled.

Within seconds, the Jedi found themselves surrounded by their own soldiers.

Anakin watched the chaos unfold from above.

"No..."

His fighter rolled violently into a dive.

Anti-aircraft fire erupted around him.

He ignored it.

The starfighter plunged toward the battlefield at terrifying speed.

Obi-Wan looked upward.

"Anakin!"

The fighter skimmed barely above the ground.

The cockpit canopy remained closed.

There was no room to land.

Anakin reached out through the Force.

"Come on..."

Obi-Wan suddenly felt himself lifted.

So did Ahsoka.

Both Jedi were pulled bodily upward.

The starfighter streaked overhead.

An instant later, both Jedi slammed into the rear compartment.

Anakin accelerated immediately.

Clone fire chased them into the sky.

Several bolts struck the shields.

"Hold on!"

The fighter climbed almost vertically.

Within moments, it escaped the atmosphere.

Then stars stretched into brilliant lines.

Hyperspace.

Only then did silence settle inside the cockpit.

Obi-Wan slowly caught his breath.

"...What was that?"

Ahsoka stared at the rear hatch.

"The clones have gone rogue!!"

Neither of them had any explanation.

Anakin kept his hands on the controls.

"We should go back to Coruscant immediately and see what's going on."

Neither Jedi argued.

Hours later, Coruscant filled the forward viewport.

Then all three froze.

Smoke.

The Jedi Temple was burning.

Entire sections of the ancient complex were engulfed in flames.

"What..."

Ahsoka whispered.

Anakin accelerated toward the Temple.

Republic gunships filled the skies.

Clone forces surrounded the complex.

At their head stood a hooded figure carrying a crimson lightsaber.

The mysterious Sith Lord personally directed the assault.

Obi-Wan looked horrified.

"A Sith."

Anakin had not seen the Jedi Temple since he had visited it as a frightened 9 year old.

Now the magnificent structure stood scarred by explosions and fire.

The fighter landed.

The three sprinted inside.

Chaos greeted them.

Temple Guards fought desperately.

Knights and Masters defended corridors room by room.

Padawans escorted terrified younglings toward safer chambers.

Clone troopers attacked relentlessly.

Anakin ignited his lightsaber.

"I'm with you."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"Protect the younglings."

Battle after battle followed.

The three fought side by side through smoke-filled halls.

Eventually they reached one of the Temple's great central chambers.

The hooded Sith awaited them.

"So."

His voice was calm.

"You survived."

Obi-Wan ignited his lightsaber.

"We'll stop you."

The duel began.

The Sith moved with terrifying speed.

One moment he pressed Obi-Wan.

The next he attacked Ahsoka.

Then Anakin.

None of them alone could match him.

Together they somehow endured.

Obi-Wan covered openings.

Ahsoka attacked from unexpected angles.

Anakin used both the Force and his growing lightsaber skill to keep the pressure divided.

Minutes became an exhausting struggle.

Neither side gained a decisive advantage.

Then another voice echoed through the chamber.

"Stand aside!"

Mace Windu charged into the fight.

He had returned from an offworld campaign the instant news reached him that the Temple was under attack.

His purple blade crashed against the Sith Lord's crimson weapon.

The impact drove the attacker backward.

Obi-Wan immediately recognized the opportunity.

"Mace!"

Without warning, another red blade entered the battle.

Count Dooku.

He moved directly between Mace and the others.

"No farther."

A wave of the Force separated the combatants.

Obi-Wan, Ahsoka, and Anakin were forced back together.

Mace found himself isolated against the hooded Sith.

Elsewhere within the Temple, another nightmare unfolded.

General Grievous had accompanied the attack.

His four lightsabers whirled through smoke-filled corridors.

Several Jedi fell before him.

Finally three Masters intercepted him.

Kit Fisto.

Agen Kolar.

Saesee Tiin.

The three surrounded the cyborg.

Their duel echoed throughout the Temple.

Every chamber became its own battlefield.

Obi-Wan, Ahsoka, and Anakin fought Dooku with everything they possessed.

Dooku's mastery remained extraordinary.

Yet this time he faced three coordinated opponents.

Obi-Wan's disciplined defense.

Ahsoka's speed.

Anakin's increasingly powerful use of both the Force and his lightsaber.

The battle seemed endless.

Across the Temple, Mace Windu and the hooded Sith battled with astonishing ferocity.

Neither yielded.

Elsewhere, Kit, Agen, and Saesee slowly cornered Grievous despite heavy casualties around them.

The fighting continued for what felt like an eternity.

Then, one by one, the battles reached their conclusions.

Mace's blade struck true.

The hooded Sith collapsed.

At nearly the same moment, Obi-Wan created a brief opening.

"Ahsoka!"

She attacked immediately.

Dooku turned to defend.

Anakin stepped inside Dooku's guard.

Three coordinated strikes landed almost together.

Count Dooku fell.

Not long afterward, Grievous finally succumbed beneath the combined assault of Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin.

Without their leaders, the clone assault gradually collapsed.

The surviving Jedi regained control of the Temple.

Hours later, silence finally returned.

The cost became impossible to ignore.

Entire wings of the Temple lay in ruins.

Broken columns littered the halls.

Smoke drifted through shattered windows.

Clone armor and battle droid wreckage mixed with the bodies of fallen Jedi.

Medical teams moved constantly.

No one understood what had happened.

One question echoed everywhere.

"Why?"

Why had the clones suddenly turned?

Why had they fought beside Dooku?

Why had a Sith Lord personally led the attack?

Obi-Wan slowly approached the hooded body lying where Mace had defeated it.

Several Jedi gathered nearby.

Mace stood silently.

Obi-Wan knelt.

He reached toward the hood.

"Let's see who you are."

He pulled it back.

The chamber fell completely silent.

No one spoke.

No one moved.

Anakin stared in disbelief.

Ahsoka's eyes widened.

Mace remained frozen.

Obi-Wan looked down at the lifeless face.

"...Palpatine."

The former Supreme Chancellor of the Republic lay dead upon the floor of the Jedi Temple, revealed at last as the Sith Lord who had commanded the assault.

# Part 7

When the last blaster fire faded and the surviving clone troopers inside the Jedi Temple had either been defeated or forced to retreat, the wounded were carried to improvised medical stations.

Smoke still drifted through shattered ceilings.

Jedi, Temple Guards, and medical personnel moved continuously through the ruined halls.

Mace Windu wasted no time.

Standing in one of the few communications rooms that remained intact, he opened two priority channels.

The first reached every Jedi communication frequency that was still operational.

The second reached the Galactic Senate.

His image appeared across countless holographic projectors.

"This is Master Mace Windu."

"The Jedi Temple on Coruscant has been attacked."

"The attack was led personally by the Sith Lord responsible for orchestrating today's events."

He paused only briefly.

"The Sith Lord has been identified."

"It was former Supreme Chancellor Palpatine."

Silence answered him.

He continued.

"Count Dooku fought beside him."

"General Grievous also participated in the assault."

"Palpatine, Dooku, and Grievous have all been killed."

"The Temple remains standing."

"However, clone forces throughout the galaxy have turned against the Jedi."

"If you receive this transmission..."

"...trust no clone unit until further notice."

"...Survive."

The message spread rapidly.

Only minutes later, Chancellor Bail Organa convened an emergency session of the Senate.

The chamber remained filled with senators still struggling to comprehend the day's events.

Bail stood before them.

"The Senate has received confirmation from Master Windu."

"The individual who served as Supreme Chancellor..."

"...was a Sith Lord."

Quiet disbelief spread through the chamber.

Bail continued.

"He personally led an assault upon the Jedi Temple."

"He fought alongside Count Dooku and General Grievous."

"Our citizens deserve to know the truth."

A senator stood.

"If this information remains secret..."

"...rumors will become more dangerous than facts."

Another nodded.

"I support immediate public disclosure."

The motion passed overwhelmingly.

Within the hour, the Republic broadcast the information across the HoloNet.

Every major world received the same announcement.

Palpatine's identity.

His role as a Sith Lord.

His alliance with Dooku and Grievous.

The deaths of all three.

Across the Confederacy of Independent Systems, reactions varied dramatically.

On some worlds, citizens watched the broadcast in stunned silence.

One merchant on Raxus Secundus looked toward his neighbor.

"If that's true..."

"...what have we been fighting for?"

Another replied quietly,

"We wanted independence."

"Did we get used?"

Elsewhere, others rejected the broadcast entirely.

"A Republic fabrication."

"They're trying to divide us."

"They're lying."

Military officers argued fiercely.

Politicians debated.

The deaths of Dooku and Grievous, however, proved impossible to deny.

Too many independent reports confirmed them.

Even those who distrusted the Republic accepted that both leaders were gone.

Despite the confusion, surviving CIS military commanders continued their offensives.

Republic fleets still came under attack.

Border systems still changed hands.

The war refused to end by itself.

On Coruscant, Bail Organa met repeatedly with military leaders.

One meeting stretched late into the night.

An admiral looked exhausted.

"We cannot fight two wars."

"No," Bail agreed.

"The Separatists continue attacking."

"Our clone army has become unreliable."

Another officer spoke.

"Even if we stabilize one front..."

"...the other collapses."

Silence settled over the room.

Finally Bail stood.

"There may be another option."

A few days later, the Chancellor addressed the galaxy.

His speech reached both Republic and Confederacy worlds.

"I speak today not only to the citizens of the Republic..."

"...but also to the people of the Confederacy of Independent Systems."

He folded his hands.

"The war has already taken too much from all of us."

"If your objective is independence..."

"...the Republic is prepared to recognize it."

"If your objective is sovereignty..."

"...the Republic is prepared to recognize it."

"If your objective is self-government..."

"...the Republic is prepared to recognize it."

He looked directly into the camera.

"In return, I ask only one thing."

"Stop attacking the Republic."

"Let this war end."

The speech stunned both governments.

On Raxus Secundus, the CIS Senate gathered immediately.

Debate lasted for days.

One senator argued,

"We've fought too long to surrender now."

Another answered,

"Is accepting independence surrender?"

A third rose.

"Dooku is dead."

"Grievous is dead."

"Our military leadership has suffered enormous losses."

"We sought the right to govern ourselves."

"If the Republic is finally willing to acknowledge that..."

"...why should more people die?"

Eventually the vote was held.

The proposal passed.

Soon afterward, a ceasefire between the Republic and the Confederacy officially took effect.

Across the galaxy, weapons slowly fell silent.

While diplomats worked, another crisis demanded immediate attention.

Order 66 had devastated the Jedi.

Many had escaped the initial attacks.

Many had not.

Those who survived often found themselves stranded.

Some had lost their transports.

Others remained trapped on hostile worlds.

Many hid from clone units still hunting them.

Obi-Wan, Ahsoka, and Anakin redirected nearly all of their efforts toward rescue operations.

One rescue followed another.

Again and again, Anakin volunteered for the most dangerous assignments.

Ahsoka eventually asked him,

"How do you keep finding ways through impossible situations?"

Anakin smiled.

"I fly."

"I know."

"I've spent my whole life looking for paths other people miss."

One mission took them into a canyon where surviving Jedi had been trapped for days.

Another required landing under heavy clone patrols.

Another involved recovering stranded Padawans before enemy forces reached them.

Obi-Wan watched Anakin complete another seemingly impossible extraction.

"You've become remarkably good at this."

Anakin looked toward the rescued Jedi boarding his transport.

"They deserve to come home."

Weeks became months.

Although Order 66 claimed many lives, enough Jedi survived both the Temple assault and the attacks across the galaxy to begin rebuilding.

The damaged Temple itself became a symbol of that determination.

Construction crews worked beside Jedi volunteers.

Collapsed chambers were rebuilt.

Broken towers rose again.

Libraries reopened.

Training halls were restored.

Slowly, the Temple returned.

Meanwhile, another mystery demanded answers.

Captured clone troopers underwent extensive medical examinations.

Doctors searched desperately for an explanation.

Eventually one physician summoned representatives from both the Republic and the Jedi.

A detailed holographic scan appeared above the examination table.

"You should see this."

A tiny object was highlighted within the clone's brain.

"What is it?" Obi-Wan asked.

"A biological implant."

The physician enlarged the image.

"We believe it's an inhibitor chip."

"Can it be removed?"

"We're going to try."

The procedure was delicate.

When the first operation succeeded, everyone waited.

The clone slowly regained consciousness.

He looked around the room.

His eyes settled on Obi-Wan.

He froze.

"What..."

He looked down at his own hands.

"What have I done?"

Obi-Wan remained calm.

"Do you remember?"

The clone nodded weakly.

"I remember everything."

Tears filled his eyes.

"I couldn't stop myself."

The doctors quickly confirmed their findings.

Removing the inhibitor chip immediately ended the clones' compulsion to attack Jedi.

Many reacted with horror as memories returned.

Others openly wept.

One physician addressed the Senate.

"We have identified the cause."

"The chips manipulated behavior."

"They compelled obedience."

"They were responsible."

A galaxy-wide medical program began.

Hospital ships.

Military facilities.

Civilian clinics.

Every surviving clone received priority treatment.

The effort required several months.

Eventually the final inhibitor chip was removed.

The clone army was finally free.

With the immediate crises addressed, Chancellor Bail Organa opened formal negotiations with the Confederacy.

The talks proved difficult.

Borders.

Trade.

Navigation rights.

Recognition.

Disarmament.

Every issue required careful compromise.

Months passed.

Finally representatives from both governments gathered to sign the agreement.

Bail Organa placed his signature upon the treaty.

Across from him, the Confederacy's representatives did the same.

The Republic formally recognized the Confederacy of Independent Systems as an independent and sovereign state.

The Confederacy retained legal jurisdiction over every world under its control at the moment the treaty was signed.

After three years of devastating conflict, the Clone Wars officially ended.

The peace that followed transformed the galaxy.

The cloning facilities on Kamino ceased military production.

The Republic enacted legislation recognizing every surviving clone as a legal person with full citizenship and individual rights.

Many clones chose civilian lives.

Others remained in military service voluntarily.

Others pursued entirely new careers.

Across Confederacy space, battle droid foundries dramatically reduced weapons production.

Many converted to manufacturing civilian labor droids, medical assistants, agricultural machines, and industrial equipment.

The Republic also turned inward.

Palpatine's rise had exposed dangerous weaknesses.

Constitutional reforms followed.

Emergency powers became tightly restricted.

Executive authority received stronger legal limits.

The Senate expanded oversight mechanisms.

Additional democratic safeguards were established to prevent any future Chancellor from accumulating such sweeping authority again.

The galaxy entered an unfamiliar political age.

Two sovereign powers now existed.

The Galactic Republic.

The Confederacy of Independent Systems.

Relations remained cautious.

Distrust lingered.

Yet after years of relentless warfare, few leaders wished to begin another conflict.

For the first time in years, genuine hope for lasting peace appeared possible.

Padmé Amidala remained on Coruscant.

She continued serving Naboo as its senator under Chancellor Bail Organa's administration.

Her work shifted from wartime emergency legislation to reconstruction, diplomacy, and constitutional reform.

Once the final Jedi rescue missions concluded, Anakin submitted his retirement from active military service.

Obi-Wan met him shortly before his departure.

"So."

"Going home?"

Anakin nodded.

"I've been away long enough."

"You've earned the rest."

"I don't think I'll ever completely stop flying."

"I would be shocked if you did."

Anakin smiled.

"Probably."

His transport landed in Theed several days later.

Ruwee waited outside.

"So..."

"Back for good?"

Anakin smiled.

"Back for good."

Jobal embraced him tightly.

"We've missed you."

"I missed you too."

Ryoo and Pooja, now much older than when he had first left, hurried outside.

"Uncle Anakin!"

He laughed.

"You two have grown."

"So have you," Ryoo replied.

Sola smiled from the doorway.

"Took you long enough."

"It's good to be home."

Within the rebuilt Jedi Temple, life slowly returned.

Obi-Wan remained with the Order.

So did Ahsoka.

Not long after the war's conclusion, the Council gathered once more.

Yoda smiled.

"Completed your trials, you have."

Mace nodded.

"Ahsoka Tano."

"You have served with courage, wisdom, and compassion."

"We recognize you as a Jedi Knight."

Ahsoka bowed respectfully.

"Thank you."

One quiet afternoon, two familiar visitors entered the Temple.

Captain Rex.

Commander Cody.

Neither wore helmets.

Both looked deeply uncomfortable.

A Temple Guard escorted them to Obi-Wan and Ahsoka.

Rex spoke first.

"We wanted to say something."

Cody nodded.

"The inhibitor chips are gone."

"We remember everything."

Silence lingered.

Finally Rex lowered his head.

"I'm sorry."

"So am I," Cody added quietly.

"We turned our weapons on you."

"We would never have chosen that."

Obi-Wan stepped forward.

"You don't owe us an apology."

Both clones looked up.

Ahsoka smiled gently.

"It wasn't your fault."

"You didn't choose what happened."

Rex struggled to answer.

"But we still—"

Obi-Wan rested a hand on his shoulder.

"The responsibility belongs to the one who placed those chips inside you."

"And to the man who activated them."

Cody slowly nodded.

"We'll spend the rest of our lives making sure nothing like that ever happens again."

"I believe you will," Obi-Wan replied.

The four stood together in the restored Temple, no longer divided by suspicion or war.

Outside, Coruscant's endless skyline stretched beneath a calmer sky.

The Clone Wars had ended.

The Sith who had hidden at the heart of the Republic were gone.

The Republic itself had changed.

The Confederacy now stood as a recognized neighboring state rather than an enemy to be conquered.

The Jedi Order had survived its darkest trial and begun rebuilding.

And on Naboo, the former slave boy who had once arrived with nowhere to go had finally returned to the family that had chosen him, carrying with him the hope that the peace so many had fought to achieve would endure.
