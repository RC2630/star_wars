# Part 1

The Clone Wars had stretched the Galactic Republic to its limits. Across countless battlefields, Jedi Knights led clone armies against the Separatists, while on Coruscant the aging Supreme Chancellor, Sheev Palpatine, had accumulated extraordinary emergency powers in the name of winning the war.

Anakin Skywalker had become one of the Republic's greatest heroes. Gifted beyond measure and fiercely loyal to those he loved, he was also burdened by fear. Terrible visions haunted his sleep—visions of his wife, Padmé Amidala, dying in childbirth. Desperate to prevent that future, Anakin found himself increasingly drawn toward the Chancellor, who claimed that the dark side of the Force held secrets capable of preventing death itself.

Then everything changed.

During a private meeting, Chancellor Palpatine finally revealed the truth.

"I know the ways of the Force," he had said with a calm smile. "I have waited a long time for you, Anakin."

Anakin stared at him.

"What are you saying?"

"The Force is a pathway to many abilities some consider to be... unnatural."

Anakin's eyes widened.

"You're the Sith Lord the Jedi have been searching for."

Palpatine did not deny it.

"I can help you save Padmé."

Anakin left the office in turmoil. The revelation shook him to his core, but despite everything the Chancellor had done for him over the years, there was one duty he could not ignore.

He went directly to Master Mace Windu.

"The Chancellor is the Sith Lord," Anakin said. "He's the one we've been looking for."

For the briefest moment, even Mace Windu's composure faltered.

"Are you certain?"

"Yes."

Windu nodded once.

"Then our worst fears have been realized."

He activated his communicator.

"Masters Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, Agen Kolar. Meet me immediately."

Turning back to Anakin, Windu's expression remained firm.

"If what you've told me is true, then you will have earned my trust."

Anakin looked toward the doors.

"I'm coming with you."

"No." Windu's voice allowed no argument. "You will remain in the Jedi Temple. This matter is for the Council."

Reluctantly, Anakin watched the four Jedi Masters depart.

Hours seemed to pass in only minutes.

He paced the Temple corridors, unable to silence the conflict raging inside him.

If the Jedi arrested Palpatine...

If they killed him...

What would become of Padmé?

Finally, Anakin could endure the waiting no longer.

He rushed to his starfighter, launched from the Temple hangar, and sped through the skies of Coruscant toward the Senate District.

When he reached the Chancellor's office, silence greeted him.

The grand doors slid open.

Anakin froze.

The room was devastated.

Deep lightsaber scars blackened the walls. Furniture lay shattered. Smoke drifted lazily through the chamber.

At the center of the office stood Chancellor Palpatine.

His crimson lightsaber remained ignited, raised confidently in one hand.

Around him lay four motionless bodies.

Master Mace Windu.

Master Kit Fisto.

Master Saesee Tiin.

Master Agen Kolar.

Blood stained the polished floor.

Palpatine smiled.

Anakin's grief instantly transformed into burning anger.

His hand clenched into a fist.

"Palpatine," he said, his voice trembling with rage, "what have you done?"

Palpatine threw back his head and laughed.

"The Jedi are SO WEAK."

He gestured carelessly toward the fallen Masters.

"Four masters arrived. I single-handedly killed all four of them."

Anakin ignited his blue lightsaber.

The familiar hum filled the ruined office.

Palpatine grinned.

"Oh, you want to be the fifth?"

Anakin answered with action instead of words.

He charged.

Their blades collided with an explosive crack.

Palpatine attacked immediately, his crimson blade moving with terrifying speed. Each strike carried immense power, forcing Anakin backward across the ruined chamber.

Anakin barely managed to block a thrust aimed directly at his heart.

Palpatine spun effortlessly, unleashing a relentless series of cuts that battered Anakin's defenses.

The young Jedi was driven across the office.

Glass shattered behind him.

Another strike.

Another.

Another.

Anakin gritted his teeth.

*He's stronger than Count Dooku... stronger than anyone I've ever fought.*

Palpatine laughed as they dueled.

"You disappoint me, Skywalker!"

His blade hammered downward.

Anakin caught it with both hands on his lightsaber hilt, knees bending beneath the force.

Palpatine kicked him backward.

Anakin crashed into a broken desk before rolling away just as a red blade sliced through the wreckage.

The duel continued.

Furniture exploded into splinters.

Windows cracked.

Sparks showered across the chamber.

Again and again Palpatine pressed the attack.

Yet as the minutes passed, something changed.

His movements remained deadly...

...but they were becoming slower.

His breathing grew heavier.

The duel against four Jedi Masters had taken its toll.

Anakin noticed it.

His own stamina, forged by years of war, remained strong.

He began forcing the pace.

Every blocked strike became a counterattack.

Every retreat became an advance.

Palpatine's smile slowly disappeared.

Their blades blurred through the air.

Blue and red flashed across the chamber in rapid succession.

Palpatine attempted another whirlwind assault.

Anakin met it head-on.

He drove Palpatine backward one step.

Then another.

Then another.

The Sith Lord's breathing became ragged.

"You... learn quickly..." Palpatine hissed.

Anakin said nothing.

His anger no longer clouded his judgment.

It sharpened his resolve.

He attacked relentlessly.

A powerful overhead strike forced Palpatine into a desperate block.

Another slash.

Another.

The Sith Lord stumbled.

For the first time, Anakin saw genuine strain on his opponent's face.

Palpatine lunged with one final burst of speed.

Anakin sidestepped.

Their blades locked.

For a heartbeat they struggled, faces only inches apart.

Then Anakin twisted his wrists and broke the lock.

With one sweeping strike, he knocked Palpatine's lightsaber from his hand.

The crimson weapon flew across the office and clattered onto the floor beside Mace Windu's body.

Palpatine staggered backward.

His legs gave way.

He fell onto one knee.

Anakin stepped forward, placing the glowing blue blade against the Sith Lord's throat.

Silence filled the office.

Palpatine slowly raised his hands.

"Wait... wait."

Anakin's expression remained hard.

Palpatine spoke quickly.

"Remember. Only I have the knowledge to help you save Padmé."

For a brief instant, Anakin saw her face in his mind.

Her smile.

Her laughter.

The terrifying visions of her dying.

His grip tightened around his lightsaber.

"You killed four Jedi Masters," Anakin said quietly. "I cannot let you live regardless."

Palpatine leaned forward slightly.

"So you don't care about saving Padmé anymore?"

Anakin did not answer.

He had already made his decision.

Before Palpatine could speak another word, Anakin swung.

The blue blade passed cleanly through the Sith Lord's neck.

Palpatine's body collapsed onto the floor.

His yellow eyes stared blankly upward for only a moment before all life left them.

The crimson lightsaber lying across the room shut itself off.

The office became utterly silent.

Anakin stood motionless among the fallen.

Four Jedi Masters had died.

The Sith Lord was dead.

Only the distant sounds of Coruscant drifted through the shattered windows as Anakin looked upon the bodies, knowing that nothing in the Republic would ever be the same again.

# Part 2

The silence inside the Chancellor's office lingered long after the duel had ended.

Anakin Skywalker extinguished his lightsaber and looked one last time at the fallen.

Master Mace Windu.

Master Kit Fisto.

Master Saesee Tiin.

Master Agen Kolar.

Then his eyes shifted to the lifeless body of Sheev Palpatine.

The Sith Lord was dead.

There was nothing more to be done here alone.

Anakin turned and hurried from the office, making his way back through the Senate District to his starfighter. Within minutes he was flying toward the Jedi Temple.

When he landed, he moved quickly through the corridors until he found Master Shaak Ti.

She immediately noticed his expression.

"Skywalker," she said. "What happened?"

Anakin stopped before her.

His voice was heavy.

"Master Windu... Master Kit Fisto... Master Saesee Tiin... Master Agen Kolar..."

He paused.

"They're dead."

Shaak Ti stared at him.

"What?"

"They confronted Chancellor Palpatine."

He took a slow breath.

"Palpatine truly was the Sith Lord we had been searching for."

For a brief moment, Shaak Ti said nothing.

Then she asked quietly,

"Tell me everything."

Anakin recounted the events from the beginning.

"I discovered that Palpatine was a Sith Lord and reported it to Master Windu. He gathered Masters Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar, and ordered me to remain here while they arrested the Chancellor."

"You disobeyed."

"I did."

"What did you find?"

"I arrived after the battle had already ended."

His jaw tightened.

"Palpatine was standing over all four of them. They were already dead."

"And then?"

"I challenged him."

Shaak Ti looked directly into his eyes.

"You fought Darth Sidious alone?"

"I did."

"Who won?"

"I killed him."

Silence settled between them.

Finally Shaak Ti nodded.

"There is no time to lose."

She immediately activated a Temple communications console.

"Recall every surviving member of the Jedi Council."

She transmitted identical emergency orders across the galaxy.

"Master Yoda."

"Master Obi-Wan Kenobi."

"Master Plo Koon."

"Master Ki-Adi-Mundi."

"Master Stass Allie."

"Master Coleman Kcaj."

"Return to Coruscant immediately. This is an emergency of the highest priority."

No further explanation was given through the initial message.

Within minutes, replies began arriving.

When each Council member learned that four Jedi Masters had been killed, none questioned the order.

On Kashyyyk, Yoda ended his coordination with the Wookiees at once.

On Utapau, Obi-Wan entrusted command of the operation to the clone officers before departing immediately.

Plo Koon, Ki-Adi-Mundi, Stass Allie, and Coleman Kcaj likewise abandoned their respective Clone Wars campaigns without hesitation.

Every surviving Council member made Coruscant their highest priority.

Some time later, the Council Chamber, once nearly full, felt strangely empty.

Only eight seats were occupied.

Master Yoda.

Master Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Master Plo Koon.

Master Ki-Adi-Mundi.

Master Shaak Ti.

Master Stass Allie.

Master Coleman Kcaj.

And Jedi Knight Anakin Skywalker, standing before them.

The empty chairs of Mace Windu, Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar served as silent reminders of the day's losses.

Yoda looked toward Anakin.

"Tell us what happened, Skywalker."

Anakin nodded.

"It began when Chancellor Palpatine revealed enough that I became convinced he was likely the Sith Lord we had been searching for."

Obi-Wan frowned.

"He admitted it?"

"He spoke of the dark side and powers beyond death. It was enough that I immediately came to the Temple and informed Master Windu."

Ki-Adi-Mundi folded his hands.

"And then?"

"Master Windu assembled Masters Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar. They left to confront Palpatine and ordered me to remain at the Temple."

"You did not obey."

"No."

Anakin lowered his eyes briefly.

"I couldn't remain here."

He continued.

"When I reached the Chancellor's office... the battle was already over."

His voice grew quieter.

"The four Masters were dead."

The Council listened in complete silence.

"Palpatine was standing over them with his lightsaber ignited. He mocked them. He said, 'The Jedi are SO WEAK. Four masters arrived. I single-handedly killed all four of them.'"

Obi-Wan's face hardened.

"So you attacked."

"Yes."

"Describe the duel."

"He was stronger than anyone I'd ever faced."

Anakin paused.

"But he had already fought the four Masters."

"I realized he was becoming exhausted while we fought."

"I kept pressing him."

"Eventually I disarmed him."

"I held my lightsaber to his throat."

"And then?"

"He tried to persuade me to spare him."

"What did he say?" asked Plo Koon.

"He claimed only he possessed knowledge that could save Padmé."

Anakin looked around the chamber.

"I refused."

"I told him that he had murdered four Jedi Masters and that I could not allow him to live."

"I struck him down."

Silence returned.

At length, Yoda closed his eyes.

"The Sith Lord... destroyed, he is."

"Yes," Anakin answered.

The Council remained deep in thought.

Finally Obi-Wan spoke.

"For more than a decade we searched for the Sith Lord directing this war from the shadows."

Ki-Adi-Mundi nodded.

"Many Jedi devoted their lives to that search."

Plo Koon looked toward Anakin.

"You defeated him alone after he had already overcome four Council Masters."

Shaak Ti added,

"No one should mistake how extraordinary that accomplishment is."

Yoda slowly opened his eyes.

"Long denied you the rank of Master has been."

He looked directly at Anakin.

"No longer."

The Grand Master rose.

"By unanimous decision of this Council, Master Anakin Skywalker you are."

Anakin stood speechless for a moment.

Then he bowed.

"Thank you, Masters."

After a brief silence, Anakin spoke again.

"There is something else."

The Council turned toward him.

"When I first discovered Palpatine's identity..."

He hesitated.

"I had a chance to kill him."

Several Masters exchanged glances.

Anakin continued.

"I already had my lightsaber at his throat."

"I could have ended it immediately."

"But I didn't."

"I reported him to Master Windu instead because I believed he should be arrested."

He looked down.

"Looking back..."

"I regret that decision."

"If I had simply killed Palpatine right then and there..."

He glanced toward the four empty Council seats.

"...the final outcome would have been the same, except Masters Windu, Fisto, Tiin, and Kolar would still be alive."

The chamber fell quiet once more.

It was Obi-Wan who answered first.

"Anakin."

His voice was calm.

"You did exactly what a Jedi should have done."

Anakin looked at him.

"You had reason to suspect the Chancellor."

Obi-Wan continued,

"But suspicion is not certainty."

Ki-Adi-Mundi nodded.

"At that moment, you had not yet established beyond all reasonable doubt that Chancellor Palpatine was truly a Sith Lord."

"He was still the Supreme Chancellor of the Republic," Stass Allie said. "Killing him immediately would have meant killing a man who, from the perspective of the law and the evidence then available, had not yet been conclusively proven guilty."

Plo Koon folded his hands inside his robes.

"You chose to report your discovery to the Council."

"That was the proper course."

Shaak Ti added,

"You did not know that Masters Windu, Fisto, Tiin, and Kolar would fall."

"No one here knew."

Coleman Kcaj nodded solemnly.

"Do not judge your decision using knowledge you did not possess at the time."

Finally Yoda spoke.

"Hindsight, dangerous it is."

He looked steadily at Anakin.

"Different choices imagine, the mind always can."

"But judged by what you knew then, correct your actions were."

"The deaths of our fellow Masters, deeply tragic they are."

"Perhaps prevented, had events unfolded differently."

"But blame yourself, you should not."

"You acted as a Jedi."

Anakin slowly nodded.

"I understand."

Later that day, the surviving members of the Council traveled together to the Chancellor's office.

The devastation remained exactly as Anakin had left it.

Broken furniture lay scattered throughout the room.

Scorch marks covered the walls.

The shattered windows overlooked the endless skyline of Coruscant.

The bodies of Mace Windu, Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar remained where they had fallen.

Near the center of the room lay the body of Sheev Palpatine.

Before disturbing anything, the Jedi carefully documented the entire scene.

Temple personnel recorded the positions of every body, every lightsaber, every damaged section of the office, and every mark left by the battle.

Obi-Wan examined the wounds.

"They match lightsaber injuries."

Plo Koon nodded.

"The Senate will require a complete record."

Shaak Ti said,

"Nothing here is to be altered until every piece of evidence has been preserved."

Only after the documentation was complete did the Jedi begin recovering the fallen.

Master Windu's body was lifted with the utmost care.

Then Kit Fisto's.

Then Saesee Tiin's.

Then Agen Kolar's.

Palpatine's body remained where it lay.

No Jedi touched it.

Together, they bore their fallen comrades back to the Temple.

Soon afterward, the Jedi Order gathered in solemn remembrance.

Jedi from every rank filled the great hall.

No speeches attempted to soften the loss.

The names of the fallen Masters were spoken with honor.

"Mace Windu."

"Kit Fisto."

"Saesee Tiin."

"Agen Kolar."

Their lives of service to the Republic and the Jedi Order were remembered by those who had fought beside them through years of war.

When the funeral concluded, the surviving members of the Jedi Council addressed the assembled Order.

Shaak Ti spoke first.

"Our Council has suffered grievous losses."

Yoda continued.

"Four seats empty now are."

Ki-Adi-Mundi looked across the gathered Jedi.

"In time, those vacancies will be filled."

Obi-Wan added,

"But there are more pressing matters before us."

"The Republic has lost its Chancellor."

"The Jedi Order has lost four Council Masters."

"The war continues."

Yoda gave a slow nod.

"Much to do, there is."

With that, the assembly dispersed, and the surviving Council turned its attention to the uncertain days ahead.

# Part 3

After the funeral, the weight of the day finally began to settle upon Anakin Skywalker.

He left the Jedi Temple and made his way to Padmé Amidala's apartment.

When the door opened, Padmé rushed toward him.

"Anakin!"

She embraced him tightly.

"I heard there was some kind of emergency at the Temple. What happened?"

Anakin held her for a long moment before answering.

"So much has happened."

They sat together, and Anakin recounted everything from the beginning.

He told her how Chancellor Palpatine had revealed himself as a Sith Lord.

How he had immediately reported the truth to Mace Windu.

How Windu, Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar had confronted the Chancellor.

How he had disobeyed orders and gone to the Chancellor's office himself.

He described finding all four Masters dead.

He described the duel.

And finally, he described the Council meeting.

"...then Master Yoda said I was to be made a Jedi Master."

Padmé smiled despite everything.

"You finally received the rank."

Anakin nodded faintly.

"I wish it had happened under different circumstances."

He continued describing the discussion afterward.

"I told them Palpatine begged for his life."

"What did he say?" Padmé asked.

Anakin answered without thinking.

"He claimed only he possessed knowledge that could save you."

"Did you tell that to the Council?"

"Yes."

Padmé blinked.

"...Anakin."

"What?"

"Did you really say, 'He claimed only he possessed knowledge that could save Padmé,' in front of the Council?"

"Yes."

Padmé stared at him.

"You can't say that!! Now they will wonder why you would care about saving me."

Anakin's expression froze.

Then he sighed deeply.

"...Right."

He rubbed his forehead.

"I forgot."

Padmé leaned back.

"Oh, we are in trouble now."

She looked at him with growing concern.

"They will definitely be suspicious."

The following day, the surviving members of the Jedi Council assembled once again.

Anakin was invited into the Council Chamber.

Yoda regarded him quietly.

"Questions remain, Skywalker."

Anakin nodded.

"I expected as much."

Obi-Wan spoke first.

"Yesterday, you told us that Darth Sidious attempted to persuade you to spare him."

"Yes."

"He said only he possessed knowledge that could save Senator Amidala."

"Correct."

Obi-Wan folded his hands.

"There is something we do not understand."

Ki-Adi-Mundi continued.

"Why would the Sith Lord believe that promise would influence you?"

Stass Allie added,

"Why would saving Senator Amidala matter to you personally?"

Plo Koon tilted his head.

"And why, for that matter, would Senator Amidala require saving at all?"

The chamber fell silent.

Anakin remained standing.

"I..."

He hesitated.

"I simply..."

He stopped.

The Masters waited.

Nobody interrupted.

Nobody looked away.

Anakin realized that the entire purpose of this meeting was to understand precisely what Palpatine had been exploiting.

He sighed.

"You're not going to stop until I tell you everything."

He looked around the chamber.

"There are things I have kept secret."

Obi-Wan nodded gently.

"We are listening."

Anakin took a deep breath.

"I am married."

The room became perfectly still.

"To Padmé Amidala."

Nobody spoke.

Anakin continued before anyone else could.

"We married in secret after the Battle of Geonosis."

"I concealed it from the Order."

"I know the Code forbids attachment."

He lowered his head.

"I chose her anyway."

After several moments, he continued.

"Some time ago, I began having visions through the Force."

"What kind of visions?" asked Yoda.

"I saw Padmé dying."

His voice grew strained.

"She was giving birth."

"I couldn't save her."

"I saw it again."

"And again."

"I became convinced it was going to happen."

Obi-Wan's face softened.

"So that is why."

Anakin nodded.

"Palpatine noticed something was troubling me."

"He asked questions."

"I eventually admitted I feared losing someone."

"He told me a story."

"The tragedy of Darth Plagueis."

The Council listened carefully.

Anakin repeated the tale exactly as Palpatine had told it.

"He claimed Darth Plagueis had discovered knowledge capable of preventing those he cared about from dying."

"He said Plagueis was eventually killed by his own apprentice."

Anakin paused.

"Palpatine implied that this knowledge could still be learned."

"He said together we could discover it."

"That was why he believed offering to save Padmé would persuade me."

Silence filled the Council Chamber.

At length, Obi-Wan spoke.

"I wondered for some time why Palpatine took such an unusual interest in you."

Anakin looked at him.

"I understand now."

Ki-Adi-Mundi folded his hands.

"The Sith Lord sought to exploit your fear."

"He did."

Shaak Ti nodded.

"But that same fear led you to discover his identity."

Plo Koon continued the thought.

"Without your relationship with Senator Amidala..."

He glanced around the chamber.

"...Palpatine might never have revealed himself."

Obi-Wan looked toward the other Masters.

"It was precisely because Anakin feared losing Padmé that Palpatine believed he had found the perfect opportunity to recruit him."

"He exposed himself."

"And once he did, Anakin informed Master Windu immediately."

Yoda closed his eyes.

"Destroyed, the Sith Lord was."

He opened them again.

"Perhaps the last Sith, he was."

Coleman Kcaj spoke quietly.

"If that proves true..."

"...then Anakin has accomplished something without precedent."

Stass Allie nodded.

"The possibility cannot be ignored."

The Council entered a lengthy private discussion.

Anakin waited silently.

Eventually, Yoda spoke for them all.

"Broken the Code, you have."

Anakin lowered his eyes.

"I know."

"But hidden nothing now."

"No," Anakin answered.

"You came before us willingly."

"I did."

Obi-Wan stood.

"There is no question that the rules were violated."

He paused.

"There is also no question that your relationship with Senator Amidala became the very circumstance that led Darth Sidious to reveal himself."

"Had he never attempted to exploit that relationship..."

"...he might never have exposed his identity."

Ki-Adi-Mundi nodded.

"The Republic itself may have been spared a catastrophe because of what followed."

Yoda looked directly at Anakin.

"Exceptional these circumstances are."

"Expel you from the Order, we will not."

Anakin looked up in surprise.

Shaak Ti smiled faintly.

"The Council has reached a unanimous decision."

Obi-Wan continued.

"An exemption will be granted."

"You may remain both a Jedi Master and Senator Amidala's husband."

For several seconds Anakin could not find any words.

Finally he bowed deeply.

"...Thank you."

After the meeting had nearly concluded, Anakin hesitated.

"There is one request."

Yoda nodded.

"Speak."

"I would like unrestricted access to the Jedi Archives."

"What seek you there?" asked Plo Koon.

"Anything."

He did not hide the desperation in his voice.

"Any knowledge that might help save Padmé."

The Council exchanged glances.

Finally Obi-Wan answered.

"You may search."

"If such knowledge exists within the Archives, you have our permission to seek it."

Over the following weeks, Anakin spent countless hours inside the Jedi Archives.

He searched ancient medical texts.

Force treatises.

Historical records.

Accounts of miraculous healings.

Legends.

Holocrons.

Anything that mentioned preserving life.

The librarians assisted him whenever they could.

Nothing answered the question he sought.

Each failure only drove him deeper into the search.

His concern had not lessened after Palpatine's death.

If anything, it had grown stronger.

Palpatine had claimed to possess the knowledge Anakin needed.

Whether that claim had been true or false no longer mattered.

Palpatine was dead.

If he had truly known such a secret, it had died with him.

Again and again Anakin returned to the Archives.

Obi-Wan eventually found him reading another stack of ancient texts.

"You've been here for hours."

"I know."

"You were here yesterday."

"I know."

"And the day before."

Anakin did not look up.

"I have to keep searching."

Obi-Wan placed a hand on the table.

"You have searched nearly everything relevant in the Archives."

"There has to be something."

"Anakin."

"There has to be."

Another day, Yoda found him in much the same state.

"Still searching?"

"Yes."

"Found anything?"

"No."

Yoda studied him.

"Consumed by this search, you are becoming."

"I can't stop."

"You must."

"I can't."

Yoda exchanged a glance with Obi-Wan, who had arrived only moments earlier.

Neither Master believed more arguments would change Anakin's mind.

Later that day, Obi-Wan and Yoda visited Senator Amidala.

Padmé welcomed them inside.

She looked from one to the other.

"Masters."

Obi-Wan smiled politely.

"We've come to ask a favor."

That evening, Anakin returned home.

Padmé was waiting for him.

"You spent the entire day in the Jedi Archives again."

Anakin looked away.

"...Yes."

"You've barely done anything else lately."

"I'm looking for answers."

"I know."

She sat beside him.

"But you're becoming obsessed."

"I can't just do nothing."

Padmé gently took his hand.

"Listen to me."

He met her eyes.

"I know you're afraid."

"So am I."

She smiled softly.

"But Palpatine is already dead."

Anakin remained silent.

"Whatever he knew..."

"...whether he truly knew anything at all..."

"...it cannot be changed now."

She squeezed his hand.

"You did the right thing."

"I know."

"You saved the Republic."

"I know."

"You cannot undo that choice."

He closed his eyes.

"No."

"So stop torturing yourself."

She rested her forehead against his.

"Trust me."

"I will survive the childbirth."

Anakin's voice was barely above a whisper.

"You don't know that."

"No."

She smiled gently.

"Neither do you."

He gave a weary laugh.

"I suppose not."

"Let nature play itself out."

"We'll face whatever comes together."

For a long time, neither of them spoke.

Finally Anakin nodded.

"...All right."

"You'll stop spending every spare moment in the Archives?"

He hesitated.

Then nodded again.

"I'll stop."

It was not an easy promise to make.

But because Padmé had asked it of him, he intended to keep it.

# Part 4

The death of Supreme Chancellor Sheev Palpatine left the Republic without its head of government.

Within days, the Galactic Senate convened in emergency session.

The vast Senate Rotunda filled with representatives from thousands of systems.

The first matter before the Senate was the election of a new Supreme Chancellor.

Numerous names were proposed.

After debate and several rounds of voting, Senator Bail Organa of Alderaan secured the support of a clear majority.

As he stepped to the Chancellor's podium, the chamber grew quiet.

"I accept the responsibility you have placed in me."

He looked around the immense assembly.

"Our Republic has endured war, uncertainty, and profound loss."

"My first duty will be to restore confidence in our institutions and work toward a lasting peace."

The Senate applauded.

Once the new Chancellor had formally taken office, the chamber turned to another issue that demanded resolution.

The death of the previous Chancellor.

The Jedi Order submitted the evidence it had preserved from Palpatine's office.

Detailed recordings of the scene were presented before the Senate.

The damaged office.

The positions of the bodies.

The lightsaber wounds suffered by Mace Windu, Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar.

The injuries sustained by Palpatine himself.

Medical examinations.

Physical evidence recovered from the room.

Everything the Jedi had documented before removing their fallen Masters.

At Chancellor Organa's request, Anakin Skywalker was called to testify.

The seven other members of the Jedi Council chose to stand beside him.

Yoda.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Shaak Ti.

Plo Koon.

Ki-Adi-Mundi.

Stass Allie.

Coleman Kcaj.

Together they faced the Senate.

Bail Organa addressed Anakin first.

"Master Skywalker, please describe the events in your own words."

Anakin nodded.

"I discovered that Chancellor Palpatine was a Sith Lord and immediately informed Master Mace Windu."

"He assembled Masters Kit Fisto, Saesee Tiin, and Agen Kolar to confront the Chancellor."

"They instructed me to remain at the Jedi Temple."

"I disobeyed those instructions and went to the Chancellor's office."

"What did you find?"

"The four Masters had already been killed."

A murmur spread through the Senate.

"Palpatine attacked me."

"We dueled."

"I ultimately defeated him."

"I killed him."

One senator stood.

"Did Chancellor Palpatine surrender?"

Anakin answered honestly.

"After I disarmed him, he attempted to persuade me to spare his life."

"What happened then?"

"I refused."

"Why?"

Anakin looked around the chamber.

"Because he had already murdered four Jedi Masters."

"He remained an immediate threat."

"I believed allowing him to live would endanger others."

Another senator addressed the Council.

"Masters, do you dispute any part of Master Skywalker's testimony?"

Obi-Wan answered.

"No."

Plo Koon continued.

"The evidence collected from the office is consistent with his account."

Ki-Adi-Mundi gestured toward the recordings.

"The four Masters died before Master Skywalker arrived."

Shaak Ti added,

"The investigation found no evidence contradicting that conclusion."

Yoda spoke last.

"Faced a Sith Lord responsible for four deaths, Skywalker did."

"Stopped him, he did."

The Senate questioned witnesses for hours.

Jedi investigators.

Temple healers.

Forensic specialists.

Clone personnel who had secured the Senate District after the incident.

Every account supported the same sequence of events.

Eventually Bail Organa rose.

"The Senate will now decide whether Master Anakin Skywalker bears criminal responsibility for the death of former Chancellor Palpatine."

The senators cast their votes.

The results appeared across the great displays surrounding the chamber.

A clear majority had voted that Anakin's actions were justified under the circumstances presented.

Bail Organa announced the decision.

"By vote of the Galactic Senate, Master Anakin Skywalker is released without punishment."

The matter was formally concluded.

With that question settled, the Jedi Order turned its attention to rebuilding its leadership.

The Council Chamber still held four empty seats.

Candidates were discussed over several meetings.

Experienced Masters from across the Order were considered.

Eventually a consensus emerged.

Two former Council members agreed to return to active service.

Master Depa Billaba accepted the Council's invitation.

Master Oppo Rancisis likewise agreed to resume his place among the Order's leadership.

The remaining two seats were offered to distinguished Jedi who had never before served on the Council.

Master Luminara Unduli accepted.

Master Aayla Secura accepted as well.

At the next full meeting, all twelve seats were once again occupied.

Yoda looked around the restored Council.

"Our Order begins to heal."

The Council now consisted of:

Yoda.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Anakin Skywalker.

Coleman Kcaj.

Plo Koon.

Ki-Adi-Mundi.

Stass Allie.

Shaak Ti.

Depa Billaba.

Oppo Rancisis.

Luminara Unduli.

Aayla Secura.

Much remained uncertain throughout the galaxy, but the Jedi Order once again possessed a full Council to guide it.

At nearly the same time, Chancellor Bail Organa focused his government on ending the Clone Wars.

Count Dooku was dead.

General Grievous was dead.

The Confederacy of Independent Systems had lost both of its principal military leaders.

Across Separatist space, many worlds had little appetite for continuing the conflict.

Delegations from the Republic and the Confederacy met repeatedly under heavy security.

The negotiations proved difficult, but not impossible.

Bail Organa addressed the Republic delegation.

"We have spent years fighting."

"If peace is now possible, we must pursue it sincerely."

Representatives of the Confederacy reached similar conclusions.

One delegate said plainly,

"Our people are exhausted."

Another added,

"We sought independence."

"We did not seek endless war."

Weeks of negotiation gradually produced an agreement.

The Republic would formally recognize the Confederacy of Independent Systems as a sovereign state.

Its member worlds would not be compelled to rejoin the Republic.

In return, the Confederacy agreed to cease all military operations against Republic territory immediately.

The treaty further required a substantial reduction of Separatist droid foundries to prevent another massive military buildup.

Representatives of both governments signed the treaty.

After years of conflict, the Clone Wars finally came to an end.

Coruscant celebrated.

So did countless worlds on both sides of the former front lines.

For the first time in years, soldiers began returning home.

Jedi who had spent nearly their entire adult lives commanding armies found themselves once more serving as guardians of peace rather than generals.

Despite these historic events, Anakin's attention remained fixed elsewhere.

Every passing day brought Padmé closer to childbirth.

Although he had stopped obsessively searching the Jedi Archives, the old fear had never vanished completely.

He smiled for Padmé's sake.

He carried out his duties.

But every evening he silently wondered whether the visions had truly been escaped.

Then one evening, Padmé smiled at him from across their apartment.

"I think it's time."

Anakin was beside her instantly.

"The babies?"

She nodded.

"They're coming."

Medical personnel escorted Padmé to the Republic medical center.

Hours passed.

Anakin remained beside her the entire time.

Padmé squeezed his hand through each contraction.

"You look more nervous than I am," she managed with a tired smile.

"I'm trying not to be."

"You aren't succeeding."

He laughed despite himself.

"No."

The delivery continued.

Doctors and medical droids worked calmly.

Everything proceeded normally.

Then the first cry echoed through the room.

A newborn child.

One of the doctors smiled.

"A healthy boy."

Anakin looked down at the infant in amazement.

Before he had fully gathered himself, another cry followed.

The second child had arrived.

"A healthy girl."

Padmé looked at both children with tears in her eyes.

"They're beautiful."

Anakin could scarcely speak.

He looked immediately toward Padmé.

"How are you?"

She smiled weakly.

"I'm here."

The physicians completed their examinations.

One finally approached the new parents.

"Both children are healthy."

He smiled warmly.

"And Senator Amidala is expected to make a full recovery."

For several seconds Anakin simply stood there.

Then he closed his eyes.

A long breath escaped him.

Months of fear seemed to leave him all at once.

Padmé noticed.

She reached for his hand.

"I told you."

He laughed, tears forming in his eyes.

"You did."

"You trusted me."

"I should have done so sooner."

He leaned forward and kissed her gently.

"I'm just glad you're here."

"So am I."

After recovering from the birth, Padmé returned home with the twins.

There was no longer any reason to conceal their family.

The Jedi Council had granted Anakin an exemption to remain married.

The marriage was no longer a secret.

Anakin and Padmé openly shared their Coruscant apartment.

Friends visited freely.

Obi-Wan stopped by one afternoon carrying a small gift.

"So," he said with an amused smile, "this is what all the secrecy was about."

Anakin laughed.

"It turns out I'm not very good at keeping secrets."

"No," Obi-Wan replied, smiling at the sleeping twins, "you never were."

Life settled into a new rhythm.

Anakin balanced his responsibilities as a Jedi Master with the joys and challenges of fatherhood.

Padmé devoted herself both to her family and to her work in the Senate.

Their apartment, once quiet, was now filled with the sounds of two newborn children.

For the first time in many months, Anakin found himself able to look toward the future without the shadow of fear hanging over every thought.

Padmé was alive.

Their children were healthy.

The war had ended.

And together, they began building the peaceful life they had fought so hard to reach.
