# Part 1

The battle above Bothawui raged across the darkness of space as General Grievous drove the Separatist fleet toward the planet. Republic starfighters darted between torrents of turbolaser fire while Jedi General Anakin Skywalker led a daring counterattack that caught the droid fleet off guard. Forced into retreat, the Separatists broke formation and fled into hyperspace.

The victory came at a cost.

Anakin's Delta-7 starfighter shuddered violently as enemy fire struck its starboard side.

"Artoo!" he shouted over the shrieking alarms.

The little astromech gave a rapid burst of worried whistles from the socket behind the cockpit.

Another explosion rocked the fighter.

The damaged droid's clamps gave way.

"No!"

R2-D2 disappeared into space, tumbling helplessly away as Anakin's crippled fighter spun out of control.

Obi-Wan Kenobi's voice crackled through the comm.

"Anakin, break off pursuit! Your ship is coming apart!"

"I can't leave him!"

"You'll have no choice if you want to survive."

Grinding his teeth, Anakin finally turned his damaged fighter away. By the time rescue craft reached the area, R2-D2 was gone.

Days later, inside the Jedi Temple, Anakin stood stiffly before the Jedi Council while a gold-plated astromech rolled into the chamber.

Master Yoda rested both hands on his cane.

"A replacement, this is. R3-S6."

Anakin barely looked at the droid.

"With respect, Master, I don't want a replacement."

"You require an astromech," Mace Windu replied evenly.

"I require *my* astromech."

Ki-Adi-Mundi folded his hands into his sleeves.

"Your concern is understandable. However, the greater issue is the Republic military information contained within R2-D2's memory banks."

Anakin nodded immediately.

"Exactly. If the Separatists find him—"

"Then recovering him becomes a military necessity," Obi-Wan finished.

Anakin looked toward the golden droid.

"I'll use him until we find Artoo."

R3-S6 gave an innocent electronic chirp.

None of the Jedi suspected that the astromech had already been placed among Republic forces by Separatist agents.

Far from Coruscant, the Trandoshan scavenger Gha Nachkt had already retrieved R2-D2 from space.

The blue-and-white astromech struggled constantly against magnetic restraints as the Trandoshan laughed.

"You'll fetch quite a price."

R2 emitted an angry stream of whistles.

"Oh, I know exactly who wants you."

Weeks later, Anakin finally tracked Gha Nachkt's ship.

The Trandoshan spread his hands innocently.

"The droid? Sorry. Already gone."

Anakin stared at him.

"Gone where?"

"Sold."

"To whom?"

"No idea."

It was a lie.

After the Jedi departed, Gha Nachkt smiled smugly and transmitted fresh coordinates to General Grievous.

The exchange took place on a hidden Separatist listening post built into one of Ruusan's moons.

Grievous strode forward, towering over the Trandoshan.

"You'll receive the droid," Gha Nachkt spoke, gesturing toward R2's restraints. "The astromech's intact. Just like you asked."

The cyborg general leaned down until his yellow eyes were level with the prisoner.

"This insignificant machine has caused considerable inconvenience."

R2 responded with an indignant whistle.

Grievous laughed.

"Soon your secrets will belong to me."

Back aboard the Resolute, R2 managed to secretly transmit the listening post's location before Separatist technicians isolated his communications.

The signal reached the Republic fleet.

Anakin immediately turned toward Obi-Wan.

"We have his location."

"We should assemble a larger task force," Obi-Wan answered. "If Grievous is there, we cannot afford to underestimate him."

"We don't have time."

"The station isn't going anywhere."

"The Separatists won't wait forever before extracting Artoo's memory."

Obi-Wan sighed.

"I strongly advise patience."

Anakin was already walking toward the hangar.

"I'm going."

Obi-Wan closed his eyes for a moment before following.

"If you insist on making this difficult..."

The infiltration began successfully.

Clone troopers secured corridors while Ahsoka Tano and demolition teams moved through the station planting explosives.

Anakin hurried ahead alone.

"I've found the detention section," he whispered into his comlink.

"Wait for us," Obi-Wan replied immediately.

"No."

He cut the transmission.

Inside the detention chamber, Anakin ignited his lightsaber.

"Artoo!"

The little astromech whistled excitedly.

Before Anakin could reach him, blast doors slammed shut throughout the complex.

Red emergency lights began flashing.

R3-S6 rolled away from the Republic forces and plugged directly into a Separatist terminal.

Its photoreceptor shifted color.

General Grievous' voice echoed through the station.

"Intruders detected."

Anakin stared toward the sealed blast doors.

"No..."

Battle droids flooded every corridor.

The rescue mission dissolved into a desperate fight for survival.

Obi-Wan cut through wave after wave of droids while Ahsoka struggled to regroup the clone troopers.

"Master!" she called over the comlink. "We're completely cut off!"

"We're pulling back!"

Anakin hacked futilely at the blast door imprisoning R2.

"Just hold on!"

Grievous himself appeared at the opposite end of the chamber, MagnaGuards surrounding him.

"So eager to reclaim your little machine."

Anakin rushed forward.

Their lightsabers met in a shower of sparks.

The duel lasted only moments before additional blast doors crashed between them.

Grievous retreated behind layers of reinforced security barriers.

The station shook as planted explosives detonated prematurely.

Smoke poured through the corridors.

Obi-Wan reached Anakin at last.

"We have to leave."

"I'm not leaving him!"

"The station is collapsing!"

Anakin looked through the transparent durasteel window separating him from R2.

The astromech let out a desperate series of beeps.

"I'm coming back," Anakin promised.

Another explosion ripped through the complex.

Obi-Wan seized Anakin's shoulder.

"Now!"

The Republic strike team barely escaped aboard their shuttle before sections of the listening post collapsed inward.

From the viewport, Anakin watched helplessly.

R2 remained inside.

Silence filled the cockpit.

No one spoke during the return flight.

Deep within the surviving core of the station, emergency systems stabilized the remaining sections.

Grievous emerged from the smoke.

The cyborg turned toward Gha Nachkt.

"You nearly allowed them to escape with the droid."

"They didn't."

"You nearly allowed it."

The Trandoshan frowned.

"My payment."

Grievous ignited one lightsaber.

"There will be no payment."

The blade pierced straight through Gha Nachkt's chest.

The scavenger collapsed without another word.

Grievous deactivated the weapon.

"Dispose of the body."

He looked down at the restrained astromech.

"Begin memory extraction immediately."

Separatist engineers moved R2 into a laboratory.

Instead of attempting a conventional data download, they carefully dismantled the astromech piece by piece, preserving every memory core intact.

Hours passed.

Banks of computers processed the recovered files.

Tactical maps.

Republic fleet deployments.

Encrypted communications.

Military supply routes.

The information alone justified the operation.

Then one technician stopped.

"General..."

Grievous approached.

"What is it?"

"We've found something... unexpected."

A recording appeared on the monitor.

A quiet lakeside retreat on Naboo.

Senator Padmé Amidala stood beneath a flowering archway dressed in white.

Facing her stood Anakin Skywalker in simple ceremonial robes.

The protocol droid C-3PO stood nearby.

R2-D2 watched silently from another angle, faithfully recording everything.

The technician replayed the audio.

"I, Anakin Skywalker, take you, Padmé Naberrie Amidala..."

Grievous watched without speaking.

The recording continued uninterrupted.

Vows.

Rings.

A kiss.

The entire wedding ceremony had been preserved perfectly within R2's memory.

One officer stared in disbelief.

"A Jedi..."

"...has secretly married," another finished.

Grievous' eyes narrowed.

"So."

He watched the recording again.

"So the Republic's celebrated young Jedi cannot even obey his own Order."

A tactical droid tilted its head.

"The military intelligence remains valuable."

Grievous gave a cold laugh.

"This is worth far more."

"The Republic could deny any accusation."

"They cannot deny a recording."

He turned toward the communications officer.

"Prepare every available transmission array."

"The military files?"

"Keep them classified."

"And the recording?"

Grievous smiled.

"Broadcast it."

Within hours, Separatist transmitters flooded every major HoloNet frequency they could penetrate.

Across the Republic, civilians, soldiers, senators, and Jedi alike watched the same unmistakable recording.

There was no editing.

No narration.

No fabrication.

Only R2-D2's unwavering recording of Anakin Skywalker and Padmé Amidala exchanging marriage vows before the witnesses C-3PO and R2-D2.

Inside the Jedi Temple, silence filled the Council Chamber as the recording ended.

No one spoke for several moments.

Obi-Wan finally closed his eyes.

"Oh, Anakin..."

The doors opened.

Anakin entered under summons.

He immediately recognized the frozen image still displayed above the Council.

His face drained of color.

Padmé.

The wedding.

Every moment.

Every word.

Recorded.

Broadcast to the galaxy.

He did not even attempt to deny it.

Master Windu looked directly at him.

"Is this recording authentic?"

Anakin lowered his head.

"...Yes."

Yoda's ears drooped.

"Hidden this from us, you have."

"I know."

"Why?" Plo Koon asked quietly.

Anakin looked from one master to another.

"Because I loved her."

No one challenged the answer.

No one needed to.

The evidence had already spoken.

Over the following days, the revelation spread across the Republic. Newspapers, HoloNet commentators, and senators debated endlessly. The Separatists had not merely exposed classified military intelligence. They had exposed one of the Republic's own Jedi breaking the very code he had sworn to uphold.

The Jedi Council met repeatedly behind closed doors.

When their deliberations ended, Anakin was summoned one final time.

He entered the chamber wearing his Jedi robes.

No one smiled.

Mace Windu spoke first.

"The Council has reached its decision."

Anakin stood silently.

"You knowingly violated the Jedi Code," Windu continued. "Not through a momentary lapse, but through deliberate deception carried out over many weeks."

Ki-Adi-Mundi added, "The matter is now public. Any response we make will shape how every Jedi understands the Code."

"If we permit this without consequence," Saesee Tiin said, "others will believe they may secretly marry and simply remain in the Order if discovered."

Obi-Wan remained silent.

He could not argue against the reasoning.

Yoda finally spoke.

"A choice, we offer."

Anakin lifted his eyes.

"You may remain within the Jedi Order."

Hope flickered briefly across his face.

Yoda continued.

"But ended, the marriage must be."

The hope vanished.

"Renounce your relationship with Senator Amidala. Seek a legal divorce. Accept disciplinary measures within the Order."

Mace Windu listed them calmly.

"You would be relieved of command over clone forces."

"Ahsoka Tano would no longer remain your Padawan."

"You would be suspended from active military service."

"Further measures would be taken to ensure you and Senator Amidala remain separated indefinitely."

Silence filled the chamber.

"And the second choice?" Anakin asked quietly.

"You may leave the Jedi Order."

The room remained still.

Obi-Wan finally found his voice.

"Anakin... think carefully."

"I have."

The answer came immediately.

"I won't abandon Padmé."

No one appeared surprised.

Yoda slowly nodded.

"Then leave the Order, you must."

Anakin unclipped his lightsaber from his belt and held it in both hands.

For a long moment he stared at it.

Then he extended it toward the Council.

Mace Windu looked at Yoda.

The Grand Master shook his head.

"Keep it."

Anakin looked up.

"A Jedi no longer are. But your lightsaber... earned, it was."

Obi-Wan stepped forward.

"I wish things had been different."

"So do I."

There was nothing more to say.

Anakin fastened the lightsaber back onto his belt.

Without another word, he turned and walked out of the Council Chamber.

The great doors closed behind him.

His place in the Jedi Order had come to an end. The marriage he had chosen to protect remained intact, but the life he had built since childhood was over.

# Part 2

The release of the wedding recording did not end with the wedding itself.

Separatist broadcasters soon began releasing more recordings recovered from R2-D2's memory cores. Unlike the wedding, these were carefully selected and edited together to achieve maximum propaganda value.

Across countless HoloNet frequencies, viewers watched a young slave boy on Tatooine staring at Queen Amidala.

"Are you an angel?"

The clip abruptly changed.

A teenage Anakin walking through Naboo beside Padmé.

"I don't like sand. It's coarse and rough and irritating... and it gets everywhere."

Another cut.

The two laughing together.

Another.

Anakin gazing awkwardly at Padmé.

Another.

Moments aboard her yacht.

Walks through Naboo's fields.

Small private conversations R2 had silently recorded while faithfully accompanying his mistress.

None of the footage had been fabricated.

None of it required narration.

The Separatists simply allowed the recordings to speak for themselves.

General Grievous watched one of the broadcasts from the bridge of his flagship.

"Excellent."

Count Dooku stood beside him.

"The military intelligence was valuable."

Grievous folded his mechanical hands behind his back.

"This has done far greater damage."

On Coruscant, every public holoscreen seemed to replay the recordings.

Citizens gathered around displays.

Some reacted with disbelief.

Others with amusement.

Within days, comedians throughout the capital were incorporating the recordings into performances.

Actors dramatically reenacted the now-famous exchange.

"Are you an angel?"

The audience erupted into laughter.

Elsewhere another performer exaggerated every word.

"I don't like sand!"

More laughter.

People began quoting the recordings in marketplaces, restaurants, government offices, and military barracks.

Children repeated the lines without understanding their origin.

Street performers imitated Anakin's awkward attempts to impress Padmé.

The recordings became impossible to escape.

Inside the apartment overlooking the Galactic Senate, Padmé switched off another holoprojector in silence.

She had not intended to watch it.

It had simply appeared as part of a news report.

Threepio shifted nervously.

"Oh dear... they're showing it again."

Padmé covered her face with one hand.

"I know."

The protocol droid hesitated.

"They... do seem to find it terribly amusing."

She gave a weary smile that lasted only a moment.

"I noticed."

Across the room, Anakin stood at the window, staring down at the endless city.

He said nothing.

Padmé walked toward him.

"They'll grow tired of it eventually."

He did not answer.

"They're laughing at us."

"They're laughing now," she corrected gently.

"They're laughing because the most private moments of our lives are suddenly public entertainment."

His hands tightened against the windowsill.

"They dismantled Artoo."

The words came quietly.

"They tore him apart."

Padmé closed her eyes.

"I know."

"He spent years serving you faithfully."

"I know."

"They destroyed him just to search his memory."

"I know."

His voice cracked.

"And it was my fault."

Padmé stepped beside him.

"No."

"If I hadn't insisted on bringing him everywhere..."

"They chose to do this."

Anakin lowered his head.

"I couldn't even save him."

The apartment fell silent.

Everywhere he looked, reminders surrounded him.

His astromech was gone forever.

The Separatists possessed every memory R2 had ever recorded.

The galaxy had witnessed his wedding.

The Jedi Order had cast him out.

His military command had been revoked.

His Padawan had been reassigned.

Even walking through Coruscant had become unbearable.

People stared.

Some whispered.

Some laughed openly.

Others quoted lines from the recordings as he passed.

Anakin stopped leaving the apartment unless absolutely necessary.

Padmé, meanwhile, attempted to continue her duties in the Senate.

She had served as Naboo's senator for 6 years.

Many senators respected her judgment.

Many remembered her years as queen.

She believed that reputation might allow her to weather the storm.

For a short time, it seemed possible.

Some senators privately assured her of their support.

"You've always acted in Naboo's interests," Bail Organa told her.

"This changes nothing about your work."

Padmé smiled gratefully.

"Thank you."

Mon Mothma nodded.

"The scandal will pass."

"I hope so."

But it did not.

Instead, debate surrounding her presence grew louder with each passing session.

Some senators argued that her private life had no bearing on her public service.

Others disagreed.

"She knowingly entered a marriage with a Jedi," one senator declared during committee discussions.

"She was fully aware that the Jedi Code forbids such attachments."

Another senator answered immediately.

"The question before us concerns legislation, not her marriage."

"It concerns judgment."

"It concerns her right to represent Naboo."

"It concerns whether the Separatists now possess leverage over members of this Senate."

Arguments that once centered on military appropriations and planetary aid increasingly became arguments about Padmé herself.

Conversations stalled whenever she entered a room.

Committees divided over unrelated issues simply because senators no longer wished to appear aligned with or opposed to her.

She watched legislation become delayed as debates drifted back toward her personal life.

One evening, Bail Organa found her alone in her office.

"You look exhausted."

"I am."

"You don't have to face this alone."

Padmé looked out the office window toward the Senate rotunda.

"They're spending more time arguing about me than the war."

"That isn't your fault."

"It doesn't matter whose fault it is."

She turned toward him.

"The Senate is supposed to function."

"It still is."

"No."

Her voice remained calm.

"It keeps stopping."

She sat quietly before continuing.

"I thought the respect I'd earned over these past 6 years would be enough."

"It still means something."

"It does."

She smiled sadly.

"But not enough."

Bail remained silent.

Padmé folded her hands.

"I've been asking myself one question."

He waited.

"If I disappeared tomorrow..."

She looked toward the Senate chamber.

"...would the Republic stop functioning?"

"No."

"It would continue."

She nodded.

"The Republic is bigger than one senator."

Several days later, the Senate chamber fell silent as Padmé approached the central podium.

She activated the microphone.

"My fellow senators."

Conversations ceased.

"I have spent many years serving Naboo to the best of my ability."

She paused.

"It has been the greatest honor of my life."

She looked around the chamber.

"In recent weeks, however, it has become increasingly clear that my continued presence has become a source of division."

Some senators attempted to interrupt.

She raised one hand.

"I ask that you allow me to finish."

Silence returned.

"The Republic faces a difficult war."

"It cannot afford unnecessary distractions."

"I have concluded that the Senate will continue to serve the Republic without me."

A murmur spread through the chamber.

"For that reason..."

She took a slow breath.

"...I hereby resign my position as Senator of Naboo."

Gasps echoed throughout the rotunda.

"I do so freely."

"I do so willingly."

"And I do so because I believe the stability of this institution matters more than any office I hold."

She bowed her head.

"Thank you."

Without another word, she stepped away from the podium.

Bail Organa watched silently as she left the chamber.

No one attempted to stop her.

Back at the apartment, Anakin read the official announcement in stunned silence.

"You resigned?"

Padmé nodded.

"It was the right thing."

"You shouldn't have had to."

"I know."

"They forced you out."

"No."

She shook her head.

"I made the decision."

"You loved serving Naboo."

"I still do."

She stepped closer.

"But the Senate doesn't need me as much as..."

She reached for his hand.

"...you do."

He looked away.

"I'm fine."

"No, you're not."

He tried to answer.

Nothing came.

She guided him to sit.

For several minutes neither of them spoke.

Finally she broke the silence.

"What are you thinking?"

He stared at the floor.

"I don't know anymore."

"You don't know what?"

"Anything."

His voice sounded hollow.

"I spent my whole life training to be a Jedi."

He laughed bitterly.

"That's all I know."

"You know much more than that."

"I don't."

He looked at his hands.

"When something was wrong..."

He searched for the words.

"...the answer was always the same."

"Control yourself."

"Push it down."

"Don't feel it."

Padmé listened quietly.

"I don't know how to do anything else."

He closed his eyes.

"I lost Artoo."

"My life became public entertainment."

"I lost the Order."

"I lost the army."

"I lost Obi-Wan."

"I lost Ahsoka."

He swallowed.

"I don't know who I am anymore."

Padmé took both his hands.

"You are my husband."

He remained silent.

"And right now..."

She squeezed his hands gently.

"...you're hurting."

For the first time since leaving the Jedi Order, Anakin allowed himself to cry.

Days passed.

His condition improved little.

He rarely slept.

He barely ate.

Sometimes he spent hours simply sitting without speaking.

Whenever they ventured into public, whispers followed them.

Occasionally someone would loudly repeat one of the now-famous recordings.

"I don't like sand!"

Laughter would follow.

Anakin would immediately turn away.

One afternoon, Padmé watched him silently return home after another encounter like that.

He did not remove his cloak.

He simply sat down and stared into nothing.

She made her decision.

That evening she approached him.

"We're leaving Coruscant."

He looked up slowly.

"What?"

"We're going home."

"Naboo?"

She nodded.

"No Senate."

"No Temple."

"No crowds."

"No holocameras."

"No comedians."

"Just peace."

He remained quiet.

"And Threepio is coming with us."

The golden protocol droid looked up in surprise.

"Oh!"

Padmé smiled.

"You've been with us through all of this."

Threepio bowed.

"I would be honored, milady."

Several days later, a small civilian transport departed Coruscant.

There were no ceremonies.

No reporters.

No public farewells.

Only Anakin, Padmé, C-3PO, and the few belongings they chose to take with them.

When Naboo finally appeared beyond the viewport, Anakin simply watched in silence.

Padmé reached over and rested her hand upon his.

"We're almost there."

The transport landed quietly.

Rather than returning to Theed, Padmé led them into Naboo's peaceful countryside.

The rolling green hills gradually replaced cities.

The noise of the galaxy faded behind them.

Eventually, Varykino Villa came into view beside the lake.

Threepio looked around in admiration.

"What a beautiful residence."

Padmé smiled softly.

"It'll be quiet here."

She looked toward Anakin.

"There won't be people staring."

"No one will care about recordings."

"No one will be waiting around every corner to remind us."

The breeze carried only birdsong across the water.

For the first time in many weeks, Anakin heard no laughter.

Only silence.

Padmé looked out across the lake.

"This is where we begin again."

# Part 3

The first weeks at Varykino Villa were quiet.

The silence that had been impossible to find on Coruscant surrounded them every morning. Gentle waves rolled across the lake. Wind drifted through the trees. There were no reporters, no senators, no Jedi, and no strangers repeating recordings that had become the galaxy's newest joke.

Yet peace outside the villa did not bring peace within it.

Anakin spent long hours repairing old machinery around the estate simply to keep his hands occupied.

Padmé often sat on the veranda overlooking the water with reports from Naboo, unable to stop herself from following the affairs of a Senate she no longer belonged to.

At first they tried to comfort one another.

Over time, that became more difficult.

One afternoon, Padmé looked up from another HoloNet report and sighed.

"They're debating the agricultural relief bill today."

Anakin continued tightening a power coupling without looking at her.

"You don't have to watch it."

"I know."

"You resigned."

"I remember."

The words carried more sharpness than either of them intended.

Another evening, Anakin accidentally knocked over a tool case while working in the workshop.

The metal clatter echoed through the room.

Padmé entered.

"Everything alright?"

He nodded without meeting her eyes.

"I'm fine."

"You don't sound fine."

"I said I'm fine."

She stood quietly for a moment before leaving.

Neither apologized.

Small conversations increasingly ended in silence.

Meals became quieter.

Threepio quickly learned to recognize the tension.

"Oh dear," he muttered to himself while clearing the table one evening. "I do hope this unpleasant atmosphere passes soon."

It did not.

The resentments were subtle.

Neither truly wished to blame the other.

Yet painful thoughts appeared anyway.

One morning, while watching another transport leave Theed in the distance, Padmé found herself thinking:

*If I had never met Anakin... I would still be serving Naboo.*

The thought horrified her.

She immediately pushed it away.

But it returned again days later.

Across the villa, Anakin struggled with thoughts of his own.

He looked at the lightsaber still hanging from his belt.

The weapon he still possessed.

The life that had disappeared.

*If I had never met Padmé...*

He closed his eyes.

*...I would still be a Jedi.*

He hated himself for thinking it.

But the thought lingered.

Neither spoke these feelings aloud.

Instead they slowly became colder toward each other without fully understanding why.

Several weeks later, visitors arrived.

A familiar landspeeder rolled toward the villa.

Padmé's face brightened for the first time in days.

"My family."

Ruwee Naberrie climbed out first, smiling warmly.

"There you are."

Jobal embraced her daughter tightly.

"We've been worried."

Sola followed with Ryoo and Pooja, who immediately ran toward the lakeshore.

"Look!" Ryoo shouted.

"The water's beautiful!"

Pooja pointed toward the gardens.

"Can we explore?"

Padmé laughed softly.

"Of course."

Anakin stepped forward.

"It's good to see all of you."

Ruwee offered his hand.

"It's good to see you too."

Jobal embraced him before he could react.

"You've lost enough people recently."

She smiled gently.

"We're family."

Anakin returned the embrace awkwardly.

"...Thank you."

Over the next day, the villa became livelier than it had been since Anakin and Padmé arrived.

The children filled the quiet halls with laughter.

Threepio happily answered endless questions.

"Oh yes, Master Anakin was quite an exceptional podracer when he was only a boy."

"Really?" Ryoo asked excitedly.

"Oh my, yes."

Even Anakin managed a small smile.

Still, the older members of the family noticed something.

Whenever Anakin and Padmé disagreed, the warmth between them vanished almost instantly.

The conversations became clipped.

Polite.

Distant.

That evening, after the children had gone to bed, Ruwee spoke quietly.

"I'd like to ask both of you something."

Padmé looked up.

"What is it?"

"You don't seem happy."

"We're just tired."

Ruwee calmly shook his head.

"No."

Jobal nodded.

"Something's wrong."

Padmé forced a smile.

"Everything's alright."

"It isn't," Jobal answered gently.

Sola leaned forward.

"You don't have to hide it from us."

Silence followed.

Anakin looked down at the table.

Ruwee waited patiently.

Finally he asked,

"What are you both angry about?"

No one answered.

Minutes passed.

Then Padmé quietly spoke.

"I resent him."

Anakin looked up.

She immediately looked ashamed.

"I didn't want to admit that."

Jobal reached across the table.

"Why?"

Padmé swallowed.

"I keep thinking..."

She closed her eyes.

"...that if I had never met Anakin..."

Her voice became almost a whisper.

"...I'd still be a senator."

Silence filled the room.

Anakin stared at the table.

Ruwee slowly turned toward him.

"And you?"

Anakin took a long breath.

"I've been thinking..."

He struggled to say the words.

"...if I'd never met Padmé..."

He looked at the lightsaber resting nearby.

"...I'd still be a Jedi."

The admission hung heavily over the table.

Neither looked at the other.

Jobal quietly asked,

"Do either of you truly believe the other wanted this?"

"No," Padmé answered immediately.

"No," Anakin agreed just as quickly.

Sola folded her hands.

"Did Padmé make the Separatists dismantle Artoo?"

"No."

"Did Anakin order Grievous to broadcast those recordings?"

"No."

Ruwee continued.

"Did either of you ask the Jedi Council to separate you?"

"No."

"Did either of you force the Senate into turmoil?"

Padmé shook her head.

"No."

Ruwee looked from one to the other.

"So why are you blaming each other?"

Neither answered.

Jobal spoke softly.

"When people suffer together, it's easy to mistake the nearest person for the cause of the pain."

She reached for Padmé's hand.

"But that's rarely true."

Sola nodded.

"You both lost almost everything."

She looked toward Anakin.

"The Order."

"The army."

"Your droid."

She turned toward Padmé.

"The Senate."

"Your public reputation."

"Your career."

She smiled sadly.

"Pain always wants someone to blame."

No military commanders.

No expectations.

His voice was barely audible.

"...Thank you."

Jobal embraced him again.

"You don't ever have to thank family for acting like family."

After the Naberries returned to Theed, life at Varykino Villa slowly settled into a healthier rhythm.

The bitterness faded.

Arguments became discussions again.

Meals became conversations instead of silence.

Anakin slept through the night more often.

Padmé smiled more frequently.

Months passed.

The wounds left by Coruscant did not disappear, but they gradually stopped defining every day.

One evening, Anakin sat beside Padmé overlooking the lake.

"I've been thinking."

She smiled.

"That's usually dangerous."

"I mean about what comes next."

They accepted no employers.

They sought no rewards.

They pledged loyalty to no chain of command.

Padmé brought diplomacy, negotiation, political judgment, and years of experience resolving conflicts through words before violence.

Threepio offered translation, protocol, cultural knowledge, and communication wherever misunderstanding threatened to become conflict.

For the first time since leaving Coruscant, all three had found a purpose that belonged entirely to themselves.

# Part 4

The months that followed changed the galaxy more completely than anyone on Naboo could have imagined.

At Varykino Villa, Anakin, Padmé, and C-3PO devoted themselves to helping where they could, traveling only when they believed their own judgment and abilities could genuinely make a difference before quietly returning home.

Far away on Coruscant, Supreme Chancellor Palpatine received regular intelligence reports concerning Anakin Skywalker's activities.

One report was particularly brief.

"He has permanently settled on Naboo, Chancellor."

Palpatine dismissed the messenger and remained alone in his office.

"So."

He stared out over the city.

"You've chosen your own path."

For some time, the Chancellor considered whether Anakin could still be drawn back under his influence.

Each new report made that prospect appear increasingly remote.

Anakin accepted no military commission.

He sought no political office.

He answered to no authority.

He remained almost entirely beyond Palpatine's reach.

Eventually, the Chancellor reached a different conclusion.

"If Skywalker cannot be reclaimed..."

He folded his hands behind his back.

"...then my plans will proceed without him."

Count Dooku received the summons in secret.

Inside a heavily shielded chamber, he bowed.

"My master."

Palpatine remained seated.

"The time has come."

"For the Jedi?"

"For the Republic."

Dooku understood immediately.

"As you command."

Not long afterward, the Republic awoke to shocking news.

The Chancellor announced that the Jedi had attempted to betray both him and the Republic itself. Emergency broadcasts repeated the accusation throughout the galaxy, presenting carefully manufactured evidence and testimony designed to justify immediate action against the Jedi Order.

Clone commanders received sealed transmissions carrying a single instruction.

Order 66.

Across countless worlds, clone troopers turned their weapons upon the Jedi they had served beside throughout the Clone Wars.

Many Jedi never saw the attacks coming.

Others fought desperately before being overwhelmed.

Some escaped only through luck and quick thinking.

Within hours, reports of Jedi deaths spread from one end of the Republic to the other.

As chaos consumed the Order, another announcement stunned the galaxy.

Separatist command had agreed to an unconditional surrender.

Count Dooku and General Grievous publicly accepted the terms, declaring the Clone Wars at an end.

To the public, the conflict had concluded with an overwhelming Republic victory.

Citizens poured into the streets celebrating.

The war was over.

The Confederacy had surrendered.

Peace, many believed, had finally returned.

Few questioned how quickly former enemies now appeared beside the Chancellor.

Publicly, Count Dooku and General Grievous accepted new positions under the Republic government, answering directly to Palpatine as senior military officials responsible for maintaining security during the transition to peace.

Privately, nothing had changed between master and apprentice.

When the celebrations had barely subsided, Palpatine summoned both men.

"The Jedi Temple."

Grievous inclined his head.

"It still stands."

"Not for much longer."

Dooku asked calmly,

"The clones?"

"They will accompany you."

A legion of clone troopers assembled outside the Jedi Temple.

Marching beside them were General Grievous and Count Dooku.

The Temple's defenders, already devastated by the galaxy-wide executions carried out through Order 66, struggled desperately against the assault.

Lightsabers flashed through ancient halls.

Clone blaster fire echoed beneath towering ceilings.

Grievous carved through defenders with relentless precision.

Dooku advanced methodically beside him.

By nightfall, the Temple had fallen.

Smoke rose above Coruscant.

The Jedi Order no longer possessed a home.

Combined with the deaths already inflicted across countless worlds, the destruction of the Temple shattered the Jedi Order as an institution.

Those who survived scattered into hiding.

Not long afterward, Palpatine addressed the Senate.

"The Republic," he declared, "shall be reorganized into the first Galactic Empire."

Thunderous applause filled the chamber.

Palpatine accepted the title of Emperor.

Standing prominently beside him were Count Dooku and General Grievous, now introduced to the galaxy as trusted Imperial enforcers charged with preserving order in a new era.

Far from Coruscant, surviving Jedi struggled simply to remain alive.

Some hid alone.

Others disappeared into distant systems.

A small number, however, reached a different conclusion.

Hiding would not answer the questions that haunted them.

Several survivors secretly gathered in abandoned outposts and forgotten safehouses.

One Jedi placed a datapad onto the table.

"Something happened to the clones."

Another nodded.

"They turned on every Jedi at nearly the same moment."

"That cannot be coincidence."

They began collecting every fragment of evidence they could obtain.

Clone communications.

Military dispatches.

Eyewitness testimony.

Recovered transmissions.

Months became years.

Gradually a picture emerged.

One survivor pointed toward a sequence of intercepted orders.

"Everything traces back here."

He highlighted a single origin.

"Palpatine."

Silence filled the room.

Another Jedi folded his arms.

"If he orchestrated this..."

He looked around the table.

"...why?"

No one answered immediately.

Finally, an elderly Jedi spoke.

"There is one explanation."

The others looked toward him.

"A hatred this profound..."

He paused.

"...is difficult to explain unless he is something the Jedi have opposed for generations."

"A Sith Lord."

The room fell silent once more.

One by one, the others nodded.

It was the explanation that best fit everything they had uncovered.

Eventually they made a decision.

"We confront him."

The attempt ended in blood.

Deep within the Imperial Palace, the surviving Jedi cornered the Emperor himself.

For a brief moment, neither side moved.

Palpatine regarded them calmly.

"So."

"You finally arrived."

One Jedi stepped forward.

"You orchestrated the destruction of the Order."

Palpatine smiled faintly.

"You have no proof."

"You gave the order."

"I gave many orders."

"You are behind all of this."

Palpatine's expression hardened.

"I see."

The lead Jedi ignited his lightsaber.

"Your reign ends today."

At last, Palpatine stood.

"You leave me little choice."

A crimson blade burst from his own lightsaber.

Every Jedi in the room froze.

Red.

The unmistakable weapon of a Sith.

Palpatine no longer bothered to conceal what he was.

The duel erupted immediately.

The chamber became a storm of blue and green blades clashing against crimson.

Several Jedi fell.

Others fought desperately to create an opening.

Realizing victory was impossible, a handful broke away and escaped before Palpatine could finish them all.

For the survivors, the duel had answered every remaining question.

The Emperor himself had ignited a red lightsaber.

He had openly fought as a Sith Lord.

No speculation remained.

Only certainty.

The survivors disappeared once more into the shadows.

Through hidden contacts, encrypted messages, and trusted allies, they quietly spread what they now knew.

"The Emperor is a Sith Lord."

The warning passed from survivor to survivor.

Years later, few remaining Jedi doubted it.

Eventually, the message reached Naboo.

Anakin read the coded transmission several times before lowering it.

Padmé watched him carefully.

"What is it?"

He looked up slowly.

"The surviving Jedi finally know."

"They know what?"

He answered without hesitation.

"Palpatine is a Sith Lord."

Padmé remained silent for several moments.

Then she quietly asked,

"Are they certain?"

"They confronted him."

He looked back at the message.

"They saw him ignite a red lightsaber."

He folded the transmission.

"There's no doubt."

Life at Varykino continued.

The information remained known only to a handful of trusted people.

There was no reason to believe the Emperor suspected Anakin had learned the truth.

Several years after proclaiming the Empire, Palpatine finally traveled personally to Naboo.

His shuttle descended toward Varykino Villa beneath a calm afternoon sky.

Anakin watched it land from the veranda.

Padmé stood beside him.

"He came himself."

Anakin nodded.

"He must want something."

Palpatine emerged smiling warmly.

"My dear Anakin."

Anakin offered only a polite nod.

"Chancellor."

The title lingered deliberately.

Palpatine smiled.

"Those days are long behind us."

He looked around the peaceful estate.

"You've built quite a life."

"We have."

"I've been watching your work."

Anakin remained silent.

"You've helped countless people."

"We've tried."

"The Empire needs men like you."

Palpatine stepped closer.

"You possess abilities unlike anyone else."

He spread his hands.

"I can offer you a high-ranking position."

"There would be considerable authority."

"A generous salary."

"The opportunity to serve the entire galaxy."

Anakin looked at him without expression.

"And if I decline?"

"I believe that would be unfortunate."

Silence settled between them.

Then Anakin spoke.

"I have one question."

"Of course."

"How long have you been a Sith Lord?"

For the first time, Palpatine's smile disappeared.

His eyes narrowed.

"...What?"

In a single motion, Anakin ignited his blue lightsaber.

The blade flashed to life as he lunged forward.

Palpatine's eyes widened.

He had never imagined Anakin already knew.

The attack came a heartbeat sooner than expected.

Only instinct saved him.

A crimson blade ignited just in time.

The two lightsabers collided with an explosive crack.

Palpatine sprang backward.

"Who told you?"

Anakin answered with another attack.

"You murdered the Jedi."

Palpatine parried.

"So they survived."

Their blades spun through the gardens of Varykino.

Blue and crimson light reflected across the lake.

Palpatine unleashed blistering combinations born from decades of mastery.

Anakin answered each one with relentless determination.

Neither combatant gave ground willingly.

The duel carried them across stone walkways, through open courtyards, and into the villa's gardens.

Padmé watched from a safe distance with C-3PO.

"Oh my," Threepio whispered.

"I do hope Master Anakin prevails."

Palpatine drove forward.

"You could have ruled beside me!"

"I don't want your Empire!"

"You throw away greatness!"

"I already know what happens when I follow you."

Palpatine's expression darkened.

"You know nothing."

Their blades locked.

For several long seconds neither moved.

Then Anakin forced the crimson blade aside and pressed the attack.

Palpatine retreated step by step.

The Emperor had expected a recruit.

Instead he had found an opponent who had prepared himself for years without Palpatine ever realizing it.

That single surprise altered the entire battle.

Anakin feinted high.

Palpatine raised his blade.

The real strike came low.

The blue lightsaber cut cleanly through the Emperor's guard.

Palpatine staggered.

He attempted one final attack.

Anakin moved first.

The decisive blow landed.

Palpatine collapsed onto the stone path.

His crimson lightsaber slipped from lifeless fingers and rolled across the courtyard before deactivating.

Silence returned to Varykino Villa.

Only the wind disturbed the gardens.

Anakin slowly deactivated his own lightsaber.

Padmé approached cautiously.

"It's over."

He looked down at the fallen Emperor.

"Yes."

The Sith Lord who had engineered the destruction of the Jedi Order, transformed the Republic into an Empire, and ruled the galaxy through deception had been defeated on the peaceful world of Naboo, far from the throne he had fought so long to obtain.

# Part 5

News of Emperor Palpatine's death spread through the Empire with astonishing speed.

The official announcement described the event as a murder.

Imperial broadcasts showed images of Varykino Villa, identifying Anakin Skywalker as the man responsible for killing the Emperor.

Within hours, Coruscant transmitted a formal demand to Theed.

Queen Apailana received the Imperial delegation inside the Royal Palace.

The lead envoy placed a sealed document upon the table.

"By order of the Imperial government, Naboo is to immediately arrest Anakin Skywalker and surrender him to Coruscant for trial on charges relating to the murder of Emperor Palpatine."

Apailana calmly opened the document.

She read every page before looking back up.

"And if Naboo refuses?"

"The Empire will consider such refusal an act of rebellion."

The Queen remained silent for a moment.

Then she carefully closed the document.

"No."

The envoy blinked.

"I'm sorry?"

"Naboo will not surrender him."

"You understand the consequences?"

"I understand them perfectly."

"The Empire demands compliance."

"Naboo declines."

The envoy's expression hardened.

"Then you leave us no alternative."

After the delegation departed, Apailana immediately convened her advisors.

One minister spoke first.

"They'll come."

"Yes," the Queen answered.

"They will."

Another advisor asked quietly,

"Should we reconsider?"

Apailana shook her head.

"If we surrender a man without even permitting him justice simply because the Empire demands it..."

She looked around the chamber.

"...then Naboo no longer governs itself."

Several days later, the Imperial government addressed the galaxy.

Mas Amedda, now serving as Emperor following Palpatine's death, stood before the Imperial Senate.

"The world of Naboo has openly rejected lawful Imperial authority."

He paused.

"It has chosen rebellion."

His voice echoed throughout the chamber.

"The Empire therefore declares war upon Naboo until lawful order has been restored."

The declaration had consequences far beyond Naboo.

For years, countless worlds had accepted Imperial rule only reluctantly.

Many had obeyed because Palpatine's authority appeared unshakable.

His death changed everything.

Planet after planet began issuing public statements.

Alderaan pledged support to Naboo.

Then another world.

Then another.

Systems that had quietly resented Imperial rule finally saw an opportunity.

"We stand with Naboo."

"The Empire no longer represents us."

"We reject Imperial authority."

Across the galaxy, local governments began openly choosing sides.

The conflict rapidly grew beyond Naboo itself.

A galactic civil war had begun.

The Empire faced not merely one rebellious world, but an expanding alliance determined to oppose Imperial rule.

Hidden within this growing movement was an organization that had quietly existed for years.

On a secure communications network, Mon Mothma addressed trusted leaders.

"The time for secrecy has ended."

Faces appeared one after another.

Bail Organa.

Military commanders.

Resistance leaders.

Covert operatives.

"Our work in the shadows has prepared us for this moment."

She looked around the room.

"The Alliance now comes into the open."

For years, the Rebel Alliance had quietly organized cells, gathered intelligence, and prepared for the possibility of open resistance.

Now it publicly declared itself.

Mon Mothma announced its support for Naboo and every world resisting Imperial rule.

"The Alliance stands with Naboo."

"We stand for the restoration of representative government."

"We stand against tyranny."

The Alliance formally joined with Naboo and its allies.

Together they established a united military and political front.

It became known simply as the Rebel Coalition.

Elsewhere, another message spread through hidden channels known only to surviving Jedi.

"The Coalition has declared itself."

"The time has come."

Across distant worlds, Jedi who had spent years hiding began emerging from exile.

Obi-Wan Kenobi quietly left the remote refuge that had concealed him.

Yoda departed his own place of hiding.

Many other survivors answered the same call.

Some had not seen another Jedi since Order 66.

Now they reunited once more.

Inside one Coalition headquarters, Obi-Wan entered a briefing room.

Several officers immediately stood.

General greetings gave way to stunned silence.

Then another familiar voice spoke.

"Master."

Obi-Wan turned.

Ahsoka Tano stood near the doorway.

For a moment neither moved.

Then Obi-Wan smiled.

"Ahsoka."

She returned the smile.

"It's been a long time."

"I've heard stories."

"So have I."

Mon Mothma approached them.

"Ahsoka has been serving the Alliance for years."

Obi-Wan looked at his former student's former Padawan with quiet admiration.

"You've done remarkable work."

"We all have."

Bail Organa also remained where he had been for years, though now openly.

As an Imperial senator, he had spent years resisting Imperial excess through every legal means available.

With war now declared, his opposition no longer needed to remain subtle.

Within the Coalition, every contribution mattered.

Diplomats.

Military officers.

Intelligence agents.

Former senators.

Planetary governments.

Smugglers.

Citizens.

And Jedi.

On Naboo, Mon Mothma personally met with Anakin and Padmé.

"We're grateful you've agreed to see me."

Padmé welcomed her into Varykino Villa.

"You've always been welcome here."

Anakin joined them.

Mon Mothma looked toward both of them.

"The Coalition could use your help."

Anakin answered honestly.

"We don't want military rank."

"We aren't looking for command."

Padmé nodded.

"We've spent years choosing our own work."

Mon Mothma smiled.

"I'm not asking either of you to become officers."

"What are you asking?"

"Help us."

"When you believe you can make a difference."

"As you've always done."

Anakin glanced toward Padmé.

She smiled.

"I think we already know the answer."

He nodded.

"So do I."

Mon Mothma extended her hand.

"Welcome."

Anakin shook it.

"We'll help."

"But we'll stay together."

"Of course."

Threepio cleared his throat.

"If I may, I should be delighted to continue providing translation and diplomatic assistance wherever required."

Mon Mothma smiled warmly.

"I was hoping you'd say that."

Thus Anakin, Padmé, and C-3PO entered the war not as officers, not as politicians, and not as members of any chain of command.

They remained independent, choosing their own assignments while coordinating with the Coalition whenever their efforts could best serve the larger cause.

Meanwhile, the Empire struggled to recover from the loss of its founder.

Mas Amedda worked tirelessly from Coruscant.

Meetings followed meetings.

Governors demanded guidance.

Military commanders requested reinforcements.

Regional leaders questioned Imperial authority.

Without Palpatine's personal influence holding countless competing interests together, fractures widened throughout the government.

Mas Amedda did everything within his power to preserve unity.

"We cannot appear weak."

He addressed senior officials.

"The Empire must endure."

Count Dooku stood nearby.

"We still possess enormous military strength."

Grievous added coldly,

"Then we destroy the rebellion."

The Empire fought with everything it possessed.

The Rebel Coalition answered in kind.

The war spread across star systems throughout the galaxy.

Battles erupted over shipyards, trade routes, industrial worlds, and vital hyperspace junctions.

Worlds changed hands repeatedly.

Civilian populations endured years of hardship.

Former friends sometimes found themselves fighting on opposite sides of the conflict.

The struggle lasted two long years.

Gradually, the balance shifted.

More systems abandoned the Empire.

Imperial supply lines weakened.

Coalition victories accumulated.

The Empire found itself defending more territory than it could effectively control.

Finally, Imperial leadership accepted what could no longer be denied.

The war could not be won.

Representatives from both governments met to negotiate terms.

Mas Amedda signed the final surrender documents in solemn silence.

"The Empire," he said quietly, "accepts these terms."

The Galactic Civil War ended.

Across the galaxy, celebrations erupted.

Weapons fell silent.

Ships returned home.

For the second time in only a few years, a galaxy exhausted by conflict looked toward peace.

The Rebel Coalition, having achieved victory, immediately turned its attention toward government.

Mon Mothma addressed delegates from countless member worlds.

"The Coalition was created to win a war."

She looked across the assembly.

"That war has ended."

"It is now time to build something lasting."

The Coalition formally reorganized itself into the New Republic.

Representative democracy returned as the foundation of galactic government.

Worlds once again elected representatives.

Power became distributed rather than concentrated in a single ruler.

The long years of dictatorship came to an end.

On a quiet day sometime afterward, Yoda stood before a small gathering of surviving Jedi.

The old Master's voice remained gentle.

"Much lost, we have."

The others listened silently.

"But gone, the Jedi are not."

He looked around the room.

"Many survivors remain."

"Hidden."

"Afraid."

"Alone."

He rested both hands upon his cane.

"Find them, we must."

"Only those willing."

"No one forced will be."

The surviving Jedi nodded.

Thus began the slow and difficult task of rebuilding.

Small groups traveled quietly throughout the galaxy searching for survivors of Order 66 who still wished to dedicate themselves to the Jedi way.

Some accepted the invitation.

Others declined, choosing different lives after years spent in hiding.

Yoda accepted every decision without judgment.

The Order that would emerge could not simply recreate what had been lost.

It would have to grow patiently, one willing Jedi at a time.

Elsewhere on Naboo, peace finally returned to Varykino Villa.

Anakin stood beside Padmé overlooking the lake where they had once come seeking refuge.

The galaxy had changed beyond recognition since they first arrived there carrying little more than grief.

Padmé slipped her hand into his.

"It finally feels quiet again."

Anakin smiled.

"It does."

Threepio stepped out onto the veranda carrying refreshments.

"Oh, this is much nicer than living through galactic wars."

Padmé laughed.

"I think everyone agrees with you."

The evening sun settled over the waters of Naboo.

For the first time in many years, the future no longer seemed defined by war, secrecy, or survival, but by the difficult, hopeful work of rebuilding a peaceful galaxy together.
