# Part 1

The bombing of the Jedi Temple had shaken Coruscant to its foundations.

An explosion had torn through one of the Temple's hangars, killing clone troopers and civilians alike. During the investigation, evidence had steadily mounted against Padawan Commander Ahsoka Tano. Security recordings, testimony, and seemingly irrefutable proof had painted her as the architect of the attack. Before long, the Jedi Council had expelled her from the Jedi Order so she could stand trial before the Galactic Republic without Jedi protection.

Ahsoka had escaped custody briefly, believing someone had framed her, only to become the target of a massive manhunt. Her closest friend among the Jedi, Barriss Offee, had aided her pursuit, earning Ahsoka's trust while secretly directing suspicion toward her. Eventually, Anakin Skywalker had refused to believe his Padawan guilty. While everyone else prepared for the trial, he pursued the truth alone.

Now, inside the Grand Convocation Chamber of the Galactic Senate, that trial was reaching its climax.

Rows of senators watched from their floating platforms. Clone guards lined the walls. Chancellor Palpatine observed from his elevated seat, his expression unreadable.

At the center stood Ahsoka.

Her wrists were bound with restraint cuffs. Though exhausted from days of accusation and pursuit, she stood with quiet dignity.

Standing beside her was Senator Padmé Amidala of Naboo, serving as her legal advocate.

Republic prosecutor Tarkin addressed the court.

"The evidence presented is overwhelming. The accused possessed both motive and opportunity. She fled lawful custody, assaulted Republic personnel, and was found in possession of incriminating evidence. We therefore ask this court to return a verdict of guilty."

Murmurs spread throughout the chamber.

Padmé stepped forward.

"The Republic prides itself on justice, not convenience."

She looked directly toward the judges.

"My client fled because every avenue available to her had already declared her guilty. The investigation ignored inconsistencies. It accepted every piece of evidence at face value while refusing to consider that someone deliberately orchestrated these events."

Tarkin folded his arms.

"Speculation."

"It is not speculation to ask for proof beyond planted evidence."

Padmé turned toward Ahsoka.

"This young woman fought for the Republic since she was barely more than a child. She has risked her life countless times for worlds she had never seen before. Does that history count for nothing?"

One of the judges replied evenly.

"Senator Amidala, character testimony cannot erase physical evidence."

"No," Padmé admitted. "But neither should physical evidence erase the possibility of deception."

The judge nodded.

"Have you uncovered the individual responsible?"

Silence.

Padmé lowered her head slightly.

"...No."

The chamber became deathly quiet.

Without the true culprit, her defense rested only on reasonable doubt.

The judges exchanged glances.

"We shall recess briefly before delivering our judgment."

Just as the presiding judge reached for his gavel—

The massive chamber doors burst open.

Everyone turned.

Anakin Skywalker strode inside.

His robes were scorched. Dust covered his boots.

One hand held an ignited lightsaber.

The other gripped the arm of a struggling Jedi Knight.

Barriss Offee.

Her hands were bound.

Clone guards immediately raised their blasters.

"Stand down!" Tarkin shouted.

Anakin ignored him.

He marched directly into the center of the courtroom and shoved Barriss forward.

She stumbled to her knees.

Gasps echoed through the Senate.

The members of the Jedi Council watching the proceedings stared in disbelief.

Mace Windu's eyes narrowed.

"Barriss?"

Padmé looked from Barriss to Anakin.

"What happened?"

Anakin deactivated his lightsaber.

"I found the one who framed Ahsoka."

The courtroom erupted.

The presiding judge struck his gavel repeatedly.

"Order! Order!"

Tarkin pointed accusingly.

"Skywalker, explain yourself immediately."

Anakin's voice was calm but carried throughout the chamber.

"I followed evidence that everyone else ignored. The trail led to Barriss Offee."

Barriss remained silent.

Anakin continued.

"She orchestrated the Temple bombing. She manipulated Letta Turmond. She murdered her to silence her. She planted evidence against Ahsoka. Then she pretended to help hunt her while steering everyone away from the truth."

The judge looked toward Barriss.

"Knight Offee... how do you answer these accusations?"

Silence.

Anakin stepped closer.

"You can stop this now."

Barriss looked up at him.

There was exhaustion in her eyes.

And something else.

Relief.

Slowly, she spoke.

"...He's right."

The chamber froze.

Barriss laughed once, softly.

"It was me."

Whispers exploded across the Senate.

She looked toward the Jedi Council.

"You've become soldiers."

Then toward the Chancellor.

"The Republic worships war."

Finally toward Ahsoka.

"I'm sorry."

Ahsoka simply stared.

Barriss continued.

"I believed the Jedi had lost their way. I wanted everyone to see the corruption beneath the peacekeepers' robes."

She closed her eyes.

"So I became something worse."

No one spoke.

The confession echoed through the chamber.

The presiding judge finally broke the silence.

"Are you confessing freely and without coercion?"

"...Yes."

"You admit responsibility for the Temple bombing, the murder of Letta Turmond, and the framing of Ahsoka Tano?"

"...Yes."

The judge struck the gavel.

"The court recognizes the confession."

He turned toward Ahsoka.

"All charges against Ahsoka Tano are hereby dismissed."

Another strike.

"She is immediately released."

Clone guards stepped forward and removed Ahsoka's restraints.

For the first time in many days, she rubbed her wrists freely.

Padmé smiled with visible relief.

Anakin finally exhaled.

Ahsoka looked at him.

"You never stopped."

He gave a small smile.

"I told you I wouldn't."

She embraced him.

The Senate watched in silence.

—

Later that day, Ahsoka returned to the Jedi Temple.

Not as a prisoner.

Not under guard.

She walked through its great entrance beside Anakin while Jedi throughout the Temple stopped to watch.

Some lowered their heads.

Others simply looked ashamed.

Word of Barriss's confession had spread quickly.

Inside the Council Chamber, every member of the Jedi High Council stood waiting.

Yoda leaned upon his cane.

Mace Windu stepped forward first.

"Ahsoka Tano."

She stood quietly before them.

Mace took a slow breath.

"The Council was wrong."

The admission seemed to weigh heavily upon every Jedi present.

"We allowed ourselves to be guided by evidence that was manipulated and by the urgency of politics."

Ki-Adi-Mundi lowered his head.

"We failed you."

Plo Koon, who had discovered Ahsoka as a child and brought her into the Order, looked deeply saddened.

"I should have trusted you."

Obi-Wan Kenobi spoke next.

"I am sorry, Ahsoka."

Even Yoda's ancient voice carried regret.

"Failed you, we have."

Silence filled the chamber.

Finally Mace continued.

"We ask that you return to the Jedi Order."

He gestured toward the center of the room.

"The Council wishes to restore your rank and standing immediately."

No one spoke.

Every eye rested upon Ahsoka.

She looked around the room.

She saw familiar faces.

The place where she had grown up.

The room where so many missions had begun.

She spoke softly.

"When I needed you..."

Her voice trembled only slightly.

"...you didn't trust me."

Nobody interrupted.

"I understand why you believed the evidence."

She looked at Mace.

"I understand that the Republic demanded answers."

Then at Yoda.

"And I know you all believed you were doing the right thing."

She smiled sadly.

"But I can't pretend nothing happened."

She took a slow breath.

"I've spent my whole life believing the Jedi would always stand by what was right."

She looked down briefly.

"When everything fell apart... I stood alone."

Anakin lowered his gaze.

She continued.

"I had one person who believed me."

She looked at him.

"Just one."

The Council remained silent.

"I've learned something these past few days."

Her voice had become steady.

"I need to figure out who I am."

She unclipped the braid from her belt—the Padawan braid she had worn for years.

Holding it carefully, she placed it on the floor before the Council.

"I can't come back."

Plo Koon closed his eyes.

Yoda sighed.

"So be it."

Ahsoka bowed respectfully.

"Thank you... for everything you've taught me."

She turned toward the exit.

Anakin watched her begin to walk away.

She reached the great doors.

Then—

"Wait."

Everyone looked toward Anakin.

He stood motionless.

Obi-Wan frowned.

"Anakin?"

Anakin looked from Ahsoka to the Council.

Then he spoke.

"If she's leaving..."

He took a breath.

"...I'm leaving too."

The words struck the room harder than any shout.

Obi-Wan stared at him.

"What?"

Mace's expression hardened.

"Skywalker, think carefully."

"I have."

Yoda's ears lowered.

"Strong emotions, these are."

"No."

Anakin shook his head.

"This isn't anger."

He looked directly at the Council.

"You expelled my Padawan because it was politically convenient."

Mace interrupted.

"We have admitted our mistake."

"You admitted it after she proved innocent."

His voice never rose.

"When she needed us most, the Order abandoned her."

Obi-Wan stepped closer.

"Anakin..."

He spoke gently.

"You don't have to do this."

"I do."

Anakin looked toward him.

"You taught me to stand by the people I care about."

Obi-Wan's face tightened.

"I also taught you not to make decisions you'll regret."

"I won't regret this."

He turned back toward the Council.

"Ahsoka is family."

The room fell silent again.

"I trained her."

"I watched her become a leader."

"I asked her to trust this Order."

He looked toward Ahsoka.

"And now she's walking away because we failed her."

Ahsoka shook her head.

"Anakin..."

"No."

He smiled faintly.

"You don't have to carry this alone anymore."

She stared at him in disbelief.

"You don't owe me this."

"I know."

"Then why?"

"Because it's my choice."

He unclipped his own lightsaber from his belt.

Every member of the Council watched.

Slowly, he placed it beside Ahsoka's Padawan braid.

"I resign from the Jedi Order."

Obi-Wan's face went pale.

"Anakin..."

For several long moments, no one moved.

Finally Yoda spoke.

"Certain, are you?"

"I am."

Mace looked toward the other Council members.

No one objected.

No one could force either of them to remain.

The old Master drew a slow breath.

"Then the Jedi Order accepts your decision."

His voice carried unmistakable sorrow.

"You leave with our respect."

Obi-Wan stepped forward until he stood before Anakin.

"So this is goodbye."

Anakin met his former Master's eyes.

"You'll always be my teacher."

Obi-Wan's voice almost broke.

"You were like a brother to me."

Anakin smiled sadly.

"You still are."

Without another word, the two embraced.

When they stepped apart, Obi-Wan placed a hand on Anakin's shoulder one final time.

"May the Force be with you."

"And with you."

Plo Koon approached Ahsoka.

He rested a hand gently upon her shoulder.

"You will always have a place in my thoughts, little Soka."

Her eyes glistened.

"Thank you... Master."

She embraced him.

Even Mace Windu inclined his head.

"May you both find the path you seek."

Anakin retrieved neither his lightsaber nor Ahsoka's braid.

Both remained where they had been laid, symbols of choices freely made.

Together, Anakin Skywalker and Ahsoka Tano walked through the Temple's long halls one final time.

Jedi stopped their training to watch.

Younglings whispered.

Knights and Masters alike stood aside in respectful silence.

No ceremony marked their departure.

No procession followed.

Only two figures walked side by side toward the Temple's great entrance.

At the top of the broad staircase, Ahsoka paused.

She looked back once.

"So..."

A small smile appeared on her face.

"What now?"

Anakin looked out over the endless skyline of Coruscant.

For the first time since either of them could remember, neither belonged to the Jedi Order.

He smiled back.

"I don't know."

Ahsoka laughed quietly.

"That's a little terrifying."

"Yeah."

"It also feels..."

She searched for the right word.

"...free."

Anakin nodded.

"Then let's find out what's next."

Together they descended the Temple steps, disappearing into the vast city below—not as Master and Padawan, nor as Jedi, but as two companions determined to forge their own path through a galaxy still consumed by war.

# Part 2

The towering spires of the Jedi Temple disappeared behind them as Anakin Skywalker and Ahsoka Tano descended into the endless streets of Coruscant.

Neither of them wore Jedi robes anymore.

The city hardly noticed.

Airspeeders roared overhead. Crowds flowed through plazas and skybridges, each person absorbed in their own concerns. The Clone Wars had become part of daily life. Two former Jedi walking away from the Temple barely registered among the countless lives moving through the capital.

For several minutes they simply walked.

Finally Ahsoka broke the silence.

"So..."

Anakin glanced over.

"So?"

She gave a crooked smile.

"I just realized something."

"What?"

"I don't have a home anymore."

Anakin frowned.

"The Temple was all I ever knew."

She looked around at the unfamiliar city.

"I don't exactly have an apartment."

She laughed quietly.

"I don't even know where I'd start looking."

Anakin stopped walking.

"Come stay with us."

Ahsoka blinked.

"Us?"

He nodded.

"Padmé and me."

She raised an eyebrow.

"I wasn't fishing for an invitation."

"I know."

"And you don't have to—"

"You've already seen the apartment."

She smiled knowingly.

"I suppose I have."

Several months earlier, Anakin had finally trusted her with the truth about his secret marriage to Senator Padmé Amidala. Ahsoka had been shocked at first, then amused that the two of them had managed to hide it from nearly everyone for so long.

She had kept the secret without hesitation.

"Are you sure she'll be okay with it?" Ahsoka asked.

Anakin smiled.

"I think she'll understand."

"I'd rather not accidentally become the reason your marriage falls apart."

He laughed.

"If anything, she'll probably insist you stay."

"You're very confident."

"I know my wife."

Ahsoka folded her arms.

"Well... until I figure out what comes next..."

She nodded.

"...I'd appreciate it."

"Then it's settled."

Together they headed toward Padmé's apartment.

—

Padmé opened the apartment door almost immediately after Anakin pressed the chime.

The moment she saw both of them standing there with travel bags, she knew something significant had happened.

Her eyes settled on Anakin.

"You left too."

He nodded once.

"I did."

She stepped aside.

"Come in."

The apartment felt unusually peaceful compared to the chaos outside.

Threepio hurried over.

"Oh! Master Anakin! Miss Tano! I wasn't expecting—"

Padmé smiled.

"It's all right, Threepio."

The protocol droid fell silent.

Padmé led them into the living room.

"What happened after you left the Temple?"

Anakin sat down.

"The Council apologized."

"I imagine they did."

"They offered Ahsoka her place back."

Padmé looked at Ahsoka.

"You said no."

"I couldn't go back."

Padmé nodded with complete understanding.

"I thought you might."

Anakin continued.

"Then I resigned."

Even though she had guessed as much, hearing the words spoken aloud made Padmé sit quietly for a moment.

"You actually did it."

"I couldn't stay."

She reached over and took his hand.

"I know."

There was no judgment in her voice.

Only concern.

Then she looked toward Ahsoka.

"So."

A warm smile crossed her face.

"I hear you need somewhere to stay."

Ahsoka looked almost embarrassed.

"Only until I can figure things out."

Padmé stood.

"I think we can manage that."

She began walking through the apartment.

"The guest room has become more of a storage room..."

She opened one door.

"...definitely not that."

Another.

"...not that either."

Finally she opened a smaller room.

"This should work."

It contained a modest bed, a desk, and a window overlooking the city.

"It isn't large," Padmé admitted.

Ahsoka smiled.

"It's bigger than my room at the Temple."

Padmé laughed softly.

"Then I suppose it'll do."

She turned back toward Anakin.

"You're both welcome here."

Ahsoka bowed her head slightly.

"Thank you."

"No."

Padmé smiled.

"After everything that's happened, you don't need to thank me."

—

The arrangement settled into an easy routine.

Ahsoka occupied the spare room.

Padmé continued her Senate work.

Anakin adjusted to life without the responsibilities of the Jedi Order, though old habits remained difficult to break.

Several days later, the apartment chime rang.

Anakin opened the door.

"Obi-Wan."

Obi-Wan stood outside holding a long case.

"So this is where you've been."

Anakin smiled.

"What brings you here?"

Obi-Wan stepped inside.

"I brought something."

He placed the case upon the table and opened it.

Inside rested three lightsabers.

Anakin's blue lightsaber.

Ahsoka's green-bladed lightsaber.

Her yellow-green shoto.

Ahsoka stared.

"I thought those stayed with the Order."

"They did."

Obi-Wan picked up Anakin's weapon.

"I requested permission from the Council to return them."

"You did?"

"I argued that the Clone Wars have not ended."

He looked at both of them.

"You may no longer be Jedi, but the galaxy remains dangerous."

He offered the lightsaber to Anakin.

"I believe you should keep this."

Anakin accepted it slowly.

"I... wasn't expecting that."

Ahsoka accepted hers just as carefully.

"Thank you."

Then Anakin frowned.

"Wait."

Obi-Wan looked up.

"How did you know we were here?"

A faint smile appeared.

"Anakin."

"...What?"

"I've known you've been staying at Padmé's apartment from time to time for years."

Silence.

Anakin blinked.

"You... knew?"

"I had my suspicions."

"You never said anything."

"I didn't."

"Why?"

Obi-Wan shrugged.

"You clearly believed it was important to keep private."

Padmé tried very hard not to smile.

"So you just..."

"I chose not to ask questions."

Anakin looked utterly defeated.

"I thought we were being careful."

"You were."

Obi-Wan smiled.

"Just not quite careful enough."

Ahsoka burst into laughter.

"I can't believe you thought he'd never notice."

Anakin groaned.

"Not helping."

Obi-Wan's smile softened.

"I truly do wish both of you well."

He looked toward Ahsoka.

"The Council does as well."

She nodded.

"I appreciate that."

Obi-Wan headed for the door.

"Take care of yourselves."

"And you," Anakin replied.

The door closed behind him.

For a long moment no one spoke.

Finally Ahsoka grinned.

"So..."

Anakin sighed.

"He knew."

"He absolutely knew."

"I can't believe this."

Padmé couldn't suppress her laughter any longer.

—

A few days later, Padmé returned from the Senate much earlier than expected.

She opened the apartment door.

"...What are you two doing?"

The living room had become an improvised training arena.

Furniture had been pushed aside.

Anakin and Ahsoka circled one another with training sabers humming.

The coffee table had somehow ended up against a wall.

R2-D2 watched enthusiastically.

Threepio hid behind a chair.

"Oh dear..."

Anakin parried a strike.

"I almost had her."

"You've been saying that for ten minutes," Ahsoka replied.

Padmé crossed her arms.

"You are both aware this is an apartment."

Another exchange of blows.

"...Yes."

"Not training grounds."

They froze.

Ahsoka looked around.

"...Fair point."

Padmé pointed toward the door.

"There are literally thousands of public squares on Coruscant."

Anakin sheepishly deactivated his lightsaber.

"We'll spar somewhere else."

"Good."

Padmé looked at the overturned chair.

"And you're putting everything back."

"Yes, Senator."

She smiled despite herself.

—

The peace did not last.

Several days later, Anakin and Ahsoka finished another sparring session in one of Coruscant's open public training squares.

Sweat dripped from Anakin's forehead.

Ahsoka tossed him a towel.

"You've been distracted."

"I'm fine."

"No."

She folded her arms.

"You've been distracted for days."

He remained silent.

She waited.

Eventually he spoke.

"I've been having dreams."

"What kind?"

"They feel..."

He searched for the words.

"...different."

His expression darkened.

"I see Padmé."

Ahsoka listened quietly.

"She's pregnant."

He swallowed.

"And she dies."

Silence settled between them.

"I keep seeing it."

He looked away.

"I can't stop it."

Ahsoka's face softened.

"Did you tell her?"

"...No."

She stared.

"No?"

He shook his head.

"Why not?"

"I..."

He stopped.

"I don't know."

"You don't know?"

"I just..."

He sighed.

"I didn't want to frighten her."

Ahsoka stepped in front of him.

"Anakin."

"What?"

"If you were in her place..."

He already knew the answer.

"...I'd want to know."

"Exactly."

He rubbed his forehead.

"I've been acting like an idiot."

She smiled faintly.

"Only a little."

—

That evening the three of them sat together for dinner.

Halfway through the meal, Anakin set down his fork.

"Padmé."

She looked up.

"There's something I should have told you."

He explained everything.

The visions.

The childbirth.

The fear that gripped him every night.

Padmé listened without interrupting.

When he finished, she reached across the table.

She took his hand.

"I won't pretend hearing that isn't frightening."

"I'm sorry."

"You don't need to apologize."

She squeezed his hand gently.

"But promise me something."

"Anything."

"Don't make any rash decisions because of a vision."

He hesitated.

"I'll try."

"I mean it."

"I know."

She smiled sadly.

"We'll face whatever comes together."

Ahsoka nodded in agreement.

"Together."

—

Not long afterward, Chancellor Palpatine invited Anakin to accompany him to the Galaxies Opera.

The performance itself scarcely held Anakin's attention.

Instead, Palpatine spoke quietly throughout the evening.

He told an ancient tale.

The story of Darth Plagueis.

A Sith Lord who had supposedly learned to prevent death itself.

That night, after returning home, Anakin found Ahsoka on the apartment balcony.

"I need your opinion."

She listened as he repeated everything Palpatine had told him.

When he finished, she was silent for several moments.

Then she said,

"Maybe you should stop meeting with him."

Anakin frowned.

"What?"

"He's filling your head with stories about cheating death."

"But what if—"

"No."

She looked directly at him.

"Listen to yourself."

"He might know something."

"He also might be manipulating you."

"He wouldn't."

"You sound awfully certain."

Anakin looked away.

"If there's even the slightest chance he knows how to save Padmé..."

"I can't ignore it."

Ahsoka became deeply uneasy.

"You've changed."

"I'm trying to save my wife."

"I know."

She sighed.

"But this isn't you."

He said nothing.

Later that night, while Anakin busied himself repairing a speeder component with R2-D2, Ahsoka quietly approached Padmé.

"Can we talk?"

Padmé immediately noticed her serious expression.

"Of course."

They stepped onto the balcony.

Ahsoka repeated every word she could remember from Anakin's conversation about Darth Plagueis.

Padmé's expression steadily darkened.

"So Palpatine told him someone could prevent death."

"Yes."

"And Anakin believes him."

"He wants to."

Padmé folded her arms.

"I don't like this."

"Neither do I."

After a long silence, Padmé nodded.

"We need to convince him to stop seeing Palpatine."

"Together?"

"Together."

—

The following morning, Anakin entered the living room to find both women waiting.

He immediately became suspicious.

"...What's going on?"

Padmé gestured toward the sofa.

"Sit."

He sighed.

"This feels like an intervention."

"It is," Ahsoka replied.

Over the next hour they spoke openly.

Neither accused him.

Neither mocked his fears.

Instead they reminded him that fear could be exploited.

That grief clouded judgment.

That Palpatine's promises rested on stories rather than proof.

Finally Padmé said quietly,

"If someone keeps offering exactly what you're desperate to hear..."

She looked into his eyes.

"...you have to ask why."

The room fell silent.

Eventually Anakin nodded.

"...All right."

Ahsoka blinked.

"Really?"

"I'll stop meeting him."

Padmé smiled with relief.

"Thank you."

—

The change did not go unnoticed.

Within days, Palpatine's invitations resumed.

Anakin politely declined.

Another invitation arrived.

He declined again.

Then the Chancellor requested private meetings.

Anakin refused those as well.

When they crossed paths in government buildings, Palpatine spoke softly.

"You continue to reject my offers."

"I've been busy."

"Busy protecting Senator Amidala?"

Anakin stiffened.

"You still fear your visions."

Palpatine's voice became almost sympathetic.

"I alone have offered hope."

Anakin said nothing.

"You need only ask."

Every conversation ended the same way.

With temptation.

With the promise that there might yet be a way to save Padmé.

Anakin found his resolve wavering.

Padmé noticed.

One evening she watched him staring silently out the apartment window.

She made her decision.

"We're leaving Coruscant."

He looked over.

"What?"

"For a few days."

"The war—"

"The Senate can function without me."

She smiled.

"Jar Jar has filled in for me before."

Ahsoka looked surprised.

"You're serious."

"I am."

She turned to Anakin.

"You need distance from Palpatine."

Within two days everything had been arranged.

Jar Jar Binks reluctantly accepted responsibility for Padmé's Senate duties during her absence.

R2-D2 accompanied Anakin.

C-3PO naturally insisted upon accompanying Padmé.

Ahsoka remained behind.

"You'll have the apartment to yourself," Padmé said.

"I'll try not to break anything."

Anakin smirked.

"That's aimed at me."

"Mostly."

—

Their transport landed on Naboo beneath clear blue skies.

They returned to Varykino Villa beside the peaceful waters of Lake Paonga.

The contrast with Coruscant could not have been greater.

No endless traffic.

No Senate.

No Temple.

No Chancellor.

Only quiet hills, open skies, and shimmering water.

For the first day, Anakin remained restless.

The visions lingered.

He woke from nightmares.

Padmé sat beside him.

"I'm not going anywhere."

"I saw you die."

"You saw a vision."

"It felt real."

She brushed a hand against his face.

"So does this."

Over the following days she noticed something.

Whenever Anakin became absorbed in something else, the anxiety eased.

So she kept him occupied.

They sailed across the lakes.

Visited nearby islands.

Walked through flowering meadows.

Explored waterfalls hidden among the cliffs.

Sometimes they said very little.

Sometimes they talked for hours about anything except war and death.

Little by little, the constant grip of the visions loosened.

Not completely.

But enough that Anakin occasionally found himself smiling again without forcing it.

Padmé quietly took that as a victory, however small.

---

# Part 3

Life at Varykino Villa gradually settled into a peaceful rhythm.

Each morning, Padmé found new excuses to take Anakin somewhere away from his thoughts. They sailed across Naboo's lakes, wandered through gardens, and visited quiet villages where few people recognized them. The nightmares had not disappeared entirely, but the constant tension that had consumed Anakin on Coruscant had begun to ease.

One afternoon, while they were eating lunch on the villa's terrace overlooking the water, R2-D2 suddenly emitted an urgent series of whistles.

Anakin looked over.

"What is it, Artoo?"

The astromech projected a Republic news bulletin.

A holographic news presenter appeared.

"This is a Republic-wide emergency broadcast. Count Dooku, leader of the Separatist Alliance, has been captured aboard General Grievous's flagship, the *Invisible Hand*. Following his capture, Count Dooku was executed."

Anakin stared at the hologram.

"...Executed?"

The report continued.

"The Chancellor has praised the Republic forces responsible for bringing this conflict closer to its conclusion. Military analysts now believe the Separatist leadership has suffered an irreparable blow."

The transmission ended.

Silence settled over the terrace.

Padmé slowly lowered her cup.

"I didn't expect it to end like that."

Neither had Anakin.

He had imagined facing Dooku again someday. After all that had happened during the war, it almost felt strange that someone else had defeated him.

He leaned back in his chair.

Anakin nodded sadly.

"I hate it too."

After a long silence she sighed.

"You're probably right."

She abandoned the idea.

Instead she devoted herself to Luke, Leia, and the people of Naboo in quieter ways.

News occasionally reached them from Coruscant.

One report in particular stood out.

Jar Jar Binks had been removed from the Imperial Senate.

Officially, no explanation was given.

Unofficially, people whispered that the Emperor had finally grown tired of Jar Jar's outspoken nature.

Afterward, Jar Jar simply disappeared.

Rumors spread that he had returned to Naboo.

Some claimed he lived somewhere in exile.

Others insisted they had seen him wandering remote settlements.

But despite living on Naboo themselves for years afterward, neither Anakin nor Padmé ever encountered him.

Whether the rumors were true remained a mystery.

As Luke and Leia grew older, another challenge became obvious.

Anakin watched them practicing basic lightsaber forms one afternoon.

He smiled.

Then sighed.

"I can't teach them."

Padmé rested a hand on his shoulder.

"You already have."

"No."

He shook his head.

"They're going to surpass anything I can show from this chair."

Ahsoka stepped forward.

"I'll train them."

From that day onward she visited almost daily.

She proved to be an excellent teacher.

Patient.

Firm.

Encouraging.

Luke and Leia progressed rapidly.

When they became old enough to wield proper lightsabers, Ahsoka presented two familiar weapons.

She laid them carefully upon a table.

Anakin recognized them immediately.

"My lightsaber."

"And Obi-Wan's."

"I recovered them after the battle."

She picked up Anakin's blue lightsaber.

Luke accepted it reverently.

Then she handed Obi-Wan's blue lightsaber to Leia.

Leia smiled.

"I'll take good care of it."

"I know you will," Ahsoka replied.

By the time the twins reached thirteen years of age, Ahsoka's responsibilities had expanded.

She had quietly begun working against the Empire under the codename Fulcrum, organizing intelligence networks and assisting the growing resistance movement.

Luke and Leia increasingly accompanied her.

Eventually they spent more time living with Ahsoka than at Varykino Villa.

Their parents missed them.

But both understood why.

"They're where they need to be," Padmé said.

Anakin smiled proudly.

"They're becoming exactly who they're meant to become."

Years into Ahsoka's covert work, an old enemy returned.

Maul.

He approached them unexpectedly.

"I have no love for Sidious."

Neither did Luke nor Leia.

Nor Ahsoka.

Maul continued.

"We share an enemy."

After careful discussion, Ahsoka reluctantly agreed to cooperate temporarily against the Empire.

For a short time, the uneasy alliance held.

Then Maul began speaking privately with Luke and Leia.

"The dark side is strength."

"You've barely scratched your true potential."

Ahsoka confronted him immediately.

"What are you doing?"

"I'm teaching."

"No."

She ignited her lightsabers.

"You're corrupting them."

Maul smiled.

"They deserve the truth."

The confrontation exploded into battle.

This time Luke and Leia fought beside Ahsoka.

Their training was put to the ultimate test.

The duel ranged across abandoned ruins for nearly an hour.

Finally the combined efforts of all three overwhelmed Maul.

Luke disarmed him.

Leia struck away his last defense.

Ahsoka delivered the final blow.

Maul fell.

This time there would be no escape.

When they returned to Naboo and told Anakin what had happened, he could not stop smiling.

"You beat Maul."

Luke nodded proudly.

"It wasn't easy."

Leia laughed.

"He nearly got away twice."

Anakin looked toward Ahsoka.

"Their first real duel."

"They handled themselves exceptionally well," Ahsoka agreed.

Anakin studied his children carefully.

"I always knew you had potential."

He smiled.

"But now..."

He looked toward Padmé.

"...I think they can actually do it."

Ahsoka nodded slowly.

"I think so too."

"One day."

"They'll surpass all of us."

"And when that day comes..."

She looked toward Luke and Leia.

"...the Emperor should be afraid."

As the years continued, Luke and Leia matured into capable young Jedi in everything but name.

When they reached approximately nineteen years of age, they announced another decision.

"We're joining the Rebel Alliance," Luke said.

Leia nodded.

"It's time."

Ahsoka looked at them both with unmistakable pride.

"I have nothing left to teach either of you."

Luke smiled.

"Really?"

She nodded.

"You've reached the level of a Jedi Knight."

Leia looked almost disappointed.

"So training is over?"

Ahsoka laughed softly.

"Formal training."

"You'll keep learning your entire lives."

Anakin and Padmé watched their children prepare to leave.

It was difficult.

But neither tried to stop them.

Anakin wheeled himself forward.

"I wish I could stand here."

Luke knelt beside him.

"You don't have to."

Anakin embraced both of his children.

"I'm proud of you."

"So am I," Padmé said.

Ahsoka looked toward the two young Jedi she had raised.

"The future belongs to you now."

As Luke and Leia departed to join the fledgling Rebel Alliance, those remaining behind watched with hope rather than despair.

Years earlier, Anakin had accepted that he and his friends might never overthrow the Empire themselves.

Now, watching the next generation depart, he believed with quiet certainty that the future of the Jedi—and the fate of the galaxy—rested in capable hands.

For the first time in many years, that was enough to make him believe the Empire's reign would not last forever.
