# Part 1

The shattered windows of the Supreme Chancellor's office opened onto the endless lights of Coruscant. Wind howled through the room, scattering fragments of transparently strong glass across the polished floor.

Mace Windu had already been hurled out through the broken window by Darth Sidious, his body lost somewhere in the darkness beyond the tower. The office was filled with the smell of scorched fabric and ozone.

Anakin stared at what had happened, his breathing ragged.

"What have I done...?"

Sidious slowly rose from where he had collapsed, his face twisted and scarred from the reflected energies of his own power. Despite his appearance, his yellow eyes burned brighter than ever.

"You have done what was necessary," the Sith Lord said. "You have saved my life. Without you, the Jedi would have overthrown the Republic."

Anakin looked toward the shattered window one last time.

"I... I couldn't let him kill you."

"And now," Sidious said, extending a pale hand, "your journey is complete."

Anakin hesitated only a moment before kneeling.

"I pledge myself... to your teachings."

A thin smile spread across Sidious' ruined face.

"Good. Very good."

"You shall be known as..."

The old man's voice echoed through the ruined office.

"...Lord Vader."

Anakin lowered his head.

"Thank you, my Master."

Sidious walked slowly toward the center of the office.

"The Jedi have attempted to assassinate their Chancellor. They have revealed themselves as traitors. If they are allowed to regroup, they will plunge the Republic into civil war."

He turned to face his new apprentice.

"First I want you to go to the Jedi Temple. We will catch them off-balance. Do what must be done. Lord Vader. Do not hesitate. Show no mercy."

Silence.

Anakin remained where he was.

His eyes drifted toward the broken window.

The Jedi Temple rose in the distance, illuminated against Coruscant's night.

He remembered training rooms.

Master Yoda's lessons.

Obi-Wan's guidance.

Younglings laughing through temple corridors.

Sidious' voice cut through the silence.

"Go."

Anakin looked back at him.

"I..."

He swallowed.

"I can't."

The smile disappeared from Sidious' face.

"What did you say?"

"I'll help stop the Jedi leadership if I have to. I'll face those who attack us." Anakin's voice remained steady despite the conflict raging inside him. "But I won't slaughter everyone in the Temple."

Sidious stared at him.

"You refuse your Master?"

"I refuse that order."

"You have no right."

Anakin stood.

"I just saved your life."

"You fulfilled your destiny."

"If I hadn't intervened, Master Windu would have killed you."

"I know exactly what would have happened."

"Then you owe me something."

For the first time since revealing himself as a Sith Lord, Sidious laughed.

It was not amused laughter.

It was cold.

"You misunderstand the nature of the Sith."

He stepped closer.

"The relationship between Master and apprentice is absolute. There is no negotiation. No bargaining. No debts."

"You said I was your apprentice."

"I did."

"Then hear me."

Anakin met the older man's eyes without flinching.

"I refuse to massacre the Jedi Temple."

The office became deathly still.

"So be it."

With startling speed, Sidious reached beneath his robes.

A second lightsaber flew into his hand.

Anakin's eyes widened.

"You had another—"

The crimson blade ignited with a violent hiss.

Sidious lunged.

Anakin barely managed to ignite his own blue lightsaber before the red blade crashed into it.

The impact sent sparks flying.

"You disappoint me," Sidious snarled.

Anakin shoved him back.

"I won't become that monster!"

"You already have!"

The duel exploded across the Chancellor's office.

Sidious attacked with blinding speed, every strike fueled by fury. Though fatigued from his battle with Mace Windu, the Sith Lord remained terrifyingly fast.

Anakin answered with powerful two-handed blows, forcing Sidious backward through sheer strength.

Furniture shattered.

Statues toppled.

Fragments of transparently strong glass continued blowing into the room from the broken windows.

Blue and crimson light flashed across the office.

Sidious spun, striking high, low, then high again.

Anakin blocked every blow.

"You think mercy makes you strong?" Sidious hissed.

"No."

Their blades locked.

"I think it keeps me human."

Sidious suddenly thrust out his free hand.

An immense Force push struck Anakin squarely in the chest.

He flew across the office, smashing through an ornate table before crashing against the far wall.

His lightsaber nearly slipped from his grasp.

Sidious did not pursue.

Instead he turned toward the Chancellor's desk.

A concealed holonet transmitter emerged from within it.

He activated it instantly.

The holographic projector flickered to life.

Sidious leaned toward it.

"Execute Order—"

Anakin reacted on instinct.

Ignoring the pain shooting through his body, he hurled himself forward.

His blue blade swept downward in a perfect arc.

The transmitter split cleanly into two halves.

The hologram vanished.

The severed pieces sparked violently before falling silent.

Sidious hissed with rage.

"You dare—"

Their blades collided again.

This time Anakin drove him backward.

The Sith Lord's breathing had grown noticeably heavier.

His movements remained deadly, but tiny signs of exhaustion were beginning to appear.

Mace Windu had already forced him to spend enormous reserves of energy before Anakin had ever entered the office.

Now another relentless duel demanded even more.

Still, Sidious refused to relent.

Lightning erupted from his fingertips.

Anakin barely managed to catch the energy on his lightsaber, angling the crackling bolts aside as they carved smoking scars across the walls.

The barrage ended only when Sidious charged again.

Their lightsabers became blurs.

Minutes seemed to stretch endlessly.

Neither combatant spoke.

Only the shriek of lightsabers and the roar of the wind filled the office.

Then Sidious abruptly disengaged.

He leaped backward toward another doorway.

For the briefest instant, space separated them.

His hand slipped inside his robes.

A compact comlink appeared.

He activated it.

"Execute—"

Anakin lunged with every ounce of strength left in him.

His lightsaber flashed.

The comlink split neatly in half.

Its transmission died before another word escaped.

The broken device bounced across the floor.

Sidious' eyes burned with absolute hatred.

"You insolent fool!"

He attacked with renewed desperation.

The duel became savage.

There was no elegance left.

No measured precision.

Only survival.

Anakin's muscles screamed with exhaustion.

Sidious' breathing became harsher.

The older man's attacks slowed by fractions of a second.

Fractions were enough.

Anakin saw an opening.

He pivoted beneath a descending strike.

His shoulder slammed into Sidious' chest.

The Sith Lord staggered.

Anakin struck once.

Sidious barely blocked.

Twice.

The red blade trembled.

Three times.

The second crimson lightsaber flew from Sidious' hand, spinning across the floor.

The old man stumbled backward until his heels reached the edge of the shattered window.

The endless city stretched beneath him.

He raised empty hands.

"Wait."

Anakin stood several paces away, chest rising and falling rapidly.

His lightsaber remained pointed at Sidious.

"You've lost."

Sidious' voice suddenly softened.

"If you kill me..."

He smiled faintly.

"...Padmé dies."

Anakin's expression faltered.

"You don't know that."

"I alone possess the knowledge you seek."

"You promised to teach me."

"And I still can."

Sidious extended one hand.

"Spare me, my apprentice."

"You need me."

"I need no one."

"You need me."

"You need me more."

The office fell silent except for the wind.

Anakin thought of Padmé.

He thought of the nightmares that had haunted him.

He thought of Mace Windu being hurled out through the shattered window.

He thought of every lie.

Every manipulation.

Every word Sidious had whispered over the years.

His grip tightened.

"You've controlled my life long enough."

Sidious' expression hardened.

"You will never save her without me."

Perhaps he was telling the truth.

Perhaps he was lying.

At that moment, Anakin no longer cared.

With a cry born of grief, rage, and despair, he rushed forward.

His blue lightsaber swept through the air.

Sidious tried to summon his fallen weapon with the Force.

He was too slow.

The blade struck home.

The Sith Lord collapsed to the floor.

His eyes widened.

Then the light faded from them.

Silence returned to the Chancellor's office.

The wind continued blowing through the shattered windows.

Anakin remained frozen where he stood.

His lightsaber hummed softly before he deactivated it.

The blue glow disappeared.

He looked down at Sidious' lifeless body.

Then toward the shattered window where Mace Windu had been thrown out into the night.

One dead by his own hand.

The other dead because of his choice.

His knees gave way.

He sank to one knee in the center of the ruined office.

"What have I done?"

His voice barely rose above a whisper.

"I helped kill Master Windu..."

He looked toward the body of the Chancellor.

"...and now I've killed the Supreme Chancellor."

He buried his face in one hand.

"The Jedi will never trust me."

He laughed once.

It held no humor.

"The Senate won't either."

Minutes passed.

The silence slowly steadied his breathing.

He lowered his hand.

One disaster at a time.

There was nothing more he could do here.

He would decide his next step later.

Rising slowly to his feet, Anakin clipped his lightsaber back onto his belt.

He gave the office one final look—the broken windows, the ruined furniture, the body of Darth Sidious lying on the floor, and the shattered opening through which Mace Windu had been thrown into the darkness below.

Without another word, he turned and walked through the office doors.

The doors slid shut behind him, leaving the dead alone in the darkness of the Chancellor's office.

# Part 2

The corridors of the Senate Executive Building were eerily quiet.

Anakin Skywalker walked through them in silence, his dark robes torn from battle and his face marked by exhaustion. No guards challenged him. No aides stopped him. The few people he passed instinctively stepped aside, sensing something terrible had happened even if they could not yet understand what.

He reached the landing platform, climbed into his Jedi starfighter, and departed without transmitting a word.

By the time he reached Padmé Amidala's apartment, it was deep into the Coruscant night.

The apartment was dark.

Only the soft lighting left on by the protocol droid illuminated the main room.

The front door slid open.

C-3PO turned toward the entrance.

"Oh! Master Anakin. I wasn't expecting—"

Anakin walked past him without speaking.

"My, you do look terribly exhausted."

Still saying nothing, Anakin made his way directly to the bedroom.

Padmé was already asleep.

Her breathing was calm, completely unaware of the catastrophe unfolding elsewhere on Coruscant.

Anakin stood beside the bed for several moments.

Looking at her eased the storm inside him, if only slightly.

Without changing clothes, he quietly climbed into bed beside her.

Within moments, sheer physical and emotional exhaustion overwhelmed him.

He fell asleep almost instantly.

The following morning, sunlight filtered through the apartment's windows.

Padmé stirred first.

She blinked sleepily before turning over.

Her eyes widened.

"Anakin?"

He was asleep beside her, still wearing the same clothes as the previous day.

His face looked drained.

There were faint burns on his robes and signs of recent combat.

She gently brushed a strand of hair from his forehead.

He did not react.

Padmé frowned.

Normally he would wake at the slightest touch.

Choosing not to disturb him further, she quietly climbed out of bed and entered the living area.

"Threepio."

The golden protocol droid immediately approached.

"Good morning, Senator."

"When did Anakin come home?"

"Oh, it was very late last night, milady."

"Did he say anything?"

"I'm afraid not."

"He simply walked in, went straight to your bedroom, and retired for the evening. I attempted to ask whether he required anything, but he appeared far too tired to answer."

"So you don't know where he'd been?"

"I'm sorry, Senator. I don't know anything beyond that."

Padmé looked back toward the bedroom.

"What happened to you...?"

At nearly the same time, unease was spreading rapidly through the Jedi Temple.

Hours had passed since Mace Windu and the three Jedi Masters who had accompanied him—Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin—had departed to confront Supreme Chancellor Palpatine.

None had returned.

No communication had been received.

Jedi Master Shaak Ti finally rose from her seat.

"I'll go myself."

No one objected.

She boarded a transport and headed directly to the Senate Executive Building.

The Chancellor's office doors stood partially open.

Shaak Ti cautiously entered.

The destruction was immediately apparent.

Furniture lay smashed across the room.

The panoramic windows had been shattered, allowing Coruscant's wind to sweep through the office.

Near the entrance lay Agen Kolar.

A short distance away was Saesee Tiin.

Further inside rested Kit Fisto.

All three Jedi Masters were dead.

Shaak Ti slowly continued forward.

Near the broken windows lay another body.

Supreme Chancellor Palpatine.

His face was horribly disfigured, and he, too, was dead.

Shaak Ti searched the office.

There was no sign of Mace Windu.

No lightsaber.

No body.

She searched again.

Nothing.

Then another realization struck her.

"Skywalker..."

Anakin Skywalker had disappeared from the Jedi Temple after Mace Windu ordered him to remain there.

He was nowhere to be found.

Shaak Ti immediately activated her comlink.

"This is Jedi Master Shaak Ti. Emergency priority."

The Temple acknowledged instantly.

"I have located the Chancellor's office."

Her voice remained calm despite the shock.

"Masters Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin are confirmed dead."

Silence answered her report.

"Mace Windu is missing."

Another stunned pause.

"So is Anakin Skywalker."

She looked down once more at Palpatine's body.

"The Supreme Chancellor is also dead."

The Temple's communications officer struggled to find words.

"Master... what are your orders?"

Shaak Ti answered without hesitation.

"Recall every Council member immediately."

"They're engaged across multiple fronts of the Clone Wars."

"Forget about the Clone Wars."

Her tone became firmer.

"This is an emergency."

"Tell Masters Yoda, Obi-Wan Kenobi, Ki-Adi-Mundi, Plo Koon, and Stass Allie to return to Coruscant as fast as they can."

"Immediately."

"Yes, Master."

After ending the transmission, Shaak Ti contacted the Senate.

"This is Jedi Master Shaak Ti."

"I regret to inform you that Supreme Chancellor Palpatine has been found dead in his office."

Within minutes, shock spread throughout the Senate District.

Several senators hurried to the Executive Building.

Among them were Bail Organa, Mon Mothma, and numerous others.

They entered the devastated office.

Bail slowly surveyed the destruction.

"The Force..."

Mon Mothma looked toward the Chancellor's body.

"He's really dead."

No one spoke for several moments.

Finally another senator broke the silence.

"The Senate must convene immediately."

Bail nodded.

"There is no alternative."

An emergency Senate session was called at once.

Throughout Coruscant, notifications reached every senator.

One of them appeared on Padmé Amidala's datapad.

She read the message.

"Emergency Senate session. Immediate attendance required."

She looked toward the bedroom.

Anakin still had not moved.

She quietly entered the room again.

He remained deeply asleep.

She sat beside him.

"No."

She shook her head.

"I'm not leaving until I know what's happened."

She ignored the summons.

Across the galaxy, Jedi Council members disengaged from their battlefronts.

On Utapau, Obi-Wan Kenobi immediately entrusted command of operations to Commander Cody before departing aboard his starfighter.

On Kashyyyk, Yoda left the Wookiees after receiving Shaak Ti's urgent message.

Ki-Adi-Mundi abandoned his campaign.

Plo Koon turned his fighter toward Coruscant.

Stass Allie likewise withdrew from her front.

As quickly as hyperspace allowed, each made for the Jedi Temple.

By late that day, the surviving members of the Jedi Council had assembled.

Yoda.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Ki-Adi-Mundi.

Plo Koon.

Stass Allie.

Shaak Ti.

Coleman Kcaj.

The Council Chamber felt strangely empty.

Seven seats occupied.

Several others vacant.

Shaak Ti finished recounting everything she had witnessed.

"...there was no sign of either Mace Windu or Anakin Skywalker."

Silence settled over the chamber.

Finally Obi-Wan spoke.

"Then we begin with the obvious."

He reached for the communications console.

"We contact them."

Mace Windu's comlink was tried first.

The signal continued attempting to connect.

No answer.

Again.

Nothing.

Yoda lowered his head.

"Troubling, this is."

Next Obi-Wan entered Anakin's frequency.

Far across Coruscant, inside Padmé's apartment, a comlink resting on a nearby table began ringing.

Padmé looked at it.

She immediately recognized the device.

Anakin's comlink.

The display identified the caller only as the Jedi Temple.

She hesitated.

"If I answer..."

She glanced toward the sleeping Jedi Knight.

"...they'll know I'm here."

The ringing continued.

And continued.

Finally she sighed.

"I suppose I don't have much choice."

She accepted the call.

Obi-Wan's hologram appeared.

"Senator Amidala."

Padmé forced a calm smile.

"Master Kenobi."

Obi-Wan looked surprised.

"I was attempting to reach Anakin."

"He's... here."

Relief briefly crossed Obi-Wan's face.

"Good. Put him on."

Padmé hesitated.

"I'm afraid he isn't available at the moment."

Obi-Wan frowned.

"This is an emergency, Senator."

"I understand."

"We must speak to him now."

Padmé muted the transmission for a moment and hurried back into the bedroom.

She gently touched Anakin's shoulder.

"Anakin."

No response.

She shook him slightly.

"Anakin."

Still nothing.

Her concern deepened.

She pressed harder.

"Anakin, wake up."

He remained completely asleep.

She tried again.

Nothing.

Padmé returned to the comlink and reactivated the transmission.

Obi-Wan immediately asked, "Well?"

Padmé looked uncomfortable.

"I... I tried."

"And?"

"He's sleeping."

Obi-Wan blinked.

"Sleeping?"

"I couldn't wake him."

Around the Council Chamber, concerned glances were exchanged.

"Couldn't wake him?" repeated Plo Koon.

Padmé nodded.

"I've tried several times."

Obi-Wan's expression grew serious.

"Senator, has he been injured?"

"I don't know."

"He came here very late last night."

"He didn't tell you where he'd been?"

"No."

Yoda's ears lowered.

"Disturbing, this is."

Obi-Wan made his decision almost immediately.

"I'll come myself."

He rose from his seat.

"If Anakin truly cannot be awakened, I want to examine him personally."

Padmé looked toward the bedroom once more.

"I'll be here."

The transmission ended.

Without another word, Obi-Wan turned and strode from the Council Chamber, heading for his starfighter and Padmé Amidala's apartment.

# Part 3

Obi-Wan Kenobi's starfighter settled onto the landing platform outside Padmé Amidala's apartment.

The apartment door slid open before he had a chance to knock.

"Master Kenobi," Padmé said, her concern obvious.

"Senator."

He stepped inside.

"Has anything changed?"

She shook her head.

"No."

"He still hasn't woken up."

Obi-Wan glanced toward the bedroom.

"How long has he been asleep?"

"He came in very late last night."

"And it's nearly mid-morning now."

Padmé nodded.

"I've tried everything I could think of short of hurting him."

"He just keeps sleeping."

Obi-Wan walked quietly into the bedroom.

Anakin lay exactly where Padmé had left him.

His breathing was steady.

His face was peaceful.

Yet no amount of calling his name or gently shaking his shoulder produced any response.

Obi-Wan frowned.

"This isn't natural."

He activated his comlink.

"Council, I'm with Anakin now."

The holograms of Yoda, Shaak Ti, Plo Koon, Ki-Adi-Mundi, Stass Allie, and Coleman Kcaj appeared. The Council Chamber was visible behind them.

"Can you wake him?" Shaak Ti asked.

"I've tried."

"And?"

"No response."

The Council members exchanged uneasy looks.

Finally Obi-Wan looked toward the astromech waiting quietly near the doorway.

"R2."

The little droid let out a questioning whistle.

"I need you to give Anakin a small shock."

R2-D2 whistled again, sounding almost apologetic.

"Just enough to wake him."

The astromech rolled over to the bedside.

A small electric probe extended from one of his compartments.

Padmé looked uncertain.

"Is that really necessary?"

"I'm afraid we've run out of gentler options."

R2 emitted one last apologetic chirp.

Then...

*Zap!*

Anakin's entire body jerked.

His eyes flew open.

"AH!"

He bolted upright in bed.

For a moment he looked completely bewildered.

His eyes darted around the room.

"Obi-Wan?"

Then toward Padmé.

"Padmé?"

He swung his legs over the side of the bed, intending to stand.

The lingering effect of the electrical shock immediately betrayed him.

His legs buckled beneath him.

"Whoa—"

He collapsed awkwardly back onto the mattress.

Padmé hurried to steady him.

"It's all right."

She gently pushed him back against the pillows.

"Just stay in bed."

Anakin rubbed one of his legs.

"What..."

He looked accusingly at R2.

"Did you zap me?"

The astromech let out an innocent whistle.

Obi-Wan folded his arms.

"You wouldn't wake up."

Anakin blinked.

"How long was I asleep?"

"Since last night," Padmé answered.

"It's nearly mid-morning."

His eyes widened.

"I slept that long?"

"You did."

Padmé sat beside him.

"Anakin..."

She took a breath.

"Where have you been?"

Obi-Wan quietly activated his comlink again.

"The Council is listening."

The holograms brightened slightly.

Anakin looked from Obi-Wan to Padmé, then to the miniature Council members projected from the device.

He closed his eyes for a moment.

"I suppose... there's no point hiding any of it."

He opened them again.

"It started yesterday."

"I discovered that Chancellor Palpatine was a Sith Lord."

Every member of the Council listened in complete silence.

"I went straight to Master Windu and told him."

Obi-Wan slowly nodded.

"So it was you who informed him."

"Yes."

"He believed me."

"He gathered Masters Kit Fisto, Agen Kolar, and Saesee Tiin."

"They left immediately to arrest the Chancellor."

Anakin lowered his head.

"Before they left, Master Windu ordered me to remain at the Temple."

"But I had a bad feeling."

"I couldn't stay there."

"So after a while..."

He sighed.

"...I disobeyed him."

"I went to the Chancellor's office."

The room remained completely silent.

"When I arrived..."

Anakin swallowed.

"...the fight was already over."

"Master Windu had defeated Palpatine."

"He'd disarmed him."

"I saw Palpatine lying on the floor."

"He couldn't defend himself."

Obi-Wan frowned.

"And then?"

"Master Windu raised his lightsaber."

"He said Palpatine was too dangerous to be left alive."

"I thought..."

Anakin's voice became quieter.

"...I thought he was only going to arrest him."

"I panicked."

He looked directly at Obi-Wan.

"I cut off Master Windu's hand."

Every hologram remained frozen.

Padmé stared at him.

Obi-Wan's face went pale.

"You... what?"

"I cut off his hand."

The words sounded even heavier the second time.

"Palpatine immediately unleashed another blast of Force lightning."

"He threw Master Windu through the shattered window."

"He fell."

No one spoke.

Obi-Wan slowly lowered himself into a nearby chair.

"Oh..."

He covered part of his face with one hand.

"Oh, Anakin..."

The Council Chamber was equally silent.

Finally Obi-Wan looked back up.

"Why?"

"Why would you do such a thing?"

"Why cut off Master Windu's hand?"

Anakin answered immediately.

"I didn't want Palpatine dead."

"I only wanted him arrested."

Obi-Wan stared at him.

"He was a Sith Lord."

"I know."

"He was responsible for this entire war."

"I know."

"He deserved to stand trial if possible," Obi-Wan admitted. "Perhaps arrest would have been preferable."

He leaned forward.

"But why did you care so desperately that he survive?"

Anakin looked away.

He already knew there was no avoiding the answer.

"You won't stop asking until I tell you."

"I won't."

Anakin drew a long breath.

"I've been having visions."

Padmé looked at him.

"What kind of visions?"

"You."

She blinked.

"Me?"

"I kept seeing you die."

The room became still once more.

"You were giving birth."

"I couldn't stop it."

Padmé instinctively rested a hand on her abdomen.

Anakin continued.

"I was terrified."

"I didn't know what to do."

"Then one night..."

He looked toward Obi-Wan.

"...Palpatine told me the story of Darth Plagueis."

"The Sith Lord who supposedly learned how to prevent the people he cared about from dying."

Yoda's expression darkened.

Anakin continued.

"After that..."

"He told me that only he possessed the knowledge I needed."

"He said only he could teach me how to save Padmé."

Padmé stared at him.

"So..."

"You wanted him alive because you thought he could save me."

Anakin nodded once.

"Yes."

"If he died..."

"...that knowledge would die with him."

Nobody interrupted.

Obi-Wan slowly absorbed every word.

After several moments he spoke softly.

"Go on."

Anakin closed his eyes briefly.

"After Master Windu died..."

"I thought there was no turning back."

"I'd helped kill a Jedi Master."

"I thought the Order would never forgive me."

"I thought..."

"...I had already crossed the line."

He opened his eyes again.

"So I pledged myself to Palpatine."

Silence.

"He accepted me as his apprentice."

"He gave me a new name."

Anakin forced himself to say it.

"Darth Vader."

Padmé recoiled as though struck.

"No..."

Her voice was barely audible.

Obi-Wan simply stared.

The Council holograms remained motionless.

Anakin continued before anyone could interrupt.

"Then Palpatine ordered me to go to the Jedi Temple."

"He told me to massacre everyone there."

Padmé covered her mouth.

"And?"

Obi-Wan asked quietly.

"I refused."

Obi-Wan let out a breath he had not realized he had been holding.

The Council members visibly relaxed.

"I told him I wouldn't do it."

"He said I had no right to refuse."

"I reminded him that I'd just saved his life."

"I said he owed me something."

"He said a Sith Master owes an apprentice nothing."

"I refused again."

"What happened then?" Shaak Ti asked.

"He attacked me."

"He had another lightsaber."

"I didn't even know he carried a spare."

"We fought."

"It was a long duel."

"He was already exhausted from fighting Master Windu."

"Eventually I gained the advantage."

Anakin looked down at his hands.

"He kept trying to reach a transmitter."

"He almost said, 'Execute Order...'"

"I destroyed the transmitter before he could finish."

"Later he got hold of a comlink."

"He managed to say only 'Execute...' before I cut that apart too."

Ki-Adi-Mundi frowned.

"'Execute' what?"

Anakin shook his head.

"I don't know."

"He never finished the sentence."

Obi-Wan exchanged uncertain looks with the Council holograms.

Anakin continued.

"In the end..."

"I defeated him."

"He begged me to spare him."

"He reminded me that only he could save Padmé."

"I..."

He looked at Padmé.

"...I ignored him."

"I killed him."

The apartment fell silent.

"After that..."

"I stayed in the office for a while."

"I couldn't believe what had happened."

"Then I left."

"I came here."

"I didn't say anything."

"I just went to bed."

He looked around the room.

"And the next thing I remember..."

His gaze settled on R2.

"...is waking up after that little droid shocked me."

No one answered.

Padmé sat perfectly still beside him, trying to reconcile the man she loved with everything he had just confessed.

Obi-Wan remained seated, his eyes fixed on the floor.

The holograms of Yoda, Shaak Ti, Plo Koon, Ki-Adi-Mundi, Stass Allie, and Coleman Kcaj were equally silent.

No one knew what to say.

The weight of Anakin's confession hung over the apartment as every person present struggled to process everything they had just heard.

# Part 4

Silence lingered in the apartment.

No one moved.

No one seemed willing to be the first to speak.

At last, Obi-Wan broke the silence.

"Anakin."

Anakin looked up.

"Are you a Sith Lord now?"

# Part 7

The weeks of house arrest settled into a routine.

Freed from the constant urgency of war, Anakin decided to follow Padmé's advice.

One morning he found C-3PO organizing schedules and correspondence in the apartment.

"Threepio."

The protocol droid turned.

"Oh! Master Anakin."

"I was wondering if you had a moment."

"Certainly, sir."

"You wanted me to learn more about Padmé."

"I did."

"Then teach me."

Threepio's photoreceptors brightened.

"It would be my pleasure."

Over the following days, Anakin and Threepio spent hours talking.

Threepio described Padmé's work in the Senate.

He explained how carefully she prepared for debates, reading countless reports before speaking on legislation.

He described the endless meetings, negotiations, and committee hearings that filled her days while Anakin had been leading clone troopers across the galaxy.

"You see, Master Anakin, Senator Amidala rarely enters a discussion without first understanding every side of the issue."

Anakin listened closely.

Threepio recounted humanitarian missions that had received little public attention.

He spoke of long nights spent drafting speeches.

He described Padmé's habit of answering correspondence personally whenever possible instead of relying entirely on aides.

He told stories of difficult negotiations with senators from dozens of systems.

By the end of the week, Anakin realized how little he had known about the life Padmé lived while he had been away fighting.

One afternoon, after another long conversation, Threepio folded his hands.

"There is one final piece of advice I should offer."

Anakin smiled.

"I'm listening."

"Master Anakin..."

"It would be wise of you not to argue with Senator Amidala."

Anakin blinked.

"...What?"

"Listen to her."

"Do not argue with her."

"Why?"

Threepio answered without hesitation.

"Because I have witnessed enough."

"Every time senators argue with her..."

"...they get destroyed by her."

"The successful ones are the ones who listen."

For nearly an hour, Threepio enthusiastically recounted examples.

He described senators who had entered debates convinced they would outmaneuver Padmé, only to leave having been methodically dismantled by her arguments.

Anakin laughed more than once.

"I've never seen that side of her."

"You were usually elsewhere saving the Republic."

Threepio inclined his head.

"I had an excellent view."

Anakin scratched the back of his head.

"Surely this doesn't apply to me."

"I'm not a senator."

"That is true, sir."

Threepio paused.

"However, from my experience, this is simply how Senator Amidala interacts with people generally."

"My observations suggest that everyone who argues with her has a difficult time."

"And everyone who listens to her has a good time."

"You should try it."

"I believe your life will improve significantly."

Anakin considered the advice.

"...All right."

"I'll give it a try."

Over the next several days, he did exactly that.

Whenever Padmé suggested something, he agreed.

Whenever she offered an opinion, he accepted it.

Whenever she explained her reasoning, he simply nodded.

At first, the atmosphere in the apartment became noticeably calmer.

Their disagreements nearly vanished.

The friction between them eased.

But after several days, Padmé narrowed her eyes at him over breakfast.

"Anakin."

"Yes?"

"Why are you agreeing with everything I'm saying now?"

He looked up innocently.

"Because you're right."

Padmé folded her arms.

"No."

"I know that's not the whole story."

She smiled suspiciously.

"What's the real reason?"

Anakin hesitated.

"...Fine."

He sighed.

"Because..."

"...Threepio told me to."

Padmé closed her eyes.

She already knew where this was going.

"What exactly did Threepio tell you?"

"He said you'd absolutely destroyed a bunch of senators during the Clone Wars."

Padmé slowly lowered her head into one hand.

"And?"

"He said if I didn't want to end up like them, I should listen to you instead of arguing."

Padmé inhaled deeply.

"THREEPIO!"

The protocol droid hurried into the room.

"Yes, Mistress Padmé?"

She looked directly at him.

"Anakin is not a senator."

"No, Mistress Padmé."

"So trying to put him into the same category as the senators you've watched me debate is not the right thing to do."

Threepio tilted his head.

"I... see."

"The point of a partner is not to agree with everything I say."

"It isn't?"

"No."

She smiled patiently.

"A healthy relationship isn't a Senate debate."

"I don't want him to become another senator who simply decides it's safer not to disagree with me."

"I want him to think for himself."

Threepio's processors seemed to pause.

"I confess..."

"...that distinction may be more complicated than I appreciated."

Padmé laughed.

"I suspected it might be."

She gently patted his shoulder.

"Don't worry."

"You were trying to help."

"I was indeed, Mistress Padmé."

"I know."

Threepio bowed politely before returning to his duties.

After he left, Padmé smiled at Anakin.

"I think I'll take over from here."

She did.

Instead of asking Anakin to agree with her, she encouraged him to explain his own thinking.

When he reacted too quickly, she challenged him to slow down.

When he became impatient, she asked him to consider another perspective before acting.

When he disagreed, she expected him to explain why instead of simply becoming defensive.

Gradually, the conversations became easier.

Anakin learned to listen without surrendering his own judgment.

Padmé learned to challenge him without making every disagreement into a lesson.

He no longer saw her as an idealized figure he had to win.

He saw the woman herself.

Her convictions.

Her habits.

Her strengths.

Her flaws.

In turn, Padmé found herself speaking to him less like someone she needed to guide and more like an equal.

He remained unmistakably Anakin—passionate, impulsive, and determined—but those qualities became tempered by a greater willingness to understand before acting.

Several weeks after the Senate had first placed him under house arrest, a formal summons finally arrived.

The investigation had concluded.

Anakin returned to the Senate chamber alongside Padmé.

Once again, senators filled the vast rotunda.

After final remarks from the Senate investigators and a brief statement from Anakin, the chamber entered one last debate.

The debate eventually came to an end.

The presiding officer rose.

"The Senate will now determine its verdict."

"The question before this body is whether Anakin Skywalker shall be punished or released."

Voting commenced.

The senators of the Delegation of 2000 unanimously voted for release.

Elsewhere throughout the chamber, opinions were more divided.

Lights continued changing across the vast rotunda.

Finally the results appeared.

More votes had been cast for release than for punishment.

The presiding officer announced,

"By decision of the Galactic Senate, Anakin Skywalker is formally acquitted."

"He is free to leave."

A murmur spread throughout the chamber.

Anakin let out a slow breath he had not realized he had been holding.

Padmé smiled with unmistakable relief.

After brief congratulations from Bail, Mon Mothma, Obi-Wan, and Yoda, Anakin and Padmé quietly departed together.

For the first time in weeks, there were no legal restrictions waiting for them outside the Senate Building.

Anakin was free to go wherever he wished.

As they stepped into the Coruscant sunlight, Padmé smiled at him.

"So."

"Where do you want to go first?"

Anakin thought about it.

Only a few weeks earlier, he would have answered with somewhere exciting simply because he could.

Now he looked at her instead.

"Honestly?"

She smiled.

"Yes?"

"I'm happy just going home with you."

Padmé laughed softly.

"Good answer."

He grinned.

"This time I actually mean it."

And he did.

He was no longer restless in her company.

For the first time since they had met, he genuinely understood the difference between being in love with an idea of Padmé Amidala and loving Padmé Amidala herself.

He still had much to learn.

So did she.

But now they would learn together, as partners rather than as two people endlessly chasing their next reunion.

# Part 8

The days following Anakin's acquittal passed more peacefully than anyone had dared hope.

Padmé's pregnancy reached its final days.

Although Anakin still remembered the terrifying visions that had driven so many of his choices, each passing day without incident weakened their hold over him. He remained at Padmé's side almost constantly, unwilling to let fear consume him again but determined to be there when their children arrived.

When the time finally came, Padmé was taken to one of Coruscant's finest medical facilities.

Anakin remained beside her throughout the long hours.

"You can do this," he whispered, holding her hand.

Padmé smiled despite the pain.

"So can you."

Hours later, the cries of two newborn infants echoed through the delivery room.

The attending physician carefully wrapped the babies before approaching the exhausted parents.

"A healthy boy."

The tiny infant was placed gently into Anakin's arms.

"And a healthy girl."

Padmé accepted the second child with tears of joy.

Anakin looked from one child to the other in amazement.

"They're beautiful."

Padmé smiled.

"Luke."

She looked at the boy.

"And Leia."

She turned toward the girl.

"They're perfect."

The physician checked Padmé once more before smiling reassuringly.

"Everything went well, Senator."

"No complications."

"You should make a full recovery."

Anakin closed his eyes in relief.

The visions had not come true.

Padmé had survived the childbirth.

A burden he had carried for months finally lifted from his shoulders.

Only a short time later, another chapter of the war came to an end.

With Darth Sidious dead and the Republic under new leadership, Chancellor Bail Organa opened negotiations with the Confederacy of Independent Systems.

Months of bloodshed had left both sides exhausted.

Without Palpatine secretly directing both governments, the political will to continue fighting quickly evaporated.

Eventually representatives of the Republic and the Confederacy signed a formal peace treaty.

The Clone Wars were over.

For the first time in years, the galaxy experienced peace.

Several months after Luke and Leia's birth, Anakin received an unexpected message.

The invitation bore the seal of the Jedi Council.

He smiled as he read it.

Padmé looked up from where she was feeding Leia.

"What is it?"

"The Council wants me to visit the Temple."

She raised an eyebrow.

"I thought you'd already used up your visitor privileges."

Anakin laughed.

"I don't think that's how they work."

"So why do they want you?"

"They say they'd like me to describe my duel with Darth Sidious."

Padmé nodded thoughtfully.

"That makes sense."

"You were one of the very few people to survive fighting a Sith Lord."

"And the only one to defeat him."

A few days later, Anakin entered the Jedi Temple for the first time since resigning from the Order.

Several Jedi recognized him as he walked through the familiar corridors.

Some greeted him warmly.

Others simply nodded respectfully.

No one challenged his presence.

His visitor authorization was accepted without issue.

When he reached the Council Chamber, the doors opened before him.

Inside sat a full Council once more.

Yoda occupied the Grand Master's seat.

Obi-Wan Kenobi sat nearby.

Plo Koon.

Ki-Adi-Mundi.

Stass Allie.

Shaak Ti.

Coleman Kcaj.

Returning to the Council after previous service were Depa Billaba and Oppo Rancisis.

Three newly appointed members completed the twelve seats.

Luminara Unduli.

Quinlan Vos.

Kelleran Beq.

Obi-Wan smiled.

"Welcome back, Anakin."

"It feels strange hearing that inside this room."

"I imagine it does," Obi-Wan replied.

Yoda inclined his head.

"Thank you for coming."

Anakin bowed respectfully.

"Thank you for inviting me."

Shaak Ti began.

"We've reviewed your earlier account several times."

"However, we'd like to understand the duel itself in greater detail."

Plo Koon added,

"Very few Jedi have fought Sith Lords in recent centuries."

"And fewer still survived."

Luminara continued,

"If there is anything we can learn from your experience..."

"...we hope to incorporate it into future Jedi training."

Anakin nodded.

"I'll tell you everything I remember."

The Council listened as Anakin began recounting the battle from beginning to end.

He described Sidious producing a second lightsaber after Mace Windu had disarmed the first.

"He attacked immediately."

"I barely had enough time to ignite my own lightsaber before the first strike landed."

Quinlan Vos leaned forward.

"How aggressive was he?"

"Very."

"He never gave me time to settle into a rhythm."

"He constantly mixed quick attacks with sudden changes in direction."

Kelleran Beq asked,

"Did he rely mostly on lightsaber technique?"

"No."

"He mixed lightsaber combat with the Force whenever he saw an opening."

Anakin demonstrated several movements.

"At one point he hit me with a Force push so powerful that I flew across the entire office."

He gestured across the Council Chamber to illustrate the distance.

"I crashed through a table before I could recover."

Depa Billaba listened carefully.

"How quickly did he transition back into lightsaber combat?"

"Immediately."

"He never let me assume one attack was finished before another began."

Oppo Rancisis asked,

"What ultimately shifted the battle in your favor?"

Anakin thought for a moment.

"He was already exhausted."

"He'd just fought Master Windu."

"I could feel it."

"He was still incredibly dangerous."

"But he couldn't maintain that pace forever."

Obi-Wan nodded.

"So endurance mattered."

"Very much."

Anakin continued describing every stage of the duel.

Every strike.

Every parry.

Every feint.

He explained how Sidious repeatedly attempted to create even a moment of separation.

"He wasn't retreating."

"He was trying to communicate."

Anakin looked around the chamber.

"The first time, he reached a holonet transmitter."

"He managed to say, 'Execute Order...'"

"I cut the transmitter in half before he could finish."

Several Council members exchanged thoughtful looks.

"The second time," Anakin continued, "he pulled out a comlink."

"He only managed to say, 'Execute...'"

"I destroyed that too."

Ki-Adi-Mundi folded his hands.

"So whatever order he intended to give..."

"...never reached its recipients."

"I don't know what the order was," Anakin admitted.

"I only knew I couldn't let him send it."

For several more hours the Council questioned him.

How Sidious moved.

How he anticipated attacks.

How he used Force lightning.

How he exploited hesitation.

How he reacted under pressure.

Anakin answered every question as accurately as memory allowed.

At last Yoda spoke.

"Helpful, this has been."

Shaak Ti nodded.

"The Order has not possessed firsthand knowledge of Sith combat like this in generations."

Luminara added,

"It will prove valuable when developing future training."

Obi-Wan smiled warmly.

"Thank you, Anakin."

Anakin inclined his head.

"I'm glad I could help."

He looked around the chamber one final time.

The room no longer felt like the place where his future would be decided.

It had become a place he could visit without fear.

A place filled with old friends rather than judges.

He bowed once more.

"May the Force be with you."

"And with you," several Council members replied together.

Anakin turned and left the Council Chamber.

He walked through the Temple corridors at an unhurried pace.

Younglings hurried between classrooms.

Jedi Knights discussed assignments.

Life within the Temple continued much as it always had.

When he finally stepped outside into the sunlight of Coruscant, he paused for a moment and looked back at the great Jedi Temple.

His first visit since leaving the Order had passed without incident.

No arguments.

No suspicion.

No hostility.

Only questions, answers, and mutual respect.

The Jedi were pleased that Anakin had come.

Anakin was equally glad that returning to the Temple had felt less like reopening an old wound and more like visiting old friends.
