# Part 1

The swamp of Dagobah lay silent beneath its endless mist. Ancient trees rose from black water, their roots twisted like the bones of forgotten giants. In the small clearing where an old hut rested beneath drooping vines, Luke Skywalker stood frozen, his eyes distant.

He had felt it.

A cry through the Force.

Leia.

Han.

Pain.

His lightsaber rested at his belt. R2-D2 whistled anxiously beside him.

"I have to go," Luke said, already turning toward the X-wing half-hidden among the swamp reeds. "They're in trouble."

Yoda remained seated upon a gnarled root, his eyes closed.

"Strong is the disturbance," the old Jedi Master murmured. "Yes. Suffering, your friends endure."

"Then I can't stay here!"

A familiar voice answered before Yoda could.

"You already know why this is dangerous."

Luke turned.

The shimmering blue figure of Obi-Wan Kenobi stood quietly between the trees.

"Ben..."

"You have come far," Obi-Wan said. "But your training is unfinished."

"They need me."

"They may."

Luke's voice rose.

"Han and Leia are my friends!"

"And because they are your friends," Obi-Wan replied gently, "you are thinking with your heart instead of with wisdom."

Luke looked between the spirit and Yoda.

"If I stay, they could die."

Yoda slowly opened his eyes.

"If you leave... perhaps they die anyway. Perhaps you fall. Then all hope may die with you."

Luke clenched his fists.

"I can't just abandon them."

"You do not abandon them," Obi-Wan said. "You trust them."

"They're facing Darth Vader!"

"Yes."

"He'll kill them!"

Obi-Wan stepped closer, his glowing form calm.

"Luke... Vader wants you."

The words struck him.

"He wants your fear. Your anger. Your impatience."

Luke looked away.

"If you rush to him now, you will fight as you are—not as you must become."

Silence settled over the swamp.

Yoda rose slowly, leaning upon his cane.

"A Jedi acts because the Force guides him. Not because fear drives him."

Luke closed his eyes.

He could still feel them.

The pain.

The uncertainty.

Every instinct screamed for him to run.

Then another feeling emerged beneath the panic.

Leia.

Steady.

Alive.

Afraid, but not broken.

He drew a slow breath.

"If... if I stay..."

"You finish what you came here to do," Obi-Wan answered.

"And afterward?"

"Afterward," Yoda said quietly, "ready, you will be."

Luke remained motionless for what felt like an eternity.

Finally he unclipped the ignition key from his X-wing and walked back toward the hut.

"I'll stay."

Obi-Wan smiled.

"It is not the easier choice."

"No."

Luke looked toward the fog where his starfighter waited unseen.

"It's the harder one."

Yoda nodded.

"Much to learn, Skywalker still has."

Far across the galaxy, the Millennium Falcon emerged from hyperspace above the floating city of Cloud City.

Leia Organa watched through the cockpit viewport as the graceful white towers floated above the orange clouds of Bespin.

Lando Calrissian welcomed them warmly.

"Han! You old pirate!"

Han grinned.

"Lando."

They embraced.

Leia watched cautiously.

"You trust him?" she asked quietly afterward.

Han shrugged.

"I trust him enough."

Chewbacca gave a doubtful growl.

Lando laughed.

"Come on. You all look exhausted."

Cloud City's elegant halls seemed peaceful.

Too peaceful.

Then the trap closed.

Stormtroopers burst from every entrance.

Blasters were raised.

Before Han could draw his own weapon, Darth Vader stepped from the shadows.

Han fired immediately.

Vader caught every bolt upon his lightsaber.

The red blade swept once.

Han's blaster flew from his hand.

The group was surrounded.

Leia stared at Lando.

"You sold us out."

Lando couldn't meet her eyes.

"I'm sorry."

Han lunged toward him.

Chewbacca roared.

Stormtroopers forced them apart.

Vader looked at the prisoners.

"The princess. The Wookiee. The protocol droid."

His gaze shifted to Han.

"And Captain Solo."

Hours later Han Solo stood imprisoned inside the freezing chamber.

Lando watched helplessly.

"This wasn't part of the agreement."

Vader ignored him.

"We will test the carbon-freezing process."

Leia swallowed hard.

"I love you."

Han managed the smallest smile.

"I know."

The machinery roared to life.

The platform descended.

Moments later the carbonite block rose from the pit.

Silent.

Frozen.

Boba Fett examined the slab.

"He survived."

"As promised," Vader said. "Take him to Jabba the Hutt."

Boba Fett activated his jetpack.

"I'll collect my payment."

He departed with the frozen smuggler.

Lando stared after him.

Then he turned sharply toward Vader.

"You said this was all."

"I have altered the arrangement."

Lando's face hardened.

"You gave your word."

"I have altered it."

Stormtroopers marched into the chamber.

"The princess and her companions will remain here."

Lando's expression darkened.

"For what?"

"Skywalker."

Realization spread across Lando's face.

"You're using them as bait."

"They will remain until he arrives."

Lando stepped backward.

"No."

Vader looked at him without emotion.

"You will cooperate."

Lando turned and walked away in silence.

Outside the chamber he hurried through the corridors until he found Leia, Chewbacca, and C-3PO under guard.

Leia's eyes narrowed.

"What do you want now?"

"I was wrong."

"You've said that already."

"I mean it this time."

Leia folded her arms.

"Why should I believe you?"

"Because Vader lied to me."

Chewbacca growled angrily.

"I deserve that," Lando admitted.

"But if you don't come with me right now, none of us are leaving."

Leia studied him.

She saw fear.

Regret.

Not deception.

She nodded once.

"Then let's move."

Cloud City erupted into confusion.

Lando overrode security systems, redirected elevators, and sealed corridors behind them.

Stormtroopers flooded the city.

Blaster fire echoed through polished halls.

Chewbacca carried the repaired C-3PO upon his back while roaring defiance at every Imperial squad that blocked their escape.

Leia fired with calm precision.

Lando led them through maintenance passages known only to city administrators.

"Hurry!" he shouted.

"The Falcon's this way!"

They burst onto the landing platform.

The Millennium Falcon sat waiting.

Chewbacca sprinted aboard first.

Leia covered the ramp.

Lando jumped into the cockpit.

The engines roared.

Far behind them Darth Vader strode onto the platform just as the Falcon lifted away.

His cape snapped violently in the wind.

"The ship!" an officer shouted.

Without hesitation Vader turned.

"My fighter."

Moments later his black TIE Advanced blasted from its hangar.

The chase began immediately.

Laser fire streaked across Bespin's skies.

The Falcon twisted through clouds and industrial towers.

Leia looked over her shoulder.

"He's still behind us!"

"I know!" Lando answered, gripping the controls.

Chewbacca barked urgently from the engineering station.

"We've got one chance."

The hyperdrive engaged.

Stars stretched into brilliant lines.

The Millennium Falcon vanished into hyperspace.

Vader reached the jump point seconds later.

Empty space greeted him.

He searched in vain.

The Falcon was gone.

Some time later, hidden within the Rebel Alliance fleet, the Falcon settled into a concealed hangar.

General Rieekan welcomed them grimly.

"Han?"

Leia lowered her eyes.

"Boba Fett took him."

Silence filled the room.

Lando stepped forward.

"I betrayed them."

Every Rebel soldier stared.

Lando continued before anyone could interrupt.

"I thought I was protecting Cloud City. Vader lied to me. I helped Leia and the others escape."

Leia looked toward the Rebel officers.

"He risked everything to get us out."

Lando turned toward her in surprise.

"You don't have to defend me."

"I'm telling the truth."

General Rieekan studied him.

"What do you intend to do now?"

Lando looked around the command center.

"If you'll have me..."

He drew a slow breath.

"I'd like to fight the Empire."

Leia answered before anyone else.

"We need every good pilot we can get."

Rieekan nodded.

"Then welcome to the Alliance."

That night Leia stood alone beside the Falcon.

The stars seemed impossibly distant.

Lando approached quietly.

"I've been thinking about Han."

"So have I."

"I owe him."

Leia rested one hand against the Falcon's hull.

"So do I."

"We could go after Jabba."

Leia closed her eyes.

"For all we know, Han is already deep inside Hutt Space."

"We could try."

She was silent for a long time.

Finally she shook her head.

"No."

Lando frowned.

"No?"

"The Rebellion comes first."

She forced herself to continue.

"I want to save him more than anything."

Her voice almost broke.

"But we don't know where he is. We don't know how to reach him. Chasing shadows while the Empire hunts us would only waste lives."

She looked up at the stars.

"Han would tell us to keep fighting."

Lando nodded slowly.

"So we fight."

"We fight."

Meanwhile, on Dagobah, the days became weeks.

The weeks became months.

Luke never left.

Every sunrise began with meditation.

Every afternoon brought exhausting physical trials.

Every evening ended with long discussions beneath ancient trees.

Yoda drove him relentlessly.

Again and again Luke lifted stones until they no longer strained him.

He balanced upon swaying branches while blindfolded, guided only by the Force.

He navigated the deepest marshes without sight.

He learned patience.

Stillness.

Discipline.

Failure no longer frustrated him.

It became another lesson.

Obi-Wan's spirit appeared often.

"You rely less on your eyes now."

"I'm beginning to understand."

"The Force is not something you grasp."

Luke smiled faintly.

"It's something you listen to."

Obi-Wan returned the smile.

"Exactly."

R2-D2 remained faithfully at Luke's side.

The little astromech complained loudly whenever swamp creatures interrupted lessons, but even he seemed to settle into the strange rhythm of Dagobah.

Luke laughed more often.

He spoke less.

His movements became smoother.

His lightsaber seemed to flow rather than strike.

When anger surfaced, Yoda immediately recognized it.

"Stop."

Luke lowered his blade.

"What did I do?"

"You wished to defeat me."

"I was sparring."

"No."

Yoda tapped Luke lightly with his cane.

"You wished to win."

Luke reflected quietly.

After a moment he nodded.

"You're right."

Months passed.

Across the galaxy, Leia Organa, Lando Calrissian, Chewbacca, and C-3PO flew mission after mission aboard the Millennium Falcon.

They carried intelligence through Imperial blockades.

Delivered supplies to isolated Rebel cells.

Extracted agents before Imperial forces could capture them.

Lando proved himself an exceptional pilot and daring commander.

Chewbacca's loyalty never wavered.

Threepio complained endlessly but served faithfully.

Leia led every mission with calm determination.

At quiet moments she still found herself staring at Han's empty seat in the cockpit.

Chewbacca would sometimes rest one massive hand upon the chair without saying a word.

Leia understood.

She felt the same ache.

But every time another mission came, she stood.

"The Alliance needs us."

No one argued.

The Falcon would rise into the stars once more.

And far away, beneath the endless mist of Dagobah, Luke Skywalker continued his uninterrupted training under Yoda's patient guidance, growing steadily stronger in the Force, wiser in judgment, and more deeply connected to the living energy that bound the galaxy together.

# Part 2

A full year passed beneath the ancient trees of Dagobah.

Luke Skywalker's life became one of constant discipline. Every day began before dawn with meditation. Every day ended with long conversations about the Force beneath the swamp's endless canopy. He learned to quiet fear before it arose, to recognize anger before it controlled him, and to let the Force guide his judgment instead of his emotions.

His lightsaber became an extension of his body.

His connection to the Force deepened until even Yoda occasionally smiled at his progress.

One quiet morning, as mist drifted between the towering roots, Luke completed another sparring session.

His blue lightsaber hummed as it met Yoda's green blade.

The exchange was swift.

Luke attacked with confidence.

Yoda answered with precision.

For several minutes neither found an opening.

Finally, Yoda deactivated his weapon.

Luke lowered his own.

"Was there something wrong with my technique?"

"No."

"My balance?"

"No."

Luke frowned.

"Then why did we stop?"

Yoda smiled.

"Because finished... your training is."

Luke blinked.

"What?"

Yoda leaned upon his cane.

"A Jedi Knight you are."

For a long moment Luke said nothing.

After everything he had endured...

The endless failures.

The exhausting lessons.

The difficult choices.

It was over.

Obi-Wan Kenobi's shimmering spirit appeared beside them.

"I told you that patience would matter."

Luke smiled.

"I almost didn't believe you."

"But you stayed."

Luke looked at both of his teachers.

"I couldn't have done it without either of you."

Yoda chuckled softly.

"Much accomplished, you have."

Luke bowed deeply.

"Thank you... Master."

Not long afterward, Luke, Yoda, and R2-D2 lifted off from Dagobah aboard Luke's X-wing, leaving the swamp world behind for the first time in a year.

The Rebel Alliance welcomed them with astonishment.

Luke's calm confidence was unmistakable.

Even veteran commanders noticed the difference.

General Madine remarked quietly to Admiral Ackbar, "He's not the same young man who arrived here before."

Ackbar gave a slow nod.

"No. He carries himself like a Jedi."

The Alliance had little time for celebration.

Fresh intelligence from Rebel spies had revealed the location of the shield generator protecting the Empire's newest battle station.

The generator was hidden on the forest moon of Endor.

As long as it remained active, the Death Star II could not be destroyed.

Mon Mothma addressed the assembled commanders.

"The Empire believes this battle station will secure its rule over the galaxy. We cannot allow it to become fully operational."

A projection of the moon rotated above the briefing table.

"Our ground forces will destroy the shield generator."

The image shifted to the immense battle station hanging in orbit.

"Once the shield falls, our fleet will attack immediately."

She looked around the room.

"This battle will decide the fate of the galaxy."

Preparations began at once.

The Rebel fleet emerged from hyperspace above Endor.

Hundreds of ships filled space.

The unfinished Death Star II loomed ahead like an enormous wound in the stars.

On the forest moon below, Leia Organa led the strike team assigned to destroy the shield generator.

Chewbacca marched beside her carrying his bowcaster.

C-3PO walked nervously behind them.

"Oh dear... I have a terrible feeling about this."

R2-D2 beeped confidently.

"You always say that," Leia said with a small smile.

The strike force advanced through Endor's towering forests until Imperial patrols discovered them.

Blaster fire erupted.

The Rebels took cover.

"We've lost the element of surprise!" one soldier shouted.

Leia fired back.

"Keep moving!"

The fighting intensified until unexpected cries echoed through the trees.

Hundreds of Ewoks emerged from the forest.

Small though they were, they attacked with astonishing determination.

When they saw C-3PO, many immediately fell to their knees.

The golden protocol droid looked around in confusion.

"Oh my..."

The Ewoks chanted excitedly.

Leia smiled.

"They like you."

Threepio cleared his throat awkwardly.

"My friends!"

The Ewoks looked up expectantly.

"We... must help these people."

He pointed toward the Rebels around him.

"They are our allies."

The Ewoks roared in agreement.

Within moments logs crashed into walkers.

Stone volleys rained from hidden slings.

Imperial formations collapsed beneath relentless attacks from every direction.

Leia shouted over the battle.

"Now!"

Chewbacca charged the bunker entrance.

Rebel commandos followed.

Explosives were planted.

Everyone sprinted clear.

The shield generator vanished beneath a tremendous explosion.

Flames burst into the forest sky.

Far above Endor, Admiral Ackbar watched the explosion bloom across the moon's surface.

"The shield is down!"

Cheers erupted across the Rebel fleet.

"All ships!"

Ackbar commanded.

"Attack the Death Star!"

The Rebel armada surged forward.

Among them flew the Millennium Falcon.

Lando Calrissian sat in the pilot's seat with complete concentration.

"Nien Nunb," he said, "let's show them what this ship can do."

The Falcon darted between turbolaser fire with impossible agility.

Around them, X-wings, A-wings, B-wings, and cruisers unleashed everything they possessed.

Meanwhile, hidden among the confusion, Luke's X-wing approached the Death Star itself.

Yoda sat behind him.

Luke guided the fighter into one of the unfinished hangars.

"We're here."

They landed.

The station's corridors were strangely quiet.

Neither Jedi needed directions.

The Force drew them onward.

Inside the Emperor's throne room, Darth Vader stood motionless.

Beside him waited Emperor Palpatine.

Both had already sensed the approaching Jedi.

The massive doors opened.

Luke and Yoda entered together.

Luke stepped forward.

"It ends today."

Palpatine smiled coldly.

"It certainly does."

Vader reached for his weapon.

With a sharp snap-hiss, his red lightsaber ignited.

Luke answered immediately.

His blue blade illuminated the chamber.

Yoda ignited his green lightsaber.

Then, to Luke's surprise, Palpatine also drew a lightsaber.

A crimson blade burst to life.

Without another word, the battle began.

The throne room exploded into motion.

Luke met Vader head-on.

Blue and red blades crashed together in a shower of sparks.

Every strike came with tremendous force.

Vader's relentless power met Luke's refined precision.

Neither yielded.

Nearby, Yoda became a blur of green light.

Palpatine answered with astonishing speed, his red blade weaving intricate patterns while arcs of Force lightning burst from his free hand.

Yoda absorbed the energy upon his lightsaber before redirecting it harmlessly aside.

The duel spread throughout the chamber.

Massive pieces of machinery tore free under telekinetic assaults.

Platforms buckled.

Railings twisted.

Luke reached out with the Force, hurling shattered metal toward Vader.

Vader caught it in midair and sent it flying back.

Luke leaped clear.

Palpatine unleashed another torrent of lightning.

Yoda answered by lifting entire sections of the floor, forcing the Emperor to cut through them before they struck.

Minutes became what felt like hours.

Neither side could gain the advantage.

Eventually the combat naturally separated.

Luke and Vader battled across the upper platforms.

Every exchange tested skill, endurance, and concentration.

Neither spoke.

Neither wasted movement.

Elsewhere Yoda and Palpatine fought with impossible speed.

Their lightsabers flashed through the air while invisible waves of telekinetic force shattered walls and hurled enormous debris throughout the throne room.

Neither ancient master gave ground.

The duel became a stalemate.

Finally, as the battle shifted again, both pairs converged once more.

The throne room descended into chaos.

Blue, green, and twin red blades crossed in every direction.

Lightning flashed between combatants.

The Force shook the chamber itself.

Luke blocked Vader's heavy strikes while Yoda intercepted Palpatine.

Moments later they switched opponents instinctively.

Every opening lasted only an instant before someone closed it.

The battle seemed endless.

Then Yoda saw the opportunity.

"Luke!"

Luke understood immediately.

Master and student attacked together.

Vader blocked Luke's strike.

Yoda's green blade slipped beneath his guard.

Vader staggered.

Luke's blue lightsaber followed through.

The Dark Lord fell.

Silence lasted only a heartbeat.

Palpatine attacked with furious intensity.

Force lightning erupted across the chamber.

Luke and Yoda stood together.

They advanced one measured step at a time despite the storm of energy.

Yoda deflected part of the lightning.

Luke pushed the remainder aside with the Force.

Palpatine struck with his lightsaber.

Both Jedi answered together.

Blue and green overwhelmed red.

The Emperor stumbled backward.

A final coordinated assault ended the duel.

Palpatine collapsed beside Vader.

The throne room fell silent.

Luke extinguished his lightsaber.

He looked toward Yoda.

"We have to go."

Yoda nodded.

"Hurry."

Throughout the Death Star, Imperial personnel were already descending into confusion.

Reports flooded every command station.

"The Emperor is dead!"

"Lord Vader has fallen!"

"No orders from central command!"

"What do we do?"

Officers argued.

Captains issued conflicting commands.

Entire sections of the station became paralyzed by uncertainty.

Luke and Yoda reached an escape pod bay without serious resistance.

The hatch sealed.

The pod blasted free from the battle station and descended toward Endor.

By then, the destruction of the shield generator had transformed the battle in space.

Admiral Ackbar watched as the Death Star struggled to coordinate its defenses.

"Keep pressing the attack!"

The Rebel fleet obeyed.

Heavy cruisers unleashed repeated broadsides.

Fighter squadrons poured fire into unfinished sections of the station.

Turbolaser batteries answered unevenly.

Entire Imperial formations reacted too slowly or not at all.

Lando guided the Millennium Falcon through exploding debris.

"They're falling apart!"

Another Rebel pilot laughed over the comm.

"Keep hitting them!"

The bombardment intensified.

The Death Star's defenses weakened further with every passing minute.

Unable to organize an effective response amid the chaos consuming its command structure, the massive battle station absorbed blow after blow.

Finally, brilliant explosions raced across its unfinished superstructure.

One detonation triggered another.

Then another.

The entire station disappeared inside a blinding sphere of fire.

The explosion expanded into space, consuming the Death Star completely.

Cheers erupted across every Rebel frequency.

"The Death Star's gone!"

"We did it!"

"The battle station has been destroyed!"

On the forests of Endor, Luke's escape pod settled safely among the trees.

The hatch opened.

Luke stepped out first.

Yoda followed.

Not long afterward, the Millennium Falcon descended into the Rebel landing zone.

Its ramp lowered.

Lando emerged smiling.

Leia hurried toward him.

"You made it."

"So did you."

Chewbacca let out an enormous triumphant roar.

Threepio clasped his metallic hands together.

"Oh, what a marvelous day!"

Luke approached.

Lando extended his hand.

"Good to see you again, Jedi."

Luke shook it warmly.

"You too."

Around them, Rebel soldiers embraced one another.

Ewoks celebrated throughout the forest with songs, dancing, and enormous fires.

Mon Mothma arrived later to address the assembled Alliance.

"The Empire has suffered a devastating defeat."

Applause swept through the gathering.

She continued.

"Our struggle is not yet over."

The celebration quieted.

"But today has changed everything."

It had.

Across the galaxy, entire Imperial sectors began questioning orders from leaders who no longer agreed with one another.

World after world declared support for the Rebellion.

Imperial authority weakened month after month as resistance spread.

Eventually, representatives from liberated systems gathered together.

Before delegates from across the galaxy, Mon Mothma formally proclaimed the establishment of the New Republic.

She was unanimously chosen to serve as its inaugural Chancellor.

The announcement was greeted with celebration throughout countless worlds that had waited years for freedom.

Yet amid the victories, there remained an absence that no celebration could erase.

One evening, after another ceremony had ended, Leia sat quietly outside a temporary command center.

Chewbacca sat beside her.

Neither spoke for a long time.

Finally Leia looked toward the stars.

"I keep wondering where he is."

Chewbacca answered with a low, mournful rumble.

"I know."

She smiled sadly.

"We've won so much."

Chewbacca rested one hand on the ground between them.

"But Han isn't here."

The Wookiee looked upward into the night sky.

Leia followed his gaze.

Somewhere beyond those countless stars, Han Solo remained a prisoner.

Neither of them had forgotten him.

# Part 3

The forests of Endor had changed.

Where once they had hidden Rebel command posts and Imperial patrols, they now bustled with diplomats, engineers, soldiers, and representatives from liberated worlds. Until a permanent capital could be established, the moon served as the provisional heart of the New Republic.

One quiet afternoon, away from the settlements and celebrations, two old veterans walked beneath the towering trees.

Yoda leaned upon his cane.

Chewbacca matched his slow pace.

For a long while they simply enjoyed the peaceful sounds of the forest.

Finally Chewbacca gave a thoughtful rumbling growl.

Yoda smiled.

"Kashyyyk, thinking of, are you?"

Chewbacca nodded, letting out another series of deep growls.

"Yes... remember, I do."

Yoda's eyes seemed to look far beyond Endor's trees.

"Many years ago. Different, the galaxy was."

Chewbacca rumbled warmly.

"Brave, the Wookiees were. Fiercely, they fought."

Chewbacca let out an amused growl.

"Hmm?" Yoda chuckled. "Old already, I seemed to you?"

Chewbacca answered with a playful bark.

"Liar, you are."

The Wookiee laughed in his own booming way.

Their conversation drifted from one memory to another.

The beaches of Kashyyyk.

The towering wroshyr trees.

The desperate fighting.

Old comrades who had never returned home.

Chewbacca lowered his head at several names.

Yoda quietly bowed his own.

"Forgotten, none of them are."

Chewbacca's next growl carried gratitude.

"Much honor, your people showed."

They continued walking until they reached a quiet clearing where sunlight filtered through the leaves.

Chewbacca stopped.

His shoulders slumped.

After a long silence, he gave a low, mournful growl.

Then another.

Then a longer one.

His voice carried sadness.

Hope.

Determination.

It almost sounded like a plea.

Yoda listened without interrupting.

When Chewbacca finally fell silent, Yoda closed his eyes.

"So."

He nodded slowly.

"For Han, you ask."

Chewbacca looked directly into Yoda's eyes and gave one firm growl.

"Our friendship... you invoke."

Another growl.

"One final favor."

Chewbacca lowered his head.

Yoda stood quietly for several moments.

Then he smiled.

"Help you... I will."

Chewbacca looked up immediately.

His roar echoed through the forest.

Yoda laughed.

"Happier than this old Jedi, you sound."

The next morning, before sunrise, Yoda departed Endor alone aboard a small shuttle.

No escort followed.

No announcement was made.

Only a handful of friends knew where he had gone.

As the stars stretched into hyperspace, Yoda rested quietly in the pilot's seat.

"So long..."

He looked out into the endless tunnel of light.

"Many years, lived I have."

His hands rested peacefully upon his cane.

"Strong, the Force remains."

His voice became almost a whisper.

"My final journey among the living... this is."

He smiled gently.

"Ready, I am."

Hours later the deserts of Tatooine filled the viewport.

The shuttle descended toward Jabba's sprawling palace.

Yoda stepped onto the burning sands and slowly approached the great entrance.

The Gamorrean guards crossed their weapons.

One snorted.

The other pointed toward the desert.

Yoda looked up at them.

"Pass."

The guards laughed.

One shoved him backward.

Yoda sighed.

"Very well."

He raised one hand.

With a sharp snap of his fingers, both guards instantly collapsed unconscious onto the sand.

The great doors remained shut.

Without touching them, Yoda extended his hand.

The massive stone entrance slowly groaned open.

Inside, more guards rushed forward.

A Weequay drew his blaster.

Yoda tilted one finger.

The weapon flew from the guard's hands.

Another lunged with a vibro-axe.

A gentle wave of Yoda's hand sent him tumbling harmlessly across the corridor.

Two Nikto fired together.

Their blasters twisted upward through the Force.

The bolts struck the ceiling.

A Gamorrean charged.

Yoda looked at him.

"You wish to sleep."

The guard blinked once.

Then collapsed where he stood.

More guards poured from side passages.

One after another they found themselves stunned, gently hurled aside, frozen in place, or calmly convinced that there was suddenly something far more important elsewhere in the palace.

Yoda never once reached for his lightsaber.

He simply walked.

Every obstacle disappeared before him.

The deeper he traveled into the palace, the more frantic the defenders became.

None slowed him for more than a moment.

Eventually the great audience chamber came into view.

Jabba the Hutt sat upon his dais, surrounded by his remaining attendants.

The enormous crime lord narrowed his eyes.

Yoda walked calmly to the center of the chamber.

"Release Han Solo."

Jabba answered with a deep, mocking laugh.

He barked an order in Huttese.

"No."

Yoda looked directly into Jabba's eyes.

"You will release him."

For a brief moment the Force reached toward Jabba's mind.

Then it slipped away.

Jabba grinned wider.

Unaffected.

"Hmm."

Yoda nodded once.

"Strong-minded, you are."

Jabba's massive hand drifted toward a hidden control.

Before his fingers reached it, Yoda had already moved.

The floor exploded open exactly where he had been standing.

The old Jedi was already several meters away.

Jabba snarled and slapped another control.

A section of ceiling crashed downward.

Yoda had already leaped clear.

Dust settled around empty stone.

Jabba growled furiously.

Then he slammed his palm onto a large crimson button.

An alarm screamed throughout the palace.

Hidden doors burst open.

Passageways slid aside.

Within seconds dozens upon dozens of armed guards flooded into the audience chamber from every direction.

Blasters were raised.

Weapons surrounded Yoda completely.

The old Jedi finally unclipped the lightsaber from his belt.

Its green blade sprang to life.

The chamber became a blur.

The guards barely had time to react.

Green light flashed between them faster than their eyes could follow.

Blasters flew apart.

Weapons spun across the floor.

One guard after another collapsed unconscious.

Others found themselves disarmed before they even realized Yoda had reached them.

Several simply spun harmlessly through the air under invisible telekinetic force before landing in untidy heaps.

The entire ambush ended within seconds.

Silence returned.

Smoke drifted lazily through the chamber.

The guards lay scattered across the floor.

Yoda stood exactly where he had begun.

His lightsaber hummed quietly.

Jabba stared in disbelief.

The green blade disappeared.

Yoda looked up.

"Finished?"

Jabba slowly raised both hands.

He barked an order.

Servants hurried away.

Moments later Han Solo's carbonite block was brought into the audience chamber.

Yoda approached it.

He placed both hands upon the frozen prison.

Carefully, patiently, he began reversing the freezing process.

Behind him, Jabba's eyes narrowed.

One remaining hidden guard quietly took aim.

The instant before the trigger was pulled, Yoda spoke without turning around.

"No."

The blaster flew sideways under the Force.

Its shot discharged wildly.

The bolt struck Jabba squarely in the chest.

The enormous Hutt jerked violently.

His laughter ceased forever.

The chamber fell completely silent.

The surviving attendants stared in horror.

No one moved.

Yoda simply continued thawing Han.

At last the carbonite released its prisoner.

Han collapsed forward, gasping.

"I... can't..."

His eyes remained unfocused.

"I'm blind..."

Yoda caught him gently with the Force before he could fall.

"Temporary, it is."

Han struggled to breathe.

"Who... who's there?"

"Come."

Han felt himself slowly rise into the air.

He blinked uselessly.

His feet no longer touched the ground.

Invisible hands carried him through the palace.

No one attempted to interfere.

The palace had fallen silent.

Outside, Yoda floated Han aboard the shuttle.

Soon afterward, the ship lifted away from Tatooine.

For much of the journey Han rested quietly.

Little by little his vision began returning.

Shapes emerged.

Colors.

Light.

Finally he blinked several times and looked toward the cockpit.

A tiny green figure sat calmly at the controls.

Han stared.

"...You're kidding."

Yoda looked over his shoulder.

"No."

Han rubbed his eyes.

"So... you're the one who rescued me?"

"Yes."

Han laughed in disbelief.

"I was expecting..."

He gestured vaguely.

"I don't know. An army."

"No army."

"You went into Jabba's palace?"

"Yes."

"Alone?"

"Yes."

Han stared for several more seconds.

"...You're serious."

"Hmm."

Han leaned back.

"Who are you?"

"Yoda."

"Yoda?"

"Yes."

Han waited.

"That's it?"

"Enough, it is."

Han frowned.

"You aren't going to explain anything?"

"When Endor you reach, your friends will."

Han looked at the little green Jedi for another moment.

Then he shrugged.

"You know what? Fine."

He laughed again.

"I don't think anything surprises me anymore."

The ship continued through hyperspace.

Eventually Han spoke again.

"So... how exactly did you get me out?"

Yoda answered without hesitation.

"The Force."

Han looked out the viewport.

"The Force..."

He shook his head slowly.

"I used to think it was just some old story."

Yoda remained silent.

Han smiled to himself.

"I've really been an idiot."

He looked back toward Yoda.

"If the Force can walk into Jabba's palace alone... and walk back out carrying me..."

He let out a quiet laugh.

"I was wrong."

Yoda smiled.

"Learned something, you have."

The shuttle descended through Endor's skies.

Word spread quickly across the settlement.

"They're back!"

The landing ramp lowered.

Han stepped outside.

For a heartbeat he simply looked around.

Then Chewbacca charged toward him with an enormous roar.

Han barely remained standing as the Wookiee wrapped him in a crushing embrace.

"Easy! Easy!"

Chewbacca laughed joyfully.

"I missed you too, pal."

Leia reached them next.

She stopped only long enough to make certain he was real.

Then she embraced him tightly.

Han smiled.

"Hey."

She looked up at him.

"Took you long enough."

"I had transportation issues."

Lando arrived grinning.

"Welcome back."

Han pointed at him.

"I've got questions."

"So do I."

Luke walked over smiling.

"It's good to have you back."

Han looked at him.

"You look... different."

Luke laughed softly.

"It's been a busy year."

R2-D2 whistled excitedly.

"There you are, buddy."

Han patted the astromech.

Threepio approached with perfect dignity.

"Captain Solo! It is simply wonderful to see you again!"

Han smiled.

"It's good to see you too, Goldenrod."

That evening the friends gathered together beneath Endor's towering trees.

There was food.

Music.

Stories.

Laughter.

Han repeatedly asked everyone about Yoda.

Each answer only made him stare at the little Jedi with greater amazement.

"You mean he fought in wars before I was even born?"

Luke nodded.

"Long before."

Han looked toward Yoda.

"And he never mentioned any of that."

Yoda smiled.

"Asked, you did not."

As the evening gradually quieted, Yoda rose slowly to his feet.

The conversation faded.

Everyone turned toward him.

"My friends..."

Luke immediately noticed something different.

"Master?"

Yoda looked at him kindly.

"Old, I am."

No one spoke.

"My time has come."

Luke stepped forward.

"No."

Yoda raised one small hand.

"Listen."

Luke stopped.

Yoda turned toward Luke and Leia together.

"A truth... you must know."

Luke and Leia exchanged puzzled looks.

"You are siblings."

Silence fell.

Leia stared.

"What?"

Luke blinked.

"Siblings?"

Yoda nodded.

"Twins."

Neither could find words.

Then Yoda continued.

"Your father... Darth Vader was."

The silence became even deeper.

Han slowly looked from Luke to Leia.

"No..."

Lando's eyes widened.

Leia struggled to speak.

"How... how is that possible?"

Yoda shook his head gently.

"Long is the story."

He pointed toward R2-D2.

"Memories, he carries."

The little astromech beeped.

"Recordings from many years before your birth."

Luke looked down at R2.

"You knew?"

R2 gave a quiet series of electronic tones.

"Ask him, you can."

Yoda's gaze shifted to Leia.

"Strong in the Force... you are."

Leia looked at him uncertainly.

"I am?"

"As strong as Luke."

Luke looked at his sister with surprise.

"I never knew."

"Nor did she."

Yoda smiled.

"Train her, you must."

Luke looked at Leia.

She met his eyes.

After everything that had happened, there was no hesitation.

"I'll do it."

Leia nodded.

"I'll learn."

Luke turned back toward Yoda.

"Master, there's still so much I need to ask—"

He stopped.

Yoda was smiling peacefully.

"Luminous beings are we."

His form began to fade.

"Not this crude matter."

"Master!" Luke stepped forward.

Yoda's smile never left him.

"The Force... with you... always."

His body vanished.

Only his robe remained upon the ground.

A gentle breeze stirred the empty cloth.

No one spoke.

Above them, the forests of Endor stood silent beneath the stars.

# Epilogue

In the days following Yoda's passing, Luke Skywalker, Leia Organa, and Han Solo gathered each evening around R2-D2.

The little astromech had become unusually quiet after Yoda's final request.

Luke knelt beside him.

"R2."

The droid answered with a questioning whistle.

"Master Yoda said you have recordings."

R2 emitted a series of beeps before extending a small holographic projector.

A tiny blue image flickered to life.

One recording became another.

Then another.

For days, the three of them watched history unfold through R2-D2's faithful memory.

They saw a young slave boy on Tatooine.

"So that's Anakin Skywalker," Han murmured.

The cheerful child smiled brightly as he worked on machines twice his size.

"He was just a kid," Leia whispered.

Another recording showed a solemn farewell as Anakin left his mother behind.

Luke watched silently.

The images changed.

A younger Obi-Wan Kenobi stood beside a teenage Anakin carrying a training lightsaber.

"He became Obi-Wan's apprentice," Luke said quietly.

R2 projected scenes of difficult lessons.

Failures.

Victories.

Years passed within the recordings.

Then came another series.

The Clone Wars.

Anakin Skywalker led clone troopers into battle.

He fought with remarkable courage.

He rescued civilians.

He laughed with his fellow soldiers.

Time after time he risked his own life to save others.

Han folded his arms.

"He was one incredible pilot."

"He was," Luke agreed.

More recordings followed.

A woman appeared.

Padmé Amidala.

The way she and Anakin looked at one another needed no explanation.

Leia smiled softly.

"They loved each other."

The recordings showed stolen moments together.

Private conversations.

Small smiles exchanged during dangerous times.

Then the images darkened.

Arguments.

Fear.

Anakin became increasingly troubled.

His frustration grew.

His anger became easier to awaken.

Luke watched in complete silence.

The final recordings were the hardest.

Anakin pledged himself to the dark side.

The Jedi Temple burned.

Friends became enemies.

The Republic collapsed.

No one spoke for a long time.

Finally another image appeared.

A medical facility on Polis Massa.

Padmé lay weakly upon a bed.

Obi-Wan Kenobi stood nearby.

Yoda was present.

Bail Organa waited quietly.

Then came the cries of newborn children.

Luke looked toward Leia.

Neither spoke.

Padmé smiled weakly as she looked upon them.

She whispered something too faint for R2's recording to capture clearly.

Moments later, she closed her eyes for the last time.

Leia reached over and quietly took Luke's hand.

The final recording showed Obi-Wan, Bail Organa, and Yoda speaking together.

"The twins must be separated," Obi-Wan said.

Bail nodded solemnly.

"I will raise the girl as my own daughter."

"I will take the boy to Tatooine," Obi-Wan answered.

Yoda lowered his head.

"Hidden, they must remain."

Another image appeared.

Captain Antilles stood beside C-3PO.

"The protocol droid's memory should be erased."

Threepio protested.

"Oh, really?"

The recording ended abruptly.

Silence filled the room.

Threepio looked around nervously.

"My goodness..."

Han slowly exhaled.

"So that's what happened."

Leia wiped away a tear.

"My mother..."

Luke looked down.

"Our father wasn't always Darth Vader."

"No," Leia replied.

"He wasn't."

Luke remained thoughtful.

"He fell because fear became stronger than hope."

Leia nodded.

"We won't."

Luke looked at his sister.

"No matter what happens."

"Never."

They clasped hands.

"We'll never walk that path."

Not long afterward, a small ceremony was held beneath Endor's towering trees.

There were no grand crowds.

No elaborate celebrations.

Only close friends.

Luke stood beside Han.

Lando smiled warmly nearby.

Chewbacca towered proudly behind them.

R2-D2 whistled happily.

C-3PO polished himself one final time before the ceremony began.

Leia wore a simple white gown.

Han looked at her with an expression that needed no words.

"You look beautiful."

"You clean up surprisingly well."

Han laughed.

"I had help."

Luke smiled.

"I think you're both ready."

Han reached for Leia's hands.

"I've never really been good at speeches."

"I noticed."

"But there's nowhere else I'd rather be."

Leia smiled.

"I feel the same."

They exchanged quiet vows before their closest friends.

When it was done, Chewbacca's joyful roar echoed throughout the forest.

Lando applauded enthusiastically.

Threepio dabbed at his photoreceptors.

"Oh dear... I do believe I'm becoming emotional."

Life gradually settled into peace.

Luke soon began Leia's Jedi training.

It differed greatly from the path Yoda had chosen for him.

There were still demanding exercises.

Meditation.

Lightsaber practice.

Lessons about the Force.

But Luke rarely pushed Leia beyond her limits.

Instead he encouraged patience.

Questions.

Understanding.

One afternoon they stood together in a quiet clearing.

Leia frowned as several stones hovered uncertainly before her.

"I keep forcing it."

Luke smiled.

"Then stop forcing it."

"Easy for you to say."

He picked up a small leaf.

"When I was training with Yoda, I thought strength meant making the Force obey me."

Leia listened carefully.

"It doesn't."

He released the leaf.

The wind carried it gently upward.

"The Force isn't something to command."

Leia closed her eyes.

She slowed her breathing.

The stones rose again.

This time they floated effortlessly.

Luke smiled.

"There."

Leia opened one eye.

"I barely did anything."

"Exactly."

Across the galaxy, the New Republic continued its advance.

Imperial strongholds fell one after another.

After a major victory, New Republic forces entered Chandrila.

Its liberation marked a turning point.

Mon Mothma addressed the Senate.

"Today we establish Chandrila as the permanent capital of the New Republic."

Thunderous applause filled the chamber.

Endor's role as the provisional capital came to a peaceful close.

Years of warfare slowly gave way to reconstruction.

One by one, Imperial governors surrendered.

Fleets laid down their arms.

At last, after years of conflict, the Empire formally surrendered to the New Republic.

The Galactic Civil War had finally come to an end.

Not every victory was military.

After Kashyyyk was liberated, Han and Chewbacca stood together beneath the towering wroshyr trees.

Han looked around at the Wookiees welcoming their old friend home.

He smiled.

"You know..."

Chewbacca looked down at him.

Han placed a hand on the Wookiee's arm.

"You've repaid that life debt so many times I've lost count."

Chewbacca gave a questioning growl.

"You don't owe me anything anymore."

The Wookiee remained silent.

Han smiled warmly.

"If you want to stay here with your family..."

He gestured toward the gathered Wookiees.

"You've earned it."

Chewbacca looked toward his family.

Then back toward Han.

He slowly wrapped his old friend in one last enormous embrace.

Han laughed.

"I'll miss you too."

Chewbacca gave one final affectionate roar.

Han watched him walk toward his waiting family.

He smiled all the way until the Wookiee disappeared among the giant trees.

Around the same time, Bespin was finally freed from the last Imperial occupation forces.

Lando Calrissian returned home.

Standing once more upon a familiar balcony overlooking Cloud City, he smiled at the endless orange clouds below.

An aide approached.

"Welcome home, Baron Administrator."

Lando nodded.

"It's good to be back."

Peace brought new choices.

Leia found herself increasingly divided between two callings.

The New Republic frequently sought her advice.

The Senate respected her judgment.

Many hoped she would devote herself entirely to rebuilding the government.

One evening she sat beside Luke overlooking wide plains.

"I've been thinking."

Luke waited quietly.

"I can't do everything."

"No."

"The Republic needs good leaders."

"It does."

Leia looked toward the sunset.

"But it already has them."

She smiled faintly.

"Mon Mothma."

She counted on her fingers.

"So many senators."

"So many diplomats."

Luke nodded.

"It isn't short of politicians."

She turned toward him.

"But there are only two known Jedi."

Luke smiled.

"You've already decided."

"I have."

She drew a deep breath.

"I want to dedicate my life to the Jedi."

Luke's smile widened.

"I was hoping you'd say that."

Together they continued training.

Year after year Leia's understanding of the Force deepened.

She became calm in meditation.

Precise with her lightsaber.

Wise in judgment.

Eventually Luke found fewer and fewer corrections to offer.

One afternoon, after another training session, Leia lowered her lightsaber.

"You didn't interrupt me once."

Luke smiled.

"I didn't need to."

They stood quietly for a moment.

Then Luke looked across the open landscape.

"The Jedi shouldn't end with us."

Leia nodded immediately.

"No."

"We need somewhere to begin again."

She thought for a long moment.

"What about Ossus?"

Luke looked at her.

"The old Jedi world."

"It has a long history."

Luke smiled.

"It feels right."

Stone by stone, they began building a new Jedi Temple upon the ancient world.

It was far from the great population centers.

Far from politics.

Far from the temptations of power.

A place devoted to learning.

Reflection.

Balance.

Luke, Leia, and Han made their home there.

Han never pretended to understand every lesson Luke and Leia discussed.

Whenever conversations became especially philosophical, he would grin.

"I'll leave the Force to you two."

Leia would laugh.

"And who keeps fixing the roof after every training accident?"

Han folded his arms proudly.

"Exactly."

R2-D2 remained as loyal as ever.

The little astromech still carried the memories of generations.

C-3PO continued faithfully serving the family, though he often insisted that the Jedi Temple would be much tidier if everyone simply followed proper organizational procedures.

Life settled into a quiet rhythm.

Morning lessons.

Shared meals.

Friendly conversations beneath Ossus' skies.

The burdens of war gradually gave way to peace.

The galaxy had been scarred by conflict, but it had been given another chance.

And on the ancient world of Ossus, a brother and sister carried forward the teachings of the Jedi, determined that the mistakes of the past would never be repeated.
