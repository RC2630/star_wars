# Part 1

The celebration on Yavin had lasted long into the night.

Pilots sang loudly over stolen Imperial liquor. Mechanics danced on supply crates. Someone had painted a crude starbird across the wall beside the hangar entrance. Every corridor of the hidden base carried the strange, disbelieving energy of people who had expected to die only hours earlier.

Luke Skywalker should have been among them.

Instead, he sat alone in a quiet observation alcove near the upper levels of the temple, staring through ancient stone arches into the jungle darkness beyond. The medal from Princess Leia rested around his neck, cold against his chest.

The Death Star was gone.

Ben was avenged.

The Rebellion had survived.

Everything he had dreamed about while staring at Tatooine’s twin suns had somehow happened all at once.

Yet the victory felt strangely hollow.

Han Solo was gone. The Millennium Falcon had vanished from Yavin almost immediately after the battle. No farewell. No explanation. Just gone into hyperspace with Chewbacca and the reward money.

Luke told himself he didn’t care.

He had other things now.

Purpose. Destiny.

And Leia.

Footsteps approached softly behind him.

“You disappeared,” Leia said.

Luke turned. She had changed out of her ceremonial gown into a simple white jacket and dark trousers, though her hair still carried traces of the elaborate braids from the ceremony. Without the royal formality, she looked younger. Tired, too.

“I needed quiet,” Luke admitted.

Leia leaned against the stone beside him. For a while neither spoke.

Outside, distant jungle insects hummed beneath the stars.

“It’s strange,” Leia finally said. “Everyone’s celebrating, but all I can think about is Alderaan.”

Luke looked down.

“I know.”

“No,” she said softly. “You really don’t.”

He almost argued, then stopped himself. She was right. He had lost his aunt and uncle. It hurt more than he could describe. But Leia had watched an entire world die.

After a moment, she exhaled shakily.

“I keep thinking I should have done more.”

“You did everything.”

“That’s easy for you to say. You destroyed the Death Star.”

Luke gave a faint smile.

“Barely.”

“But you did it.”

Their eyes met.

For the first time since they had escaped the Death Star, there was no emergency around them. No alarms. No battle plans. No fear of being overheard.

Just silence.

Leia stepped closer.

“When I saw you flying toward that station…” she said quietly, “I thought you were going to die.”

Luke’s heartbeat quickened.

“I thought the same thing about you back there.”

She laughed softly through her exhaustion.

“You came back for me anyway.”

“You would’ve done the same.”

“Yes,” she admitted. “I would have.”

The distance between them disappeared almost without either noticing.

Luke touched her hand first.

Leia did not pull away.

The kiss was hesitant at first—uncertain, searching—but after weeks of danger and grief and impossible survival, restraint gave way to relief. To closeness. To desperately needing someone alive to hold onto.

Then came an explosive electronic SHRIEK.

R2-D2 let out a scandalized series of beeps so loud they echoed through the stone chamber.

Luke and Leia jerked apart.

“Artoo!” Luke exclaimed.

The astromech rolled forward aggressively, dome swiveling back and forth with furious indignation. More sharp whistles erupted from his vocabulator.

Leia blinked at him.

“What is wrong with him?”

“I don’t know,” Luke said. “Artoo, what—”

The droid interrupted with another furious chirp.

Then his projector lens extended.

A blue holographic image flickered into existence between them.

Luke frowned.

“Artoo?”

The image stabilized.

A medical chamber appeared in shimmering blue light.

Luke and Leia watched in confusion as figures materialized within the recording: Obi-Wan Kenobi, Yoda, Bail Organa… and a woman lying exhausted on an operating platform.

Something about her face struck both of them instantly.

Not familiarity exactly.

Something deeper.

The hologram spoke.

“Don’t give up, Padmé,” Obi-Wan said gently.

The woman winced in pain.

A medical droid lifted a newborn infant.

“It’s a boy.”

The woman managed a weak smile.

“Luke…”

Luke froze.

The hologram continued.

“…and a girl.”

The woman struggled to speak.

“…Leia.”

Silence filled the alcove.

Luke and Leia slowly turned toward one another, confusion spreading across both faces.

R2-D2 emitted a smug little beep.

Then the next recording began automatically.

Obi-Wan leaned over the dying woman.

“You have twins, Padmé. They need you… hang on.”

“I can’t…”

Luke felt cold.

Leia’s expression tightened.

“There… is good in him,” Padmé whispered weakly. “I know there is… still…”

Her body went still.

Obi-Wan lowered his head.

The recording dissolved.

Leia stared at the empty air.

“No,” she whispered.

R2 projected another image before either could speak.

Now they saw Yoda, Obi-Wan, and Bail Organa seated together aboard a starship.

“Hidden, safe, the children must be kept,” Yoda said.

“We must take them somewhere the Sith will not sense their presence,” Obi-Wan replied.

“Split up, they should be.”

Bail Organa folded his hands.

“My wife and I will take the girl. We’ve always talked of adopting a baby girl. She will be loved with us.”

“And what of the boy?” Obi-Wan asked.

“To Tatooine,” Yoda said. “To his family, send him.”

“I will take the child and watch over him.”

Luke stopped breathing.

Leia looked physically ill.

The scene changed once more.

Bail Organa approached Captain Antilles in a starship corridor.

“I’m placing these droids in your care,” Bail said. “Treat them well. Clean them up. Have the protocol droid’s mind wiped.”

C-3PO’s horrified voice rang out:

“Oh, no!”

The hologram vanished.

R2-D2 gave several intensely satisfied beeps.

Then, without another explanation, he turned sharply and rolled away down the corridor.

Luke and Leia remained frozen beside one another.

Their hands were still touching.

Neither moved them.

Neither seemed able to.

Leia stared after the departing droid as if the universe itself had suddenly stopped making sense.

Luke’s voice emerged faintly.

“We…”

Leia slowly looked at him.

The truth settled over both of them in terrible silence.

Brother and sister.

The same mother.

The same father.

Every memory of the past weeks suddenly rearranged itself inside their minds with horrifying clarity.

The detention block.

The trash compactor.

The Falcon.

The ceremony.

The kiss from only seconds ago.

Leia abruptly pulled her hand away as though burned.

Luke stepped backward at the exact same moment.

For several seconds neither could even look directly at the other.

Finally Leia spoke, very quietly.

“Why didn’t anyone tell us?”

Luke swallowed hard, still staring at the floor.

“Ben knew.”

“Yes. And my father Bail too.”

Luke leaned heavily against the stone wall, dizzy.

The jungle sounds outside continued as if nothing in the galaxy had changed.

But everything had.

# Part 2

The revelation from R2-D2 remained one of the most closely guarded secrets in the Alliance.

Luke and Leia told almost no one.

There was no official announcement, no ceremony, no attempt to explain how the Princess of Alderaan and a farmboy from Tatooine had somehow turned out to be twins. The Alliance had larger concerns. The Empire was wounded, but far from defeated. Entire fleets were searching for Rebel bases. Every victory seemed to be followed by three new crises.

For a time, the twins simply focused on surviving.

Then Han Solo came back.

The Millennium Falcon dropped out of hyperspace near Yavin several weeks after the destruction of the Death Star.

When word spread through the base, Luke was among the first to reach the landing area.

The Falcon's ramp lowered.

Han Solo emerged with the familiar swagger of someone attempting to look far more confident than he actually felt.

Chewbacca followed behind him, rumbling something that sounded suspiciously amused.

Luke stared.

"You came back."

Han shrugged.

"Yeah, well."

"What happened?"

Han rubbed the back of his neck.

"Turns out finding Jabba the Hutt isn't always easy when half the galaxy's suddenly looking for you."

Luke laughed.

"So what now?"

Han looked around the Rebel base.

"Thought about it."

"And?"

"And I figured somebody around here needs a decent pilot."

Chewbacca barked loudly.

Han pointed at the Wookiee.

"Besides him."

Luke smiled.

For the first time since Han's departure, something felt complete again.

The reunion became considerably more awkward when Leia arrived.

The princess folded her arms immediately.

"So you came back."

Han grinned.

"I did."

Leia studied him carefully.

"Why?"

Han opened his mouth.

Stopped.

Thought better of whatever he had been about to say.

Then he sighed.

"Honestly?"

Leia raised an eyebrow.

"Honestly."

Han gestured vaguely.

"It doesn't matter anymore."

"What doesn't?"

Han glanced toward Luke.

Then back toward Leia.

Then shook his head.

"I left."

"Yes."

"You stayed."

"Yes."

Han shrugged.

"Pretty obvious how that story ends."

Leia frowned.

"What story?"

Han looked genuinely confused.

"The one where you pick Skywalker."

Luke nearly choked.

Leia blinked.

Han continued.

"I mean, come on. Kid destroys the Death Star. Saves the Rebellion. Can use the Force. Meanwhile I disappear with a bag of money."

"Han—"

"So whatever. I get it."

Leia stared at him.

For perhaps the first time since meeting Han Solo, she found herself speechless.

Han spread his hands.

"I don't have a chance."

Understanding finally dawned on Luke.

It dawned on Leia as well.

Neither could quite believe what they were hearing.

Han noticed their expressions.

"What?"

Leia suddenly laughed.

Han looked offended.

"What's funny?"

Luke laughed too.

Now Han looked deeply suspicious.

"Why are both of you laughing?"

Leia finally managed to regain control of herself.

"Han."

"What?"

"You did help rescue me from the Death Star."

"Yeah."

"You also helped Luke destroy the Death Star."

"Yeah."

"And leaving to try to settle your debt with Jabba was completely reasonable."

Han blinked.

"Wait."

A hopeful expression appeared.

"Really?"

"Really."

Han looked remarkably pleased.

Then Leia continued.

"Besides, Luke is my brother."

Han froze.

"What?"

"My twin brother."

Silence.

Han stared at her.

Then at Luke.

Then back at Leia.

Then back at Luke.

Then once more at Leia.

The realization hit him like a proton torpedo.

His shoulders visibly relaxed.

An expression of enormous relief spread across his face.

"Oh."

Luke folded his arms.

"That's all you've got?"

Han pointed a finger.

"No. No. That explains a lot."

Chewbacca released a tremendous roar that sounded suspiciously like laughter.

Han ignored him.

"Twin brother?"

Leia nodded.

Han thought for a moment.

Then pointed toward the departing Wookiee.

"He knew, didn't he?"

Chewbacca barked again.

Han groaned.

"Of course he did."

Despite the embarrassment, everyone present found themselves smiling.

The Alliance had regained one of its best pilots.

The Falcon was back.

And whatever complicated misunderstandings had existed before were now gone.

Mostly.

Afterward, Luke became increasingly focused on a different implication of R2-D2's recordings.

If they were truly twins, then perhaps his connection to the Force wasn't unique.

At first Leia dismissed the idea entirely.

"Luke, I spent my entire life in the Imperial Senate."

"So?"

"So nobody ever told me I had mystical powers."

"Ben didn't tell me either."

Leia crossed her arms.

"Your evidence is not encouraging."

Still, Luke persisted.

Eventually he convinced her to try.

The experiment took place aboard the Millennium Falcon while it was docked at a temporary Rebel outpost.

Luke carried Anakin Skywalker's lightsaber.

The weapon had become one of his most treasured possessions.

Now he handed it to Leia.

She accepted it carefully.

The blue blade ignited with a snap-hiss.

Leia examined it.

"I still think this is ridiculous."

Luke activated a training remote.

The small sphere floated into the air.

"You said that before I tried it too."

"Because it is ridiculous."

The remote fired.

Leia yelped and barely avoided the bolt.

Luke grinned.

"See?"

"I hate this already."

The remote fired again.

And again.

And again.

The first session was not particularly successful.

Leia accumulated several painful stings.

Luke accumulated several bruises after she blamed him for the exercise.

Yet she continued.

The next day.

And the day after that.

Slowly, subtle changes appeared.

She began reacting before the remote fired.

She sensed movement she couldn't see.

She developed an awareness that was difficult to describe but impossible to deny.

One afternoon Luke repeated the same exercise Obi-Wan had once used on him.

He lowered a blast shield over Leia's eyes.

She immediately objected.

"This is absurd."

"Trust me."

"I don't trust you."

"Trust the Force."

"That sounds even worse."

The remote fired.

Leia instinctively turned.

The bolt missed.

Both twins froze.

The remote fired again.

She dodged.

Again.

And again.

By the end of the session, she was consistently avoiding shots she physically could not see.

Leia slowly removed the helmet.

Neither sibling spoke for several seconds.

Then Leia said:

"Oh."

Luke smiled.

"Told you."

That discovery transformed the years that followed.

Neither of them possessed a proper teacher.

Obi-Wan was gone.

The mysterious Yoda mentioned by Ben's spirit-like voice during the Death Star battle remained little more than a name.

The Jedi Order itself had been extinct for decades.

There were no manuals waiting on library shelves.

No academies.

No masters.

Only two young people trying to reconstruct fragments of an ancient tradition from scattered clues.

So they experimented.

They meditated.

They practiced with the lightsaber.

They compared experiences.

Sometimes one would discover something first and teach the other.

Luke developed stronger telekinetic abilities.

Leia proved unusually skilled at sensing emotions and intentions.

Both learned to trust instincts that increasingly seemed impossible to explain through ordinary means.

Mistakes happened frequently.

Equipment broke.

Training remotes exploded.

At least one cargo crate accidentally ended up embedded in a hangar ceiling.

Han eventually stopped asking questions.

His standard response became:

"I don't want to know."

Chewbacca's response was usually laughter.

Year after year, the Alliance continued its war against the Empire.

Luke became one of the Rebellion's most celebrated pilots.

Leia grew into one of its most important leaders and strategists.

Han evolved from a reluctant ally into a respected Rebel officer, though he loudly denied enjoying any form of responsibility whenever someone mentioned it.

The three of them found themselves repeatedly thrown into missions together.

Rescues.

Raids.

Intelligence operations.

Supply runs.

Escapes.

Somehow, despite impossible odds, they kept surviving.

And in the quiet moments between battles, Luke and Leia continued their private training.

Neither knew exactly where the path would lead.

Neither claimed to understand the Force fully.

But they knew one thing with certainty.

They were not alone.

For the first time since the fall of the Jedi, two children of Anakin Skywalker were learning the ways of the Force together, one discovery at a time.

# Part 3

The Rebel Alliance's hidden base on Hoth had never truly felt safe.

The frozen world was remote, difficult to reach, and miserable to live on, which made it ideal for hiding from the Empire. Yet everyone understood that Imperial search fleets were constantly looking for them.

Three years had passed since the destruction of the Death Star.

Three years of raids, escapes, victories, and losses.

Three years during which Luke Skywalker and Leia Organa had quietly developed their Force abilities together.

Three years during which Han Solo had become one of the Alliance's most valuable officers.

And during those same years, the relationship between Han and Leia had steadily deepened.

What had begun as mutual attraction hidden behind arguments and sarcasm had eventually become impossible to deny.

By the time the Alliance settled on Hoth, they were already a couple in all but name.

Most people in the Alliance had noticed long before either of them openly acknowledged it.

Chewbacca certainly had.

Luke found the entire situation amusing.

Leia found Luke's amusement annoying.

Han found both siblings annoying.

Everything felt normal.

Then the Empire found them.

—

The evacuation of Hoth became a desperate race against time.

Luke was briefly separated from the base after being attacked by a wampa in the frozen wilderness. He survived only through a combination of stubbornness, luck, and the Force.

After recovering in a bacta tank, he began experiencing increasingly vivid encounters with Obi-Wan Kenobi's spirit.

One vision proved especially important.

The translucent image of Obi-Wan appeared before him.

"You must go to the Dagobah system."

"Dagobah?"

"There you will learn from Yoda, the Jedi Master who instructed me."

Luke immediately thought of Leia.

When he brought up the idea later, Leia was not enthusiastic.

"Absolutely not."

Luke blinked.

"What?"

"The Alliance needs me."

"The Alliance also needs Jedi."

Leia crossed her arms.

"We barely know what we're doing now."

"Exactly."

"That isn't helping your argument."

Luke persisted.

Days later, during evacuation preparations, he brought it up again.

And again.

And again.

Eventually Leia groaned.

"You are never going to stop asking, are you?"

"No."

Leia sighed heavily.

"The Rebellion needs leadership."

"And a fully trained Jedi would help even more."

Leia hated how reasonable that sounded.

There was another issue.

Han.

The thought of disappearing into some distant swamp world for an unknown period bothered her more than she wanted to admit.

Luke's response was characteristically direct.

"Leia."

"What?"

"Get over it."

Leia stared at him.

"You are incredibly annoying."

"That's what you always say before agreeing."

Several hours later she agreed.

—

The evacuation scattered Rebel forces across the galaxy.

Han, Chewbacca, and the Millennium Falcon escaped under heavy pursuit.

Luke and Leia departed separately toward Dagobah.

The siblings reunited there shortly afterward.

Their first impression of the planet was not favorable.

Dagobah was a swamp.

An endless, humid, muddy swamp.

Leia stepped out of the ship and immediately sank ankle-deep into mud.

"I hate it already."

Luke looked around.

"I think it's kind of peaceful."

Leia glared at him.

A nearby creature made a strange croaking noise.

Leia pointed.

"Even the wildlife disagrees with you."

—

Finding Yoda was considerably stranger than either of them expected.

The ancient Jedi Master was tiny.

Ancient.

Unpredictable.

And seemingly delighted by confusing both of them.

Yet beneath the eccentric behavior lay tremendous wisdom.

Yoda quickly confirmed what Luke had suspected.

Leia was Force-sensitive.

Strongly so.

The old Jedi regarded the twins thoughtfully.

"The children of Skywalker."

Neither Luke nor Leia entirely understood what expression crossed Yoda's face then.

Pride.

Sadness.

Hope.

Perhaps all three.

—

Training began immediately.

Luke continued practicing with his father's old lightsaber.

Leia faced a different challenge.

She had no weapon of her own.

Fortunately, Yoda had prepared for such possibilities long ago.

Deep within his humble dwelling, hidden for decades, he possessed a small collection of kyber crystals rescued from the destruction of the Jedi Order.

One crystal responded to Leia almost immediately.

Its glow was pale blue.

Brilliant.

Clear.

Alive.

Together, Yoda, Luke, and Leia assembled a lightsaber around it.

The construction process required patience and concentration.

Every component had to align perfectly.

Every piece had to become part of a greater whole.

When the weapon was complete, Leia activated it.

A light blue blade emerged with a sharp hum.

Leia stared at it.

Luke smiled.

Yoda nodded approvingly.

"Good. Very good."

For the first time, both twins possessed lightsabers.

For the first time, both were training as Jedi together.

—

Months passed.

The training was difficult.

Yoda pushed them relentlessly.

Physical exercises.

Meditation.

Control.

Discipline.

Understanding.

Luke often excelled through instinct.

Leia often excelled through focus.

Each balanced the other's weaknesses.

Together they progressed far more quickly than either could have alone.

Yet Yoda remained dissatisfied.

"Much to learn, both of you still have."

—

Elsewhere in the galaxy, Han Solo's situation deteriorated rapidly.

The Millennium Falcon's hyperdrive had been damaged during the escape from Hoth.

Imperial forces pursued relentlessly.

Unable to make the jump to hyperspace, Han eventually sought refuge in Cloud City on Bespin.

The floating metropolis was governed by an old associate of his: Lando Calrissian.

At first, the welcome appeared genuine.

Then everything went wrong.

Lando had already been pressured by the Empire.

Darth Vader was waiting.

Han Solo was captured.

Chewbacca was captured.

Lando realized too late the consequences of his cooperation.

And somewhere far away on Dagobah, two Jedi trainees suddenly felt a disturbance in the Force.

—

Luke jerked upright during meditation.

Leia did the same.

They looked at each other immediately.

Neither needed to speak.

Both had seen it.

Han.

Suffering.

Captured.

In danger.

The vision had been overwhelming.

Leia's face had gone pale.

"He needs help."

Luke nodded.

"Now."

Yoda was not pleased.

When they explained what they had seen, the old Jedi shook his head.

"Difficult to see. Always in motion is the future."

"He'll die if we don't go," Leia said.

"Perhaps."

Luke frowned.

"Then we're going."

Yoda looked between them.

"Not ready, you are."

"Maybe not," Luke replied.

"But Han is our friend."

Leia's expression hardened.

"I'm not leaving him."

The old Jedi closed his eyes.

For a long moment he said nothing.

Finally:

"Decide for yourselves, you must."

The twins had already decided.

Their training ended that day.

—

Cloud City became the site of catastrophe.

Luke and Leia arrived together.

Both carried lightsabers.

Both expected a rescue.

Instead they walked directly into Darth Vader's trap.

The confrontation unfolded within the industrial depths of the city.

Vader faced them alone.

Dark armor.

Black cape.

Mechanical breathing.

An overwhelming presence in the Force.

Luke and Leia fought together.

For a time their coordination surprised even Vader.

Years of training alongside one another allowed them to move almost instinctively.

Twin blue blades flashed through the darkness.

Yet Vader remained vastly more powerful.

He separated them.

Manipulated the environment.

Forced them apart.

Gradually the battle shifted in his favor.

Then came the revelation.

The one truth Vader had concealed for decades.

The one truth Obi-Wan had never spoken.

The duel reached a narrow platform overlooking a seemingly endless abyss.

Luke was exhausted.

Leia was struggling to rejoin the fight.

Vader advanced.

"There is no escape."

Luke raised his lightsaber.

Vader's blade crashed downward.

"You do not yet realize your importance."

Luke glared at him.

Vader's voice became quieter.

"Obi-Wan never told you what happened to your father."

"He told me enough."

"No."

Vader stepped forward.

"I am your father."

Everything stopped.

The words struck Luke like a physical blow.

For an instant he simply stared.

Then another voice cut through the silence.

Leia had finally reached the platform.

The realization hit her almost immediately.

If Vader was Luke's father—

"That makes you my father too."

For the first time since either twin had met Darth Vader, he appeared genuinely surprised.

The dark mask concealed much of it.

But the shock rippled clearly through the Force.

"You..."

Vader turned toward her.

Understanding arrived.

The princess.

Alderaan.

The prisoner he had interrogated years ago.

The young woman standing beside Luke.

His daughter.

He had never known.

Not once had he suspected.

Now everything suddenly made sense.

Vader stood motionless.

Luke and Leia both felt the emotional turbulence beneath the armor.

Shock.

Disbelief.

Wonder.

Pain.

Then the moment passed.

The battle resumed.

And Vader returned to being Darth Vader.

The outcome remained unchanged.

Luke fought desperately.

Vader overpowered him.

A final strike severed Luke's right hand.

Anakin Skywalker's lightsaber tumbled away into the darkness below.

Lost.

Luke collapsed.

The truth of Vader's revelation echoed through his mind.

Father.

Vader was his father.

Their father.

Before Vader could complete his attempt to turn them to the dark side, Luke and Leia escaped together.

Barely.

—

The aftermath was grim.

Han Solo had been frozen in carbonite and delivered to bounty hunter Boba Fett.

Boba Fett departed for Tatooine.

His destination was Jabba the Hutt.

Chewbacca survived.

Lando escaped with them and immediately dedicated himself to finding Han.

Luke survived but lost his hand.

A sophisticated prosthetic replaced it shortly afterward.

The mechanical fingers moved almost naturally.

Still, every glance at it reminded him of Cloud City.

Of Vader.

Of failure.

Of the truth.

—

Neither Luke nor Leia returned to Dagobah.

The war no longer allowed such luxuries.

There was too much to do.

Too many people depending on them.

Too many plans to make.

Too many resources to gather.

And somewhere on Tatooine, Han Solo remained imprisoned.

Rescuing him became a priority.

Not merely for Leia.

For all of them.

—

During the following year, the twins continued their Jedi training alone.

Once again they found themselves learning through experimentation and persistence.

Without Yoda's guidance, progress became slower.

But it never stopped.

Luke eventually constructed a new lightsaber.

The weapon's blade emerged green rather than blue.

A symbol of a new stage in his journey.

Leia continued wielding the light-blue saber she had built on Dagobah.

The two siblings sparred whenever time permitted.

Meditated together.

Compared insights.

Refined techniques.

Their understanding of the Force deepened steadily.

At the same time, they coordinated Rebel operations across the galaxy.

Battles.

Intelligence gathering.

Fleet movements.

Preparation.

And always, in the background, plans for Han Solo's rescue.

Because no matter how large the war became, neither Luke nor Leia intended to leave him in Jabba's hands.

Eventually the time for rescue would come.

And when it did, both Jedi twins would be ready.

# Part 4

The moons of Tatooine hung low over the Dune Sea as a lone figure approached Jabba the Hutt’s palace.

Massive stone walls rose from the desert like the remains of some ancient beast half-buried in sand. Torchfire flickered along the battlements. Somewhere deep within the fortress came the muffled echo of music, drunken laughter, and creatures growling in chains.

The armored bounty hunter paused before the great metal gate.

Two GAMORREAN GUARDS lowered their axes suspiciously.

The disguised figure spoke through a distorted voice filter.

“I seek an audience with Jabba the Hutt.”

The massive doors groaned open.

Inside, the palace stank of sweat, smoke, spilled liquor, and decay. Alien musicians played discordant music from a raised platform while Jabba’s court lounged in obscene luxury around the enormous Hutt.

Jabba’s yellow eyes narrowed as the armored hunter approached.

Beside the throne stood C-3PO, polished gold plating reflecting torchlight nervously.

The protocol droid translated.

“His Excellency wishes to know your business.”

The bounty hunter gestured toward the carbonite slab hauled in behind them.

“I bring Captain Solo.”

A ripple of excitement passed through the court.

Jabba laughed deeply, his immense body shaking with satisfaction.

Leia Organa lowered her head slightly beneath the helmet, disguising the fury in her eyes.

Han Solo hung frozen in carbonite nearby like a man trapped in nightmare.

Even after all this time, seeing him like that struck her harder than she expected.

Jabba barked orders in Huttese. Payment was arranged. The disguised Leia accepted the bounty with calculated calm while silently counting guards, exits, weapons, firing angles.

Everything Luke had taught her flowed instinctively now:
awareness,
patience,
control.

She could feel minds around her like currents in water.

Greed.
Cruelty.
Fear.

And beneath it all, Jabba himself:
immense, vile, gluttonous.

Leia waited.

Hours later, the palace darkened into uneasy silence.

One by one, drunken creatures collapsed into sleep around dying torches.

Leia slipped through the corridors unseen.

At last she reached the carbon-freezing chamber.

Han Solo’s slab stood upright against the wall.

Leia removed her helmet.

Cool air touched her face.

For a moment she simply stared at him.

So much pain. So much searching. So many months believing she might never see him alive again.

She touched the carbonite gently.

“Hold on,” she whispered.

Activating the controls, she stepped back as machinery hissed and steam erupted into the chamber.

The carbonite began to melt away.

Han sagged forward as the freezing shell released him.

Leia caught him before he collapsed completely.

“Just relax for a moment,” she said softly. “You’re free of the carbonite.”

Han touched his face weakly and groaned.

“Shhh,” Leia whispered. “You have hibernation sickness.”

“I can’t see.”

“Your eyesight will return in time.”

“Where am I?”

“Jabba’s palace.”

Han frowned weakly.

“Who are you?”

Leia smiled despite herself and lifted away the helmet.

“Someone who loves you.”

Han’s clouded eyes widened.

“Leia!”

“I gotta get you out of here.”

She helped him stand.

Then a deep, hideous laugh rolled through the chamber.

Han stiffened instantly.

“What’s that? I know that laugh.”

A curtain on the far side of the alcove pulled aside.

Jabba the Hutt lounged beyond it surrounded by guards, dancers, and grinning criminals. Bib Fortuna stood at his side while armed mercenaries closed in from every entrance.

Han muttered under his breath.

“Hey, Jabba. Look, Jabba, I was just on my way to pay you back, but I got a little sidetracked. It’s not my fault.”

“It’s too late for that, Solo,” Threepio translated anxiously. “You may have been a good smuggler, but now you’re Bantha fodder.”

“Look—”

“Take him away!”

Guards seized Han immediately.

Han struggled blindly.

“Jabba! I’ll pay you triple! You’re throwing away a fortune here. Don’t be a fool!”

Leia let them drag Han away.

Not because she was helpless.

Because she was waiting.

Lando Calrissian, disguised among Jabba’s guards, moved carefully toward her as another guard grabbed her arm.

Jabba’s heavy voice boomed again.

“Bring her to me.”

The court laughed cruelly as Leia was shoved forward.

“You have powerful friends,” Leia warned coldly. “You’re gonna regret this.”

Jabba’s massive tongue lolled as he chuckled.

“I’m sure.”

He leaned closer.

Leia turned her face away in disgust.

“Ugh.”

Threepio winced.

“Ohhh, I can’t bear to watch.”

Then everything changed.

Leia moved.

The Force exploded outward from her in a violent invisible wave.

Jabba’s guards flew backward across the chamber as though struck by a detonator blast. Bib Fortuna slammed into a pillar. Musicians scattered in panic. Lando ducked instinctively as overturned tables crashed across the floor.

Jabba himself lurched violently sideways on his throne with a startled roar.

The entire room fell silent.

Leia stood perfectly still amid the chaos.

Then a brilliant blue blade ignited in her hand with a violent SNAP-HISS.

Gasps erupted throughout the throne room.

The light-blue lightsaber cast sharp illumination across Leia’s face as she leveled the weapon directly at Jabba.

Even the Hutt looked stunned.

Several guards reached for blasters.

Leia’s voice cut through the chamber like a blade.

“No one moves.”

A Nikto mercenary fired anyway.

Leia turned effortlessly.

The blaster bolt ricocheted from her lightsaber into the ceiling in a shower of sparks.

Panic detonated across the room.

More guards opened fire.

Leia became motion itself.

Blue light spun through smoke-filled air as bolts deflected wildly around the throne room. One guard collapsed after his own shot rebounded into him. Another lost his weapon as Leia sliced the blaster cleanly in half.

Lando grinned suddenly.

“Well,” he muttered, drawing his hidden weapon, “that answers that.”

Chaos erupted.

Gamorreans charged with axes raised.

Leia thrust out her hand.

The Force struck them like a landslide.

Both guards crashed backward through a banquet table.

Han, still half-blind, shouted from somewhere beyond the confusion.

“Leia?!”

“I’m working on it!”

Jabba roared furiously and reached for a hidden control panel on his throne.

Leia sensed the movement instantly.

She leapt.

The lightsaber flashed once.

Jabba’s massive hand slammed onto the controls—
severed cleanly at the wrist.

The Hutt screamed.

The throne room recoiled in horror.

Leia landed atop the dais before him, blade humming inches from his face.

For one brief moment, silence fell.

Jabba glared at her with pure hatred.

Leia remembered Alderaan.

Remembered Imperial prisons.
Chains.
Fear.
Humiliation.
Everything men like Jabba represented in the galaxy.

“You should have let us leave,” she said quietly.

Then her blade swept downward.

The blue lightsaber carved through Jabba’s enormous body in a burst of steam and burning flesh.

The Hutt’s scream died instantly.

His upper body slid grotesquely sideways from the lower half of his corpse.

For one heartbeat nobody in the palace moved.

Then the room exploded into terror.

“Jabba’s dead!”

“Run!”

Creatures stampeded for exits. Blaster fire erupted wildly. Smoke filled the chamber.

Leia deactivated her lightsaber and sprinted toward Han.

Lando was already cutting him loose from the guards.

Han blinked rapidly as his vision slowly returned.

“I can see a little now,” he said weakly.

His eyes focused on Leia standing amid the wreckage with a lightsaber in hand.

Han stared.

“You killed Jabba?”

Leia grabbed his arm and pulled him upright.

“We can discuss it later.”

Luke Skywalker appeared in the shattered doorway moments later, black robes sweeping behind him. R2-D2 rolled beside him while Chewbacca barreled into the chamber with a roaring Wookiee battle cry.

Luke took one look at Jabba’s bisected corpse.

He blinked once.

Leia gave him an innocent look.

“What?”

Luke glanced at the ruined throne room, the unconscious guards, the fleeing criminals, and the smoking ceiling.

Then he nodded slowly.

“Remind me never to underestimate you again.”

Leia smirked.

“About time.”

Together the Rebels fought their way from the palace under blaster fire and screaming chaos, disappearing into the Tatooine night before Jabba’s remaining forces could reorganize.

Behind them, Jabba the Hutt’s criminal empire collapsed before dawn.

# Part 5

The rescue of Han Solo marked the end of one chapter.

The war itself remained.

The Empire still controlled most of the galaxy.

A second Death Star was under construction.

And somewhere, waiting at the center of it all, sat Emperor Palpatine.

Before confronting any of that, however, Luke and Leia had one promise left to fulfill.

—

Dagobah had not changed.

The swamp remained damp, ancient, and seemingly eternal.

The same twisted trees emerged from the mist.

The same strange creatures called from distant waters.

The same feeling of deep connection to the Force permeated everything.

Luke and Leia landed near Yoda's hut.

The old Jedi Master was waiting.

He looked smaller than either remembered.

Older.

Frailer.

Yet his eyes remained bright.

"You returned," Yoda said.

"We said we would," Leia replied.

The old Master nodded.

"Good."

Over the following days, Luke and Leia spent every possible moment with him.

There were no dramatic lessons.

No impossible trials.

No great revelations.

Only final guidance.

Final corrections.

Final wisdom.

Yoda observed them sparring.

Meditating.

Discussing the Force.

He watched the two children of Anakin Skywalker and saw how far they had come.

The frightened farmboy from Tatooine was gone.

The stubborn senator from Alderaan was gone.

In their place stood two Jedi.

Different from the Jedi of old.

But Jedi nonetheless.

One evening, after a long period of silence, Yoda finally spoke.

"Complete, your training is."

Luke and Leia exchanged glances.

Neither had expected the words to affect them so strongly.

Leia smiled softly.

Luke bowed his head.

Yoda studied them.

"Pass on what you have learned."

"We will," Luke promised.

The old Master closed his eyes.

Satisfied.

At peace.

For a few moments he simply breathed.

Then the Force seemed to gather around him.

Luke felt it first.

Leia felt it immediately afterward.

Neither moved.

Neither spoke.

Yoda's body grew still.

His features relaxed completely.

The Force flowed through the little hut like a gentle wind.

Then Yoda vanished.

His physical form disappeared.

Leaving only empty robes behind.

The twins stood in silence.

A Jedi Master who had lived for centuries had become one with the Force.

—

Not long afterward, the Alliance received its greatest opportunity.

Imperial intelligence had been compromised.

The Emperor himself would be present aboard the second Death Star.

A massive Rebel strike was planned.

Three objectives would determine the battle.

A strike team would disable the shield generator on Endor.

The fleet would destroy the battle station.

And somehow, Darth Vader and the Emperor would have to be confronted.

Luke and Leia both understood who would handle the third objective.

—

The moon of Endor was beautiful.

Forests stretched across vast landscapes beneath towering redwoods.

The Rebel strike team landed successfully.

Han Solo led the operation.

Chewbacca accompanied him.

R2-D2 and C-3PO joined them as well.

The mission quickly became more complicated than expected.

Imperial patrols appeared.

Local Ewoks appeared.

The protocol droid somehow became regarded as a divine figure by the Ewoks.

Han stopped trying to understand events after that.

Meanwhile, Luke and Leia made their decision.

Neither intended to hide from Vader.

Neither intended to avoid the confrontation any longer.

Together they surrendered themselves to Imperial forces.

When stormtroopers approached, neither resisted.

Lightsabers remained clipped to their belts.

The twins simply raised their hands.

Vader soon arrived personally.

His dark gaze moved from Luke to Leia.

His son.

His daughter.

Both standing before him willingly.

Neither afraid.

Neither turning away.

Without a word, Vader ordered them brought aboard the Death Star.

—

The Emperor was waiting.

The throne room overlooked the unfinished battle station's vast reactor shaft.

Stars glimmered beyond enormous windows.

Palpatine sat patiently upon his throne.

Ancient.

Frail-looking.

Terrifying.

The dark side radiated from him like poison.

Luke and Leia stood before him together.

Vader remained nearby.

Silent.

Watching.

The Emperor smiled.

"Welcome."

Neither twin answered.

The old man seemed amused.

"So much strength."

His eyes moved between them.

"The offspring of Anakin Skywalker."

Luke felt hatred rising.

Leia felt it too.

Palpatine noticed.

And smiled wider.

—

Far below, the battle began.

The Rebel fleet emerged from hyperspace.

The second Death Star revealed itself fully operational.

Imperial Star Destroyers surged forward.

Turbolasers illuminated space.

The largest battle in galactic history erupted.

At the same time, Han's strike team launched its assault against the shield generator.

Everything depended on success.

—

Inside the throne room, the final confrontation began.

Palpatine taunted.

Manipulated.

Provoked.

He spoke of inevitable defeat.

The destruction of the Rebel fleet.

The collapse of hope.

Eventually Luke snapped first.

He ignited his green lightsaber and charged.

Leia activated her light-blue blade an instant later.

Vader met them.

The duel began.

For a time, the twins fought together.

But Vader was still one of the most powerful Force users alive.

The battle carried them through walkways and maintenance platforms surrounding the throne room.

Steel shook beneath lightsaber strikes.

Sparks erupted.

The Force collided against the Force.

Then Vader struck Luke down.

A brutal exchange ended with Luke thrown violently against a support structure.

His head struck metal.

Darkness consumed him.

Leia felt the shock through their bond.

"Luke!"

No answer.

Only unconsciousness.

Now she stood alone.

Vader advanced.

Red and blue blades collided.

Leia fought with everything she possessed.

Years of training.

Years of loss.

Years of anger.

All of it poured into every strike.

The duel intensified.

Gradually Vader began giving ground.

Leia pressed forward relentlessly.

Their lightsabers locked.

And suddenly words emerged that had waited years to be spoken.

"Four years ago," Leia said through clenched teeth, "you captured me on the first Death Star."

Vader remained silent.

"You interrogated me."

The blades separated.

Clashed again.

"You tortured me."

Another exchange.

Faster.

Harder.

"You destroyed my homeworld."

For the first time Vader hesitated.

Leia seized the moment.

Her blade drove him backward.

"You took everything."

Vader stumbled.

The Force itself seemed to tremble around her.

"Back then I was powerless."

Another strike.

"And now?"

Leia's blade flashed.

Vader's defenses nearly collapsed.

"I am a Jedi."

The words echoed across the chamber.

"You can no longer do those things to me."

Vader retreated another step.

Then another.

The Emperor watched with growing interest.

And growing concern.

Leia was winning.

Not easily.

Not completely.

But unmistakably.

Winning.

At last Vader fell to one knee.

His weapon barely remained in his grasp.

The Emperor rose slowly.

"Good."

Leia turned toward him.

"Good."

Palpatine's voice dripped with approval.

"Your anger has made you powerful."

Leia immediately understood.

The trap.

The same trap that had destroyed countless others.

The same trap that had consumed Vader.

"Kill him."

The Emperor pointed toward Vader.

"Take your father's place."

Leia looked down at the black-armored figure.

The man who had tortured her.

The man who had destroyed Alderaan.

The man responsible for unimaginable suffering.

One strike would end him.

The temptation existed.

Briefly.

Then she deactivated her lightsaber.

"No."

Palpatine's smile vanished.

Leia stepped back.

"I won't."

At that moment Luke regained consciousness.

He saw Vader defeated.

Saw Leia standing firm.

Saw the Emperor.

The dark side surged within him.

For a moment he charged.

Lightsaber raised.

Palpatine's smile returned immediately.

"Yes."

Luke reached striking distance.

Then stopped.

The green blade hovered motionless.

Luke looked directly at the Emperor.

"No."

Palpatine's expression hardened.

Both children of Skywalker had refused.

The old Sith Lord rose completely from his throne.

"So be it."

His hands erupted with blue-white energy.

Force lightning exploded outward.

Luke screamed.

Leia screamed.

The energy tore through both of them simultaneously.

Their bodies collapsed.

The attack continued.

Palpatine showed no mercy.

No hesitation.

Only hatred.

The twins writhed in agony.

The Emperor laughed.

And Darth Vader watched.

—

Across the moon of Endor, Han Solo's team finally succeeded.

Imperial defenses collapsed.

The shield generator exploded.

Fire consumed the bunker.

The protective barrier around the Death Star vanished.

—

In space, Admiral Ackbar immediately recognized the opportunity.

The Rebel fleet surged forward.

Lando Calrissian guided the Millennium Falcon directly into the battle station.

Fighters followed.

The final attack run began.

—

Inside the throne room, Luke and Leia continued to suffer beneath the Emperor's lightning.

Palpatine remained completely focused on killing them.

The Emperor never noticed Vader rise.

Never noticed Anakin Skywalker's final decision.

Vader looked at Luke.

His son.

He looked at Leia.

His daughter.

The children he had lost before ever knowing them.

The children he had spent decades searching for without realizing it.

The children now dying before his eyes.

Something finally broke.

Or perhaps something finally healed.

With a roar, Vader seized the Emperor.

Palpatine shrieked in surprise.

The lightning ceased.

The Emperor struggled.

Too late.

Vader lifted him bodily.

Carried him toward the reactor shaft.

And hurled him into the abyss.

Palpatine disappeared into darkness.

A final explosion erupted far below.

The Emperor was dead.

The Sith were finished.

—

Moments later, the Death Star itself began to die.

The shield was gone.

The reactor had been destroyed.

Explosions spread throughout the station.

Alarms screamed.

Fire erupted through corridors.

Luke and Leia supported the badly damaged Vader between them.

The three escaped aboard a shuttle.

Barely.

Behind them, the Death Star exploded in a brilliant sphere of light.

Lando and the Millennium Falcon escaped only seconds ahead of the blast.

The battle was over.

The Empire had lost.

—

Back on Endor, Luke and Leia carried Vader from the shuttle.

The life-support systems within his armor were failing rapidly.

They laid him gently upon the forest floor.

For the first time, neither saw Darth Vader.

Only their father.

Anakin Skywalker.

His voice emerged weakly.

"Help me take this mask off."

Luke hesitated.

"You'll die."

Anakin nodded.

"Nothing can stop that now."

Together, Luke and Leia removed the mask.

For the first time they saw his face.

Scarred.

Pale.

Aged by suffering.

Yet undeniably human.

Leia knelt beside him.

The anger she had carried for years felt strangely distant now.

Not gone.

But changed.

Anakin looked at both of them.

His children.

The children he had never expected to know.

"You were right."

Leia blinked.

"What?"

"There was still good in me."

The words reminded neither twin of anything.

But somewhere, long ago, another person had once said exactly the same thing.

Anakin smiled faintly.

Then his gaze drifted toward the stars.

His breathing slowed.

And finally stopped.

The Force moved gently around him.

Peacefully.

The way it had around Yoda.

The way it had around Obi-Wan.

Luke and Leia sat silently beside him.

The battle was over.

The Emperor was gone.

The Death Star was destroyed.

Their father was dead.

And somehow, despite everything, redeemed.

—

That night the forests of Endor became a celebration unlike any the galaxy had ever seen.

Imperials surrendered.

The Rebel Alliance rejoiced.

Ewoks danced around enormous fires.

Music filled the air.

Han found Leia among the celebrations and pulled her into a fierce embrace.

For a long time neither spoke.

Neither needed to.

Nearby, Luke smiled.

For the first time in years, there was no battle waiting tomorrow.

No desperate mission.

No impossible odds.

Only peace.

As the celebration continued, three familiar figures appeared beyond the firelight.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Yoda.

And Anakin Skywalker.

Not the scarred man who had died on Endor.

The Jedi he had once been.

The twins looked toward them.

The Force spirits smiled.

Master.

Master.

Father.

The three luminous figures stood together beneath the stars for a few quiet moments.

Then slowly faded into the Cosmic Force.

The night continued.

And for the first time in a very long time, the future seemed bright.

# Epilogue

The celebration on Endor lasted for days.

For most of the galaxy, it was remembered as the moment the Empire lost its Emperor and its greatest weapon.

For the people who had actually been there, however, the memories were more personal.

Friends reunited.

Wars survived.

Lives reclaimed.

And, in some cases, wounds finally noticed.

—

Han first realized something was wrong when he embraced Leia during the celebration.

At first he could not identify it.

Leia looked healthy.

She was smiling.

She was walking normally.

Yet something felt off.

A stiffness.

An exhaustion hidden beneath determination.

A faint tremor she seemed to be consciously suppressing.

Later, after the celebrations had quieted somewhat, Leia described everything that had happened aboard the Death Star.

The duel.

The Emperor.

The Force lightning.

Han listened carefully.

Then his expression changed.

"You got hit by what?"

Leia blinked.

"The Emperor's lightning."

Han looked toward Luke.

"You too?"

Luke nodded.

Han stared at both of them.

"You two are going to a medical facility."

Leia immediately objected.

"I'm fine."

"No."

"Han—"

"No."

Luke tried.

"We've had worse."

Han pointed at him.

"That sentence is exactly why I'm not taking medical advice from either of you."

—

Within hours, both Jedi found themselves under the supervision of Rebel physicians and several highly concerned medical droids.

The examinations quickly confirmed Han's suspicions.

The Force lightning had left significant injuries.

Not necessarily obvious ones.

But real ones.

Nerve damage.

Cellular trauma.

Exhaustion that neither twin had fully appreciated.

Both were ordered into bacta treatment.

Neither enjoyed it.

Leia hated being immobilized.

Luke complained constantly about boredom.

Han enjoyed reminding them that he had been right.

Several days later the doctors finally released them.

The damage had largely healed.

The twins were cleared to resume normal activity.

Han celebrated his victory by refusing to let either of them argue about medical treatment ever again.

The twins ignored this declaration immediately.

—

Not every gathering on Endor was celebratory.

One ceremony carried a much heavier mood.

Anakin Skywalker's funeral.

The decision had not been simple.

Many Rebels had suffered directly because of Darth Vader.

Many had lost friends.

Family.

Entire worlds.

Leia herself had spent years hating him.

No one expected forgiveness.

No one expected forgetting.

Yet everyone present understood another truth.

In the end, Vader had destroyed the Emperor.

Without that act, the Battle of Endor would almost certainly have ended in disaster.

The Rebel Alliance would have died.

The Empire would have endured.

So a funeral was held.

Not for Darth Vader.

For Anakin Skywalker.

Jedi Knight.

Father.

The man who had returned in the final moments of his life.

Luke and Leia stood together throughout the ceremony.

Han stood nearby.

Chewbacca.

Lando.

Mon Mothma.

Pilots.

Soldiers.

Commanders.

People who had once feared Vader more than anyone else in the galaxy.

The mood remained complicated.

Respectful.

Uncertain.

But genuine.

When the ceremony ended, Luke felt a strange sense of completion.

The story that had begun before either twin was born had finally reached its end.

—

Only a few days later, Endor hosted a much happier event.

Han Solo and Leia Organa's wedding.

The contrast with the funeral could not have been greater.

The forest clearing chosen for the ceremony overlooked one of Endor's rivers.

Sunlight filtered through towering trees.

Ewoks decorated the area enthusiastically.

No one was entirely certain what some of the decorations represented.

It seemed impolite to ask.

Luke served as officiant.

A role he took very seriously.

Han immediately regretted allowing it.

"You practiced this speech, didn't you?"

"Of course."

Han groaned.

Leia laughed.

Nearby, Chewbacca rumbled approvingly.

Lando looked far too entertained by the entire situation.

Mon Mothma attended as well, along with many of their closest comrades from the Alliance.

R2-D2 occupied a place of honor.

C-3PO served as translator.

Unfortunately, the Ewoks still regarded him as a divine being.

This complicated matters.

Several times during the ceremony, groups of Ewoks interrupted to bow reverently toward him.

Threepio appeared simultaneously embarrassed and pleased.

"I really wish they wouldn't do that."

The Ewoks ignored him completely.

The ceremony proceeded anyway.

Luke eventually reached the important part.

Han and Leia exchanged vows.

Neither cared much about elaborate speeches.

Neither had ever been particularly formal.

The promises were simple.

Sincere.

Honest.

The sort of promises forged through years of surviving impossible situations together.

When it was finally over, the entire clearing erupted into applause.

Chewbacca's celebratory roar nearly startled several Ewoks into a nearby river.

Han kissed Leia.

The crowd cheered.

Threepio translated the cheering into six different languages before anyone asked him to stop.

—

The war, however, was not finished.

The destruction of the Emperor had changed everything.

But it had not instantly erased the Empire.

Thousands of Imperial ships still existed.

Entire sectors remained under Imperial control.

Governors, admirals, and generals continued fighting.

Mon Mothma acted quickly.

Almost immediately after Endor, she formally proclaimed the New Republic.

The Rebel Alliance became the foundation of a new government.

A government dedicated to replacing Imperial rule.

The struggle continued.

Campaign after campaign followed.

System after system was liberated.

Imperial fleets were driven back.

The New Republic advanced relentlessly.

Victory had become possible.

Now it needed to be secured.

—

Meanwhile, Luke and Leia focused on a different mission.

A promise.

Yoda's final instruction remained with them.

Pass on what you have learned.

The Jedi Order had nearly vanished.

If it remained extinct, the knowledge accumulated over thousands of years would disappear forever.

Neither twin could accept that.

So they began searching.

Ancient archives.

Forgotten temples.

Hidden repositories.

Old records scattered across the galaxy.

Anything connected to the Jedi.

The work was painstaking.

Some discoveries consisted of little more than fragments.

A damaged holocron.

A partial manuscript.

A ruined training site.

Yet each piece mattered.

Slowly, a foundation emerged.

Luke often focused on recovering knowledge.

Leia frequently proved more effective at organizing it.

Together they made a formidable team.

Eventually they began searching for students.

Young people sensitive to the Force.

Individuals who might become the next generation of Jedi.

The project consumed increasing amounts of their time.

Months passed.

The effort grew.

And one day Han found himself watching Leia prepare for yet another expedition connected to rebuilding the Order.

She was discussing ancient Jedi records with Luke.

Again.

Maps covered a nearby table.

Again.

Neither sibling had noticed Han standing there for several minutes.

Again.

Eventually Han folded his arms.

"A few years ago," he said, "I thought I lost Leia to Luke."

Leia looked up.

Luke looked confused.

Han shook his head.

"Turns out instead I lost her to the Jedi."

Leia laughed.

Luke laughed.

Even Han eventually smiled.

Because despite the complaint, he knew exactly why she was doing it.

The galaxy had spent decades under darkness.

The Empire was being defeated.

The Republic was returning.

And somewhere in the future, new Jedi would rise.

Not because of ancient legends.

Not because of forgotten masters.

But because two twins had refused to let hope die.

The children of Anakin Skywalker had inherited a broken galaxy.

Together, they helped rebuild it.
