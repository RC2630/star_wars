# Part 1

By 7 ABY, the galaxy looked very different than it had three years earlier on Endor.

The Empire had officially surrendered at Jakku two years ago.

The New Republic Senate had been established.

Entire sectors that had spent decades under Imperial rule were rebuilding.

Though scattered Imperial warlords still operated along the edges of known space, they no longer represented an existential threat to the galaxy.

For the first time in generations, peace seemed attainable.

Which was fortunate.

Because Luke Skywalker and Leia Organa Solo were busier than ever.

Not with war.

With archaeology.

Han Solo often insisted there was very little difference.

—

The twins had devoted themselves to rebuilding Jedi knowledge.

Every fragment mattered.

Ancient texts.

Ruined temples.

Lost holocrons.

Forgotten worlds.

They chased rumors across half the galaxy.

Sometimes they found priceless discoveries.

Sometimes they spent weeks traveling only to uncover a cave full of completely unrelated rocks.

Luke maintained that this was still valuable because it eliminated possibilities.

Han maintained that only a Jedi could say something like that with a straight face.

The missions continued regardless.

And they took a lot of time.

An extraordinary amount of time.

More time than Han particularly appreciated.

Especially after Ben was born in 5 ABY.

—

Being a father was not something Han Solo had ever expected.

Yet from the moment he first held his son, something fundamental changed.

Not that Han would ever admit that publicly.

At least not willingly.

Ben inherited Leia's dark hair.

His grandfather Anakin's blue eyes.

And, unfortunately for everyone involved, the stubbornness of both parents simultaneously.

The child was barely two years old and already seemed determined to challenge every authority figure he encountered.

Han privately suspected this was some kind of cosmic punishment.

Leia found it adorable.

Which did not help.

—

The real problem was scheduling.

Leia remained one of the New Republic's most important political leaders.

Luke remained focused on rebuilding Jedi knowledge.

And both of them frequently disappeared together on lengthy expeditions.

This left Han with a recurring dilemma.

Every time Leia departed on another mission, Ben stayed behind with him.

Under ordinary circumstances this would have been manageable.

Unfortunately Han was also a New Republic general.

Imperial warlords did not conveniently stop causing problems simply because he had a toddler at home.

As a result, Han often found himself trying to balance military responsibilities with raising Ben.

The experience was exhausting.

After the liberation of Kashyyyk, he had even lost his most reliable source of assistance.

Chewbacca had finally returned home to reunite with his family and people after decades away.

Han was genuinely happy for him.

He also missed him terribly.

Now, whenever Luke and Leia vanished on another Jedi expedition, Han found himself alone.

Or alone with Ben.

Usually the latter.

—

One evening on Chandrila, Han finally reached his limit.

It happened during dinner.

Ben had already managed to spill something on himself.

Threepio was attempting to help.

The attempt was not going well.

Luke was discussing ancient Jedi navigation records.

Leia was listening attentively.

Han set down his utensil.

A little harder than necessary.

Neither twin noticed.

Han cleared his throat.

Still nothing.

Luke was describing some obscure Jedi reference to a world called Ahch-To.

Leia immediately became interested.

Han closed his eyes.

Counted to five.

Opened them again.

Still talking.

Finally:

"Oh, good."

The twins looked up.

Han smiled.

"Glad to know you're both having a nice evening together."

Leia blinked.

"What?"

Han gestured between them.

"You know, sometimes I wonder if I'm actually married to you."

Leia immediately recognized the tone.

"Han..."

"No, really."

Luke was beginning to look nervous.

A wise instinct.

Han pointed at him.

"Because from where I'm standing, it seems like you're more married to Luke than you are to me."

Threepio made a choking noise.

R2 emitted what sounded suspiciously like laughter.

Han pointed at the astromech.

"Don't encourage me."

Leia was trying very hard not to smile.

Unfortunately this only made things worse.

"Oh, you think that's funny?"

"A little."

"Great."

Han folded his arms.

"You disappear for weeks at a time."

"It's important work."

"So is raising our son."

Leia winced slightly.

Luke immediately found something fascinating about the ceiling.

Han continued.

"Every time there's another ancient Jedi mystery, off you go with your brother."

"Han..."

"Then I get Ben."

Ben looked up proudly.

"Dada."

"Yes, buddy, you get me too."

Leia laughed despite herself.

Han pointed again.

"See? You're laughing."

"Because you're being ridiculous."

"I am not."

"You absolutely are."

"I have spent the last month simultaneously commanding military operations and explaining to a two-year-old why he cannot eat starship fuel."

Ben frowned.

"Fuel."

"No."

"Fuel."

"No."

Luke quietly looked away before anyone could ask whose side he was on.

—

The argument ended the way most of Han and Leia's arguments ended.

Nobody actually won.

Everyone understood the other's point.

And everyone remained stubborn.

Yet Leia found herself thinking about it afterward.

Because beneath the exaggeration, Han was not entirely wrong.

The Jedi restoration effort consumed enormous amounts of time.

And she had indeed spent more time traveling with Luke than with her own husband lately.

The realization bothered her.

—

Several days later, Luke arrived carrying a collection of old star charts.

Leia was reviewing reports.

Luke looked unusually excited.

Which generally meant trouble.

"I found it."

Leia immediately knew what he meant.

"Ahch-To?"

Luke nodded.

According to scattered records recovered from multiple sources, Ahch-To might be the site of the first Jedi Temple.

The place where the earliest Jedi tradition had begun.

If the legends were true, the world could contain knowledge older than the Republic itself.

The discovery was potentially enormous.

Leia immediately understood why Luke was excited.

Then she remembered Han.

And Ben.

An idea occurred.

A strange idea.

An unprecedented idea.

An idea Luke was absolutely not expecting.

—

When Han arrived home later that evening, he found Leia waiting.

This immediately made him suspicious.

Experience had taught him that Leia waiting calmly often meant she was about to ask for something.

"What's wrong?"

"Nothing."

Han narrowed his eyes.

"What do you want?"

Leia smiled.

"I have a proposal."

"That sentence has never ended well."

Luke happened to be present.

Which made Han even more suspicious.

"What are you two planning?"

Luke looked offended.

"We're not planning anything."

Han pointed.

"See? That's exactly the kind of thing people say when they're planning something."

Leia sighed.

Then explained the Ahch-To expedition.

Han listened.

Expression growing steadily more resigned.

When she finished, he groaned.

"Another Jedi mission."

"Yes."

"Of course."

"Han—"

"And you'll be gone for weeks."

"Maybe."

Han threw his hands upward.

"There it is."

Then Leia delivered the part he wasn't expecting.

"So come with us."

Silence.

Han blinked.

"What?"

"You and Ben."

Han stared.

Luke smiled.

Leia continued.

"The mission isn't especially dangerous."

"As far as you know."

"As far as we know."

"That's not reassuring."

"You keep complaining that we're always leaving."

"I do."

"So don't stay behind."

Han looked genuinely confused.

The possibility had apparently never occurred to him.

"You're serious?"

"Yes."

Luke nodded.

"It'll be fun."

Han looked at him.

"That's the least convincing thing you've ever said."

"Why?"

"Because every time you say something will be fun, somebody ends up getting chased by something ancient."

R2 whistled.

Luke pointed.

"That only happened a few times."

Han stared.

"A few?"

Luke considered.

"...more than a few."

Leia finally laughed.

Han looked between the twins.

Then toward Ben, who was attempting to climb onto a chair.

Then back toward Leia.

The idea was admittedly appealing.

No separation.

No being left behind.

No spending weeks wondering whether his wife and brother-in-law had accidentally uncovered another ancient death trap.

And, perhaps most importantly, actual time together.

As a family.

Han exhaled.

Slowly.

Then nodded.

"Fine."

Leia smiled immediately.

Han pointed a warning finger.

"If some giant ancient Jedi monster tries to eat us—"

"There aren't any giant ancient Jedi monsters."

Luke and Leia both said this a little too quickly.

Han noticed.

His eyes narrowed.

"There are giant ancient Jedi monsters, aren't there?"

The twins exchanged a glance.

Neither answered.

Han groaned.

"Why did I agree to this?"

—

A few days later, preparations were complete.

The expedition assembled.

Luke.

Leia.

Han.

Two-year-old Ben Solo.

R2-D2.

C-3PO.

Chewbacca had been invited but politely declined, preferring to remain on Kashyyyk with his family.

For perhaps the first time since the fall of the Empire, the entire group was embarking on one of Luke and Leia's Jedi expeditions together.

Ahead lay Ahch-To.

A world hidden in ancient records.

A world that might contain the earliest origins of the Jedi.

And, although none of them knew it yet, a journey that would become one of the most important discoveries in the history of the restored Jedi Order.

---

# Part 2

The argument began the moment Luke suggested using the Millennium Falcon.

"The Falcon is bigger," Luke said. "It has room for everybody."

Leia nodded.

"It makes the most sense."

Han stared at both twins as though they had just proposed throwing the ship directly into a star.

"No."

Leia blinked.

"No?"

"No."

Luke frowned.

"Why not?"

Han pointed emphatically at the star charts.

"Because that destination is in the Unknown Regions."

There was a brief silence.

Then Leia said:

"Yes."

Han looked horrified that she apparently thought this supported her argument.

"The Unknown Regions."

"Yes."

"The place literally called the Unknown Regions."

"Yes, Han."

Han pointed toward himself.

"I fly a lot."

"We know."

"I've been almost everywhere."

"We know."

"I avoid the Unknown Regions."

Luke folded his arms.

"You've never actually been there."

"Exactly."

Luke blinked.

"That's your argument?"

"That's a fantastic argument."

Leia sighed.

"Han."

"No."

"Han."

"No."

"Han."

"No."

Eventually Leia won.

Han never quite understood how.

The process involved approximately two hours of discussion, one kiss, an accusation that he was being dramatic, a counter-accusation that she was being reckless, another kiss, and a promise that if anything looked remotely dangerous they would turn around.

The promise was immediately identified as suspicious by everyone present.

Especially Han.

Yet somehow he still agreed.

—

The Millennium Falcon departed Chandrila shortly after nightfall.

The stars stretched into brilliant lines.

Hyperspace enveloped the ship.

And the journey began.

Thirty-four thousand one hundred thirty-two light-years.

Approximately sixty-six hours.

Nearly three full days.

Even the Falcon would need time.

—

The first night passed quietly.

Han configured the initial hyperspace route through heavily charted Core Worlds.

The navigation systems handled most of the work automatically.

For once, there was little danger.

The Falcon simply accelerated through known space.

Everyone settled in.

Luke reviewed old Jedi records.

Leia reviewed them with him.

Threepio complained about the humidity settings.

R2 ignored him.

Ben immediately discovered that starships contain buttons.

Many buttons.

Far too many buttons.

Han caught him attempting to activate something important less than twenty minutes after departure.

"No."

"Button."

"No."

"Button."

"No."

Ben looked personally offended.

Leia found this hilarious.

Han did not.

—

Eventually the adults slept.

The Falcon continued onward through hyperspace.

Autopilot handled the route flawlessly.

Luke occupied one bunk.

Leia and Han another.

Ben somehow migrated between all available sleeping locations throughout the night.

Nobody understood how.

Threepio eventually found the child asleep underneath a maintenance console.

The protocol droid remained concerned for several hours afterward.

—

Morning arrived.

Or at least what passed for morning aboard a starship.

The Falcon had already crossed much of the Core.

The stars outside remained hyperspace blue.

Breakfast was chaotic.

Ben discovered he could throw food.

Han discovered he lacked the reflexes to prevent this.

Leia laughed.

Luke wisely remained silent.

Threepio spent fifteen minutes cleaning.

R2 watched the proceedings with visible amusement.

—

As the ship crossed from the Core into the Colonies and then toward the Inner Rim, life aboard settled into a rhythm.

Luke spent much of the journey studying.

Leia tried to do the same.

Han objected.

Frequently.

One afternoon he entered the lounge area to discover Luke and Leia once again surrounded by ancient records.

They had apparently been discussing Jedi philosophy for almost three hours.

Han stood there.

Waiting.

Neither noticed.

Finally he sat directly on top of one of the documents.

Leia looked up.

"Han."

"Leia."

She smiled.

He pointed around the room.

"We're on a family trip."

"This is a family trip."

"You've been discussing Jedi archives since breakfast."

Luke checked a chronometer.

"Actually since before breakfast."

Han glared.

"You're not helping."

Leia laughed.

Han sat beside her.

Very deliberately.

Then draped an arm around her shoulders.

Leia raised an eyebrow.

"What are you doing?"

"Winning."

"You are impossible."

"And yet you married me."

Luke stood up immediately.

"I'll go somewhere else."

"Good idea."

—

By the second night the Falcon had reached the Expansion Region.

The journey remained smooth.

R2 occasionally monitored navigation.

Han occasionally checked systems.

Mostly everyone relaxed.

For perhaps the first time in years, nobody was actively being shot at.

That alone felt unusual.

Leia spent part of the evening simply sitting beside Han in the cockpit.

No archives.

No Jedi discussions.

No missions.

Just watching hyperspace.

Han appreciated it.

"See?"

"What?"

"This is nice."

Leia leaned against him.

"You're right."

Han looked delighted.

"Don't sound so surprised."

—

The second night was interrupted when Ben somehow escaped his sleeping area.

Again.

Threepio eventually located him inside a maintenance compartment.

Again.

Han spent twenty minutes extracting him.

Luke suggested the Force might be involved.

Han immediately rejected this theory.

"I'm not accepting that my two-year-old is already using Jedi powers."

Leia looked thoughtful.

Han pointed.

"Don't."

—

The third day began as the Falcon crossed the Outer Rim.

This was where familiar space gradually ended.

Navigation updates became less frequent.

Star charts became less complete.

Hyperspace lanes became less reliable.

Han grew quieter.

Luke noticed.

Leia noticed.

Han insisted nothing was wrong.

Everyone knew this was a lie.

—

By midday the Falcon reached the edge of mapped civilization.

Ahead lay the Unknown Regions.

The transition was subtle.

At first.

Then it wasn't.

The Falcon suddenly experienced turbulence.

Minor at first.

Then increasingly frequent.

Navigation warnings began appearing.

Sensor anomalies appeared.

Gravity distortions.

Uncharted masses.

Strange energy signatures.

Things that had no business existing where they appeared to exist.

Threepio became alarmed almost immediately.

"Oh dear."

Nobody responded.

"Oh dear."

Still nothing.

"Oh dear."

Han sighed.

"What now?"

Threepio pointed at the sensor display.

"None of that appears normal."

"That's because it isn't."

"Oh dear."

—

Several hours later the situation deteriorated further.

The Unknown Regions felt different.

Even through hyperspace.

Routes twisted unpredictably.

Mass shadows appeared where no stars should exist.

Sensors frequently contradicted themselves.

The Falcon lurched unexpectedly several times.

Han's grip on the controls tightened.

Leia quietly observed him.

Then blinked.

Through the Force she sensed something.

Fear.

Significant fear.

Han was panicking.

Not visibly.

Not externally.

Internally.

Leia smiled.

"You are terrified."

Han looked offended.

"I am not."

"You absolutely are."

"I'm focused."

Luke looked up from a navigation display.

"He's terrified."

Han pointed.

"Not helping."

—

The next turbulence event nearly ended the discussion.

The Falcon suddenly rolled.

Alarms flashed.

Han corrected immediately.

But not quite fast enough.

The ship narrowly avoided emerging from hyperspace directly into an unexpected gravitational disturbance.

Several warning systems erupted simultaneously.

Ben laughed.

Han did not.

Silence filled the cockpit.

Finally Han exhaled.

"Okay."

Luke nodded.

"Okay."

Han stared ahead.

Then reluctantly admitted:

"Maybe this is worse than I expected."

Threepio made a noise that sounded suspiciously like vindication.

—

Luke moved toward the pilot's seat.

"I can help."

Han frowned.

"I can handle it."

The ship lurched again.

Harder this time.

Everyone grabbed something.

Han stared at the controls.

Then at Luke.

Then back at the controls.

Leia remained wisely silent.

Luke sat down beside him.

"Navigation through the Unknown Regions isn't really normal piloting."

Han looked annoyed.

"It looks like piloting."

"It isn't."

Luke gestured toward the displays.

"Most of the danger comes from things we don't know are there."

Han glanced at him.

Luke continued.

"The Force helps."

Leia nodded.

"You've done this before?"

Luke shrugged.

"Not exactly."

Han looked horrified.

"That's not reassuring."

"But I've used the Force for navigation."

"That sentence is also not reassuring."

—

Eventually practicality won.

Han surrendered the primary controls.

Reluctantly.

Very reluctantly.

Luke took over.

The difference became apparent almost immediately.

He wasn't reacting to hazards.

He seemed to anticipate them.

Tiny course corrections occurred before warnings appeared.

Unexpected obstacles were avoided before sensors fully registered them.

The route became smoother.

Safer.

More controlled.

Han watched for several minutes.

Then frowned.

"I hate that this is working."

Leia smiled.

"You're welcome."

"I wasn't talking to you."

—

The final hours passed with increasing caution.

The Unknown Regions remained dangerous.

But now the Falcon moved through them with surprising confidence.

Luke relied partly on instruments.

Partly on instinct.

Partly on the Force.

Sometimes he altered course for reasons he could not fully explain.

Those changes invariably proved correct later.

Han eventually stopped questioning it.

Mostly because the alternative appeared to be crashing into things.

—

Late on the third afternoon, sixty-six hours after departure, the destination finally appeared.

Ahch-To.

An ocean world.

Remote.

Ancient.

Hidden.

The Falcon emerged from hyperspace above endless blue waters.

Everyone gathered in the cockpit.

Even Ben.

For several moments nobody spoke.

The planet was beautiful.

Ocean stretched in every direction.

Only scattered islands broke the surface.

Ancient.

Peaceful.

Waiting.

Luke felt something immediately through the Force.

Leia did too.

A presence.

A history.

Something old beyond measure.

R2 emitted an excited whistle.

Threepio looked uncertain.

Han stared at the ocean-covered world.

Then at Luke.

Then at Leia.

Then back at the planet.

"So."

Leia smiled.

"So."

Han sighed.

"I knew I should've stayed home."

Yet despite the complaint, he smiled too.

The Falcon descended through clouds toward a small rocky island surrounded by endless sea.

After three days of travel across the galaxy, they had arrived at the legendary world of Ahch-To.

And the real adventure was only beginning.

---

# Part 3

The Millennium Falcon settled onto the rocky island with a gentle hiss of landing struts.

For a few moments nobody moved.

After three days of travel across the galaxy, they had finally reached Ahch-To.

The legendary world supposedly connected to the earliest days of the Jedi.

Luke was the first down the boarding ramp.

Leia followed.

Han carried Ben.

R2-D2 rolled down after them while C-3PO carefully descended behind, complaining about moisture, wind, and uneven terrain.

The island itself was beautiful.

Steep cliffs rose above crashing waves.

Green grass covered much of the rocky terrain.

Sea birds circled overhead.

The air smelled of salt.

And the Force...

Luke frowned.

Something felt wrong.

Not dangerous.

Just wrong.

He closed his eyes.

Reached outward.

The Force answered immediately.

Not here.

Elsewhere.

A different direction.

A stronger presence.

Much stronger.

Luke opened his eyes.

Leia could already tell from his expression.

"This isn't it, is it?"

"No."

Han folded his arms.

"We crossed thirty-four thousand light-years and landed on the wrong island?"

Luke pointed toward the horizon.

"The Force is calling from over there."

Han stared.

Then looked at the ocean.

Then looked at Luke.

Then looked back at the ocean.

"I hate Force navigation."

—

They returned to the Falcon.

This time Luke remained in the pilot's seat.

The flight proceeded cautiously.

Unlike normal travel, he moved slowly.

Very slowly.

The Falcon drifted over endless oceans.

Sometimes Luke altered course without explanation.

Sometimes he simply sat quietly, sensing.

The Force guided him.

Eventually another island emerged from the mist.

Larger.

Older.

Different.

The moment Luke saw it, he knew.

"This is the place."

Leia felt it too.

The Force saturated the island.

Ancient.

Deep.

Powerful.

The Falcon landed.

And this time they had arrived correctly.

—

The island was not empty.

That became obvious almost immediately.

Small figures emerged from stone structures built into the cliffs.

The beings watched the newcomers with curiosity.

Lanai.

The caretakers of Ahch-To.

Among them were the elderly leaders who seemed responsible for maintaining the island's ancient traditions.

Threepio stepped forward nervously.

Fortunately, translation proved possible.

The caretakers spoke.

Threepio translated.

Introductions followed.

Luke identified himself as a Jedi.

Leia did the same.

The word immediately drew attention.

The caretakers exchanged surprised glances.

Apparently visitors claiming to be Jedi were rare.

Extremely rare.

Possibly unprecedented in recent memory.

After considerable discussion, the caretakers agreed to show them around.

—

The tour lasted most of the day.

The island was remarkable.

Ancient stone pathways wound between weathered structures.

The caretaker village sat nestled among cliffs overlooking the sea.

Every building seemed impossibly old.

Yet everything remained carefully maintained.

The caretakers clearly viewed their role as a sacred responsibility.

Luke absorbed every detail.

Leia listened attentively.

Han mostly tried to ensure Ben didn't wander off.

A task that rapidly proved difficult.

—

Eventually they reached the structure Luke had most wanted to see.

The First Jedi Temple.

The building stood atop a rise overlooking the ocean.

Simple.

Ancient.

Weathered by centuries of storms.

Luke entered almost reverently.

Leia followed.

The Force felt strong here.

Strong enough to make the hairs on the back of Han's neck stand up despite not being Force-sensitive himself.

The twins carefully explored every accessible chamber.

They examined carvings.

Symbols.

Architecture.

Anything that might contain knowledge.

Unfortunately they found very little.

No sacred texts.

No holocrons.

No hidden archive.

No great repository of Jedi teachings.

Luke tried not to look disappointed.

Failed.

The caretakers appeared somewhat apologetic.

As if they wished there were more to show.

—

Then they visited the tree.

A massive uneti tree grew upon a high plateau overlooking the sea.

Its trunk was enormous.

Its roots spread deep into the island.

The caretakers treated it with obvious reverence.

Leia immediately sensed something unusual.

She stopped walking.

Closed her eyes.

Reached out with the Force.

The response surprised her.

The tree was Force-sensitive.

Not conscious exactly.

Not sentient.

Yet undeniably connected to the Force.

Old.

Ancient.

Alive in a way few trees ever became.

Leia opened her eyes.

Luke had sensed it too.

Neither needed words.

Then disaster struck.

Or rather, Ben struck.

The two-year-old had already begun climbing.

—

The caretakers gasped collectively.

One nearly dropped a basket.

Another made a horrified noise.

Threepio translated frantically.

"They say the child is climbing the sacred tree!"

Indeed he was.

Ben appeared delighted.

The higher he climbed, the happier he became.

Leia looked up.

"Ben."

No response.

"Ben."

Nothing.

Ben continued climbing.

Han rubbed his forehead.

Luke was trying very hard not to laugh.

The caretakers looked increasingly distressed.

"Ben."

Leia pointed downward.

"Come down."

Ben looked down.

Smiled.

Then climbed higher.

The caretakers nearly fainted.

Threepio translated overlapping concerns about traditions, sacred spaces, and historical significance.

Ben ignored all of them.

Apparently sitting in a sacred Jedi tree was the most entertaining thing that had ever happened to him.

Finally Leia sighed.

"Fine."

She reached out with the Force.

Ben suddenly found himself gently floating through the air.

His eyes widened.

He giggled.

The caretakers watched in amazement as Leia telekinetically lowered him back to the ground.

Ben immediately demanded to be lifted again.

Leia declined.

—

Afterward the caretakers asked several questions.

One in particular surprised them.

Through Threepio's translation, they cautiously inquired why Leia was married.

And why she had a child.

The old Jedi Code, they explained, prohibited such attachments.

Luke and Leia exchanged a glance.

Then smiled.

The conversation that followed was one they had increasingly often these days.

"The old Jedi Order is gone," Luke explained.

Leia nodded.

"We're rebuilding something new."

Threepio translated.

The caretakers listened attentively.

"Our version of the Jedi doesn't forbid attachment."

Leia placed a hand on Han's shoulder.

"We don't believe love makes someone weaker."

Han looked pleased by that answer.

The caretakers exchanged thoughtful looks.

Eventually one simply nodded.

They seemed willing to accept the explanation.

After all, these two were the only Jedi they had met.

Arguing about Jedi traditions with actual Jedi seemed difficult.

—

As afternoon approached, Luke began seriously considering remaining on Ahch-To for an extended period.

The island fascinated him.

Its history fascinated him.

The Force was strong here.

Surely there was more to learn.

The caretakers were considerably less enthusiastic.

Through Threepio, they politely explained their concerns.

The island operated according to ancient routines.

Ancient traditions.

Long-term guests would disrupt that balance.

Visitors were welcome.

Permanent residents were not.

Luke looked disappointed.

Leia looked relieved.

Han looked even more relieved.

The caretakers' position settled the matter.

Luke eventually accepted their reasoning.

Though somewhat reluctantly.

—

Then came the incident.

Later, nobody could quite agree on exactly how it happened.

Only that it happened very quickly.

One moment Ben was running near the shoreline.

The next moment he slipped.

And vanished into the ocean.

"BEN!"

Han reached the water first.

Leia only moments later.

Fortunately the fall itself had not injured him.

The water wasn't terribly far below.

Ben surfaced immediately.

Spluttering.

Startled.

But otherwise fine.

Leia remained calm.

The shoreline wasn't impossibly steep.

The water wasn't especially rough.

She watched carefully.

"He can climb back up."

Han looked horrified.

"He's two."

"I'm monitoring him."

"He's two."

Leia continued watching.

Then her expression changed.

Suddenly.

Sharply.

Because something else had entered her awareness.

A presence beneath the water.

Large.

Moving quickly.

Predatory.

A pallaskean.

A sea serpent.

And it had noticed Ben.

Everything happened at once.

The creature surged upward through the water.

Massive.

Fast.

Directly toward her son.

Leia moved instantly.

She dove from the rocks without hesitation.

Han shouted something behind her.

The ocean swallowed her whole.

—

Underwater, the situation became immediately apparent.

The pallaskean was enormous.

Long.

Serpentine.

Its jaws could easily swallow a child whole.

Leia instinctively reached for her lightsaber.

Then realized the obvious problem.

Water.

Not ideal.

Instead she reached for the Force.

The creature lunged.

Leia thrust both hands forward.

The Force exploded outward.

The underwater shockwave struck the serpent like a starfighter collision.

The massive creature was hurled away.

Far away.

Disoriented.

Stunned.

It vanished into deeper waters.

Leia immediately turned toward Ben.

The child looked remarkably unconcerned by any of this.

She seized him.

Then propelled both of them back toward shore.

Moments later they emerged from the ocean.

Soaked.

Breathing heavily.

Alive.

Han immediately grabbed Ben.

The child laughed.

Apparently he considered the entire experience exciting.

Leia stared at him.

Then at the ocean.

Then at Luke.

Then at everyone else.

"We are leaving."

Nobody argued.

Han nodded immediately.

"Best idea you've had all day."

The caretakers seemed understanding.

Luke looked as though he wanted another few hours.

Maybe another few days.

Possibly another few weeks.

Then R2 emitted a series of firm beeps.

Luke looked offended.

R2 beeped again.

Even the droid appeared ready to leave.

Luke finally sighed.

"Fine."

—

The farewells were cordial.

The caretakers thanked them for visiting.

Luke thanked them for the tour.

Leia apologized one final time for Ben climbing the sacred tree.

The caretakers graciously accepted.
