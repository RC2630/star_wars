# Part 1

The twin suns hung low over Tatooine when Rey climbed the ridge overlooking the old Lars homestead.

The place was silent except for the wind.

She stood for a long moment beside the weathered moisture farm, holding two lightsabers in her hands.

One had belonged to Luke Skywalker.

The other had belonged to Leia Organa.

"So this is where it began," she murmured.

The desert offered no reply.

Rey knelt and dug into the sand.

The first lightsaber disappeared beneath the dunes.

Then the second.

She covered them carefully.

"Your journey's over," she said softly.

The wind swept across the graves.

After a moment, Rey unclipped the lightsaber she had built herself.

A yellow blade sprang to life.

She smiled faintly.

Then she turned and walked away into the fading light.

—

Several weeks later, a Jawa screamed.

"UTINNI!"

The shout echoed across the desert.

A dozen Jawas came running toward a half-buried metallic object protruding from the sand.

Tiny hands immediately began digging.

Sand flew everywhere.

Before long, two cylindrical hilts lay exposed.

The Jawas gathered around them.

One poked Anakin's old lightsaber with a tool.

Another picked up Leia's.

Confused chatter filled the air.

"What is it?"

"No idea."

"Maybe valuable."

"Maybe explosive."

"Maybe both."

That possibility interested them greatly.

The artifacts were brought aboard the sandcrawler.

For nearly an hour the Jawas examined them without learning anything.

Then one accidentally pressed an activation switch.

BZZZZZZZT.

A blue blade erupted from the hilt.

Every Jawa in the room shrieked.

The unfortunate Jawa dropped the weapon.

The lightsaber spun through the air.

A storage crate fell neatly into two pieces.

Silence.

The Jawas stared.

One slowly pointed.

Another slowly nodded.

A third immediately grabbed the lightsaber.

The room exploded into excited shouting.

"UTINNI!"

Five minutes later, the Jawas had organized an entirely unsafe demonstration.

The blue blade hummed.

A Jawa swung it at an abandoned engine block.

The engine block ceased being one object.

Cheers erupted.

Leia's lightsaber was activated next.

A light blue blade flashed into existence.

More cheering.

A support beam disappeared.

Nobody noticed.

Another support beam vanished.

Still nobody noticed.

Then one particularly enthusiastic Jawa pointed the blue blade upward.

FWOOOOM.

A glowing line appeared across the ceiling.

Sunlight poured through.

The Jawa looked up.

The other Jawas looked up.

A long pause followed.

Then a piece of metal crashed onto somebody's head.

Angry shouting erupted.

The culprit gestured defensively.

"It wasn't me."

"You were holding the laser sword!"

"The ceiling attacked first."

A second Jawa tried to prove his own competence.

Moments later he accidentally carved another hole through the roof.

Then another.

Then part of an overhead walkway collapsed.

The sandcrawler's chief mechanic buried his face in his hands.

"What are you idiots doing?"

"Science."

"This isn't science!"

"Advanced science."

By sunset, the upper deck looked like it had been attacked by orbital bombardment.

The chief mechanic surveyed the damage.

"You know what?"

"What?"

"We're selling these things."

A chorus of agreement followed immediately.

—

The collector smiled as he rotated the lightsaber under a lamp.

"Amazing."

His assistant frowned.

"They're really authentic?"

"Oh, yes."

"You're sure?"

The collector laughed.

"My dear friend, one belonged to the Skywalkers."

He carefully picked up the second hilt.

"And this one belonged to Leia Organa."

The assistant's eyes widened.

"Those Leia Organa?"

"Unless another Leia Organa helped overthrow the Empire and kill Jabba the Hutt."

The collector placed both lightsabers inside a display case.

"We'll be famous."

—

The news spread across Tatooine.

Eventually it reached a gathering of Hutt leaders.

A massive Hutt shifted his bulk atop a platform.

"Leia Organa's lightsaber?"

Another Hutt nodded.

"The very one."

A low growl passed through the room.

One elder Hutt spoke.

"Jabba never forgot her."

"He died before he could."

A few Hutts chuckled darkly.

The elder continued.

"That weapon belongs on Tatooine."

"Then buy it."

"We tried."

The elder's eyes narrowed.

"He refused."

The room grew quiet.

Then another Hutt smiled.

"Collectors can be stubborn."

"Indeed."

"But bounty hunters are persuasive."

More smiles appeared.

—

The collector's confidence lasted until midnight.

An alarm sounded.

Security guards shouted.

Blaster fire erupted outside.

The collector sat upright in bed.

"What was that?"

A second explosion shook the building.

His assistant burst through the door.

"We're under attack!"

"By whom?"

The assistant glanced toward another blast.

"I don't think they're here for antiques."

The collector reached the display room moments too late.

A tall armored bounty hunter stood beside the shattered case.

Both lightsabers hung from his belt.

The collector pointed furiously.

"You can't just take those!"

The bounty hunter looked at him.

"Apparently I can."

Then he fired a stun blast and walked away.

—

Three days later, the bounty hunter crossed a remote stretch of desert.

His speeder hummed over the dunes.

He checked his coordinates.

"Hutt rendezvous in two hours."

Easy money.

A sharp crack echoed across the desert.

The speeder exploded beneath him.

The bounty hunter hit the sand hard.

"What the—"

Another shot struck nearby.

Then another.

Tusken Raiders emerged from the dunes.

Dozens of them.

The bounty hunter cursed.

"Oh."

He drew his blasters.

The fight was brief and violent.

Several Tuskens fell.

Many more appeared.

When it ended, the desert was quiet once again.

A Tusken warrior knelt beside the body.

He noticed the lightsabers.

The warrior picked one up.

Confused murmurs spread through the group.

They carried both weapons back to their village.

—

That evening, elders gathered around a fire.

One held the blue lightsaber.

The others watched carefully.

An elderly Tusken spoke.

"The stories."

Another nodded.

"The outsider."

"The one who destroyed the village."

A younger warrior looked skeptical.

"You think this belonged to him?"

The elder traced a finger along the hilt.

"My grandfather described it."

He looked toward the stars.

"He said a blue blade came from a weapon like this."

The younger warrior fell silent.

The elder continued.

"Our dead remembered him."

He raised the lightsaber.

"Now we remember him."

The village answered with approving cries.

The lightsaber was placed among the tribe's most treasured possessions.

Leia's weapon joined it.

Neither would leave.

—

Months later, aboard a transport crossing the Mid Rim, Rey listened to a merchant's story.

"Tatooine?" she asked.

The merchant nodded.

"That's what people are saying."

"The lightsabers are really there?"

"Supposedly."

"In Tusken hands?"

The merchant shrugged.

"That's the rumor."

Rey looked out the viewport.

Her expression hardened.

—

Several weeks later she stood at the edge of a Tusken settlement.

The wind whipped her robes.

A group of warriors emerged to meet her.

Rey raised empty hands.

"I don't want trouble."

No response.

She pointed toward the village.

"I came for two lightsabers."

The Tuskens remained motionless.

One finally spoke through a translator.

"They belong to us."

"They belonged to Luke Skywalker and Leia Organa."

"They belong to us now."

Rey took a breath.

"I'm asking peacefully."

The translator crackled.

"So are we."

A long silence followed.

Then Rey said, "I can't leave without them."

The warriors tightened their grips on their weapons.

The translator replied.

"Then you cannot leave."

—

The first rifle shot came without warning.

Rey ignited her yellow blade.

Energy splashed away into the sky.

More shots followed.

She charged forward.

Sand erupted around her.

Tusken warriors rushed from concealed trenches.

A gaderffii swung toward her head.

She ducked.

Another attacker lunged from behind.

Rey used the Force to throw him backward.

The battle spread across the dunes.

Every ridge seemed to conceal more enemies.

Rey deflected shot after shot.

A dozen warriors surrounded her.

She broke through.

Another dozen appeared.

"This doesn't have to happen!" she shouted.

No one answered.

The Tuskens kept advancing.

Minutes passed.

Then longer.

The heat became unbearable.

Rey was breathing heavily now.

A rifle shot slipped through her defenses.

She staggered.

Blood appeared on her shoulder.

The warriors pressed harder.

Rey knocked several aside with a Force wave.

For a moment they retreated.

Then a horn sounded.

More Tuskens emerged from the rocks.

Rey's eyes widened.

"There are more?"

The answer arrived as another volley of fire.

She blocked most of it.

Not all.

A second wound opened.

Then a third.

Her movements slowed.

A warrior struck her ribs with a gaderffii.

Something cracked.

Rey fell to one knee.

She forced herself back up.

The yellow blade still burned.

A circle of Tuskens closed around her.

One elder stepped forward.

Across his back hung Anakin's lightsaber.

Leia's rested beside it.

The elder spoke through the translator.

"Leave."

Rey laughed weakly.

"You're giving me that option now?"

The elder said nothing.

Rey looked around.

Warriors stood everywhere.

Hundreds of eyes watched.

Slowly she raised her lightsaber.

The elder nodded.

The battle resumed.

—

When it ended, the desert was silent.

A young warrior approached the fallen Jedi.

"Is she dead?"

The elder knelt beside Rey.

After a moment he nodded.

"Yes."

The yellow lightsaber lay nearby.

The young warrior picked it up.

Its blade ignited with a snap-hiss.

Golden light reflected across the dunes.

The elder looked at it.

Then at the other two lightsabers hanging from his belt.

"Take it."

The young warrior nodded.

—

That night, three lightsabers rested together inside the Tusken village.

Blue.

Light blue.

Yellow.

Anakin Skywalker's.

Leia Organa's.

Rey's.

The elder regarded them quietly.

A child approached.

"Who owned these?"

The elder pointed to the first.

"A warrior from the old stories."

Then the second.

"A leader from long ago."

Then the third.

"The one who came today."

The child stared at the collection.

"Will others come for them?"

The elder looked toward the darkness beyond the village.

"Perhaps."

The child considered this.

"Then we'll keep them safe."

The elder nodded.

"Yes."

Far away, among those who could feel the Force, some claimed they sensed a familiar presence lingering beyond death.

But on Tatooine, life continued.

The three lightsabers remained in Tusken possession.

And the effort to rebuild the Jedi ended before it had truly begun.

---

# Part 2

Three centuries passed.

The desert winds of Tatooine never changed.

They buried old trails, uncovered forgotten ruins, and sang across the dunes exactly as they always had.

The Tusken village endured through it all.

Children grew into warriors. Warriors became elders. Elders passed into memory.

The stories changed.

As generations passed, details faded.

The ancient tale of a village destroyed by an outsider carrying a blue lightsaber disappeared first.

The story of the three weapons being taken from a fallen Jedi became fragmented.

Then that vanished too.

What remained was simpler.

The three lightsabers were sacred possessions of the tribe.

Important artifacts.

Ancient treasures.

Objects that had always belonged to the village.

Nobody remembered why.

Only that they mattered.

—

"Poro!"

The young Tusken turned.

An older warrior pointed toward a moisture collector.

"You were supposed to repair that."

Poro shrugged.

"It still collects moisture."

"It leaks."

"It leaks into the collection basin."

The older warrior paused.

"...that's not the point."

Poro grinned beneath his wrappings.

The older warrior groaned.

"You spend too much time thinking about those artifacts."

Poro immediately glanced toward the village storage hut.

The older warrior noticed.

"See?"

"They're interesting."

"They're dangerous."

"So are banthas."

"Banthas don't cut your arm off when you hold them wrong."

Poro considered this.

"That's true."

—

Later that evening, Poro sat alone inside the storage hut.

Three lightsabers rested before him.

Blue.

Light blue.

Yellow.

The ancient treasures.

The artifacts everyone warned him about.

The stories were always the same.

*"Warrior Terek activated one and sliced through his own foot."*

*"Elder Rasha nearly took off her hand."*

*"Hunter Varo accidentally destroyed a wall."*

*"Don't touch them."*

*"Don't activate them."*

*"Leave them alone."*

Naturally, this only made Poro more curious.

He picked up the blue lightsaber.

The metal felt warm.

Comfortable.

Familiar.

He wasn't sure why.

His thumb found the activation switch.

A blue blade burst into existence.

The room filled with humming light.

Poro froze.

Nothing happened.

No accidental injuries.

No loss of limbs.

No panic.

Instead he felt...

certain.

The blade seemed obvious.

He knew where it was.

He knew where it wasn't.

He knew exactly how it moved.

As though his body understood instinctively.

"Huh."

He swung it slowly.

The blade moved smoothly through the air.

He increased speed.

Still perfect.

No close calls.

No mistakes.

Poro frowned.

"Why was everyone having so much trouble?"

—

The next day he tried the second lightsaber.

A pale blue blade emerged.

Again, perfect control.

The third produced a yellow blade.

Same result.

Hours became days.

Days became months.

Whenever nobody was watching, Poro practiced.

The lightsabers fascinated him.

He found himself predicting where they would move before he moved them.

Sometimes he sensed things before they happened.

A falling tool.

An approaching bantha.

A friend entering a room.

Small things.

Yet they happened again and again.

Poro never spoke of it.

—

One evening an elder entered the storage hut unexpectedly.

The old Tusken stopped.

Poro stopped.

Three active lightsabers hummed around him.

Blue.

Light blue.

Yellow.

The elder stared.

Poro stared back.

The elder finally spoke.

"...you've been doing this for how long?"

Poro thought for a moment.

"Several years."

The elder nearly fell over.

—

The village council met the following day.

A crowd gathered around.

One elder pointed dramatically.

"He has been taking the artifacts."

"They belong to everyone."

Another elder nodded.

"They are communal property."

A third elder crossed his arms.

"You cannot keep them."

Poro looked around.

"I wasn't stealing them."

"You were."

"I always put them back."

The elders exchanged glances.

One sighed.

"That actually makes this more complicated."

Eventually the chief elder spoke.

"Poro."

"Yes?"

"You must return the artifacts."

Poro glanced toward the lightsabers.

His lightsabers.

At least that's how they felt.

He didn't know why.

But they felt like they belonged with him.

Reluctantly he nodded.

"I understand."

The elders visibly relaxed.

—

That night, everyone slept.

Or pretended to.

Poro sat awake inside his dwelling.

The village was quiet.

Moonlight illuminated the dunes.

His eyes drifted toward the storage hut.

Then toward the horizon.

Then back again.

Finally he stood.

—

Several minutes later he emerged from the storage hut carrying a bundle.

Three lightsabers rested inside.

Poro looked back one last time.

The village had been his home his entire life.

Every friend.

Every family member.

Every memory.

All behind him.

He adjusted the bundle.

Then disappeared into the desert.

—

The next morning was chaos.

"He's gone."

"What do you mean he's gone?"

"He's gone."

"And the artifacts?"

The warrior hesitated.

"The artifacts are also gone."

Silence.

Then every elder began shouting at once.

—

Search parties spread across the desert.

Weeks passed.

Nothing.

Months passed.

Nothing.

Years passed.

Still nothing.

Poro had vanished.

The lightsabers had vanished with him.

The village eventually accepted that neither would return.

—

Many years later, Poro sat beside a campfire beneath unfamiliar stars.

The three lightsabers rested nearby.

He was no longer young.

Traveling the galaxy had changed him.

Yet some things remained the same.

He still couldn't explain the strange abilities he possessed.

He still sensed things before they happened.

He still occasionally heard emotions that were never spoken aloud.

And he still had no idea why.

A rustling sound interrupted his thoughts.

Poro turned.

Something small stood at the edge of the firelight.

Green ears.

Green skin.

Large eyes.

A tiny robe.

Poro blinked.

The creature blinked back.

Neither moved.

Finally Poro spoke.

"Hello."

The creature raised a hand.

A strange feeling touched Poro's mind.

Not words exactly.

More like impressions.

Curiosity.

Interest.

Recognition.

Poro frowned.

"You can hear me?"

The feeling returned.

*Yes.*

Poro sat upright.

"Wait."

The creature nodded.

Poro pointed at his own head.

"You are talking in my head."

Another nod.

"That seems useful."

The creature smiled.

—

Over the following days they traveled together.

Communication remained unusual.

The little green stranger could speak aloud, but not in a language Poro understood.

Instead they relied primarily on the strange mental connection.

One evening Poro finally asked the obvious question.

"Who are you?"

The answer came partly through gestures and partly through thoughts.

*Grogu.*

Poro repeated the name.

"Grogu."

A nod.

Then another impression.

Old.

Very old.

Almost four centuries.

Poro stared.

"You are four hundred years old?"

Grogu nodded casually.

Poro looked genuinely offended.

"You look fantastic."

—

Weeks later they arrived on a world unlike any Poro had ever seen.

Cities gleamed beneath the sky.

Mandalorian architecture stretched across the landscape.

Ships filled the spaceports.

Grogu led him through a large complex.

Students moved through halls.

Some carried lightsabers.

Others wore training robes.

Poro slowed.

"What is this place?"

Grogu smiled.

—

A protocol droid approached.

Its polished frame reflected the sunlight.

"Oh! Excellent!" it said.

"Another visitor!"

The droid bowed.

"I am F-6PO, though everyone calls me Sixpio."

Poro nodded.

"Hello."

"Master Grogu has informed me that you may require translation assistance."

"You can understand me?"

"Of course."

Poro pointed at Grogu.

"He reads my thoughts."

"Master Grogu does many impressive things."

—

Later, they sat inside a quiet chamber.

Grogu occupied one chair.

Poro sat opposite.

Sixpio stood nearby.

The droid began translating.

"Master Grogu would like to explain something."

Poro nodded.

Grogu gestured around the temple.

"The Jedi Order."

Sixpio translated.

"The New Jedi Order."

Poro listened carefully.

Grogu spoke at length.

About the Force.

About Jedi history.

About responsibility.

About training.

About serving others.

As the explanation continued, Poro's eyes widened.

Eventually he interrupted.

"Wait."

Grogu stopped.

Poro pointed at himself.

"The Force lets people sense thoughts?"

Grogu nodded.

"And predict things?"

Another nod.

"And use lightsabers?"

Again.

Poro sat back.

"Oh."

A long pause followed.

"Oh."

Another pause.

"Ohhhhh."

Sixpio tilted his head.

"Is everything alright?"

"I think I've accidentally been a Force user my entire life."

—

The conversation continued late into the evening.

Finally Grogu asked a question.

Sixpio translated.

"Master Grogu would like to know whether you would consider joining the Jedi Order."

Poro answered immediately.

"Yes."

The droid blinked.

"You didn't even think about it."

"I have three ancient laser swords and mysterious powers."

"Fair point."

—

The ceremony was simple.

Several Jedi gathered.

Grogu stood before them.

Poro stood beside him.

A few curious younglings whispered among themselves.

"Is he really a Tusken?"

"I think so."

"I've never seen a Tusken Jedi."

"Neither has anyone else."

The ceremony concluded.

Grogu placed a hand on Poro's arm.

The assembled Jedi applauded.

Poro officially became Grogu's Padawan.

The first known Tusken Jedi in galactic history.

—

Some days later Grogu approached him.

His gaze settled on the three lightsabers.

The ancient weapons rested on a table.

Grogu looked at Poro.

Poro looked at Grogu.

"I know that look."

Grogu smiled.

"What?"

A gesture toward the lightsabers.

"Oh."

Poro laughed.

"You want them?"

Grogu nodded.

Poro considered for perhaps half a second.

"Okay."

Sixpio nearly stumbled.

"That's all?"

"They're important."

"Very."

"They should probably belong to the Jedi."

"That is surprisingly reasonable."

Poro shrugged.

"I've had them for decades."

—

The lightsabers were transferred into the care of the New Jedi Order.

Historians immediately became interested.

Archivists became even more interested.

Several nearly fainted when they learned whose lightsabers they actually were.

Poro mostly ignored the excitement.

He had a different project.

Building his own lightsaber.

—

For months Grogu guided him through every step.

Meditation.

Focus.

Construction.

Crystal attunement.

Patience.

An enormous amount of patience.

Finally the day arrived.

Poro ignited the completed weapon.

A brilliant blade emerged.

For a long moment he simply stared.

Then he looked at Grogu.

Grogu smiled.

Poro smiled back.

"This one feels right."

Grogu nodded.

—

The years that followed were filled with training.

Poro learned lightsaber forms.

Force techniques.

History.

Discipline.

Wisdom.

Sometimes he succeeded spectacularly.

Sometimes he failed spectacularly.

Occasionally Sixpio had to rescue expensive temple equipment from his experiments.

One afternoon, after accidentally launching a training remote through a window, Poro sighed.

"I think I understand why the Jedi emphasize patience."

Across the room, Grogu looked at the shattered window.

"Good."

Poro glanced at the damage.

"You've been trying to teach me that lesson for years, haven't you?"

Grogu nodded.

"Yes."

Poro groaned.

And Grogu laughed.
