# Part 1

The throne chamber of Exegol shook beneath the fury of the Force.

Dark energy crackled through the air. The vast Sith citadel trembled as Emperor Palpatine stood before Rey, his body restored through the stolen power of the dyad he had drained from her and Ben Solo.

The Emperor's eyes blazed with triumph.

"You are nothing!" he snarled. "A scavenger girl is no match for the power in me. I am all the Sith!"

Lightning exploded from his hands.

Rey staggered beneath the assault. Every muscle screamed in agony. The storm of darkness threatened to consume her.

Then she heard them.

The voices of the Jedi.

Generations of guardians stretching back through history.

Anakin Skywalker.

Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Yoda.

Ahsoka Tano.

Qui-Gon Jinn.

And many more.

They stood with her through the Force.

Rey opened her eyes.

She reached down and took hold of Luke Skywalker's lightsaber. Then she drew Leia Organa's saber as well.

The blue blades ignited.

Palpatine unleashed more lightning.

Rey crossed the sabers before her and advanced.

"And I..." she said through clenched teeth.

The Force surged through her.

"...I'm all the Jedi."

The Emperor's lightning struck the crossed blades and rebounded.

Palpatine's confidence vanished.

"No!"

The torrent of dark energy turned back upon him.

The chamber filled with blinding light.

The Sith cultists screamed.

Stone cracked.

Ancient machinery exploded.

Palpatine himself began to disintegrate.

"No! No! No!"

The power consumed him.

Then, with a final eruption of light, Darth Sidious was destroyed.

The throne chamber fell silent.

The storm above Exegol began to break apart.

Across the planet, the Final Order fleet faltered.

Resistance pilots watched in astonishment as enemy vessels lost coordination.

"Their fleet is stuck here!" Poe Dameron shouted over the comms. "They're toast! Come on! Finn, you seeing this?"

Above the command ship, Finn and Jannah fought desperately as destruction spread around them.

Far overhead, Lando Calrissian led thousands of civilian ships into battle.

The galaxy had answered the call.

And the Sith were finally falling.

—

Deep within the collapsing citadel, Rey stood alone.

The effort had taken nearly everything she had.

Her legs trembled violently.

The voices of the Jedi faded.

The two lightsabers slipped from her hands and clattered against the stone floor.

She sank to one knee.

Every breath burned.

But she was alive.

Exhausted.

Weak.

Barely able to move.

Yet alive.

She looked upward and managed a tired smile.

"It worked..."

The floor shook violently beneath her.

The citadel was beginning to collapse.

Rey forced herself back onto her feet.

Then she sensed him.

Ben.

Alive.

Coming toward her.

Moments later, Ben Solo emerged through the ruined passageways.

His clothing was torn from battle. Dust covered him. Blood stained parts of his tunic.

For a brief moment, fear crossed his face.

He had felt the enormous surge in the Force.

He had felt Palpatine die.

And he had feared what the victory might have cost.

Then he saw her standing there.

Alive.

Conscious.

Looking back at him.

Relief flooded through him so powerfully that he nearly stumbled.

"Rey."

She smiled weakly.

"Hi."

A laugh escaped him despite everything.

He crossed the distance between them and caught her before her shaking legs could give way.

She leaned heavily against him.

"Tired?" he asked softly.

"Very."

The chamber groaned around them.

Chunks of stone crashed from the ceiling.

Ben looked upward.

"We should probably leave."

"That sounds wise."

Together they hurried through the collapsing ruins.

—

By the time they reached the surface, the battle was ending.

The Sith fleet was burning across the skies of Exegol.

Resistance ships filled the heavens.

Cheers erupted over every comm frequency.

The war was over.

The Final Order had been defeated.

Palpatine was gone.

And for the first time in many years, Ben Solo stood beneath an open sky free from the shadow of Kylo Ren.

—

The celebration among the Resistance was unlike anything either Rey or Ben had ever witnessed.

Pilots embraced.

Technicians cheered.

Soldiers laughed and cried.

Families reunited.

The galaxy had survived.

When Rey emerged from the transport carrying the recovered Skywalker lightsabers, dozens of people rushed to greet her.

Finn wrapped her in a fierce embrace.

Poe nearly crushed her with a second one.

Chewbacca roared so loudly that half the nearby personnel turned toward him.

Even Ben found himself receiving grateful nods from Resistance members who had witnessed his role in the battle.

Not everyone knew what to think of him.

Many likely never would.

But for the moment, the victory overshadowed everything else.

The war had ended only hours earlier.

There would be time later for difficult questions.

For now, people simply celebrated being alive.

—

Several weeks later, after the immediate work of reconstruction and recovery had begun across the galaxy, Rey and Ben departed together aboard the Millennium Falcon.

Their destination was Tatooine.

The desert world where Luke Skywalker's journey had begun.

The journey felt strangely peaceful.

For the first time since they had met, neither was hunting the other.

Neither was running from anyone.

The galaxy was finally quiet.

BB-8 rolled happily through the corridors.

D-O followed after him.

R2-D2 occupied his usual place aboard the ship.

C-3PO spent much of the journey commenting on navigation, atmospheric conditions, and various topics nobody had asked about.

Some things never changed.

—

Twin suns hung low over the horizon when the Falcon landed near the abandoned Lars homestead.

The desert wind stirred the sand.

Rey stood beside Ben at the edge of the old moisture farm.

In her hands were Luke Skywalker's lightsaber and Leia Organa's lightsaber.

Together they descended into the courtyard.

Time and weather had worn the place.

But its history remained.

Rey knelt.

Carefully, she buried both lightsabers beneath the sand.

Luke's.

Leia's.

Brother and sister resting together.

When she finished, she stood silently for several moments.

Ben stood beside her.

Neither spoke.

Words felt unnecessary.

Eventually they climbed back toward the surface.

Near the Falcon, Lando Calrissian waited with Chewbacca.

The old general smiled.

"Finished?"

Rey nodded.

"Finished."

Chewbacca rumbled softly.

Ben looked toward him.

"Chewie should go home."

The Wookiee turned toward him.

Ben continued.

"Kashyyyk."

Chewbacca's eyes widened slightly.

"You haven't seen your family in a long time."

The great Wookiee let out a quieter sound.

Almost thoughtful.

Rey smiled.

"You deserve that."

Lando crossed his arms.

"I can take him."

Ben glanced toward the Falcon.

"Actually, we'd like you to."

Lando raised an eyebrow.

"What do you mean?"

Rey exchanged a glance with Ben.

Then she smiled.

"The Falcon belongs with the people who love her."

Lando looked genuinely surprised.

"You're giving me the Falcon?"

"Take Chewie home," Ben said. "After that, she's yours."

For several seconds Lando simply stared.

Then he laughed.

"I knew I liked you two."

Chewbacca barked a protest.

Lando pointed at him.

"Yes, yes, you'll still get visitation rights."

The Wookiee growled again.

—

Before departing, Rey approached BB-8.

"Poe's probably wondering where you are."

The little droid chirped.

When they returned to the Resistance gathering point a few days later, Poe indeed came looking for his old companion.

"There you are."

BB-8 whistled happily.

Poe crouched beside him.

"Well?"

The droid beeped.

Poe looked toward Rey.

"You planning to keep him?"

"I figured he should decide."

Poe nodded.

"Fair enough."

He looked directly at BB-8.

"You want to come with me or stay with Rey?"

BB-8's antenna twitched.

He answered immediately.

A rapid burst of cheerful binary followed.

Poe laughed.

"Well, that's pretty clear."

"What did he say?" Finn asked.

Poe stood.

"He says he's staying with Rey."

BB-8 spun in a circle.

Rey smiled and scratched the top of his dome.

"I guess that's settled."

The droid emitted a triumphant whistle.

—

Eventually, their small group departed together.

Rey.

Ben.

BB-8.

D-O.

R2-D2.

And C-3PO.

For the first time in years, they had nowhere they absolutely needed to be.

So they traveled.

World after world passed beneath the stars.

Sometimes they helped communities still recovering from the war.

Sometimes they searched for lost Jedi knowledge.

Sometimes they simply explored.

Along the way, Rey and Ben spent countless hours talking.

Really talking.

Not as enemies.

Not through Force bonds filled with conflict.

As themselves.

One evening, aboard a transport crossing between systems, they sat alone in the common area while the droids occupied themselves elsewhere.

Stars drifted outside the viewport.

Neither spoke for a while.

Then Ben broke the silence.

"I still see it."

Rey looked at him.

"My father."

His voice was quiet.

"The bridge."

Han Solo's face appeared in his memory as clearly as if it had happened yesterday.

"I killed him."

Rey reached over and took his hand.

Ben stared at the stars.

"I spent years convincing myself it made me stronger."

His jaw tightened.

"It didn't."

Silence followed.

"I keep thinking about everything that happened afterward."

His voice became even quieter.

"My mother."

Leia.

"My uncle."

Luke.

"If I'd listened sooner..."

He couldn't finish the sentence.

Rey squeezed his hand gently.

"Ben."

He looked at her.

"You were manipulated for years."

He shook his head.

"That doesn't excuse what I did."

"No."

Rey's voice remained calm.

"It doesn't."

She moved closer.

"But you were a victim too."

Ben frowned slightly.

"Snoke spent years shaping your fears. Palpatine was behind him the entire time."

Rey held his gaze.

"They targeted you from childhood."

The words hung in the air.

Ben looked away.

Part of him wanted to reject them.

Another part knew they were true.

"You still came back," Rey continued.

"You chose to come back."

Her eyes softened.

"I'm just glad you're here."

Ben looked at her again.

She smiled.

"I'm glad Ben Solo returned."

For several moments neither spoke.

Then Ben laughed quietly.

Not because anything was funny.

Because the weight he had carried for so long felt slightly lighter.

"Thank you."

Rey tilted her head.

"For what?"

"For not giving up on me."

His voice was sincere.

"For believing there was still something worth saving."

Emotion flickered across Rey's face.

"I never stopped."

The stars drifted silently beyond the viewport.

Ben reached up and gently brushed a strand of hair from her face.

Neither looked away.

The distance between them disappeared.

Their lips met softly.

The kiss lingered longer than the one they had shared amid the chaos of Exegol.

There was no collapsing citadel.

No battle.

No fear.

Only the quiet hum of a ship traveling among the stars.

When they finally pulled apart, Rey rested her forehead against his.

Neither said anything.

Nothing needed to be said.

Outside, the galaxy continued turning.

And together, they watched the stars.

---

# Part 2

Months passed as Rey and Ben traveled from world to world.

The war was over, but the future remained unwritten.

Everywhere they went, they encountered reminders that the Jedi were gone. Entire generations had grown up without them. Children heard stories of Luke Skywalker as though he were a figure from myth. Ancient temples stood empty. Archives had been lost. Knowledge had been scattered.

One evening, as they sat together in the restored common room of their ship, Rey finally voiced the thought that had been lingering in both their minds.

"We can't keep wandering forever."

Ben looked up from the datapad he had been reading.

"No."

"The Jedi need to return."

He nodded.

For a moment neither spoke.

Then Rey smiled.

"I've been thinking about locations."

Ben immediately looked suspicious.

"You've made a list."

"I may have made a list."

"You definitely made a list."

She grinned.

"Coruscant."

Ben considered it.

"The center of galactic civilization."

"Exactly."

He shook his head.

"Too political."

Rey frowned.

"That's not entirely unreasonable."

"Every government in the galaxy would want influence over a new Jedi Order."

She sighed.

"Fine."

He smirked.

"Next."

"Jakku."

Ben stared at her.

"Why?"

"It was my home."

"Your home was a wrecked starship and a scavenger yard."

"That's not the point."

"It is absolutely the point."

She laughed despite herself.

"Fine. Not Jakku."

"Thank you."

"Ajan Kloss."

Ben thought for a moment.

"Too associated with the Resistance."

"You think that matters?"

"I think the Jedi should belong to the entire galaxy, not one faction."

Rey reluctantly nodded.

"Fair."

She glanced down at her list.

"Dagobah."

Ben immediately shook his head.

"The swamp?"

"Yoda liked it."

"Yoda also spent years living alone there eating roots and talking to strange visitors."

Rey crossed her arms.

"That's not a reason."

"It's a swamp."

She rolled her eyes.

"Fine."

"Thank you."

She looked down again.

"Ahch-To."

This time Ben's expression softened.

The island world had been Luke's refuge during his final years.

It was also the birthplace of the Jedi Order.

For a moment he considered it.

Then he slowly shook his head.

"The Jedi survived there."

"And?"

"We need a place where they can live."

Rey nodded.

She understood.

Ahch-To was sacred.

But it felt more like a monument than a future.

Silence settled between them.

Then Rey looked up.

"Ossus."

Ben froze.

The name hung in the air.

Rey's smile faded.

He had not immediately rejected it.

Instead, his gaze drifted away.

Far away.

His expression became distant.

Thoughtful.

Painful.

Rey suddenly understood why.

Years ago, Luke Skywalker had established a Jedi temple on Ossus.

Years ago, Ben Solo had destroyed it.

Neither spoke.

The silence stretched.

Then a familiar presence entered the room.

Warm.

Gentle.

Bright.

Ben looked up.

Luke Skywalker stood nearby, translucent and luminous.

His Force spirit regarded Ben calmly.

Ben immediately rose to his feet.

"Master."

Luke smiled.

"Ben."

Rey remained seated.

She watched quietly.

Luke folded his hands behind his back.

"You know why you hesitate."

Ben said nothing.

"You fear Ossus."

The words landed with uncomfortable accuracy.

Ben lowered his eyes.

Luke continued.

"It reminds you of what happened there."

Images flashed through Ben's memory.

The temple burning.

Students fleeing.

Darkness.

Regret.

Shame.

Luke's voice remained calm.

"Fear is not the Jedi way."

Ben looked up.

Luke met his gaze.

"If you truly believe you have returned to the light..."

His expression softened.

"...then face your past."

The room became silent again.

For several moments neither spoke.

Then Luke smiled.

A familiar smile.

The same one that had encouraged countless students.

The same one that had inspired hope across the galaxy.

And then his spirit faded into the Force.

Ben remained motionless.

Rey waited.

Several long seconds passed.

Finally Ben nodded.

"The temple should be on Ossus."

Rey smiled.

"I agree."

—

A few weeks later, a ship descended through the skies of Ossus.

Rey.

Ben.

R2-D2.

C-3PO.

BB-8.

D-O.

Their new home awaited them.

What they found was heartbreaking.

Luke's old temple still stood.

Barely.

Walls had collapsed.

Sections of the structure had been blasted apart.

Nature had reclaimed portions of the ruins.

Years of abandonment were visible everywhere.

For a long time, neither Rey nor Ben spoke.

Ben stared at the destruction.

His destruction.

Rey gently touched his arm.

"We can fix it."

He nodded.

And so they began.

—

The rebuilding took months.

Stone by stone.

Wall by wall.

Room by room.

Ben used the Force to lift collapsed sections back into place.

Rey reconstructed damaged halls.

R2 provided archived structural records.

BB-8 enthusiastically assisted in almost every task, whether needed or not.

D-O followed him everywhere.

C-3PO frequently complained about construction dust.

Nobody listened.

Slowly, the temple returned.

The scars vanished.

The damage healed.

Ancient halls once again stood proudly beneath the Ossus sky.

Meditation chambers were restored.

Archives rebuilt.

Living quarters repaired.

Training grounds reconstructed.

At last, the temple stood as it had before its destruction.

Perhaps even better.

One evening, as the sun set beyond the horizon, Rey and Ben stood outside together.

The rebuilt temple rose behind them.

Home.

For the first time in many years, Ben truly felt that word apply to him.

—

Life settled into a peaceful rhythm.

Rey frequently traveled offworld searching for Force-sensitive children.

Ben often remained behind, maintaining the temple and studying recovered Jedi texts.

One afternoon, while Rey was away, Ben sat alone within the archive chamber.

The room was quiet.

Then he felt a familiar presence.

He looked up.

Leia Organa stood before him.

Her Force spirit radiated warmth.

Ben immediately rose.

"Mom."

Leia smiled.

The sight alone nearly brought tears to his eyes.

For several moments neither spoke.

Then Leia stepped closer.

"There is something you need to understand."

Ben listened carefully.

"You spent years fascinated by your grandfather."

His expression darkened slightly.

Leia continued.

"You built fantasies around him."

Ben looked away.

"Maybe."

"You knew stories."

Leia's voice remained gentle.

"But not the whole story."

He looked back at her.

"You misunderstand who Anakin Skywalker was."

Silence filled the room.

Ben slowly nodded.

"Then how do I learn?"

Leia smiled.

She raised a hand and pointed.

Toward R2-D2.

The astromech sat quietly nearby.

"He knows."

Ben blinked.

"Artoo?"

Leia nodded.

"He was there."

The realization struck immediately.

Of course.

R2 had accompanied Anakin through most of his life.

Leia smiled one final time.

"Ask him."

Then her spirit vanished.

Leaving only silence behind.

—

A few minutes later, Ben sat beside R2-D2 in one of the temple's projection chambers.

C-3PO stood nearby.

The golden protocol droid looked delighted.

"Oh my, this should be fascinating."

Ben looked toward R2.

"Can you show me?"

The astromech beeped confidently.

A holographic projector emerged.

Blue light filled the room.

And the past came alive.

—

Ben watched a small slave boy race through the streets of Mos Espa.

Anakin Skywalker.

Nine years old.

Excited.

Energetic.

Hopeful.

Then came the Boonta Eve Podrace.

R2 projected footage from spectator areas and later recordings.

Ben watched Anakin pilot at impossible speeds.

He watched the child cross the finish line.

Victorious.

Smiling.

Alive with joy.

The scenes changed.

Anakin aboard a Naboo starfighter.

Accidentally entering battle.

Destroying the Trade Federation control ship.

Saving an entire world.

The young boy laughed afterward.

Ben found himself smiling too.

More footage followed.

Anakin and Padmé walking through Varykino Villa.

Talking quietly.

Laughing.

The famous conversation about sand.

Even Ben had to suppress a grin at some of the awkwardness.

The images changed again.

Shmi Skywalker's funeral.

Anakin standing in grief.

Silent.

Broken.

The first shadows becoming visible.

Then Geonosis.

The Petranaki Arena.

Jedi igniting lightsabers.

Battle droids everywhere.

The first battle of the Clone Wars.

Again the images shifted.

Years passed.

Now Anakin stood as a Jedi Knight.

Confident.

Fearless.

Leading clone troopers into battle.

Rescuing civilians.

Fighting impossible odds.

Laughing with Obi-Wan.

Protecting his friends.

A true hero.

The Hero With No Fear.

Ben watched for hours.

The image he had built in his mind began changing.

Growing more complete.

More human.

Finally the footage reached Polis Massa.

Padmé's death.

The birth of Luke and Leia.

The beginning of another generation.

R2 emitted a satisfied beep.

Ben sat quietly.

"Then what happened?"

The astromech answered.

C-3PO translated.

"Master Anakin became Darth Vader shortly afterward. Artoo says that unfortunately he rarely encountered Lord Vader afterward and possesses very little direct footage from those years."

Ben nodded.

That part of the story he already knew.

Too well.

The years of darkness.

The Empire.

The Sith.

The atrocities.

But one question remained.

He looked at R2.

"And the end?"

The astromech immediately projected more images.

Ben leaned forward.

The forest moon of Endor appeared.

Battle.

Chaos.

The fall of the Empire.

Then another scene.

Luke Skywalker supporting a mortally wounded Darth Vader.

Helping him aboard a shuttle.

The black armor was shattered.

The Emperor was dead.

The war was ending.

Ben watched silently.

Vader spoke.

Luke knelt beside him.

Then came the moment.

"Take off this mask."

Luke removed it.

For the first time, Ben saw Anakin's face.

Not Vader's.

Anakin's.

An old man.

Dying.

At peace.

A father looking at his son.

The hologram continued.

Luke carried the body.

Prepared the funeral pyre.

Stood alone beside the flames.

Honoring his father.

Ben watched every second.

At last the projection faded.

Silence filled the chamber.

Ben sat motionless.

For years he had idolized Darth Vader.

The conqueror.

The Sith Lord.

The symbol of power.

Now he finally understood.

That was not how Anakin Skywalker had died.

He had died redeemed.

He had returned to the light.

Just as Luke had always said.

Just as Leia had tried to tell him.

Ben lowered his head.

"Thank you, Artoo."

The astromech emitted a gentle beep.

Ben remained there for a long time afterward.

Thinking.

Reflecting.

Understanding.

—

Several days later, Rey's ship appeared in the skies above Ossus.

She returned with several young Force-sensitive children she had discovered during her travels.

Excitement filled the temple as the newcomers explored their future home.

Ben emerged to greet them.

Rey stepped down the ramp.

The moment their eyes met, she paused.

She felt it immediately.

The dyad connected them as naturally as breathing.

She sensed memories.

Reflection.

Understanding.

Healing.

She knew where Ben had spent the past few days.

She knew what Leia had told him.

She knew what R2 had shown him.

And she knew what he had finally realized about Anakin Skywalker.

Ben smiled.

A quiet smile.

Different somehow.

Lighter.

Rey smiled back.

She did not need to ask a single question.

The Force had already given her the answer.

Together they turned toward the temple, where the first new generation of Jedi waited.

---

# Part 3

Two years passed.

The temple on Ossus flourished.

The empty halls that Rey and Ben had painstakingly rebuilt no longer echoed with silence. They were alive with activity from dawn until well after sunset.

Young Jedi trainees filled the courtyards.

Some practiced meditation beneath ancient trees.

Others studied history in the archives.

Groups of initiates sparred with training sabers beneath the supervision of older students.

Laughter was common.

Curiosity was encouraged.

Questions were welcomed.

The restored Jedi Order was still small compared to the institution that had once existed during the Republic, but it was growing steadily.

Most importantly, it was healthy.

The mistakes of the past were discussed openly rather than hidden away.

Students learned not only the triumphs of earlier Jedi generations but also their failures.

Rey and Ben worked together as teachers.

Though their methods sometimes differed, they complemented each other well.

Rey was patient and encouraging.

Ben possessed a deeper understanding of fear, temptation, and recovery than almost anyone alive.

The students respected both of them.

Many looked up to them.

Neither had entirely grown comfortable with that fact.

—

One quiet afternoon, after classes had concluded, Ben found himself organizing recovered archive materials in one of the temple's upper chambers.

The work was interrupted by a familiar presence.

He looked up.

Rey stood in the doorway.

Something about her expression immediately caught his attention.

She seemed unusually nervous.

That alone was enough to make him set aside what he was doing.

"Everything okay?"

Rey walked into the room.

Instead of answering immediately, she approached him and gently took both of his hands in hers.

Ben blinked.

Now he was definitely concerned.

"Rey?"

She smiled.

Then took a breath.

"Will you marry me?"

For several seconds, Ben simply stared.

Not because he was uncertain.

Because he had not expected the question to arrive so suddenly.

The surprise was evidently visible on his face.

Rey laughed.

"That wasn't the reaction I imagined."

Ben finally recovered enough to answer.

"Yes."

Her smile widened.

"Yes?"

"Yes."

He squeezed her hands.

"Of course yes."

Relief washed across her features.

Then she laughed again.

"I probably should have planned a speech."

"You probably should have."

"I had one."

"What happened to it?"

"I forgot all of it."

Ben grinned.

"Good thing I said yes."

She rolled her eyes.

Then became slightly more serious.

"I think we should formalize this."

Ben nodded.

He understood immediately.

The temple had become a home.

The students looked to them for guidance.

Their relationship was no secret.

"Set a good example?"

Rey smiled.

"Exactly."

Ben nodded.

"I agree."

For a few moments they simply stood together.

Then Ben tilted his head thoughtfully.

"You know..."

"That phrase usually means trouble."

"It is interesting."

Rey immediately looked suspicious.

"What is?"

"Our marriage."

She waited.

Ben continued.

"It joins the Skywalker and Palpatine bloodlines."

For a brief moment, neither spoke.

"Two of the strongest Force-sensitive families in recent galactic history."

He shrugged.

"And families that spent decades trying to destroy each other."

Rey groaned.

Ben immediately realized his mistake.

"That's not what I meant."

"I know."

She smiled despite herself.

"Ben."

He looked at her.

"I already made my choice."

Her voice was gentle.

"I rejected Palpatine's legacy a long time ago."

Ben nodded.

"You did."

"I chose the Skywalkers."

"You did."

He squeezed her hand.

"I'm sorry."

Rey shook her head.

"I understand what you're trying to say."

She looked out the nearby window toward the temple grounds.

"The history is unusual."

"That's one way to describe it."

A grin appeared on her face.

Then another thought occurred to her.

"Actually..."

Ben immediately recognized the look.

"What?"

"We need a family name."

Ben thought about it.

"Solo?"

"Maybe."

"Skywalker?"

"Also maybe."

Silence followed.

Then Rey smiled.

"What about both?"

Ben blinked.

"Both?"

"Solo Skywalker."

He considered it.

Rey Solo Skywalker.

Ben Solo Skywalker.

The name felt strangely natural.

A bridge between different parts of their family histories.

He smiled.

"I like it."

"So do I."

"And if we ever have children..."

"They'll be Solo Skywalkers too."

Ben nodded.

"That settles it."

—

Preparations for the wedding spread excitement throughout the temple.

The younger students were especially enthusiastic.

Several appeared to view the entire event as more exciting than any Jedi lesson ever devised.

Invitations went out across the galaxy.

Finn arrived first.

Poe followed shortly afterward.

Rose came as well.

Jannah attended.

Lando Calrissian arrived in spectacular fashion and spent the first hour greeting nearly everyone present.

Chewbacca came from Kashyyyk, accompanied by members of his family.

The gathering quickly became one of the largest celebrations Ossus had seen in generations.

—

The wedding took place in one of the temple's central courtyards beneath a clear sky.

Ancient stone columns surrounded the ceremony.

Students filled the seats.

Friends gathered nearby.

Droids occupied several rows.

R2-D2 seemed unusually pleased with the proceedings.

C-3PO was openly emotional.

The ceremony itself was simple.

Rey and Ben stood together before their friends, their students, and the Order they had rebuilt.

Promises were exchanged.

Commitments made.

The Force flowed peacefully around them.

At the conclusion of the ceremony, applause erupted throughout the courtyard.

Several students immediately began cheering.

Chewbacca's celebratory roar startled at least three younger initiates.

Lando laughed so hard he nearly lost his footing.

For a while it seemed the ceremony had concluded.

Then a group of younger students approached.

Their spokesperson, a girl of perhaps twelve years, stepped forward.

"We have a proposal."

Ben immediately looked concerned.

Rey looked curious.

The student continued.

"We think there should be a new Jedi wedding tradition."

Poe visibly perked up.

This sounded entertaining.

Rey folded her arms.

"What kind of tradition?"

The students exchanged glances.

Then several answered simultaneously.

"A ceremonial duel."

Silence.

Complete silence.

Ben stared.

Rey stared.

Finn immediately started laughing.

Poe joined him.

The students remained perfectly serious.

Rey looked at Ben.

Ben looked at Rey.

Then back at the students.

"You can't be serious."

"We are."

"Completely serious."

"It's symbolic."

"It demonstrates mutual trust."

"It celebrates skill."

"It looks cool."

The final argument received several approving nods from other students.

Ben buried his face in one hand.

Rey was struggling not to laugh.

Unfortunately, the students were absolutely sincere.

After several minutes of discussion, both bride and groom reluctantly agreed.

—

A training circle was quickly prepared.

The students gathered eagerly.

The guests moved into position.

R2 projected a small holographic scoreboard for reasons known only to himself.

Rey ignited a training saber.

Ben ignited his own.

They faced one another.

Both were smiling.

The duel began.

What followed was less a battle and more a graceful demonstration.

Neither sought victory through overwhelming force.

Instead they moved with precision and elegance.

The students watched in fascination.

The younger initiates cheered every successful maneuver.

Several older students quietly analyzed techniques.

Poe accepted wagers from people who should have known better.

The sparring match continued for several minutes.

Neither gained a clear advantage.

Then, at the very end, Rey managed a clever disarming maneuver.

Ben's training saber flew from his hand.

A heartbeat later, Rey's blade rested lightly against his shoulder.

Silence.

Then applause erupted.

Ben laughed.

"I almost had you."

"You definitely did not."

"I was very close."

"You were not."

The students celebrated as though they had just witnessed a legendary duel.

In their minds, perhaps they had.

—

That evening, after the guests had settled into their accommodations and the celebrations had finally begun winding down, Rey and Ben sat together on a balcony overlooking the temple.

The night was peaceful.

Lights glowed throughout the complex below.

Students still wandered between buildings, talking excitedly about the day's events.

Rey leaned comfortably against him.

For a while they simply enjoyed the quiet.

Then she smiled.

"You know..."

Ben immediately recognized that tone.

"What?"

"If the old Jedi Order could see today's wedding..."

He groaned.

Rey continued anyway.

"They'd be rolling in their graves."

Ben rolled his eyes.

"Probably."

"They absolutely would."

"They might."

"Ben."

"Fine."

A reluctant smile appeared on his face.

"They absolutely would."

Rey laughed.

"So you admit it."

"I admit nothing except that Master Yoda would probably have needed several hours to process the ceremonial duel."

That made Rey laugh even harder.

Ben eventually joined her.

Together they sat beneath the stars, watching the lights of the restored temple on Ossus.

Their temple.

Their home.

And the future of the Jedi Order that they had built together.

# Part 4

Fifteen years passed.

The Jedi Temple on Ossus had grown into a thriving center of learning.

What had begun as a handful of students and a restored ruin now housed hundreds of Jedi at various stages of training. The courtyards were filled with the sounds of instruction, meditation, debate, and friendly competition.

The Order remained far smaller than the one that had existed during the Republic's height, but it was stable, respected, and deeply connected to the wider galaxy.

Among its students were two who happened to receive more attention than most.

Leiana Solo Skywalker, age twelve.

And her younger brother, Obikin Solo Skywalker, age ten.

Like many siblings, they spent a great deal of time arguing, competing, and occasionally cooperating.

This particular afternoon was no exception.

—

The temple courtyard echoed with the hum of training sabers.

Several groups of students practiced under the supervision of instructors while others sparred independently.

At the center of one training circle, Leiana and Obikin faced each other.

Their duel had already attracted a small audience.

Mostly because everyone knew exactly how this would go.

Obikin charged first.

Leiana sidestepped.

He attacked again.

She blocked.

A quick exchange followed.

Training blades flashed.

The two circled one another.

Then, with a movement so fast that several younger students missed it entirely, Leiana twisted her wrist and struck Obikin's saber hilt.

His weapon flew from his hand.

It landed several meters away.

Silence.

Leiana lowered her blade.

"I win."

Obikin stared at her.

Then pointed accusingly.

"You cheated."

Leiana blinked.

"How?"

"You did that thing."

"What thing?"

"The thing you always do."

Leiana folded her arms.

"You mean disarming you?"

"Yes."

"That's not cheating."

"It should be."

Several nearby students laughed.

One instructor quietly walked away before becoming involved.

Leiana smirked.

"Maybe you'll win next time."

Obikin immediately pointed at her again.

"See? That's exactly what a cheater would say."

—

Later that evening, most students had returned to their residential quarters.

The temple gradually settled into its nighttime quiet.

Leiana and Obikin, however, had other plans.

A question had been bothering them for weeks.

After dinner, they found their parents in their private quarters.

Rey sat reading at a small table.

Ben was organizing several datapads nearby.

The children entered.

Both parents immediately recognized the look.

Questions were coming.

Rey closed her book.

Ben set down the datapads.

Leiana spoke first.

"So you guys defeated Palpatine?"

Rey nodded.

"Yes."

Obikin jumped in.

"And there are no more Sith?"

Rey nodded again.

"As far as we know."

Leiana exchanged a glance with her brother.

Then asked the question.

"If there are no more Sith, why do we still need Jedi?"

The room became very quiet.

Rey looked at Ben.

Ben looked at Rey.

Neither answered immediately.

To their surprise, they realized they had never really considered how to explain this to a child.

Or, more accurately, two children who were currently examining every flaw in their reasoning with merciless precision.

Finally Ben attempted an answer.

"Well..."

He thought carefully.

"If somebody falls to the dark side, other Jedi can stop them from taking over the galaxy."

Obikin frowned.

He clearly wasn't impressed.

"But if there are no Jedi..."

Ben immediately sensed danger.

"...then there is nobody who can fall to the dark side."

Silence.

Obikin looked very pleased with himself.

Leiana nodded.

"That makes sense."

Rey considered it.

Then shrugged.

"Fair point."

Ben turned toward her.

"Not helping."

"I said it was a fair point."

"You're supposed to support me."

"I'm supporting intellectual honesty."

Ben groaned.

The children grinned.

—

A few minutes later, Rey stood up.

"I think we need a better answer."

She motioned toward the door.

"Come on."

The family left their quarters together and headed deeper into the temple.

Their destination was the library.

Over the years, Rey and Ben had collected an enormous amount of recovered Jedi knowledge.

Ancient texts lined the shelves.

Databanks contained restored records from many eras.

The library had become one of the most valuable collections of Jedi history in existence.

Rey walked among the shelves until she found what she wanted.

She pulled out a volume.

The Jedi Order in the High Republic.

She sat down at a large table.

The others joined her.

Rey opened the book.

"You see," she began, "about five hundred years ago, there were a lot of Jedi."

Leiana leaned forward.

"So before the Empire?"

"Long before."

Rey turned a page.

"Back then, the Sith were believed to be extinct."

Obikin blinked.

"Really?"

Ben nodded.

"The Jedi genuinely thought the Sith were gone."

Rey continued.

"Now, they weren't actually gone."

She tapped the page.

"They were hidden."

"Functionally, though, the Sith weren't openly operating against the Republic."

"No invasions?" Obikin asked.

"No."

"No galactic wars?"

"No."

"No giant fleets?"

"No."

The boy looked surprised.

"Then what were the Jedi doing?"

Rey smiled.

"Exactly."

She pointed at the book.

"The Jedi continued to exist."

"They continued growing."

"They trained new generations."

"They served the Republic."

Leiana thought about that.

"So you're saying the Jedi stayed around even when they thought the Sith were extinct."

"Yes."

"Which means no Sith wasn't enough reason for them to disband."

"Correct."

The girl considered this.

Then nodded slowly.

"I think I understand."

She glanced at Obikin.

"Sort of."

Obikin folded his arms.

"So you're saying the Jedi stayed around because maybe the Sith weren't really extinct."

Rey hesitated.

"Partly."

"And we're rebuilding the Jedi because maybe the Sith come back."

Ben nodded.

"That's certainly part of it."

"The dark side isn't something that simply disappears forever."

"It's wise to be prepared."

He leaned forward.

"But that's not the whole story."

Rey smiled.

"The Jedi weren't specialized anti-Sith warriors."

The children looked surprised.

"They weren't?"

"No."

Rey turned several pages.

"The Sith were enemies of the Jedi."

"But the Jedi had many responsibilities besides fighting Sith."

She pointed toward an illustration.

"They preserved peace."

"They mediated disputes."

"They negotiated conflicts before they became wars."

"They protected communities."

"They confronted criminals, pirates, and other dangerous groups."

"They explored."

"They studied."

"They preserved knowledge."

"They taught."

"They tried to better understand the Force."

Ben nodded.

"The Jedi Order existed to serve the galaxy."

"Not just to fight the Sith."

The children sat quietly.

Thinking.

The answer was clearly more complicated than either had expected.

Finally Ben reached toward the book.

"Let's keep reading."

Rey smiled.

"Good idea."

—

The hours passed unnoticed.

Pages turned.

Questions were asked.

More questions followed.

The history of the High Republic unfolded before them.

Explorers.

Diplomats.

Teachers.

Guardians.

Researchers.

Healers.

The Jedi had filled many roles throughout their history.

Some succeeded.

Some failed.

The family discussed all of it.

Sometimes they debated.

Sometimes they laughed.

Sometimes they stopped to consider difficult lessons.

Long after the rest of the temple had gone to sleep, the four of them remained gathered around the table.

By the time they finally closed the book, everyone had learned something.

Leiana had gained a broader understanding of what the Jedi could be.

Obikin had discovered that apparently every answer led to three more questions.

Ben had been reminded that rebuilding the Order was about more than preserving the past.

And Rey found herself appreciating once again how much there still was to learn.

Together they left the library and walked through the quiet halls of the temple toward their quarters.

Above them, the stars of Ossus shone through the night sky.

The Jedi Order endured.

Not because the Sith existed.

But because there was always more to learn, more to teach, and more ways to serve the galaxy.

---

# Part 5

Several years after Leiana and Obikin's late-night discussion in the library, the Jedi Order on Ossus continued to grow.

New students arrived regularly.

Knights traveled the galaxy on assignments of diplomacy, exploration, and service.

The temple had become a respected institution once again.

One afternoon, Rey and Ben were seated in a council chamber reviewing logistics reports.

The work was not particularly exciting.

Supply inventories.

Transportation schedules.

Construction requests.

The sort of responsibilities that seemed to multiply whenever an organization became successful.

A knock sounded at the entrance.

"Enter," Rey called.

The door slid open.

A Zabrak Jedi Knight stepped inside.

Devon Dabila.

Only recently knighted by Rey herself.

He had been away from Ossus for several months on a journey that had eventually taken him to Tatooine.

Beside him stood a young Tusken Raider.

The child was wrapped in traditional desert clothing and carried himself with obvious curiosity.

Devon bowed respectfully.

"Masters, I have returned with a promising new student and some... interesting artifacts."

Ben immediately noticed the peculiar tone in Devon's voice.

Then Devon placed two objects on the table.

The room fell silent.

Rey's eyes widened.

Ben stared.

Neither needed more than a second to recognize them.

Luke Skywalker's lightsaber.

Leia Organa's lightsaber.

The same two lightsabers they had buried years earlier near the Lars homestead.

Devon folded his arms.

"Poro claims that these belonged to his village."

Rey blinked.

"What?"

She looked toward the young Tusken.

Then smiled.

"Well, hello, Poro. Nice to meet you."

The child replied in Tusken.

C-3PO, who happened to be present nearby, immediately stepped forward.

"Master Rey, he says it is an honor to meet you."

Poro continued speaking.

Rey listened carefully.

Eventually she gestured toward the lightsabers.

"How exactly did those end up in your possession?"

Poro began his explanation.

Threepio translated.

"One day, a group of Jawas found two interesting metal tubes buried in the sand near the Lars homestead."

Ben slowly lowered his head into one hand.

Rey already looked concerned.

"The Jawas scavenged the tubes and sold them to a collector."

The story continued.

"Word eventually reached the Hutts."

Ben groaned quietly.

Poro continued.

"The Hutts realized the tubes were valuable and sent a bounty hunter to steal them."

Rey looked at Ben.

Ben looked at Rey.

Neither said anything.

The situation was somehow becoming worse with every sentence.

"The bounty hunter was intercepted and ambushed by warriors from my village."

Poro spoke proudly.

"The tubes became our prize."

The young Tusken paused before continuing.

"Later, our elders examined them."

His voice became more serious.

"They recognized one of the tubes."

Rey and Ben exchanged another glance.

Neither needed to guess where this was going.

"The elders said it belonged to a mysterious sky-lord."

Poro continued.

"A long time ago, that sky-lord descended upon another Tusken settlement and slaughtered everyone there."

Silence filled the room.

Neither Rey nor Ben spoke.

They both knew exactly what he was describing.

Years earlier, R2-D2 had shown them extensive records of Anakin Skywalker's life.

They knew about Shmi Skywalker's death.

They knew about the massacre that followed.

And they knew exactly whose lightsaber had been used.

Poro continued.

"The elders decided to keep the two tubes."

"Why?" Obikin would have asked immediately had he been present.

Fortunately, he wasn't.

Poro answered anyway.

"They became symbols of victory over the mysterious sky-lord."

Threepio translated with perfect neutrality.

"My village developed many stories about them."

The Tusken gestured enthusiastically.

"Many people wanted to examine them."

Then his tone became noticeably less enthusiastic.

"Many accidentally removed their own limbs."

Ben winced.

Rey closed her eyes briefly.

"The elders eventually decided the objects were too dangerous and forbade anyone from using them."

Rey nodded.

"That makes sense."

Poro tilted his head.

"But I did not think so."

The statement immediately sounded ominous.

"The more they told me not to look at them, the more I wanted to."

Ben muttered something under his breath.

Rey tried not to smile.

Eventually Poro continued.

"I snuck into the storage room."

Devon already looked like he had heard this story many times.

"I activated them."

The young Tusken spread his hands.

"The blades felt natural."

His voice became thoughtful.

"I always knew where they were."

"I never felt like I would accidentally lose a limb."

"I thought perhaps the elders had invented the danger stories."

Ben coughed suspiciously.

Poro continued.

"Then a guard caught me."

"Ah," Rey said.

The Tusken nodded.

"I was going to be punished."

Another pause.

"I did not wish to be punished."

Rey already knew where this was heading.

"So I stole the tubes."

Ben nearly laughed.

Poro remained completely serious.

"And left."

"For months I wandered the deserts alone."

The room became quieter.

That part was less amusing.

Then Poro pointed toward Devon.

"One day Master Devon found me."

The young Tusken's posture visibly brightened.

"He stopped immediately."

"He asked if I was okay."

"I said yes."

"He asked why I was alone."

Poro looked toward Devon.

"I told him everything."

Devon smiled slightly.

Poro continued.

"Then he told me there was another home."

"He asked if I wanted to come with him."

The young Tusken nodded.

"I said yes."

He spread his arms.

"And now I am here."

Threepio finished translating.

The room remained silent for several moments.

Then Poro continued.

"During the journey, Master Devon explained the Jedi Order."

"And the Force."

His tone became slightly uncertain.

"I think I understand."

A pause.

"Well."

Another pause.

"Mostly."

Everyone smiled.

Poro brightened again.

"I am looking forward to my new life on Ossus."

Rey nodded warmly.

"We're glad you're here."

Then she glanced at the two lightsabers.

"Poro?"

The Tusken looked at her.

"Would you mind if I kept these?"

He looked at the weapons.

Then shrugged.

"Of course."

Threepio translated.

"They seem important to you."

Rey nodded.

"They are."

She carefully picked them up.

Then looked toward Devon.

"I think you've already demonstrated excellent judgment."

Devon looked mildly relieved.

"Thank you, Master."

"I want you to formally take Poro as your Padawan learner."

The young Tusken's eyes widened beneath his wrappings.

Devon smiled.

"It would be an honor."

Rey nodded.

"And Poro."

"Yes?"

"You should build your own lightsaber."

The child looked down at Luke and Leia's weapons.

Then back at Rey.

"A lightsaber of my own?"

"One that suits you."

A broad smile appeared beneath the wrappings.

"I would like that very much."

A few minutes later, Devon and his new Padawan departed.

The council chamber became quiet once again.

Only Rey and Ben remained.

Along with two very familiar lightsabers resting on the table.

For several seconds neither spoke.

Then Ben finally broke the silence.

"I cannot believe that when we buried these things we completely forgot about the Jawas."

Rey groaned.

"Yeah."

She looked at the sabers.

"The way we buried them, in hindsight we were practically feeding them to the Jawas for free."

Ben laughed.

"Not just the Jawas."

He counted on his fingers.

"The Hutts."

"The bounty hunter."

"The Tuskens."

He shook his head.

"Oh, Tatooine..."

Rey nodded.

"Yes."

She folded her arms.

"Tatooine is not a child's playground."

Ben looked at the recovered weapons.

"I honestly cannot believe that after all of that..."

He gestured vaguely.

"...we somehow still managed to get them back."

Rey laughed.

"At this point that's pure luck."

Ben nodded.

"Agreed."

He looked toward the lightsabers.

"I assume we don't bury them again."

Rey immediately shook her head.

"Absolutely not."

"We do not repeat our mistakes."

Ben pointed at the table.

"Good."

Rey carefully gathered the two weapons.

"We'll keep them here."

She looked around the temple.

"Where they belong."

Ben nodded.

"Absolutely."

—

The conversation eventually drifted toward the story they had just heard.

Specifically one part of it.

The massacre.

Neither found it easy to ignore.

"It really is strange," Rey said quietly.

Ben nodded.

He already knew what she meant.

The weapon Anakin Skywalker had used during one of the darkest moments of his life had somehow ended up in Tusken possession generations later.

Not only that.

The Tuskens had preserved it as a symbol of victory over the very figure who had once attacked them.

And now the weapon had somehow found its way back into Jedi hands.

Again.

The chain of events felt almost impossible.

Ben looked down at Luke's lightsaber.

His grandfather's weapon.

For a moment he thought about how little Poro actually knew.

To the Tusken villagers, the attacker was simply a mysterious sky-lord.

Nothing more.

No name.

No history.

No understanding of what had happened afterward.

They had no idea that the man responsible would eventually become Darth Vader.

No idea that he would later be redeemed.

No idea that his descendants now helped lead the Jedi Order.

The irony was impossible to miss.

—

Years passed.

Poro became one of the temple's most promising Padawans.

Devon proved an attentive and demanding mentor.

The young Tusken thrived under his guidance.

He constructed his own lightsaber.

Developed his own strengths.

And steadily grew into a capable Jedi.

Just as importantly, he made friends.

Leiana and Obikin quickly took a liking to him.

The three were often seen together.

Sometimes they trained.

Sometimes they studied.

Sometimes they simply wandered the temple grounds talking for hours.

Students grew accustomed to seeing them as a group.

A Solo Skywalker daughter.

A Solo Skywalker son.

And a Tusken Padawan from Tatooine.

Together.

Laughing.

Learning.

Growing.

Many decades earlier, the Tuskens had become Anakin Skywalker's first true enemy.

The massacre had marked one of the first moments he reached for the dark side.

One of the first steps down a path that would eventually lead to Darth Vader.

Yet now, generations later, something very different could be seen on Ossus.

Anakin Skywalker's descendants trained alongside a Tusken who knew the story of the mysterious sky-lord.

They sparred together.

Studied together.

Shared meals together.

Became friends.

There was no hatred between them.

No desire for revenge.

No ancient grudge waiting to be settled.

Only friendship.

And on many evenings, as students crossed the courtyards beneath the setting sun, it was not uncommon to see Leiana, Obikin, and Poro walking side by side through the temple grounds, talking about lessons, lightsaber techniques, and whatever adventure they hoped to find next.
